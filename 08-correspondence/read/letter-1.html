Letter from Don Glendenning to David Cadogan discussing governance and tax implications of the Cadogan Foundation's relationship with CFPEI. Demonstrates Don's expertise in charitable law, CRA interpretation, and his hands-on involvement in foundation governance decisions.

Mr. David Cadogan

PO Box 397

Miramichi, NB

E1N 3A8


Dear David:

Cadogan Foundation


This is a follow-up to our recent telephone conversation about community foundations generally and the possibility of a “home” for the Cadogan Foundation within the Community Foundation of Prince Edward Island.


First of all, I apologize for the long delay in responding to your questions.  The idea of direct delivery was not on our radar screen as such.  On only one or two occasions that I recall, did we have to make arrangements with a “qualified donee” to supervise a grant to an organization that itself was not a qualified donee.  In addition, we have been asked a number of times recently about writing receipts for fund raisers in which the charitable component of a contribution may accrue to the benefit of an organization not considered a qualified donee by CCRA...  


At our most recent board meeting, our Board decided that it will only issue receipts for the charitable portion of a fund raiser if the proceeds are to be credited to an endowment we now administer. 


I have had a number of discussions with CRA about community foundations also being a direct deliverers of charitable services; this does not present a problem to CRA provided the letters patent allow for it.  If there is a doubt about a foundation’s ability to direct deliver, a letter can be directed to CRA and they will give an interpretation.  Community foundations of Canada, however, are not keen on the idea although, I am told, that a few community foundations do exercise this prerogative.  In any case, our Board decided not to pursue the issue at this time.  


During my discussions with CRA, I raised the matter of an award to an individual that was not a scholarship.  Again, CRA has no objection in principle provided that our letters patent allow us to do it, the process is an open one and the population to which it applies is not too narrowly defined.  I would be quite prepared to seek a CRA interpretation if needed. 


At our most recent board meeting, I raised the issue of CFPEI administering the Cadogan Foundation; our Board would be most open to the idea.  There are a number of possible arrangements; each is explained briefly below:


A Managed Fund under which a charitable organization places funds with us primarily for investment purposes.  Managed funds are owned by the charitable organization and may be withdrawn in whole or in part at any time.  Under this scenario, the Cadogan Foundation would withdraw funds annually and make its own decisions as to grants including the issuance of cheques.

An Agency Fund owned by CFPEI with the earnings being paid annually to the agency in whose name the fund was established.  Earnings can be withdrawn from an agency fund but not the principal.  Again, under this scenario, the Cadogan Foundation makes decisions about grants.

An Interest Fund owned by CFPEI with decisions about grants being made by CFPEI for the purposes defined in the agreement.  In making grants, CFPEI would welcome recommendations from you and/or the Cadogan Foundation.


We have developed a number of pro forma agreements.  I am enclosing one for each of the scenarios noted above.  They are proforma only and leave room to accommodate other possibilities of interest to the donor.


I hope, David, this is of some help in your deliberations.  Do not hesitate to write or call if you need further information.  As mentioned, I will be out of country until October 16.  Sometime after that, likely in late October, I plan a visit to the Miramichi and would be delighted to meet at a mutually convenient time to talk further about community foundations in general and the Cadogan Foundation in particular.


Regards


Don
