Letter from Don Glendenning to Secretary to the Governor General discussing proposals for an Order of Canada Association. Presents Don's vision for fellowships, conferences focused on Canada's future, and community engagement—reflecting his thought leadership on national service and civic responsibility.

Stephen Wallace

Secretary to the Governor General

1 Sussex Drive

Ottawa, Canada1A 0A1


Dear Sir:


I am delighted to receive your letter and certainly support the establishment of an Order of Canada Association as outlined in your letter; while I do not know all the Members of the Order on PEI personally, I expect there will be considerable interest in such an activity.

As for particular initiatives, my mind turns such thing as:

  • An annual local or provincial gathering with national gatherings perhaps every five years, during which we welcome new members, network with other members and raise the profile of the Order.  This would be useful if we did nothing more.
  • I Like the idea of periodic conferences with a future focus; Sessions not designed to give answers but rather to provide participants with a framework for addressing issues.  Could even focus on the future of the community where we are meeting.
  • Stimulste public discussion on Canada at age 20-0.  While I understand the value of scholarships I would not favour such  an initiative; yhe link between scholarship and sercive to one’s country is tenuus to say the least and, from my perspective, the education system generally lacks a service orientation.
