Philosophical letter defining quality education through UNICEF criteria (healthy learners, safe environments, relevant content, learner-centered processes, outcomes including attitudes/skills/knowledge). Don situates education as support for the "road of life" journey and argues these criteria reflect community concerns better than standardized test results alone. Advocates supplementing PISA/PCAP with broader quality measures.

March 21, 2016


Dear Editor:


A Quality Education


The road of life is a way of describing our journey between birth and death, and education provides help as we travel.  Help comes in many forms and from many people and its focus is on increasing and improving the knowledge, skills and attitudes needed to meet challenges along the way.  As a result, our quality of life will depend, to a large degree, on the quality of education that we experience.  


In 2000, UNICEF identified the characteristics of a quality education as one with:

Learners who are healthy

Environments that are healthy and safe

Content that is relevant

Processes that are learner centered

Outcomes that include attitudes, skills and knowledge


It seems to me that these are a good reflection of community concerns, lend themselves to large scale assessment, would give us a broader view of quality in our schools and be a welcome supplement the results of PISA, PCAP and school tests. 

Sincerely


Don Glendenning

A long-time student of education
