A different draft letter responding to questions about education received during the Christmas season. Uses a bullet-point format to outline organizational and systemic issues in PEI's education system, including lack of autonomy for schools, limited accountability, and politicization. Notes plans for future commentary on teaching/learning models.




Editor

Dear Sir

Re-setting direction

Over the Christmas season, I was asked on a number of occasions for my views on education; I thought I would make those views the subject of this, my first letter in the new year. In brief, public concern is growing an optimism yhat improvement is on the way is falling. On the organization side:

  • Information is an education service and should be expanded.

  • Role of Department should expanded from that of delivering programs to one of encouraging and supporting learning

  • Schools need greater autonomy (OECD)

  • Programming should reflect increased learner centeredness

  • Fear actor probably reflects lack of autonomy, nature of organization and degree of politicization.

  • Broadening services would likely increase participation rates

  • System noted for lack of accountability

  • Care should be taken when using common educational (education, learners, outcomes, etc.

I’ll try to have comments about the teachb=ng/learnibg modl in a later letter

Sincerely



Don Glendenning

A longtime student of education
