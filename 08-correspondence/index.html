This is a fundraising letter dated July 10, 2019, written by Don Glendenning introducing the concept of numeracy to potential donors. Don explains that while PEI math test scores have risen, practical numeracy skills remain weak on the shop floor. He proposes a DACUM analysis to identify the 100+ math skills needed for everyday island life at a cost of $15,000, noting his prior success using this methodology at Holland College. The letter invites donations through the Community Foundation of PEI. This document showcases Don's continued professional engagement, his understanding of applied mathematics education, and his fundraising acumen in retirement.

Fundraising letter from Don Glendenning soliciting donations for the Citizen's Math project through Community Foundation of PEI. Explains that Education 20/20's innovative approach starts with surveying math skills needed in daily life rather than traditional curriculum development. Notes the $15,000 project cost with half for facilitator fees, the rest for analysis and local support. References that most people capable of leading such analysis have retired or moved away, demonstrating Don's resourcefulness in bringing in Meg Savory from British Columbia. Shows his vision of practical math education and commitment to educational innovation in PEI.
