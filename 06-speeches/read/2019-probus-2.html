About public education in PEI


Members of the Men’s Charlottetown PROBUS Club WWW.PROBUSpei.ca were briefed at their recent meeting on some of the issues facing education in Prince Edward Island. A survey in late 2018 in the Souris area by Education 20/20 Inc. revealed an interest in on-line courses but respondents were not sure that the local internet can accommodate the latest technology. Residents also pointed to the extra cost involved when travelling to courses located away from their home area.


Although numbers may be small, local agriculture, fisheries and tourist operations are continuing to invest in technology and need training for themselves and their employees. While they appreciate the need for advanced courses, they also spoke of the need for practical and hands- on training as well; they’d also like to see a greater emphasis on information on up-coming courses, employability skills and career planning.


The presentation by Dr. Don Glendenning, President, Education 20/20 Inc. drew many questions and comments from members on such issues as mathematics, arts and literature, school autonomy and the matter of testing – local, national and international.

 

The Club meets monthly on the 2nd Thursday and the program usually includes a local speaker on a subject of interest to the members.
