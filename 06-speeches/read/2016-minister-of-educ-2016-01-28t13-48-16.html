Presentation notes to Minister Doug Currie expressing concern about PEI's education system. Don emphasizes education's importance to personal and community development, notes favorable conditions for change, and raises critical questions about spending on remedial education, lack of challenge-for-credit options, outdated provincial education philosophy, and the need for systemic engagement and innovation.
Speeches & Presentations

Presentation 

To

Minister Doug Currie


Opening comments

  • Thanks
  • Respect you for what you have done
  • You did not cause the problems but you have the responsibility to solve them


My concern is about education

  • Importance to each person
  • Importance for future of the Island
  • The need for change
  • We could be a demonstration centre for quality education
  • Conditions are favourable for change


Questions I am being asked:

  • How come we justify spending millions of dollars te teach adults the basic skills that were already taught at school?
  • PEI only province that does not have a challenge for credit option for high school students; PLAR, exemptions? Even though the study commissioned by thew Department recommended it
  • How come we are still operating under a philosophy approved in 1989 which has so many unmeasurable goals?
  • We ask schools to engage; why shouldn’t the Department staff be expected to engage also
  • Why the commitment to one of the most expensive delivery models yet conceived; compare with health
  • Why doesn’t the Department  recognize the importance of information to the improvement of education
