These personal notes capture Don's key takeaways following a project presentation. He identifies community needs for better information about educational offerings, a local person to promote new programs, and hands-on practical education emphasis. Key possible projects include supervised online courses with a local learning manager, support for transforming Souris School into a community school, encouraging post-secondary institutions to offer program modules locally, and initiating a research project on workforce training delivery to rural communities. The notes emphasize the importance of local coordination and community-level programming.

SOURIS PROJECT

My takeaway from presentation

Souris 1919-1

Needs  

  • More information about offerings
  • A person in the community to promote new offerings
  • A local learning manager
  • Increased emphasis on hands-on, application, practical education (Need to clarify what this means)
  • Training support needed for all domains:  attitudes, skills and knowledge
  • Encourage local cooperation and support

 

Possible projects:

  • Supervised on-line courses, need a facility with 10 computers and a local learning manager; Offer training in 2-hour blocks on Monday, Wednesday and Friday thereby reserving time for learning manager to promote
  • Practical education was mentioned a number; my search of the internet doesn’t define practical, investigation needed as to what “practical” means in Souris.
  • Support Souris School in becoming a full-service or community school
  • Encourage postsecondary institutions to offer modules of regular programs in Souris Area
  • Encourage manufacturers to provide product demonstrations in Souris Area to which students and the public would be invited
  • Increase short, part-time night classes some of which could be self supporting.
  • A research project focused on delivering workforce training to small/rural communities.
