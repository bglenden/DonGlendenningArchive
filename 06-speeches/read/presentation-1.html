A comprehensive presentation document (January 16, 2022) synthesizing Glendenning's education reform analysis for public communication. Structured around PEI context (160,000 residents needing continuous learning), Key Definitions (Education, Educated, Teacher, Student, School, Education System, Good Education), Typical Education Services (Governance, Instruction, Products/Technology, Information, Counselling, Professional Support, In-service Training), and Principles (learners learn at own pace, collaboration benefits learning). Major section addresses Structural Issues (school/education confusion, clarity gaps, unclear purpose, product-focused structure, autonomy constraints, unstated accountabilities, cultural barriers, absence of market-driven self-correction mechanisms). Education Issues include competency vs. outcome confusion, collaboration barriers, inadequate progress reporting, relevance gaps, passive development role, narrow field focus, and student ranking problems. Conclusion: "System needs an overhaul." The document reflects his mature thinking on systemic reform synthesized for presentation to community and government audiences.

INTRODUCTION

PEI

  • 160,000 residents

  • All need to learn; change/develop what they know, believe feel, can do, value

  • That change process is education

  • Education – important for individuals and for the community

  • All should be encouraged to learn.

  • Some changes can be made by selves; sometimes learners need help/support

  • People/government think it important that citizens be educated

  • Government set up a Dept devoted to helping citizens get an education– encourage, support and regulate education – problem is task not adequately described.


KEY DEFINITIONS

  • Educate - Help people change the way they think, feel or act

  • Education-a sector of the economy concerned with helping people learn

  • Purpose of education system – to help people change the way they way they think, feel or act

  • Teacher- a person, usually paid, who organizes and creates experiences for other people as a way of helping them change the way they think, feel or act.

  • Student – a person on a teacher’s list

  • Education system – services put in place by a community to support learning

  • School – a facility, usually purpose built, where students and teachers meet; also defined as a method or pattern of helping others learn

  • Education system – services put in place by a community in support of furthering the education of its citizens

  • Good Education – stage or level of educational development that allows a person to deal with demands of daily life
