Visionary notes on contemporary classroom transformation through information/communication technology and collaborative approaches. Emphasizes reimagining students as creators and discoverers rather than instruction targets. Advocates project-based learning enabling autonomy and target-specific achievement. Notes classroom physical design relatively unchanged for decades despite pedagogical evolution (references Winnetka, Illinois L-shaped room example and University of Illinois Centre for Innovation in Teaching/Learning). Briefly conceptualizes connecting students with broader world through technology. Highly sketchy notes suggest early ideation stage or presentation outline. Contrasts "classroom little to do with education process" with learning evolution. Addresses "[learning evolving] fast but environment haven't changed in 00 years" (typo suggesting "100"). Represents Don's engagement with pedagogical innovation trends.
