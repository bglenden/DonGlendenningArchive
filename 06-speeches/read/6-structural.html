An editor's letter or public essay examining structural issues in PEI's education system. Defines education as helping people change how they think, feel, and act; outlines six life roles and seven essential education services. Argues that government embedding of education services restricts professional autonomy and suggests education needs broader community engagement and clearer purpose definitions.

Editor

Issues in public education

Guardian

For some time now, I have engaged Islanders in conversations about public education, learning paths, continuing education, citizen math, curriculum development, professional autonomy and accountability, politicization, transition to employment and the list goes on. 

My own view of education is quite simple.  Learning is a very personal matter and is about people changing the way they think, feel and act; education is about people helping  other people change the way they think, feel or act and a good education is about developing skills, knowledge and attitudes needed to function as a member of a democracy. The role of the state is to encourage, support and regulate education; it also usse education as a tool to grow and maintain Prince Edward Island as a place we will continue to call home. My search of the literature reveals that  typically, Islanders fill six life roles: individual, citizen, family member, learner, consumer and producer and  that, typically an education system provides the following services: instruction, human resources, learning products and technology, , information and counselling, funding, quality control and governance.  

  • Governance & leadership: leadership, liaison, support, research, advocacy; quality assurance. 
  • Instruction: presentation, demonstration, practice, diagnosis, coaching, record keeping, guided practice
  • Learning products and technology: texts and references, self-instructional material , assistive technologies, internet, software, moocs
  • Human resources: teachers, administrators, teaching assistants, specialists, guidance counsellors
  • Quality assurance: credentials, qualifications, proficiency level
  • Funding: capital, operating: transportation
  • Information and counselling: information, counselling, assessment, diagnosis, referral. advocacy, promotion
