Catering to a range of achievement levels


The Sydney Morning Herald of Saturday, July 22, 2015 carried an item based on a report by the Grattan Institute on the wide range of achievement levels in a typical classroom in Australian schools. In Math, for example the range could be as much as 8 years. I’m told that the same applies to classrooms in PEI and results in both frustrated teachers and students. I disagree, however, with the conclusion that, and I quote, “it is not possible to remove the spread of grade levels and, therefore teaching methods must be adjusted.” That’s thinking inside the box and come from being wedded to grouping by age level for all activities and at all costs.


Based on my own experience and a brief scan of the literature, I found the following alternatives:

  • Flexible grouping. I visited a high school once where all students studied math at 9:00 am, English at 10:00 am and science at 11:00; for the remainder of the day students were with their peers.

  • Peer teaching where students at higher levels in math help others at lower levels.

  • Part time teacher teachers or teachers that move from school to school.

  • The application of more, better or different technology.

  • Greater us of teaching assistants.

  • Clinics and tutoring services (basic skills come quickly to mind)


Our current system of age-based grading is convenient and easy to administer, but it does so at a cost to student achievement.
