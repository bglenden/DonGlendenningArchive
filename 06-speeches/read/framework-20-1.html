This comprehensive education framework document articulates Don's fundamental philosophy on education and learning. It provides key definitions distinguishing learners, education, instruction, learning, teaching, and education systems. Operating beliefs emphasize that education occurs beyond schools, that home is the major contributor, and that institutions' importance is declining. The document identifies typical functions and support services of education systems, provincial government mandates, and multiple service providers. It catalogs education issues including service gaps, politicization, lack of autonomy, and over-emphasis on knowledge. The framework identifies barriers to change (politicization, bureaucracy, inertia) and characteristics of quality systems (accessible, affordable, flexible, accountable, transparent). Key intervention points address assessment, mission, government role, and autonomy.

About Education

An Education Framework

0-1



INRODUCTION

Prince Edward Island, Canada’s smallest Province has a population of 155,000 people. Like Canadians everywhere, they are individuals, family members, citizens, consumers, producers and learners (1). All are educated; nature and range varies; many want and need more. Education is a Provincial responsibility; most provinces are focused on instruction in institutions. This brief document attempts to broaden the education horizon and sketch out a framework for improvement. It will likely be of greatest use to new entrants and volunteers.


KEY DEFINITIONS

  1. Learner – a person engaged in changing behavior

  2. Education – any experience that has a formative effect on the way a person feels, thinks or acts.

  3. Instruction (Sometimes known as schooling) – learning under the direction of teacher(s)

  4. Purpose of education – to help people gain the attitudes, skills, knowledge of citizenship needed to thrive in later life.

  5. Role of schools – to assist young people develop essential skills needed of citizens in a liberal democracy society.

  6. Learning – changing the way we think, feel or act

  7. Teaching – helping others change the way they think, feel or act

  8. Education system – a set of components, relationships and services put in place by a community to encourage and support the education of its citizens A school system - institutions, programs and services provided and encouraged by or with the approval of a

  9. community through its government to provide formal education for its residents


OPERATING BELIEFS -

  1. Each learner is unique as a result of genetics, prior experience and aspirations.

  2. We are educated whether or not we go to school

  3. Home is the major contributor to one’s education

  4. Parents want their children to be educated not just schooled;

  5. Each person is responsible for his/her own education

  6. Educating is a developmental process.

  7. The Importance of institutions (schools, colleges and universities) in acquiring an education is on the decline

  8. Education systems lack incentives to improve instruction, increase number served or save time

  9. Neither establishing nor awarding levels of achievement are educational functions

  10. The objective is to deliver educational services if, as and when needed
