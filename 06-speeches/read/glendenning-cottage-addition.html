(100-200 words):** Design analysis document for exterior siding options for the Glendenning cottage addition/renovation project. Don methodically evaluates four siding options based on multiple criteria: colour, board width, surface finish, groove patterns, and cost. Options evaluated include cedar rough-sawn (Option One), Cape Cod style (Option Two), vinyl (Option Three), and spruce panels (Option Four). The document demonstrates systematic decision-making: comparing existing stained vertical siding characteristics with alternatives, noting aesthetic considerations like colour matching and paint/stain durability, and evaluating cost implications. The preferred option ultimately recommends using spruce 4x8 panels while painting the entire cottage to achieve uniform appearance and address matching concerns. Reflects Don's methodical, analytical approach to problem-solving.

Glendenning cottage Addition

Exterior siding

Factors:

  • Colour
  • Width of board
  • Matching colour, pattern, surface
  • Groove – width and depth
  • Cost


Present siding

  • 4x8
  • Stained
  • Vertical
  • 5/8
  • 6 inch panel
  • Rough sawn


Options:

One:

  • 1x6 cedar rough side out;  results in V groove
  • Available at Atlantic    -Wholesalers; Garfield Street
  • Natural colour; would have to be painted/stained
  • Readily available


Two:

  • Cape Cod
  • 1x6 with groove but less pronounced that current siding
  • Painted
  • Will fade at different rate than existing
  • Factory will color match but the original is stain and this is paint


         Three

  • Vinyl

Four 

  • Spruce 4x8 x5/8 panels
  • Natural 


PREFERRED OPTION

  • Remove existing siding from North face and north end of west side to sheathe east side of extension (gable  end) including gable
  • Use 4x8 spruce panels for north face  and west side as needed
  • Pray for matching paint
  • Paint the entire cottage
