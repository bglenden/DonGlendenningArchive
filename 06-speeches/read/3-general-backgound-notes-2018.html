Foundational framework notes from 2018 establishing Don's educational philosophy and analysis approach. Opens with rhetorical "Why am I here?" reflecting on education's importance despite limited public discussion, institutional narrow views, and knowledge of improvement possibilities. Identifies Premier as responsible for all Islander education. Analyzes learners (150,000 Islanders with diverse ages, interests, cultures, aspirations), introduces "Road of Life" concept (six roles: individual, family member, learner, citizen, consumer, producer), articulates education functions/beliefs, and defines key concepts (education, schooling, education quality). Advocates eight-year improvement timeline with sequential steps from mandate/mission definition through functional analysis, organization, autonomy/accountability, competency frameworks, and instructional models. Lists contemporary education "Turmoil" (curriculum handbooks, provincial reforms). Extends to regional education office proposals, learning managers/advisers, learner-centered approaches, and self-paced learning. Establishes Don's foundational thinking framework.
