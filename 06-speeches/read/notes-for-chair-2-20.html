Chair's notes for the January 6, 2016 second meeting, framing the discussion within a human-centered "Islanders on the road of life" metaphor. Don's notes establish the meeting's purpose: understanding what help people need and what kinds of support are available. The notes define basic skills categories: reading, writing, speaking, listening, mathematics, digital communication, and personal management. Don's preparatory research included outcomes/competencies from multiple jurisdictions (PEI, Quebec, Alberta), OECD frameworks, entrance requirements, and essential/employability skills standards. The notes outline inquiry into what employers and institutions seek, possible gaps causes, next steps, and the goal of identifying a common master list of basic skills. The notes acknowledge social promotion as a fundamental challenge and address employer capacity to differentiate between students with and without basic competencies.
