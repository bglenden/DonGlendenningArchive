Speaking notes for dedication ceremony at Milton/North Yorke River Road on November 5, 2008. Structured as welcome remarks explaining CFPEI's mission, the Living Trust origin story honoring Dr. Keuper's conservation work, and recognition of donors and honorees. Names specific individuals being remembered (Martha Burka, Roddie and Avis Cummings) and Island Sandblasting employees. Concludes with thanks to organizers.

Notes for Living Trust

North Milton

November 5, 2008


1 Welcome 

All

Introduce CFPEI people present

A special occasion as we 

remember family, friends and colleagues

In so doing, we

enhance the natural beauty of this community

protect our environment

2 CFPEI 

A volunteer driven, non-profit and Island wide charitable organization

A new breed of charitable organization

More than 150 sister institutions across the country.

Our Mission: “To improve the quality of life in PEI 

by Linking people who care with causes that matter

today’s event is a good example of the role of a community foundation

Also build and administer endowments towards the same end


3 Origin of Living Trust

Dr. Charles Keuper 

Moved to PEI after retirement

Involved in many activities

loved PEI landscape

Family decided to honour and remember him through support for a living trust


4 Today we remember and honour friends  family and colleague

Generally through trees planted elsewhere along the trail

Specific friends family and colleagues through named trees
