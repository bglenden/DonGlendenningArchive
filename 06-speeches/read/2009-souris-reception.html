Speaking notes for Souris community reception (November 21, 2009) featuring co-presenters. Don's segment emphasizes community foundation innovation in charitable giving, noting 160 Canadian foundations. Highlights geographic model of giving (Souris Fund supporting local initiatives), describes innovative examples including US couple establishing nurse scholarships for Souris, Cumberland-Rocky Point Women's Institute converting school funds to book grants. November 5, 2009


COMMUNITY FOUNDATION OF PRINCE EDWARD ISLAND


Speaking Notes for Reception 

November 21, 2009 2:30 to 4:00 pm 



INTRODUCTION - CAROL

Good afternoon and welcome.

I have been a big fan of the Foundation for the past 5 or 6 years – from the time that a friend of mine (Margaret Ching) was elected to the Board of Directors.  The philosophy of the Foundation made so much sense to me and I was very pleased and honoured to be asked to be a member of the Board.


The business of The Community Foundation of PEI is to connect people who care with causes that matter.  Your donation to the Foundation (for which you will receive a tax receipt) will remain intact with a percentage of the revenue being donated annually to a community cause of your choice


Locally, there is a Souris Fund – income can be used for any charitable purpose in the Souris area.  This is a result of an anonymous donation some years ago and at this time there is roughly $30,000.00 in the fund.  Some of the recipients of grants from this fund are The Souris High School Band, Harbourview Training Centre and The PEI Agricultural Fair (Dundas Plowing Match).   


.  Dr. Glendenning will give you much more information on how your donation can be used.
