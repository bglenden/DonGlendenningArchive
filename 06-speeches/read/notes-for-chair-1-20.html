Personal notes prepared for Don Glendenning as chair of the first stakeholder meeting. These notes outline his opening remarks, establishing the meeting's scope (not a general education review), acknowledging student-reported gaps, and framing the discussion around youth and community benefit. Don's notes reference preparatory research gathered: outcomes/competencies from PEI, Quebec, and Alberta; OECD competencies; college/university entrance requirements; essential and employability skills; school course outlines. The chair's notes outline the discussion flow: hear diverse perspectives (students, employers, post-secondary, school boards, Department of Education), brainstorm possible causes (documentation, communication, unclear/unreasonable requirements, inadequate learning, social promotion, changed graduation criteria), and propose next steps including identifying a common master list of basic skills. These notes reveal Don's thorough preparation and strategic thinking.
