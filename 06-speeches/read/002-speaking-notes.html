April 2014 speaking notes on education system faults, emphasizing lack of learner-centeredness, accountability, and government-run rigidity. Proposes immediate and long-term changes including external qualification authority and educational think tank, reflecting Don's lifelong education advocacy.
Speeches & Presentations

April 1, 2014

SPEAKING NOTES  



Opening remarks:

Education is most important issue of the day

Cost of speaking out too high for most Islanders

It is my profession

My comments are not unique to PEI

Those in charge today did not create the system today but they have the responsibility


System faults

Have a school system masquerading as an education system 

Lacks service that is equivalent to that of a doctor, lawyer or accountant 

System is not capable of changing itself

Because government runs schools, every decision becomes a political one

Only sector where no one benefits from an improvement in productivity

Not learner centered

Significant gap in expectations between what public wants and what system delivers

Performance standards

Lacks accountability


Changes - Immediate 

Challenge for credit/exemptions

Initiative to improve math and communication 

Public statement on role of schools

 advisory committee


Changes – long term

External qualification authority

Educational think tank

School autonomy

Engage public in developing a new vision statement


Implementation:

Premier – vision statement

Minister – Public engagement in futuring exercise

Increasing school 

Downward delegation of decision

Asking the Minister to put in place a broadly consultative process to develop a vision statement for education for the next 50 years.
