November 2004 Probus presentation profiling Don's biography from Miramichi farming family, teacher roles, and founding presidency of Holland College. Traces his educational philosophy development and commitment to vocational education, capturing his journey from youth to professional expertise.

Notes for Probus Profile Presentation

Charlottetown, PEI

Thursday, November 12, 2004


Family

Like all Miramichiers, born young and got an early start

Youngest of nine.

Dad came from down shore near Bathurst

Mother from Miramichi

Most members of both families went to work in USA

Heavy construction – mostly dams in Wisconsin, Tennessee. Alabama, Texas, Connecticut

Langley Field Virginia

Came to Miramichi in 1919 and farmed until his death in 1938


Married 46 years

Carol a US citizen

Millionaire’s family, two granddaughters in university

Wife, children and grand children are all dual citizens


Youth

Mixed farm 

Everything but plow

One room rural school

First rural high school in NB

Electricity arrived in 1948

My peers either worked with their fathers, or went to Moncton and worked with Eaton’s or the CNR

Sold grease, oil, paint etc to neighbours

Introduced the paint roller to my community

Sold cookware and gave cooking demonstrations


Experience

Teacher in one room school

Teacher in high school

Teacher of te4achers

Federal employee working in Ottawa promoting vocational education 

Founding President of Holland College

Consultant in education

Retiree

Variety of roles in local, regional and national organizations

Volunteer










Educational interests and philosophy


Mother saw teaching as a stepping-stone - community

Teachers college for six months

A former minister then a civil servant owed the family some money – I went to his office every two

         weeks to collect

Started taught in Kouchibouguac when still 17 for $110 per month

Govt was encouraging males to go into teaching so I received an extra $200 at the end of

 the year for being male

Got hooked on Manual training, 

My studies thereafter were in technical and vocational education 

Not available in Canadian universities so studied in USA

Taught for nine years before I really began to understand what teaching was all  about

Beaverbrook Scholar quite an eye-opener for a young farm boy


Normal schools/Teachers colleges played an important transitional role for poor people

Community colleges followed

Seems to be a gap now


Philosophy 

A person’s word is his bond

Work for others s you would work for yourself

Most important kind of education is that which helps people improve their job prospects.

I’ve devoted my life to this

Education is vocational, regardless of the subject studied, when one uses it to improve job prospects 

whether it be in elementary, secondary school , college or university.

Unfortunately, that kind of education is not valued.

Our society is the worse for this attitude

Storm days

Emphasis on application not acquisition

Lack of will to implement changes

Takeover of education by the professionals


Holland College experience

Story About Samuel Holland College of Technology is true

Education community did not have high expectations for HC

First year budget was $100,000 and we did not spend all

My last year was $18 million

Pioneered a modular and competency based delivery model that has not been duplicated yet anywhere 

else.

If given the opportunity, would implement the same model but hopefully do even better


Consulting


Variety of contracts

Initially home based but later had office downtown

CIDA-design projects, monitor, evaluate, 

Women training in Pakistan, technical training in Kenya, evaluation of a water technician training

 program in Swaziland, 

Training project in the ASEAN region resulting in organizing 20 short courses for government 

Officials

CRSP – design of a competency based delivery model for secondary schools


Personal Interests


Summer gardener

Amateur genealogist

Reader- historical, biographies and generally stories based on fact

Recreational woodlot owner

Handyman

Writer – education booklets, history of my home church, inventory of neighbours in 1939, co-author

of a number of books including one in Mexico, regular columnist in CVA Journal, occasional letters to the editor, occasional family newsletters on website

Traveller – 40 countries
