November 23, 2015 meeting notes with Hon. Richard Brown (Workforce and Advanced Learning Minister) and Deputy Sharon Cameron. Don, as Education 20/20 President, presented organizational goals: keeping education debate alive, promoting quality education for all Canadians, federally incorporated non-partisan status. Discussion centered on "preparedness gap"—the mismatch between educational credentials and employer-required qualifications. Key points: Canada and US uniquely lack formal qualification authorities; high school diplomas have declining employment value; Education 20/20 proposes establishing separate qualification authority (like apprenticeship model). The meeting demonstrates Don's sophisticated policy engagement, bringing workforce development and credential reform into educational conversation through evidence-based analysis.
