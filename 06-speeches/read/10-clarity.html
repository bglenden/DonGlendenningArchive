A CALL FOR CLARITY


As a frequent participant in conversations about education, I am struck by the absence of definitions and boundaries; communication and understanding may suffer as a result.

The Dictionary of Education (Good) defines definition as “the process of determining the definition or meaning of a word, idea or proposition in general and within a given context; the process of assigning meaning enhances both precision and clarity.”


. Those who teach, or other-wise serve learners, must be very clear in their communication


. Conceptual clarity heightens our understanding of key words and concepts;

Lack of clarity may arise from an idea itself, from the person promoting the idea or from the person receiving the idea; Lack of clarity could also signal conflicting ideas and levels of discomfort or disagreement from within the education family group itself. Conceptual clarify is also a must in planning and assessing performance.

In a report in 2018, the World Bank reminded us that education and going to school are not the same. Education is a service; school is a place. Experiences in school are major contributors to one’s education but it is the experience not the school that counts. It is like the preparation of the food rather than the management of the restaurant.

Conceptual clarity is a must to understand the people we serve, the services we provide, learner needs, product, processes and values especially when the subject carries layers of subjectivity.

We certainly need schools but it is time to focus on the learning experiences rather than the places where they happen.
