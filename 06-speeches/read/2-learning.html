Educational philosophy piece defining learners and the learning process. Explains that all people are learners with different capacities and life roles (individual, student, citizen, family member, producer, consumer). Details that learning involves measurable change in knowledge, beliefs, feelings, and abilities. Presents teaching as arranging experiences for others' learning. Includes comprehensive critique of PEI education system's failure to define terms clearly, conflation of school attendance with education, and recommendations for adopting shared definitions and implementing systemic change to address organizational accountability and program advisory structures.


About Education

Learners and Learning

About 4-16

Learners are people who, for whatever reason, are trying to bring about change in their lives – trying to change an acritude, gain new knowledge, or add skills. But learners are neither empty jars or blank slates; each learner carries three pieces of luggage to every new learning situation:

  • Certain physical, mental nd emotional conditions with which a learner is born

  • Prior learning resulting from home circumstances, formal study, travel, state of health, personal interests, culture in which learner operates and learning style

  • Aspirations and imagined future including where one will live, the work 0ne will do and the lifestyle one will have, nature of the family, quality of life, etc.


We are all learners. Learners have different interests, experiences, capabilities and needs. Learners also fill a variety of life roles Not all earners are students. Learners often need help; helping learners learn is called teaching; teaching is also described as “creating experiences to help others learn.


Education is multifaceted; it can happen any place and any time, when one is alone as well as when people gather together, it may be simple as a bit of information or as complex as understanding the laws of the universe

  • Learning is about change by a person as to what they know feel, believe, value or are able to do.

  • Teaching is about arranging experiences for others that change what they a know, believe, feel and are able to do

  • one’s education is the result of experiences that have had a measurable effect on what

  • One’s level of education is a judgement by a community as to

  • Well-educated usually means that the person can deal satisfactorily with most issues that arise in their everyday lives.

  • A school is a purpose-built facility; running a school is not an educational function
