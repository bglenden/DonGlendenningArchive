Opening remarks for December 4, 2012 panel discussion "Community Engagement & Early Childhood Learning." Don introduces Education 20/20 (renamed from Education First) as an independent national educational organization. Emphasizes engagement from both learner and school perspectives. Introduces Alice Taylor as "experienced teacher, former instructor at Holland College, published author and active innovator" and frames the evening as the organization's first action event.

Panel Discussion

December 4, 2012

Welcome:  My name , welcome, delighted to see so many, 

Background

  • For the past few months, a number of us have been meeting in a variety of combinations
  • We’ve focused on education in the broadest sense
  • Share a similar view of education, nature, importance
  • Compared to other sectors, there are few opportunities for people with an interest in education to come together
  • So we decided to do something about it

Education 20/20

  • Established a new, national, independent educational organization
  • Now incorporated and developing bylaws, website, logo, etc.
  • Current focus is on information, encouragement, engagement, and  recognition
  • The handout provides a bit of information about who we are, what we believe and what we intend to do
  • Education 20/20 is a big tent, we invite you to help erect it and to make use of it
  • Current Directors: Alice Taylor, Eileen Conway-Martin, Don Glendenning

Tonight

  • First action event
  • Education is improved through engagement; 
  • Our focus for tonight is engagement and at the Early childhood level
  • Engagement from the learner’s perspective - engagement with learning materials, other students, teachers, parents, and other community members
  • Engagement from a school perspective including  - the learning model, advisory committees, learning job shadowing, work placement, various forms of communication, etc.
  • Look forward to hearing some views on engagement from front line  practitioners 


  • Call on Alice Taylor, experienced teacher, former instructor at Holland College, President of the Early Childhood Education Association, a published author and an active innovator


Wrap-up: Thank you, special thank you to our speaker and panelists; 

Open to ideas, need volunteers, look forward to keeping in touch
