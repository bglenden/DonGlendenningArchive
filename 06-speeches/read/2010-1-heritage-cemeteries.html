Strategic proposal for a Heritage Cemetery Network (HCN) as a non-profit organization to address cemetery maintenance concerns across PEI. Don documents community concerns about physical maintenance and trustee recruitment, particularly in cemeteries unattached to active churches. This document demonstrates his commitment to preserving heritage infrastructure and addressing rural community needs through innovative organizational solutions.

COMMUNITY FOUNDATION OF PRINCE EDWARD ISLAND

Heritage Cemetery Network 

 Background


On a number of occasions community leaders have expressed concern about the care of cemeteries throughout the Island, especially cemeteries not directly attached to an active church; the most recent being on Monday with heritage activist, Dr. Catherine Hennessey.


The concerns are twofold.  One concern is that of the actual physical care and maintenance – lawn, fences, shrubs, sunken graves, signage, etc.  The other is concern about the ability to recruit future trustees.  Regarding the latter, it was pointed out that many trustees are often carrying on a family tradition, are aging and, with the out migration of people from rural areas, new trustees are becoming difficult to recruit.


Concept  

The concept is of a Heritage Cemetery Network, (HCN) as a not-for-profit organization whose main purpose would be to offer care and maintenance of heritage cemeteries.  Funds could come from many sources and the organization would seek registration as a charitable organization thereby allowing it to receive and receipt donations. 

Individual cemeteries would enter into an agreement whereby, for a fee,  HCN would provide maintenance services.  The costs of maintenance could be met in a variety of ways;

The local community could raise funds annually for the purpose

Families with relatives in the cemetery could pay an annual fee

Interested people and organizations could establish endowments for the purpose

Existing surplus funds and trusts could be used to establish endowments with CFPEI

Proposal 

CFPEI proposes to establish a commission to explore the idea further and to make recommendations. 2010-02-02
