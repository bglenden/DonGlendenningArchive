A keynote address delivered to school principals in Shediac, New Brunswick in May 1970, where Don presents provocative ideas about educational systems and alternatives to traditional schooling. He defends discussing controversial matters, critiques the supervisor role with humor, addresses automation and human capacity, and poses fundamental questions: Why do we need teachers? Why Departments of Education? Why administrators? Why schools? He proposes alternative models ranging from historical apprenticeship through contemporary professional models where teachers work independently, learners choose educators, and payment follows service delivery. Don envisions a future where instructional aids and programmed materials support home-based learning, specialists work in professional clinics, and state responsibility focuses on licensing and ensuring basic literacy. This visionary speech demonstrates Don's radical thinking about institutional change and futures thinking in education.
