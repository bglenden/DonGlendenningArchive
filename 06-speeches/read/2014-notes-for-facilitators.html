Education and Preparedness

Notes for facilitators


Education 20/20 is organizing a series of workshops on Education and preparedness throughout Prince Edward Island. The basis of the workshops is a survey of youth issues undertaken by the Community Foundation of Prince Edward Island; the survey gathered information and sought the views of more than 200 young people between the ages of 15 and 25. The report may be read on the Foundation website, www.cfpei.ca. The report identifies a number of ‘concerns and bright spots”; one of the concerns is that of “preparedness gaps” in regard to postsecondary education and the workplace.


Because we believe that the issues raised in the report are important, Education 20/20 is hosting a series of workshops throughout the Island so that Islanders can learn first-hand of the findings. Each workshop, therefore, will start with a presentation by Steve MacQuaid, Vice President of the Community Foundation of Prince Edward Island, and Katherine MacDonald, Researcher, on survey methodology and findings, a period of 30-45 minutes depending on questions from the group.


The second part of the evening will consist of round-table discussions, the number of discussion groups will depend on the number of participants, but our objective is to have many discussions rather than consolidate around fewer tables. Each table will have a facilitator, someone to keep the discussion moving, to ensure that all have a chance to voice opinions and that the discussion is respectful. To allow the facilitator to focus on the discussion, we suggest a separate recorder to provide a summary of key points at the end of the evening and to pass a set of notes to the chair at the end of the evening. It is usually useful to open round-table discussions by “going round the table” and inviting comment from each person before opening the general discussion.
