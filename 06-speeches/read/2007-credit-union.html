Presentation proposal (March 12, 2007) to Credit Union Central of PEI for "Community Challenge Initiative." Proposes supporting communities in establishing undesignated charitable endowments through matching grants model: CFPEI matches community contributions at rate where $5,000 community fundraising generates $1,000 annual CFPEI grants. Initiative builds $25,000 endowments over five years, enabling perpetual community giving. Demonstrates Don's strategic thinking about leveraging CFPEI resources to catalyze community development and long-term charitable sustainability.

Presentation Notes

2 March 12, 2007


Community Challenge Initiative


Background:


The Community Foundation of Prince Edward Island works with Islanders and Island organizations with an interest, and often a passion, for bettering their community many also have the means to make a difference.


Based on our experience, we find that communities respond to charitable needs on a crisis basis i.e. new hospital equipment, band instruments or a family emergency; communities could benefit, we believe, from taking a broader and longer range view of existing and potential needs in the charitable sector and developing a “rainy day” fund to help meet them.


We believe that there is an opportunity to assist communities to build local funds to help address future needs of local communities, hence the Community Challenge Initiative.


Concept:


We propose an initiative through which communities will be encouraged to establish an undesignated but named “community fund” the income from which may be used for any charitable purpose in the community consistent with Revenue Canada Regulations. The purposes for which grants are made may change from year to year depending on emerging local priorities. How communities organize for the task may vary, however, participation should be broadly representative of the community. Community endowment funds will be held by the Foundation in perpetuity and invested in a manner that ensures both long term growth and the making of grants on a regular basis. While a community is building a fund and as a further incentive to the initiative, the Foundation will make grant or grants to charitable organizations in the community as recommended by the local committee.


Example:


As an example, if a community agrees to establish a fund and add to that fund at the rate of $5,000 each year for five years, the Foundation will make grants totaling $1,000 per year to a charity or charities in the community. At the end of five years, each participating community will have an endowment of $25,000 which is sufficient to continue granting at the same level in perpetuity.


Proposed Guidelines:


The initiative will operate under the proposed guidelines:

  • Open to any Island community

  • Will operate on multiples of five, each $1,000 available for granting will be released only when $5,000 added to endowment.

  • Endowments to be administered by CFPEI

  • Initiative available to a community for five years



Proposal:


The Community Foundation of Prince Edward Island invites the Credit Union Central of Prince Edward Island to partner in this initiative
