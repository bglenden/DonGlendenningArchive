In the interest of clarity, below are some standard definitions. 

  • Learner – a person who, for whatever reason, needs, wants or is trying to bring about change in his/her life – trying to change an acritude, gain new knowledge, or add skills. But learners are neither empty jars nor blank slates; each learner carries three pieces of luggage to every new learning situation (1) certain physical, mental and emotional conditions with which a learner is born, (b) prior learning resulting from home circumstances, formal study, travel, state of health, personal interests, culture in which learner operates and (3) aspirations and imagined future including where one will live, the work one will do and the lifestyle one will have, nature of the family, quality of life, etc. . Learners have different interests, experiences, capabilities and needs. Learners also fill a variety of life roles, that of an individual, family member, student, citizen, consumer and producer
  • Learning- a process of engaging in experiences that have a formative effect on the way we think, feel and act. Learning is multidimensional; it can happen any place and any time, when one is alone as well as when people gather together, it may be satisfied by something as simple as a bit of information or as complex as understanding the laws of the universe. Although only the learner can learn, learning may occur in group setting
  • Education-one’s education is the result of experiences that have had a measurable effect on one’s life – of one’s thoughts, actions and feelings
  • Education System- a set of services put in place by a community to encourage and support the development of its people
  • Teaching - arranging experiences for others resulting in changes to what they know, believe, feel and are able to do.
  • School- a space or   facility designed to spport grops oof learners
  • School system- schools in a district reporting to a single senior executive member
  • Educated means the extent to which person can deal satisfactorily with issues that arise in their everyday lives.
  • Life roles –bases on Oregon Education belief that each person fills six life roles – individual, family member, student, citizen, consumer and producer. Education can support any and all
