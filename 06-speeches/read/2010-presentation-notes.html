Comprehensive presentation notes by Don Glendenning outlining CFPEI's role and importance to PEI communities. Defines community foundations as development organizations and third-sector coordinators focused on improving quality of life. Emphasizes characteristics of continuity, transparency, flexibility, and long-term vision. Highlights CFPEI's 50 funds totaling $1.5 million with annual grants of $50-60,000. Includes PEI examples and community funds, outlines CFPEI activities (endowment building, grant-making, leadership/liaison, office operations), and frames community foundations as problem-solving mechanisms complementing government and town council efforts.

COMMUNITY FOUNDATION OF PRINCE EDWARD ISLAND


Presentation Notes



COMMUNITY FOUNDATIONS ARE


Community development organizations

Coordinators and leaders in the third sector

Committed to improving the quality of life in their communities 

Serve all sectors: health, social development, religion, environment, heritage, education, 



CHARACTERIISTICS


Continuity

Transparency and accountability

Low administration fee

Professional investment management

Maintenance of principal

Adaptive to changing circumstances

Flexibility

Have the long view, build for today and tomorrow



CFPEI

Island wide

Serve the sector through the making of grants

Create value through calling for proposals, long term stability to sector, selecting best candidates, making grants

Long term focus, build endowments,  grants in perpetuity, maintaining the principal, grant only the earnings, grow the endowments at the rate of inflation, 

Trustees worry about changing fiduciary requirements and succession; we avoid those problems

Provide recognition or anonymity 



PEI NUMBERS


50 funds: named and anonymous, designated and undesignated, from endowments and flow-through,

$1.500,000

Public awards event

Annual report

Award $50-60,000 annually, from investments, anonymous donors, US citizens and other flow-through funds, class action funds, plant trees, annual fund raising dinner, YIP

Who: individuals, organizations, memorials, cemetery funds, Masonic funds

Offer receipts acceptable to both Cdn and US govt.

Grantees include: individual scholarships, QEH, PCH, Boys clubs, libraries, Charlottetown, 

Charge 1% for administration
