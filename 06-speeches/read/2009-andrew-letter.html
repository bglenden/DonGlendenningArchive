April 29, 2009 solicitation letter to William E. Andrew (Penn West Energy Trust, Calgary) from Don Glendenning as CFPEI Director. Provides context on Canadian community foundations (150 total) and explains CFPEI's Living Trust. Details the 2007 Wood Islands inaugural planting and subsequent Milton community planting at North Yorke River Road, highlighting its popularity with walkers, bikers, joggers, and snowmobilers. Solicits Andrew's interest in funding additional plantings given his connection to the area and upcoming UPEI convocation.

April 29, 2009


Mr. William E. Andrew

Director and Chief Executive Officer

Penn West Energy Trust, Suite 2200

425 First Street S.W

Calgary, Alberta T2P 3L8


Dear Mr. Andrew:


Community foundations, and there are 150 in Canada today, administer charitable trusts on behalf of individuals and organizations.  The Community Foundation of Prince Edward Island serves PEI and is governed by a group of 12 volunteers drawn from across the Island.  


Among our endowments is the Living Trust initiated by the Keuper Family for the purpose of enhancing public spaces throughout Prince Edward Island.  In 2007, in cooperation with the Government of Prince Edward Island, a cluster of red oaks was planted on each side of the entrance to the Confederation Trail at Wood Islands.  We plan to plant similar clusters at other trail entrances as funds permit so that, when the oak trees mature, trail entrances will be very recognizable by their oak clusters.  In addition to oak clusters, however, there will be both designated and undesignated plantings along the trail.  


While not limiting ourselves to Trail entrances, due to interest and financial support from individuals in the Milton community last year, we planted trees along the Confederation Trail where it intersects the North Yorke River Road at Carleton Station near Route 2.  We are told, and as you may already know, this is a very popular site and is used by many – walkers, bikers, joggers and snowmobilers in winter.  Because of the continued interest by the community in further planting and the fact that the Department of Tourism plans to erect a story board in the area, we will focus on that area again this year.  We are currently awaiting confirmation from the Department of Public works and Transportation that they will shortly clean up and prepare the area at least to the first bridge.


Because of your connection to the area, this letter seeks to explore any interest that you may have in supporting the planting of trees at the North Yorke River Road location.  That location lends itself to a number of possibilities including:


A cluster of three oak trees on each side of the Trail entrance and on both sides of the road, for a total of 12, have been identified for donor named trees.  

Named trees in a number of areas along the trail

General planting of trees in the area

Some benches and a picnic table




Should you have an interest, I would be delighted to discuss the idea further.  At the same time, should you have other ideas as to how the Foundation can serve you, please do not hesitate to let me know.


I am enclosing some information about the Foundation; you may also find our website,  HYPERLINK "http://www.cfpei.ca" www.cfpei.ca, of interest.  


I am not certain how often you return to PEI but expect that you will be here shortly for the UPEI convocation activities.  If so, I would be delighted to meet you and to discuss the idea in detail.  On the other hand, please feel free to phone me at my home, 902-892-5859, should you have any questions.


Thanks in advance for your consideration


Sincerely



Don Glendenning

Director
