Progress notes dated November 26, 2009, documenting Don's conversations with arts organizations about endowment partnerships. Notes College of Piping (Ken Gillis, Chair) is interested but prioritizing capital needs; Carrefour seeking presentation opportunity; PEISO uninterested due to current fundraising focus; Harbourfront (Maurice Gallant) to discuss with chair; Victoria Playhouse to be handled by John Robinson; and Indian River Festival (Rene Hurtubise, Chair) expecting board approval and completing federal application.
Speeches & Presentations


November 26, 2009


Status of Arts Agreements


College of Piping - spoke with Ken Gillis, Chair.  While interested in an endowment, College is in great need of capital and expects  to focus on that in the near future.  Wants to be kept informed and would like to be invited to any meetings

Carrefour - spoke with Michelle Blanchard – She would like us to make a presentation to the board but probably not until December.


PEISO – interested but cannot see their way clear to campaign for funds at present time. 


Harbourfront – spoke with Maurice Gallant; he will discuss with chair and get back to us



Victoria Playhouse – probably let John handle



Indian River –  met with Rene Hurtubise, Chair.  Application being completed and expect their Board approval tonight.  We also need to complete a portion of the agreement.  If application is received by Ottawa, Indian River would qualify for matching grants this year.
