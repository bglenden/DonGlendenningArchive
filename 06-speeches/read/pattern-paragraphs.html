Opening


1A As a follow-up to earlier conversations, I wanted to provide you with some detailed information about the Community Foundation of Prince Edward Island and also let you know of a new initiative to build a charitable legacy over a period of years.  First, however, let me say a few things about community foundations.


1B This letter comes to introduce you to the Community Foundation of Prince Edward Island (CFPEI)


Community Foundations


Community foundations, and there are 170 in Canada today, create and administer permanent endowments for charitable purposes.  The goal is to improve the lives of individuals and their communities.  This is accomplished by making grants for the purposes, and only the purposes, designated by individual donors.   Funds from donors are pooled and invested in perpetuity; earnings are used to make grants.  



The Community Foundation of Prince Edward Island serves the entire Province, is governed by a volunteer board widely representative of Island life, maintains an office in Charlottetown, has assets of well over a $1 million, makes grants annually in excess of $50,000, is being used increasingly by Islanders to host their charitable legacies, is registered with both the Canada Revenue  Agency and the IRS,  is transparent and open while following sound business practices. Investments are professionally managed, books are audited annually and reports are public.  Although one of the newer community foundations in Canada, we are well experienced in the latter approach having created and now administering over 60 endowments for the  benefit of Island organizations and individuals.


Community Foundations, and there are now 160 in Canada, administer endowments on behalf of individuals and organizations.  The donor transfers money to CFPEI, the Foundation issues a tax receipt, invests the monies, and makes grants annually in the Fund’s name and based on the donor’s directions.  The Donor can direct that grants be given as scholarships, or as gifts to charities or causes for which they have a passion, or for causes which the Foundation identifies, or for some combination of all of these.



Endowments


Community foundations are relatively new on the scene and are unique in that they focus on the building and administration of charitable endowments.  Endowments are usually set up by individuals as part of their estate planning, to mark a significant event in their lives or to remember a loved one; We also administer funds for other charitable organizations that want to take advantage of our expertise.  Having been involved in one way or another with the establishment of more than 60 endowments, I feel comfortable in saying that CFPEI is the most experienced organization on the Island when it comes to designing charitable legacies reflecting donor interests and community needs.


Granting

Grant from the income are made in perpetuity in support of the interests of the person(s) setting up the fund.  Among our grantees we include UPEI and Holland College students, hospitals, youth groups, health organizations, the planting of memorial trees, heritage, arts, etc. 


CFPEI Activities 

CFPEI also plays a role as “conveners” meaning that we bring people or organizations together to explore common interests in improving our community.  As an example, a few years ago through our initiative, more that $800,000was provided to the Arts in PEI over a four year period.  A second service is that of flow-through grants to other charities. For example, one US donor, who wishes to remain anonymous, makes a gift to CFPEI along with instructions as to where grants are to be made.

The establishment of a fund with CFPEI is quite simple; it involves a one page agreement setting out the name and purpose of the fund together with the names of the causes or organizations to which grants are to be made.  In the case of your Dad, interests could include his church, Camp Gencheff, the Bangor cemetery, or any number of other causes.  Should your interest be in a capital project, we could be of assistance also.

Services

Our normal services include:

Designing and establishing endowments. Investing and monitoring performance, and auditing and reporting on to both the public and the Canada Revenue Agency

Making grants that reflect a donor’s wishes and create value within the charitable sector; a donor’s wishes could be as simple as naming charities of interest to which grants are to be made

providing information and advice to individuals, organizations and other foundations on existing and potential community needs

accepting donations of any kind including cash, real estate, personal property, stocks, bonds and other financial instruments; this could include acting as Executor to an estate


All of us are faced with decisions on a regular basis as to how much we can give and to what causes; there are many needs and all are worthwhile, so we make our decisions based on our unique set of criteria.  Too often, I feel we leave decisions about a rainy day legacy fund until very late in life.  We at the foundation believe that legacy decisions should be made earlier and built over time 


Decisions about one’s estate are both important and personal; increasingly people tell me that, after family have been taken care of, they want to give back to the community that supported them, not just an annual contribution but in the form of a permanent legacy.  Many are opting to make that provision now rather than after death.

I

We chose the Community Foundation because:

CFPEI is managed by local volunteers who know the needs and can monitor the impact of donations in perpetuity

 CFPEI provides great flexibility in being able to make grants to several different causes as directed by the donor when the Fund is set up

Funds are professionally managed according to a comprehensive Statement of Investment Policies and Guidelines and monitored by a volunteer Investment Committee.

CFPEI has the capacity to provide significant tax savings through the gifting of equities and other gifts with appreciated capital gains.

Administrative costs at community foundations are very reasonable and much lower than those I’ve found elsewhere.


Increasingly, people are using community foundations as a one stop operation to handle their charitable interests.  For example, they set up a fund from which the Foundation can make grants to their church, scholarships and favourite charities; also, donors are leaving some funds undesignated in order to respond to emerging community needs

Administration


If donors are comfortable with their financial advisor and would like them to continue to manage their account, the Foundation can certainly accommodate but it may be beneficial to talk with us before you make that decision.  We would also be happy to brief your lawyer on the nature of community foundations and their work.


The community foundation model for charitable trusts has been so successful that many financial institutions are now adopting it.  The difference being is their main interest in managing funds while community foundations work to ensure that your charitable interests are honoured in perpetuity.


There are financial benefits to making charitable contributions through the transfer of stocks; if this interests you, we would be delighted to discuss this further with you.
