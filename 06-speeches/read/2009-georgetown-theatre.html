COMMUNITY FOUNDATION  OF PRINCE EDWARD ISLAND


Notes for presentation at Georgetown, PEI


Monday, April 21, 6:30 pm


Introduction

Introduce Lou Ann Thomson and Don Macdonald


Thanks for opportunity to present


Aware of revitalization of Georgetown; among other things, I have driven past the Holland College welding facility, eaten at your restaurants, attended events at the Georgetown theatre, visited the shops including xxx craft shop, and enjoyed the beauty of the gardens (acknowledge Bea Mair)

 

We represent the Community Foundation of Prince Edward Island but we come neither carrying money with us nor seeking money from you


Community foundations, and there are 150 in Canada today, have a community focus and are designed to help people do the things for their community – whether that community is Georgetown or PEI.  They are a development tool, a part of the infrastructure.  They help communities do the things tea community wants to do.  They may also be likened to a savings account, set up to meet long term needs and interests.


Let me tell you a bit more about CFPEI


Closing Remarks
