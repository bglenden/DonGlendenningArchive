Handwritten-style notes prepared by Don Glendenning for John Robinson's US Citizens Day remarks. Includes historical context about Islanders migrating to US for work (lumbering in Maine, railway/port jobs in Portland, etc.), describes present-day connections, and outlines CFPEI's mission as volunteer-governed community foundation. Reflects Don's deep understanding of PEI-US historical ties and his role in conceptualizing the Foundation's American engagement strategy.

Possible remarks

US citizens Day

July 4, 2006


Welcome We are delighted to see so many here today


Historic ties A century or more ago, one could catch a train any day of the week from

 Charlottetown heading for Portland and Boston 

Islanders lumbered in Maine and New Hampshire, worked at the railroad

 and marine terminals in Portland

Served as nannies and companions to gentry of Boston

They also worked in Minnesota, Wisconsin, on the west coast and through

 the south

some settle and some returned


And today - I know personally many young Islanders living and working in the USA

Vast quantities of goods flow back and forth across the border daily

And I continue to marvel at our long, undefended border


From a PEI perspective 

-we see and hear you in our grocery stores and theatres

-we see your license plates on our main highways and on the

 country roads

--we know you as friends and neighbours


CFPEI - is a community foundation – you may be familiar with them in your

 community

governed by volunteers

Serving all PEI

Mission – to improve the quality of life of Islanders


Provide a vehicle for people who want to use their talents and their resources for the betterment of the community


We do this through: 

Building and managing endowments

Making grants that reflect donor interests 

Making grants for youth, health, the arts, heritage, scholarships, etc

Playing a convening and leadership role in order to address needs

  as they arise

The things we do also support the things that you find enjoyable


copy of our newsletter on the table by the door

We’d be delighted to tell you more about what we do.


That includes you, hence this reception where we can meet together, we can say thanks for visiting, thanks for your support, thanks for being a good neighbour





Reception You are part of our community

Many of you already participate in the life of the community 

Many provide financial support for our cultural institutions.


Those who do contribute financially should be aware that CFPEI can offer receipts acceptable to the IRS.


So we decided to organize this reception Occasion your National

 holiday

to meet you

to provide an opportunity for you to meet fellow citizens

to thank you for coming

to thank you for being good neighbours

Wrap up Recognize and thank Garth and committee


Thank you for coming

Plan to do this again next year

Comments and suggestions welcome

Enjoy your stay
