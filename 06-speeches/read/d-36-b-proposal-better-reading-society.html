An August 2011 proposal to establish an independent reading improvement association to increase Islander capacity to address reading challenges. Don provides compelling evidence of the reading crisis: 42% of adults lack Level 3 literacy, students underperform nationally, and projections show 30% of Canadian Grade 6 students struggling with literacy. He advocates for reading focus based on its foundational importance, multiple intervention points, and existing community readiness. The proposal includes objectives for raising awareness, organizing public events, supporting advocacy, and raising initial funds. Budget details include part-time promotion staff, incorporation, public information sessions, and media launch, showing Don's understanding of the infrastructure needed for successful public campaigns.

August 4, 2011

Better Reading - Proposal #2-B

Proposal:

  • To increase the future capacity of Islanders to participate in the improvement of reading skills among its people by establishing an association dedicated to the improvement of reading among Islanders.

Background:

  • Education is an important key to future life of both individuals, communities and countries
  • Support for change is widespread among both the public and professionals.  
  • Reports and discussions do not differentiate between education and schooling
  • We have a school system rather than an education system
  • Scan of media in both the USA and Canada supports need for substantial changes to the system
  • Analysis reveals both gaps and weaknesses in content and delivery
  • The system is not capable of making the changes needed
  • A major first step is to develop capability of citizens to engage in informed public debate.
  • The education sector suffers from a lack of non-aligned public bodies with a focus on education

Why Reading:

  • Key skill for any age
  • Often referred to as “The golden skill”
  • Many providers/contributors
  • Reading abilities most raised issue in discussions
  • C current priority in PEI
  • Many parents/grandparents enroll children in private schools or provide tutors
  • Important and useful changes can be made without incurring either great expense of disruption to the system
  • Little coordination among providers
  • support literacy program announced by Atlantic Provinces 
  • Island students perform below the national average on reading tests
  • While literacy numbers are improving, literacy rates are not
  • 42 percent of the adult population does not possess level 3 literacy – the level thought to be needed to take full advantage of opportunities present in the global economy.  
  • IALSS Study “Literacy rate did not change between 1994 and 2003
  • Globe and Mail Sept 15, 2009:children who aren’t reading well by the end of Grade 3 are at risk of dropping out or failing to graduate
  • Globe and Mail Sept 15, 2009: estimates that by the end of Grade 6, at lest 30 percent of Canadian students can’t read or write well enough to succeed in school
  • New York Times, March 4, 2011: “About three-quarters of the 17,500 freshmen at the community colleges this year have needed remedial instruction in reading, writing or math, and nearly a quarter of the freshmen have required such instruction in all three subjects. In the past five years, a subset of students deemed “triple low remedial” — with the most severe deficits in all three subjects — has doubled, to 1,000.”


Objectives:

  • Convene regular meetings of Society
  • Raise public awareness
  • Organize public information and events
  • Support the establishment of an education improvement society
  • Raise initial funds

Budget notes:

  • Part time person with experience in promotion and fund raising
  • Incorporation
  • CRA registration
  • Five public information sessions at $750 each
  • Contract with CFPEI for admin services
  • Media launch
  • Logo/letterhead/website/advertisements
  • Hold regular meetings of the society
