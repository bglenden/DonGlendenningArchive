Road of Life


Learner – every person is a learner on the road of life


At birth, we are launched on the road of life; it is likely to be long, complex and sometimes rocky.


The good news is that there is help available, help may be in a number of forms, but this presentation deals with help in the form of education.


The heart of education is learning; it is a process a learner goes through to grow, to change, to develop, to be different.

To understand education, therefore, we need a clear, well articulated understanding of learning.


This presentation reflects my view of learning and the learning process.


It is not an academic presentation, it is intended for users, learners, parents, politicians, trustees and the public at large - people who are served by education rather than those who provide it.


Learning is a lifelong process that engages us from birth to death. What we’ve learned or not learned, determines to a large degree who we are, and what we are likely to become.


Learning is not limited to what we do at school; much of our learning happens outside school. Some of our most important learning occurs at home, at church, in our communities, through sports, conversations around the kitchen table, from family and friends. In fact, we are educate whether or not we ever go to school, college or university; we are learners wherever we are.










The road of life may be described in many ways; I’ve adopted the Life roles” model developed in 1990 by the State of Oregon it is easy to visualize, simple to understand and easy to explain.


Life roles*

  1. Individual

  2. Learner

  3. Producer

  4. Citizen

  5. Consumer

  6. Family member

*Oregon Educational Act, 1990


From the moment of birth to the moment of death, each of us is engaged in one or more of those roles, and usual several simultaneously. The boundaries are fuzzy, the emphasis changes, success are not easy to define, some roles are crowded, and navigation may not be easy. All of us fill some roles; most of us fill all roles. We do so at different ages and stages and we do so in different emphases and combinations. While we may travel the road with friends, most of the important decisions must be made alone – and we must deal with the consequences.
