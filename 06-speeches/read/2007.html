Comprehensive speaking notes (February 12, 2007) for CFPEI information session articulating foundation mission and operations. Emphasizes CFPEI's 15-year history, volunteer governance structure, and positioning among 130+ Canadian community foundations. Highlights differentiation from traditional charities through donor-directed model, endowment focus, and emphasis on perpetuity. Showcases Don's ability to communicate foundation value proposition to prospective supporters and community leaders.

6 Windup


As you know, community foundations are a new breed of charitable organization in Canada in that they are community based rather than cause based, and focus on carrying out a donor’s wishes.  Increasingly community foundations are becoming the vehicle of choice for people who wish to customize their legacies in the form of endowments.  Reasons why a donor should consider a community foundation are many including:

Continuity

Transparency and accountability

Low administration fee

Professional investment service

Maintenance of principal

Adaptive to changing circumstances

Flexibility
