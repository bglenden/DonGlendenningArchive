Master of ceremonies remarks by Don Glendenning at Living Memorial Trust inaugural event at Wood Islands. Opening welcomes Keuper family (Elinore, Ann from Arizona, John and Anne living locally, grandchildren). Outlines special occasion: launch Living Memorial Trust and inaugural oak tree planting honoring Dr. Charles Keuper. Provides expanded explanation of living memorials concept with Norman Carruthers (CFPEI President) speaking to benefits: environmental enhancement, wildlife shelter, erosion reduction, air quality improvement, visitor comfort. Extensive biography of Dr. Keuper. Acknowledges cooperative agencies: George Coade (Forestry), John Callaghan (Tourism Trails Manager), Leo Gill (Transportation), Mary Elliot (Wood Islands Development Corp). Thanks: Brendon McGinn, Bruce Lewis, Gary Schneider, Keuper family, John. Refreshments at Train Station hosted by Volunteer Social Committee/Plough the Waves Centre.

Living Memorials  


                                                                                                                                     

Living memorials are green spaces set aside in memory of a person, place or

 event;

 the key characteristic is that it is a natural space and plant life continues to grow.   May be as simple as a single tree or bush but may be a garden, a park or a forest.  


are more than just a tribute to loved ones; 

enhance and beautify our environment, 

provide shelter for our wildlife, 

reduce erosion and 

improve the quality of the air we breathe.  

bring comfort, hope and peace to visitors and ensure that generations to come will know the beauty of a green plant.  














Living Memorial Trust


The Keuper family is well known to all of you


Dr. Keuper was born in Schulenburg, Texas, received his higher education at Elmhurst College, Illinois and the University of Texas.  

After a stint as a Medical Officer with the United States Army in Japan, Dr. Keuper opened a practice in Boston and taught for a number of years at Harvard Medical School.  


Married Elinore Willaiamson: Anne, John and Charles


Upon retirement, the Keuper family set up residence in Wood Islands.  

He continued to practice but also enjoy the Island, its people, its arts and of course, its natural beauty.


Dr. Keuper was an avid and tireless worker in the establishment of Trails PEI and a person who savoured outdoor life to the full.  


To honour his memory, continue support for the Island landscape, and to encourage others along the same lines,  the Keuper family provided initial funding to establish the Living Memorial Trust at the Community Foundation of Prince Edward.  Funds may be made for the beautification oa any public space but, in view of Dr. Keuper’s interest, we chose this site for the inaugural planting.


We are here today to launch that Trust and complete the inaugural planting
