ATLANTIC PROVINCES QUALIFICATION AUTHORITY



Background


The acquiring of an education and the certification of qualifications are two separate functions although the two are closely linked and frequently one is thought to be the other.  The fact that the delivery of both functions is, to a large degree, provided or driven by educational institutions often works to the individual’s disadvantage in terms of both education and certification.  While separate certification bodies exist, they appear to place undue emphasis on results of study and too little emphasis on what has been learned outside the formal education system.


Concept


The idea behind this proposal is to establish an independent, private sector driven body with authority to assess and certify a person’s level of performance on a task or set of tasks such as found in an occupation or field.


Characteristics


AQUA will:


Be an independent organization with its own governing structure

Operate on a fee for service basis

Accept clients on direct application or by referral

Issue certificates based on demonstrated performance

On request, provide applicants with information on gaps to be filled to meet certification requirements. 

Make extensive use of advisory/working panels.


Benefits/Advantages


It is anticipated that the availability of AQUA will:


Remove barriers to certification encountered in the current approach

Focus certification more clearly on performance

Encourage more self study

Allow educational providers to focus on the teaching function

Allow a certification body to focus on certification without regard to teaching.

Removes length of learning time as a component of certification



Implementation Steps


Undertake feasibility study

Prepare a business plan

Conduct a pilot of the model
