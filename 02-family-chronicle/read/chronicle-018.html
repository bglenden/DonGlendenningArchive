Early Names


This is a follow-up to Issue No. 17 with additional information about early names of the area was taken from AGeographical Names of New Brunswick, 1875.@ Chatham was once known as Spruce Tree after a large Spruce tree growing on Water Street. The name changed to Chatham about 1800 after William Pitt, the Earl of Chatham, Prime Minister of England.


Although Wells Brook, Weldfield and Redmondville are shown, the origin of the names is not given. McKenzie Settlement was likely named after John and Roderick MacKenzie who were each granted 100 acres there in 1871 and 1873. (Could this have been the Roderick MacKenzie who married my Great Grandfather=s sister, Annabelle MacDonald? John K. MacKenzie gas granted lot #163 (100 acres) in 1907. Would this have been Aunt Tine= second husband? In 1909 Mary Anderson was granted 300 acres of land at the Little Branch end of the McKenzie Road


Point aux Carr was once known as Cape Beduin; It is also known as Pointe au Quart. Robert Logie came from Scotland around 1790 and the Black Brook Post Office was renamed Loggieville when Andrew Logie became Postmaster in 1895.


Land at the Meadows


My mother always referred to the place where she was born as The Meadows; the Post Office, however, would have been Black River. The map on the reverse side shows the location of the Watling property and some the properties immediately surrounding it. Starting at Route 11 on the south side of the river and moving west we find:


26 Terrence Cook 150 Ac. 1833-07-24
