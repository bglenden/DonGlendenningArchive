80th anniversary Celebrated


Cutting the cake, from the left, Rev. Krunys, Minister, Mrs. Edith (Cameron) Bremner a member of the choir in 1930, Kenneth Glendenning, Clerk of Session and Don Glendenning who was baptized the day the church as officially opened..


On July 18, 2010, St. Paul’s Presbyterian Church, Black River Bridge celebrated its 80th anniversary. Special speaker was Rev. Blaikie, son of a former minister.


The Rev. Dr. James MacGregor, who had come to Pictou, Nova Scotia from Scotland, first ministered to the Presbyterians in Black River in 1797. The first Presbyterian Church was built in Bay du Vin; in 1836, St. Stephen’s Presbyterian Church was opened in Black River, In 1906 a new sanctuary was constructed which, after 1925, became St. Stephen’s United Church. The current Presbyterian Church, St. Paul’s, was officially opened on July 18, 1930 on land donated by John Gibson.
