Fifth Family Chronicle (October 31, 1998) analyzes Don's mother's autograph album, dating entries from 1910-1915. First entry: mother Jane Watling Glendenning, Portland Maine, December 1, 1910. Earliest entry by Don's father E. A. Glendenning from Wausau, Wisconsin, September 18, 1910 (before their marriage). Describes their wedding in New Brunswick October 18, 1910, followed by December 1910 Christmas entries in Bonny Eagle, Maine, and February 11, 1911 in Parksville, Tennessee (dam construction). Documents entries from family and friends from various locations including Tennessee, Nova Scotia, and Maine. Notes interesting entries by Uncle Guy Glendenning (1915, between enlistment dates) and mentions mysterious references to Pine Point and other southern locations. Shows Don's mother maintained a rich social circle during early married years.

The Family Chronicle

No.5           1998 10 31


Recently, I reviewed Mum=s Autograph Album. Many of the pages are loose and some may be missing, however, here are some observations based on the  entries we have:

The entry on the first page reads as follows:


Dear Friends:

My album=s open.  Come and see.

What! Won=t you waste a line on me?

Write but a line, a work or two

That memory may revert to you.

J. Watling Glendenning

Portland, Maine

December 1st, 1910

Bonny eagle, Maine


I am puzzled to find this entry later in the album:


I have looked these pages ore and ore

And seen what others wrote before

And in looking in one lovely spot

I see in scribble forget me not.

E. A. Glendenning

Wasau, Wisc.

Sept 18th/10


This is the earliest dated entry in the album, and was dated before Mum and Dad were married.  My guess is that Dad gave Mum the album when he returned to Portland from Wasau, Wisconsin.


Later in the album, I find this entry:


Around went the album

and hither it came

for me to write in So here is my name.

E.A. Glendenning



Portland, Me.

Nov 6th/10


This would have been shortly after they returned from their wedding in New Brunswick.  There are no other entries on that date.


There were a number of entries on December 28, 1910.  They may have had a few friends in during the holidays especially because they were leaving shortly for work in Tennessee.  The entries on this date are by Amy B. Usher, Alta M. Usher, LaMont C. Usher all of Bonny Eagle, Maine.  In a book where Mum recorded  the purchase of wood, the name@Preston Usher@ appears but he was not at the party or, if he was, he did not sign the autograph album.


There is an entry on that date also by Margaret Glendenning, Dad=s sister, who gave her address as Falmouth Foreside, Maine.  At the bottom of the page she wrote ASo. Portland High School@ Aunt Margaret would have been 17 years old at the time.


I believe that Falmouth Foreside is where Aunt Venetia Doring lived.  It is a bit north of Portland near Freeport (now famous for its outlet stores).  Bonny Eagle, on the other hand, was on the south west side of Portland and a few miles outside the city near, I believe, Sebago Lake where Uncle Frank had his cottage property.


Could Aunt Margaret have gone to Bonny Eagle to Spend a few days with Dad and Mum over the Christmas holiday?



On January 2, 1911, Gladyce Pearle Beane of Readfield, Maine signed the album; then there is an entry, undated, by Willis Sawer of West Buxton.  On January 31, Leola S. Blake, Buxton Ctr.  signed the album.


It looks as thought the Album was then packed for the journey to Parksville, Tennessee where Dad worked on the first of three dams on the Ocoee River.  Mum noted in another book that she @Commenced housekeeping, Parksville, Tennessee, Feb. 11th, 1911".


On April 27, 1911 , there is an entry by Mrs Stanley Franke and Miss Mae Franke.  They give their addresses as Pleasant River, Queens Co. Nova Scotia.  


There are a number of entries from Parksville, Tennessee dated October 13, 1911.  One is by Florence Davis.  The second entry is by AJDW@.  I wonder if it is a Watling but cannot think of who it would be.  Also on that date we find:


Parksville, Tenn

Oct 13/11

Dear sister Janie:

Be thine: the true flower of a blameless life.

Yours lovingly

Janet

Little Branch North=d Co

N.B., Canada


We know that Aunt Janet went to Parksville when Grace was born, September 23, 1911.


Then there is an undated entry from Goforth Creek, Camp #2 from Goldie Ratcliffe (later Goldie Kennedy), a life-long friend.


Two entries appear on November 13, 1912


Goforth Creek,

Parksville, Tenn

Dear Sister

May your life be like the snowflake which leaves a mark but not a stain.

D. Ernest Watling

Little Branch, N.B.


And


Away back here and out of sight

I=ll write my name down

Just for spite.

Hughy

Kenny=s Hill 


Hugh MacDonald , Known as AMac@ was killed while working on the Newcastle (Morrissy) Bridge.


On June 27/13, M.J. Miller of River Louison, Rest. Co. N.B. entered a verse.  The last entry follows:


Little Branch

July 9, 1915

This book went round and here it came.

In it for me to write my name.

I would write better if I could

But nature said I never should.

Guy W. Glendenning

Canobie, N.B.

Portland, Maine


This is interesting because of the three addresses and because it occurred between the date Uncle Guy enlisted (Bathurst, July 2, 1913) and the date he was inducted at Sussex, July 14, 1915.  Mum would have been home when Venetia was born, May 14, 1915.  Perhaps Dad was in N.B. at that time also.
