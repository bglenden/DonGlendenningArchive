Significant Dates in the Life of Black River


This is a first attempt to develop a list of significant dates for Black River; dates are based on best available information. Additions, comments and corrections welcomed.  


Year Event


1785 Northumberland County established

? Land grant awarded to Desaulnier and Marsten near mouth of Black River

1812 Granting of initial lots on lower Black River and the Little Branch

1814 Glenelg Parish established (included Nelson, Hardwicke, and part of Rogersville)

1823 Presbyterian Church built in Bay du Vin

1817 First school in Black River —in home of Hugh MacDougall

1823 Robert MacBeath, first person buried at Church Point, now Black River

Community Cemetery

1820’s     First grants, MacNaughtons, in Upper Black River

1825 First school building erected near original St. Stephen’s manse

   “ Great Miramichi fire


1833 Saw and grist mill established on the Little Branch by George Fowlie

1836 St. Stephens Presbyterian Church constructed in Black River

1840 First informal census of Glenelg Parish

Abt 1845 Schools erected in Middle Black River and Bay Side

Abt 1850 School erected at Little Branch

? John and Roderick MacKenzie granted lots in McKenzie settlement

1851 First formal Northumberland County census

1871 First Postmaster, Alexander Cameron, appointed at Little Branch


1880 St. Stephen’s Manse constructed

1890 First Harvest Home held at St. Stephen’s Church

1900 Glenelg took its final and present form in 1900

   ? Last settler left Mackenzie Settlement   

1904 Tornado destroyed school and barn in Upper Black River


1906 Current St Stephen’s Church sanctuary opened

1911 Watling Post Office opened 

1925 Earthquake felt in Black River

1925 St. Stephen’s congregation voted to become a United Church

1928 Watling Post Office closed

1928 Little Branch Post office closed

1930 St Paul’s Presbyterian Church dedicated 

? Telephone service came to Black River


1932, Tornado destroyed Godfrey and MacDonald barns 

1932 Black River Community Hall opened

1933 Black River Hall burned

1937 High school classes commenced it St. John’s Anglican Church, Bay du Vin

1938 Larry Brideau and John Paul drown at Victoria Bridge


1939 School transportation provided to MRHS students by Goymer Williston

1940 (Jan) Classes transferred from Bay du Vin to Miramichi Rural High School 

1940 (June) First Miramichi Rural High School graduation

1941 Bus service to Chatham offered by George and MacK Godfrey

1942 Plane crashed along Herb MacDonald’s lane, Hexham


1943 Plane crashed at Alf MacLean’s shore- pilot killed

1948 Electrification of Black River

1967 Black River Centennial Hall renovated and re-opened

1973  Black River Centennial Hall burned


Other


Grist mill erected at Upper Black River 

Opening or original Cameron school

Opening or original Victoria School

Opening of original Upper Black River School

Establishment of sawmill by John W. MacNaughton

Establishment of blacksmith shop by Sinclair Williston

Opening of grocery store by George Adams

Victoria Bridge badly damaged in storm

Traveller’s Inn opened at the Forks, Little Branch

Agricultural Society #103 formed

Sawmill built by Brockway on Sturgeon Brook

Founding of Dorcas Club

Founding of Orange Lodge, #49 L.O.A
