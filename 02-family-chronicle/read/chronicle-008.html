Issue 8 · May 30, 1999
Eighth Family Chronicle (May 30, 1999) documents Little Branch Post Office history through archival records showing postmasters from 1871-1928, confirming Don's parents Elmer and Jane Glendenning served November 1, 1914 to August 1, 1916. Notes that Aunt Tine MacKenzie likely ran the post office in the Jessie MacDonald house 1902-1907 (between her husbands Tom Adams and John MacKenzie). Information about a separate Watling Post Office (1911-1928). Clarifies the Daly/Daley name change (Carson Daley changed spelling after WWII). Provides detailed genealogy of Aunt Tine's families: children from Tom Adams marriage (James, Jubal Willard, Gertrude) and from John MacKenzie marriage (Norman, Jean, Edith). Corrects earlier error: Alex MacDonald's first wife was Ethel MacTavish (not Catherine McGraw), second wife Beatrice Shuttleworth. Discusses cemetery records and the Alexander MacDonald property division.

The Family Chronicle

No.8           1999 05 30


Little Branch Post Office


The Archives has a database, searchable through the Internet, on post offices and postmasters. In checking the Little Branch Post Office, I found the following:

Postmaster Dates Cause of vacancy

Alexander Cameron 1871-11-01   to 1876-03-30 Resignation

John MacNaughton 1876-08-01 to 1883-08-20 Resignation, closed

Alexander Cameron 1884-11-01  to 1887-03-22 Resignation

           (Re-opened)

George Fowlie 1887-09-01 to 1899-10-02 Death

Mrs. Marjorie Fowlie 1900-02-01 to 1903-09 Change in site

Jubal Watling 1903-12-01 to 1910-12-02 Resignation

A.E. MacDonald 1911-03-01  to 1914-08-10 Resignation

Elmer Glendenning 1914-11-01  to 1916-08-01 Resignation

Donald MacNaughton 1916-09-01 to 1928-10-31 Closed


Although Jubal Watling was the Postmaster, I believe that Aunt Tine MacKenzie ran the Post Office in the Jessie MacDonald house. (Where Norman Glendenning now lives).  This would be, as I understand it, between 1902 when her first husband, Tom Adams, died and 1907 when she married John MacKenzie 


I always thought that Dad and Mum ran the Post Office but didn=t have any proof.  The above information confirms my understanding.  The dates are interesting because we know that Mum and Dad were in Tennessee prior to this (1911 to 1912 or more likely until 1913) The next dates we have are New Haven, Conn. in 1917 where Lillian was born on January 8.  It looks as though they may have returned to Little Branch between jobs.  



Watling Post Office


There was Post Office named Watling which opened in 1911 and closed in 1928.  The Postmasters were Donald Watling, A. H. McKay and A. D. Watling


Daley/Daley


Thanks to Carson Daley, for the following information:

the original name was Daly but he (Carson) changed his name to Daley after he left the service at the end of WW2  This explains why Herbert spells his name, Daly and Carson spells his name, Daley







Grandfather Glendenning=s sister, Margaret, married Richard Daly not Irvin Daly as I earlier thought.


Richard Daly married Elizabeth Dempsey and they had eight children. His wife died and Margaret Glendenning moved in to look after the children.  She was 20 years younger than Richard.  She and Richard also had eight children.  After Richard died, Margaret married Arthur Jagoe and I believe is buried with him in the Stonehaven Cemetery.  

One of Richard and Margaret=s sons, Irvin married Katie Branch and they had three children, Annie, Leslie and Austen.  Leslie married Cora Knowles; their children are Herbert, Carson and Earl

John MacDonald and Elspeth Jardine


I find it strange that we have no birth or death dates for our Great grandparents, Mr. and Mrs. John MacDonald.  If anyone has dates or other information, please let me know.


Cemetery Information


Increasingly, names on tombstones in cemeteries are being copied and distributed, usually for a fee.  Recently, I purchased a copy of cemetery listings for all Protestant cemeteries in the Bathurst area.  A similar listing is available for Roman Catholic cemeteries.  I also have a listing of all names in the Black River Cemetery.  Lists can usually be found through the local historical or genealogy group.


Move to Little Branch


Based on the above Post Office information, Jubal and Grace Watling moved from Upper Black River to Little Branch on or before December 1, 1903
