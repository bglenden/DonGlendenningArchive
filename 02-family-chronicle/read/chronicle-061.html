NEIGHBOURS AROUND THE BRANCH


This is an attempt to recall our neighbours around the Little Branch as best I can remember, in 1939.  I am grateful for assistance provided by Norm and Ken, and George Watling, in remembering who lived where.


Readers are invited to provide corrected  and additional information.  


Elmer & Jane (Watling) Glendenning


Starting at the Little Branch School you came first to the Glendenning home.  My father died in 1938 and Mother continued to live at home.   


Grace and Venetia have married and moved away from home.  Walter is soon to be married and Elinor, Ken and Norm attend high school classes in the Bay du Vin church and I attend the Little Branch School.   


Jim & Kate (Mills) Watling


Uncle Jim and Aunt Kate Watling live directly across the road.  Marjorie is soon to marry and move to Loggieville.  Win is about to join the RCAF.   Roy attends the Miramichi Rural, George attends the Little Branch School and Neil hasn’t started school yet.   


Ian & Bertha (MacDonald) MacNaughton


Ian’s mother, Mrs. Donald (MacMurray) MacNaughton, lives with them; Murray and Vance attend the Little Branch School.  Ian’s sister, Kathryn, is a teacher  and Betty is about to join the WACs.


Alf & Elizabeth (Cleveland) MacLean


The MacLean home is in a long lane.  The first Mrs MacLean, Emma MacKay, died and left four children.  Allen has a physical disability and is not able to work; Guy is about to join the army; Harry and Mabel attend the Little Branch School.


Arch Cameron


Arch and his mother live on the left hand side of the road, almost to the corner in a small house.  Occasionally, one would see Arch but I have never seen his mother. 


No one lives up the MacKenzie Road so we turn left at the corner.    


John & Rebecca (Godfrey) Fowlie


They live in an older house on the right.  They have five children  Jim, Jessie, Dot, Elizabeth and Dougald.  John’s father, George, also lives with them.


Sam & Mae (Gillis) Fowlie


This is a newer full two-storey house on the right overlooking the dam.  They have four children, Connie and Ruby attend the Branch School; Neill and Lynn are not yet of school age.  


Add & Jessie (MacLean) Fowlie


Add and Jessie live across the road in a relatively new one and one half story house; they have no children.


At the bottom of the hill is a small bridge which crosses the Little Black River (The Branch River); a dam provides a reservoir of water to run a saw mill.  The old gristmill, now empty, is getting dilapidated.  The original sawmill was built by George Fowlie about 1833; the current sawmill is run by his descendants.  


  We cross the bridge and, On the crest of the hill we see five Fowlie houses belonging to Herb, George, Eldon, Minnie and Fred, Sr.    

Herb Fowlie


Herb was married to Bertie MacKnight; he operates the sawmill. One of his sons, Edgar married Iona Cameron lives with him.  


George & Kate (Sweezey) Fowlie


George lives next to his father, Herb.  They have three children.  Marjorie and Bertha attend the Miramichi Rural High and Byron attends the Little Branch School.


Eldon & Minerva (   ) Fowlie


Eldon Fowlie married to Minerva xxx.  They have two girls, Shirley and Doris.


After we turn the corner and reach the flat ground we find a house on each side of the road.  


Fred & Bessie (Watling) Fowlie


On the right in an older home live Fred and Bessie (Watling) Fowlie.  They have no children. 


Minnie (Williston) Fowlie


Minnie (Williston) Fowlie is a widow and has two children, Patty and Theodore.  Her husband, Freddie, was a son of Mr. F. A. Fowlie and his first wife, Minnie S. Robertson.  Patty is married and lives in Newcastle; Theodore lives at home and farms.  


Now we drive along the north side of the river towards the mouth.  All of the houses are on the river side of the road because, when they were built, the river was the main means of transportation.


Jonathon Adams


The empty house on the left belonged to Jonathon Adams; his widow, Emeline (Emmie) keeps house for Jim Finno who lives near the Little Branch School.



George & Minnie (LaBelle) Adams


The family consists of Gordon, Walter, Eunice, Curtis, Jesse Burns died 1934), Rena, Lawrence, Annie, 


Gordon & Jessie (MacLeod) Adams ???


Simon & Evelyn (   ) MacDonald


They have no children; Simon is going blind.


Gordon & Peggy (Murray) MacDonald


Gordon Macdonald lives on the back side of the road.  


George & Ada (Taylor) MacDonald


Bobby, a son of George and his first wife, Marguerite Bell, attends the Branch School. 


Wallace & ?  (Dwyer)  MacDonald


They have three boys and three girls, Tom, George, Jack, Margaret (Moggie), Mary and Marion.


Hugh Cameron 


Hugh Cameron, husband of Charlotte Mills, is a widower and lives alone.  Daughter, Marion, married Frank MacLean and son, Albert married Merle Mick and lives in the USA,


Jim and Janie Cameron


The Camerons live on the right at the corner as we join the main road.  Dorothy has died,   Rita, Allen, Elspeth, Dugal, John, Kenneth, Isabel, and Edith had moved away.  Wilna and Blanche were still at home with Blanche attending the Miramichi Rural High School. .
