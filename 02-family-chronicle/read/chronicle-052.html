I have not been reporting family events although I thought this time I would make an exception.  A couple of first cousins are celebrating 90th birthdays, however, so I’ve decided to wish them a Happy Birthday.  (I am taking these notes from memory so will apologize in advance for any errors.)  If there are other cousins who have reached this milestone, please let me know.


My oldest living first cousin, I think, is Guy Edwin Glendenning who celebrated his 90th birthday on April 15.  According to my records, he is the oldest male Glendenning that ever lived.  Guy grew up in Canobie and is the son of George Raymond Glendenning and Hilda Ellis.  He is one of thirteen children.  Although his father worked for a while in Portland, Maine, I believe all the children were born in Canobie where he farmed.  Guy married Verna Daley and they had six children.


Guy was one of the first settlers in Springfield, a new settlement back of Canobie.  Guy worked at the paper mill in Bathurst.  In retirement, he had a maple sugar operation, now operated by the next generation, works around his farm and still attends lodge.  He enjoys his new truck.  Recently my brother, Ken, visited Guy and Verna and Guy took him on his ATV to visit the maple syrup operation.  Guy still visits the Napan Agricultural Fair and drops in at Norman and Kenneth’s for a brief visit.  The following is the lates information on Guy’s family.  Please let me know of changes or corrections.

George Elbridge Watling will celebrate a 90th birthday on November 24.  Elbridge, as he was known by our family, was born in Augusta, Georgia on November 24, 1913 just a month after my brother, Walter.  His father, George Jubal Watling worked in the south including, I believe on Ocoee #1 which was the first of three dams on the Ocoee River.  My father, Uncle Ern Watling, Mac MacDonald and maybe others from Black River or the Miramichi worked there also.  Elbridge’s mother was Emma Ronan whose family came from Redbank, New Brunswick.  Elbridge’s Dad did construction work and moved from place to place (as did my Dad).  


Elbridge served in the US navy during WW2 and settled in Newport N.H.  I believe that he also worked for a while in Boston.  He married Laila Anderson and they had two children, Christine and Sarah.  Laila died in 1988 and Elbridge married Helen Hockman.  Please send changes or corrections.
