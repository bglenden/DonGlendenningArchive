Happy New Year


I=ve been doing some work on Carol=s side of the family so will share a few notes.


Ancestral Names


Direct ancestral names on Carol=s side include Chesney, McChesney, Scott Merritt, Murphy, Frank, Vogt, Walder, Unrah,  


William McChesney, Carol=s GGGG Grandfather, was born in Scotland and came to Maryland about 1740.  His son, Benjamin, seems to have used the name Chesney and not McChesney.  Later Chesneys lived in Virginia before moving to Illinois between 1831 when he was born and 1856 when he married Huldah Desiree Merritt.


Theodore Kaifer, Carol G Grandfather, was born in Germany in 1825 and moved to Illinois before 1855 when his daughter, Mary Anita, was born.


J.J. Frank, another GGG Grandfather was born in Rinich, Bavaria on April 24, 1734.  His son, John Jacob and Grandson, Henry, were both born in Bavaria;  the Grandson came to Illinois between 1834 when he was born and 1863 when he was married.  


Lincoln Connection


Family lore says that Carol=s Grandmother, Flora Elizabeth Murphy, was a cousin of Abraham Lincoln.





American Revolution


Carol=s GGG Grandfather, Benjamin Chesney  enlisted as a soldier in the ARevolutionary War@ (against Britain) in May, 1775 and served for 6 years.  He was in a number of battles in Maryland including Brandywine, Stony Point, and  Guildford  Courthouse.  (There are other names of skirmishes but I cannot decipher them.)  He was wounded in the leg.


In 1818 he petitioned for, and was granted, a pension of eight dollars a month.  In his petition, he states that he did not receive discharge papers because his officer did not have money to buy paper and that he was disabled and could not make a living. 


Successful Farmers


Early settlers in the US settled along the 

East coast. In the 1800's, there was a great migration of Americans and immigrants to the west including Illinois and later all the way to the Pacific shore.  The Chesneys, Kaifers and Franks were part of that movement.  Land was fertile and cheap ($1.00 per acre).  They seemed to have prospered - at least the Kaifers and Chesneys.  I know little about the other ancestors.  In later years they planted mostly corn and soybeans.


The Kaifers soon gathered large tracts of farmland; Theodore Kaifer, the original immigrant, gave each of his children, and there were six, 80 acres of land when they came of age and the land appears to have stayed in the family for the next 100 years.


Carol=s Grandfather, William Chesney, was engaged in farming but also bought cattle , brought them to Illinois, fattened and sold them.  Carol=s father also farmed.


Carol=s parents


Carol=s father, Ernest Merritt Chesney, was born in 1903 and was the youngest of six children.  He graduated from Princeton  High School in 1924; was a good student and a member of the debating team.


Carol=s mother, Lola Irene Kaifer, was born in 1903 and was the younger of two children.  She graduated from Henry High School in 1924 and went to Northwestern University on a scholarship.  She was a good student, an accomplished pianist, a champion tennis player and knew several languages.


Ernest and Lola were married in 1927 and moved, I think, her father=s farm and later to his father=s farm.  Marjorie was born in 1930 and Carol in 1936.  Carol=s mother died a week after Carol was born.  Her father had a housekeeper for a couple of years then Marjorie went to live with her Aunt Electa Hunt in Milwaukee and Carol went to live with her aunt, Esther Jardine in Peoria.  Ernest died in 1943.


Grammie Jardine


Esther (Chesney) Jardine was born on May 9, 1895 in Tiskilwa, Illinois.  Her father was one of the first, if not the first, in the community to own an automobile.  Grammie Jardine, as a young girl of 16, would drive him around as he bought and sold cattle.


Edmund and Esther (Chesney) Jardine moved to Peoria in 1933 where Edmund  managed a local grocery chain.  Later he worked for


 Caterpillar Tractor Co.  Grammie worked at Block and Kuhl and Carsons in the ladies= wear department after Ed died, first on a full-time basis and later part time.  She was active in the Eastern Star, a literary club, a member of Westminster Presbyterian Church and played the piano. 


She continued to live alone and drove her own car until she was 90 years of age; then she fell and broke a hip and soon moved to the Lutheran Home in Peoria.  After her husband died, her brother in law, Bill Jardine, moved into basement apartment so that she always had company in the house.  She died on December 1, 1992 at age 971/2.


Longevity


Longevity runs in Carol=s family; some of the longest-living:

On the Frank side, ages were: Aunt Kitty - 104; Aunt Freda - 94; Uncle William - 102; Uncle George - 87; Uncle Jacob - 100; one other Uncle - 100.

On the Kaifer side, Great Grandfather  - 82; Grandfather - 84; Uncle Ivan - 90.

On the Chesney side, GGG Grandfather - 80; G Grandfather - 82; Grandfather - 91.
