Detailed genealogical chart tracing Jubal Watling (married Susan Leach, Nov 1814 in Metfield, Suffolk, England) through multiple generations with children's names and dates. Notes contradictions between two possible Jubals (one from Suffolk, one from Norfolk/PEI) and explores whether they might be the same person. Includes 1841 PEI census data. Also documents Bill Glendinning of Moncton (descended from Dumfriesshire settlers in Galloway, NB) and the 25-year Glendenning family reunion tradition at Ken and Verne's cottage in Point Aux Carr (last Saturday in July, averaging 40-50 attendees). Published August 19, 2003.

The Family Chronicle

No.48             August 19, 2003

                                                                                                                                 ________________Descendants of Jubal Watling


1.  JUBAL1 WATLING1.  He married SUSAN LEACH1 November 10, 1814 in Metfield, Suffolk County, England.  


Notes for JUBAL WATLING:

Family lore says he came to Black River from either Cape Breton or Prince Edward Island.

Children of JUBAL WATLING and SUSAN LEACH are:

i. GEORGE2 WATLING, d. USA (?).


Notes for GEORGE WATLING:

Not shown on original records; believed to have stayed in Prince Edward Island, moved to USA, married and had 2 daughters


ii. MARY ANN WATLING1, b. 1816; m. WILLIAM MAXWELL1.


Notes for WILLIAM MAXWELL:

No children


iii. EMMA WATLING1, b. 1819, Suffolk, England; d. March 28, 1896; m. WILLIAM EDDY1; b. April 20, 1814, County Cork, Ireland; d. May 25, 1893, Bathurst, NB.


Notes for EMMA WATLING:

Or is it Emily? No children; adopted a boy; lived in Bathurst, NB.


Notes for WILLIAM EDDY:

The Nipisiquit library carries the history of the Eedy family.


iv. WILLIAM WATLING1, b. Abt. 1822; m. MARY MACNAUGHTON1, July 27, 1847; b. Abt. 1822.


Notes for WILLIAM WATLING:

Census of 1851 shows a "step-brother" (presumably a step-son) named Henry aged 14, living in the household.


v. JUBAL WATLING1, b. May 6, 1823, Syleham, Suffolk, England1; d. 1900, Black River, NB1; m. CHRISTINA MACNAUGHTON1, Abt. 1845; b. 18231; d. 19011.


Notes for JUBAL WATLING:

Census of 1851 shows Jubal as age 28; Note in Kenneth Watling's database says "Migrated to USA   Marriage date found in Anne Dunne’s database.

On April 9, 1851, Jubal Watling petitioned for 50 acres of land south of William MacBeath's Meadow lot and adjacent to a lot owned by John Edge.   




New Watling Information


Janet Watling shows Jubal and Susan with two additional children – John and Susannah.  Any further information? Dates?


Prince Edward Island Archives show a Jubal Watling living in Lot No. 52 at the time of the 1841 census.  Lot 52 is at the Eastern end of PEI near but not including Montague and Cardigan.


According to the 1841 census, Jubal Watling was between 45 and 60 years of age, head of the family, his trade or occupation was that of a mason, he had 100 acres of land but one cannot tell if he rented or owned the land.  The household consisted of Jubal as head, one female between the ages of 45 and 60 presumably his wife, and 4 males under the age of 16.  Four members of the household were born in England and two were natives of the Island.


Other information in the PEI archives shows Jubal Watling married Louisa on 14 July 1831 and that they came from Norfolk County, England.


An examination of the two sets of data shows that:


One Jubal came from Norfolk and the other came from Suffolk.

The PEI Jubal has a different spouse; Susan Leach may have died and Jubal remarried.

The age brackets fit Jubal and Susan.

While my notes show only Jubal and Emily actually born in England, it is likely that the older children would have been born there also.   

Why would the girls not have been enumerated?


Bill Glendinning


Loisanne (Glendenning) Gregan often talks with Bill Glendinning of Moncton whose ancestors come from Dumfries shire in Scotland.  I’ve since been in touch with him and look forward to meeting him.  


His father was Kenneth Glendinning, Grandfather was William, Great Grandfather was Walter and Great Great Grandfather was John.  Bill’s ancestors settled in Galloway, NB and are buried in the Galloway cemetery; his Grandfather moved to Charlo/New Mills, NB and his father moved to Moncton where Bill now lives.  I’ve compared names he provided with information I have about my family and, although we share many common family names, have not found any direct relationship.


Reunion


For 25 years, the Glendennings have gathered at Ken and Verne Glendenning’s cottage in Point Aux Carr for a potluck family reunion on the last Saturday in July.  The weather cooperated and most of the mosquitoes stayed away.  Over the years we averaged 40 to 50 people; this year, however, there were about 30.  Those traveling the longest distance included Vernon and Donna (Russell) Goodfellow from China, Anne Goodfellow from Australia and Doreen (Glendenning) Nicol from Ottawa.  Thanks to Ken and Verne for hosting this annual event.
