Black River Community Hall 

On October 20, 1930, the first loads of gravel and sand were taken to the site of the first Black River Community Hall; on May 25, 1931 the first dance was held.  (Chronicles 96, 97 and 103) The Chatham Gazette of Friday, August 26, 1932 reported that the hall had been completely destroyed by fire.   Reconstruction commenced on April 1, 1933 and continued as funds became available.  Again, 1n 1973, the hall burned to the ground and was again rebuilt.  My father, Elmer Glendenning, was the Secretary Treasurer.


I found this among my papers; it is neither signed nor dated.  It may have been an early draft or a duplicate.  Readers may find it of interest.



BE IT RESOLVED that the following shall constitute the bye-laws and constitution of the Black River Community Hall Northumberland County New Brunswick


The hall shall be known as the Black River Community Hall, the said community to include the entire settlement of both sides of Black River in the County of Northumberland and, from Hexham corner to the upper district, also, both sides of Little Branch.

The hall shall be held in trust for the people of the community by a Board of Trustees to comprise twelve members, ten members shall be elected at the annual public meeting of the community and the other two shall be elected from among the members of the Womens’ Institute.

The Trustees shall, following their election in each year, elected from among their number a Chairman, Secretary and Treasurer and five members of the Board shall constitute a Quorum for the transaction of business it being the true intent and meaning of these bye-laws that the said Board of Trustees shall be elected annually, the said election takes place on the third week of November or such other date as shall be determined by said board of Trustees. 

The Chairman shall preside over the meetings of the Board of Trustees and shall vote only in case of a tie.

The Secretary shall keep a proper record of all meetings held by the Board of Trustees, in a minute book,  and shall conduct any correspondence that may be necessary and essential in connection with the conduct of the said community hall.

The Treasurer shall take  care of all money received in rent for the Hall or from any other source, shall be required to keep a proper account of all monies received and expended and shall pay all bills in connection with  the conduct of the Hall subject to the approval of the Trustees.  Shall deposit all sums exceeding Fifty Dollars ($50.00) in a chartered bank and the money thus deposited shall not be withdrawn except by authority of the Trustees, the order to be signed by the Chairman and counter-signed by the Secretary, payable to the order of the Treasurer.

The Community Hall shall be used for any entertainment for the benefit of the Presbyterian and United Churches and any of the schools in the community; and also for any other meeting in connection with said churches and schools, the Dorcas Club, and any society in the community.

Orange Lodge, #49 L.O.A shall have the use of the Hall for regular meetings and all other meetings and entertainments held by the Orange Association.

The Hall may be used for political or other meetings and for any performance or concert from outside the community, providing the latter are not of an unobjectional nature, decision as to their fitness to be left to the discretion of the Trustees.

A rental shall be charged to churches, schools, societies and any other persons or parties for the use of the hall.  The amount of such rental in the different instances shall be determined by the Trustees and the proceeds thereof shall be applied towards defraying the cost of fuel, light, and the upkeep and care of the Hall.

No part of these bye-laws shall be altered annulled, appealed or amended nor shall any addition be made thereto unless and until after full discussion at the annual public meeting  of the community and then only on a two-third vote of the members of community present adopting the same.
