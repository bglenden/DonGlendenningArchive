Comprehensive census-like listing of Black River households in 1939, updated and expanded from Newsletter 61. Lists neighbors by location (Little Branch Bridge, bridges, schools, churches, community hall) with family names and some occupations. Over 100 households documented, including Watlings, Fowlies, MacDonalds, Camerons, Gibsons, and others. Compiled with assistance from Harold MacLean, Jack Wilson, Don Edge, and George Watling. Don notes anomalies in naming conventions (single Kelly home vs. multiple Watling households). Explicitly solicits reader corrections and additions to build a complete, accurate census. A valuable genealogical artifact and community history snapshot of Don's childhood neighborhood.

 Black River Households


This Family Chronicle updates and extends my list of neighbours living in Black River in 1939.  (Newsletter No. 61).  The list arose out of a conversation with brothers Kenneth and Norman while on a visit to the Miramichi.  The list was expanded thanks to the assistance of a number of people including Harold MacLean, Jack Wilson, Don Edge and George Watling.  To the households, I have added schools, bridges, churches and the Black River community hall.  The list starts at the Little Branch Bridge, proceeds around the Branch, up the North side of Black river, then back down the south side including Wells Brook.


Readers will notice some anomalies.  Because there was only one Kelly home, it was simply known as Kellys.  Since there were many Watling households, however, each had to be separately identified.
