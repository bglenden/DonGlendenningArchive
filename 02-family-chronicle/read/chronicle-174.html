Thanks to Barry Mackenzie for the following:


School Life in Upper Black River in April 1880:

Teacher: Maggie M. MacIntosh; Class II; Salary of $130.00/year.

Trustees: Donald McBeath, Robert McNaughton, and Donald Cameron.

Total of teaching days in term (3rd Nov. 1879 to 30th April 1880): 117.

Grand total of days’ attendance by students: 1661.

On average, there were 14.39 students present during each day of teaching, or approximately thirty percent of those enrolled. There were 43 children in the district between 5 and 15 years of age; 12 did not attend during this term.

There were desks for 50 students and the schoolroom measured 32’ X 22’. Four visits were made by trustees during the term, one by a clergyman, and 18 by other visitors.

April Examinations in the following subjects: Reading Composition, Writing, Printing, Drawing, Grammar, Geography, Arithmetic, History, Chemistry, and Dictation.

Select pieces recited: “Canada” by H. Ullock; “Meddlesome Matty” by Margery C. Watling; “Spring” by Susie McDonald; “The Soldier’s Dream” by Barbara McNaughton; and “God is in Heaven” by John W. MacNaughton.

Trustees Return was signed by the Trustees and the teacher in the presence of William McNaughton, Justice of the Peace.

School Life in Middle Black River  (Cameron School) in April 1880:


Teachers: Thomas George McKay/Annie J. McLeod; both Class II.

Trustees: Hugh McDonald, Hugh Cameron, and James McDonald.

Total of teaching days in term (3rd Nov. 1879 to 30th April 1880): 114.

Grand total of days’ attendance by students: 1209.5.

On average, there were 10.79 students present during each day of teaching, or approximately forty-three percent of those enrolled. There were 50 children in the district between 5 and 15 years of age; 25 did not attend during this term.

There were seats for 45 students and the schoolroom measured 21’ X 31’. Three visits were made by trustees during the term, two by clergymen, three by teachers from other schools, and six by other visitors.

April Examinations in the following subjects: Reading, Writing, Spelling, Drawing, Grammar, Geography, Arithmetic, History, and Dictation.

Select pieces recited: “The Life Boat”; “The Cat Eater”; “Boy Talk”; “Bee, Clock, and Broom”; “Little Kindnesses”; “Welcome”; and “Give Me Three Grains of Corn.”

Trustees Return was signed by the Trustees and the teachers in the presence of John McRae, Justice of the Peace. It appears that it was Annie J. McLeod who finished out the term, as she taught for 80.5 of the 114 teaching days.
