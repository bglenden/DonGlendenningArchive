Church Report – 1913


Some interesting bits of information from St. Stephen’s (then Presbyterian) Church for the year 1913.

Envelope giving amounted to $463.93

Pastor’s salary and supply was $912.00

Eight cord of wood was purchased for $3.00 per cord.

A church bell was purchased for $137.50, there as a charge of $1.18 for its delivery, wages were $20.00 (for installation, perhaps?) and A&R Logggie's charged $6.36 (reason not shown)

Church service was at 11:00 am, Sunday School at 3:00 pm, and Christian Education at 7:30pm.  In addition, there was a weekly adult Bible class and a Prayer Meeting.

Rev. Grant was the Minister, Donald MacNaughton was the Clerk of Session and Olivia MacDougall (later married Sinclair Williston) was the organist.

Guy Wellington Glendenning


The Herald Advocate, Wauchula, Florida of February 24 1967 reported the following:


Guy W. Glendenning

Guy W. Glendenning, 77, Died February 8 after an extended illness.

He was born in Canobie, New Brunswick, Canada and was a Canadian Veteran of World War l.  He came to Bowling Green in 1930 from Peru where he was affiliated with the Masonic Lodge in 1921, serving as Worshipful Master a few years later.

He was Past Master of Bowling Green Lodge 121 and Past Patron of Bowling Green Chapter 23, Order of the Eastern Star, a Scottish Rite Mason, and a member of the Wauchula Elks Lodge.

Survivors are his widow, Hazel Stultz Glendenning; a son Robert Glendenning of Chesterton, Md.; a daughter, Cornelia Farrell, Huntington Station, Long Island, N. Y; one brother, Ray Glendenning of Canobie, New Brunswick, Canada and four grandchildren.

Funeral services were held February 13 at the Bowling Green Methodist Church with the Rev. E. J. Halter officiating.  Burial was in the Bowling Green Cemetery."


Hazel (Stultz) Glendenning


The Herald Advocate, Wauchula, Florida (1979 - I forgot to record date) reported the following:


Hazel (Stultz) Glendenning


"Hazel Glendenning, 85, a resident of Huntington, N.Y. and former resident of Bowling Green for 46 years passed away Wednesday, July 4 in New York.  She was born in Brooklyn, N.Y.  She was a member of O.E.S.

She is survived by one daughter, Cornelia Traetta of Huntington, N.Y; one son, Robert W. Glendenning of Chesterton, Md., and four grandchildren.

Graveside services were held Saturday, July 7, with interment in Bowling Green Cemetery.  Rev. John Springer officiated."


Watling Island


While on a cruise in the Caribbean earlier this year, the Captain announced that we had passed Watling’s Island during the previous night.  As I reported earlier, I thought the name had been changed to San Salvador Island.  Watling’s Island was named after Captain George Watling who was known as the “pious pirate because he would not let his crew play cards or indulge in piracy on the Sabbath.”  According to one source, there was, and maybe still is, a Watling Castle located at Sandy Point on the Island


Slot Machines Company


Was browsing the internet yesterday and discovered that, from the late 1890’s to the mid 1950’s there was a Watling company that made slot machines in Maine.


History


Lillian (Glendenning) Murray gave me some papers from her mother.  Included was a clipping from the Northumberland News dated June 3, 1981 entitled History of Black River – Part ll.  The article quoted a History of Black River that appeared in the Chatham Gleaner on June 25, 1833.  Does anyone have Part One?  If could find the complete article, I would include it in a future Family Chronicle


Helena (Smith) Kingston


I was saddened to learn of the recent death of  Helena Kingston.  We taught together at  MRHS in, I believe, 1948-49.  I remember stopping to chat one day and she gave a piece of wise advice that I will always remember.  She said, “One must learn to disagree without being disagreeable”.  Sound advice at any age.
