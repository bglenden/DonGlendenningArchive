Media release about Prince Street Elementary School receiving the 2015 Education 20/20 Award for its values education program based on the acronym PRINCE (Proud, Respect, Inclusive, Never-give-up, Caring, Empathy). Documents how the school used award funds to upgrade its stage and support student-led initiatives including creating a school song and mascot.


_____________________________________________________________________________


For immediate release

Date: January10, 2016


A boost to values education


The gym at the Prince Street Elementary School is a busy as we pock our way down the stairs to look at an upgrade to the stage. As the principal, Kelly Gillis explains it “ “The school had launched a pro-active learning strategy based on the name of their school, PRINCE - Proud, Respect, Inclusive, Never-give-up, Caring and Empathy. Students have been busy; they picked a school mascot and logo, wrote a song and then recorded it and also hold monthly assemblies.”


During assemblies, students actually speak from the stage but the stage had gotten run down. So they used the money that came with the award to spruce up the school stage, including buying new curtains. It is all part of the school’s values education program. “We were thrilled to receive the Award”, said Kelly Gillis, Principal, “and decided to use it to enhance our values education program.”


The Award for Excellence in Education was established in 2014 by Education 20/20 Inc. to recognize school engagement; the first award went to Kinkora Regional High School and the 2015 Award to Prince Street Elementary School. Next year’s award is on the application of technology to the improvement of education.


There is increasing support for the view that incentives and awards help to improve student result but they are largely missing in education. Compared to other sectors, education lacks appropriate economic incentives for either the student, teacher, institution to provide a better product or service and do so ahead of schedule. In the case of engagement, The National Parent Teacher Association in the USA points out that “when families, schools, and communities work together, student achievement improves and teacher morale rises.
