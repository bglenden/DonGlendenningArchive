Education 20/20 Award Committee discussion notes with Don Glendenning, Donald DesRoches, and Peter Russell brainstorming new award categories and criteria. The meeting explored expanding from school engagement awards to include journalism awards for education reporting, community group awards for education leadership, and "Greenfield" case studies. The notes reflect Don's involvement in shaping Education 20/20's strategic direction and award program development.

Education 20/20

Award Committee discussion notes

July 29, 2015

1-3-4-0


In attendance: Donald DesRoches, Peter Russell and Don Glendenning


Brain-stormed a number of ideas and decided to pursue the following:


  • Continue school engagement award subject to availability of funding
  • Survey last year’s nominators for views on the award and the process; also to find out why entries dropped from 11 to three
  • Narrow the focus include: literacy or basic skills, technology, environment, innovative or outside the box
  • Journalism:
  • Original idea was an award to a journalism student at Holland College for a series of articles (at least three) about education and published in the media
  • Perhaps open to students at College Acadie IPE, Holland College and UPEI.
  • Maybe an open award to any journalist
  • Should be substantive articles – curriculum, standard, student-staff ratio, class composition, teacher education.
  • Community group award
  • For a leadership role in improving education
  • Examples: employers came together to provide work experience; Charlottetown or Summerside Rotary for their work in literacy; Provincial libraries for their work in literacy
  • Continuing education activities such as Bridges, Workplace learning, 


  • “Greenfield” 
  • Greenfield – like a case study with usual restrictions removed 
  • Examples: student take on a school or community problem; Graduate education students develop a staffing strategy for tomorrow’s schools, two or more departments working together to resolve an issue; design a school system for a hypothetical community
