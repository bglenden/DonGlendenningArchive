Comprehensive 2018 project proposal for community education needs assessment in Souris. Provides extensive demographic and socioeconomic context, describes Education 20/20's consultations, identifies continuing education need, and outlines objectives: inventory existing services, assess adult needs/interests, identify employer training needs, and assess organizational capacity. Budget of $13,600 covers professional oversight, guide preparation, interviews, youth engagement, analysis, reporting, meetings, and administration. Shows Don's systematic approach to community research and planning.


COMMUNITY EDUCATION

Souris proposal-2

Background

Souris is a year-round port in Eastern PEI with a population of 1,053 (Statistics Canada, 2016 Census), a decline of 10% from the 2011 Census. Compared to PEI as a whole, Souris has a smaller proportion of residents under age 15 (13.8% vs. 15.9%) and age 15 to 64 (57.6% vs. 64.7%) and a higher proportion of residents age 65 and older (28.6% vs. 19.4%). Souris also had a much lower median total household income ($42,368) compared to PEI as a whole ($61,163). Wealth generating activities are focused around tourism, farming, marine services, fishing and related food processing, and the normal trades and services of any small community. Compared to the PEI population generally, Souris had a higher percentage of residents with high school education only and a higher percentage without any postsecondary educational certification as of the 2011 Census. Note: The education, labour, and mobility data from the 2016 Census will be available on November 29, 2017.


Education 20/20 Consultations

Education 20/20 Inc. is a not-for-profit organization devoted to ensuring a quality education for all Canadians, including residents of Souris. Since June 1016, Education 20/20 Inc. has met a number of times in Souris to explore the nature and extent of current education services and how, if at all, Education 20/20 could be helpful. Discussion was wide ranging but quickly focused on needs and interests of out-of-school youth, both employed and seeking work. Consultations included Education 20/20 and residents including employers, providers, key informants, PEI Community Economic Development Agency and representatives of SkillsPEI. We have also been in touch with several Island agencies who offer educational services that may be of interest to the area.

Continuing Education

Educational services in Souris are provided by variety of people, institutions and organizations including Eastern Kings Early Learning Academy, Souris Regional School (K-12), Ecole La Belle Cloche (Francophone K-12) Holland College, Harmony Training Projects, Harbourview Training Centre, and an office of Career Development Services. Continuing education is also available from many sources online, but local uptake is unknown. We know that this list is not complete and plan, funding permitting, to develop a complete list of continuing education assets.


Need

Continuing education is a critical element of community, workforce, and economic development and is one factor that supports rural population recruitment and retention. While there are several providers of continuing educational services in Eastern Kings County, there is no comprehensive assessment of their offerings or the continuing education needs of residents. There doesn’t appear to be any community input into overall continuing education priorities for the region. There is no local body for planning or coordinating educational services available or needed in the area. Finally, supports to adults in the area for educational planning are limited primarily to any advice provided via career counselling services

Objectives

  • Develop an inventory of existing continuing education organizations and their services (assets)

  • Assess the continuing education needs and interests of adults who have completed or left public school relevant to community, workforce, and economic development (all ages 18 and above)

  • Identify anticipated needs of the employment community for training of existing and expected employees

  • Identify the capacity of community organizations and individuals to meet the identified continuing education needs


Approach

  • Forming a project steering committee

  • Review and analyze any relevant and available documentation and statistics

  • Develop key Informant interview guides and the adult continuing education survey instrument

  • Prepare survey interview instruments and interview guides

  • Document nature and extent of current assets

  • Interview key informants

  • Survey employers

  • Survey young adults (18-30 years) to determine training needs and interests

  • Analyze data collected, draw conclusions and draft report.

  • Report to participants

  • Share the results of the project with relevant organizations and the community.
