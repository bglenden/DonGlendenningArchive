Components of an Education System


Background:

Some 140,000 Islanders are currently travelling life’s road, a road of varying lengths and for most, a road with many twists and turns. While on that road, each engages in a number of life roles including that of an individual, family member, learner, citizen, consumer and producer. The preparation for life’s roles is called education and the better prepared we are, the better we are able to cope with all that we encounter or create. That learning is complicated by the fact that each person is unique; unique in our basic physical, mental, emotional and social make up, unique in our combination of previous experiences, unique in our perceived futures for ourselves, but unique also in terms of our pace and style of learning. Our education consiste of what we have learned or perhaps, whate we have retained from our learning esperiences, or even what we are able to do as a result of ehat we have learned


It is widely recognized that education is a key factor in determining each citizen’s quality of life; in the same way, the collective education of our citizens will determine to a significant degree the quality of the community in which we live. Recognizing all of the above, our communities invest heavily in programs and facilities; the state has two interests. One is to encourage and support a high level of personal growth and development for every citizen; the other is to ensure that our communities have the skills needed to provide a quality of life that our citizens need and expect.


There is a gap between what Islanders have and what they want in the way of education. This gap, the writer believes, arises primarily from our sloppiness with the language. Let me explain. Educators and the public at large tend to use schooling and educationa interchangeablyThey do not do so with agriculture and farming, health and hospitals, justice and law enforcement, religion and eorship nor sports and recreation. Educators I speak know the difference but even they fail to differentiate. It may well have something to do with the lack of public information and participation by the public at large in the education sector, lack of a large and well identified private sector component, Perhaps a few definitions are in order:


Definitions:

  • Education – any experience that has a formative effect on the way we think, feel or act.

  • Learning - Learning is a process of engaging in experiences that have a formative effect on the way we feel, think and act

  • Purpose – help people to develop their capacities to be full participating members of a community

  • Education system – a set of components, relationships and services put in place by a community to encourage and support the education of its citizens

  • Education system

  • A school system refers to the institutions, programs and services provided and encouraged by or with the approval of a community through its government to provide formal education for its residents
