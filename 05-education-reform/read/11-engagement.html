Short reference notes on student engagement drawing from Willms and OECD research. Defines engagement as students feeling they belong, accepting societal values, and participating in school affairs. Notes engagement measures include student feelings and attendance, varies with age, and associates with higher achievement. Lists characteristics of schools with higher literacy: strong disciplinary climate, good teacher relations, high expectations. Notes engagement is integral to instructional design and school culture (reflecting principal and teachers). Documents that engaged students more likely to continue education and find employment, contributing to health, wellbeing and quality of life. Lists components: strong core program, elimination of tracking/streaming, parental involvement, strong discipline, autonomy, authentic evaluation, and curriculum reform. - **Key People**: Willms, OECD researchers - **Key Organizations**: OECD - **Tags**: EDUCATION, PROFESSIONAL, HOLLAND_COLLEGE - **Eulogy Relevance**: MEDIUM - Reference notes showing Donald's focus on research-based educational practices and the importance of engagement in school effectiveness.
