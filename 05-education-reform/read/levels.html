Dear Editor:

Levels

Public education Is complex; there are fields  of  study,  levels of study and contexts.  In addition, there are a variety of life roles,  each having an impact on nature and depth of study, The letter was triggered by proposed changes to  the education system in New Brunswick in which they have adopted  two stages (I say levels:

  • Youngsters who are ready to learn and
  • Students who have mastered tools needed to learn (reading, writing and numeracy).


Seems to me that there is at least one more level of educatiol, that needed to function as a citizen in a democratic society.  Would that be a reasonable way of describing the education level of a high school graduate?  Perhaps more appropriately, it is the message carried by  awarding a Graduation diploma.


Fiving education a life context certainly adds to its meaning and our undeestanding


Sincerely


Don Glendenning
