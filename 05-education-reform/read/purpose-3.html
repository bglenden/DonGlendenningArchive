Don provides comprehensive analysis of educational purpose statements, examining how different organizations define their mission and why clarity of purpose drives employee satisfaction and productivity. He surveys various purpose statements from education literature and contrasts them with PEI's existing system purpose, finding them inadequate as guides for implementation. Don argues that meaningful purpose statements must be clear, understandable, and actionable, then argues PEI's current statement lacks sufficient specificity to guide educational reform. He advocates for revising purpose statements and distributing them widely to stakeholders, positioning purpose clarity as foundational to all downstream decision-making in education policy.

PURPOSE-3

About: 4-11


Education is complex; learners have different interests, experiences, capacities and needs; every Islander is said to fill six life roles, that of citizen, family member, individual, student, consumer and producer. The system offers a variety of programs, subjects, courses, grades, credits, certificates, diplomas and degrees. Citizens improve their education as a way of improving their lot in life, the reason tor governmet funding is less clear.


Purpose provides the fundamental reason for establishing an organization and is the key element in the structuring process; to be meaningful, a purpose statement must be clear, easy to understand and guide the alignment of activities. Studies show that when an organization demonstrates a clear purpose and plan of action, employees are happier and more productive. In order to be useful, purpose statements should explain why the work is important, contributes to alignment of activities and appear manageable.


Purposes vary; the literature provides the following examples:

  • Help people navigate life and contribute to society,

• Academic, political, socialization and economic

• To produce a learning society

• Prepare people for life, work and citizenship

• Support citizens seeking truth

• Help people develop skills, knowledge, attitudes and other attributes needed by citizens in a democratic society.

• Bring people to the full realization as to what it means to be a human


The PEI School Administrative Handbook gives the purpose of the PEI Public Education system as being “to provide for the development of children so that each child may take a meaningful place in society.”; the Website of the Department of Education and Lifelong Learning states the goal of the Department of Education is to “to develop and deliver high quality programs and curriculum in English and French to Island children”. Neither, I would argue, are adequate on which to build an education program or measure its success.


I raise the issue because I believe that a clear purpose is essential both for planning offerings and assessing results It is the writer’s view that the statement should be revised and widely circulated to all stakeholders.
