Dear Mr. Schurman:


As you may have gathered from my letters to the Charlottetown Guardian, I am concerned about the state of education in PEI.  I say this, based on my own experience, on what Islanders tell me, and what I read and hear. Without doubt, education is important, changes are needed, and there is wide support for change both inside and outside the system, but changes fall short of what is actually required and fail to deal with a number of underlying weaknesses such as autonomy, accountability and the mismatch between skills taught and skills needed.  It is also my view that the required changes are not likely to happen unless the public, you and I, demand it. Traditional efforts to bring about change have not worked.  Some innovative, perhaps even bold, measures are required, but we think that a better education system is worth it.  


Over the past few months, I’ve been working with a small group of Islanders who are of the same mind and are willing to devote time and energy to the change process. At this point, we have launched the following initiatives:


  • Ongoing consultation with education officials 
  • An award to an Island school, or family of schools, that best demonstrates the principle of engagement in its every-day activities.
  • A test of a new piece of “engagement software”.
  • A broadly representative group beginning to address the issue of low levels of literacy, and especially the improvement of reading.
  • Publication of information through a newsletter, occasional papers, the traditional and social media.




Our group cannot make this happen alone; we are now looking for partners and supporters; our short term goal in support of the above initiatives is $25,000.  We are pleased to be able to say that the Community Foundation of Prince Edward Island supports this educational initiative and will administer our funds.  Contributions to Education 20/20 Inc. should be directed to the Foundation (Community Foundation of Prince Edward Island, 119 Queen Street, Charlottetown, PE  C1A 4B3), who will issue charitable receipts.


While we do not have much printed matter to show, the enclosed may provide some insight into our efforts and our beliefs.


I’ll be in touch in a few days seeking an appointment to talk further about education and to seek your support.


Sincerely



Don Glendenning

President
