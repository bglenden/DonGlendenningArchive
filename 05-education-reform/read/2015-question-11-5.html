QUESTION 11A -PURPOSES AND ACTIVITIES -5

For application to CRA for charitable status


APPROVED OBJECTIVES

The objectives of Education 20/20 Inc. as found in our documents of incorporation are as follows:

1 To promote education and educational opportunity for every Canadian

2 To exercise leadership in the improvement in all facets of education

3 To foster cooperation between and among all stakeholders

4 Undertake and support research and innovation

5 improve public understanding of the education system


We realize that the above objectives should be revised to better reflect our activities and also to meet the requirements for registration as a charitable organization. The review and rewriting have benefitted the organization; based on our experience since our incorporation and future plans, we propose the following objectives:


A) TO ADVANCE EDUCATION BY ORGANIZING PROFESSIONAL DEVELOPMENT AND LEADERSHIP ACTIVITIES FOR PEOPLE WHO PLAN, ADMINISTER AND DELIVERING EDUCATIONAL PROGRAMS.


Recent Workshops


In the past year, Education offered a number of workshops; all have been of 2 hours duration, led by volunteers, held in the evening and included a presentation and round table discussions.


  1. Community engagement and Early Childhood Learning with Alice Taylor as speaker and Anne Miller, Kim Watts and Brenda Goodine as panelists, (Announcement and press clipping included with supporting documents) held at the Haviland Club on December 4, 2012 (No charge)

  2. A Framework for Learning, a workshop on learning, was held at the Quality Inn, Charlottetown, (Announcement and press clipping included with supporting documents) on October 21, 2013 and included presentations by Alice Taylor, PEI partner of Handle with Care Program, Eileen Conway-Martin, and Communications Director, Native council of PEI, and Brenda Goodine, Learning Manager, Early Childhood Education, Holland College. Don Glendenning, President Emeritus of Holland College was chair the session. (No charge)

  3. From a Learner’s Perspective, a workshop about learning was held on October 22, 2014 in the Stratford Town Hall (Announcement and press clipping included with supporting documents) The evening started with a youth panel identifying the kinds of help they considered most useful when learning new things. The room was provided free of charge by the town of Stratford, the panel was chaired by Tanya Craig, a Director of Education 20/20 Inc. The owner of the McDonalds franchise provided a free meal coupon to each panelist. Mike Chapman, a volunteer, recorded student comments and, later facilitated a discussion to refine the list. The results are being brought together in the form of a “Learning Path” and used in future sessions and posted on our website. (No charge)

Webinars


There is a large unserved and even growing body of people engaged in education outside public institutions; I refer to a dozen agencies on PEI part of whose educational programs have been accepted by the Department of Education for external credits, some private schools, home schooling parents, employer based training and a number of organizations receiving funding to mount programs for underserved youth. Education 20/20 Inc. plans to address that need through a series of webinar-style 3-5 minute video clips for use by educators either alone or in groups. Our first series, “A Learning Framework” is under development and consists of four sessions:

  • Learners provides background as to why learners differ

  • Learning sheds light on what happens when a person learns.

  • Levels of Learning presents options on the meaning of levels of achievement

  • The learning path follows a learner through a learning cycle.


Involved in the first series are: Presenter Donald Glendenning, CM, PhD; Henry Purdy, CM, CRA, and Sara Underwood, Office of Alumni Relations at Holland College. Target date for completion March 1, 2014; costs to be met through donations.



B) ADVANCE EDUCATION THROUGH STRENGTHENING LINKAGES BETWEEN EDUCATION PARTNERS


It takes an informed and engaged community to educate a child; evidence shows that students perform better in schools where parents and the community work together; there is a movement towards greater engagement with community organizations; in PEI, during the last few months, the Department of education has dramatically expanded the number of external credits. Education 20/20 would like to see cooperation between schools and other community organizations expanded even further. We plan to:




Make presentations to Policy makers


  • On September 25, 2014 Education 20/20 Inc. made a presentation to the English Language School Board on Prior Learning Assessment and Recognition during which we advocated for PLAR, Challenge for Credit and exemptions as a way of broadening studies, putting students in charge of st least some elements of their program, and increasing engagement. (Copy of presentation included with enclosed documents)

  • On September 30, 2014, Education 20/20 Inc. made a presentation to the Prince Edward Island Standing Committee on Education and Innovation on A Framework for Decision-making. (Copy included with enclosed documents)

  • We are seeking opportunities to address other bodies such as the Provincial Cabinet, the French Language School Board, Chambers of Commerce and Council of Maritime Premiers.


Work with employers:

About a year ago, the Community Foundation of Prince Edward Island conducted a survey of over 200 young people to better understand their issues. (www.CFPEI.ca). On reviewing their report, Education 20/20 was struck by the “preparedness gap”, that is the preparedness of young people for further education and for work. Early in 2014, Education 20/20 Inc. organized workshops in six communities across the Island to publicize the findings, determine the extent of the concern, and identify actions, if any, that could or should be taken. Perhaps the most significant finding was the lack of communication about educational matters between providers and users of educational services. Survey results have informed the following activities:


  1. Working with employers and educators seeking alternative ways for employers to obtain the information they want about an applicant’s basic skills. A mechanism for PEI is now emerging that will see the Holland College Assessment Centre and Workplace Learning PEI offer a testing service for employers and employees.

  2. Development of a “math ladder” as an alternative to grade-level outcomes and high school marks, as a way define levels of achievement. Based on discussions to date with employers, UPEI and Holland College, the likely outcome will be a system, similar to that practiced in music, whereby the establishment of levels is handled by the Royal Canadian Conservatory of Music rather than local teachers of music; exploration continues.

  3. One of the conclusions resulting from our preparedness workshops is the need for direct dialogue between education and the community it serves. To bridge the gap, we are organizing a series of discussions, each session will have a one page background paper, a presentation if appropriate, followed by open discussion. The objective is to increase communications rather than to reach agreement. The pilot run of discussions will begin in early 2015 between 4:30 and 6:00 pm with a group not to exceed 20 people; additional groups will be organized if numbers warrant.

  4. We will continue to engage employers and educators in projects and panels to increase communication; service clubs will be encouraged to invite educators, especially principals, as speakers, and will make a special attempt to produce occasional papers and media releases about items of interest to them.





Workingwith community-based education organizations


We are all familiar with schools, colleges and universities as providers of educational services; we are less familiar with, and often overlook, the many other providers of educational services including youth organizations, cadet programs, sports development programs, etc. More to the point, staff in these groups are seldom included in conferences, workshops or other educational activities. Education 20/20 Inc. views them as an important and growing segment of our education system and will actively recruit their participation in ongoing activities but also survey them as to their needs and interests.



Working with the community at large


Parents play a pivotal role in the education of their children; children perform better when their parents and the community are engaged in the education process and yet, broadly speaking, the school system does not encourage deep engagement. To encourage engagement, Education 20/20 Inc. has instituted an award of Excellence to a school or learning centre that best demonstrates a high level of engagement in its day to day activities, especially those activities over which the school has control. (Announcements, descriptions and other supporting information provided with supporting documents).


Surveys of parents and other stakeholders are being planned:

  • A survey of interests of stakeholders as a basis for program planning

  • A survey of stakeholder views on education issues with results publicly reported.


C) TO ADVANCE EDUCATION BY CATERING TO NEEDS OF LIFE-LONG LEARNERS:


There are a number of significant gaps in our instructional services:


Continuing Education


Education is a lifelong process; most of our educational funding goes to institutions and for the education of the young. But in rapidly changing times and internationalization of the business world, continuing education becomes even more important. Although technology has put knowledge building within reach of many adults, maintaining and progressing in the workplace may also require new skills and attitudes that can best be dealt with in group settings. Our first step will be to undertake a survey to better define the gap. With that in hand, we can develop a plan of action. Terms of reference for such a study are now being written.


Basic Education


We have identified at least two groups of young people who need help:

  • One group with a high school graduation diploma but insufficient skills to enter further studies or work. Education 20/20 plans to help by offering a tutoring program fashioned on the Sunshine tutoring program offered in Dartmouth Nova Scotia. Each session will be of two hours duration once a week from 4-6:00 pm. Tutors will be volunteers; space has been offered by the PEI Association of Professional Engineers; costs will be minimal and will be met through donations from individuals and businesses. If demand warrants, we will grow this service into a full service study centre where youth can get customized help to meet their particular needs. That help may be as simple as counselling and advice but also may include diagnosis, access to resources, tutoring, counselling or referral to another source of help. Our longer term plan is to expand the service as needed and as resources allow including career information counselling and advice but also may include diagnosis, access to resources, tutoring, counselling or referral to another source of help. The services may be expanded to other communities


  • A second group with the skills that employers need/want but do not have the documentation to prove it. Education 20/20 Inc. is actively engaged with the business and education communities (Holland College, the University of Prince Edward Island, and community educational organizations) seeking a resolution to the problem. The resources and good will are available in the community; information and scheduling should solve the problem. Ongoing


Computer coding

Our world is dominated by technology, not only must we learn how to use it; we must learn some basic tools that will allow us to speak to computers, a language that tells a computer how to do things. Anyone can learn computer coding and once you know how to code, you can create virtual worlds. We want to put this power into the hands of more people, especially young people. We are in discussion on an introductory course in coding on a part time basis for 20 hours at a cost of $100 per participant; We anticipate some sponsors which will allow us to reduce fees. Other courses are expected to follow.



D) TO ADVANCE EDUCATION BY SUPPORTING RESEARCH AND INNOVATION AND PROMOTING BEST PRACTICES

Although Education 20/20 Inc. realizes the importance research and innovation; while not planning to engage in research projects, Education 20/20 will:

  1. Encourage research and researchers; publicize research findings, and feature researchers and support change based on research findings.

  2. Undertake surveys of providers and users of education services. The first, planned for early in 2015, is a survey of stakeholder views on mission statements ( draft survey instrument enclosed)

  3. Publish a series of papers on issues related to ensuring a quality education for Canadians. Papers may report research findings, best practices, innovations, and points of view. Papers will be aimed at the general public and be widely available electronically. The first is a brief report on a series of workshops held throughout the Province earlier this year. Another, on Charter Schools, arises from a request following a public meeting, a third responds at least in part, to providing a framework making decisions about education. (A copy of the first paper is included in our supporting documents).
