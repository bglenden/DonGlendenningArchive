CURRENT SCHOOL SYSTEM

Conclusions


1-4-1 Conclusions


Conclusions:

  • There is widespread concern about the quality of public school education

  • Complaints are mostly about skills with communications and math mentioned most frequently.

  • Urgent problems are structural

  • The system is unlikely to change itself

  • There is widespread support, both inside and outside the system, for change.

  • Important and useful changes can be made without incurring either great expense of disruption to the system.

  • Little evidence of components of the system planning and working together

  • Education is school centered

  • Teachers feel than they have little influence or control in what they do.

  • The system is bureaucratic and top directed

  • There is little public conversation or engagement in policy setting and direction
