About 22

Alignment 


It’s not unusual at this time of year to wonder if we should take our cars for an alignment check.  We may not realize it, but school systems also need realignment from time to time.  If, as seems generally the case,  the role of a school is to help young people to become good citizens, here are a few checkpoints:

  • Outcomes  - Curriculum developers have considerable leeway in the wording of outcome statements; if good citizens is the objective, outcomes should reflect the outcomes that describe good citizenship. 


  • Assessment criteria – Spadey refers to “criterion referenced instruction; criteria should be those used in life outside school.


  • Instruction – – if instruction is outcome based, the end users, teachers, students and parents should have copies of course outcomes


  • Student reports - students should be given a rating on each outcome.


  • Relationship of study time needed to study time available – since the pace of learning varies from student to student, it is important that schools be able to adjust time allocations to accommodate differing learning paces 


  • Scoring rubrics –which has been defined as "a standard of performance for a defined population


  • Benchmarks – a level of performance which is widely recognized by practitioners
