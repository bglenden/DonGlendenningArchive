Mathematics Gap proposal addressing flawed education organization and proposing remedies. Identifies education system issues including age-based curriculum, education-based sorting, batch processes, and lack of criterion-referenced criteria. The president (identified as seasoned educator) met with Holland College and UPEI faculty, chamber representatives, apprenticeship director, and NGO representatives. Concludes Holland College can provide required services. Proposes establishing independent testing service/authority to award qualifications, separating teaching and assessment functions. MATH GAP \proposal


statistics



It is widely recognized that education is a key factor in determining one’s quality of life; in the same way, the collective education of our citizens will determine to a significant degree the future of our community and country. One of the most frequently mentioned concerns by Islanders is the low literacy levels among adults as reported via PISA results, recent CFPEI survey of Island youth, presentation to the Standing Committee by the Charlottetown Area Chamber of Commerce, a 2014 survey by survey by the PEI Home and School Federation and a series of workshops on Preparedness held throughout PEI by Education 20/20 Inc. The most frequently mentioned concern among Islanders, and especially among employers, is the low level of basic skills, especially mathematics, communication and personal management skills. Education 20/20 held a series of workshops across PEI on the results of the CFPEI survey; the Mathematics Gap initiative is a further step in our exploration of math skills needed to function in a modern economy. For the purpose of these notes I refer to math skills although others refer to numeracy, essential skills, math outcomes and math competencies.

Establishment of a small working committee to improve transition from secondary schools to both employment and further studies. The working group includes representatives of Holland College, the University of Prince Edward Island, the Greater Charlottetown Area Chamber of Commerce and the Government of PEI. Using mathematics as a pilot project, of employers, post secondary institutions and the public at larege Mathematics was chosen as a pilot. Four issues have been identified:


The gap: what constitutes the gao? What do employers expect in the way of mathematics that recent graduates do not have. Solving this issue will require an identification of what is being taught, what has been learned an how to convey the information.


Math levels: While what is taught clearly identified and regulated, what learners have learned is less clear and the same grade from different schools may differ significantly. The committee is of the view that a common set of outcomes or competencies is required; so far, a suitable analysis has not been found with the likely result that one will need to be developed, after which, a standard set of levels will have to be agreed upon.
