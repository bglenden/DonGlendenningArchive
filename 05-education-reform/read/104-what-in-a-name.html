My view of education is very simple; education is the result of “experiences that have had a formative effect on the way I think, feel or act”.  If I need a more formal definition, I usually refer to the OECD view that education is “organic and sustained communication to bring about learning” or Good’s Dictionary which describes education as “the aggregate of all processes by which a person develops abilities, attitudes and other forms of behavior”.   


The above  are inclusive definitions; they cover people of any age; with any postal code, regardless of previous education or experience, for any life role and any length of time.  I cannot think of any Islander or educational process that would be excluded.  While I expect that educati   taxpayers believe that education is important both for the “benefit” of individual Islanders and to ensure that PEI is the place we want to live, governments will offer encouragement and support .

It appears to me that the Island education system reflects quite a different and narrower view.  For example:

Currently our Department operates as the “Department of Education and Life Long Learning.  The former Government operated as “The Department of Education and Early Childhood Development”  Education in Manitoba falls under the Department of Education and Training.  I could understand training, lifelong learning and early childhood being divisions of a Department of Education but the practice of  including them in the name is misleading.  

When I read articles emanating from the Department of Education and Lifelong Learning it seems that education and the help provided in  school are virtually the same.  That view is at odds ith what I read in the education literature.  The functions of  an education are typically thought of as leadership. governance, funding, instruction, information, counselling, quality management and education products.  Most of our instruction takes place purpose built facilities (schools, colleges and universities) but the function is that of instruction.  We readily distinguish between agriculture and farming or health and hospitalization and believe that education deserves the same treatment
