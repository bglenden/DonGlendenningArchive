Educational framework document using metaphor of "road of life" to explain how individuals experience different learning roles and obstacles. Identifies six key life roles (individual, family member, student, consumer, producer, citizen) and discusses natural versus man-made barriers to learning. Reflects Don's holistic approach to understanding education as developmental across the lifespan.

Road of Life

No. 1-4 June 1, 2017


We are all on the road of life; the road is a metaphor for the experiences .one encounters between birth and death. Each experiences life in his/her own way and to differing degrees. Differences commonly include:

  • Timing and extent to which we engage in various life roles or themes that engage us, that of an individual, a family member, a citizen, a student, a consumer and producer.

  • Natural obstacles, restrictions and hurdles such as time, age, personal and family responsibilities, geography, finance,

  • Obstacles resulting from or even compounded and compromised by people we meet along the way.

  • Man-made obstacles that allow or prohibit access to learning opportunities such as an entrance requirement, a qualification, an age level, physical characteristics, level of education, level of wealth, temperament or a credential.

Personal baggage including what we were born with, what we learned since and our aspirations.
