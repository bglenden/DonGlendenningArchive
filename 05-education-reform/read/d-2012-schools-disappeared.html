Published September 5, 2012, this letter references Don's memory of Patrick Watson's 1969 keynote at the PEI Teachers' Federation convention titled "The Day the Schools Disappeared," which explored hypothetical scenarios if communities lost school infrastructure. Don poses a more provocative question: what would change if schools lost connection to the School Board and departmental oversight? He asks whether the loss of bureaucratic layers would affect student numbers, instruction quality, achievement, operations, or costs. The letter reveals his conviction that institutional structures often hinder rather than help educational effectiveness.

September 5, 2012


Editor 

Guardian

Charlottetown, PEI


Dear Sir:

The Day the Schools Disappeared


The year was 1969; I had just arrived on PEI.  The PEI Teachers’ Federation was holding its annual convention.  The Confederation Centre was packed.  I arrived late and actually sat on the top step while listening to Patrick Watson.  “The Day the Schools Disappeared” was a presentation I will never forget.   For 40 minutes or more, Watson led us through some likely scenarios should a community find itself without schools. What would happen to the children? Where would classes be held? What subjects were most important?  What should they do first?  What kind of schools, if any, are needed? Could classes be held in the library?  And so on.  We need that kind of discussion today, but let me pose a different question


 If,  tomorrow morning, schools should lose all contact with School Board and Department staff, what difference would it make to a local school and community?  And even more catastrophic would be the loss of all Departmental regulations, policies, guidelines,  etc.  What would be the impact on:  


Number of students?

Quality of instruction?

Student achievement levels?

Standards?

Daily operation?

Educational focus?

Cost?

Engagement?

Teacher workload?

Administration?

Teacher health and morale?

I raise this issue because of the view that the pressing issues in education result from an out moded delivery system rather thhan out of date pedagogy, but I would be delighted to hear your views at  HYPERLINK "mailto:standards2020@gmail.com" standards2020@gmail.com.


Sincerely



Don Glendenning
