Editor

Charlottetown Guardian


Dear Editor:

Our education purpose


It is not clear just what we are trying to accomplish through public education in PEI. Department literature speaks of “creating opportunities for student success”, ‘helping students secure a place in society” and the role of the Department as one of “developing and delivering high quality programs and curriculum” and there may be others.  None of the above, however, seem to capture the role and spirit of what education is doing or needs to be done.  Neither do they provide sufficient detail on which to design programs or measure results. 


Education is a service to people; people fill a number of roles including that of a citizen, family member, individual, student, consumer and producer. All roles deserve support. Based on my conversations with Islanders, however, citizen and employment “destinations” are suggested most often as preferred contexts. This could be translated in a statement like “the purpose of the public education system is to help Islanders develop skills, knowledge, and other attributes needed for citizenship”. A recent New Brunswick statement proposes that “every child will leave Grade 5 having mastered the tools to learn – reading, writing and numeracy”.  A statement of this sort wouldcertainly  sharpen the focus for PEI. 


A clear purpose not only directs an organization. it provides a focus for instruction and assessment; Instead of simply teaching a subject or a grade level, teachers would be helping students gain skills needed to function or thrive in the years ahead. Report cards would report what has been learned and how well. 


Covid years were difficult for education; a “post Covid” public conversation on future direction is called for. 


Sincerely


Don Glendenning
