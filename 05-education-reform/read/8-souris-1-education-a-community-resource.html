Letter dated January 15, 2019, to Charlottetown Guardian and Journal Pioneer. Don discusses Education 20/20's community survey in Souris revealing employer struggles to find skilled workers and learner demand for practical, part-time, hands-on training. He argues education is "bundled" bureaucratically and proposes "unbundling" services (grading, qualifications, quality assurance) separate from school functions. References OECD recommendations on school autonomy and accountability.

Dear Sir: 

Education: Done to, for or with its community? 


My initial venture into matters of education in Souris started with a simple question which popped into my mind when sipping coffee at  main street restaurant; where do residents of Souris get the information they need to make decisions about their education?  Today, I would rephrase the Question to ”Who represents Souris at the education decision-making table?  


During the past few weeks, Education 20/20 gathered information from people in Souris about their education interests and needs; we learned that employers are finding it difficult to find employees that can run their new  hi-tech equipment; trainees would like their training to be more hands on and practical, part-time and night classes  seem to have disappeared, and trainees would like better access o on-line courses.   This is valuable information for planning puroses. Who they should tell or who can help is unclear; so too, is how Education 20/20 can help. 


The above points to an issue about the way we organize education. I call it a bundling issue. Educating is a complex business, it involves a variety of services and specialties.  We’ve bundled to the point of being bureaucratic.  As examples, grading and the granting of qualifications are not school functions so hive them off to somewhere else; The same could apply to quality assurance.  The OECD tells us to allow schools more autonomy band increase their accountability.  And I’m sure there others. We need to identify the range of services and ask how each can best be made available.  My analysts suggests the Premier is the  only person who has responsibility for education in Souris so I.ve sent him a copy.


For readers interested in the survey, it is available at  HYPERLINK "http://www.education2020.ca" www.education2020.ca


Sincerely, 


Don Glendenning 

A long-time student of education
