Puzzled about math

As readers may recall, I’ve been exploring the matter of math education in PEI; exploration has included many conversations over cups of coffee, discussions with math educators, examination of instructional material, identification of citizen math competencies and reading articles along the way.  None of that makes me a mathematician but it certainly makes me more comfortable in what I am about to say.

First a word about education; in my view, we are all educated, all could use more education; we get that education by learning, by improving, adding and changing skills, knowledge and attitudes.  Communities know that raising level of education is good for the individual and for the community in which they live, so communities, through their government provide encouragement and support. Unfortunately, as the World Bank points out in their 2018 report, Ministries of Education have now come part of the education problem.  Now to mathematics.
