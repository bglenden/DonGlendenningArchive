Numeracy Award Proposal

FROM:  French Immersion Kindergarten classes at Westwood Primary

5 homeroom classes with a total of 73 students from the Cornwall area


There are a lack of concrete manipulatives for teaching and learning number sense in French Immersion Kindergarten. Given the play-based, hands-on approach in Kindergarten these classes require more manipulatives for numeracy. 

We are seeking to acquire large format Rekenreks for our 5 classes.  These materials will facilitate the teaching and learning of counting to 10 and simple addition.  

As we explore number sense in Kindergarten it is vital to learn the abstract concepts and to represent them with concrete materials.  

On behalf of the other 4 homeroom teachers in FI Kindergarten at Westwood Primary I would like to submit this request for your consideration.

I will be sending a link about Rekenreks to supplement our proposal.

Thank you, 

Pamela Kennedy

Sure,  the rekenreks are basically modern versions of the abacus.


https://youtu.be/JBEKOb7tWEY

 

There are red and white beads used to count and add.  I sent a link to a youtube video of how they work with my proposal.  A quick Google search of REKENREK will provide an abundance of examples.

 

They are an excellent manipulative for 5 year olds to learn addition and to play with number sense from 1-20.  They are portable and easy to use.  Kindergarten students would greatly benefit from these devices as they slide the beads to count and add.

 

The Department of Education recommends the rekenreks for use in the Math curriculum however there are limited resources to purchase them for the entire province.  This grant would certainly go a long ways to supplement the resources that we can use in Kindergarten at Westwood Primary.

Thank you for your consideration,
