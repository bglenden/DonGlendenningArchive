Letter dated August 2018 from Don to Charlottetown Guardian cataloging emerging educational innovations and reforms he finds noteworthy: gradeless report cards in BC, competency-based education in Canadian medical schools, democratic school models, holistic assessment scales in PEI, and full-service schools in Toronto. His list demonstrates wide reading across North American education systems and reflects his interests in alternative educational models. The tone suggests both curiosity and concern about rapid change.


August, 2018

Draft/Let- 11

Editor,

Charlottetown Guardian

Charlottetown, PEI


Dear Sir: 

What in the world

Is education coming to?



Gradeless report cards in British Columbia

Teachers who cannot pass Grade 7 math exams in Ontario

Adoption of CBE in Canadian medical programs – Queens University being one of the most recent

Paid agents and advocates for students -primarily for foreign students at universities 

Tech companies that do not require new hires to have diplomas and degrees – reported recently in the Washington Post

Schools for one in New York City

Schools without walls in a number of US cities

Independent qualification authorities – most developed countries other than Canada and the USA

Schools operating on democratic principles, Hanover high, New Hampshire

Holistic rating scales, PEI

Work colleges in the southern USA

Full service schools in Toronto

Independent student t the BC SAIL Academy

Incoming students at Jason Lee school join  multi-grade advisory group and stay as a group through school. 

Students at Bishop Carrol High School in Xlgary have an academic advisor throughout their high school years.



Sincerely,



Don Glendenning

A long-time student of education
