IMPLIED CONTRACT

Is there an implied contract between a school and each student accepted? If so, would any of these conditions apply to the student?


  • 12 years of instruction in school(s) of government’s choice.

  • 12 years of instruction in a range of subjects of Government’s choice.

  • Some subjects of learner’s choosing.

  • Instruction in basic skills to a level needed to function as a citizen?

  • Instruction leading to entry level employment?

  • Experience in living in a democracy?

  • Basic skills needed for entry to post secondary studies?

  • Skills need to care for one’s self?

  • Skills of a life long learner?

  • A work qualification?

While I have examined Government documents a number of times, including the Philosophy of Education, it is not clear to me what each young Islander can expect.


What clauses do you think should be includen in our contract with students?

?




CALCULUS

Science Daily of July 10, 2018 reports a Harvard University study of 6,000 college freshmen at 133 colleges to the effect that mastering “algebra, geometry and trigonometry”, rather than the study of calculus, is key to college success.


The Hechinger Report includes a Chicago finding that “The schools that develop soft skills produced students with higher grades, fewer absences and fewer disciplinary problems and arrests in high school. Later, the students who attended these high schools graduated and went to college in higher rates. In a few more years, we may learn that the students who attended schools that are strong in soft skills earned more college degrees.”


London Times: Much of secondary school maths is as useful as ancient Greek in adult life, the head of a numeracy charity has told The Times.

Mike Ellicock, who runs National Numeracy, spoke in a personal capacity to suggest separating maths GCSE into two subjects — classical and modern — with the former made optional.
