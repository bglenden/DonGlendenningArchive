Presentation notes for Education 20/20 Inc. workshop introducing organization and philosophy. Education 20/20 is independent, nationally incorporated, not-for-profit organization focused on quality education for every Canadian. Don served as President with board including Alice Taylor, Brenda Goodine, Bill Whelan, Owen Parkhouse, Tanya Craig. Organization operates without formal membership, employing network model emphasizing learners rather than education providers. Key insights center on learning models and community support. Activities include newsletters, website, workshops, awards. References OECD PISA accountability findings and UNICEF emphasis on child-centered, relevant content beyond knowledge acquisition.

Introductory Notes



Welcome


What is Education 20/20:

  • Small group came together to share views
  • Decided to work together
  • Formed Education 20/20
  • Independent
  • Incorporated nationally
  • Open
  • Unaligned
  • Vision- A Quality education for every Canadian- copy on table
  • Have a network, no members as such
  • Big tent, happy to host groups
  • Network but no members
  • Accommodate most interests
  • Shouldn’t need a new organization every time there is a new interest.
  • Recognize support of CFPEI, handle our finances, issue receipts
  • Work with people and organizations

Key beliefs:

  • Education Is important
  • Will determine to a large degree what we become as individuals
  • Will determine to a large degree what our community becomes
  • Is not limited to schooling
  • Requires all our efforts
  • Can benefit   from greater involvement by the community


Quality Education

  • The OECD, the home of PISA testing, tells us accountability, autonomy, and choice are strongly and positively associated with the level of student performance, on PISA results.  
  • UNICEF tells us to focus on content that is relevant, processes that are child centered, and outcomes that go beyond acquiring knowledge.
