D-2015-1

Award for schools and learning centres


An Excellence in Education Award is available to PEI schools and learning centres again this year. The Award, in the amount of $2,500, will recognize a school or learning centre in PEI for its application of technology to the instructional process. Anyone may nominate and the Award is open to any school or learning center operating at the K-12 level. In making the award, Education 20/20 Inc. recognizes the commitment by the Province to provide an additional $4m to upgrade information technology in PEI schools in the coming years.


This marks the third year for the Award, the 2014 award going to Kinkora Regional High School and the 2015 Award to Prince Street Elementary School. Whereas earlier awards focused on the level of engagement by the school and community in the daily operation of the school, the 2016 award will take into account the nature and extent to which technology is used to support the school philosophy, relevance to curriculum outcomes, ease and extent of access by all students, implications for student learning, adequacy of equipment to task, and engagement by students, teachers and community.

Decisions will be made solely on the basis of information presented. Anyone may nominate a school or learning centre, further information and nomination papers are may be downloaded from the Education 20/20 website, www.education2020.ca. Nominations close on April 30, 2016 with the award being made in mid-May. All decisions about the Award are the prerogative of Education 20/20 Inc.


Education 20/20 Inc. is a broad based, independent national, not-for-profit education organization, located in Charlottetown, PEI with an interest in improving the quality of education for every Islander. Further information about the organization, and the Award, may be found at www.edication2020.ca
