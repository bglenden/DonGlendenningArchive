Bridging the preparedness gap


Basic skills, especially in math and communications, are on everyone’s list of needs for young people finishing high school, whether going directly to work or taking further studies. But young people should also have good personal management skills and a high comfort level with technology regardless of their after school plans.


These were a few of the ideas put forth during a series of workshops organized across the Island by Education 20/20 earlier this year on the subject of preparedness. The idea for the workshops arose out of an earlier survey by the Community Foundation of Prince Edward Island (www.cfpei.ca)of over 200 Island youth on a number of issues that directly affect them.


As to narrowing the preparedness gap, the report suggested such things as greater student engagement in the learning process, increased communication between parents and teachers, developing independent study habits, and schools developing a “can do” atmosphere.
