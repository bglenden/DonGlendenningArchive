A CALL FOR CLARITY

Some time ago, I set out to explore public education with a view to increasing my knowledge and understandings. Education is complex; information plays a key role. Learners have different interests, experiences, capabilities and needs; they also fill a variety of life roles.

.

The conclusions that follow are based on my own experience, exploration of literature, agendas and minutes, conversations and review of school websites.

  • In a report in 2018, the World Bank reminded us that education and going to school are not the same. Education is a service; school is a place. School and schooling may also be thought of as a pattern of help for learners. Experiences in school are major contributors to one’s education but it is the experience not the school that counts. It is like the preparation of the food rather than the management of the restaurant.

  • Governments usually structure around branches or fields. PEI Agriculture lists its divisions as land, water, crops, animals, etc. Health is organized around primary care, drugs, Covid, hospitals, etc. The Department of Education should focus on areas of learning such as math, science, and self management rather than running schools.

  • The PEI Department uses “Education and Early Years” in title; doesn’t education include early years?

  • Documents refer to helping young people “secure a place” in society; what do we mean by “a place”?; what criteria would one use to design a program or assess progress?

  • The Department refers to its role as one of “developing and delivering programs”; the literature normally describes the role as one of helping people gain “skills, knowledge and attitudes needed to thrive in future years? The two are not the same.

  • Recently I decided to check the PEI “Education Act” for deinitins. Imagine my surprise to find that PEI no longer had a definition. Thinking it was my inability to use the current technology, I phoned the Legislative Library for help. The Libarian was most helpful but failed to produce a definition. Later I learned that “education” had been dropped from the act in favour of “school”. I find it strange indeed that a Department as large and complex as “education” lacks a simple definition.


Conceptual clarity is a must to understand the people we serve, the services we provide, learner needs, product, processes and values especially when the subject carries layers of subjectivity. Perhaps the Department could produce and circulate a list of standard definitions.
