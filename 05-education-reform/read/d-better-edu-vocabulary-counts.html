Editor 

Guardian

Charlottetown, PEI


Dear Sir:

Better Education: Starts in the Home


I am an advocate of change in the way we deliver education and, therefore, welcome the Minister’s announcement that a review of school governance is planned.  Needs are diverse and the delivery system should encourage, and be able to accommodate, a variety of providers.    


Government’s role is to provide leadership, standards and resources – and then get out of the way.  The Province of Alberta appears to include the greatest range of diversity within its school system, and Alberta learners consistently garner high test results.  Is there a connection? I don’t know.  


The number of administrative units, whether one or five, will in itself do little to improve the quality of education.   What school boards need is greater freedom and accountability; the same could be said for teachers.  My scan of delivery systems includes the following:


Private schools   

Teacher led schools such as Palmer Park in Detroit, MSLA in Denver, Colorado, or similar arrangements in Milwaukee, Minnesota, Boston and elsewhere.

Charter school – alberta has, I believe, a dozen.

The Edmonton Model although details are not clear to me at this time

A Crown corporation may give a government the control it wants and education the freedom it requires

Creating a model unique  to PEI 


In 1969, the College Planning Committee opted for a Board of Governors; it appears to have worked.  Forty years later I am convinced that Holland College would have been quite a different institution had the College Planning Committee chosen, instead, to follow the governance model in place in sister provinces.  So, why not consider the College model for our public schools?  


The cause is important, a review is timely, and there is considerable support for change from both within the system and the community at large.   It’s time to think outside the box.  Feel free to write me at  HYPERLINK "mailto:standards2020@gmail.com" standards2020@gmail.com. 


Sincerely


Don Glendenning

A long-time student of education
