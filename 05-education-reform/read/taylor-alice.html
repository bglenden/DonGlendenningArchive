A July 10, 2015 nomination letter supporting Alice M. Taylor for the PEI Senior Islander of the Year Award. Don praises Taylor's 35-year career in early childhood education at Holland College, her program's quality and national recognition, and her continued professional development. He highlights her guest speaking, board service including the Kindergarten Transition Team, leadership in the Early Childhood Development Association, and co-authorship of "The Dance of Bullying." Don emphasizes her role as Education 20/20 Inc. founding member and Secretary-Treasurer, her commitment to the Handle with Care mental health program, and her breadth of community engagement. He characterizes her as a "Wise Woman" embodying lifelong learning and community dedication.
Education Philosophy & Reform






July 10, 2015


Senior Islanders of the Year Award Nomination Committee

Attention: Catherine Freeze

Department of Community Services and Seniors

PO Box 2000Charlottetown, PEC1A 7N8



Dear Ms. Freeze:


I am pleased to provide a letter of support for the nomination of Alice M. Taylor for the PEI Senior Islander of the Year Award.


Alice retired as head of the Early Childhood Education Department at Holland College after 35 years as an early childhood educator, college instructor and life coach; under her leadership, the Early Childhood Education program became recognized as a quality program at the cutting edge of the field. Alice didn’t just deliver quality instruction, she also “walked the talk” in that her program modelled what she expected her students to do and be when they had students of their own. The program quickly gained recognition beyond PEI and soon included students from other Provinces as well. Alice was and still is the classic lifelong learner; hers was a sandwich education of work and study and even though retired, she continues to keep on top of her field. The early childhood sector in PEI today owes a debt to Alice Taylor, and the Early Childhood program she led, for the presence of well-qualified ECE teachers in Centres across the Island.


Not surprising, her insights and services were in high demand and she was often a guest speaker and resource person, served on many boards and committees including the Kindergarten Transition Team during the recent expansion of Kindergarten programs, played a leadership role in the formation of the Early Childhood Development Association and was president for a four year period. As well, she is a published co-author, along with Psychologist, Ken Pierce, of The Dance of Bullying; she continues to advance the anti-bullying agenda in society


Alice is a founding member and Secretary Treasurer of Education 20/20 Inc., an independent, broad based, national, not-for-profit education organization, located in Charlottetown, PEI but with national incorporation and national aspirations; the focus of the organization is on ensuring a quality education for every Canadian, her record fits the mandate precisely. Every new organization needs capable, experienced, wise and willing members who are committed to the cause and prepared to give above and beyond the call of duty. Not surprising, Alice is always ready to do her part – plus a bit more.








Alice is a firm believer in the centrality of the learner in the teaching learning process, the importance of social and emotional awareness and the connections between mind, body, spirit and human growth and development. In this regard, Alice has been one of the leaders in the research, development and implementation of the Handle with Care program, a program designed to help parents and caregivers promote good mental health among children under the age of six. Her commitment to Early Childhood Education in general and the Handle with Care Program in particular, is outstanding. And her leadership in its expansion throughout PEI and the Maritime Provinces appears to have no limits.

What always strikes me about Alice is the breadth and depth of her engagement, first of all with her family which is scattered all over North America, then with her community including in the Strawberry Hill Green Community in Stratford, her book club and, along with her husband, Park Royal United Church. Alice has lived through many trends and philosophies, and, given her breadth of interest and experience, can offer the wisdom of being part of those trends and their likely effects on issues of the day.

I have known Alice for many years and could describe her qualities in many ways; she is knowledgeable, engaging, open minded, perceptive, and caring for others, especially for children. In terms of this award, however, perhaps the best way to describe her would be as a senior who continues to be engaged, not just in her community, but engaged in making our community a better place for all of us. In the words of a colleague, Alice is a mother, grandmother, entrepreneur and “Wise Women” who is looked up to by peers and younger persons alike, a champion of respect and self-esteem


I am pleased to support the nomination of Alice M. Taylor for the PEI Senior Islander of the Year Award.



Sincerely



Donald Glendenning, C.M.

President
