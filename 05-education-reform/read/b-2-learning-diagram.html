About education


Learning path

No. B-2 August 1, 2014


The first step in understanding education is to understand learning and the learning process. So, what do we know about learning. First of all learning is an individual matter; only the learner can learn. To learn is to change behaviour; a person who has learned has new knowledge a new skill or a different attitude. Learning involves engaging in an experience that has a formative effect od us.


To better understand the learning process, Education 20/20 invited a panel of young people to tell us how they went about learning and what kinds of things they found most helpful. Key words were listed on a flip chart; the list was lomg, however, on further discussion the panel members settled on the following:

  • Gathering information

  • Gather Information

  • Attend lecture

  • Seek counselling

  • Observe demonstration

  • Attempt task

  • Undergo assessment

  • Motivate self

  • Reflect on experience

  • Take diagnosis

  • Obtain feedback

  • Take instruction

  • Self evaluate

  • Coach others


Ten we asked about the order in which they took those steps and found, not surprisingly, that they followed quite different paths. As an example one student said his first step would be to google the topic; another said if it involved a new skill, he would probably attempt it first, while others liked the traditional lecture as a place to start. Since panel members were in school or recent graduates, much of the discussion revolved around gaining new knowledge rather than skills or attitudes.


It also became evident that different purposes for learning did not substantially change the help required, it may well make the path longer. Although not part of the original discussion, we’ve added the manner of recognition likely to occur at the close of the learning cycle.


The chart on the back of this page reflects an attempt to chart the support that students found useful and to do so in a manner that would respect many learning styles. It’s our first attempt.
