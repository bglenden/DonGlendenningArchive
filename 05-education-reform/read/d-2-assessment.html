A May 2015 proposal from Education 20/20 Inc. for establishing an independent certification service for subject proficiency. Don identifies problems with public school reliance on social promotion and age-based grading, proposing instead a third-party assessment system for subjects like mathematics. The service would operate independently from government, open to individuals of all ages, certifying competency levels aligned with employer needs and postsecondary entrance requirements. Key characteristics include identifying competency levels, providing unbiased assessment, and supporting both educational providers and users. This innovative approach demonstrates Don's focus on transparency, standards-based accountability, and removing institutional barriers to accurate skill recognition.


Assess

May 1, 2015

EDUCATION 20/20 INC.

Assessment Service-1

Background

  1. Public schools moving to social promotion

  2. Conflict between teaching and ranking and sorting

  3. Age-based grading is not defensible

  4. Credentials are losing their value


Conclusions

  1. The following deserve further exploration:

  • Construction of a “math ladder” for describing  levels of math 

  • Current documentation by schools of a person’s actual math skills

  • Need for an independent math testing service

  • Description of math level(s) of local employers

  • Description of math needed to meet entrance requirements of postsecondary institutions

  • Establish a level of math expected for high school graduation



Proposal

To establish an independent assessment service to certify a person’s proficiency in selected subjects and fields.


Characteristics of Service

  • Identify levels on a field or subject area

  • Certify applicants level

  • Provide service to individuals and organizations

  • Provide assessment service to both providers and users

  • Independent of government

  • Open to individuals of any age

  • By application

  • Certificate not a credential

  • Results provided to person assessed and, on his request, to others

  • Partner with schools, colleges, private schools

  • Not limited to paper and pencil tests



Work plan

  • Develop a workplan

  • Establish a project team


Benefits:

  • Independent

  • Removes bias

  • Improves consistency

  • Reduces teacher workload


Notes:

  • Feasibility study

  • Funding

  • On-line course providers moving into the credit field
