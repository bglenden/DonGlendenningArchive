Letter to the Minister addressing public education's clarity problem. Don identifies four specific issues: undefined education in policy, confusing department literature, the problematic equation of education with going to school, and understated departmental role. He calls for a dictionary of key terms and emphasizes that education's future depends on clarity in purpose and definitions.


Dear Minister:

1

A Need for Clarity

My morning reading includes The Conversation, a daily independent online publication out of Toronto; a recent article on reforming public education triggered this letter.

Public education has a clarity problem. In the interest of being inclusive, those who develop policy for education often overlook the need for specificity. Some PEI examples follow.

  1. The Department sometimes adds “Lifelong Learning” or”Early Childhood Development” to the Department Name.; doesn’t “Education” include “lifelong learning”?

  2. The PEI Administrative Handbook gives the purpose of education as being “to provide for the development of children so that each child may take a meaningful place in society”. Doesn’t include auls as well?

  3. Elsewhere, the PEI literature speaks of helping students find a “a meaningful place”. While I understand the intention of the statement, I find it insufficiently clear as a basis to design programs or measure results.

  4. The PEI education website refers to the role of the Department being “to develop and deliver high quality programs and curriculum in English and French to Island children”. This is a major understatement of the Department’s role; my understanding, and my interpretation of public understanding, is that the Department’s role is to “encourage, support and regulate” education for all Islanders.

Education is important; the nature and quality of our futures depend on it. A dictionary of key words would be appreciated.

Sincerely

Don Glendenning

May 30, 2024
