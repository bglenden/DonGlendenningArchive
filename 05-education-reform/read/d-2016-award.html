For immediate release


$2,500 Award Announced


Education 20/20 will offer an award to a PEI school or early learning centre again next year. Earlier awards focused on engagement by the school and community in the daily operation of the school; the 2016 award will focus on the nature and extent to which technology is used to enhance learning and teaching. The award, $2,500, may be used for any purpose. The Excellence in Education Award is open to any school or learning center operating at the K-12 level. The focus on technology was chosen, in part, to recognizes the commitment by the Province to provide an additional $4m to upgrade technology in PEI schools in the coming years.


In announcing the Award, Dr. Glendenning, President of Education 20/20 thanked Dr. Regis and Joan Duffy for their continuing interest in and support for education in PEI. The 2014 award went to Kinkora Regional High School and the award money, in the words of Jaime Cole, Principal, “was used to make the school a more comfortable environment for the students to learn.” The 2015 Award went to the Prince Street Elementary School.


“Schools use technology in a variety of way depending on the school’s philosophy”, said Glendenning ”and it will be interesting to see the particular applications presented by schools.” Technology has a variety of applications; some teachers may use it to replace their own lectures even as an “upside classroom” where students use technology as homework and class time for internalizing and massaging what has been learned; others may use it to broaden a course or program or as an aid to diagnosing and assessing a student’s level of achievement. When reviewing nominations, the reviewing committee will consider the ease of access by students, relevance to curriculum outcomes, and the level of engagement by students, teachers and the community. “I’m sure that there will be some new applications”, said Glendenning.


Anyone may nominate a school or early learning centre, further information and nomination papers may be downloaded from the Education 20/20 website, www.education2020.ca. Nominations close on April 30, 2016 with the award being made sometime in May. Decisions will be made solely on the basis of information presented; all decisions about the Award are the prerogative of Education 20/20 Inc.
