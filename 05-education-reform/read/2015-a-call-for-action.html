Date: April 9, 2015


A call for action on education



Education 20/20 Inc. today released a seven-point action plan for the improvement of education in PEI. The plan calls for the Department of Education to provide more autonomy and accountability to schools and teachers, and to provide leadership in developing a learning culture that supports all citizens. A learning culture includes an engaged citizenry, a clearly stated philosophy and a learner centered approach that delivers help to students when needed.


While noting many improvements to the PEI system over the years, Glendenning, President of Education 20/20, noted that the impact of the changes has been blunted by an outdated model that focuses on delivery of services rather than the development of people. The seven point plan includes a clear vision, a learner centered delivery model, the Department providing leadership rather than the delivery of services, delegating the function of instruction, a separate qualification authority, a professional model of service delivery and the encouragement of innovation.


Education 20/20 Inc. is an independent, broad based, national, not-for-profit organization located in Charlottetown and committed to ensuring a quality education for all Canadians.
