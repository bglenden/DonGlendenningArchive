For immediate release

D-2015-1

Call for nominations


Nominations are now being accepted by Education 20/20 Inc. for the Excellence in Education Award offered by Education 20/20 Inc.. The award in the amount of $2,500 will recognize a school or learning centre in Prince Edward Island that best demonstrates the application of technology to the education process. Anyone may nominate and the Award is open to any school or learning center operating at the K-12 level. In making the award, Education 20/20 Inc. recognizes the commitment by the Province to upgrade information technology in PEI schools in the coming years.


This marks the third year for the Award, the 2014 award going to Kinkora Regional High School and the 2015 Award to Prince Street Elementary School. Whereas earlier awards focused on the level of engagement by the school and community in the daily operation of the school, the 2016 award will take into account nature and extent to which technology is used to support the school philosophy, relevance of application to curriculum outcomes, ease and extent of access by all students, implications for student learning, adequacy of equipment to task, and engagement by students, teachers and community. Nominations close on April 30, 2016


Decisions will be made solely on the basis of information presented. Anyone may nominate a school or learning centre, further information and nomination papers are may be downloaded from the Education 20/20 website, www.education2020.ca. Nominations close on March 29, 2014 with the award being given in early May. Education 20/20 Inc. reserves the right not to make the award.


Education 20/20 Inc. is a broad based, independent national, not-for-profit education organization, located in Charlottetown, PEI with an interest in improving the quality of education for every Islander. Readers are invited to view the website and add their name to the mailing list for Open Education, a monthly bilingual electronic newsletter and to participate in other public activities
