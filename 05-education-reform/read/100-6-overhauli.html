Education: Product or Service

Customized learning

Education can be viewed as a service or a product; institutions provid boyh, it seems, focus on products; service is under-served and an area for growth. Community schools, Seniors’ College, night classes and continuing education all  address the need but unserviced areas remain and the need is likely to grow.

Education can be viewed as a service (skills, knowledge, attitudes and attributes to thrive in the years ahead) as a developer and provider of products (MOOC’s, courses, programs, diplomas and degrees).  Most institutions, it seems, focus on products; service is under-served and an area for growth. Community schools, Seniors’ College, night classes and continuing education all  address the need but unserviced areas remain and the need is likely to grow.


The need  for education is being fed changes in the world around us – technology, changing nature of work, people changing jobs and the gig economy; both people and the community help. Demand for education services is on the increase in the form of information, counselling, learning products, instruction and support.  Obviously, institutions could, and do, do both but more is needed and the need is likely to grow.  

Education service could be providde in a number of ways:

  • Integrating to existing program offerings
  • Through extended days and years
  • Creating new organizations such as Seniors’ College and Community Schools but with a much broader mandate.

One can visualize a variety of configurations depending on the nature of the community:

  • Essential skills 
  • Soft skills 
  • Technologyeducation service centresWhat would an education service centre look like?  It could be likened to a pharmacy stocked with education products and someone who can advise on them; it may also be like a training centre where a person checks in for up-dating or up-grading, or a place to which a person may be referred, or maintain contact via IT. An education service would draw heavily on institutions but would likely not be institution based.  On the surface, combining both in the same institution makes  sense but, given the record of most institutions with continuing education services, it’s likely that separate institutions will be needed.

My search suggests that success as a service institution will depend in a large measure upon availability of learning centered support materials, experience of teachers, student records. Service management systems, ? ? ? Souris would be a great place yo start.b   

Sincerely

 

Don Glendenning

A long- time student of education.


Obviously, we need schools; they are the hubs of our communities; they are more than just places to learn math, literacy and Shakespeare.  Their contributions include socialization and transferring the culture; they provide a “whole” experience at a critical time in one’s life.
