This document appears to be a fuller version of the Windsor Foundation proposal, reiterating Education 20/20's mission to ensure quality education for every Canadian. The organization operates with volunteer leadership, has applied for charitable status, and benefits from Community Foundation administration. The Sunshine Initiative targets disadvantaged and vulnerable youth experiencing preparedness gaps, and post-secondary students struggling with basic skills. Innovative features include unified curriculum mapping with multiple profiles (rather than grade-specific maps), DACUM-model competency analysis, Bloom's Taxonomy-aligned rating scales, and engagement of broad stakeholder ranges. PROGRAM NARRATIVE

Relationship to Foundation Priorities

The Windsor Foundation has two major areas of interest

  • Disadvantaged or vulnerable youth: These are tough times for young people in transition from public school to further education and to work. While there are many causes, this proposal addresses the issue of basic skills, those skills needed to gain entry to the next phase of life and, without which many are branded as failures. We are aware that sometimes this may from poor decisions by students themselves; on the other hand, we are also aware that the gap may from incorrect information, biased counselling, streaming, out dated course content, poor teaching, inflexible scheduling, lack of encouragement and mentoring, and the list goes on.

  • Higher education initiatives: Although not as well documented, we’ve also been made aware of the many young people now in college and university who struggle with their studies because of low level of basic skills; the writer actually visited make-up classes as part of the information gathering process.Although we do not have the actual figures, we know that the duplication of services is a strain on taxpayer’s dollars.


Our proposal has several innovative features including (1) a curriculum map to be used by teachers and learners alike, it also ensures tha students and teachers have the same view of the content and process, ot ensures that common map to be used by learners and teachers alike so that students and teachers have the same view of the process, (2) it ts a single analysis with multiple profiles instead of the current approach of creating a map for each grade, (3) engaging s broad range of users and providers in the curriculum development process and (4) encourages gonf all educators to see themselves as a team.

Appendix A

Education 20/20 Inc.

Preparedness 2- Skills of Citizenship

Introduction:

In late 2013, The Community Foundation of Prince Edward Island (CFPEI) undertook a survey of over 200 Island youth to identify issues that directly affect them; the report, Taking the Pulse of Island Youth, available on the Foundation’s website at www.cfpei.ca, provides a snapshot of the findings. Bright spots include a positive sense of community, emerging entrepreneurial spirit, volunteerism and new postsecondary offerings.


The main concerns include a lack of jobs, surviving on low-paying jobs, educational deficiencies and drug and alcohol addictions. On the subject of education and learning, the study reports a high rate of completion of high school and postsecondary education, a high level of inclusivity and relatively affordable tuition costs; young people also expressed concerns about their readiness for the next stage of life including:

“preparedness gaps with regard to readiness for

post- secondary study and the workplace” – Vital Signs, CFPEI, 2013

Following the publication of the CFPEI Report, Education 20/20 organized five public discussions for the purpose of informing the community of the report and its findings, learning more about youth issues in our communities, exploring issues of education and learning, and to identify actions to be taken to close gaps. Discussions were lively and wide ranging, and carried on well after the formal session ended. The final report included a number of conclusions and recommendations of which the recommendation below triggered this proposal.

Further study be undertaken to clarify the gaps in preparation for further study and for work - (Preparedness, Education 20/20 Inc., Occasional Paper No. 1)

Education 20/20 has continued its exploration under the title, “citizenship skills” meaning those required of any citizens and considered to be a basic foundation for life changes that are likely to occur including skills needed for entrance into employment and expected for acceptance into postsecondary studies. As evidence I refer to PISA test results for Prince Edward Island; a 2013 survey of issues of Island youth conducted by the Community Foundation; a presentation to a Standing Committee of the PEI Legislative Assembly by the Charlottetown Area chamber of Commerce; How Canada Performs, a Provincial and Territorial Ranking, Education and Training; Education and Skills, and a series of workshops on Preparedness held throughout PEI by Education 20/20 Inc. The PEI Department of Education has not taken exception to the findings nor, to the best of our knowledge, assumed a leadership role that includes corrective action. Based on our consultations, a pilot in the area of math has the most likely chance of success.

Based on our explorations, it is the view of Education 20/20 that:

  • The math gap is real

  • Low basic skill levels on PEI are an issue

  • Low basic skill levels are dragging down the economy of PEI

  • Basic skill levels are not consistently defined

  • Skills thought to have been learned while in high school, are being taught again in Adult Basic Education program at Holland College.

  • PEI lacks a master list of recognized basic skills

  • PEI lacks a single body to give direction to its education efforts

  • Basic skills do not appear to be a priority


Proposal: That Education 20/20 Inc. take steps to the nature, extent and causes of the gap.
