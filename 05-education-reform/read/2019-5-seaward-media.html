April 2019 press release announcing Education 20/20's math skills mapping initiative. Organization proposes surveying everyday math skills used by Island residents, noting that despite math's frequency as a complaint topic, no empirical study of practical math skill use exists. Results will inform teachers, curriculum developers, standard-setting, counseling, career planning, and skill documentation. As part of annual meeting, Dr. Glendenning reviewed year's activities, particularly Souris continuing education survey revealing demand for online courses and hands-on training. Glendenning noted continuing education's importance but insufficient advertising and planning priority. Officers: Bill Whelan (Vice President), Ross Reid (Secretary); fundraising for math skills mapping underway.

2019-4

Math skills to be mapped

Education 20/20 proposes to map the every day math skills of Island residents. Although mathematics is a frequent subject of complaint, it appears that no one has actually surveyed Islanders to determine the MATH skills they In the run of a day. When completed, the results will be made available to interested parties but the results may be of interest to teachers , curriculum developers. They have ben found useful when setting standards, counselling and career planning, and even as a transcript. Fund raising for the initiative is now under way.


Tas part of the annual meeting, Dr. Glendenning, President of Education 20/20 reviewed activities carried out during the year; he noted especially the survey of continuing education needs in Souris. Souris residents would like access to on-line-courses, more hands on training. Glendenning suggested that continuing education is important is often not well advertised and should be given a higher priority in educational planning of Education 20/20 Inc. Other officers, not shown, are Bill Whelan, Vice President and Ross Reid, Secretary.
