Another version of Don Glendenning's congratulatory correspondence to Kinkora Regional High School, emphasizing the breadth of engagement activities and the school-community partnership evident in the nomination. The letter notes that the proposal "presented a picture of a school and community working in harmony for the benefit of students," capturing the ideal of educational practice Don championed through Education 20/20. Multiple versions suggest careful editorial consideration of messaging.
Education Philosophy & Reform

June 7, 2014


Ms Janet Needham-Payne

PO Box 122, Kinkora,

PE C0B 1N0


Dear Ms. Needham-Payne:


Kinkora Regional High School


On behalf of Education 20.20 Inc. I want to thank you for your nomination of Kinkora Regional High School for the first ever Education Award by Education 20/20 Inc.


We received eleven nominations and collectively they painted a picture of engagement activities in Island schools. Nominations came from across the Island and representing all levels, early childhood centres, elementary schools and high schools. Some nominations covered a wide range of engagement activities while others were quite focused; some were generated by members of the school or centre while others came from parents and the public.


We were impressed with your nomination, that the proposal was parent-led and provided such a wide range of engagement activities; it also presented a picture of a school and community working in harmony for the benefit of students.


Again, I thank you for the nomination and congratulate you, your committee and the Kinkora Regional High School. I will be in touch again shortly about the actual presentation.


Regards




Don Glendenning

President

Education 20/20 Inc.


Cc: Jaime Cole, Principal





Others

On behalf of Education 20.20 Inc, I want to thank you for your nomination for the Engagement Award.

There were eleven nominations in all and together they painted a picture of a wide range of engagement activities in Island schools. Nominations came from across the Island and representing all levels, early childhood centres, elementary schools and high schools. Some nominations covered a wide range of engagement activities while others were quite focused; some were generated by members of the school or centre itself while others came from parents and the public.


I want to thank you for your efforts and for documenting the positive activities put in place to encourage and support learning. Unfortunately, however, your nomination was not selected for the award. We expect to offer a similar award next year and look forward to your further participation.


Regards


Don Glendenning

President

Education 20/20 Inc.
