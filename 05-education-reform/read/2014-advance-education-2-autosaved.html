Original application for charitable status provided list of activities under the following objectives as found in our incorporation documents:

  • To promote education and educational opportunity for every Canadian

  • To exercise leadership in the improvement in all facets of education

  • To foster cooperation between and among all stakeholders

  • Undertake and support research and innovation

  • lmprove public understanding of the education system




Based on our experience during the past year, we are seeking a change of objectives and submitting the following a list of activities based on the revised objectives:


1 Advance education by gathering, preparing, developing and disseminating information to stakeholders and the public at large:

  • We publish a monthly newsletter and distribute it electronically to a mailing list of about 200 people; we also irected to the public reader, to help inform Islanders and to distribute to our mailing list, post it on our website, hand it out at meetings, training sessions, workshops and to individuals as opportunities arise. Newsletters are written for the public, include items of interest, a commentary on a current educational topic, announces upcoming activities and events and points to items of interest in professional journals and the public media. Copies of newsletters are included in the supporting documentation

  • Thanks to one of our Directors, we maintain, a website (www.education2020.ca) as a communication hub for organizational activities; our hub currently hosts newsletters, notices of events, recent articles of interest, links, quotations.

  • We are currently introducing blogs, tweets and Facebook activities; we are also planning to add and expect to add instructional video clips.

  • We are developing a series of short occasional papers geared to the public but also distributed to educators; topics will include recent research, innovation and best practices

  • We plan to survey students, parents, employers and the public as to their information needs, to publish our findings and to reflect interests and concerns in future writing.

  • both to inform readers of current matters and views and to encourage Islanders, especially teachers and administrators to expand and clarify views and share them with others

  • Include a youth component of our information activities




2 Advance education through involving people and communities to increase both their understanding and participation in local education activities and issues

  • Organize workshops and other events on a range of issues such as essential skills, learning framework, Challenge for Credit, Charter schools, Handle with Care, application of technology and report cards.

  • Facilitate/establish a qualification authority

  • Organize an electronic forum for the public at large on schooling matters.

  • Host discussions to build relationships that support quality programming

  • We have been requested to make a presentation to the Standing Committee on Education and Innovation and will to do so on a regular basis.

  • Directors will seek opportunities to make presentations to other bodies such as English and French School Boards, Chambers of Commerce, Holland college, the University of Prince Edward Island, and others as seem appropriate from time to time

  • Undertake surveys of stakeholders (parents, learners, teachers, employers, public, etc.) to keep in touch with issues and interests of stakeholders



3 Advance education through encouraging participating in, and supporting, research and innovation in educational matters

  • Engage educators in writing education-related papers as a way to improve their professional development

  • Organizing sessions for researchers to present their finding

  • Convene meetings and discussions where educators can exchange information and views

  • Organize sessions for teachers to learn of innovative ideas and best practices

  • Monitor and disseminate information about current, valid research

  • Include both links and articles on our website



4 Advance education by recognizing leadership and high levels of performance of people, organizations and institutions for their contributions to education in their community:

  • Awards to schools and learning institutions who demonstrate high levels of engagement in their every day school activities

  • Encourage service clubs to include education issues in their programs and educators as speakers.


5 To advance education by providing instruction and suppor:

  • Offer testing and assessment services, especially in such basic areas as reading, writing and mathematics to individuals who want or need to establish a level.

  • Take a leadership role in establishing community tutoring and study centres, hopefully in schools for people of any age.

  • Encourage clubs and competitions– math, robotics, environment, arts, etc.

  • Work for greater credit for independent study

  • Campaign for Independent community based counselling

  • Provide support for community based instruction

  • Providing service

  • Advance education by doing things directly for students

  • Offer short courses, camps and other after-school activities for youth coding, robotics, gaming
