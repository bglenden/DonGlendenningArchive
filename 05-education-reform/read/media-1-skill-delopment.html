For immediate release

Public invited to share views


Islander will have a chance to shape the future of skill development in Prince Edward Island. A public meeting is being organized by Education 20/20 Inc. on Wednesday April 5 at 7:00 pm in the Stratford Town Hall. The session will focus on skill development and will provide an opportunity for people to voice their views, ideas and priorities. Following the session, views will be summarized and presented to the federal and provincial governments


The Governments of Canada and Prince Edward Island enter into Labour Market Development Agreements (LMDAs) under which the Federal Government provides funds to Province for employment related services including skills development. The Province, through the Department of Workforce and Advanced Learning, administers the agreement in PEI. LMDA documents show figure of $28 million for Prince Edward Island in the current year for apprenticeship and certification, adult upgrading, GED testing, skills training, job development and other employment related services.


Speaking in support of extra funding announced in the recent budget, Don Glendenning, President of Education 20/20 pointed out the importance investing in education and recommended that funds would be available for self- study, on line courses and part time instruction; he also believes there is a need for an independent skill testing service.
