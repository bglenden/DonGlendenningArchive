Missed opportunity


There was much ado last week about changing the way that our school system is managed; one might call it a corporate realignment. As a result, the Premier is clearly the de facto Minister of Education and the person who holds the title, Minister, remains focused, not on education but on the operation of schools. Whether questions in the future will be answered more quickly and more fully remains to be seen.


As far as improving the quality of public education is concerned:

  • While school principals will now have a Council to improve the flow of information, a school’s level of autonomy remains unchanged

  • The philosophy of education found on the Department of Education website is still 25 year old.

  • Time, rather than achievement, remains the major consideration for scheduling students.

  • Students continue to graduate without the level of skills needed to function as citizens in the 21st century.

  • Exemptions have not been reinstated in spite of a recommendations by a committee shared by Dean Goddard to the contrary and recommendations favouring a “challenge for credit” option by the Senior High Working Group.


With the organizational changes out of the way, hopefully the Department can focus on the matter of improving quality.
