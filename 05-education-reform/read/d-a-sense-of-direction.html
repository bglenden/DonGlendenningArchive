Letter to editor by Don praising the Minister's statement on preparing students for success in higher learning and workforce, arguing this direction-setting should become measurable outcomes and be posted on every school website.


July 7, 2015


Editor,

Charlottetown Guardian

Charlottetown, PEI


Dear Sir: 

Pushing a rope

It’s hard to push a rope; the leading edge seldom goes where it should. There is no real alternative to getting out front and pulling it along.  So it is with education.

Which brings me to a media release from Education, Early Learning and Culture dated September 9, 2015.  Granted most of the release talks about efforts to push the education rope but one short sentence in the early paragraphs actually acts as a direction-setting tug.  In it, the Minister refers to “preparing students for success in higher learning and the workforce.”  No doubt, the statement will undergo further shaping in the hands of Department wordsmiths but we shouldn’t underestimate its value.

For those who deliver services on behalf of Government, the statement becomes a measurable outcome; for the rest of us it becomes one of the criteria against which we measure; we shouldn’t underestimate its value.  It deserves to be posted on every school website. 

Sincerely,

Don Glendenning

A long-time student of education
