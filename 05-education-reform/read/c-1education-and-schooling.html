Seminal document defining the distinction between education and schooling. Education is defined as any formative experience developing abilities, attitudes, and behaviors of positive social value (farm work, philosophy courses, backpacking in Europe, international consulting projects). Schooling refers to formal institutional training. Donald argues these terms are confused in contemporary discourse. Uses Oregon's life roles model (individual, family member, learner, citizen, consumer, producer) to frame education's ultimate purpose: helping people function better in life roles. Education and Schooling


No. 01 June 1, 2014 C-4


Education - Any and all experiences that have a formative effect

on a person’s attitude, knowledge or skill.


We often confuse schooling and education; we do not similarly confuse health and hospitalization, religion with church attendance or citizenship with court appearances. Seldom do we conclude that a person is healthy because they spent time in a hospital or are religious because they attend church, mosque or synagogue regularly. Why, therefore, do we continue to speak of education when we mean schooling?


To make my point, I was asked recently about my education. I proceeded to tell about the work habits learned on the farm, a cour5se in philosophy while in backpacking in Europe, a consulting project in Nairobi, etc. all of which had an impact on me. It soon turned out, however, that all my questioner wanted to know was where I went to school


As to definitions

Good’s Dictionary of Education defines Education as “the aggregate of all processes by means of which a person develops abilities, attitudes and other forms of behaviour of positive value in the society in which he lives.” A persons education is often described in terms of such skills as creativity, citizenship, social and civic responsibility, communication, respect and cooperation, self-reliance, problem solving, analysis, planning, etc.



Schooling, on the other hand is described as “individual training or education received in an educational institution, military or civilian.” My working definition describes schooling as that part of one’s education resulting from experiences planned and organized for a learner while in school.

. Schooling, on the other hand, is usually described by the number of years of study, the institutions at which one studied or from which one graduated, the results of examinations and the diplomas and degrees received


The purposes of education are many, the ultimate purpose, however, is to help people function better in their current and future life roles. And what are life roles? I’ve adopted a life roles model developed in Oregon in 1990. I find it convenient, easy to understand and simple.


According to the Oregon model, I the course of a lifetime, most people fill roles of:

  • An individual

  • A family member

  • A learner

  • A citizen

  • A consumer

  • A producer.


The role of an education system is to help people fill those roles better. What do we mean by better? That is certainly open to interpretation. One article I came across suggested the five following discernable levels of education:

  1. Exercise leadership in some aspects of one or more life roles

  2. Functions well in most life roles with obvious special abilities in some

  3. Functions adequately in life roles with minimum and occasional support from community

  4. Able to function in most life roles with assistance and direction

  5. Unable to function in life roles without assistance and direction

  6. Unable to function in life roles





Blurring the differences between schooling and education likely to:

  • Discourage lifelong learning

  • Create dependence rather than promote self-reliance

  • Limit learning to what is available in school or organized by teachers

  • Reinforce that education is limited to the study of traditional disciplines,

  • De-emphasize the importance of personal skills such as self-reliance, problem solving, entrepreneurship and creativity.

  • Blur accountability
