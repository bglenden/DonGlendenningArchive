This policy brief (No. 102) argues that PEI's Department of Education has narrowed its mandate over 50 years, now limited to school-based instruction. Don contends the department should shift to encouraging, supporting, and regulating education services for all Islanders rather than merely delivering school programs. He identifies systemic problems: lack of direction, fragmentation, narrow focus, unmet public expectations, bureaucracy, and politicization. First steps for reform include developing clear vision, functional organization, school autonomy, and accountability structures. This document exemplifies his consistent critique of institutional mission drift and his advocacy for systematic educational reform.

Role of government in education

102 

The role of the Department of Education has narrowed during the past 50 years.  At one time it offered full-time and continuing education to Islanders whatever their interests; current services, as spelled out on the Department’s website, appear limited to instruction in schools.  


Education in PEI needs a new mandate; it needs on focus on encouraging, supporting and regulating education services for all Islanders.  Instead, it remains focused on delivering programs to schools.
