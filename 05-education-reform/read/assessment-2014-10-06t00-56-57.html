Credentials and qualifications


Background


Education is a lifelong process of gaining experiences that have a formative effect on the way we feel, think and act; it is a process of changing attitudes, skills and knowledge. Education is a key factor in determining one’s quality of life; in the same way, the collective education of our citizens will determine to a significant degree the future quality of our community and country. Because of the importance of education, our country and community have invested heavily in a variety of educational support services including schools colleges and universities. As part of the teaching process, teachers assess what learners have learned and, depending on the results, design and implement new teaching strategies


Transcripts, I am told, were first issued by teachers at the request of students and provided to students information about what had been studied to carry with them when they returned to their home community or to another country. Educational institutions today issue an official document detailing the courses taken together with dates, course names, credit hours, grades received and perhaps number in the class and student ranking. In addition, schools, colleges and universities sort students and award an additional credential to students who successfully complete one, of a number, of specified groups of courses, believed to be worthy of extra recognition.



A Qualification on the other hand, speaks to the issue of attitudes, skills and knowledge required to successfully perform in a role or at a task. They usually emerge because of issues of safety and consistency, and the mobility of our work force. Employment advertisements often identify both a credential, such as a high school diploma, and a set of qualifications usually including training and or expe rience.




The problem/conflict


Schools are teaching institutions; their role is to help learners learn. Each learner decides what he or she will learn, although others, including teachers, influence that decision.


Acquiring a credential is a sorting or ranking process; although the two functions are different, they appear closely linked and frequently one is thought to be the other. h functions is, to a large degree, provided or driven by educational institutions often works to the individual’s disadvantage in terms of both education and certification. Even where separate bodies exist for their issuance, confusion remains. While separate certification bodies exist, they appear to place undue emphasis on results of study and too little emphasis on what has been learned outside the formal education system.


Unfortunately, over the years, the differences between credentials and qualifications has become blurred. Used interchangeably in media

Qualification authority



A qualifications authority is a body established under law and given the authority to assess and evaluate, evaluate and certify a person’s level of performance on a task or set of tasks, competencies, outcomes. Qualification authorities operate under a government legislation but often have their own governing structure



Concept


The idea behind this proposal is to establish an independent body with authority to assess and certify a person’s level of performance on a task or set of tasks such as found in an occupation or field.


A qualification authority would reduce if not eliminate the confusion faced by teachers between teaching and sorting, focus certification more clearly on performance that matters, support lifelong learning and self-directed study, clearly focus school evaluation on helping learners learn, and reduce major for distractions for teachers caused by operating the sorting process. It would, in effect, have two clients, the organizations desiring an assessment service and individuals wanting to confirm thelevel and suitability of their qualifications. A qualification authority would take its cues from the destination of the learner, focus on what a client can do and how well, examine each skills carried and assess their suitability also identify gaps for the individual would not simply administer . A qualification authority could also play a useful role in helping people identify gaps in their preparation for a given field of work.

Atlantic region - AQUA

Characteristics


AQUA will:


  1. Be an independent organization with its own governing structure

  2. Operate on a fee for service basis

  3. Accept clients on direct application or by referral

  4. Issue certificates based on demonstrated performance

  5. On request, provide applicants with information on gaps to be filled to meet certification requirements.

  6. Make extensive use of advisory/working panels.


Benefits/Advantages


It is anticipated that the availability of AQUA will:


  1. Remove barriers to certification encountered in the current approach

  2. Focus certification more clearly on performance

  3. Encourage more self study

  4. Allow educational providers to focus on the teaching function

  5. Allow a certification body to focus on certification without regard to teaching.

  6. Removes length of learning time as a component of certification

  7. Provide an avenue to certification for self directed learners
