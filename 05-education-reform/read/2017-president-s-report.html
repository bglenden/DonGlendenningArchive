Glendenning's 2017 president's report highlighting expanded organizational activities and impact. The organization awarded $2,500 to STEAM PEI for innovative math instruction combining science, technology, engineering, and mathematics education. Glendenning maintained ongoing education policy discussions with provincial government officials. The report documents participation in the Canadian Education Association's second regional workshop in Halifax, informal discussions addressing mathematics education challenges, and exploratory work on educational service gaps in Souris, PEI. The organization received a significant WISEAtlantic grant for the Project STEAM initiative. Glendenning's report demonstrates his focus on practical educational problems and collaborative problem-solving across government, nonprofit, and institutional sectors.

EDUCATION 20/20 INC.

President’s Report

For the Year ending December 31, 2017


I am pleased to present the following report of activities for the year ending December 31, 2017.


  • Newsletter: Open Education, a one-page electronic newsletter published monthly in both official languages; our thanks to Rachelle Bellaire for translation services until her relocation in September
  • Social media: Thanks to all but mostly to Tom White-Hassler
  • Web site: Thanks to Bill Whelan for leading us through the reconstruction of our website.
  • Education Award: $2,500 to STEAM PEI in recognition of their work in combining instruction and entertainment into a math magic session.
  • Liaison: 
  • Occasional informal meetings with Education Minister others interested and involved in education 
  • Christmas reception at Haviland Club for members of Education 20/20 network. Wednesday, December 13, 2017
  • Regional Workshop - participated in the second Regional workshop in Halifax hosted by the Canadian Education Association; CEA is trying to establish a network of educators across the country.
  • Mathematics: a series of informal discussions with an ad hoc group of Islanders interested in improving student transition from school to employment
  • Souris: exploration of educational services in Souris, PEI with a view to increasing educational opportunities, especially for post school residents.  
  • STEAM Grant: Received: a grant from WISEAtlantic for Project STEAM, a series of workshops on science, technology, engineering, and mathematics in Island schools. Project being carried out by STEAM PEI
  • Thanks for ongoing financial  support: Regis and Joan Duffy,  Provincial Credit Union, Roger Birt 
  • Thanks also to CFPEI for adopting us, Rachel Bellaire for translation, other donors both named and anonymous, Directors and volunteers.



Respectfully submitted

Don Glendenning, President
