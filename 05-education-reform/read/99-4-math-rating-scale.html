The refined and most complete version of the Citizen Math assessment framework. Don describes the community-led process: a skilled facilitator worked with islander panels to identify 74 practical math skills organized under six categories (whole numbers, measurement, fractions/decimals/percentages, spatial reasoning, patterns/relationships, statistical information). UNIVERSAL RATING SCALE

A few weeks ago, I circulated a Citizen Math Map; skills were identified by a panel of islanders with the help of a skilled facilitator. Panelists were asked about skills used in everyday life; the list reflects what they told us.  The list consists of some 74 skills under the following headings:

  • Estimate and Calculate with Whole Numbers
  • Use measurement
  • Use factions, Decimals, Percentages Rations and Rates
  • Use spatial reasoning
  • Recognize and use patterns and relationships
  • Interpret Statistical Information

In our review of the literature, we noted that rating scales generally were adopting criteria of a kind that applies to life generally. Rating systems also included self- rating as a first step. We convened   a second panel and they told us that they assess community skills based on: 

  • Accuracy or quality. 
  • Assistance needed 
  • Speed- time to complete task, 
  • consistency

Although still a work in progress, we decided to include it for your information.  We decided to weave these together into descriptions of six levels of performance. The results of our deliberation are shown on the right.
