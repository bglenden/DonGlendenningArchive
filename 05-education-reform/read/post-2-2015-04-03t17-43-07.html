Reflective blog post comparing the historical shift in education's role from providing answers to defining questions. Don explores how industrial-era grandfathers wanted stable employment for their children, while modern parents seek knowledge-industry careers requiring curious questioners. He proposes examining what people actually need in terms of education in contemporary society.

You uncover an interesting switch in the role of education from that of providing the answer to that of defining the question.  Presumably that results, at least in part, from the vast storehouse  of information available at out fingertips.  That certainly applies to the cognitive domain but how about the support for affective and psychomotor domains?   

I was also struck by your comment that our grandfathers wanted their offspring to have a lifetime of work in the industrialized economy, and the workplace found  questions and questioners  “threatening and irritating”.  It seems that the reverse is true today, fathers and grandfathers want their children to have a lifetime of work  in the knowledge industry, a work place that wants people who are  questioners and questioning.  it’s education that finds questioners “threatening and irritating.

So, in terms of education, the firstm question that I would like to explore is “What do people need in the way of education?
