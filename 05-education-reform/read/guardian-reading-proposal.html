This August 16, 2012 proposal is directed to The Guardian newspaper, soliciting a $4,000 grant for Education First Canada's Reading Engagement Project. The organization, newly incorporated as a non-aligned national body focused on quality education, proposes workshops on reading-related matters including outcomes, competencies, learning materials, and teaching strategies. Don requests funding for research assistance ($2,500) and facility rental for ten meetings ($1,500). The proposal demonstrates Don's early education reform efforts before Education 20/20 Inc.'s formation and his strategic focus on reading as the foundational literacy skill. The letter references endorsement by the Community Foundation of PEI, showing early partnership development.

Description of how project relates to education and literacy:


Reading is the basic skill of literacy and certainly the “golden skill” of education.   In our discussions about the improvement of public education, reading achievement/performance topped the list of concerns: Education First Canada decided, therefore, to make reading a priority.  We believe that improving reading requires the involvement of all stakeholders.  We propose, therefore, to convene a number of workshops focused on reading-related matters such as outcomes/competencies, learning materials, performance criteria, teaching strategies, rating scales and reporting.  Bringing stake holders and providers together on neutral ground is a natural role for an unaligned organization such as Education First Canada.   


While we could use funds for many useful purposes, the following are the most pressing for Education First Canada at this time:


  • While the participants themselves will bring much to discussions, there will also be a need to gather, copy, distribute data and information as background and support.  We would like, therefore, to contract with someone or ones who, under direction, will search for information between meetings.  My own reading, for instance, has turned up statistics on literacy but few on reading, little in the way of competencies/outcomes, competency based rating scales, performance levels, benchmarks, or costs.  The same could be said for information about best teaching practices , weaknesses in our current approach and others.    Perhaps we can find a retiree, a teacher, or even a student teacher in training to help.  We are projecting a cost for this item of $2,500.


  • While we are grateful to the organizations that have provided meeting space either free or at a minimum charge, the arrangement does not allow us to book space with either certainty or continuity; booking a room for group meetings and breakouts, projection capability, flip charts, etc.  Securing facilities, it seems, requires more time than preparing the program; meetings also often require photocopying, supplies and other support.   We would like, for example, to be able to book a room every two weeks between now and Christmas, a room that could accommodate 25-30 people.  Although we want to engage people across the Island, at the moment, we are holding most of our discussions in the Charlottetown area; convenience to the downtown area and parking are important considerations.  The estimated cost of facilities for ten meetings, including tax, is $1,500.


Please provide a breakdown of how the funding will be used:


1 Admin/research assistance to gather, copy and distribute  information, as required: $2,500

2 Room and support facilities for 10 half day meetings plus tax at $150: $1,500

Total: $4,000
