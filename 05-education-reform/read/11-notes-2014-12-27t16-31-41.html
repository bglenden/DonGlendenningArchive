EDUCATION 20/20 INC.

July 15, 2014

Mathematics

NOTE: The notes that follow arise from an exploration of math education by Education 20/20 to identify what steps, if any that we can take to improve the level of math education of Island youth

BACKGROUND


It is widely recognized that education is a key factor in determining one’s quality of life; in the same way, the collective education of our citizens will determine to a significant degree the future of our community and country. The most frequently mentioned concern among Islanders including employers, is the low level of basic skills, especially mathematics, communication and personal management skills

.

Concerns among Islanders about the quality of public education have been widely reported:

  • PISA results

  • CFPEI survey

  • Charlottetown Area Chamber report

  • Home and School survey

  • Education 20/20 workshops on preparedness


The most frequently mentioned concern among Islanders, and especially among employers, is the low level of basic skills, especially mathematics, communication and personal management skills.


Goals of mathematics education are to prepare students to (PEI Education Website MAT613A)

  • use mathematics confidently to solve problems;

  • communicate and reason mathematically;

  • appreciate and value mathematics;

  • make connections between mathematics and its applications;

  • commit themselves to lifelong learning;

  • become mathematically literate adults, using mathematics to contribute to society.


  • School transcripts do not describe skills in sufficient detail for employers to understand skill levels of new employees.

  • We have not been able to locate a comprehensive list of math outcomes or competencies.

  • No evidence of a quality control procedures

  • Each grade level appears to have separate outcomes; no evidence of spiralling

Possible 20/20 actions:

  • Meet with some math consultants and teachers

  • Identify master list of math outcomes or competencies

  • Identify qualification levels

  • Find or develop assessment instruments and procedures

  • Provide an external qualification mechanism (model after Canadian Conservatory of Music)

  • Math Round table

  • Other


TOWES – (Test of Workplace Essential Skills)

  • Developed at Bow Valley College, Calgary, Alberta

  • Offers valid, reliable and effective assessments, curriculum and training support for organizations and individuals

  • Nine Essential Skills: Reading Text, Document Use, Numeracy, Writing, Thinking Skills, Oral Communication, Continuous Learning, Working With Others, and Computer Use. TOWES assesses the first three skills, which are often referred to as adult literacy skills.

  • Services include: placement, measuring learning gains and determining work readiness in college or institutional training programs.

  • Holland College is a distributor; depending on our volume, we could either contract with the College or if numbers warranted, contract directly with TOWES in Calgary.

Oregon Essential Skills

  • To earn a high school diploma, students need to complete the credit requirements, demonstrate proficiency in essential skills, and meet the personalized learning requirements.
