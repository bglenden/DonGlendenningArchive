FROM SCHOOL TO EDUCATION CENTRE


The essence of education is helping Islanders gain or enhance the knowledge, skills and attitudes needed to thrive on the road of life. In support of education, Provincial governments provide a number of services such as information, counselling, funding, human resources, products and technology and quality assurance.


Schools, colleges and universities are purpose- built facilities where services can be accessed by potential learners. Delivering all educational services, even just to the young, through schools is certainly administratively convenient but also leaves some significant gaps.


Some schools, have become full services schools delivering instructional services to other segments of the population at the end of the day and on weekends. (In the US, such schools are known as community schools and from anything I’ve read, appear to be quite successful.)


There is a trend, however, is for communities to develop, not just school hubs, not even education hubs, but true community hubs where community members meet for all kinds of community purposes. Community hubs in Calgary are partnership between the United way and the City and ensure that “every Calgarian :can easily access the right supports services and resources.”


Hubs house service and resources needed by a community but do not deliver them. The nature of education makes it a natural partner,
