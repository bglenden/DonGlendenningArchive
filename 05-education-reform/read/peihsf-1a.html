Dated November 1, 2013, this letter invites Ms. Pam Montgomery (President of PEI Home and School Federation) and her organization's leadership to a discussion on October 26, 2013 (note: date discrepancy suggests draft revisions). Don introduces Education 20/20 as a new, independent, broad-based organization committed to ensuring quality education for every Canadian. He highlights the Home and School Federation's long history of education advocacy and expresses interest in discussing the role of parents in education and what support would be most helpful to them. The letter projects warmth and genuine interest in partnership with this established community organization.

November 1, 2013

Ms. Pam Montgomery, President

PEI Home and School Federation

PO Box 1012

Charlottetown, PE C1A 7M4


Dear. Ms. Montgomery:


On behalf of Education 20/20 Inc., I am pleased invite you, your Directors and Executive Director to join us on October 26, 2013 for a discussion about education.


As background, Education 20/20 Inc. is a new and independent organization in Prince Edward Island with an interest ensuring a quality education for every Canadian. We believe education to be important to our individual and collective futures. It is also our belief that providing a quality education requires the combined efforts of the whole community. Enclosed is a copy of our Vision statement, our most recent newsletter and a list of current Directors.


Home and School Associations have a long history both in PEI and across the country of working for the improvement of education. We would be interested, therefore, meeting both to gather information and share views about future needs.


While we are open to discussions on any and all aspects of education, we would be especially interested in discussing the role of parents in the education process, and even in identifying kinds of support that would be most helpful to them.


We look forward to meeting you on November 26 at 4:30 pm in Room 125 at Royalty Centre, 40 Enman Crescent, Charlottetown.


Sincerely


Don Glendenning

President
