Don outlines five indicators of genuine educational progress: updated education philosophy clarifying purpose and goals, community members on curriculum committees, department reorganization based on functional analysis, replacing "grade" terminology with achievement-level language, and restoring high school exemptions. He uses the metaphor of spring robins—one or two small signs suggest change, three or four indicate a new season—emphasizing that transformative improvement requires coherent, multi-faceted reform.
Education Philosophy & Reform


March 29, 2016


Editor,

Charlottetown Guardian

Charlottetown, PEI


Dear Sir: 

Signs of an educational spring


I’ve been asked what I am looking for in the way of signs that education is improving; I know that the government is planning some major organizational changes but here are five simple, inexpensive and non-disruptive changes that would indicate a change of thinking:

An updated statement of philosophy of education to clarify the focus especially around purpose and goals.

The appointment of community members to curriculum committees. 

A reorganization of the Department based on a functional analysis. 

Finding a word, other than grade, to describe levels of student achievement.

Return of high school exemptions.


Any one of the above would be like the first robin in spring; three or four would indicate that a new season has arrived.


Sincerely,


Don Glendenning

A long-time student of education
