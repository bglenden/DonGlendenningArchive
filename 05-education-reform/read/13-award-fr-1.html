The March 3, 2014 thirteenth issue, edited by Don Glendenning, announces a new $2,500 Education Award offered by Education 20/20 Inc. The award recognizes schools and learning centers demonstrating high engagement in daily activities across five domains: student-to-student engagement, student-to-teacher engagement, student-to-learning-materials engagement, student-to-community partnerships, and parental involvement. Selection criteria focus on the school's direct control areas: family-community communication respecting cultural values, community-based events, and parent/community curriculum development involvement. The announcement appears in both French and English, reflecting Education 20/20's bilingual commitment. Award decisions are made solely on nomination information submitted, with an Awards Committee reviewing nominations and recommending recipients for announcement in early May. The award demonstrates Don's commitment to recognizing and incentivizing schools that prioritize meaningful engagement over standardized metrics.

Prix de l’Éducation - $2,500


Le comité de sélection accepte présentement des mises en candidatures pour un nouveau prix de l'éducation offerte par Éducation 20/20 inc. L'objectif de ce prix est de reconnaître les écoles et les centres de la petite enfance qui exercent un haut niveau d'engagement dans les activités journalières.


Les cinq domaines d’engagement suivants sont éligibles:

  • engagement des enfants et élèves à l’égard de leurs amis de classe,

  • engagement des enfants et élèves à l’égard des éducateurs,

  • engagement des enfants et élèves aux outils d’apprentissage,

  • partenariats entre enfants et élèves et la communauté, et

  • participation parentale.

Critères

L’objectif du prix est de reconnaître les façons de faire quotidiennes qui démontrent l’engagement dans les domaines que contrôle l’école ou le centre de la petite enfance comme :

  • communication avec la famille dans le respect de la culture et de la communauté

  • activités au sein de la communauté

  • participation des parents et de la communauté dans l’élaboration et la planification de programmes d’études


Processus de mise en candidature

L’on peut mettre en nomination une école au centre de la petite enfance. Le site web, www.education2020.ca, présente de plus amples informations sur le prix ainsi que sur le processus de mise en candidature. La prise de décisions se fera seulement en fonction de l’information accompagnant la mise en candidature. Le comité annoncera le gagnant tôt au mois de mai 2014.


Education Award - $2,500


Nominations are now being received for a new Education Award offered by Education 20/20 Inc. The purpose of this award is to recognize schools and learning centres that demonstrate a high level of engagement in their day to day activities.


The five following areas of engagement will be considered:

  • student to student engagement,

  • student to teacher engagement,

  • student to learning materials,

  • student to community partnerships, and

  • parental involvement in schools.

Criteria

The focus of the award is on engagement in the daily operation of the school and only those items over which the school has substantial control such as:

  • communication with family, culture, community

  • frequency of community based events

  • parent/community involvement in curriculum development and planning


Nomination process

Readers are invited to nominate a school or learning centre. Further information on the award and the nomination process are available on our website: www.education2020.ca Decisions will be made solely on the basis of information presented. An Awards Committee will review nominations and recommend a recipient;. Award will be given early May of 2014.
