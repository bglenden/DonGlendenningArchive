Don Glendenning's opening and closing remarks for the Wellington workshop, clearly articulating Education 20/20's mission and philosophy. Opening introduces the organization's founding by learner-centered educators with a vision of quality education for all Canadians, names six directors including Don, and outlines key beliefs about education's role in individual and community development. Closing remarks emphasize continuing the conversation on learner engagement and direct participants to start locally. Lists Education 20/20 board: Alice Taylor, Brenda Goodine, Bill Whelan, Owen Parkhouse, Tanya Craig, and Don Glendenning.

January 20, 2014

EDUCATION 20/20 Inc.

Introductory Notes



Introduction

Welcome


What is Education 20/20:

  • Small group came together who share interests and concerns
  • All learner centered
  • Formed Education 20/20
  • Independent and incorporated nationally
  • Vision- A Quality education for every Canadian-
  • Have a network, no members as such
  • Shouldn’t need a new organization every time there is a new interest
  • Fortunate to have support of CFPE and a small number of donors


Initial Focus

  • Learners and learning
  • Those served by education rather that those who provide it
  • Information, engagement and awards


Directors:

  • Alice Taylor
  • Brenda Goodine
  • Bill Whelan
  • Owen Parkhouse
  • Tanya Craig
  • Don Glendenning


Key beliefs:

  • Education Is important
  • Will determine to a large degree what we become as individuals
  • Will determine to a large degree what our community becomes
  • Is not limited to schooling
  • Requires all our efforts
  • Engagement and student achievement go hand in hand


Quality Education

  • The OECD, the home of PISA testing, tells us accountability, autonomy, and choice are strongly and positively associated with the level of student performance, on PISA results.  
  • UNICEF tells us to focus on content that is relevant, processes that are child centered, and outcomes that go beyond acquiring knowledge. 
  • Independent information
  • Sound management model


 Current focus

  • Start the conversation
  • Information – website and newsletter
  • Engagement – such as these discussions
  • Incentives and awards - $2,500 award to school who demonstrates a high level of engagement

So what next:

  • What next?
