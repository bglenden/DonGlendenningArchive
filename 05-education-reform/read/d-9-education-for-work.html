Education for workool business models

Mention of business models to educators usually brings the sharp response that schools are not businesses, however, a scan of the literature suggests otherwise. Even to mention the matter leads to the suspicion that one is suggesting private, for profit schools. Not so, at least not in my camp. According to Forbes, there are four components of a business model:


Value –goods and services ov value offered to either individuals or the community


Resources – that an organization assembles to develop and provide goods and services; schools are certainly resource rich with a highly educated workforce, modern facilities and significant advanced technology


Processes – that the organization goes through to deliver its services, include curriculum development, testing and evaluation and sophisticated teaching strategies


Revenue - gathered in support of its work, and certainly the past few years schools have gathered significant and increased financial resources even in the face of declining enrolment.


The educational model is certainly being tested at the college and university level through on-line-courses; don’t be surprised at discovering similar tests of public schools as well.

This paper encompasses a broad definition of quality and examines research related to learners, content, processes, environments and outcomes.
