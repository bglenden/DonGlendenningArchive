Premier meeting presenting Education 20/20's stakeholder research findings, identifying educational gaps and challenges, and offering recommendations for systemic improvement.

Meeting with Premier

Wednesday, February 17 at 10:00 A.M.


  • Introduction
    • How Education 20/20 started, who we are, what our mission is
    • Lack of community support groups
  • What Education 20/20 has been doing
    • Listening to Island education stakeholders
    • Facilitating presentations on Island education as well as education in general
    • Sponsoring, evaluating, and making Education Excellence awards
    • Gatherings disparate groups in discussion
  • What we have learned/issues

Organizational matters

    • PEI has the know-how to make the changes
    • There is a lot of general concern about education and the future of the Island
    • Engagement especially at the Department level
    • Some education stakeholders don’t feel safe talking about their thoughts on education
    • School autonomy and accountability
    • The interacting factors that determine education success are numerous and complex, e.g., home life
    • Upward delegation of decision making


Professional

    • Information
    • There is a perception of a “basic skills gap”, especially in math
    • Emphasis on schooling rather than education
    • Conflict of role when it comes to assessment and grading
  • Ideas and Suggestions
    • Exemptions and challenge for credit
    • To get a pulse reading of Islander feelings about the current state of education, there should be an Island-wide community needs assessment
    • Provide a math tutoring/remediation system that can assess, tutor, and monitor students in need
    • Provide computer software coding program
  • How can we help
