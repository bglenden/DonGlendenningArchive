Don proposes creating stand-alone educational organizations modeled on independent health advocacy groups (Heart and Stroke Foundation, Diabetes Association). He specifically advocates for a reading improvement association operating independently from schools but in collaboration with them. Such organizations could provide public awareness, early detection support, research promotion, service lobbying, information clearinghouses, and policy influence. Open membership would include teachers, parents, employers, and community members. Don envisions these as complementing institutional support, extending reach beyond schools to libraries, workplaces, and homes.
Education Philosophy & Reform

June 22, 2011


Editor

Charlottetown Guardian

Charlottetown, PEI


Dear Editor:

Another Voice in Education


The field of health has many voices. Some are associated with hospitals or other health care

institutions; others are, what I call, stand-alone organizations that, while working with

institutions, have a broader agenda. ln the latter category, I think of the Heart and Stroke

Foundation, Canadian Diabetes Association, Canadian Cancer Society, and many others. The

latter provide information and support but are not focused on support for people in the

hospital. Educational organizations, on the other hand, operate in support of schools, The PEI

Home and School Association being the best example.


Both kinds of organizations are needed but, at least in education, I like to see more of the stand-

alone variety. l'd start with an association focused on the improvement of reading, wherever it

occurs, in schools, libraries, workplace or home. One could also make the case for citizenship

and consumers.


Operating like those in the health field, such an organization could:

Support the efforts of others working in the field

Help raise public awareness of the importance of reading proficiency

Encourage early detection of reading difficulties

Stimulate and promote research

Lobby for special services

Act as a clearing house for information on the improvement of reading skills, and 

lnfluence public policy in support of improved reading


Membership should be open to anyone with an interest in improving the level of reading among

lslanders of all ages and include teachers, parents, employers and others. Although we need

discussions at the local level, a provincial umbrella organization may well be required to provide

support. i


I believe that free-standing and unaligned health organizations make significant contributions to

our health; l'd like to see similar organizations in support of education. Drop a note at standards2020@gmail.com and let me know what you think.


Sincerely




Don Glendenning
