Another iteration of the Windsor Foundation proposal, now framed as the "Preparedness Initiative." Education 20/20 Inc. addresses the foundational issue of low basic education skills in youth, evidenced by PISA results, CFPEI youth surveys, and presentations to the PEI Legislative Assembly. Proposes engaging stakeholders to narrow the preparedness gap for post-secondary study and employment. Identifies three core problems: educational silos (separate systems with poor coordination), curriculum alignment issues, and system inflexibility. Proposes establishing common curriculum maps and reducing inter-institutional fragmentation. Features include creating shared learning maps, single analysis with multiple profiles, separating teaching from assessment functions, and fostering collaborative teacher teams. DACUM methodology and Bloom's taxonomy referenced throughout. - **Key People**: Donald Glendenning, steering committee members - **Key Organizations**: Education 20/20 Inc., Windsor Foundation, PEI Department of Education, Holland College, Community Foundation of PEI - **Tags**: PROFESSIONAL, EDUCATION, CORRESPONDENCE, PROPOSAL - **Eulogy Relevance**: MEDIUM - Demonstrates Glendenning's persistent focus on education quality and systemic improvement in PEI.

INTRODUCTION


Mission/purpose of organization

Education 20/20 Inc. is federally incorporated as an independent, broad based, national, not-for-profit education organization, located in Charlottetown, PEI and is governed by a volunteer Board of Directors.  Our vision is a quality education for every Canadian and mission is to support and strengthen a culture of learning for all Canadians; Education 20/20 Inc. is unique among educational organizations in Canada in that it is unaligned with any educational institutions, grade level, subject field or cause.   The organization operates with ten Directors, a network of some fifty people, and a mailing list of about 250; an application for charitable status has been filed with Canada Revenue Agency; in the meantime, The Community Foundation of Prince Edward Island accepts donations and pays invoices on our behalf. Attached please find a list of activities carried out to date, activities planned for the coming year and a brief biography of our Directors.  Although most Directors have participated in externally funded projects in their other roles, this is the first such project for Education 20/20 Inc. and certainly our first request for funding to the Windsor Foundation.


Issue being addressed

 The proposal addresses one of the major issues of the day in education, namely the low level of basic education skills demonstrated by young people, including high school graduates.  As evidence I refer to PISA test results for Prince Edward Island; a 2013 survey of issues of Island youth conducted by the Community Foundation; a presentation to a Standing Committee of the PEI Legislative Assembly by the Charlottetown Area chamber of Commerce; How Canada Performs, a Provincial and Territorial Ranking, Education and Training; Education and Skills, and a series of workshops on Preparedness held throughout PEI by Education 20/20 Inc.  The PEI Department of Education has not taken exception to the findings nor, to the best of our knowledge, assumed a leadership role that includes corrective action.  Based on our consultations, a pilot in the area of math has the most likely chance of success.


Proposal 

Engage stakeholders in narrowing the gap that young Islanders are experiencing on the way to further study and employment. The attached “one-pager” describes how we arrived at this decision.

Relationship to Foundation Priorities

The Windsor Foundation has two major areas of interest

  • Disadvantaged or vulnerable youth: These are tough times for young people in transition from public school to further education and to work.  While there are many causes, this proposal addresses the issue of basic skills, those skills needed to gain entry to the next phase of life and,  without which many are branded as failures.  We are aware that sometimes this may be the result of poor decisions by  students  themselves; on the other hand, we are also aware that the gap may from incorrect information, biased counselling, streaming, out dated course content, poor teaching, inflexible scheduling, lack of encouragement and mentoring, and the list goes on. We believe that our proposal will help.
  • Higher education initiatives: Although not as well  documented, we’ve also been made aware  of the many young people now in college and university who struggle with their studies because of low level of basic skills and I have actually visited them in their make-up classes at Holland College and other adult upgrading programs scattered throughout PEI.  Although we do not have the actual figures, we know that the duplication of services is a strain on taxpayer’s dollars.

STEPS/ACTIVITIESTIMELINE:

This proposal is focused on confirm the nature and extent of the education gap as perceived and experienced by users of basic education services, namely students, employers, postsecondary institutions and the community at large.  Steps include:

  • Establish a steering committee
  • Searching the literature to gather resource materials relevant to the project
  • Fine tuning the analysis, based on the DACUM model. 
  • Prepare a criterion reference rating scale reflective of Bloom’s Taxonomy 
  • Identify profiles of math skills reflective of gap - citizenship, work entry and entry into postsecondary institutions, profiles on a single analysis.  
  • Establish a benchmark for each profile
