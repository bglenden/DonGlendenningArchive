Media release by Don Glendenning critiquing time-based school delivery models and advocating for developmental, outcomes-based education. Argues that schools' current system is ineffective because it assumes students at the same grade level have uniform progress, and proposes using learning paths and technology to provide individualized support based on student achievement of specific outcomes rather than seat time.

Time to change the school delivery model


I’m told that one of the early kings of England went crazy trying to adjust palace clocks so all would chime at the same time; in a similar way, teachers in the public school system are burning out under a system that pretends that students at any grade level at the same level in the subject being taught. It is just not trueworking.


“It is just not true,“ says Don Glendenning, President of Education 20/20 Inc., “schools impose a time based delivery model on a process that is developmental.” Educators report on outcomes covered” says Glendenning. “is the level on each of the outcomes reached by their sons and daughter – and the two are quite different.” If students have a copy of outcomes for each course they can monitor their progress on a day to day basis


To illustrate the change in approach, Education 20/20 demonstrated a “learning path” at its meeting on Thursday, May 14; with the aid of a flow-chart, one could visual students pursuing their individual interstss; thanks to technology, we are able to think differently about how and when to provide support.
