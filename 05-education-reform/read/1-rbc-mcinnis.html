Dear Ms. McInnis:

A better education for Islanders


For some time now, Education 20/20 Inc. has been s voice for change in education in PEI; we’ve been doing this through a bilingual monthly Newsletter, public presentations, letters to the Editor, occasional papers, an Educational Award and meetings with education decision-makers - and anyone else who would listen.


Some issues on which we are working:

  • A survey of continuing education needs in the Souris area

  • Transition in mathematics of young people through public school, to postsecondary education and to employment. We’ve made some progress and are now in the early stages of planning a pilot project.

  • The measurement of student performance - what is measured, when, why and how we measure, and who does the measuring. We are of the view that grading, (determining a person’s level in a subject), is regulatory function and not the role of schools. Establishing an independent assessment center would be a second stage in this project

  • Increased opportunities for part time, short course and often non-credit instruction to meet both personal and employment related interests.


We know that education can be improved but the system is unlikely to make the required changes on its own. This letter is an invitation to consider s sustaining grant, say $500 a year for the next three- year period in support better public education on PEI.


Thanks in advance for your consideration; I’ll be in touch early next week to answer any questions.


Sincerely


Don Glendenning

President
