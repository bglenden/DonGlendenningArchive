A blog post (Blog D-5) responding to "People for Education," an Ontario organization launching a Canada-wide initiative to define young Canadians' education rights. Don proposes that rather than mandating school attendance until age 18, provinces could require demonstrated education competencies (literacy Level 3, numeracy, self-care, interpersonal relations, work entry) before citizens can own cars or open bank accounts, applying the six life roles model.

:

A right to Education



People for Education, an Ontario education organization, is launching a Canada -wide initiative to define education rights of young Canadians; It’s a good question and one that should be addressed too Canadians of all ages. It will be interesting to see how the project unfolds.


As it stands now, young people are required to attend school until age 18; since going to school and getting aan education different, wouldn’t it be better to have young people demonstrate some level of education before they can own a car or open a bank account.


Citizens play a number of life roles; I like the Oregon classification - individual, family member, citizen, student, producer and consumer.  Seems to me that education should prepare people to cope, nay survive, in these roles in a democratic society. 


A Province may require, for instance that a citizen be able to read at Level 3 in order to purchase or drive a car.   The same could be said for other essential skills – literacy, numeracy, self care, interpersonal relations and work entry.


Simply requiring school attendance may not be enough
