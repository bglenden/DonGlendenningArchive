Titled "Making Learning Visible," this August 2024 letter addresses learner progress tracking. Don identifies best practices from education literature: report progress on each outcome/competency, engage learners in the rating process, discuss results with learners, and post results. He acknowledges this approach is time-consuming but argues it's valuable for the learning process. The letter reflects Don's advocacy for transparent, learner-engaged assessment systems that prioritize feedback and visible progress, aligning with his broader vision of learner-centered education reform.
Education Philosophy & Reform

August 17, 2024

Dear Editor:

Making Learning Visible

This article was triggered by an item I encountered in my morning reading about tracking learner progress. According to the article:

  • We should report progress on each outcome or competency
  • Engage learners in the rating process
  • The results should be discussed with the learner
  • Results should be posted

A time consuming but valuable part of the learning process.

Sincerely

Don Glendenning
