Letter from Don Glendenning as President of Education 20/20 Inc. to Paul Daley soliciting financial support for the organization's education reform initiatives, including workshops, newsletters, school awards, and research on reading performance. Requests annual donations to sustain momentum for systemic educational change.

101 Kent St., PO Box 624

Charlottetown

PEI C1A 1M0 Canada

Tel: 902-892-2060

May 3, 2013


Mr. Paul Daley, Vice President
APM Group

APM Place, 3 Lower Malpeque Road
Charlottetown, PE C1A 1R4



Dear Mr. Daley:



We spoke in February about education, the need for change, the newly created Education 20/20 Inc. and about plans for workshops, a newsletter, and an award to schools; since then workshops have been held, the school award is on schedule and a copy of our first newsletter was mailed to you a few days ago.


Recently a committee was put in place to organize additional workshops in a number of Island communities, finishing touches are being put on a website, additional Directors have been added and we are investigating the matter of poor reading performance. The attached invitation to a workshop on May 14 on “on-line course delivery” may also be of interest.


There is widespread agreement that education needs to change, but change does not come easy. Change in education will be driven primarily through the impact of information and public support; your financial support does both. While any and all financial support is appreciated, a donation of $1,000 a year for three years would provide some stability to the initiative.


As I mentioned earlier, contributions on our behalf should be directed to the Community Foundation of Prince Edward Island, 119 Queen Street, Charlottetown, PE C1A 4B3, who will issue charitable receipts. Please let me know if you would like further information about education on PEI or about activities of Education 20/20.


Together, we can make a difference.

Sincerely



Don Glendenning

President
