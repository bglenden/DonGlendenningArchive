This December 17, 2012 letter thanks Regis Duffy for supporting Education 20/20 Inc.'s education reform efforts. Don outlines the organization's progress: successful small-group meetings, federal incorporation, upcoming charitable status, and multiple initiatives including early childhood development workshops, school-community engagement assessment, an education excellence award, dialogue with educators on reading skills, and plans to publish educational materials. The letter demonstrates that Education 20/20 had moved from exploratory phase to operational organization with concrete programs. Duffy is being solicited for ongoing financial support, and the letter invites him to an educational workshop in Stratford. This shows Don's fundraising and donor cultivation skills.

Dear Regis:


This note comes to thank you and Joan for your support in our efforts to bring about change in our public education system. 


As you know, we’ve held a dozen or more meetings with small groups on a variety of to explore mutual concerns and interests.   As a result, we now have a cadre of people who share our philosophy, who understand the need for change and are willing to help make change happen.  We are also federally incorporated as Education 20/20 Inc., are working on a logo and website, and are now seeking charitable status with Revenue Canada.  In addition, we:

  • Organized a workshop in Early Childhood Development during which a panel of front line practitioners, shared information and their experiences about approaches that work for young children; participants included other early childhood workers, parents, educators and members of the community at large.  We plans include workshops in other Island communities.
  • Are preparing to test of a new software package, developed in Canada, to assess the range and nature of school- community engagement..  This test is being undertaken because there is a direct relationship exists between student achievement and community engagement.   In early January you will receive an invitation to a workshop being convened for the purpose of identifying criteria for the award. 
  • Are launching  an award to an Island school that best exemplifies a high level of engagement in its every day school activities; criteria, policies and procedures are now being drafted.
  • Commenced dialogue with educators, especially decision makers, about educational issues, with reading skills of high school graduates being at the top of our list.
  • Intend to gather, produce and publish information of interest to the public at large.


We thank you for both your encouragement of our efforts and your financial support – and we’ll keep you posted on our progress. Let’s have coffee early in the New Year.


Enjoy a great family Christmas and warmest wishes for the New Year. 


Sincerely


Don Glendenning


PS:  Reserve January 30 from 4:00 to 6:00 pm for a workshop at the Stratford Town Hall; details will follow early in the new year
