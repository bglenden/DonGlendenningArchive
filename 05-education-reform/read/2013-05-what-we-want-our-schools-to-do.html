An unpublished piece responding to Walter Wilkins' question about school purpose. Don reframes the discussion by asking first "What kind of education do we want?" before defining school roles. He notes that much important education occurs outside schools and advocates for starting a community conversation. He organized a May 7 meeting at Sports PEI to discuss these fundamental questions with interested community members.
Education Philosophy & Reform

Submitted but not published


May 1, 2013


Editor, Charlottetown Guardian

Charlottetown, PEI


Dear Sir:

A good question

Walter Wilkins’ question (‘what do we want our schools to do? (’Walter Wilkins, Guardian of April 18, 2013) is a good place to begin.  Perhaps the first question should be “What kind of education we want for our young people? then we can identify the role of schools in providing it.  Schools are important but increasingly I read articles about the importance of the education we receive outside school.

Let’s start the discussion now. I’ve booked a place to meet - Room 125 at Sports PEI, Royalty Centre for 7:00 pm on Thursday, May 7.  Let’s meet and talk; other readers are invited to come along and join the conversation.

If you are interested but can’t be there, send me a note at  HYPERLINK "mailto:standards2020@gmail.com" standards2020@gmail.com 

See you on May 7

Don Glendenning

A long-time student of education
