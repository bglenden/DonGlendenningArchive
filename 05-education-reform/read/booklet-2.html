Comprehensive 2024 document (November 23, 2024 - possibly Don's final year of active work) presenting Don's framework for PEI education system design. Emphasizes conceptual clarity as fundamental to education planning and policy. Defines learners as individuals with diverse interests, experiences, and aspirations. Outlines typical education services (information, counselling, instruction, quality assurance, products/technology, funding, human resources), system standards (public administration, comprehensive, universal, portable, accessible), and organizational structure. Includes learning levels (Bloom's taxonomy), emerging changes (autonomy, profiles, tutors, detailed tracking), and recommended reading from OECD and World Bank. Documents organizational structures and extensive notes reflecting his continued intellectual engagement with education policy into his mid-90s.

Education is complex; information plays a key role. Learners have different interests, experiences, capabilities and needs; they also fill a variety of life roles.Those who teach, or otherwise serve learners, must be clear in their communication.

Conceptual clarity heightens our understanding of key words and concepts; lack of clarity is a significant issue for public ducation. Lack of clarity could signal conflicting ideas and levels of discomfort or disagreement within the education family; some say “clarity s related to self-confidence. Conceptual clarity is also a must in planning and assessing performance.



LEARNERS AND LEARNING


A learner is a person who for whatever reason is trying to bring about change in his or her life – trying to change an attitude, gain new knowledge or add to skills. But learners are neither empty jars or blank slates; each learner carries thee pieces of luggage to each learning situation:

  • Certain physical, mental and emotional conditions

  • Prior learning resulting from formal study, home circumstances, state of health, personal interests

  • Culture and learning style

  • Aspirations and imagined future

MEANING

Education has several meanings:

  1. A set of services put in place by a community to encourage and support the education of its citizens,

  2. A sector of the economy having to do with the, promotion supply and regulation of education services,

  3. A process of creating experiences which result in others learning skills, knowledge, attitudes and other attributes, and

  4. The state or level of a person’s ability to deal with daily living











KEY CONCEPTS

  2. Only a learner can learn

  3. Learning may occur in a group setting and sometimes requires other people as part of the process

  4. Learning results in a change of behaviour

  5. Everyone can learn the skills, knowledge attitudes and other attributes needed by citizens in a democracy

TYPICAL EDUCATION SERVICES


As identified by the Business Roundtable:

  1. Information - recent and independent information needed in the making of decisions about their own education or the education of others

  2. Counselling – help to people in making decision about their education interests and needs.

  3. Instruction - The provision of focused help to others in their learning endeavors including diagnosis, counselling, presentations, practice and access generally to a range of other resources

  4. Quality assurance - a system that assures the community that their learning experiences, including experiences with service providers, meet community standards.

  5. Products and Technology - Access by citizens to educational materials, technology and other resources to help learners develop attitudes skills, knowledge and other at-tributes needed to meet their needs and objectives.

  6. Funding -funding to a level and in a manner that is equitable for all Islanders.

  7. Human resources - Teachers, administrators, counsellors, advisors. assistants, leaning managers and others with the knowledge and experience needed to help others learn.
