Don articulates the broader education clarity problem affecting not just PEI but internationally. He identifies multiple sources of confusion: rarity of definitions in government literature, lack of public discussion, unclear visibility, replacement of "education" with "school" in the Education Act, absence of education/school agendas, interchangeable terminology use, international research citations (World Bank, OECD, Montreal Economic Institute), absence of national standards, and lack of community information. He emphasizes education's importance and complexity, calling for a "taxonomy of key education terms" to provide helpful clarity for public understanding.

Dear Editor:

A Puzzler

Education has a clarity problem and I am nt limiting that to PEI.  We confuse I with going to school. Having a credential and being learned. I raise the issue after searching school websites, Department literature, CAMET, CMHEC, CEA and others

Among my confusions:

  • The rarity of finding a definition in the government education literature.
  • The lack of public discussion 0n education
  • Visibility?
  • The replacement off education with “school” in a recent revision of the Education Act.
  • The absence of “education” on “school” agendas
  • The tendency to use “education” and “school” interchangeably.
  • References to the subject in documents from the World BANK, OECD and MEI
  • Absence of national education standards
  • Jmportance o the subject
  • Need to kep the community informed
  • Lack of community information 

Education is a large and complex operation; a taxonomy of key education terms would  be helpful.

Sincerely

Don Glendenning
