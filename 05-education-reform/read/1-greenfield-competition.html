Proposal for "Greenfield Competition" to stimulate creativity and innovation among educators through awards and recognition. Outlines benefits of incentivizing innovation in education, proposes competitions for educators and university students in education faculties to design ideal schools. Includes annual awards for education journalists and competitions spanning ECD (early childhood development), elementary, and secondary levels. Proposes bilingual judges including architects, curriculum specialists, and public members. Suggests national scope with provincial, regional, national and international recognition, monetary awards, and media partners (CBC, Guardian, Irvings). - **Key People**: None specifically named - **Key Organizations**: CBC, Guardian (newspaper), Irvings - **Tags**: PROFESSIONAL, EDUCATION, COMMUNITY - **Eulogy Relevance**: MEDIUM - Shows Don's commitment to innovation and public recognition in education

GREENFIELD Competition

Purpose: Stimulate creativity and innovative thinking among educators through the use of
promotions and awards. 

Background: 

Every sector can benefit from innovation 

Offering incentives and awards for innovative practices is widely used in many fields other than
education 

Innovative ideas can come from many sources 

Desire for change in education 

Benefits: 

Generates recognition for the sector and the community 

Increases public engagement 

Stimulates change 

Brings cash to community efforts 

Helps build communities 

Has broad application to the field 

Examples: 

A community comes together to plan its education needs: one level or all levels; Shirlene and
West Prince; 

Award for university students in faculties of education to design their ideal school; private,
excluding residence; provincial, regional and national; 

Annual award to journalist for articles on education 

Proposal:  To stimulate innovation in education through: 




Soliciting proposals 

Offering awards 

Organizing events 

Providing publicity 

Criteria: 

Sound educational principles 

Meet or exceed federal and provincial statutes 

Not bound by contractual or labour agreements 

National in scope 

Individual or team 

Judges: 

Bilingual 

Architect, curriculum, specialist, public members, HRD specialist 

• 

Award: 

Local, regional and national 

Public presentation 

Winners and runners up 

Different focus each year: ECD, elementary, secondary, etc; provincial, regional, national and
international recognition; 

Monetary award 

Recognition for the host university 

Options: 

Private school, Grades 9-2. Provincial regulations for private schools apply, ignore need for
residen ces, , or Grades 1-12 

Sponsors:CBC, Guardian, Irvings, 

Elementary, secondary, post secondary and adult
