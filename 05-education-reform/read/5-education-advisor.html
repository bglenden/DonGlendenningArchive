This document proposes establishing community-based educational advisory services for Islanders outside the formal school system. Don argues that while schools employ guidance counselors for enrolled students, there is no equivalent "family education advisor" to assist people of all ages in making educational decisions. He draws parallels to family doctors in healthcare and lawyers in legal matters, proposing an independent professional advisor who would provide personalized services including information, counseling, assessment, and referral. The advisor would serve entire communities rather than school districts and operate from accessible locations like libraries or municipal offices. The proposal emphasizes the complexity of education in the modern world and the critical need for individualized support.
