Memo to file discussing proposed amendments to Education 20/20's Articles of Incorporation, drafted by or reflecting input from Don Glendenning. Proposes changes to mission statement from "A Quality Education for Every Canadian" to "Learning to live—living to learn," refines membership definitions emphasizing research and advocacy, and clarifies scope of services. Reveals Don's thoughtful leadership approach to organizational governance and educational philosophy.

EDUCATION 20/20 INC.

Date: August 18, 2014

To: File

Subject: Possible Amendments to our Act of Incorporation


  • Some directors suggested a change from “A Quality Education for Every Canadian” Learning to live-living to learn
  • Membership:
  • We need a statement as to what it means to be a member of Education 20/20 Inc.  Presumably a member shares our interest in providing a quality education for every Canadian; presumably we should provide for a membership fee even if we  set it at zero.  Are there other criteria? We’ve also spoken of our network which could be as simply anyone who is on our mailing list.  What do you think? 
  • Here’s a stab at a draft membership statement:  “Members of Education 20/20 translate their earnest interest in Canadian education into focused studying, sharing, monitoring, encouraging of the continuous improvement of education policy and practice.”
  • Here is another stab building on Tom’s: “Members of Education 20/20 are individuals who value a quality education for every Canadian and, through research, evaluation and advocacy, encourage continuous improvements in education policy and practice.” 
  • Members of Education 20/20 Inc. are individuals who value a quality education and encourage continuing evidence- based improvement.

 

  • Sub-groups:
  • Geographic groups: Regional, provincial, local ?
  • Interest groups:  math, essential skills, community educators (JA, Cadets, 4H)
  • Students:  newcomers, math 
  • I am ambivalent about the geographic groups; I would add information technology to #2; I would collapse 3 and 4 into “ad hoc groups as interest suggests
  • Under the group section, you may want to consider adding official languages in education groups (such as Canadian Parents For French and the francophone parents group) 
  • Interest groups as interests suggest such as languages, technology, community educators, newcomers to a community


  • Services we will not provide:
  • When asked in our original submission to name an activity that we would not be take on, I said “not engage in instruction except for demonstration projects”;  perhaps we should change that.   Your thoughts?
  • I think so, as it might constrain us if we wanted to sponsor workshops or a model course.
  • I can suggest we add that we “will not provide any direct front line educational services that are the responsibility of provincial governments.” 
  • We will not provide normal front line instructional services
