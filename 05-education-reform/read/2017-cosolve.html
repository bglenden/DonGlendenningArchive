For release: Immediate

Date: August 23, 2017


Education Award announced


coSolved Consulting was named this year’s winner of an Excellence in Education Award offered by Education 20/20 Inc. The award recognizes combining instruction and entertainment into a “Math Magic” session. The show is designed to reduce math anxiety among students and parents alike.


Events will showcase some of the wonders of mathematics outside the school curriculum, through comedy, mathematical magic, applied geometry and physics, fun math facts, and some awe-inspiring real examples of math in action. The presentations will revolve around basket balls, Rubik’s Cube, a mobius strip and knot theorists untangling DNA.” These will be audience participation events; “Math Magic” events will be held at the Confederation Centre Public Library in Charlottetown and at the Summerside Library during “global math week”..


The show, intended to change the image of math in the minds of young people, is being developed by Amber Jadis, a practicing Engineer and STEAM advocate and Nellie Keating entertainer and co-founder Halifax Circus School in consultation with the School of Mathematical Science at UPEI and the Department of Education.


In announcing the Award, Dr. Glendenning, President of Education 20/20 thanked Dr. Regis and Joan Duffy for their continuing interest in and support for education in PEI. This award is presented annually by Education 20/20 to recognize excellence in some element of education, this time to a community group for promoting the study of math. Past awards by Education 20/20 Inc focused on engagement and technology.
