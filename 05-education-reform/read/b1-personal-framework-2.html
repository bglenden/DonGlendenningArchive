Comprehensive personal educational philosophy framework articulating definitions, operating beliefs, barriers, and change recommendations for PEI education system. Identifies fundamental organizational problems including lack of written philosophy, school system masquerading as education system, poor decision-making structures, and politicization. Proposes five major recommendations: develop compelling vision, implement learner-centered service model, establish government leadership role, delegate instruction provision, and create independent qualification body to certify achievement levels.

A Personal Framework for change-3

(An evolving draft)



Definitions:

  • Learners – a person engaged in changing behaviour
  • Education – any experience that has a formative effect on the way we feel, think or act
  • Schooling – learning under the direction of teachers
  • Purpose of education – to help people gain the attitudes, skills, knowledge of citizenship wanted or  needed in later life
  • Role of schools – to help people develop the skills of citizenship and to ensure that our community has the skills needed to grow and prosper 
  • Learning – changing the way we think, feel or act
  • Teaching – helping people change the way they think, feel or act
  • Education system – a set of components, relationships and services put in place by a community to encourage and support the education of its citizens
  • A school system - institutions, programs and services provided and encouraged by or with the approval of a community through its government to provide formal education for its residents


Operating beliefs - education:

  • Each person carries his/her own combination of traits based on genetics, previous experiences and aspirations. 
  • We are educated whether or not we go to school
  • The home is the major contributor to one’s education
  • Parents want their children to be educated not just schooled; 
  • Each person is responsible for  his/her own education 
  • Educating is a developmental activity.
  • Most educators view education through a school lens 
  • The Importance of institutions (schools, colleges and universities) in acquiring an education is on the decline 
  • There are no apparent benefits for the education system to do a better job or students to reach higher standards or reach them earlier than planned 
  • Awarding credentials and qualifications is not an educational function; in fact, for institutions to do so creates a conflict of interest
  • Establishing levels of achievement is not a school function

A Call- implementation


1) Recommendation - Develop a “clear and  compelling” vision for education on PEI.


Rationale. 

  • Comprehensive statement does not currently exist
  • Currently have a compliance model
  • Current philosophy applies only to schools
  • One size fits all
  • Adults and preschool children not included
  • Statement need to give overall direction
  • Needs to reflect current educational thinking
  • Measurement has become a goal


Implementation

  • Recognize that education is intended to increase ones capacity in one’s life roles
  • Prepare a document not do a study
  • Public consultation
  • Small working group
  • For public use
  • Distribute widely

 

2) Recommendation - Develop a learner centered service model that provides support in all domains 

to learners as, when and where needed.  


Rationale

  • Current system is time based
  • System is knowledge based; Education is developmental
  • Outcomes should be learner centered
  • Lacks criterion referenced assessment, 
  • Emphasizes summative and not formative assessment
  • Flexible scheduling, 
  • Challenge for credit arrangements,reater autonomy and accountability for teachers.
  • Challenge for credit
  • Stage based alternatives


Implementation

  • Address learner needs
  • Allow challenge for credit
  • Provide autonomy to teachers to solve learner problems
  • Flexible scheduling
  • Replace age based grading with staged based grading


3) Recommendation - Government assume a leadership role in creating a learning culture 

and providing a quality education for every Islander. 

Rationale

  • currently lacking, and education suffers as a result.
  • Operating and planning role in conflict
  • The role to include planning, setting priorities, funding, monitoring, quality control, ensuring the availability of professional resources.
  • An increased role in delivering support to families with pre- school children, continuing education classes and even in providing information and services to children and their parents.


Implementing


4) Government delegate the role of providing instruction


Rationale

  • Schoop operation politicized
  • Bureaucratic
  • Decision making only at high levels
  • Teachers lack autonomy to make professional decisions


Implementation

  • Delegate to School boards, schools or municipalities
  • Expand instructural authority to community agencies, professionals in private practice and learners themselves.
  • Expand role of libraries as study centres
  • Consider community based essential skills centres

 

5) Recommendation 5  - Establish a qualification body for the purpose certifying individual levels 

of achievement to persons wanting or needing such documentation.  


Rationale

  • Ranking and establishment of levels is not a teaching function; 
  • Limited to summative assessment
  • When a teachers rank their students they are putting their ability to teach at risk; 
  • Assigning student grades, selecting students for prizes and awards and awarding diplomas and degrees is a sorting process and should not be part of a teacher’s role.  
  • Apprenticeship with certification through a Provincial Department of Labour and music through the Royal Canadian Music Conservatory are examples of a preferred arrangement.  
  • Establish levels of achievement in each field not related to age of learner
  • Disconnect entrance requirements from school studies


Implementation

  • Start with essential skills



Recommendation 6 - Establish a professional model of service delivery where professionals are directly accountable to clients for the nature, extent and quality of service provided.  


Rationale

  • Teachers should be able to dignose, help and manage
  • Teachers need a close professional-client relationship withstudents
  • Teachers need professional autonomy 
  • Teachers should be accountable for student progress
  • Allow teachers to set up specialized clinics
  • Arizona exchanged 7 teaching positions for 14 teaching ssistants and results improved dramatically
  • School without a principal in Michigan
  • English school – all students study math at 9:00 am

  • Rationale
  • Incentivize

  • Incentive to continually approve
  • Autonomy is a form of incentive


Implementation

  • Set up a separate innovation fund
  • External granting committee
  • Expers in residence
  • Winning
