WHAT PART OF EDUCATIONSHOULD GOVERNMENT

PAY FOR?

Canadians boast about their health care system; it covers the cost of many, but not all, health care service and does not include medicines.. Government encourages people to adopt healthy practices, regulates health care workers and actually requires, provides or pays for many services. Service is triggered by a person with a health concern. Help is provided by or under the direction of a family doctor. I wonder how this would work for education?


Let me fabricate some clauses:

  • Essential skills – full cost for every resident regardless of age

  • Work entry skills training to entry level for any occupation practised on PEI

  • Counselling – one session annually for learner and guardian

  • Qualification examination – once per year

  • Updating – one week annually

  • Basic superv9si0n – available on recommendation of employer

  • Management – available after five years of full time employment

RECENT READING

Learning pods

According to the Washington Post (September 2, 2020) Learning pods are here to stay. The only question I have, is “will they be an official component of educational service ?


Mini courses

Fifty years ago. Interest in part time and short courses was very high; although it seemed to have waned in recent years, interest seems to be on the rise again, especially with layoffs resulting from the pandemic.


Learning context

One of the conclusions arising from the Education 20/20 “Citizen Math” study is the need to rethink the importance of context in the learning process. Some schools, so called magnet schools organizing around the arts, sports, health services. Aviation and technology. One school, although I’ve list contact information, selected engineering or business graduates as math teachers.
