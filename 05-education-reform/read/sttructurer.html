This comprehensive working document outlines Don's vision for structural reform of Prince Edward Island's education system. The document establishes foundational premises: that future community well-being depends on citizen education quality, that learning is personal, and that education is a service focused on arranging beneficial experiences. Don proposes seven major structural reforms: expanding departmental scope beyond youth to serve all Islanders, establishing clear terminology, articulating explicit educational purpose, clarifying service offerings, restructuring to reflect function rather than age-groups, granting operational autonomy to front-line workers (citing OECD evidence), and implementing accountability frameworks based on clarity, communication, and trust. The document demonstrates sophisticated understanding of organizational design and cites research supporting school autonomy.

A Call for Structural changes

D-structure

Background:

  • Our future as individuals and as a community will depend in large measure on the nature and level of education of our citizens.

  • Learning is an individual and personal matter through which people bring about changes in their lives

  • Education is a service focused on arranging experiences that help others learn

  • Nature of education is complex

Proposed structural changes:

  • General - Department should serve all Islanders not just youth and schools

  • Terminology – given the complexity of the field, definitions and understandings need to be widely disseminated,

  • Purpose of providing education services should clearly stated; A clear purpose not only directs the organization, it also provides a focus for instruction and assessment,

  • Services should be clarified and promoted.

  • Structure – normally reflects he dictum “form follows function” Our education system, it seems, focuses instead on groups being served.

  • Front line workkkers in Service organizatons need autonomy so that they can customize ervice; OECD report that students from schools wih autonomy in their day o say operation do better on PSA est resuls.

  • Accountability is about defining mission, values, goals as well as everyone’s role in accomplishing them. Accountability starts with clarity and expectations followed by communication and trust. The OECD, who gives us PISA tests, tells us that students do better in schools that have significant autonomy in their every day operation; thats because people who provide service need freedom to respond to students needs
