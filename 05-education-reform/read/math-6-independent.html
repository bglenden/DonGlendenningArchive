This proposal recommends establishing a pilot project enabling students to challenge high school mathematics courses for credit through independent learning. The initiative recognizes that learners progress at different paces and should be encouraged to meet standards in their own timeframe, with a focus on allowing higher achievement through supplementary instruction and support mechanisms.
Education Philosophy & Reform

20-06

INDEPENDENT LEARNING- MATHEMATICS


Purpose: To recognize for credit individual efforts to continue learning in mathematics


Background:

  • Education  is key to individual well being
  • Education is key to economic future of our community
  • Increasingly quality courses are available on line
  • Need to do more to encourage independent study
  • Some learners could complete courses, or gain higher credits in courses if given a bit more instruction
  • Each learner learns at his or her own pace.
  • Individuals should be encouraged to meet standards not just to learn in the available time.
  • Higher grades are in reach of most students if given more time


Proposal:

  • A pilot project 
  • Allow students to challenge for credit any high school mathematics course
  • An examination fee
  • Need to identify list of people who are qualified to award credit
  • Limited to high school courses
