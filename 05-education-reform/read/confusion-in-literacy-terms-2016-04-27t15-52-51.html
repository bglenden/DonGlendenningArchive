Make literacy less confusing


I find literacy confusing; I’m prepared to use any definition but it would be nice if, at least in PEI, we could agree on a meaning.  Sometimes I confuse literacy with basic education, citizenship skills, Basic Training for Skill Development and even with Adult Basic Education.  Let me try to explain. 


The Department of Education and Early Childhood Education introduced a Secondary Literacy  Assessment (SLA) of levels achieved by the end of Grade 9; it includes two elements, Reading and Writing.  The system also offers a Secondary Literacy Course, presumably including the same two subjects and, it appears one must successfully pass the SLA or the course in order to graduate.  The Department of Workforce and Advanced Learning also offers literacy training which appears to include Adult Basic Education (Biology, Chemistry, English, GED Preparation, Math and Physics), GED, PEI School Graduation Certificate for mature students, and GED instruction.  Also , the PEI Literacy Association refers to four kinds of literacy, prose, document and quantitative or numeracy.  I understand and support all of these efforts, my only point is that it would be helpful if the three agencies used the same version.  Then there is the matter of levels of literacy; the high school program has three levels,  adult literacy program identifies five levels. and there may be other schemes.


It seems to me that standardizing the terminology and levels of literacy within the provincial setting would improve both communication and understanding.  An analysis would, I am sure, produce the same outcomes or skills, the criteria should be the same, the number of levels could be adjusted to serve all Islanders. A common framework would allow for shares curriculum development, present a common “language” to all ages, allow for better student flow, confirm a common set of criteria during assessment and even form a common basis for transcripts. 


Perhaps above all, working through such a project could provide a great professional development from all levels and institutions.


Sincerely



Don Glendenning

President.
