Education Philosophy & Reform

Dear Editor:

Clarity

Some time ago The World Bank cautioned developing countries about confusing education and schooling; I’m afraid we do that too. 

Education is an umbrella word that includes all kinds of learning whenever and wherever it comes about – at home, in school, through travel and self-exploration. School and schooling, on the other hand, refers to a building or room set aside and equipped to learning and teaching. Recent years, thanks to Covid, have brought about a significant increase in the number of home based and independent learners.

A couple of weeks ago I went looking for the definition of “education” used by the Department of Education; couldn’t find it. (IT is not a personal strength)  I couldn’t even find the definition in the Education Act. 

On enquiry, learned that the definition of “education” had been replaced in the Act by a definition of “school”. Not sure if this applies to all schools or only to public schools

Definitions are crucial to the clarity of communication Seems to me that a change of this magnitude deserves some public discussion.

Sincerely

Don Glendenning
