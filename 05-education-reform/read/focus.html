Dear Editor:

 A CHANGE OF FOCUS


The World Bank, the world’s largest funder of public education, cautions countries about confusing education with going to school. I am afraid that we have also fallen into that trap.  


Put simply, education is a process; it may happen anywhere and at any time. Learning experiences may result from our own efforts or from the efforts of others. It is the experiences that results in change, not the meeting place where the experience occurs.  Schooling is one pattern of help but there are others; advancement in technology expands the possibility of education on demand. 


The role of government is to encourage, promote and regulate education; I think of this group as the “architects” of education. it taps into the latest research and thinking in the field. The “architects of education” continuously scan the field with a view to helping Islanders obtain the education they want and need. 

My reading of the literature suggests tht oversight and operation be separated. PISA and MEI make the case for increased school autnomy; the creation of “Health PEI” as the delivery agent for health services presents a second model. 


Sincerely


Don Glendenning
