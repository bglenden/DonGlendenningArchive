Description

Name

Math for every day living

Target Group

Islanders who want and need and need fluency in math skills for every day life.

Challenge/ Issue

This proposal arises out of conversation with providers and users of math education services and frequent media reports abut education services.


Mathematics is a key skill for all citizens; differences in skill levels can be attributed to the number of skills person can perform, he level reached on each skill, the degree and extent of clustering and the nature and relevance of benchmarks. Our exploration reveals a concern expressed by citizens, and especially by employers, at the nature and level of math skills demonstrated by Islanders.


Our exploration included an examination of a number of math curriculum outlines; next we searched for a “maser list” of math competencies, skills or outcomes needed or practised by citizens in their every- day lives. While our search produced a number of comprehensive lists of math outcomes and competencies, none revealed the extent to which those outcomes reflected the math skills actually used by citizens on a regular basis. We believe that such a list is needed to give direction to the curriculum development process


To fill our knowledge gap, Education 20/20 proposes to document math skills used by islanders in used in every-day life. The analysis model, known as DACUM, is an internationally recognized approach to gathering such information, although not well known in public education. When complete, the results of the t wo analyses can be compared.

Description

In carrying out the survey, Education 20/20 propose to:

  1. Convene a small group of representative Islanders , perhaps 10 people, such as parents, teachers, employers. Community workers, etc.

  2. With the aid of a trained facilitator and, using a recognized analysis process, identify a map of math skills used in everyday life.

  3. Strike a profile of skills deemed appropriate for an Island citizen in the 21st century.

  4. Strike a profile of skills needed for work entry level

Outcomes

The project will deliver a basic map or master list of competencies used by typical Islanders in their day to day operation. Copies will be made available at cost,

Benefits

Such a list will also be useful to teachers, tutors,. counsellors, employers, credentialing and certification bodies and others. It is also a communication tool between and among various providers ad users of math services
