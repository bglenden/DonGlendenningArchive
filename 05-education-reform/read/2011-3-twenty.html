Responding to coverage of Grade 3 math difficulties, Don advocates for summer instruction programs targeting the 20 percent of students struggling with mathematics. He contends that timely intervention prevents cumulative learning gaps and suggests minimal-cost summer sessions could provide crucial remediation. The letter illustrates Don's commitment to equity and early intervention, arguing that preventing failure is more cost-effective than addressing compounded deficiencies later. His practical tone reflects experience with both budget constraints and student learning needs.
Education Philosophy & Reform

February 25, 2011


Editor

Charlottetown Guardian

Charlottetown, PEI


Dear Editor:

The Twenty Percent Group


ln a letter to the Editor on February 23, Lloyd McKenna refers to the "20 percent (of Grade 3

students) who had difficulty in recent math tests."


The problem of failing math in Grade three, or any Grade, should be addressed; if not given

help, students will fall even further behind. More time on task should help. If help cannot be

rescheduled now, why not offer the "20 percent group" extra days of instruction during the

summer months so that they can raise their grades and better prepare for the work ahead?


The cost would be minimal; the payoff could be tremendous.


Sincerely


\

Don Glendenning \
