Don addresses confusion from using "grade" to denote both age-based cohorts and achievement standards. Teachers report 4-8 year achievement ranges within single grades, creating cascading problems: advanced students become bored, struggling students discouraged, teachers frustrated, parents confused, diploma value declines, public confidence erodes. He notes International Baccalaureate, Red Cross, PISA, P-CAP, and qualification authorities all use "level" terminology for achievement. Renaming would allow students to pursue different subjects at different levels (Grade 11 student simultaneously at Level 7 Math, Level 4 Physics, Level 6 Literature). This proposal directly addresses systemic heterogeneity by replacing age-based lockstep progression with achievement-responsive pathways. Reflects his consistent advocacy for clarity, standards, and flexible personalization within structured frameworks.


January 13, 2016


Editor,

Charlottetown Guardian

Charlottetown, PEI


Dear Sir: 

Achievement levels


Achievement levels confuse me especially when we assign two meanings to the same word. In one instance we use Grade to describe a grouping if young people of roughly the same age; in the other case, we use it to describe a level of achievement such as Grade 9 Math.   What make it even more confusing is that, because students learn at different rates, teachers report subject matter gaps of 4, 6 or even 8 years in an age group.  As a result:

Students who do well become bored.

Students who fall behind become discouraged

Teachers become frustrated

Parents wonder what is going on

The value of a graduation diploma falls. 

The public loses faith in the public school system.


The International Baccalaureate program refers to your achievement as a level; so does the does Canadian Red Cross for swimming.  PISA and P-CAP results are also reported by level. And I find level to be used by some qualification authorities.  Changing the term would allow a student to study any subject at any level.  As a result for instance, a grade 11 student could be taking Level 7 Math, Level 4 Physics and Level 6 Literature. 

Sincerely,

Don Glendenning

A long-time student of education
