Second findings iteration (November 21, 2024) reinforcing and refining conclusions from Finding 1. Reiterates lack of clarity, departmental structure misalignment, documentation deficiencies, transparency failures, and lack of professional education organizations. Emphasizes distinction between education and schooling as central problem. Focuses on teaching function documentation needs (learning models, assessment frameworks, curriculum clarity). Slightly more condensed than Finding 1 but maintains core themes.

Glendenning note

Nov, 21, 2024

Finding and Conclusions -2


Summary

  1. Lack of clarity - purpose, role of Department, branches, levels, schooling,

  2. Department structured to run schools rather than oversee education needs

  3. Oversight and operation should be separated

  4. Lacks a self correcting mechanism – choice

  5. Lack of documentation about the teaching function– learning model, assessment model, curriculum

  6. Teachers need better/easier access to research results

  7. Lacks transparency; little communication with community served

  8. System should accommodate community requests/needs

  9. Lacks definition of education; in fact, definition of “education” replaced with definition of “School”

  10. Should Department decide services to be offered or services it will pay for

  11. Services should be identified

  12. Role of Department (develop and deliver curriculum) does not reflect need

  13. Lacks transparency

  14. Teachers need better access to relevant research
