August 20, 2019 letter to Charlottetown Guardian advocate for standard educational definitions. Criticizes confusion between education and schooling, notes inconsistent use of "learner/learning excellence," and points out that school grades provide administrative convenience rather than reliable indicators of learning. Argues employers are often disappointed by grade-credential mismatch.


August 20, 2019

105

Editor,

Charlottetown Guardia

Charlottetown, PEI


Dear Sir: 

Some standard definitions

My morning reading included an article on the need for standard definitions when reporting hate crimes; the lack of standard definitions also leaves us unsure of just what is happening in education.  


On the other hand, I become confused when other educators fail to do the same. Here are some examples:

Education is an umbrella term like agriculture or transportation; it includes people of any age, all functions and services, in any field of study and for any purpose.  I cannot recall ever seen a Department of Agriculture nd Farming. Or a Department of Transportation and Ferries.  Education documents, however, seem to treat education and schooling as though  they are one and the same.

 Learner/learning excellence has been adopted although I found a  by some colleges and universities. Not sure whether it applies to level, process, 

School grades are an administrative convenience and tell little about what a student has learned yet employers continue to treat them as levels and become disappointed.







Sincerely, 



Don Glendenning

A long-time student of education
