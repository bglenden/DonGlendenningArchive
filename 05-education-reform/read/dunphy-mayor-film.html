In this January 6, 2016 letter, Don invites Stratford Mayor David Dunphy to attend the "Most Likely to Succeed" film screening and solicits the City of Stratford as a sponsor ($100). The letter describes the film's project-based learning approach and its coverage in major U.S. newspapers. Don reserves seating for the mayor or a representative and invites participation in post-film discussion. This demonstrates Don's engagement of municipal government in educational advocacy and his use of cultural events as platforms for advancing reform ideas.



January 6, 2016




Mayor David Dunphy

234 Shakespeare Dr,

Stratford, PE C1B 2V8



Dear Mayor Dunphy:


As you know, our group has been promoting changes in education. Recently, both the New York Times and Washington Post carried accounts of a new film about education being unrolled across the US; both papers gave the film a high rating.


The film, Most Likely to Succeed, was produced by education philanthropist, Ted Dintersmith, and shows a project based approach to education being followed by a ninth grade class at High Tech High in San Diego, California. It argues for less focus on content and more emphasis on relational skills with students given more latitude in what, when and how they learn.


The film will be shown at The City Cinema, 64 King Street, Charlottetown on Sunday, January 17, 2016 from 2-4:00 pm. (See attachment) We would welcome the City of Stratford as a sponsor ($100) and will reserve a couple of places(ticket at door) so that you, or a representative can be in the audience to view the film and participate in the discussion to follow.


Wishing you the compliments of the season and looking forward to seeing you on

January 17.


Sincerely




Don Glendenning

President
