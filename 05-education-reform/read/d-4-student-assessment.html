Analysis of student assessment purposes based on Robert Paul Wolff's 1969 framework identifying three functions: Formative criticism for designing learning strategies and feedback; Evaluation establishing achievement levels (criterion-referenced); and Ranking showing student comparison. Glendenning argues formative assessment is legitimate school work but ranking and grading represent conflict of interest for teachers. Proposes separating teaching and ranking functions like sports do with coaches and referees, suggesting schools should not handle ranking/grading of their own students. - **Key People**: Robert Paul Wolff (cited), Don Glendenning - **Key Organizations**: Education 20/20 Inc. - **Tags**: EDUCATION, PROFESSIONAL - **Eulogy Relevance**: HIGH - Core theme of Glendenning's educational philosophy on separation of teaching and assessment

Student assessment


While searching for information on grades and grading, I came across an item by Robert Paul Wolff (Wolff: A discourse on Grading, 1969) in which he identified three purposes for assessing students:


Criticism or analysis, firmative which is for the purpose of designing additional strategies for the purpose of helping a learner learn. Criticism prepares a teacher to provide meaningful feedback and is part of the ongoing give and take of the learning process.


Evaluation is designed to establish a person’s level in a subject or field and should be criterion referenced


Ranking intended to show how one stacks up against others; ranking results in students being winners or losers as for ranking, schools could take a lesson from sports with both coaches and referees. Ranking results from competitions; students should be encouraged to participate and teachers and students can use the results but teachers should play no part in the ranking process.


My theory is that ehe first kind , formative is definitely the work of schools and teachers but I feel equally strongly that ranking and grading is not a school function, perhaps not even an education function. Teachers will use the results of grading and ranking but actual ranking and grading their own students represents a conflict of interest. Sports teams have it right; they separate the functions of coaching and referring. Soooooo should schools.
