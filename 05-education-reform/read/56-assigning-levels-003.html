Job training is not what you teach but how you measure - Unknown

Student achievement levels


The practice of grading students is thought to have originated at Yale in the late 1700’s and, in spite of being described in 1913 as “an absolutely uncalibrated instrument”, grades remain a significant factor in determining the future of young lives, but are grades fair?

Establishing grades is a regulatory function and should not be confused with testing for the purpose of improving instruction. Teaching and sorting are separate roles; vesting authority to sort in in the hands of people who teach constitutes a conflict of interest. The grading carried out by those who teach, however carefully undertaken, will always be suspect; current course descriptions and student report cards do little to allay such fears.

Most developed countries have qualification authorities, Canada and the USA are the exceptions. The New Zealand Qualification Authority (NZQA) acts as a standards- setting body; it moderates internal assessment practices and manages external assessments for secondary schools; it also assesses external courses and credentials. The Ontario Qualification Framework operates in the field of apprenticeship and postsecondary education. “It outlines the knowledge and skills expected of holders each type of qualification; and it shows the relationship and differences between qualifications.”


PEI could use an independent testing service, probably not for all courses but certainly for courses considered essential for citizenship, employment and postsecondary studies. If we move quickly, perhaps we could supply the service for a fee to other Atlantic Province.
