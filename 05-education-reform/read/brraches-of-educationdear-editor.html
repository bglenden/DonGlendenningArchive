Dear Editor:

A Education Puzzler

A friend of mine has a habit of referring to something that he does not understand as a “puzzler”; my puzzler is about branches or divisions of education. 

I raise this matter because of  a recent scan of Department of Education literature. PEI Government Departments are structured to reflect branches or fields of endeavour. Agriculture includes land, crops, animals and potatoes; Health reflects Covid, drugs, E-Health and hospitals; Fisheries includes angling, hunting, wildlife and trapping. Education, on the other hand. reveals levels of study – elementary, secondary and postsecondary studies.

Education is about creating experiences through which people change or increase knowledge, skills attitudes or other attributes. As a result of these experiences people can speak another language, build and maintain roads, thrive in their chosen community, provide health serv ices to others and both create and enjoy artistic pieces of art. The organization of schools focuses on education; so should the Department that leads them. 

Sincerely

Don Glendenning
