(170 words):** Don Glendenning defines learning, teaching, and education with clarity and precision. He argues all PEI residents are potential learners who seek measurable change in knowledge, skills, attitudes, or values. Learners bring three elements to education: inherent physical/mental/emotional conditions; prior experiences from varied sources; and personal aspirations. Don defines learning as changing what one knows, feels, believes, and can do; teaching as arranging experiences enabling such change; and education as resulting from meaningful experiences. He notes that being "well-educated" means handling everyday life issues competently. Critically, Don rejects the conflation of school attendance with education—the World Bank warns this is problematic. He observes PEI educators lack shared definitions, potentially reflecting mission uncertainty, struggles with contemporary changes, and unclear educational services. He recommends reviewing common terminology, adopting shared definitions, implementing low-key systemic changes, ensuring conceptual clarity, and reconsidering educational goals and lifelong learning integration.
