Closing the preparedness gap


The preparedness gap can be closed and closed at a faster rate, and closing the gap benefits all Islanders. This was the message from Gerry Seaward, a long time adult educator, in a public presentation recently at the Stratford Town Hall. Seaward described courses at Holland College designed to help young people beyond high school age to pick up credits they need for the next step in their career path; for most young people in the program, the next step is postsecondary education and for others, it’s a job. The present program includes courses in - biology, chemistry, English, math and Physics.


The term, “Preparedness Gap” arises from a survey of young Islanders in late 2013 by The Community Foundation of Prince Edward Island and arises, it seems from a number of causes including students dropping out of high school before they graduate, not taking or not passing courses demanded by postsecondary institutions and choosing the wrong high school program. Lack of preparedness may block otherwise promising careers.


Most people enrolled in the program are young. A recent survey carried out by Holland College shows that most students enrolled in the program, 76%, are under age 25, two thirds are looking for high school credits, thirty seven percent already have a high school diploma, fifteen percent have already completed a college or university program and another 15 % have some college or university credits. The program, funded through the Department of Workforce Development and Higher Learning, is offered on a full and part-time basis in eight centres across the Island


The presentation generated a vigorous discussion. People wondered why the College is teaching high subjects, why students have to take subjects already taken in high school, why employers and institutions insist on a high school graduation diploma when it no longer signifies that students have the skills they need. There was even discussion of a separate grading body like that in apprenticeship and the International Baccalauriate program.


The presentation was sponsored by Education 20/20 Inc. as part of its initiative to maintain a dialogue on education among Islanders; other presentations are planned.
