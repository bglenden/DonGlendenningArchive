This November 28, 2013 letter is a year-end reflective note thanking Duffy for funding the Excellence in Education Award. Don expresses pride that the award represents "the first of its kind in Atlantic Canada" and possibly elsewhere. He articulates his theory of change: systemic improvements won't originate from within the education system itself; change requires public demand and external accountability. Education 20/20 is strategically pivoting toward public engagement through a new website launch, community foundation-organized discussions, and acting as an independent body asking critical questions and disseminating information. This represents Don's mature understanding of institutional change dynamics and his belief in the power of informed public scrutiny.
Education Philosophy & Reform



November 28, 2013



Regis and Joan Duffy

31 Maplewood Crescent

Charlottetown

PE C1A 7R5


Dear

Education 20/20 Inc. Award

Just a brief note to thank you both for your encouragement and for your funding of the Education 20/20 Inc. Award.

We are excited about the award. To the best of my knowledge, this will be the first of a kind in Atlantic Canada, and maybe elsewhere. In addition to recognizing the good work of a school, we expect the award to generate public discussion about what makes a quality education. At the moment, our plans are to announce the Award on January 13, 2014 with a closing date for submissions being March 1; the actual award will be made during the first week of May.

We’ve concluded that the kind of change needed in education is not likely to be initiated by the system itself; improvements will come only when users “demand“ them. Education 20/20 therefore, plans to focus on both information and engagement directed to the public at large. I am pleased to say that our website is about ready for launch, and we are working with the Community Foundation to organize a number of discussions throughout the Island. Having an independent body asking questions and disseminating information to the public is also new to the education scene.

Again, many thanks for both your encouragement and financial support.

Warm personal regards.




Don Glendenning


Cc: Lois Woolridge
