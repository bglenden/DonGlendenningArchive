Dear Editor:

An education system

Prince Edward Island needs an education system - a system of services available to  all Islanders not just for Islanders who want or are able to go to school. We’d probably be the first in Canada but I believe that such a system would be an ideal way to structure education services to meet the changing interests and needs of both Islanders and the Island community. The world is changing and education must do likewise; otherwise, we all get left behind.  

 In order for an education system to work, we need a clear vision as to what people will need to know and be able to do 20, 40,60 years down the road.  Today, people fill a number of life roles that of an individual, family member, learner, citizen, consumer and producer roles.  Those roles will likely be there tomorrow but also fifty years from now but may not be quite the same. 

School is a common term to describe assistance to learners. I argue that is not   sufficient detail for today let alone tomorrow. They need to be broken out and would likely include information, counselling, diagnosis, instruction, tutoring and technology. Services need to be separately to individual learners consistent with their stage of development 

 So how would an   education system differ from the school system we now have? 

  • Students would enrol in the system and select  or be provided with an educational advisor
  • Advisor   would deliver some services or arrange for them to be delivered              by another (much kike a family doctor}
  • Learning context would be established and learning profile modifed as needed.
  • Learning progress would be reported on a competency by competency basis
