Foundational description document outlining eight major concerns about PEI's education system. Don identifies a core conflict of interest: teachers set standards, measure achievement, and determine student levels—contrary to practice in most industries. He highlights issues including grade inflation, social promotion, confusion between age-based grouping and achievement levels, and erosion of public confidence. The document contrasts PEI's approach with international models (apprenticeship, language authorities, IB programs) and articulates why current proficiency level systems are fundamentally flawed due to uncertain outcome origins and lack of external criteria.
