Don challenges the basis of teacher compensation, arguing that paying teachers by years of formal study and experience lacks logical foundation. He contends people should be compensated for actual services rendered—the quality and impact of their work, not the credentials or seniority they possess. The letter advocates for performance-based rather than credential-based compensation, though the piece is incomplete. Don's argument suggests teachers should be evaluated and rewarded based on educational outcomes and student learning rather than inputs like degrees and years on the job.

June 25, 2012



Editor, Charlottetown Guardian

Charlottetown, PEI


Dear Sir:

Teacher Pay
