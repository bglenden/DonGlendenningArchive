Add to that typical education services of governance, information, counselling, products and technology, quality control, assessment, instruction and education becomes  complex and  teaching difficult. No wonder some people, including the writer, become confused.

4-1-structure/overhaul






Role uncertain; a World Bank  report on education in 2018  reminded us that going to school and gaining an education are not the same thing; the difference needs to be affirmed in PEI as well. In fact, until we are clear on our education plans, it is hard to tell just were schools fit in.

Learning is about changing the way we think, feel and act; teaching is about arranging experiences that help others change the way they think, feel or act. Schools are purpose built facilities where learners can get help  with their learning. It is not surprising, as the Wolrd Bank points, that sometimes people confuse learning and going to school.  I sometimes think of a school being like a hospital or shopping centre; they house resources but those who attend may not purchase anything or return to good health.




 




Some reasons for that conclusion include:

  • Given that individuals learn at their own rate, the length of time one has been in school is not a very reliable measure of how much one has learned. The alternative is a set a standard and allow students time to meet it.
  • Education is typically comprised of a number of services -   information, instruction, counselling, assessment, learning products and technology, etc; most can be offered most anywhere. Some services require special facilities and groups.  But going to school is not itself an educational service.  I liken a school to a hospital or shopping plaza. 
  • I’m puzzled by the title “Education and Lifelong learning” in the name of the Department; doesn’t education already include lifelong learning? 

Certainly instructional  services usually benefit from being in a single location; non-instructional services such as Information and counselling , however, may serve equally well or even better  if located off campus used by the Department of Education


A lexicon of common educational terms may be in order
