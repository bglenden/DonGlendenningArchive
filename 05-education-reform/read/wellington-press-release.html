April 8, 2014 press release announcing workshop outcomes and Education 20/20's approach. April 8, 2014

For immediate release

Looking for solutions

Participants in a recent workshop at Centre Goeland in Cap Egmont moved quickly from talking about issues and challenges in education to the matter of finding solutions.  Communities need an education system that helps both Individuals and the community to develop their full potential, personal, cultural, social and economic.  The educational needs of people are holistic and seamless; institutions, on the other hand, organize by levels, roles, and offerings; to better respond to student needs, some realignment of services may be needed.  

The first half of the workshop focused on a recent survey by the Community Foundation of Prince Edward Island on issues facing Island youth.  Over 200 Island youth participated in the survey and, among other things, had identified their preparation for work and for further education as a matter of concern.  The presentation included a review of methodology and findings and was led by Steve McQuaid, Vice President of the Community Foundation of Prince Edward Island and Katherine MacDonald, Researcher; the report is available on the Foundation website, www.cfpei.ca.  

Some school issues and challenges result from the way we structure our education system others reflect our views about education.  How we group students, the link between engagement and achievement, the role of other community organizations reflect one’s view of learners and the learning process; on the other hand, flexibility, school autonomy and accountability are issues related to how we structure a system.  

Participants were thanked by Don Glendenning, President of Education 20/20 Inc., a new educational organization on PEI committed to improving the quality of education for every Canadian.  Anyone interested in its activities or to be placed on the mailing list for the monthly newsletter, should check the website, www.education2020.ca
