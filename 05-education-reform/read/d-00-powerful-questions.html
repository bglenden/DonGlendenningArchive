Don summarizes Education 20/20 conclusions: the public understands education needs better than political leadership; PEI needs a Department of Education not just Public Instruction; teachers should not participate in ranking (conflict of interest); educational goals must be clear and measurable; problems stem from organizational structure not professional inadequacy.

Open Education

01 April 23, 2013

The mind is not a vessel to be filled, but a fire to be kindled. ― Plutarch


Some conclusions


During the past few months, I’ve read widely and talked often about the state of education; here are some of my conclusions and, while they apply to PEI, most apply to education in other Provinces as well:

  • Parents and the public at large are ahead of politicians and our educational leadership in their understanding of education in today’s society

  • PEI has a Department of Public Instruction with a focus on young people; what PEI needs is a Department of Education focused on educating all our citizens..

  • Teachers are in professional conflict when the participate in ranking and grading students; while teachers may help students to obtain qualifications, teachers should not be part of the sorting process.

  • Most goals listed in the philosophy nof Education are neither clear nor measurable.

  • The reason for most of the concerns about education can be attributed to the way we organize the sector rather than to professional educational principles.

  • Advisory and dialogue


Perhaps where I differ most with colleagues is in costs/funds to implement changes
