Letter critiquing PEI's imprecise educational terminology. Don argues that using "Education and Lifelong Learning" conflates education with schooling, similar to saying "Department of Agriculture and Farming." He presents his working definition of education as all experiences with formative effect on thinking, feeling, or acting. References Good's Dictionary of Education and criticizes educators for interchangeably using "education" and "schooling."

D103

Dear Editor:

Education on PEI is plagued by an inability or unwillingness to define terms. While I support the recent move to bring lifelong learning into the education envelope; the linking implies that lifelong learning doesn’t really belong.  The same can be said  f or Education and Early Childhood Development used by the former Government or and Education and Training used in Manitoba.  An equivalent would be to establish a Department of Agriculture and Farming, a Department of Transportation and Airports or a Department of Transportation and Ferries.

My search shows education to be an umbrella term, one that applies to the acquisition or further development of attitudes, skills, knowledge and values of people of all ages,  all fields of endeavour, all levels of performance and on a full or part time basis in any field of endeavor.  My own working efinition is simply that my education includes all the experiences that had a formative effect on the way I think, feel or act.  In spite of this, educators continue to use education and schooling interchangeably,  I’ ve been asked where I got my education when the asker really wanted to know where I went to school. Documments about education frequently are only about schools.
