Don advocates for PEI to develop a comprehensive written philosophy of education that applies to all ages and learning contexts, not just schools. He argues this would provide direction, consistency, and help narrow expectations gaps, proposing it as a key first step in educational reform.
Education Philosophy & Reform

February 25, 2015

Editor, Charlottetown Guardian

Charlottetown, PEI


Dear Sir:

Educational Reform: step one


PEI lacks a written philosophy of education; so do other Provinces. By philosophy I mean a document setting out in broad and future oriented terms a context, a set of beliefs, perhaps a definition, along with some principles and goals. Although written for the public, the document is intended to set direction and increase consistency in planning and implementing programs. Such a document, if used, should go a long way in narrowing the expectation’s gap between schools and the communities they serve. 


Some readers may wonder how this idea differs from the “Philosophy of Education” now on the Government website.  The simple answer is that the existing document is limited to schools and the young.  What we need is a statement that reflects a framework for the education of Islanders of all ages and all services whether through the home, schools and colleges, on line, at work, through community organizations or self- study, 


The development of a philosophy is a Provincial responsibility and requires attention at the highest levels; perhaps, after the election, all political parties could come together in support of its writing. 



Sincerely


Don Glendenning

A long-time student of education
