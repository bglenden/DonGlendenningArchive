Notes on PEI Education Act

As requested by the Green Party

April 28, 2016


1 Opening comments:

  • Education is the most important issue of the day
  • What we do/become, what this Island becomes will be determined to a large degree, by the education that we provide
  • Education – any experience that has a formative effect on the way a person thinks, feels or acts
  • Schooling – instruction, education or training received in a school
  • The OECD points out the students fare better on PISA exams where the school has autonomy in its day to day operations
  • It is my firm belief that the role of Government is to plan, lead, fund, ensure quality and inform but not to directly deliver programs.

2 Having said that, however, here are some general comments about the proposed Act

  • Education needs to allow professionals freedom to act and accountability for their action , similar to the freedom of doctors and lawyers; at the moment teachers are the equivalent of nurses in a hospital but subject  to directions from politicians not  professionals educators
  • I know that it is an Act and, therefore, must be precise but it but all decisions appear to fall to the Minister.
  • The Act reeks of efficiency more so than effectiveness.


3 Focuses:

  • Narrow vision of education – what one learns in school
  • More appropriate to call it a PEI Schools Act
  • Appears focused on knowledge acquisition and credits
  • No mention of education for adults, part time learners, independent learners, the employed, adult educational upgrading, education for personal development; in spite of the name, the Act is about schooling and not education.
  • Strange that an Education Act does not define” education”
  • It appears that all decisions will be made by government employees
  • Some public advisors but no public decision makers
  • Don’t have any sense of trying to improve effectiveness


4 When Government operates schools:

  • Fairness goes out the window
  • The system becomes politicized
  • Non-operational matters, leadership, quality, standards, monitoring, public information, accountability and standards suffer
  • Little room for debate
  • The kind of education made available and the kind of education government pays for becomes one and the same





5 From my reading of the Act:

  • If the new organization will provide a better education than the former system, why are French students excluded?
  • “Education” should be defined
  • “Education program” (1-ff) should be defined also
  • I’m glad to see a description of the principal’s role, I believe that is new.
