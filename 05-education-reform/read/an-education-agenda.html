Critical policy paper arguing that PEI's Department of Education should broaden its mandate beyond schools and youth to serve all citizens in lifelong learning. Advocates for systemic change focusing on processes of changing how people think, feel, and act, rather than the current age-based, institution-centric approach. Reflects Don's long-standing views on comprehensive educational reform.

An Education Agenda

About/educ/agenda

We have a Department of Education but it is focused on schools; don’t expect educational improvement  until we change the agenda.  PEI has a population of 160,000 people.  The Department, I would think, should encourage all citizens   to continue their education, regulate the provision of educational services and support some citizens in their pursuit of improvement; instead it seem to be focus on youth and provide service through schools.

My exploration suggests that education is process of helping people change the way they think, feel and act. The statement of philosophy refers to helping Islanders find a place; not sure just what that mans.  I think that we need more than that.   Thee key to quality  education is conceptual clarity - a clear purpose, plan and benchmarks.  It is said that  each person fills six life roles – individual, citizen, family member, student, consumer and producer.  Clarity is needed to plan and valuate.

Changing the way one thinks, feels and acts typically results from providing the following services: information, counselling, instruction, human resources, learning products and technology, governance. instead, Departments tend to organize around the age of learners, education institutions,  and prior performance in the education system.
