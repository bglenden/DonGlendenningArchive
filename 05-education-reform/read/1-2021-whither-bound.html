Don questions PEI's unclear education mission, comparing it unfavorably to Health's goal of "accessible quality health services" and Agriculture's goal of innovation. He criticizes the Department's limitation to "children birth to Grade 12" as narrow, notes confusion between schooling and education (citing the World Bank), and observes that most developed nations have qualification authorities to rationalize credentials, while Canada and the USA lack these. Don calls for clear leadership that removes conceptual confusion about education's purpose and scope.


January 3, 2021


Editor,

Charlottetown Guardian

Charlottetown, PEI


Dear Sir: 

Whither bound


In the mid-1950’s I spent a year at an American; I lived off campus in a private home with cooking privileges. My landlady was legally blind; she was also deeply religious. On my bedside table was a little booklet with the title “WHITHER BOUND?”  I think of these words often these days as I reflect on the future of education in PEI; based mostly on what I read and hear, the mission of public remains unclear. 


I say this because:

The Department has limited itself only to “children from birth to Grade 12” that seems narrow, compared to the Minister of Health, for example, who describes its role as that of providing accessible quality health services throughout the Province” or the ministry of Agriculture with a goal of leading “ our Province in efforts to innovate our agriculture and agri-food sectors”.  Education needs and deserves an equivalent role.

 The education literature and dialogue, including that emanating   from the Department of Education, seems to equate going to school and getting an education; my dictionaries say otherwise. so does the World Bank.  It is impossible to insist on quality among this confusion.

Another example of confusion presents itself with the awarding of credentials – certificates, diplomas and degrees; .  Many countries resolve the issue through the establishment of qualification authorities; Canada and the USA are exceptions.  Or perhaps  a more detailed report card would serve the purpose


The Department plays a leadership role, removing confusion would be a good start.


Sincerely


Don Glendenning
