Open Education No. 1 (June 21, 2018), authored by Don Glendenning addressing federal leadership in education for employment. The article recounts historical federal initiatives in the 1960s that justified vocational education through industrial need, educational equality, and cost-sharing between levels of government. Don documents federal criteria that drove program effectiveness: programs not courses, local advisory committees, substantial skill training, experienced teachers, and relevant analysis. He expresses concern that newer funding arrangements have lost important safeguards, raising questions about whether contemporary education-for-employment programs maintain the rigor of historical models.
