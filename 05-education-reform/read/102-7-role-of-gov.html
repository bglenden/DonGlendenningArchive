Dear Sir

Re-setting direction

An education system is an array of programs and services put in place by a community to encourage and support the education of its citizens; support is usually focused on (1) helping individual citizens gain skills, knowledge, attitudes and values needed in the years ahead and (2) providing human resources needed to maintain the community as a place people want to live. Typical education services include governance, funding, regulation, information, counselling, instruction, quality assurance, learning products and human resources.

But there is a gap, some say a widening gap, between services wanted (needed?) and services provided; the Department and Lifelong Learning, like its predecessor, is focused on schools rather than education, hence many . For example, the Department of Education gives “its role as one of delivering programs”; its role should be one of encouraging and supporting people to develop attitudes, knowledge skills needed to thrive. Such a narrow view does not auger well for our future,

It’s time for a system overhaul, its structure, mandate and mission, relationship with government, bureaucracy, level of autonomy, transparency and accountability. Step one, I suggest, is review and restatement of –setting out basic beliefs and underlying principles on which our system should be built. The initiation of such a process falls normally to government; it’s preparation, however, should include substantial public engagement.

As I write, I am reminded of a quotation in a book, Setting Instructional Objectives by Robert Mager, popular educator of some sixty years ago. In making the case for clear objectives, Mager asks “If you don’t know where you’re going, how will you know when you get there?” It’s time for education in PEI to review and restate where “it’s going”

Sincerely


Don Glendenning

A long-time student of education
