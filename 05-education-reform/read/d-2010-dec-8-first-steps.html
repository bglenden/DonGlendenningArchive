Don proposes six low-cost, immediately implementable interventions to improve education: adding communication as a separate subject, publishing clear performance standards, shifting teacher licensing criteria from coursework to demonstrated skills, reviewing teaching-learning models, allowing course challenge-for-credit, and encouraging teacher-led innovation. His emphasis on minimal disruption and cost demonstrates his understanding of institutional constraints and political feasibility, positioning reform as achievable within existing budgets and structures.

December 8, 2010



Editor 

Guardian

Charlottetown, PEI


Dear Sir:

Ideas for school improvement


It seemed that everyone I met today wanted to talk about the international test results; all agree that something needs to be done.  It is time, however, to move beyond discussing the problem and to discuss remedies.  


To open the debate, here are some low cost interventions for consideration:


Add communication as a separate subject for study and include it on report cards. 

Develop and publish a clear and meaningful statement of performance levels for each subject.    

Recast criteria for awarding teaching licenses from courses taken to skills demonstrated. 

Review the teaching learning model to reflect current educational thinking.

Allow students to challenge courses for credit.

Encourage teacher-led innovation


In presenting this list, I chose changes that can be implemented with a minimum of upset and little, if any, extra cost.  I look forward to reading other views on the subject.


Sincerely


Don Glendenning
