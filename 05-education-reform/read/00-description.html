Comprehensive project framework document dated April 14, 2015, establishing the mathematics education initiative within Education 20/20 Inc. Frames the problem using PISA results and CFPEI surveys showing low basic skills among recent graduates. Explains deliberate choice of Grade 9 as the intervention point before academic streaming and commitment to learner-centered perspective. Outlines PEI mathematics education goals (confident problem-solving, mathematical communication, appreciation, real-world connections, lifelong learning, mathematical literacy). Identifies key gaps: inadequate transcript descriptions for employers, lack of comprehensive outcome lists, absence of quality control procedures, and missing spiralling evidence. Proposes actions including consultant consultations, master outcome lists, qualification levels, assessment instruments, external qualification mechanisms, and math roundtables.
Education Philosophy & Reform


EDUCATION 20/20 INC.

April 14, 2015

Math Project

Description


It is widely recognized that education is a key factor in determining one’s quality of life; in the same way, the collective education of our citizens will determine to a significant degree the future of our community and country. One of the most frequently mentioned concerns by Islanders is the low level of basic skills among recent graduates and young Islanders generally. Evidence for concern arises from PISA results, recent CFPEI survey of Island youth, presentation to the Standing Committee by, How Canada Performs; Provincial and Territorial Ranking, Education and Training; Education and Skills, and a series of workshops on Preparedness held throughout PEI by Education 20/20 Inc.

PEI Goals of mathematics education are to prepare students to (PEI Education Website MAT613A)

  • use mathematics confidently to solve problems;

  • communicate and reason mathematically;

  • appreciate and value mathematics;

  • make connections between mathematics and its applications;

  • commit themselves to lifelong learning;

  • become mathematically literate adults, using mathematics to contribute to society.
