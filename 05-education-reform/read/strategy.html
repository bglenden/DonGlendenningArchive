A September 4, 2013 strategic communications plan for Education 20/20 Inc. describing the organization's mission to improve Canadian education quality through information and engagement as "twin pillars of change." The document outlines guiding principles (encourage writing, gather local information, survey-based research, public-facing language), proposed communication components (website, blog, Twitter, newsletters, surveys, papers, publications, public gatherings, media releases, wikis, instructional materials), and operational structure including management, communications, editorial committees, and volunteer engagement.


September 4, 2013

EDUCATION 20/20 INC.


Communications strategy


Note: discussion points


Background:


The mission of Education 20/20 Inc. is to improve the quality of education for every Canadian; central to accomplishing its mission are the twin pillars of information and engagement; information being the fuel and engagement the cauldron of change. The nature and range of information about education available to the public does not reflect the significance of the subject; we propose to initiate some changes. Communication activities will focus on education, not just schooling, and the needs of learners and the learning process with our website as the hub.


Guiding principles:

  • Encourage others to write

  • Gather and disseminate local information

  • Undertake surveys

  • Writing for the public not the professionals

  • including but not limited to students

  • evidence and informed opinion

  • Focused on best practices and desired system change...

  • Language

Components

  • Webpage, Blog, Twitter, announcements, links, donation channel

  • Newsletter – Open education?, electronic but printable, monthly to report on current activities of Education 20/20 but also events in education in the community at large.

  • Surveys of to gather information of interest to our target group

  • Occasional papers on subjects consistent with our mission and priorities

  • Publications

  • Public gatherings

  • Media releases

  • Brochures

  • Wikis that allow readers to participate in the actual development of articles.

  • Posters

  • Guides

  • Booklets for new mothers –see CLIA

  • Instructional material




Operations:

  • Management Committee

  • Communications committee

  • Journalism students, teachers in training, paid journalists, retirees

  • Editorial committee

  • Staff

  • Volunteers

  • Budget

  • Writers: students, teachers in training, paid journalists, retirees,


Issues:

  1. Audience

  2. Level

  3. Focus

  4. Sponsorships/advertisements

  5. Kind of info: curriculum, standards,

  6. Public newsletter like Senior’s magazine

Initial Steps

  • Establish a communications committee

  • Establish a website

  • Seek an Editor Newsletter

  • Surveys of local issues

  • Recruit volunteers

  • Secure
