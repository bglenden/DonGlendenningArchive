Charlottetown Guardian

Charlottetown, PEI


Dear Editor:

The tyranny of time


I had my car serviced a few days ago; during the process, the mechanic discovered a slight problem, it took longer than expected to fix the problem, but fix it he did before I drove away.

During my last stay in the hospital; the doctor had me stay a couple of days longer than

Expected just to be sure everything was ok. ln fact, completing the job seems to be the motto of most service providers, that is, except schools.


The school system is a time based institution; there is little, if any, flexibility to adjust schedules to serve people needing extra help. Nor is it possible for those who are good at a subject to finish early, or to get a credit and move on to something else. Instead of establishing a standard of performance for each subject, and helping each one reach it, schools allocate time to each subject and measure how much a student has learned in the time available. Students move from subject to subject at the sound of a bell whether or not they have completed their work; that's not the message we should be giving our youth.


Change in our school system is hampered, some might say blocked, by inflexible rules; limited choice, one dominant supplier and front line workers with little freedom or accountability.

Removing these conditions would enable change, but obviously not guarantee it.


Your views on the tyranny of time are welcome at standards2020@gmail.com


Sincerely




Don Glendenning
