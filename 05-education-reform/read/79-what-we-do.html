Open Education No. 79 (August 25, 2019), bilingual English/French. Don questions whether early-years education should prioritize literacy, numeracy, self-care, community care, interpersonal relations, creativity, and lifelong learning as report-card subjects, with traditional subjects as medium. Cites evidence that boutique schools (music, sports, environment) contribute to student education. Reflects his holistic approach to learning beyond cognitive development.

We organize our education system around cognitive studies: language, geometry, math and deal informally with the soft I sometimes wonder if education is being “overdone.”skills .Given the stresses in the lives of young people today, I sometimes wonder if we should do the reverse.


Should the following be the main subjects of the early years and appear on school report cards?

  • Literacy
  • Numeracy
  • Self- care
  • Community care
  • Interpersonal relations
  • Contributor skills
  • Creativity
  • Life long learning


… with literature, the arts, history, mathematics, science as the medium in  which they all play out? 


After all, there is some evidence that boutique schools (organized aroung music, sports, environment or health occupations  ar actual contributors to a students education

 
























Don Glendenning, Editor
