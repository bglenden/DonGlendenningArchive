Don applies systems thinking to education reform, arguing that the public school system lacks normal problem-solving mechanisms. He identifies math skill gaps as an example where students, employers, and post-secondary institutions all report problems but no systematic resolution occurs. He contrasts the current system with other professions (law, medicine, counseling) that employ specialists for problem-solving. Don calls for establishing formal mechanisms to identify, define, and resolve educational problems, noting that historically the system resolved issues by allowing struggling students to drop out—an unacceptable modern alternative.
