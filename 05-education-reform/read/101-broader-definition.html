Education Philosophy & Reform


December 20, 2019

101

Editor,

Charlottetown Guardian

Charlottetown, PEI


Dear Sir: 

Education mandate letter


The Premier’s mandate letter to the Minister of Education and Lifelong Learning is an interesting read; I certainly agree that items mentioned could benefit from further attention.  I must say, however, that the soil in which schools operate probably needs some tilling, first.


The world around us is changing; new products and approaches are available; yet the education soil has become resistant to new crops and techniques.  Citizens seem ready to adapt and adopt; educators either don’t want to or aren’t allowed that option.  


The PEI vision of education  seems to have narrowed since my arrival fifty years ago.  The Call for Action published by Education 20/20 Inc. in 2016 called for a “clear and compelling vision of education”, the same call is still valid today. Given the importance of education and our future Premier’s commitment to collaboration,  perhaps representatives of all political parties  could be invited to join in developing a new document to guide education into the future.  


Sincerely


 Don Glendenning

A long-time student of education
