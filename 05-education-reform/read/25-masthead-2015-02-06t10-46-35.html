Newsletter masthead/introductory page (Open Education No. 01) articulating Education 20/20's operating beliefs about education. Core principles include recognition that education occurs both in and outside schools, that home is the major educational contributor, that parents want education not just schooling, and that educating is a developmental activity distinct from knowledge transfer.
Education Philosophy & Reform

Open Education

01 April 23, 2013

The mind is not a vessel to be filled, but a fire to be kindled. ― Plutarch


Operating beliefs - education:

  1. Each person carries his/her own combination of traits based on genetics, culture, health, emotional make-up, previous experiences and imagined future.

  2. We are educated whether or not we go to school

  3. The home is the major contributor to one’s education

  4. Parents want their children to be educated not just schooled;

  5. There are identifiable ages and stages in learning –infants, early childhood, youth, adult

  6. Parents, and later ourselves, are responsible for our education; they just need a bit of help from time to time

  7. Concept of graduation not an educational function

  8. Educating is a developmental activity; schools emphasize the transfer of knowledge
