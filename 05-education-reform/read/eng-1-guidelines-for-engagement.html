Comprehensive guiding principles document articulating Education 20/20's philosophy that engagement is key to student success. Defines engagement across five dimensions: student-to-student interaction with emphasis on respect and collaboration; student-to-teacher relationships emphasizing safe, welcoming environments and integration of learning; student-to-learning material through active engagement and real-world application; student-to-community partnerships through experiential learning; and parental involvement, citing 30+ years of research demonstrating parental engagement's impact on achievement, attendance, behavior, and graduation rates. Includes references to educational frameworks (AACU high-impact practices, invitational education, multiple intelligences) and academic citations.

Education 20/20

Guiding Principles

for Education Engagement Award






Educaton 20/20 views “engagement” as key to students’ success in school and in life. For this award, “engagement” will be considered according to the following high impact educational practices:



Student to Student:  Opportunity for individual, small group and whole class interactions; establishing relationships based on such skills as respect, cultural competency, caring, courtesy, tact, honour, cooperation and collaboration. 



Student to Teacher:  Establishment of an welcoming environment that is safe and comfortable physically and emotionally, based on the assumptions of respect, cultural competency, trust, care, optimism and intentionality.

  • Teacher encourages integration of learning across courses and invites students to discuss “big questions” that matter beyond the classroom.
  • Teacher encourages critical inquiry, writing, research, collaborative learning and other skills that develop students' intellectual and practical skills.
  • Teacher ensures he/she is on leading edge of current teaching practices and technology so as to better prepare students' for 21st century learning.


                             

Student to Learning Material: Teacher helps student engage actively in learning and helps students make meaning of the information for application in the real world. Some examples would include collaborative projects, i.e. learning to work and solve real problems in groups, by sharpening their own understand through listening seriously to insights of others especially those from different backgrounds and cultures. Students could also be encouraged to record example of their best work through a portfolio or video and discuss their learning with other classmates and teachers.

                           


Student/ Community Partnerships: Experiential learning with community partners is an instructional strategy and can be made part of a course. This involves direct experience with issues they are studying in the curriculum. The opportunity to learn, analyze information, and be a participant in community setting also reinforces the idea of good citizenship, i.e. giving back to the community. Other examples of student/community partnership may include fundraising for various community projects and involvement in diverse community service.




Parent involvement in schools: The topic of parental involvement in schools has more than 30 years of positive research. Study after study show the dramatic impact parental involvement makes to a child's success in school. In fact, engaging families in their children’s education improves school achievement, attendance, behaviour and graduation rates.
