For immediate release

Date: February 20, 2018


From quantity to quality


Education 20/20 Inc. is hosting a conversation on quality education on Thursday March 1, 2018 at the Inspire Centre in Summerside. The session will be held from 12:00 – 1:00 pm; the public is invited to attend. Attendees are also invited to bring a sandwich and coffee will be available. In case of inclement weather, readers should check the Inspire website for up-to-date information.


Education is a service industry; educators are in the business of helping others improve their levels of education. The system is intended to help Islanders learn the skills they need to cope with, enjoy and even thrive during life’s journey – new attitudes, skills and knowledge. Dr. Don Glendenning, President of Education 2020, who will lead the session, would like to see a clearer focus on services such as information, counselling, support for independent learners, and quality assurance. He notes that recommendations on quality put forth by international agencies such as the OECD and UNICEF, although relatively low cost, just seem to be ignored.


Institutions elsewhere are rethinking high stakes testing, introducing continuous progress, replacing school grades with levels of achievement, turning to external assessment bodies and recognizing that school autonomy and accountability are key to educational improvement. If institutions don’t change their ways, people will look elsewhere for help. It may even be time, he thinks, to fund services rather than institutions.


Education 20/20 Inc is an independent, broad based not for profit organization focused on the issue of improving the quality of education for all Canadians.
