Don argues that poor school attendance is a symptom rather than the primary problem within educational systems. While acknowledging the link between attendance and achievement, he notes that making attendance compulsory doesn't address root causes. He identifies underlying causes in home environments (health, income, food insecurity, transportation) and school contexts (lack of engagement or advocacy). Best practices for improving attendance include collaboration, incentives, family involvement, personalization, and support—all addressing systemic rather than compliance issues. The letter reframes attendance as a diagnostic indicator requiring investigation of deeper structural problems.
