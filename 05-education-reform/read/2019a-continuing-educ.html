Continuing Education in Souris

Souris/2019-A



Souris is an urban centre in eastern Prince Edward Island; Souris and the immediate area together make a community one of 3,798 people. The economy of the area is dominated by seasonal industries of fishing, aquaculture, agriculture and tourism.


In late 2018, Education 20/20 Inc., with financial help from the Canada- PEI Labour Market agreement, commissioned a formal survey of needs and opportunities for training leading to employment. Information gathering included a survey of existing institutions, interviews with employers and employer organizations, interviews with community leaders and government officials and a survey of the public at large. A copy of the report by MRSB: Assessment of Continuing Education and Workforce Development Needs in the Souris Area of PEI is available at www.Education2020.ca). The study engaged 2,313 households, an on-line survey, the local media and placed postrs in a number of popular locations. Business, employer and community groups were included.


The report identified a number of barriers to continuing education:

  • Lack of education and training opportunities

  • Inability to commute to other training locations

  • Timing of course offerings

  • Difficulty of accessing high speed internet services

  • Return on investment (Limited match between employment opportunities and training)

  • Lack of awareness of available training opportunities


As one would expect, training interests are wide ranging and include the fields of business, health, IT, various trades and occupations, and training in the French language Indigenous needs were noted but not separately identified.


Employers face the following challenges:

  • Shallow labour pool

  • Lack of training on specialized equipment coming into area

  • Lack of hands on training

  • Cost of travel o courses outside area

  • Lack of basic and advanced IT skills

  • Area specific matters such as a dwindling and aging population, relative isolation, seasonal industries, and the wage structure





Underlying issues:

Based on the report and ensuing discussions, Souris appears to be a community :

  • That lends itself to a multi-year project to develop and test an education system to best serve inhabitants of small, rural communities in transition.

  • Seeking more training for its citizens.

  • Where citizens have little voice in decision-making about local educational matters

  • Without an integrated education plan for he area

  • Where educators appear to operate in silos

  • Facing gaps in the delivery system – information, postsecondary offerings, interest courses, credit courses

  • Offering limited hands-on and practical training experience.


As bridge-building measures, the study recommends:

  • Better access to online training

  • Programs offer both classroom and hands- on training

  • Offer area specific skills such as fisheries and agriculture

  • Barriers be addressed

  • Other considerations including the matching of supply and demand, greater emphasis on employability skills, dwindling population, career planning and funding

  • Offer on-line courses.


Local agency

It is our considered view that a local agency should be recruited or established to lead the initiative. Leadership by the Town or a charitable agency would ensure consideration of local needs and could help with fund raising; Costs to be met by (1) in kind services, space, communication and administrative support services.; (2) Skills PEI providing both a Director and an office support person under a work placement program, and (3) additional operating funds through grants and fund-raising initiatives.

Role of the centre is to:

• Encourage and support education services for residents of the area

• Organize and host educational activities of interest to the community

• Represent the community’s interests in matters of education

• Liaise with all providers of educational services

• Seek financial support


Proposed Services:

  1. Continuing education - Self- funded (tuition and/or sponsorship) courses in any subject, for any age and at any level.

  2. Skill training modules (practical and hands on) in the form of a series of 8-week work entry level modules such aas literacy, numeracy, cabinetry, metal fabrication, IT and retail service.

  3. Credit courses modules offered by postsecondary institutions and trade and business societies such as PEI Construction Association, Tourism Industry Association of Prince Edward Island, PEI Business Women’s Association and STEM.


  • On-line courses through postsecondary institutions assist residents to identify and enroll in on-line courses consistent with their wishes,


  • Information and Counselling available to residents generally but especially to residents with an inclination or need for continuing education


  • Extended school day Support the Souris High school in its efforts to broaden its offerings to the public aat large


Staffing:

For the purpose of this paper, staffing would consist of:

  • Continuing education organizer

  • Counsellor

  • Instructors, various contract

Given the nature of the project work experience other than teaching, postsecondary education and supervisory experience an asset.

  • Bilingual an asset

  • Contract position


Community benefits to above suggestions:

  • Increase local training

  • Develops local capacity

  • Strengthens local social capital


Funding

Potential funders include:

  • SkillsPEI

  • Self supporting courses and services

  • Department of Education

  • Donors and sponsor

  • In kind services

  • Grants

  • Students in training
