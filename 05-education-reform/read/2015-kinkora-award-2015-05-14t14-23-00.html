For immediate release

Date: May 14, 2015

Student decisions count at Kinkora High


“Kinkora Regional High School was thrilled to receive the inaugural 20/20 Education Award in 2014” said Jaime Cole, Principal, and students were involved all the way from deciding how the money would be used to carrying out the changes. As the first step, students organized a survey to find out how the award money should be spent. “The number one area where the students wanted to see improvement was the cafeteria”, said Cole. Students wanted a “cozy corner”; they painted that corner with a colour of their choosing, and helped select a few pieces of furniture. Now they are creating some original pieces of art, and all will be unveiled at their Annual Coffee House.


The second project is to add a green space, an outdoor classroom so to speak, mostly for students in the agricultural program, but also available to students in other classes for projects that include outdoor activities. Tech students designed the space and other students and staff are now creating it. As part of their agricultural science program, students grow vegetables to be used in the school cafeteria by the school lunch program.


The award was created in 2014 by Education 20/20 Inc. to call the attention of the public to important characteristics of effective schools and to provide recognition to schools for their efforts to implement them. The award is based on the view that, when schools and communities work together, “student achievement improves, teacher morale rises and communication increases”. The focus of the award is on the daily operation of the school and on those matters over which a school has substantial control. Nominations for the 2015 award have now closed and submissions are being reviewed.
