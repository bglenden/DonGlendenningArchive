Final system upgrade proposal transforming PEI from a school-focused system to a comprehensive education system serving all Islanders. Glendenning outlines 23 specific actions including clarified terminology and purpose, competency-based programs, holistic assessment, school autonomy, community collaboration, education association creation, and competitive benchmarking with Atlantic provinces.

SYSTEM UPGRADE 8

Doc 5


PEI currently operates a number of school system focused on serving young people in school settings; suggestions below would see it upgraded to a full education system available to and capable of serving all Islanders.

Action

  1. Review and confirm terminology

  2. Clarify purpose(s)

  3. Review and restate Role for Department of Education as - ”Encourage, support and regulate education services”

  4. Identify functions/services

  5. Replace current school system with a full education system

  6. Create a structure based on education functions

  7. Clarify accountabilities

  8. Increase autonomy and accountabilities of schools

  9. Base programs on competencies

  10. Institute holistic assessment based on Bloom’s taxonomy

  11. Institute competency/outcome- based tracking of students

  12. Engage in collaborative activities with community

  13. Establish an Education Council.

  14. Create a PEI Education Association

  15. Become an information source for community

  16. Increase staff training

  17. Increase aAutonomy and accountability of schools

  18. Improve tracking of student learning

  19. Report progress at competency by competency level

  20. Make learning guides available to learners

  21. Focus on education not schools

  22. Stablish community collaboration and partnerships

  23. Adopt holistic rating scales

  24. Education system

  25. Collaborate with sisyteer provinces on an Atlantic forum (See South Carolina model)

  26. Create and issue individual learning profiles

  27. Outsource support as appropriate.
