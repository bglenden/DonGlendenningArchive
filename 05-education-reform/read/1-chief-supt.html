Inspired by a presentation by Dr. Heather Morrison, Chief Health Officer for PEI, Don proposes establishing a Chief Education Officer position focused solely on improving education quality. He notes historical precedent: New Brunswick's Department of Education once had separate Deputy Minister (administration) and Chief Superintendent (programming) roles; PEI had a Chief Superintendent until at least 1930. He recommends "Director" over "Superintendent" to reflect education's breadth beyond schools, aligning with modern educational scope.

January 12, 2016


Editor,


Dear Sir: 

PEI needs a chief education officer


As I write, I am reflecting on a presentation earlier today by Dr. Heather Morrison, Chief Health Officer for Prince Edward Island. Not surprising, the presentation on Island health matters was both interesting and informative.


While listening, my mind wandered back to my early teaching days in New Brunswick when the Department of Education had both a Deputy Minister and a Chief Superintendent; the Deputy Minister, as I recall, handled administrative matters and the Chief Superintended dealt with programming.  PEI, and perhaps other Provinces, had a Chief Superintendent as well at least up to 1930.


Given the current concerns about education, it may be time to establish a new senior position that of Chief Education Officer whose sole role would be to improve the quality of education in PEI.  I suggest the Director rather than Superintendent in recognition of the fact that so much of our education occurs outside our school system. 


Sincerely,

Don Glendenning

A long-time student of education
