Poor diet: More than a school problem


The PEI Home and School is right to call attention to the impact of poor diet on a child’s education but it is more than just a school problem; poor diet is also a contributor to obesity, our level of health, ability to go about our daily living. Studies also show that food insecurity contributes to one’s feelings of security and self-worth. In fact, UNICEF identifies learners who are healthy and well nourished as one of the characteristics of a quality school.


According to Dr. Don Glendenning, President of Education 20/20 Inc., thinks that diet is “primarily a health and wellness issue” but one that has implications, not only for schooling, but also for the health services, recreation and competitive sports, recreation and youth group activities generally. While schools should participate in programs to improve diet, it is not their role to lead such initiatives nor to have the cost added to their operating budget. In Glendenning’s view, “It is another example of schools taking on a problem outside their mandate.”
