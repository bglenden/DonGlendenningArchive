Lengthy letter to editor (D-22--4) on "EDUCATION FUNCTIONS." Don outlines typical education system functions: information and counselling, governance and leadership, instruction, quality assurance, products and technology, funding, and human resources. Critiques PEI's confusion of education with schooling, notes lack of benchmarks, and discusses structural issues. References OECD, UNESCO, World Bank on education structure. Proposes education should serve all ages and offer separate services (not just schools). Compares education system to health system model. Represents his most systematic articulation of education system design.

Dear Editor:

EDUCATION FUNCTIONS

D-22--4

An education system is a group of services put in place by a community to encourage and support the education of its citizens; not a lot has been written about education systems, however, typically education systems seem to serve the following functions:m


Information and Counselling

• Recent and independent information needed by Islanders in the making of decisions about their own education or the education of others

Governance and Leadership

• In the form of a body and organization with vision and responsibility to put in place and monitor educational services for the benefit of its citizens,

Instruction

• The provision of help to others in their learning that includes diagnosis, counselling, presentations, practice and access generally to resources

Quality Assurance

• A system that assures Islanders that their learning experiences, including providers of those services, meet community standards.

Products and Technology

• Access by Islanders to educational materials, technology and other resources to help learners develop skills needed to meet their responsibilities and objectives.

Funding

• Funding to a level and in a manner that is equitable for all Islanders.

Human Resources

• Teachers, administrators, counsellors, advisors. Assistants and other people who help




It is unclear to me, just what our goals and plans are for education on PEI. To a relative newcomer, we seem to confuse going to school with getting an education, benchmarks are missing, education has become a product rather than a service and there appears to be little interest in engaging with the people served.


Education’s purpose has been described as helping others gain the skills, knowledge and other attributes needed to flourish in a democratic society. We get our education by learning, We need to learn in order to survive; we need to learn more or better in order to thrive. One’s education is a stage or level of development; a good education has been described as “a state that allows a person to adequately cope with all demands of daily life.” Is this also the basis of a high school graduation certificate? But we also need people who can lead and care for this community we call home. Our community believes that it important for people, especially young people, to learn things that will help them in later life; school attendance for our children, therefore, is compulsory. Just what is our commitment to each person we accept into the school system?
