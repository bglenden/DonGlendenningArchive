Education 20/20 Inc.


Some Notes on Governance and Organization



We spoke on occasion about adding additional Directors; I agree. It is time to act.  Here are some thoughts for your consideration.  They may provide a useful starting point for our discussions


Functions

No doubt you are aware of the dictum that form follows function.  For this reason, I’ve attached a preliminary functional analysis.



Number of Directors

Our Act allows for xx directors; I am not sure that we need that number at the moment but we could use a few more.


Composition of Board

CFPEI tries to make the board representative of the community.  Since our focus is on providing a quality education, I assume that we should ensure a variety of points of view stakeholders: students, parents, educators, employers, postsecondary institutions, east and west, etc.  The Home and Sch0ol Association states that the majority of Directors shall not be employed in education


Selection of Directors


In the case of the Community Foundation, I recommended a selection committee made up of the chief Justice as Chair and the Mayors of Charlottetown and Summerside, as members.  Some community foundations continue to use a similar arrangement for board recruitment.  What would you think of striking a committee to select new Directors for Education 20/20 Inc.?



Fundy Foundation has a board Recruitment Committee consisting of six members including two current directors; the chair of that committee is external.  Both Moncton and the Nova Community Foundation, I believe, still have external nominating committees.  Such an approach, I believe, keeps the "ownership" with the community.  I am not sure if this applies to Education 20/20 or not?


If we adopted this approach, each year before the annual meeting, the Committee would meet with the Board to:

Confirm vacancies 

Learn strengths needed 

Review long range goals and plans, and 

Other relevant matters


The Recruitment Committee would then: 

Seek out potential appointees, 

Conduct initial interviews

Present candidates at annual meeting


Advisors

From time to time it may be useful to have access to the advice of prominent citizens on new directions or major initiatives.   Fundy Community Foundation accomplishes this through a group of seven advisors.  There role seems not well specified but my discussion with the Exec Dir. suggests that they are well respected citizens whose opinion is valuable.  Having such a group also adds credibility. Such a group would very seldom be involved in a current decision making but, perhaps, would meet one a year over dinner to be briefed and to offer advice on matters of import to both Education 20/20 and to education.
