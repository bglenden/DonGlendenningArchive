Strategic analysis identifying intervention points for Education 20/20 to address provincial education gaps. Concludes that the Department of Education is unlikely to drive needed changes and proposes focusing on achievement levels, key skills, and supplementary support services.
Education Philosophy & Reform


Intervention points

Planning

April, 2016


Conclusions:

  • Department of Education unlikely to make the desired changes

  • Unlikely that we can have a significant direct influence on the Department

  • Increase our chances of success if we can focus on real problems shared by other stakeholders

  • Cannot find support for the current curriculum development model

  • Establishing and certifying grade levels is not a teaching function

  • Parents and the public at large are not well served in the way of independent information


Intervention points

  1. Key skills- skills needed by all citizens regardless of the path of life pursued including – mathematics, communication (reading, writing, listening and speaking, science, self-management skills, a second language and a computer language.


  1. Achievement levels – establish achievement levels in key skills that are not related to age or grade of the learner and an assessment service for individuals wanting to use it.


  1. Supplementary support to learners of all ages including Information, assessment, counselling, supplementary instruction (short course, part time, night, seminars, workshops), surveys and learning management, assessment, instructional materials,


  1. Information and representation on issues and matters of – activities that inform and influence the public and educational decision makers including a regular newsletter, Webinar, Social and networking, Public presentations ,Media releases, Speakers bureau, Occasional papers, Feedback


Intervention strategies

  1. Build capacity of Education 20/20 Inc.

  2. Establish for profit “arm”

  3. Initiate off Island activities
