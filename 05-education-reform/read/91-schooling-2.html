Open Education

No. 91 -2 August 20, 2020

When schools and ministries are managed well, learning happens-World Bank


WORLD BANK

The World Bank invests heavily to help countries improve themselves; that includes Improving education. (I worked on one of their projects in Pakistan, a few years ago.) Their 2018 report on education suggests that the way we structure our system has an impact on the quality we deliver. i’d heard this first from PEI trustees a few years ago.


“Successful education reforms require good policy design, strong political commitment and effective implementation capacity; to quote

  • “At the central level, ministries of education need to attract and develop the multidisciplinary, technical and managerial expertise to design, and implement interventions based on evidence.

  • District or regional offices need the capacity and the tools to monitor and support schools.

  • At the school level, principals need managerial expertise to run schools, from planning the use of resources to managing their teaching workforce”


Their findings deserve consideration as PEI deliberates (again) on appointing school trustees

PROFILES

A recent report in the Australian media proposes that “high school students focus on building a learner profile that reflects the skills and knowledge acquired during their school years “ While learning pro-files are not new, including out-comes mastered is a major change and would go a long way to solving the problem of transition to both work and further studies.
