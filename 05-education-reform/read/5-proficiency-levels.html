Ladders of educational achievement

Phase One – Constructing the Ladder

5

A Background:

Views on current system of recognizing student achievement at the public school level:

  • Based on outcomes of uncertain origin and from an unclear process

  • Standards for goods and services are normally set by a body other than the producer or provider

  • Show little connection to community based criteria

  • Reflect eroding public school standards

  • PEI standings on large scale assessment (PISA, PCAP, ??)

  • Employers actively exploring alternative selection criteria

  • Perceived conflict between teaching and grading functions - ihe argument has been made that separating the teaching and qualification functions will reduces bias, improves consistency, and reduces teacher workload.

  • A low level of basic skills not only blocks entrance to postsecondary education but may also lower skill levels of people who graduate.

  • Lack of externally referenced criteria

  • School centered and, therefore, local in application

  • Frequently confused with age-based grouping

  • Rise of technology-aided instruction and assessment


Examples of alternative recognition strategies:

  • Apprenticeship where schools train but Departments of Labour certify,

  • The Canadian Language authority,

  • International Baccalaureate Program

  • Canadian Conservatory of Music.

  • Many countries have Qualification Authorities

  • French language program

B Proposal:

  • To identify rungs of a mathematics ladder a laddered approach to recognizing student achievement


Steps

  • Secure funding

  • Establish steering committee

  • Undertake literature search

  • Prepare analysis

  • Develop rating scale

  • Establish levels

  • Establish application profiles


Deliverables:

  • An analysis of the field of mathematics

  • A rating scale

  • A minimum of 5 levels

  • A minimum of 3 applications – chemical technology, actuarial science and business

  • A proposal for exploring an independent assessment centre/service


Impact/benefits:

  • Prevents grade inflation

  • Removes uncertainty about levels

  • Increases personal mobility

  • Reduces teacher workload

  • Provides basis for curriculum development
