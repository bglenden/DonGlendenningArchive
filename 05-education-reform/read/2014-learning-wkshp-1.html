Don's conceptual framework for a workshop on "A learner's perspective on learning" under Education 20/20. The proposal emphasizes stakeholder engagement, brainstorming learning supports, and creating flowcharts of learner access to educational resources. It exemplifies Don's pedagogical thinking—moving beyond traditional instruction to understand learner needs holistically.

EDUCATION 20/20 INC.


Workshops on learning

Background

It is important for l stakeholders to have a framework for dealing with education matters; central to that framework is the learner and learning.  For stakeholders, other than teachers although they should not be excluded, I am thinking about a workshop that might run something like this:


Title: A learner’s perspective on learning  


Format:

  • Opening comments about attitudes, skills and knowledge
  • Open brain storming session to identify ways to support learning: instruction, feedback, information, etc.  Select the most top 7-8 and make them visible for the remainder of the evening.
  • Break into round tables and draw a “flow chart” of a learners access to or use of the various supports
  • Post on wall for all participants to view and discuss
  • Later convene a small working group, perhaps of facilitators, to prepare a chart for publication
