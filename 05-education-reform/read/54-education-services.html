Newsletter article (Issue 54) edited by Don Glendenning defining education as a service industry. He outlines core education functions (information, instruction, diagnosis, tutoring, guided practice, feedback, materials/technology, quality assurance) and school-specific functions. Don argues that grading should not be a school function due to conflicting roles between teaching and sorting, and proposes that schools report progress while independent bodies handle official assessment.

Education services


Education is a service industry. A functional analysis would likely produce the following list:

  • Information

  • Instruction

  • Diagnosis,

  • Tutoring, mentoring or coaching

  • Guided practice

  • Feedback

  • Materials and technology

  • Quality assurance


There’s a question as to whether grading is an education function; I have my doubts so I’ve left it off the list.


Departments of Education generally have delegated the instructional function to institutions; again, I did not find a standard list but these are the school functions that the literature suggests:

  • Presentations

  • Facilitation

  • Diagnosis,

  • Tutoring, mentoring, coaching

  • Guided practice

  • Formative assessment

  • Information

  • Guidance and counselling


In practice, schools provide a grading function; should the practice be continued? Probably not on the grounds that:

  • Teaching and sorting are conflicting roles.

  • Combining the roles increases the chances of bias.

  • In most circumstances, the sorting function is carried out by a third party.


Schools should report student progress but I’ve left grading off my list of school functions.
