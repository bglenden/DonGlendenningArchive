Brief reference notes on student engagement drawing from Willms and OECD research. Defines engagement as students feeling they belong, accepting broader societal values, and participating in school affairs. Notes measures include student feelings and attendance, varies by age, associates with higher achievement. Notes schools with higher literacy show strong disciplinary climate, good student-teacher relations, and high expectations. Identifies engagement as integral to instructional design reflecting school and teacher culture. Documents engaged students more likely to continue education and find employment, contributing to health, wellbeing, and quality of life. Lists factors supporting engagement: strong core programs, elimination of tracking/streaming, parental involvement, strong discipline, autonomy, authentic evaluation, curriculum reform. - **Key People**: Willms (OECD) - **Key Organizations**: OECD - **Tags**: EDUCATION, PROFESSIONAL, HOLLAND_COLLEGE - **Eulogy Relevance**: MEDIUM - Reference notes on engagement research supporting educational practice.
