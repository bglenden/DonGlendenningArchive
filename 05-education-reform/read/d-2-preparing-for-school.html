Don challenges the concept of young children "preparing for school," questioning whether 4-6 year-olds should adapt to schools or whether schools should adapt to developmental stages. He argues that other institutions (churches, doctors' offices, farms) accommodate visiting children, so why not schools? This letter reflects a child-centered educational philosophy and questions whether rigid school preparation demands are developmentally appropriate. While Don notes this isn't his expertise area, he suggests schools have greater obligation to adapt their practices to childhood development than children have to pre-adapt to institutional structures.
