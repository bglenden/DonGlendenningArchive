October 20, 2019 letter to Charlottetown Guardian commenting on Premier's mandate letters. October 20, 2019

102

Editor,

Charlottetown Guardian

Charlottetown, PEI


Dear Sir: 

Education mandate letter


I commend the Premier for his recent mandate letters but, with respect, I suggest that, at least the education letter, is unlikely to produce  the desired results.  A number of statements simply emphasize political events (establishment of a task force,  work with stakeholders and review …) rather than  pointing to the desired end.   For example, ensuring graduates are “properly trained for employment” is an important objective; achieving that  objective may demand better curriculum, more experienced instructors, extending program length, different assessment criteria, and not just a new task force.


My view of these matters reflect my experience at Holland College which, as I recall, based its early development on some 12 to 14 foundational beliefs.


Sincerely


Don Glendenning

A long-time student of education
