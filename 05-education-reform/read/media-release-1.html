A new direction for education award


The 2017 Education Award offered by Education 20/20 Inc. will recognize a community organization or agency for its efforts to improve the education of its citizens. The first two awards focused on engagement and were awarded to Kinkora Regional High School (2014) and Prince Street Elementary School (2015). The 2016 award went to Grace Christian School for the manner and extent to which it demonstrated the application of technology in support of student learning


Community groups are invited to send submissions, not over 3 pages in length; projects should be community led, address an actual community education issue, include activities that would be undertaken if resources allowed and be based on best practices and innovative thinking. Details are available on the Education 20/20 website, www.education2020.ca; nominations close on April 30, 2017.


+“It takes a community to educate a citizen,” says Dr. Don Glendenning, President of Education 20/20 Inc., “so we want to hear from communities”. The 2017 Award will recognize educational services initiated by a community organization other than schools and learning centres.
