A brief letter proposing that education services be explicitly identified and promoted to both institutional and independent learners. Don lists six core services from internet research: information, counseling, diagnosis, learning products and technology, instruction, and assessment. He observes these services are available to school-enrolled students but should be equally accessible to independent learners. The letter advocates expanding education service accessibility beyond traditional school settings, supporting his broader theme that education systems should serve all citizens, not only school-enrolled youth.
Education Philosophy & Reform

Dear Editor:

Education Services

In health care, we encounter a number of services; in education, we simply go to school. Seems to me that it would be both interesting and helpful to identify education services as well. My internet search suggests the following:

  • Information
  • Counselling
  • Diagnosis
  • Learning products and technology
  • Instruction, and
  • Assessment

I realize that these services are available to students in schools; I think that they should also be readily to independent learners. 

Sincerely

Don Glendenning
