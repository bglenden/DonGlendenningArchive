Thank you letter dated December 16, 2012, to Roger Birt expressing gratitude for his foundational support. Details organization evolution from initial meetings to federal incorporation as Education 20/20 Inc., including logo development, website construction, and Canadian Revenue Agency charitable status application. Lists accomplishments: early childhood development workshops, engagement software testing, school excellence award program, educator dialogue initiatives, and publication planning. Shows transformation from informal initiative to formal charitable organization within eight months of initial contact with Birt.

Education 20/20 Inc.

Mr. Roger Birt, President

R. Birt and Associates

27 Brackley Point Road

Dear Mr. Birt:


This note comes to thank you for your support in our efforts to bring about change in our public education system. 


First, however, let me emphasize again the importance of education both for individuals and for the future of our community and country.  Doing more of the same is not an answer; we need a fundamental change in thinking about what constitutes an education and the role of schools.  We know at least some of the steps that must be taken, more autonomy for schools, less bureaucracy, greater freedom and accountability for teachers, more attention to the preschool years, greater choice among providers and changes in standards and reporting.    


Since we last spoke, we have held a dozen or more meetings with small groups on a variety of educational topics with educators and the public to explore mutual concerns and interests.   As a result, we now have a cadre of people who share our philosophy, who understand the need for change and are willing to help make change happen.  We are also federally incorporated as Education 20/20 Inc., are working on a logo and website, and are now seeking charitable status with Revenue Canada.  In addition, we 

  • Organized a workshop in Early Childhood Development during which a panel of front line practitioners, shared information and their experiences about approaches that work for young children; participants included other early childhood workers, parents, educators and members of the community at large.  We plans include workshops in other Island communities.
  • Are preparing to test of a new software package, developed in Canada, to assess the range and nature of school- community engagement..  This test is being undertaken because there is a direct relationship exists between student achievement and community engagement.  
  • Are launching  an award to an Island school that best exemplifies a high level of engagement in its every day school activities; criteria, policies and procedures are now being drafted.
  • Commenced dialogue with educators, especially decision makers, about educational issues, with reading skills of high school graduates being at the top of our list.
  • Intend to gather, produce and publish information of interest to the public at large.


You were our initial supporter and we thank you for both your encouragement of our efforts and your financial support – and we’ll keep you posted on our progress.

  

Enjoy a great family Christmas and warmest wishes for the New Year. 


Sincerely


Don Glendenning
