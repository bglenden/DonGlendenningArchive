School Autonomy

An OECD report, Pisa in Focus, shows a direct link between PISA test results and school autonomy; Citation


Simply stated, there is a direct relationship between school performance and school autonomy. In the words of the OECD Report:

  • “In countries where schools have greater autonomy over what is taught and how students

are assessed, students tend to perform better.

  • In countries where schools account for their results by posting achievement data publicly, schools that enjoy greater autonomy in resource allocation tend to show better student performance than those with less autonomy.

  • In countries where there are no such accountability arrangements, schools with greater autonomy in resource allocation tend to perform worse.”


Autonomy, or freedom to act, varies from country to country but may include such things as allocation of resources, hiring and dismissing teachers, formulating budgets, decisions about curricula and assessment, course offerings and textbooks. Ideally, schools should have autonomy to resolve all learning matters as they pertain to students


PEI principals

The role of school principals is described in The PEI Schools Act, however, the degree of freedom to act is not clear. It appears thwt their scope of practice is quite limited The following are clearly spelled out:

  • educational leadership in the school,

  • ensures that educational programs as described are carried out,

  • Evaluation of teachers

  • Maintain discipline

  • Foster cooperation within the school

  • Maintain order

  • Consult teachers

  • Attend to health, comfort and safety of students

  • Carry out such other duties as the Superintendent, School Board or minister may assign


So what do schools do with their autonomy?

  • Some schools continue as they always have.


  • One school decided to do without a principal and hired an administrator instead, similar to some professional offices

  • A school in Arizona received agreement from the teachers union to convert seven teaching position to 14 teaching assistant positions thus dramatically increasing the adult student ratio

  • A high school introduced alternative approach to grouping students so that all students studied math during the first period of the day, each in a class dealing with the mat needed


What sort of autonomy would be supportive of education?


  • All decisions about an ondividual student should be made by professional staff and at the school level

  • Accountability for student learning

  • Communicate regularly with local community

  • Delegate to lowest level

  • Engage teachers in process
