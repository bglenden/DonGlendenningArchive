Education 20/20 proposal to develop math ladder and establish independent assessment service for certification of mathematics performance levels. Addresses concerns about PEI graduates lacking basic skills for post-secondary or employment, inadequate report cards and diplomas, and employer difficulty in assessing applicant competency. Proposes fee-based independent testing service (open to any age, voluntary, no school credit) that provides certificate of achievement level. Outlines characteristics: independent, individual-based, results confidential, optional employer/school notification. Includes philosophical framework on competency-based assessment and institutional coordination. Math   

EDUCATION 20/20 INC.


Math Stages -1

Background

Concerns about literacy levels of Islanders have been widely reported: 

  • PISA results
  • CFPEI survey
  • Charlottetown Area chamber
  • Home and School
  • Education 20/20 workshops
  • Employers concerns
  • PEI Literacy Alliance


Of particular concern is the cohort of recent graduates and early leavers who “finished high school” and not qualified to enter post-secondary studies or secure employment because of inadequate level of basic skills.  The usefulness of report cards and high school diplomas in describing or attesting to skill levels has long been a problem for employers.  The situation exacerbated because of the practice of social promotion.


Proposal

Develop a math ladderTo establish an independent assessment service for the purpose of certifying a person’s level of performance in mathematics.


Characteristics of Service

  • Fee for service
  • Independent
  • Open to individuals
  • Any age 
  • By application
  • No school credit offered
  • Certificate not a credential
  • Results provided to testee only
  • Copy of results forwarded to employer or educational institution on request

Work plan

  • Review the literature
  • Form work team


Issues:  

  • Math or arithmetic
  • Specific math skills or facility in math
  • Level:  Citizens math, employment math, essential skills, high school graduation
  • Prepare a brochure
  • Essential skills or basic skills




To place our proposal in an educational perspective, we are of the view that:

  • Educational achievement can and should be measured in terms other than time spent on a subject or in school.
  • Credentials are no longer an adequate instrument to describe depth or breadth of study in a subject or field. 
  • The practice of having teachers act as both coach and referee of their own students is unfair to students.
  • Math is studied at all grade levels, and in both College and UPEI; it would be advantageous to students, therefore, if all institutions and accreditation agencies operated from the same master list of competencies.
