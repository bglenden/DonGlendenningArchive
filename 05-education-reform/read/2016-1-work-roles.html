Dated 2016, this document outlines work roles within Education 20/20 including newsletter, awards, promotion, workshops, math skills surveys, occasional papers, operations, small projects, transition projects, and presentations. Don's involvement spans multiple areas including leadership of surveys, coordination with other directors, and responsibility for communications and strategic initiatives—reflecting his central administrative role in the organization.



Work Roles

2016-1

Planning


No.

ROLES






Newsletter - to inform readership on matters of education

Duties: determine and write content of each issue, format, arrange for translation, distribute, maintain distribution list, respond to comments and enquiries,



Education awards – to encourage and recognize best practice in education on PEI

Duties - Identify and select particular purpose, develop description, prepare promotional material, distribute to 20/20 mailing list, develop promotional materials, arrange posting on website; chair selection committee; prepare rating sheets; communicate with winners and losers; secure banner; arrange presentation; organize photo op



Promotion – ongoing promotion of Education and the work of Education 20/20

Duties: prepare, arrange, organize photo ops, press releases and presentations



Workshops – inform groups or the public at large of education matters

Identity topics and recruit speakers; arrange for facilities; arrange room, introduce workshop leader; stimulate discussion, prepare media releases, issue thanks



Survey of math skills– add to our knowledge of education on PEI.

Develop instrument, test, administer, administer survey instruments, record data, prepare reports; distribute information, expect preliminary report in December; other surveys planned



Occasional papers – to encourage development through writing and to inform our readership

Only published 5 to date; seeking other authors



Operations – to ensure a properly functioning organization.

Administrative: pick up mail, maintain files, prepare and copy documents, organize regular and annual meetings, handle correspondence

Financial: maintain financial records

Solicit funds – prepared two requests for funds under Canada 150 Fund, made a number of asks for ongoing funding and for education award, liaise with donor






Small Projects - promote innovation at the community level and recognize its leaders

Only project so far is a “horticulture project” in Souris; met in Souris with project leaders; assisted in the development of a proposal; liaise with donor; facilitating flow of funds, hoping to expand beyond horticulture;












Transition project: to improve the process of transition from secondary school to post secondary education and wo work

Duties: Using mathematics s a pilot project to identify problems of transition and explore ways of improving the process; still in discussion stage






Self study of education - Increasing personal knowledge and development of education and our education system

matters through reading, writing and talking with others; examples include the learning path, framework for decision making; a couple of conclusions – educational counselling is not a school function, summative testing is not a school function



Presentations – occasional presentations to bodies that set direction and establish policies
