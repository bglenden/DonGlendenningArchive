Invitation to November stakeholder meeting on educational preparedness gaps for Island youth, based on Community Foundation of PEI's "Vital Signs" survey findings regarding low literacy and numeracy outcomes. The invitation references preliminary discussions on preparedness conducted by Education 20/20 Inc. and invites key players to explore the issue affecting youth transition from secondary education to postsecondary study and employment.

1-3-2

Invitation


In late 2013, the Community Foundation of Prince Edward Island conducted a survey of some two hundred young people about the issues facing Island youth.  the report found that ""literacy and numeracy outcomes are still low, and there are 'preparedness gaps' with regard to readiness for post
secondary study and the workplace."  The report, Vital Signs, is available on the Foundation's website, www.cfpei.ca; a report on a series of discussions on the report  convened by Education 20/20 Inc. is included with this invitation.  In recent weeks, I've had a number of informal conversations with Islanders and find the issue still of public concern.

You are invited you to join other key players on Wednesday, November 25 to explore the issue, especially as it relates to Island youth.
