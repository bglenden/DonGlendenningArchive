Education Philosophy & Reform

February 17, 2014


Over the past few months, I’ve been working with a small group of Islanders who believe that education is important, that it needs some changes and that the key to change is an informed and engaged public. We are also of the view that the school system is unlikely to make the necessary changes by itself; so, we’ve established an independent group, Education 20/20 Inc. (Mission statement attached). Our initial strategy is three pronged, independent information, engagement and incentives, with the objective of encouraging changes that will improve the quality of education for all.


  • Independent information: Most of our information about education comes from the people who operate the school system; to address the shortage and lack of independence, Education 20/20 Inc. produces a monthly electronic newsletter; in addition, our website has just gone live and we plan to use it as an information hub for surveys, announcements, occasional papers, social media, etc. (www.education2020.ca)


  • Engagement: Having information is one thing but understanding, internalizing and using it is another. As a first step, we’ve launched a series of workshops across PEI, the first was held on January 20 in the Stratford Town Hall, was well attended. A repeat is scheduled for Montague on February 18 and dates have been set for others. Our thinking is to encourage discussions on educational issues of all kinds in many Island communities. (As one participant pointed out, the very fact that an independent organization convened such a meeting is a breakthrough)


  • Awards and incentives: The education sector lack incentives – students cannot finish early; there is little encouragement to innovate, principals have little autonomy to make local adjustments; teachers are paid for what they know rather than what they do, each school is a cookie cutter of all other schools, and parents have little choice in choosing a school and none in choosing a teacher. As an incentive to greater engagement, and thanks to a generous donor, Education 20/20 will soon announce an educational award for a school or learning centre that best demonstrates engagement in its everyday activities; we plan to expand our incentive program into other areas and beyond the borders of PEI.


Our pressing need is for help in putting relevant information onto our website and into the public domain; our longer term horizon, however, includes facilitators to support engagement processes in local communities, an expanded incentives program, technology based micro clips on key education concepts, math clubs for students, professional support for teachers at all levels, creativity workshops, student competitions, promotion, and surveys,


Our group cannot make this happen alone; funds are needed first to continue the initiatives mentioned above, but also to develop new ones. Our current needs are modest, our short term goal for support of the above initiatives is $22,000 broken out as follows:


  • Gathering and disseminating information $15,000.

  • Promoting and awarding incentives $ 2,000

  • Workshops $ 2,500

  • Operational support $ 2,500

Total $22,000


The Community Foundation of Prince Edward Island supports our work and, in fact, administers our funds. Contributions to Education 20/20 Inc., therefore, should be directed to the Foundation (Community Foundation of Prince Edward Island, 119 Queen Street, Charlottetown, PE C1A 4B3) who will issue charitable receipts. I’m enclosing some background information about Education 20/20 Inc. and would be happy to provide additional information if needed; you may also want to browse out website.


Ensuring a quality education for every Canadian requires new thinking, new approaches and new partners. We have hope that you will join us in making the change.


Sincerely



Don Glendenning

President
