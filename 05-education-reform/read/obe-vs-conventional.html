Comparative framework contrasting Outcome-Based Education (OBE) with conventional education across multiple dimensions: learning climate (self-directed vs. teacher-directed; formal vs. informal; mutually respectful vs. authority-oriented), planning (participatory vs. teacher-directed), diagnosis (mutual vs. teacher assessment), goal-setting (negotiated vs. teacher-determined), learning design (projects vs. content units), activity types (inquiry vs. transmission), and evaluation (mutual vs. teacher assessment). This systematic comparison reveals Don's clear preference for learner-centered, collaborative OBE approaches and his critique of traditional authoritarian models.
