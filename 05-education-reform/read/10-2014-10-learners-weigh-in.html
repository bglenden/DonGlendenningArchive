Date: October 23, 2014


Learners weigh in


A youth panel weighed in on the support they find helpful in learning new things. Not surprising, they neither want nor need the same thing at the same time, and that’s the dilemma for schools, and a frustration for teachers as well as students.


As part of an initiative by Education 20/20 to diagram the learning path, panel members were asked to list things that helped with their learning; not surprising, the first thing mentioned was information, information that provides an overview of the subject and also a context; for some the initial information came via the internet and for others it came from lectures. Other kinds of help included assessment and feedback, early attempts to perform the skill such as drafting an outline of a report, and reflecting on their own performance. Panelists, like classes themselves, exhibited a wide range of views on both the problem and its resolution.


Panellists confirmed that many, perhaps all, classes include students who already know the topic of the day, students for whom the topic is new, and others with little if any interest or background for the day’s lesson. A couple of students mentioned the dilemma faced by teachers in trying to accommodate such a range of needs.


The panel, organized by Education 20/20, was chaired by Tanya Craig and included Callen Cudmore, Ryan Rioux, Iain Cudmore and Stephanie Mayne.
