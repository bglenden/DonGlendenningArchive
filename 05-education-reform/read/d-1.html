About Education

Our futures, as individuals and as a community, depend, to a large degree, on the nature and level of education available to, and actually attained by, our citizens. Education helps us to develop our individual potential to the fullest and prepares us for our many life roles.(See box on right). This means engaging in a variety of activities with a view to assembling a variety of skills, knowledge and attitudes supportive of functioning in those life roles.









Some think that education and schooling are the same. I am often asked, for example, where did you get your education? The information being sought is really what university or universities did you attend. I have also hear education described as what we remember after we leave school. Education may be defined everything that we learn in our lifetime that has a formative effect on us; it starts at birth, including the first “Da Da”. Some of our most important learning occurs in the first few year of life; what we learn in the first few years determines, to a large degree, what we become in later life.

Why Education 20/20 Inc.

What

  • We are a national not-for-profit organization whose purpose is to encourage and support a quality education for every Canadian.

  • We are governed by a volunteer Board of Directors, people who have demonstrated an interest in and knowledge of education but are not representing any particular organizations or institutional point of view.

Why

  • Education is important, our future as individuals and as a community will be shaped, in a large measure by the nature, level and quality of education we make available to our citizens.

  • Education needs to serve people of all ages, public or private institutions, training and development provided by employers or organizations for their members, and independently organized learning activities.

Who

  • We are an unaligned public organization of citizens whose purpose is to inform, educate, provoke discussion, encourage, and support quality education and lobby for change when the evidence warrants it.

  • We operate independently, but cooperatively, with other organizations in a transparent manner based on sound business practices.

How

  • Taking a leadership role in advocating for a quality education for all

  • Undertaking and sponsoring research, studies and innovative projects

  • Producing, disseminating information and opinion on educational matters

  • Encouraging public engagement in education through organizing, discussion groups, conferences, seminars, workshops and round tables

  • Fostering understanding & cooperation among stakeholders & citizens

  • Promoting high standards of education and well-being for people of all ages




Bring about change by:

  • Increase the availability of public information

  • Making awards

  • Recognizing excellence

  • Encouraging engagement

  • Encouraging innovation

  • Offering workshops and information sessions

  • Supporting research






Why this newsletter



Learning is a partnership and nowhere is that partnership more important than in improving the reading level of every citizen.





Based on this thinking, the quality of one’s education would be measured by how well one is able to function in the various life roles.




Key to success in education is reading, the golden skill, and a tool for lifelong learning. Our record is not good even though improving.Statistics reveal the magnitude of the task. We are aware of the attention given by our Ministers of Education in Atlantic Canada to increasing literacy rates, including reading, and this increased attention appears to be paying dividend. Reading is a community problem; it cannot be solved by schools alone. Early childhood development sets the stage for reading.


We recognize that learning to read, and learning to read better, occurs in the context of learning to communicate generally and includes learning to listen, to write and to speak. We also believe, however, that it is important to understand each component separately, and propose to commence our examination with the field of reading.


Education System


We believe that an education system should:

  • Focus on needs and interests of individual learners

  • Recognize educational experiences gained outside schools

  • Include engagement of learners, parents and the public at large.

  • Encourage and recognize independent learning.

  • Provide schools with significant autonomy and teachers with maximum professional freedom and accountability.
