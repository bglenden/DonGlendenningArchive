Background

Degrees had been around for nearly a thousand years and grew out of the system of guilds; having a degree certified that each holder had reached the guild status of master. Baccalaurate degrees appeared later and meant that holders had completed some basic studies on the way to becoming a master. Not surprising, public schools followed the pattern, were viewed as an earlier level of preparation culminating in a high school diploma. The matter of marking and grading, it appears, was first used to differentiate students at Yale College in 1785; until that time students were listed in alphabetical order and valedictorians were chosen by their classmates. By the early 1800’s grading matured in percentages and averages as measures of achievement.


Schools today are community centres that serve as safe places for children while their parents work. They still provide, but are not limited to, instruction in a traditional disciplines but are expected to serve a wider range of personal and social needs. The result is a clash of expectations and considerable tension between schools and the community they serve. One of the results is a decline in the value of a high school graduation diploma as a measure of academic performance.


An even more fundamental issue, however, is the whether the function of teaching and ranking the students you teach are in conflict. It is our contention that:

  • There is a continuing demand for academic sorting

  • Sorting is not a school function and may not even be an education one.

  • Asking teachers to sort places them in a position of conflict


Proposal

To establish an independent assessment service to certify a person’s proficiency in selected subjects and fields.


Characteristics of Service

  • Identify levels on a field or subject area

  • Certify applicants level

  • Provide service to individuals and organizations

  • Provide assessment service to both providers and users

  • Independent of government

  • Open to individuals of any age

  • By application

  • Certificate not a credential

  • Results provided to person assessed and, on his request, to others

  • Partner with schools, colleges, private schools

  • Not limited to paper and pencil tests

  • Laddering effect

  • Outcomes based



Work plan

  • Removes bias

  • Improves consistency

  • Reduces teacher workload

  • Provide schools with freedom to innovate
