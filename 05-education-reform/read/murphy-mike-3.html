Update letter to Michael Murphy on Education 20/20's development following federal incorporation. Don reiterates the fundamental need for educational system change: autonomy for schools, less bureaucracy, teacher freedom and accountability, early childhood focus, greater choice, and revised standards. He reports progress including: dozens of meetings with educators, a cadre of committed change agents, federal incorporation as Education 20/20 Inc., logo and website development, and charitable status application. Activities underway include early childhood workshops, engagement assessment software testing, school awards program development, educator dialogue on reading skills, and public information initiatives. Don invites Murphy's continued support and updates him on the organization's evolution.

Dear Michael:


In view of our discussion of some months ago, this note comes to update you on our efforts to effect change in our public education system. 


First, however, let me emphasize again the importance of education both for individuals and for the future of our community and country.  Doing more of the same is not an answer; we need a fundamental change in thinking about what constitutes an education and the role of schools.  We know at least some of the steps that must be taken, more autonomy for schools, less bureaucracy, greater freedom and accountability for teachers, more attention to the preschool years, greater choice among providers and changes in standards and reporting.    


Since we last spoke, we have held a dozen or more meetings with small groups of educators and others to explore mutual concerns and interests.   As a result, we now have a cadre of people who share our philosophy, who understand the need for change and are willing to help make change happen.  We are also federally incorporated as Education 20/20 Inc., are working on a logo and website, and are now seeking charitable status with Revenue Canada.  In addition, we 

  • Organized a workshop in Early Childhood Development during which a panel of front line practitioners, shared information and their experiences about approaches that work for young children; participants included other early childhood workers, parents, educators and members of the community at large.  We plans include workshops in other Island communities.
  • Are preparing to test of a new software package, developed in Canada, to assess the range and nature of school- community engagement...  This test is being undertaken because there is a direct relationship exists between student achievement and community engagement.  
  • Are launching an award to an Island school that best exemplifies a high level of engagement in its every day school activities; criteria, policies and procedures are now being drafted.
  • Commenced dialogue with educators, especially decision makers, about educational issues, with reading skills of high school graduates being at the top of our list.
  • Intend to gather, produce and publish information of interest to the public at large.


We want you to know what we are up to and would certainly welcome your support.  If you have questions about either the organization or our activities, please do not hesitate to let me know.


Enjoy Christmas and warmest wishes for the New Year. 


Sincerely


Don Glendenning
