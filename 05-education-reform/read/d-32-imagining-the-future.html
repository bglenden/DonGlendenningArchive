Responding to the Charting the Way report, Don proposes a series of public engagement sessions under the "Education First" banner to help Islanders shape the future direction of education. The initiative aims to provide information, facilitate discussion, and present policy alternatives in a participatory, learner-centered, future-oriented manner. Sessions would be volunteer-led, held in libraries and community centres, include French-language options, and avoid formal political presence. Agendas incorporate welcome, process explanation, PowerPoint presentations, small group discussions, and reporting. This proposal demonstrates Don's trust in public wisdom, his commitment to inclusive dialogue beyond institutional settings, and his understanding that educational reform requires broad community participation and shared ownership.

EDUCATION FIRST

PUBLIC ENGAGEMENT

Series 1 – Shaping the Future of Education


Background: Charting the Way, the Report of the PEI Education Governance Commission, recommended a collaborative process to renew “the philosophy of education, vision, guiding principles, and goals” of the  education system, a system that would “develop and fulfill the potential of every student, to best prepare each of them  for their place as a contributing member of a civil society.”  

Objective: To engage Islanders in setting the future direction of education in Prince Edward Island

Proposal:  Organize a variety of activities throughout the Island to:

  • to provide information to all Islanders
  • engage Islanders in discussions
  • Present policy alternatives for an appropriate education system for our future

Principles: Sessions will be:

  • Participatory
  • Future oriented 
  • Learner centered
  • Public
  • Organized under the banner of Education First
  • Include sessions in French
  • Role of Education First is to facilitate, provoke discussion, report, moderate, 
  • Volunteer led
  • Preferably held in libraries and community centres rather than schools

Typical Session:

  • Evening
  • 2 hours
  • Include handout
  • Provide discussion questions
  • Public led
  • Politicians will be recognized but not invited to speak
  • List of participants 
  • Copy of any reports sent electronically to al

Agenda:   

  • Welcome
  • Explanation of process
  • Power point/presentation?
  • Move to tables
  • Discuss questions
  • Report 
  • Wrap up
  • Invite people to stay and continue conversation
