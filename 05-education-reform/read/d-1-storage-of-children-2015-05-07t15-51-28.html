Don responds to a New Zealand Herald article about schools' expanding role as childcare during working hours, proposing that schools now have a three-part mandate: education for citizenship, workforce development, and child supervision. He suggests this expanded role should be formally included in PEI's educational philosophy.


May 7, 2015


Editor,

Charlottetown Guardian

Charlottetown, PEI


Dear Sir:

The changing role of schools


The New Zealand Herald on Monday, April 13, 2015 carried an article by Matt Heath on “School: a great storage locker for our children”.   While it was a light hearted look at how to cope with having your children home during vacation or storm days, it does raise the issue of schools providing care for children while parents work.  Without doubt, family activities are organized around the expectation that schools will care for children during most working days.


If we agree with Mr. Heath, schools of the future will now have a three-part mandate:

Help every Islander develop the attitudes, skills and knowledge needed for citizenship

Help PEI develop the skills needed to secure the future of our community, and

Keep “kids housed, happy and out of harm's way all day” while parents work.


When our philosophy of education is being updated, the latter role should be part of the discussion. 

Sincerely,


Don Glendenning

A long-time student of education
