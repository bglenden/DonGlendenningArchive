Notes from Don's facilitation of Education 20/20 Workshop #1 on April 4, 2013, at Royalty Centre on learning and teaching. Documents lessons learned about workshop moderation, including need to manage discussion length, employ interventions to redirect conversation, and maintain focus on learners rather than system criticism. Reflects on attendance (three people) and includes discussion topics about teacher outcomes and employer concerns regarding student communication and work skills.
Education Philosophy & Reform

Workshop #1

Thursday April 4, 2013

Room 236, Royalty Centre

Learning and teaching


During the course of my conversations and discussions, a few people indicated  an interest in further and focused discussions.  I attempted to organize sessions at the Community room at the superstore, but they were stormed out.  I felt obligated to try again.  Sessions were publicized only to the Education 20/20 network


Three people turned up last night; we had a great discussion 


Lessons learned include:

  • I got absorbed in the discussion and let it continue too long
  • Other than some brief opening remarks, the discussion soon takes on a life of its own
  • The moderator needs to be ready with a few interventions to help change directions
  • Moderator should be ready with, what, who, where, why, when, how
  • Focus should be on learners and learning; it is too easy to slip into discussing the faults of the current system
  • Topics included:
  • Teachers do not seem to have outcomes in mind as they go about their daily work
  • Kim employs students and find they lack both communication and work skills
