Don's foundational assumptions about education: all students can learn at higher levels, all can be taught successfully, higher expectations should be universal with differentiated instruction, and every student needs an advocate. Reflects his optimistic, learner-centered educational philosophy.

1. Basic assumptions

 Every student can learn at significantly higher levels

 Every student can be taught successfully

 Higher expectations for every student are reflected in the curriculum content although instructional strategies may vary

 Every student and every preschool child needs an advocate preferably  parent
