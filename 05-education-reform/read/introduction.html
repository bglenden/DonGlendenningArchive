Primer (textbook)

Yo artists, a primer is the first coat, perhaps an undercoat, to ready the surface for the art to follow

A primer is usually associated with reading, providing the basics, basic elements of a subject; alphabet on which to build later skills

A basic text

The Latin Enschedé Abecedarium of the late 15th century, translated into English as the Salisbury Prymer, has been identified as the earliest example of a printed primer. It presented the alphabet and several Catholic prayers.[3]

Other historical examples of primers for children include The New England Primer (1680s) and McGuffey Readers (1836) in the US, and Bala Potam (Lessons for Children, 1850 & 1851) by Arumuka Navalar in Sri Lanka.
