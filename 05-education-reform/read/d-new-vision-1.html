Don states public education has lost its way due to poor vision and outdated organizational models. He criticizes the Department of Public Instruction as focused on maintenance rather than innovation and strategy. He calls for new mental models of education and delivery systems that create conditions for learning rather than maintaining status quo.


July 7, 2015


Editor,

Charlottetown Guardian

Charlottetown, PEI


Dear Sir: 

Education lacks a vision


Public education has lost its way.  It didn’t just happen this morning, or yesterday or even last year.  It’s lost sight of its purposes, is inward looking, inflexible and remote.  dged in its thinking. And this is accompanied by a fear dwells on the minutae of maintenance – adjusting course content, PD days, storm days, new technology and air quality in buildings and eng, engagement.  It must be disheartening to be a professional in the system.  Oh, I know that there many good people in the system; I’ve met some of them and heard about others.  Consider them pockets of excellence.  They should be protected and cherished.


The problem is sectoral; they are the result of a poorly defined vision and an outdated organizational model to support it.  One only has to look at the structure of the Department to realize that we do not have a Department of Education; in its place we have a Department of Public Instruction, and even with such a truncated version some elements are missing.  And it is a body committeed to maintaining the status quo
