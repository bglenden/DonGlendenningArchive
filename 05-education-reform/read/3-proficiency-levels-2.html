Implementation proposal for Phase One of laddered achievement levels development titled "Constructing the Ladder." Details seven-step implementation approach: secure funding, establish steering committee, undertake literature search, prepare field analysis, develop rating scale, establish levels, establish application profiles. Specifies deliverables: mathematics field analysis, rating scale, minimum five proficiency levels, three application profiles (chemical technology, actuarial science, business), and independent assessment centre proposal. Articulates benefits: prevents grade inflation, removes achievement uncertainty, increases personal mobility, reduces teacher workload, provides curriculum development foundation. Recommends Education 20/20 Inc. convene committee with named members (Kelli, Mike, Tess, Gordon, Tammy, Sweet, Regis, Whelan/DesRoches), seek $10,000 Windsor exploratory funding, and schedule common core workshop.

Ladders of educational achievement

Phase One – Constructing the Ladder


3

B Proposal:

  • To develop a laddered approach to recognizing student achievement that is independent of the age of the learner

Steps

  • Secure funding

  • Establish steering committee

  • Undertake literature search

  • Prepare analysis

  • Develop rating scale

  • Establish levels

  • Establish application profiles


Deliverables:

  • An analysis of the field of mathematics

  • A rating scale

  • A minimum of 5 levels

  • A minimum of 3 applications – chemical technology, actuarial science and business

  • A proposal for exploring an independent assessment centre/service



Impact/benefits:

  • Prevents grade inflation

  • Removes uncertainty about levels

  • Increases personal mobility

  • Reduces teacher workload

  • Provides basis for curriculum development


Recommendation:

  • That Education 20/20 Inc. convene a committee to explore the matter of laddered and competency based achievement levels for mathematics
