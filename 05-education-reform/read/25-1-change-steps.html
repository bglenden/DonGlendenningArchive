Operational framework for implementing educational change, providing both strategic rationale and concrete implementation steps. Repeats five core change strategies (information provision, community engagement, innovation support, leadership recognition, research support) and adds organizational capacity development. Outlines twelve-step implementation process from idea conception through progress evaluation, including problem identification, vision development, goal setting, awareness raising, stakeholder mobilization, action planning, asset identification, implementation, risk review, and evaluation. 1  Providing relevant information


Information is the fuel of change; without Information, the change process is likely soon

to sputter and fail.  But having information by itself, is not a guarantee of change.  The

public needs recent, consistent, readily available information about education, especially

about innovation and best practices.  


2  Engaging the Community   


Public engagement refers to the interaction of specialists with the public in the decision making process.  Typically, specialists consult, that is, they arrange opportunities for the public to make its views known about a matter under development. But people want more than simply being consulted, they want to engage in the setting of policies and the making of decision.  Public engagement results in putting more decision-making powers directly into the hands of ordinary people – it is grass roots democracy in action.  Public engagement starts with the setting of the agenda, working from shared information, a transparent agenda, and ensuring inclusiveness, control and transparency.  


4 Encouraging and supporting innovation


Our world is constantly and rapidly changing.  Organizations must adapt and even reinvent themselves otherwise they are likely to become irrelevant and even disappear; education is no exception.  The education system must not only accept change but embrace it.  A learning environment that demonstrates change helps learners prepare for a future that involves change.  One size fits all is no longer good enough.  Professional educators must be given the freedom to innovate, even the freedom sometimes to fail, and encouraged even recognized for doing it.


5  Recognizing leadership of people, organizations and institutions 

Often innovators go unnoticed; we hope to change that.  We propose to recognize and reward institutions and organizations, as well as individuals for their efforts in improving the quality of education for every Canadian


6  Encouraging and supporting research

There is always a need for research into alternative and better ways to teach, administer, manage and organize.


9  Develop an organizational capacity for the task

There is a need, and one that will increase as we grow into the task, to develop a supporting education. 





Steps

  • Start with an idea
  • Identify problems to address
  • Develop a  clear vision of the future alternative. 
  • Establish primary goals
  • Raise awareness of alternatives
  • Muster support including people of influence
  • Develop action plan
  • Identify assets available and needed
  • Implement plan
  • Review risk
  • Evaluate progress
