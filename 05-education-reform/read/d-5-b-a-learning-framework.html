About Education A Framework for Learning

Occasional paper No. 4 November 1, 2015


Introduction

Gaining a quality education should be a major focus of every citizen and providing a quality education should be a major investment of every government. One gains an education through learning. In order to understand education and the education process, therefore, we first must understand the learner and learning process. Having a framework for learning increases the chances of making relevant decisions and making those decisions more consistently. It also provides common language and perspectives when collaborating with colleagues in the field.


One’s education arises from a series of learning experiences that shape our very being. There are many definitions, however, I think of my education as the experiences that have had a formative effect on the way I think, feel and act. As for its purpose, to help us acquire the skills, knowledge and attitudes needed to function in, cope with, manage and even enjoy our journey on the road of life.

The learner

The nature and extent of one’s learning is influenced by what each learner brings to the table:

  • Prior conditions

The ability to learn is impacted by state of our physical, mental, emotional and social well-being

  • Past experience

Learners differ from each other in a variety of ways including: Home experiences, School experience, travel, degree of wellness, age, ambition, interests, culture and learning styles

  • Imagined future

From birth, we travel the road of life. It is a road with many twists and turns, hills and hollows, fast tracks and speed bumps; while on life’s road, we fill a number of recognizable roles*. Where one sees one’s self in each role plays a significant role in the learning process


What happens when we learn?

To learn is to change. As a result of our prior learning, especially in the early years, each person ends up with a complex mix of attitudes, skills, and knowledge. But the futures we want may require a different mix of attitudes, skills and knowledge than the ones we now have. Gaining those skills is called getting or improving one’s education, that’s why we continue to learn. Every new experience is a learning experience, and all the experiences put together, or at least all the experiences that changed us, constitute our education. That makes both learning and education a process of changing behaviour.


Support needed

Most of us need help, at least with some of our learning experiences. That help or support may come from many sources including family, friends, neighbours, colleagues, peers, children and strangers we encounter as we go about our daily lives.
