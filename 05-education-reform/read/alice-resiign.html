Dr. Don Glendenning, Chair

Education 20/20

Charlottetown, PE



Dear Dr. G



I am writing, after a great deal of soul searching, to tender my resignation from the Board of Directors of Education 20/20, effective immediately.


While I remain interested in the work of the organization I only have so much time to give.

As you know, I am a passionate believer in the power of social and emotional wellbeing as foundational for success in life. We know from science, the early years provide the best opportunity to encourage this development, although personally I also believe social and emotional well-being/ trust and attachment needs nurturing though the life span and so it is never too late.


As my time is precious and the direction of the Board seems to be changing I have decided to devote my time and expertise to what I know best.  

Working with parents and training others who work with and care for children brings me great joy. This, most especially, as I see individuals grow and learn new ways of being inside and outside familial relationships.

This enhanced sense of well- being leads to more confidence, self- esteem and self- worth, not only for parents but for children as well.  And, to be truthful, is affirming for me as well.


I have treasured my time discussing education in the broadest terms with you over coffee and do hope we can continue to do that.   Even though I am no longer on the Board, I remain interested in its mandate.



Yours truly,


Alice
