Inspired by the Detroit-based Palmer Park Preparatory Academy, a school without traditional principal hierarchies, Don advocates for teacher-led school management models. He references historical education innovations like the Trump Plan for differentiated staffing and Schools without Walls (Philadelphia), suggesting modern education could benefit from similar distributed leadership approaches. Don argues that teacher-led schools better address educator concerns and create less stressful, more positive work environments while genuinely prioritizing learners. His reference to visiting Detroit to observe such innovations demonstrates serious engagement with best practices.
Education Philosophy & Reform

March 15, 2011


Editor

Charlottetown Guardian

Charlottetown, PEI


Dear Sir:

Palmer Park


The sacking of Eastern School Board Trustees provides an opportunity to rethink how we deliver

Schooling; let's not waste it. Calls have been made to replace the Board; other voices support a

single board. I’ve long been of the view that the school system includes too much bureaucracy.

Let's think outside the box.


Older teachers may remember the Trump plan for differentiated staffing, Schools without Walls

in Philadelphia, and Marshall Public School in Minnesota. Now we can add Palmer Park

Preparatory Academy in Detroit.


Palmer Park is a school without a principal; the school is teacher led; according to what I read, it

truly puts learners first. lt seems to address many of the issues facing teachers; teachers would

love it and, I believe, find it much less stressful. While l'm sure that it has its own problems, it

sounds like an exciting place to work - and schools could do with a little positive excitement

these days.


lf I were still in the business, l'd travel to Detroit to take a look.


Sincerely



Don Glendenning
