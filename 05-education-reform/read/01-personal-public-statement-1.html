Speaking notes presenting recommendations to public audiences on education reform. Provides accessible explanations of five main reform initiatives: developing public education vision statement; implementing learner-centered service model with flexible scheduling and challenge-for-credit; establishing government leadership role in learning culture; delegating instruction provision; and establishing independent qualification authority. Notes emphasize conflict of interest when teachers both teach and sort students, cite apprenticeship and music conservatory models, reference family education advisers and essential skills centres. A Call for Change

Speaking notes



  1. As an immediate step, Government engage in the development of a short, clear, overarching, public statement about education, who it is intended to serve, its guiding principles and basic beliefs. Although much has been written about education, a comprehensive vision statement does not currently exist for PEI. We believe that having such a statement is required, especially for a service as large and complex as education, however, we are not suggesting another study but rather a document prepared by a small working with opportunity for citizen input.





  1. Develop a learner centered service model that provides support in all domains to learners as, when and where needed. Doing so would involve student centered outcomes, criterion referenced assessment, flexible scheduling, challenge for credit arrangements, and greater autonomy and accountability for teachers.

  • Challenge for credit

  • Stage based alternatives








  1. Government reserve for itself a leadership role in creating a learning culture and providing a quality education for every Islander. Such a role is important but currently lacking, and education suffers as a result. The role to include planning, setting priorities, funding, monitoring, quality control, ensuring the availability of professional resources. Under such an arrangement, the Department may well play an increased role in delivering support to families with pre- school children, continuing education classes and even in providing information and services to children and their parents.





  1. Government delegate the role of providing instruction; actual provision of instruction to be expanded beyond schools, colleges, universities to include community agencies, professionals in private practice and learners themselves.

  • Expand role of libraries as study centres for basic skills

  • Expand



  1. Establish a qualification authority, a separate body, for the purpose certifying individual levels of achievement to persons wanting or needing such documentation. The role of teaching is to help others learn; when a teachers rank their students they are putting their ability to teach at risk; assigning student grades, selecting students for prizes and awards and awarding diplomas and degrees is a sorting process and should not be part of a teacher’s role. Apprenticeship with certification through a Provincial Department of Labour and music through the Royal Canadian Music Conservatory are examples of a preferred arrangement.

  • Establish levels of achievement in each field not related to age of learner

  • Disconnect entrance requirements from school studies





  1. Take steps to establish a more professional model of service delivery such as in health and justice services, where professionals are directly accountable to clients for the nature, extent and quality of service provided. Such a model must include closer professional-client relationship than now exist, the potential for a family education adviser for those who want it, and professional autonomy and accountability for all.

  • Allow equivalent of legal and medical clinics – in one of my letters

  • Essential skills centres a number of centres across Island

  • Arizona example
