In this reflective working paper written while at his cottage, Don Glendenning shares his ongoing preoccupation with Education 20/20's future despite being away. He acknowledges having numerous ideas for organizational initiatives but recognizes constraints of his advancing age and directors' busy schedules. Based on organizational discussions, recent planning, and Tim McRoberts's committee work, Don reaffirms that PEI's education system cannot independently institute needed 21st-century changes. He advocates for an active, broad-based, independent, member-driven organization of Islanders invested in education quality. Don identifies structural barriers to change: clarity of mission, structure, communication, autonomy, and accountability. He notes that bureaucracy and "the fear factor" hamper educational progress. Don outlines potential Education 20/20 roles (research, information, instruction, professional development) and indicates he will support whatever organizational direction the board chooses. This document captures Don's mature reflection on educational leadership and his adaptive, democratic approach to organizational governance.

FUTURE OF 2020-2-C

Planning-

Although we are now at our cottage, the future of Education 20/20 is much on my mind. I  can think of a dozen things we might do but then think of my advancing age and your busy schedules.  


As for my view of education on PEI, based on the discussion at our annual meeting, discussions over past months and the work of Tim McRoberts and his committee, I am of the view that:

  • Our current education system is unlikely, on its own, to be able to institute changes needed by education in the 21st. century.
  • Education on PEI could benefit from an active, broad based, independent, member driven,  organization of Islanders interested in the education of their fellow citizens
  • Change in education is hampered by bureaucracy and the fear factor – unwillingness by people to express a view or take a position contrary to that of the government of the day.


Structural issues interfering with change

  • Clarity of mission
  • Structure
  • Communication
  • Autonomy 
  • Accountability

       

Education 2020 options

  • Continue, and modify
  • Take a break; suspend activities
  • Wind up   


Potential roles:

  • Research
  • Information
  • Instruction
  • Professional development


Procedure

  • Once Education 2020 decides    what it wants to be, I will work with it.
