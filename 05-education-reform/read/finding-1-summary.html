First comprehensive findings document (November 17, 2024) synthesizing major systemic deficiencies in PEI's education system. Identifies lack of clarity in purpose and roles, fear inhibiting honest discourse, departmental structure focused on school operations rather than education delivery, insufficient documentation, limited school autonomy, poor transparency, lack of education organizations and professional communities, and absence of self-correcting mechanisms. Notes OECD/PISA research showing autonomy improves outcomes. Recommends independent national oversight body and comprehensive documentation improvements.

Summary

  3. Department structured to run schools rather than deliver education services

  4. Oversight and operation should be separated

  7. Financial reward for advancing personal education rather than effective teaching

  8. Community lacks education organizations, PEI Education Association, advisory committees. Math society. Home and School Association focused on schools

  9. Lack of documentation – learning model, assessment model, curriculum

  10. Need better/easier access to research results

  13. Autonomy improves student learning– OECD , PISA, MEI

  15. System should accommodate community requests/needs

  16. Lacks definition of education; in fact, definition of “education” replaced with definition of “School”

  17. Should Department decide services to be offered or services it will pay for

  18. Services should be identified

  24. Narrowness of Department role
