Letter noting that Islanders expect a broader Department role than "curriculum development." Don cites comparisons: New Brunswick (helping citizens gain lifelong learner attributes), Alberta (economy-focused skills), and anonymous sources (helping people navigate life and contribute to society). He emphasizes the vast difference between delivering curriculum and developing people, recommending an update to the Department's stated role.

Guardian

Charlottetown, PEI

Dear Editor:

The Department of Education describes its role as one of “developing and delivering curriculum.” My conversations with Islanders suggest that they expect more.

Broader roles include:

  • New Brunswick – helping citizens gain attributes needed to be lifelong learners
  • Alberta – skills needed to be successful in a rapidly changing economy
  • Anon – help people navigate life and contribute to society.

The difference between delivering curriculum and developing people is huge; an update is recommended.

Sincerely

Don Glendenning
