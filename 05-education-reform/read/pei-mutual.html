A compelling fundraising letter dated May 5, 2013, to PEI Mutual seeking financial support for Education 20/20 Inc. Don articulates his educational philosophy: education is "the most important issue of the day" and will determine individual and collective futures. He identifies systemic weaknesses including lack of autonomy, accountability, and mismatch between taught and needed skills. The letter describes Education 20/20's activities (workshops, publications, website development, school awards) and emphasizes that the public must demand change since traditional reform efforts have failed. Don notes the organization is federally incorporated and seeking charitable status, with administrative support from the Community Foundation of PEI. He requests $25,000 in total support and invites a meeting to discuss solutions.

Dear Mr.


Education is important, in fact, it is the most important issue of the day and our quality of education will be a major determinant of our individual and collective futures. Changes are needed, and there is wide support for change both inside and outside the system, but changes fall short of what is actually required and fail to deal with a number of underlying weaknesses such as autonomy, accountability and the mismatch between skills taught and skills needed. It is also my view that the required changes are not likely to happen unless the public, you and I, demand it. Traditional efforts to bring about change have not worked. Some innovative, perhaps even bold, measures are required, but we think that a better education system is worth it.



Over the past few months, I’ve been working with a small group of Islanders who are of the same mind and are willing to devote time and energy to the change process. Under the name, Education 20/20 Inc. we’ve organized workshops, published a and carried on discussions about the low reading levels among adult Islanders. A committee has been put in place to organize workshops in a number of Island communities, finishing touches are being put on a website, plans are proceeding to for a school award, additional Directors have been added and we’ve been investigating the matter of low reading levels. The attached invitation to a workshop on May 14 on “on-line course delivery” may also be of interest.


Education 20/20 Inc. is located in Charlottetown, federally incorporated, and committed to improving the quality of education of all Canadians; an application for charitable status has been filed with Revenue; in the meantime, the Community Foundation of Prince Edward Island supports this initiative and, will for the time being, handle our administrative arrangements including issuing receipts for income tax purposes. We are pleased to be able to say that the Community Foundation of Prince Edward Island supports this educational initiative and will administer our funds. Contributions to Education 20/20 Inc. should be directed to the Foundation (Community Foundation of Prince Edward Island, 119 Queen Street, Charlottetown, PE C1A 4B3), who will issue charitable receipts.





Our group cannot make change happen alone; we are now looking for partners and supporters; our short term goal in support of the above initiatives is $25,000.





I am pleased to be able to We cannot do this alone; we need your help, and the help of many Islanders, I would appreciate the opportunity, therefore, to talk with you, not so much about the problem as about some solutions. I’ll be in touch within the next few days about a meeting time and place.

Sincerely


Don Glendenning

President
