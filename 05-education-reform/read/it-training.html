A proposal for delivering 21st century information technology (IT) skills training to students through Education 20/20 Inc. Identifies background challenges: IT skills are critical for modern economy but expensive and difficult for schools to maintain current technology, teachers struggle to keep up, school time is limited, parents cannot keep current, and equipment sits unused after hours/weekends. Proposes training in local communities for children and young adults outside normal school hours and during summer. Options include credit or non-credit offerings, after-hours school facilities, library-based delivery, and fee-based or sponsored funding. Discusses integration approaches: 20/20 could secure funding and hire staff, or find funding and contract with providers. Raises implementation questions about Education 20/20's charitable status, whether IT training aligns with organizational priorities, and whether it conflicts with or supports core mission. - **Key People**: Don Glendenning - **Key Organizations**: Education 20/20 Inc. - **Tags**: PROFESSIONAL, EDUCATION, TECHNICAL - **Eulogy Relevance**: LOW - While showing Don's future-orientation regarding technology, it's a preliminary concept without substantial development.

EDUCATION 20/20 INC.

Proposal: To deliver 21st Century information technology skills to students. 


Background:

  • IT skills are critical for the new economy
  • Expensive for schools to keep abreast of technology
  • Impossible to teachers to keep up to date
  • Time in school is limited
  • Parents cannot keep either
  • Equipment in schools sits unused after school and on weekends
  • Portability of equipment makes mobile units possible

Proposal:

Offer training in local communities to children and young adults outside normal school hours and during the summer


Options

  • Credit/not for credit
  • After hours in schools
  • Offer through libraries
  • Funding – fees, sponsors
  • Linkage to 20/20 – 20/20 secure funding and hire staff; 20/20 find funding and contract with staff

Issues:  20/20 d0oes not have charitable status yet; is this a priority area for 20/20; does it conflict/support with our priorities; consistent
