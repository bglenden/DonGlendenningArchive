Education Philosophy & Reform

Question 11a- Charitable purposes and activities


Original application for charitable status provided list of activities under the following objectives as found in our incorporation documents:

1 To promote education and educational opportunity for every Canadian

2 To exercise leadership in the improvement in all facets of education

3 To foster cooperation between and among all stakeholders

4 Undertake and support research and innovation

5 lmprove public understanding of the education system


Based on our experience during the past year, we are seeking a change of objectives and submitting the following a list of activities based on the revised objectives:


1 Inform and educate the public and policy makers on the impact of policies on citizens and communities:

• Publish a monthly electronic newsletter 

• Maintain a website (www.education2020.ca) 

• Publish occasional papers 

• Make awards to people, schools, learning centres and organizations to recognize leadership or high levels of performance in support of education  


2 Engage policy makers in discussion to ensure that actions proposed and taken reflect research findings and best practices:

• Directors will meet with and make presentations to governing bodies including PEI Government, English and French School Boards, Chambers of Commerce and others as seem appropriate from time to time

• Undertake surveys of stakeholders (parents, learners, teachers, employers, public, etc.)  to keep in touch with issues and interests of stakeholders

• Engage the public through the social media


3 Engage parents and communities in activities that improve access of learners to a quality education

• Organize workshops on a range of issues such  as Essential skills, Learning framework, Challenge for Credit, Charter schools, Handle with Care, application of technology and report cards.

• Facilitate/establish a qualification authority

• Organize an electronic forum for the public at large on schooling issues

• Host discussions to build relationships and support quality programming


4   Support activities that reflects the learning  needs, developmental interests and activities of learners

• Provide tutoring and instruction

• Encourage clubs and competitions– math, robotics, environment, arts, etc.

• Include a youth component of our information activities

• Work for greater credit for independent study

• Campaign for Independent community based counselling

• Provide support for community based instruction


5 Encourage and support educators in their on-going professional development

• Engage educators in writing education-related papers as a way to improve their professional development

• Convene meetings and discussions where educators can exchange information and views

• Organize sessions for teachers to learn of  innovative ideas and best practices   

• Monitor and disseminate information about current, valid research
