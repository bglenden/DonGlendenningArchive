Letter to the editor addressing PEI Department of Education's lack of clarity. Don critiques the absence of a written definition of education, confusion between branches/fields/levels, and the Department's outdated focus on "delivering" rather than supporting education. He advocates for better public information and a taxonomy of common educational terms.
Education Philosophy & Reform

Dear Editor:

I find a shortage of public Information about education.

  • It seems strange to have a Department of Education but no definition of education; I am told that the definition was dropped from the Education Act few years ago
  • Department literature seems to confuse, branch, fields and levels of education
  • Although education is for all ages. the Department is focused on the “young”; 
  • The Department describes its role as one of “developing and  delivering curriculum”; delivering education is an outdated concept.
  • The community could benefit from more information about services available, especially those available to independent learners. 

The education literature lacks encouragement and document; a taxonomy of common terms would be a great place to start. 

Sincerely

Don Glendenning
