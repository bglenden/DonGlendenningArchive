Comments:

Massive Open On-line Courses, (MOOC) hacve caufght the attention of the public at large and are the subject of attention and concern in the education sector, especially in the university sector.  They differ from traditional on-line courses in that they are so widely available, have few if any prerequisites and are free.  The movement has, for a modest fee, introduced credit into the equation and some states have stipulate that state-funded colleges and universiities must accept MOOC credits toward degrees.   How to handle MOOC has become another significant challenge for our educational  institutions.  

Education 20/20 Inc. is pleased to host a session on MOOC, to aid in understanding what is involved, to identify some of the challenges to be faced, and to introduce others with a similar interest.

Presenter:

Tom White-Hassler, who calls himself a cybernostique, spent most of his professional career in E-learning in Alaska, Navajoland and Connecticut; he is currently living in Stratford, PEI while delivering courses to students in Connecticut.  Tom shares a first-person odyssey about online education
