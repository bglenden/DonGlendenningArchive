Quebec at the top in the math class


Island students participate in the Program for International for Student Assessment (PISA), an international test of some half million students in 65 countries. While PISA test scores do not tell us much about the performance of individuals, results do provide information about the performance of a school or a school system.


Math scores in Canada generally have fallen; the exception is Quebec. A Globe and Mail story on December 23, 2013 attributes the strong Quebec showing to “more instruction in formal mathematics, increased teacher quality, and the use of technology in classrooms.


The Quebec difference caught the attention of Dr. Don Glendenning, President of Education 20/20 Inc., and on examination, he wondered if part of the difference may be attributable to the process of curriculum development”. Using Grade nine math as an example, he points out that in Quebec, outcomes fall into two categories, arithmetic and algebra; PEI math outcomes, like other Provinces, develops outcomes under categories of number, patterns and relations, shape and space, and probability.” Glendenning thinks that the Quebec wording is more precise, is likely better understood by Grade nine students and may even be easier to assess. Readers, especially parents, are encouraged to google “grade nine math outcomes” in both PEI and Quebec so they can draw their own conclusions.
