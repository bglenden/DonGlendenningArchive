An early draft of Don's argument that grade levels are administrative conveniences devoid of educational foundation. He uses vivid metaphor, calling grade levels "mythical but sacred cows of education"—widely held but unexamined beliefs. Employing mathematics as his example, Don questions why age and achievement level should be linked. He calls for separating subject mastery from chronological age and school years, positioning this as essential educational reform. This is a foundational argument he revisits across multiple letters.

February 15, 2015


Editor,

Charlottetown Guardian

Charlottetown, PEI


Dear Sir:

Myth of grade levels   


Grade levels are an administrative convenience and nothing more.  To mix metaphors, grade levels are mythical but sacred cows of education. On the one hand, they are widely held beliefs that are exempt from criticism while, on the other, they are widely held beliefs that have no foundation in fact.  It is a control mechanism.


Take mathematics, for example, people of different ages may study mathematics and people reach different levels in mathematics regardless of their age.  but I can find no basis for tying the two together.
