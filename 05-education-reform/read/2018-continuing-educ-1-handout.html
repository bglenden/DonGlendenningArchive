This public-facing handout summarizes the Souris continuing education initiative for community distribution. It provides context about Souris's population (3,798), economy (fishing, aquaculture, agriculture, tourism), and identifies barriers to continuing education including lack of opportunities, inability to commute, timing issues, limited internet access, and lack of awareness. The document notes employer challenges and outlines Education 20/20 Inc.'s guiding principles emphasizing information access, collaboration, practical training, and online delivery with learning management. Several implementation options are proposed, including research initiatives, hiring an education organizer, and establishing training facilities.

Continuing Education in Souris

2018-1



Souris is an urban centre in eastern Prince Edward Island; it has a population in 2016 of 1,053; with additional 2745 people living in the immediate surrounding area. Together they make a community one of 3,798 people. The economy of the area is dominated by seasonal industries of fishing, aquaculture, agriculture and tourism.


In recent weeks, Education 20/20 Inc., with financial help from the Canada- PEI Labour Market agreement, commissioned a formal survey of needs and opportunities for training leading o employment. Information gathering included a survey of existing institutions, interviews with employers and employer organizations, interviews with community leaders and government officials and a survey of the public at large. A copy of the report by MRSB: Assessment of Continuing Education and Workforce Development Needs in the Souris Area of PEI is available at www.Education2020.ca).


The survey identified the following barriers to continuing education:

  • Lack of education and training opportunities

  • Inability to commute to other training locations

  • Timing of course offerings

  • Difficulty of accessing high speed internet services

  • Return on investment (Limewood of a match between employment opportunities and training)

  • Lack of awareness of available training opportunities


As one would expect, training interests are wide ranging and include the fields of business, health, IT, various trades and occupations, and training in the French language Indigenous needs were noted but not separately identified.


Employers face the following challenges:

  • Shallow labour pool

  • Lack of training on specialized equipment

  • Lack of hands on training

  • Lack of programs in the Ares

  • Lack of basic and advanced IT skills

  • Area specific matters such as a dwindling and aging population, relative isolation, seasonal industries, and the wage structure


As bridge-building measures, the study recommends:

  • Access to online training

  • Classroom and hands- on training

  • Area specific skills

  • Address barriers

  • Other considerations including the matching of supply and demand, greater emphasis on employability skills, dwindling population, career planning and funding

  • Quality assurance measures

  • On-line courses


Education 20/20 Inc. reviewed the report and identified dthe following guiding principles:

  1. Information is a function of education and may be independent of the institutions that deliver training

  2. Training responses may reflect both individual and community needs

  3. Meeting community needs can only succeed through collaboration and partnerships

  4. Practical and hands on training are key to successful programs in small communities

  5. On-line-delivery must be matched with astute learning management,

  6. Education services for small communities deserves further study and research


Option under consideration:

  1. A 3-5-year research initiative to develop models, strategies and programs focused

to deliver quality workforce training to small/rural communities

  1. Secure the services of a Continuing education Organizer (full or part-time) to give direction and continuity to continuing education serves both sponsored and self supporting

  2. Encourage Souris High School in its efforts to expand offerings beyond its current mandate and to offer become a full-service community school offering courses in any field, of varying length some of which could be self supporting.

  3. Invite postsecondary institutions to offer modules of regular programs in Souris

  1. Invite equipment manufacturers and dealers should be encouraged to provide product demonstrations in Souris Area to which students and the public would be invited

  2. Establish a fully equipped training space in Souris together with a learning manager as a resource to continuing education.

  3. Enlist Ecole Belle Cloche in efforts to better serve French speaking residents.

  4. Offer literacy and numeracy training

  5. Undertake a study of what constitutes “practical and hands on literacy and numeracy programs for use in small communities
