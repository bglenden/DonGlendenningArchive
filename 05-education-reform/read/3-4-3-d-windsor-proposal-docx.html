Further refined Windsor Foundation proposal addressing the "Preparedness Initiative." Frames the core educational problem: low level of basic skills in high school graduates impacting transition to employment and postsecondary studies. Cites PISA results, 2013 CFPEI youth survey, chamber of commerce presentations, and Education 20/20 workshops across PEI. Criticizes the silo nature of schooling, curriculum misalignment, and inflexibility. Proposes solutions including establishing steering committees, searching relevant literature, conducting DACUM-based competency analysis, developing Bloom's-taxonomy-aligned rating scales, identifying profiles reflecting citizenship, work entry, and postsecondary gaps, benchmarking, testing the model, and reporting. Describes features such as common curriculum maps for teachers and learners, multiple profiles from single analysis, engaging broad stakeholder participation, and fostering collaborative education teams. Preparedness initiative


INTRODUCTION


Mission/purpose of organization

Education 20/20 Inc. is federally incorporated as an independent, broad based, national, not-for-profit education organization, located in Charlottetown, PEI and is governed by a volunteer Board of Directors. Our vision is a quality education for every Canadian and mission is to support and strengthen a culture of learning for all Canadians; Education 20/20 Inc. is unique among educational organizations in Canada in that it is unaligned with any educational institutions, grade level, subject field or cause. The organization operates with ten Directors, a network of some fifty people, and a mailing list of about 250; an application for charitable status has been filed with Canada Revenue Agency; in the meantime, The Community Foundation of Prince Edward Island accepts donations and pays invoices on our behalf. Attached please find a list of activities carried out to date, activities planned for the coming year. Although most Directors have participated in externally funded projects in their other roles, this is the first such project for Education 20/20 Inc. and certainly our first request for funding to the Windsor Foundation.


Proposal

Engage stakeholders in narrowing the gap that young Islanders are experiencing on the way to further study and employment. The attached “one-pager” describes how we arrived at this decision.

Issue being addressed

The proposal addresses one of the major issues of the day in education, namely the low level of basic education skills demonstrated by young people, including high school graduates. As evidence I refer to PISA test results for Prince Edward Island; a 2013 survey of issues of Island youth conducted by the Community Foundation; a presentation to a Standing Committee of the PEI Legislative Assembly by the Charlottetown Area chamber of Commerce; How Canada Performs, a Provincial and Territorial Ranking, Education and Training; Education and Skills, and a series of workshops on Preparedness held throughout PEI by Education 20/20 Inc. The PEI Department of Education has not taken exception to the findings nor, to the best of our knowledge, assumed a leadership role that includes corrective action. Based on our consultations, a pilot in the area of math has the most likely chance of success.


The proposed approach responds in particular to the nature of the education enterprise:

  • Silo nature of schooling: The nature of the education enterprise reinforces silo practices, time based instructional segments, age based grouping, Institutional boundRAiessingle teacher led classrooms, lack of user input into curriculum development, and isolated professional developmentmost readily identifiable educational silos on PEI include the public school system, the university, colleges(2), literacy training, continuing education, and the many “NGO” education and training projects. We find little evidence of coordination in curriculum development, professional development activities, joint projects, etc. Nobody, it seems, except the Premier has the authority or responsibility to deal with the issue in an integrated way.

PROBLEM BEING ADDRESSED


Relationship to Foundation Priorities

The Windsor Foundation has two major areas of interest

  • Disadvantaged or vulnerable youth: These are tough times for young people in transition from public school to further education and to work. While there are many causes, this proposal addresses the issue of basic skills, those skills needed to gain entry to the next phase of life and, without which many are branded as failures. We are aware that sometimes this may from poor decisions by students themselves; on the other hand, we are also aware that the gap may from incorrect information, biased counselling, streaming, out dated course content, poor teaching, inflexible scheduling, lack of encouragement and mentoring, and the list goes on.

  • Higher education initiatives: Although not as well documented, we’ve also been made aware of the many young people now in college and university who struggle with their studies because of low level of basic skills; the writer actually visited make-up classes as part of the information gathering process.Although we do not have the actual figures, we know that the duplication of services is a strain on taxpayer’s dollars.


Our proposal has several innovative features including (1) creating a common map to be used by learners and teachers alike so that students and teachers alike have the same view of the process, (2) a single analysis with multiple profiles instead of the disjointed approach of creating a map for each school grade, (3) engaging s broad range of users and providers in the curriculum development processand (4)reducing the “silo” effect of teachers working at difference levels may see themselves as a team.

STEPS/ACTIVITIESTIMELINE:

This proposal is focused on confirm the nature and extent of the education gap as perceived and experienced by users of basic education services, namely students, employers, postsecondary institutions and the community at large. Steps include:

  • Establish a steering committee

  • Search for and compile relevant information

  • Prepare a competency analysis based on the DACUM model.

  • Develop a criterion reference rating scale reflective of Bloom’s Taxonomy

  • Identify profiles of math skills reflective of gap - citizenship, work entry and entry into postsecondary institutions.

  • Establish a benchmark for each profile

  • Test the model

  • Prepare a report


Benefits:

  • Brings clarification to the issue of basic skills

  • An aid to self study

  • An aid to employers when hiring new employees

  • Improve communications between and among stakeholders

  • Improve Demonstrate some components absent from the current educational process

  • Replace some of the existing costs

Appendix A

Education 20/20 Inc.

Preparedness 2- Skills of Citizenship

Introduction:

In late 2013, The Community Foundation of Prince Edward Island (CFPEI) undertook a survey of over 200 Island youth to identify issues that directly affect them; the report, Taking the Pulse of Island Youth, available on the Foundation’s website at www.cfpei.ca, provides a snapshot of the findings. Bright spots include a positive sense of community, emerging entrepreneurial spirit, volunteerism and new postsecondary offerings. The main concerns include a lack of jobs, surviving on low-paying jobs, educational deficiencies and drug and alcohol addictions. On the subject of education and learning, the study reports a high rate of completion of high school and postsecondary education, a high level of inclusivity and relatively affordable tuition costs; young people also expressed concerns about their readiness for the next stage of life including:


“preparedness gaps with regard to readiness for

post- secondary study and the workplace” – Vital Signs, CFPEI, 2013


Following the publication of the CFPEI Report, Education 20/20 organized five public discussions for the purpose of informing the community of the report and its findings, learning more about youth issues in our communities, exploring issues of education and learning, and to identify actions to be taken to close gaps. Discussions were lively and wide ranging, and carried on well after the formal session ended. The final report included a number of conclusions and recommendations of which the recommendation below triggered this proposal.



Further study be undertaken to clarify the gaps in preparation for further study and for work - (Preparedness, Education 20/20 Inc., Occasional Paper No. 1)

Education 20/20 has continued its exploration under the title, “citizenship skills” meaning those required of any citizens and considered to be a basic foundation for life changes that are likely to occur including skills needed for entrance into employment and expected for acceptance into postsecondary studies.


Based on our explorations, it is the view of Education 20/20 that:

  • The math gap is real

  • Low basic skill levels on PEI are an issue

  • Low basic skill levels are dragging down the economy of PEI

  • Basic skill levels are not consistently defined

  • Skills thought to have been learned while in high school, are being taught again in Adult Basic Education program at Holland College.

  • PEI lacks a master list of recognized basic skills

  • PEI lacks a single body to give direction to its education efforts

  • Basic skills do not appear to be a priority


Proposal: That Education 20/20 Inc. take steps to the nature, extent and causes of the gap.
