THIS BOOKLET


PEI has about 150,000 residents; all are potential learners, many are actual learners. Their future and our future depend to a large degree on the nature and quality of their current education and the education available to them in the years ahead. It is to their benefit and ours to make that education a quality one.


Organizing and delivering education services is a complex matter given all the variables - geography, culture, individual differences, ages, prior learning and personal interests. To this we must add the changing world of education from that of providing an academic education to that of preparing people to deal with, if not solve our community’s and the world’s ills.


This booklet is intended to help Islanders understand their education system, provide a framework for decision-making, provoke debate and encourage action.

ROAD OF LIFE


We are all on the road of life. The road of life is a metaphor for the experiences we encounter between birth and death. While on the road, each person fills a number of roles, that of an

• individual,

• family member,

• citizen,

• student

• producer and

• consumer


Hurdles

The road of life, like any road, presents its share of hills, hollows twists and rough spots including:

• Need for formal qualifications

• Limits to our own learning

• Culture we carry with us and the culture we acquire

• Personal commitments and responsibilities


Essential skills

Some skills are considered essential for everyday living; while readers could make their own list, most lists would likely include the following:

• Communications

• Numeracy

• Community awareness

• Self-management

• Interpersonal relationships

• Information technology



LEARNING


Learners: Citizens in the process of changing

the way they think, feel or act


A learner is a person who, for whatever reason, is trying to bring about change in his/her life - trying to change an attitude, gain new knowledge or add to their skills. But learners are neither empty jars nor blank slates; each learner carries three pieces of luggage to every new learning situation:

• Physical, mental and emotional conditions with which the learner was born.

• Prior learning

• Imagined futures


LEARNING

A process of engaging in experiences

that have a formative effect on the way we think, feel or act


Our futures may benefit from or even require a different mix of attitudes, skills and knowledge than the ones we now have; the process of developing the new mix is called learning, the discernable change that occurs in us as a result of learning is called education

LEARNER SUPPORT SYSTEM


OBJECTIVE – to increase the level of education of Islanders


TYPICAL SUPPORT SERVICES

  • Governance

  • Funding

  • Instruction

  • Human Resources

  • Learning products and technology

  • Quality assurance

  • Information and counselling


REASON FOR GOVERNMENT SUPPORT

Governments support education in order to 1) help citizens develop strong personal skillsets and (2) to ensure that our community has the skills needed to maintain itself as a place we want to live.


MISSING COMPONENT

Although students have guidance counsellors and classroom teachers, the system lacks personal professional advisors available to students and their parents during public school years.








ABOUT EDUCATION


  • Education: Any experience that has a formative effect

on the way a person thinks, feels or acts


  • Educate – to broaden the range and increase the level of performance in a person’s skillset


  • Education, purpose – to develop one’s capacities as participating member of a community or society


  • Education system - services put in place by a community to encourage and support the education of its citizens


  • Outcome - That which is produced or occurs as a result of an event or process


  • Standard” a readily distinguishable level of performance, demonstrated though the making of a product or provision of a service which is deemed worthy of recognition


  • School – an institution designed for students (or pupils) to learn under the direction of teachers


  • Student – someone who attends an educational institution


  • School system – institutions, programs and services provided and encouraged by or with the approval of a community through its government for the purpose of providing formal education for its residents
