GLOSSARY & DEFINITIONS

Competence - the ability to meet performance requirement 


Competency - statement of observable behaviour resulting from the application of knowledge to the solution of a problem or situation


Criteria Indicators used to judge the extent or level of performance


Education – 

Any and all experiences that have a formative effect on a person’s attitude, knowledge or skill.

Is derived from Latin  meaning to learn, to know or to lead out, that is to lead out or develop the internal, hidden talent of a person

Often used in the narrow sense of schooling 


Education – any experience that has a formative effect on the way we think, feel and act


Education outcome – a statement of change expected as a result.


Education system

services put in place by a community to encourage and support the education of its citizens

Formal and informal institutions, programs and services provided and encouraged by or with the approval of a provincial government for the education of any and all its members.


Grade level –

a group of students in a school classified by age or level of performance.

A degree or a step in a scale: A step or degree in a process; A single division of a school classified by the age or progress of the pupils


Instruct – furnish with information in a methodical way


Learning

Engaging in experiences that have a formative effect on how one feels thinks or acts.

the process of receiving systematic instruction


Occupation - competencies or related tasks grouped for the purpose of designating work roles.


Outcome- State being produced as a consequence of  a plan or process


OBE decisions are driven by learning outcomes that students should display at the end of a course




Outcome-based education – (1991 Education Omnibus Bill, Article 7, Sec. 23, that is expected to appear in Minnesota Statute 126,661) - is a student-centred,
results-oriented, standards-referenced system
premised on the belief that all individuals can learn.
In this system:

what a pupil is to learn is clearly identified; 

each pupil's progress is based on the pupil's
demonstrated achievement; 

each pupils’ needs are accommodated through
multiple instructional strategies and assessment
tools; and 

Each pupil is provided time and assistance to
realize her or his potential. 


Principal -  a teacher appointed by a school board to exercise educational leadership and supervisory and administrative responsibilities for a school  (PEI School act 1(s)


Public School System – that part of the school system …  


Purpose of education  - is to help a person gain skills, knowledge and attitudes thought to be required in performing various life roles.  


Quality A perceptual, conditional and somewhat subjective attribute and may be understood differently by different people 

-The customers perception of the value of the suppliers work output


Rating – assessing performance against criteria


School - an  HYPERLINK "http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Institution" \o "Institution" institution designed for  HYPERLINK "http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Student" \o "Student" students (or "pupils") to learn under the direction of  HYPERLINK "http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Teacher" \o "Teacher" teachers.  


A school system refers to the institutions, programs and services provided and encouraged by or with the approval of a community through its government to provide formal education for its residents




Schooling – 

That part of one’s education that occurs in or under the direction of schools, colleges and universities.

The delivery of knowledge, skills and information from teachers to students

the learning experiences deliberately planned and organized by schools


Standard - a readily distinguishable level of performance, demonstrated through the making of a product or provision of a service, which is deemed to be worthy of recognition.

Systems have  HYPERLINK "http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Interconnectivity" \o "Interconnectivity" interconnectivity: the various parts of a system have functional as well as structural relationships to each other.

Systems may have some functions or groups of functions

Competencies and Learning Outcomes

by David Gosselin, University of Nebraska-Lincoln

Competencies and learning outcomes are two related educational terms that can create confusion. Competencies and outcomes can be written to describe the learning gained by students in individual courses (course outcomes) or for the program as a whole (program outcomes). They DO NOT mean the same thing. We will follow the lead of  HYPERLINK "http://onlinelibrary.wiley.com/doi/10.1111/j.1541-4329.2004.tb00047.x/pdf" Hartel and Foegeding (2004) and use the following working definitions:


Competency: A general statement that describes the desired knowledge, skills, and behaviors of a student graduating from a program (or completing a course). Competencies commonly define the applied skills and knowledge that enable people to successfully perform in professional, educational, and other life contexts.


Key Distinction: A true learning outcome is written so that it can be measured or assessed. It focuses on what the student is able to do at end of a program (or course). Thus, learning outcomes are the basis for an assessment program that focuses on what a student can or should be able to do either upon completion of a course or upon graduation from a program. The term learning outcome is used more commonly in the context of a program or course of instruction. The term competency is more commonly used in relation to professional fields (i.e. dentistry, nursing).
