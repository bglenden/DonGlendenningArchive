Charter Schools

No. 01 July 10, 2014


By way of definition:

  • A school is an institution designed for students (or "pupils") to learn under the direction of teachers.

  • A public school is a school that is controlled by a provincial government and maintained at public expense for the education of the children of a community.

  • A charter school, at least in Canada, is a public school that functions semi-autonomously

  • A private school is a school that is not publicly funded


Characteristics:

The following describe the characteristics of charter schools in Alberta, the only Province to allow them at the moment:

  • Legislation passed in 1994

  • Number [update]22 charter schools in the Province operated by 13 charter school authorities,

  • Manage their own funds

  • Funded on the same basis as other public schools.

  • Cannot deny access to students as long as space is available,

  • Cannot have a religious affiliation,

  • All students, including students in charter schools, are required to write provincial examintions

  • Sometimes operate in existing school buildings,

  • Employ certified teachers but Alberta Teachers Association does not accept them as members

  • Operate as non-profit societies under multi-year agreements

  • Usually have specialties – arts, sports, music, gifted, at risk students.

  • Are required to teach the standard curriculum,

  • Have own Board of Governors,

  • Usually determine own staffing requirements, set budget priorities within funding and allocate teaching duties.

  • To encourage choice and innovation

Origin:

Charter schools arose as a reaction to the:

  • Perceived inflexibility of public schools

  • Desire for more attention to needs of students.

  • Excessive regulations stemming from provincial laws, school district policies and union contracts.

  • Desire of parents to play a larger role in their children’s schooling

  • Desire for higher quality, stricter standards and greater involvement in decision making.


The Debate:

Supporters argue that:

  • Uniformity of public schools is a detriment to the quality of students' education

  • More choices of programs and competition among schools are needed so that ineffective schools are closed

  • Greater accountability

  • Increase customer satisfaction

  • Expand choice, introduce competition and often incorporate technology

  • Encourage parental involvement

  • Encourage innovation

  • Add variety to programming

People opposed argue that:

  • Charter school students receive different kinds of education from others thus reducing the common core of learning available in public schools.

  • Charter schools result in a two-tier education system especially for disadvantaged group.

  • The ability of district schools to offer equal educational opportunity for all students is undermined.

  • Introduces to choice and competition

  • Segregate students

Our findings and conclusions

  • The number of students in charter schools is very small.

  • Not a panacea but not a catastrophe either

  • Success in improving student learning stands as a valuable lesson for other provinces.

  • Charter schools provide a way of addressing recommendations of OECD study to provide schools with more autonomy.

  • Charter schools are not drawing the highest-achieving students from traditional public schools

  • The loss of students to charter schools is not having negative achievement effects on traditional public schools,

  • Charter schools may not produce the hoped-for positive competitive effects in traditional public schools.

  • Charter schools may facilitate the move beyond the focus on knowledge and test scores.

  • Are a viable public alternative for teachers, students and parents

  • The fact that permission is subject to review and renewal keeps charter schools in a permanent state of flux

  • They are not considered full members of the educational community

Discussion questions:

  • Would you consider sending your child to a charter school?

  • What worries you about charter schools?

  • What do you like best about charter schools
