Activity Update-2

February 15, 2015

_____________________________________________________________________________________


I know that all have been busy and some have been travelling, and I’ll be travelling for the next 2 weeks, so here is a bit of an update:


  1. Organization; We now have an awards committee, a mathematics committee in the making, and I feel the need for a small committee to attend to information matters or is it communications? - media, sponsorships, occasional papers, social media, etc.

  2. School Award: Distributed, thanks to Imelda Arsenault

  3. Learning path: One-hour discussion on Thursday with Holly Moses and ECE students at Holland College on February 12, great participation; discussion with UPEI students delayed until March

  4. Planning session: Planning session scheduled for 4:30 pm at Haviland Club on March 4; some Directors have expressed the view that we should present our views on education to the new Premier and Leader of the Opposition; I agree and propose that that be the first order of business on March 4;

  5. Travel:  I will be out of Province on personal travel from February 17 to 26 depending on the weather

  6. ELSB presentation: Bill and I are seeking appointment with Minister of education to explore issue of “challenge for credit”.

  7. . Workshops:

  • Virtual tour of PEI education: scheduled for March 3 at 7:00 pm at the Stratford Town Hall; unless I hear otherwise,

  • Continuing Education: Preliminary discussion with Jinny Greaves and Katherine MacQuaid about a session for educators not in traditional institutional sessions agencies such as Skills PEI, Workplace Learning PEI, and Bridges,

  • DACUM/HC: I’ve been asked on occasion about a presentation on the education model used in the early years of Holland College, an approach based on the DACUM model developed in Nova Scotia; had a note from Peter Rukavina a few days ago wondering about such a session sponsored jointly with PEIHSA. As you likely know, DACUM has spread around the world and, I believe, that Holland College is the only institution that operated on a competency based model for a sustained period of time. How should we respond?
