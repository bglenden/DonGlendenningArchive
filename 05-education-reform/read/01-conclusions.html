Summary conclusions that education sector has a school system posing as an education system, widespread concerns about quality (especially literacy), system cannot self-reform requiring external intervention, widespread support for change, teachers lack influence/control, system is bureaucratic and top-down, and minimal public engagement in policy-setting. Advocates for meaningful systemic change rather than incremental improvements. - **Key People**: None - **Key Organizations**: None - **Tags**: EDUCATION, PROFESSIONAL, MANUSCRIPT - **Eulogy Relevance**: MEDIUM - Summarizes Don's critical assessment of education system status quo.

Conclusions

  • We have a school system posing as an education system
  • The  concern about the quality of public school education is widespread 
  • Level of student ability to read and write is the most common complaint
  • Most of the frustration appears to be related to the organization and delivery
  • The system is not able to change itself; outside intervention is required
  • There is widespread support, both inside and outside the system, for change.
  • Important and useful changes can be made without incurring either great expense or disruption to the system.
  • Teachers have little influence or control in what they do.
  • The system is bureaucratic and top directed
  • There is little public engagement in policy setting and direction
