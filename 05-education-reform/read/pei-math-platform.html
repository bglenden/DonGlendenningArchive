Project proposal document for the PEI Math Platform initiative by Education 20/20 Inc., with Don Glendenning listed as primary contact (pei@education2020.ca, phone 902-892-5859). Proposal identifies a "math gap" in PEI education and proposes developing a competency-based curriculum master list reflecting math skills used by citizens in everyday life. Project objectives include creating a standard competencies list, developing elaboration packages, establishing common rating scales and benchmarks. Target audience: Islanders transitioning from public education to work or further studies. Estimated budget: $15,000. Addresses diverse stakeholders (teachers, employers, certification bodies, counselors). Published circa 2019.

Challenge/ Issue 

This proposal arises out of discussion with math providers, a search of the literature on math instruction and media reports on the math gap as perceived by the public at large.   


Mathematics is a key skill for all citizens; differences in math skill levels may be attributed to:

  • the number of skills a person can perform, 
  • the level of performance reached on each skill, 
  • the nature and extent of clustering of skills,
  • fluency in the application
  • Relevance of benchmarks.  


The objective of the project is to increase competency levels of Island students. Curriculum is now developed on an outcomes base; on examination,  Education 20/20 finds competencies to a more appropriate curriculum base for both instruction and independent learning.  

  • While our search produced several lists of math outcomes and competencies, none revealed the extent to which those outcomes reflected the math skills actually used by citizens on a regular basis.  

Description 

Develop a stanard lidt of math competnciee appropriate to the needs of island citizens.ng a recognized analysis process, identify a map of math skills used in everyday life.  

  • Develop a master  list of math cometencies to be supported in public  education in PEI
  • Develop an elaboration package for each skill offered

 

Outcomes 

The project will deliver:

  • basic map or master list of competencies for use in senior high school and further general education
  • Common rating scale to be used with master list
  • Common benchmark
  • Copies will be made available at cost, to any interested person.

Benefits

Such a list will also be useful to teachers,  employers, credentialing bodies, tutors,.  counsellors, certification bodies and others.  It is also a communication tool between and among various providers and users of math services
