August 25, 2013, Education 20/20 Inc. position paper on "Reading Skills for Citizens." Identifies widespread concern about declining reading performance in Canada and PEI. Proposes community-based solutions beyond schools, emphasizing development of measurable reading competencies. Outlines plan to convene working groups of parents, employers, and teachers to establish "Reading Skills for Citizens" checklist and website resource.


August 25, 2013

EDUCATION 20/20 INC.


Reading Skills for Citizens


Concern about reading is widespread and frequently surfaces in conversations about education. Articles on low performance levels appear frequently in national and local media. The media also reports initiatives aimed at improvement of reading, yet performance levels remain unacceptedly low and have improved little if any during the past decade. For the purpose of this paper, and as our working definition, we’ve defined reading as a set of skills that enable a person to turn printed information into meaning and achieve the goals of independence, comprehension and fluency.


It is our view that reading levels can be improved in a variety of ways including more and better information and instruction, wider us of self- instructional materials, recognition and engagement, assessment and feedback. It is also our view that reading is a community problem and one that cannot be solved by schools and educators alone.


Step one in bringing about change is to develop a clear and measureable set of reading competencies; that is a check list that can be given to students, new Canadians, employers and parents, and of course to teachers, anyone and everyone who is interested in raising the levels of the reading skills of Islanders; such a list does not exist at the moment. That’s where we plan to start. We will do so by convening a small working group of parents, employers, teachers, and others and, with the help of experienced analysts, develop a generic list, we call it reading skills for citizens, and we’ll make it available to people through our website.
