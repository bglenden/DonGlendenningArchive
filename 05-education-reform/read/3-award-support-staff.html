Detailed program design for recognizing K-12 support staff in Prince Edward Island who demonstrate exceptional commitment and positive impact on student lives. Extensive criteria outline five award categories with specific requirements: Inspirational Leadership, Continuous Excellence, Spirit Award, and others addressing service quality, workplace culture, and professional development. The design includes nomination procedures, selection panels, public recognition components, and media engagement. This comprehensive framework reflects Don's belief that support staff deserve public recognition alongside traditional educator accolades.

Planning

SUPPORT STAFF



Purpose of the Award: To recognize a support staff member who consistently demonstrate commitment within the K-12 School system in Prince Edward Island who has made a significant difference in the lives of students in their institution. Pride and sense of cimmunity

In today’s rapidly advancing world, it is important to stop and say ‘thank you’ to those who have contributed silently, yet generously to impact the lives of students. Support staff are sometimes recognized for their actions within the school, but seldom recognized beyond the school community. It is time for the Province of Prince Edward Island to take a wider look at seemingly unassuming support staff who have gone beyond what they are required to do and have made a difference in the lives of students.

Description of the Award: This award will be presented to a person who went above and beyond the call of duty or job requirements and exhibited exemplary action to positively benefit one or more students. The award will carry a cash citation, that may be used at the discretion of the awardee.

Nomination process: Nominations are sought from the school including students, teachers, and administrative staff, and from parents and other family members in the community.   Nominations can be sent electronically via the link provided or via mail to the given address. Complete nominations will contain: the name of the nominee, work profile, a brief description of the action or intervention deemed meritorious or significant – indicating how students’ lives have been impacted, name of the nominator, and contact details of the nominator (pl. see if this is required – of course, contact details may not be needed).

Nominations cannot be accepted from a family member of the nominee, and from those who have evident conflict of interest.

Selection process: A selection panel will make the final decision after a thorough screening process on the basis of the selection criteria.



Selection criteria: The awardee will be selected solely on the basis of the merit, significance, and impact of their action or intervention.


Award ceremony: The awardee will be publicly recognized at an event either within the school or at a larger platform. A news article may be written up on the awardee. Radio and/or TV interviews with the awardee are other possibilities. 







Eligibility:

  • awards are open to full- or part-time staff who have been with the school or learning learning centre more than two years as of January November 1 of the nomination year.

  • Awards are given to individuals only. Nominations for a group will not be considered.

 

Awards & Criteria

The basis for each award selection will be determined by meeting most or all of the criteria for each category

 

 

Inspirational Leadership 

This award recognizes an individual who inspires others to strive for perfection. They influence without authority, often putting others' interests ahead of their own. "A champion of the employees."

Possible criteria include:

  • Fostered an atmosphere of collaboration and creativity, which contributes to the efficiency and effectiveness of productivity.

  • Showed true leadership qualities in managing their staff/service/system effectively.

  • Led transformational change for the benefit of peers.

  • Inspires and motivates others to take advantage of opportunities to enhance their own lives whether professionally or personally.

 

Continuous Excellence 

This award recognizes an individual who continuously seeks excellence in practice. They often "take it to the next level."

Possible criteria include:

  • Displayed initiative and excellence in significantly improving systems, technical procedures or administrative practices.

  • Developed and used creative approaches to work practices, adding an artistic edge that otherwise was absent.

  • Provided a creative contribution to building a brand or identity for their team, department or college.

 

Spirit Award 

This award recognizes an individual who has selflessly improved the lives of others, whether in the College of Engineering or the community.

Possible criteria include:

  • Demonstrated exceptional service to the college and/or community at large (e.g. voluntary participation in community groups, boards or charitable organizations)

  • Extended their self to help others with a high degree of excellence, professionalism and integrity in their work, which contributed significantly to improve the overall College of Engineering environment and experience.

 

Nominees:

  • Any staff member,

  • faculty member,

  • graduate student or undergraduate student may submit a nomination.

Documentation required: 

  • A letter of two pages or less justifying your position that the nominee satisfies the criteria detailed within.

  • Letters of reference from at least one but no more than two other Carnegie Mellon staff, faculty, or students. This may include letters from outside the department.


Fundamental criteria that must be met include:

  • Contributing to a positive culture within the department/work unit

    • interacts with co-workers and customers in a positive, responsive and cheerful manner

    • exhibits high levels of energy and commitment to department/work unit

    • maintains a good sense of humor

    • Actions taken have a positive jmpact on others


  • Bringing the “best” of themselves to work on a regular basis throughout the year in performing his/her regular job duties

    • consistent good performance in their regular job duties

    • exhibits good organizational and leadership skills

    • is motivated and strives for excellence

    • shows an interest in continually enhancing skills and quality of work

    • accepts responsibility for their actions

    • Duties beyond his/her role positive impact on the school community

  • Being a good ambassador for their school

    • speaks highly of the institution as a place to work

    • takes pride in work

    • enhances the image of the institution inter through interactions with others

    • volunteers for school activities, etc.


Other important factors that will be considered include:

  • Exceptional service to clients

    • knowledgeable about job duties and the University

    • presents information and executes job duties in a positive manner

    • willing to go above and beyond regular work duties to assist clients (both internal and external)

    • minimizes delays and assures timely follow up

    • excellent interpersonal relations

  • Innovative and enthusiastic approach to work

    • addresses problematic situations and suggests creative ways resolve them

    • consistently brings out the best in others

    • enhances the operating efficiency of the department/work unit

  • Team work

    • assists new co-workers integrate into the work environment

    • is willing to share their knowledge, skills and experience with others

    • one from whom others seek guidance and advice

    • contributes to a supportive and collaborative team

    • always willing to lend a hand or assist co-workers with their workload

  • Contributing to a healthy work environment/wellness

    • is a positive role model for others

    • consistently recognizes others, either publicly or privately for their accomplishments and contributions

    • treats others with dignity and respect

    • champions sound ideas for positively impacting workplace wellness

    • willing to listen and assist others with both personal and work related challenges
