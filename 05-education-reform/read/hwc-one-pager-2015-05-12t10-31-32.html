Handle With Care

A Training Program for Facilitators



Handle With Care is a program designed to help parents and caregivers promote the mental health of young children from birth to six years; it is useful for everyone who cares for or works with children and especially for parents, grandparents, teachers and consists of interactive strategies that build on the strengths of caregivers.

History

Handle with Care project began in 2002 as a project of the Canadian Mental Association and the Hincks Delcrest Centre in Totonto; Phase 1 provided a summary of recent literature and a bilingual resource booklet for child care practitioners, Phase 2involved a national initiative to train childcare practitioners and their trainers, Phase 3 was a controlled study in three Ontario communities and Phase 4 was a national pilot project, with funding from the Public Health Agency of Canada , innovation Strategy, involving 439 participants in four centres in Ontario, Manitoba and PEI. With Alice Taylor the team leader for the work in PEI. The results were extremely positive and the program is being rolled out across the country. A copy of the evaluation is available as a separate document.


The program is presented on four building blocks: trust and attachment, building and enhancing self esteem, expressing emotions and relationships with other. E ach block containing the following components: Key message, Research, Activities, Bringing it home. Bringing it together, Other resources and a summary handout.


Trained facilitators are key


Proposal

To provide a training program for facilitators of the Handle with Care program


Workshop details

  • 15 hours spread over two days

  • Not more than 18 participants

  • Participants in the program may be any age, must be parent, have some formal early childhood training and experience dealing with children

  • Participants are expected to purchase a Facilitator’s guide and pay for own lunch

  • On successful completion, participants will be recognized with a Certificate and be recognized by Hincks Delcrest as HWC facilitators
