Citizen Math

I’ve been involved in many discussions recently about education; actually mostly about mathematics but with implications for other subjects as well.  Most math curriculum seems to start with math that someone thinks everybody should know; readers can look up math for each grade on the Department of Education website. 


We thought that it would be interesting to ask Islanders about the math they use every day; we invited 10 Island citizens to help.  We came up with a list od 72 skills; apparently this approach had never been done before.  It’s a pretty good list although we may have missed a few. I’ll plan to pull the list together and post it on our website www. education2020.ca. You may find it interesting to compare it with the math now being taught.

It would be easy to tick off a profile of math skills needed to graduate from high school, or to track an individual’s progress, to work in your business or even to use as a transcript.   

My thanks to all who helped; and to a generous funder. We got a couple of other things to do and then we’ll make it available to anyone who is interested.

The Disease That Could Topple Traditional Higher Education’

In an article about the impact os Covid-19, Forbes reports “One in six college-bound respondents to the survey “appear to be near the point of giving up on the idea of attending a 4-year college or university as a fulltime student in the fall.” Add to this the fact that the survey found that “an additional two-thirds of graduating seniors” worry that the pandemic will compel them “to change their first-choice school” to one that is “likely to be less expensive, closer to home, and more familiar to them.”
