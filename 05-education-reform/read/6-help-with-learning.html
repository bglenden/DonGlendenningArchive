How to Help a Learner Learn

Learning is an experience that brings a change in what we know, what we value , believe or can do. Sometimes learning results from our own activities; sometimes we learn through experiences arranged by others. Help for learning usually comes in the form of:

  • Information – recent, accurate and independent information

  • Counselling – including diagnosis and assessment

  • Instruction -- including presentations, demonstrations, practice, conversation, questioning and more.

  • Assessment – to monitor change.

  • Learning products – books diagrams, models, photos brochures, manuals, maps, manuals, etc.

  • Technology – to provide access to educational resources, often at a distance.

  • Funding – Funding to a level and in a manner that is equitable for all Islanders.

  • Human resources – teachers, mentors, administrators, counsellors, advisors and technical specialists as needed.

  • Data – reports on the learning journey.


Learners learn at own rates; although all services should be available to all learners, the frequency and amount of use will vary depending on individual interest and needs. The growth of independent learners needs to be accommodated and will put an extra strain on the system.
