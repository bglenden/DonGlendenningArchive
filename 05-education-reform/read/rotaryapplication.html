Name 

Numeracy (Math) Map

Target Group 

Islanders seeking to improve their mathematics skills, and the people who help them

Challenge/ Issue 

This proposal arises out of a series of discussion and information gathering with providers and users of mathematics in PEI during which it became obvious that the current system is unlikely to produce the results desired by Island stakeholders. 


Mathematics is a key skill for all citizens; difference in levels can be attributed to the number of skills demonstrated, the level reached on each skill, the degree and extent of clustering by learners and the relevance of benchmarks. Each provider on PEI, it seems, has their own approach to determining skills to be taught, how to measure and how to report.  This proposal is focused on identifying and describing math skills needed by citizens in their everyday life including entry level employment.


Historically “education content” has been determined by subject specialists when selecting, sequencing and teaching math courses; fundamentals come first with increasing levels of difficulty to follow based on the view that, with fundamentals known, applications would naturally follow.  The advent of outcomes- based education changes that. Instead, we determine what to teach by identifying what people do with what they know; the role of teachers is to provide the fundamentals needed to support the applications.   

Description 

We propose to identify a master list or map of math applications used by Islanders in everyday life. 

  • Convene a small group, perhaps 10 people, such as a tourist operator, parents, a teacher, operator of the corner store, personnel officer from a larger company, etc 
  • With the aid of a trained facilitator and, using a recognized analysis process, identify a master list or map of math skills used in everyday life.  
  • Strike a profile of skills deemed appropriate for n Island citizen in the 21st century.
  • Strike a committee of representative employers to identify a work entry level math profile.   

Outcomes 

The project will deliver a basic map or master list of competencies used by typical Islanders in their day to day operation.  Copies will be made available at cost to any Islander wanting to improve his or her math skills; such a list will also be useful to teachers, tutors, counsellors, employers, credentialing and certification bodies and others.  It is also a communication tool between and among various providers ad users of math services.  

Capacity
