Education 2020: Quality Education for All

June 22, 2013 'Visioning’ Workshop

Participants: Eileen Conway-Martin, Owen Parkhouse, Don Glendenning, Alice Taylor, Bernadette Allen, and Norman Finlayson

Objectives: (1) to discuss/confirm Education 2020’s broad vision and purpose; (2) review progress to date; and (3) identify further practical steps to take to move organization toward vision and purpose.

Exercise # 1 – Imagining Success for Education 2020

Question explored: Imagine that it is the year 2020 (seven years into the future) and PEI's approach to learning and education has successfully transformed itself; what would this success look like to you?

Brief Summary of Participants Vision of Success

“education is not a means to an end….because there should be no end to education” (Owen P)

  • A culture of learning is more accepted and gaining prominence – nurturing and supporting learning is the norm, and it is a life-long journey.

  • This culture of learning includes, and is integrated with community; communities are integral to this focus – and are well engaged.

  • Education is learner-centred not teacher centred – the focus is nurturing and supporting the individual’s capacity to learn, to explore and to grow.

  • The learner-centred focus is embedded in all aspects of the learning curriculum and teaching methodologies.

  • The awareness of, and focus on, social-emotional needs and growth has equal value with such markers as literacy and numeracy (especially in the early years). The whole person is considered and supported; the process will include the learning needs and styles of the whole person.

  • The learner mentorship concept/model is in place – this role is both recognized and integrated with the learning process – especially during certain development stages (apprenticeship).

  • That learning is happening, can happen, in all types of situation and study/social environments; e.g. learning circles, book clubs, music groups, seniors’ college, etc.

  • The learner is provided with all types of opportunities to apply concepts, theories, and perspectives within context of real life.

  • That learning is a 24/7 activity/process is recognized and accepted.

  • The focus is on establishing and supporting good learning processes – and then positive results and outcomes will follow. Need to have a good learning process to ensure good outcomes are achieved.

  • There are well-established ‘networks of learning’ in place; these could be both local and global, and could take the form of ongoing institutes, annual conferences to share best practices. New social media and communication tools make ‘global learning modules’ possible.

  • There is a high value placed on evaluation – in the context of continuous improvement.

Is it possible to ‘tease out’ a crisp/succinct vision and mission statements from above images of success?

Objective # 2 - Exercise

Given that Education 2020 as a concept has been ‘on the go’ for a year or more, what has been achieved to date? What are the elements that can be leveraged to move forward?

Achievements to Date

  • There is a emerging/growing group of individuals with a shared philosophy.

  • There is a wide range and diverse level of support through the broader community (e.g. regular Islanders, community leaders, employers, educators, government policy/program people, etc) (Note: Don has a list of those he has consulted with/talked to, and who have expressed interest and support).

  • There is a better understanding of barriers and challenges to be faced in transforming from education/schooling to learning.

  • There has been a series of ‘public discussion’ type sessions to explore perceptions, direction and focus re learning.

  • Education 2020 has been incorporated, letterhead has been developed, beginning efforts re a website are at work; there is a plan in place to celebrate success re local ‘best practice’ re innovative learning through an Awards Event.

Objective # 3 – What is the ‘Go Forward’ Plan?

Given the success indicators described, what are the issues that must begin to be addressed in order to move 'down the path of success'?

The following elements/pieces of work still need to be completed.

  • Establish Education 2020’s broader vision and goal(s)

  • Develop a one-pager to document and describe this vision

  • Establish and communicate the core elements and values of Education 2020

  • Explore what other similar groups are doing – how are they approaching the ‘transformational change process’

  • Then a launch of the initiative – and event, expo, awards, etc.

Considerations and needs in terms of going forward

  • Need a researcher – someone to do the legwork in going forward

  • Where to get resources – Foundations, individual supporters, McCain Foundation??

  • Need to ensure that the project is ‘framed’ and communicated as doing something NEW – e.g. a different view and approach to learning, aimed at engaging learners, communities, employers, etc – it is not an exercise in reviewing/addressing the current education systems’ needs and issues.

Next steps

  • Confirm vision, beliefs and values – develop a draft one-pager – Don and Own have agreed to work on this (Don already has some written notes around this).

  • Research other similar movements and models re approaches (Bernadette and Eileen have agreed to work on this)

  • Complete website design work (Tom??? and ???) – build in interactive capacity

  • Look for $$$$ to hire researcher person to do the ‘leg work’ – need to develop a ‘job description’ for the research position

  • Set up an electronic repository site to house all relevant documents for the initiative and the team

  • Develop and implement a communication strategy

  • Follow through on next steps re the Award event – a lot of planning has already gone into this activity

Other points and questions that got raised – and useful to keep on the ‘Education 2020 list’

  • Explore - learn from other similar movements “Mad in America” – “Competency Works’

  • Have we defined things enough – e.g. learner-centred – what are the key elements or values that make it a learner-centred process?

  • Use of community-engagement software – engage discussion around question re need for employment skills

  • Are teachers coaches or referees – is their essential role clear?

  • Perception of students re teacher role – the referee focus may be stultifying natural student creativity and growth

  • Too much focus on getting the student to university

  • Differentiation between education and schooling – big problem

  • Need to have plain language re communications of learning and learner-centred concepts

  • Including in elements of choice in education – this would help to address the issue of evaluation

  • Have put too much re learning inside schools – this needs to change – e.g. guidance, etc – need to allow community a much larger role in assisting and supporting the learning process

  • Value and critical function of community learning needs to be more strongly acknowledged

  • A community-based educational facilitator role/function needs to be integrated into the process - to support all ages re learning

  • Need to consider the ‘whole person’ re the learning process; an integrated system that supports the whole learner

  • There is room for more liberal arts education

  • Employers need to start hiring people for what they do

  • Working with kids with issues – what can we do that will make you want to be here? (in school – engaged in the learning process)

  • Current education system – not developed around a concept of what you have learned or achieved after a series of certain interventions – not a vision of the end product

  • Outcome – criteria re performance standards – need the starting point

  • Should the state be the leader in education?

  • Room for virtual schools/modules – modular schools – don’t need the traditional ‘box’ structure

  • An education 20/20 launch at some point soon??

  • A UPEI Summer Institute 2014 type of conference event

  • Call for submissions re Award – innovative thinkers – doers
