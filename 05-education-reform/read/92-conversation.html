TIME FOR A CONVERSATION


Islanders are interested in education, not just schools, storm days, technology, teaching aides, battery powered buses and exams. They are also interested in literacy, math skills, and preparation for employment. ntario education group, has recommended that their Minister constitute a Task Force to assist in planning.

Sounds like a good idea; there will be no return to normal after kovid. Many argue that the education system has narrowed during the last 50 years and is now focused on delivering products – credits, diplomas and degrees. – rather than providing education services. Our Department’s description of role as one of “ develop(ing and delivering high quality programs and curriculum in English and French” supports that view.


Certainly, my conversations with Islanders reveal a gap between services desired and services delivered – a gap that is primarily structural – meaning not caused by people failing to act but rather by a frame-work that limits both freedom and accountability to do better. Readers may find both the OECD and the World Bank have drawn the same conclusions.



Let’s begin with some conversations – conversations about education, a few definitions, add some overarching principles and beliefs, identify typical function. Let “form follow function” be your guide to the new organization but consider first the advice of the World Bank, the OECD and Andre Bedard(See recommended reading below).


PEI educators have a habit of depending on experts from away; DON’T GO THAT ROUTE. Let Islanders lead the initiative; provide help if needed. Could it be led by MLA’s?


Education has entered a new area; It will continue to change but it should not be allowed to return to pre-Kovid models. Let’s engage Islanders in the change process.


If you do this, and it is open to the public, count me in.
