Kohn - Three main effects of grading

  1. “Grades tend to reduce students onterest in learning itself

  2. Grsdes tend to reduce students preference for challenging tasks

  3. Grades tend to reduce the quality of students thinking

  4. Grades aren’t valid, reliable or objrctive

  5. Grades distort the curriculum

  6. Grades encourage cheating

  7. Grades spoil teachers relationships with students

  8. Grades spoil students relationships with each other

  9. waste a lot of time that could be spent on learning





Bibliography:

Kohn, Alfie, From Degrading to De-Grading, High School Magazine, March, 1999

Reeves, Douglas B, Leading to Change/ Effective Grading Practices, Educational Leadership, ASCD February 2008
