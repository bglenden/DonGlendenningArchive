Dear Editor

Why we invest in education 

21-3

I think of education as a process of helping others develop or change skills, knowledge and attitudes. It’s an umbrella term and includes all learning experiences wherever, whenever and however initiated and carried out. Some of the most important learning happens in the first few years of life and is promoted and led by home and family.


Communities believe that education is good for the community as well as for individual citizens; through  government, they(1) encourage  citizens (2) regulate education matters (2) monitor school attendance by the young and (3) they provide financial support 


As a long-time participant and observer of the education scene, the writer is concerned about:

  • Government capture of public education  Embedment , licensing, pay scales.  role of professional conflict of cultures, delegation
  • The tendency to measure education by time in school
  • Absence of context in program offerings- 
  • Switch from service to product
  • Lack of accountability to clients
  • Standard/assessment
  • Unclear framework or plan
  • decline of professionals, compared to health






Sincerely


Don Glendenning
