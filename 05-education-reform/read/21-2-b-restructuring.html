First a few words about education. For me, education is an umbrella word and includes all learning experiences wherever, whenever and however initiated, carried out and paid for. Education results in changes in the a person thinks, feels or acts and many changes, some say all, are observable.. Many suggest that service is no longer the focus. students are simply looking for the piece of paper , the  “pass”, the credit, diploma or degree. 


Changes in one’s education are usually reflected in the way a person thinks, feels and acts. Purposes are expressed in a variety of ways:

  • Prepare children for further study
  • Develop skills, knowledge attitudes needed to thrive in a democratic society
  • Develop skills needed to maintain our community as a place we want to live
  • Develop ”the ability to manage one’s self.” 

In Canada, education is a Provincial responsibility; typical services include:

  • Governance and leadership
  • Funding
  • Instruction
  • Human resources
  • Products and technology
  • Quality assurance
  • Information and counselling


The problem. as I see it, is two- fold. (1) Government has adopted a narrow vision as to what constitutes education confusing it with school studies and (2) simply folded it into existing government  folded the bundle into a political body. confused it with running schools, and delivered it with military precision. We need to go back to the drawing board.

An overhaul is in order: seems like a perfect role for  the Legislative committee on Education.


Sincerely


Don Glendenning
