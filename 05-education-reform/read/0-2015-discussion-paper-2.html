Comprehensive policy analysis document examining Education 20/20 Inc.'s awards program criteria evolution from 2014 to 2015. Detailed discussion of prior year feedback ("purpose not clearly defined," "excessive documentation," "favored early childhood"), then presents refined 2015 criteria focusing on student engagement, school environment, instruction, and community partnerships. Document also analyzes external frameworks like "Schools of Opportunity," independent school hallmarks, and quality education characteristics. This strategic thinking demonstrates Don's evidence-based approach to educational program design and continuous improvement.

EDUCATION 20/20 INC.

Awards


EDUCATION AWARD-2014

Criteria for award:

  1. student to student engagement,

  2. student to teacher engagement,

  3. student to learning materials,

  4. student to community partnerships, and

  5. parental involvement.


Comments about last year include:

  1. Purpose should be more clearly defined

  2. Too much documentation to wade through

  3. Nature of criteria seemed to favour early childhood programs

  4. Procedures should be reviewed

  5. A very tight schedule

  6. Need more publicity

CHARACTERISTICS OF A QUALITY EDUCATION SYSTEM

One where:

  1. Learners are healthy

  2. Environments are safe and respectful

  3. Content is relevant

  4. Outcomes are linked to personal and community objectives

  5. Support is focused on learners and learning.

  6. Information and advice is timely and independent

  7. Teachers are professional, autonomous and accountable

INDEPENDENT SCHOOL AWARDS

The judges will consider some of the following ten hallmarks of excellence to applicants’ submissions:

  1. Measurement of effects on the school as a business: how greater efficiencies, cost-savings, reputation and/or income levels were affected.

  1. Measurement of effects on pupils: how pupil satisfaction, pupil numbers and/or pupil attainment were affected.

  1. Leadership: inspiring management in pursuit of the school’s goals.

  1. Planning: developing a strategic plan for achieving your project.

  1. Innovation: creative use of ideas and techniques.

  1. Enterprise: new ways of raising funds or support, or new methods of controlling costs.

  1. Sustainability: elements of the project that will continue after the initial input and future outcomes.

  1. Accountability: internal and external communications through which pupils, parents, staff, governors and others understand and influence activities in your school.

  1. Inclusion of stakeholders: how pupils, parents, staff, governors and others were involved.

  1. People development: how pupils, staff, governors and others were motivated and how they personally benefited from the experience.
