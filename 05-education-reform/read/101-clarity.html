101

A CALL FOR CLARITY

Education has a clarity problem. We:

  • Treat going to school and getting an education as one and the same.

  • Structure the Department around running schools rather than encouraging education.

  • Narrowed the role of the Department from one of “encouraging and supporting education” to one of “developing and delivering curriculum”..

  • Replaced the definition of “education” from the Education Act thus our services to a school system rathe than an education system.

  • Fail to inform, encourage and inform the community in the education process.

Clarity is a must especially in education, a subject which carries so many layers of subjectivity. The development and distribution of a taxonomy of key education terms would be a good first step.

Sincerely

Don Glendenning
