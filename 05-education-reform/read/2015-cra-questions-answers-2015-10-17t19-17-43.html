E) Assessment

We have continued our exploration of the role of assessment in the education process and concluded that, while formative assessment is a legitimate role for educational institutions, summative evaluation is not. Our activities to date have been exploratory in nature. We find, for example (1) at the international level, neither Canada nor the USA have “qualification authorities, (b) Canadian education authorities tend to rely on input measures (what is taught, rather than output measured (what has been learned) for purposes of comparison and (3) there appears to be no widely recognised graduation qualification available to providers and users alike. 2We expect to become more active in the exploration of alternatives in the months ahead.

Participants would be given a minimum set of criteria outlining safety and legal requirements and left to use their imagination as to a design.

G) Handle with Care

– national program who owns it, our role

Handle with Care is a program designed to help parents and other caregivers promote the mental health of young children every day! It is useful for everyone who cares for or works with children; especially parents, grandparents and teachers, and consists of sets of interactive strategies that build on the strengths of adults in their lives. The program is based on four building blocks: Trust and Attachment, Building and Enhancing Self- Esteem, Expressing Emotions and Building Relationships with Others. Each block builds upon the one before and contains the following components: Key message, Research, Activities, Bringing it Home and Bringing it together, other resources and a Summary Handout. The key to a successful program is trained facilitators. Appendix
