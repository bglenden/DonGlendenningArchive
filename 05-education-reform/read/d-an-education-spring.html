Don identifies emerging signs of positive educational change: teacher performance reviews, broadened entrance standards, increased skills emphasis, public engagement recognition, and decentralization calls. He uses seasonal metaphors to suggest these isolated signals indicate a broader warming trend in education. The optimistic piece demonstrates Don's ability to recognize incremental progress toward reform and his belief that multiple actors contribute to systemic change. It reflects his role as observer and cheerleader for educational improvement.

Dear Sir:

An Education Spring


Although it is still only February, signs of spring are in the air; some I see and others I hear about.  Even on a cold day, the snow melts along the side of the house, summer items appear in sale catalogues, more minutes of daylight, bumps and potholes appear, family members are planning summer events, and the list goes on.    Equally, but further afield, there are signs of an education spring.  And what are they, you ask:

Agreements between Departments of Education in New York and New Haven and teachers’ unions on annual performance reviews of teachers.

UBC broadening entrance requirements such things as 

A call for schools to give more attention to the teaching of skills

Recognition of the value of public engagement in improving education

Calls for decentralization of decision-making in the school system


Granted, none of the above have the impact of an occupation, but, I do believe, that the above items are harbingers of better days to come in education.


If you’ve seen a robin already or spotted other signs of warming in the education climate, I’d be delighted to hear from you at  HYPERLINK "mailto:standards2020@gmail.com" standards2020@gmail.com. 


Sincerely


Don Glendenning

A long-time student of education
