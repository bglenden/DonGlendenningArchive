Potential as a goal of education


One frequently hears that the role of education is to help people develop their full capacity or full potential. It is a statement that sets direction for the support we that provide our students. Although I like the term, and have often used it, I now think that it is incomplete.


My dictionary tells me that potential or capacity is the capability of holding something, of doing something or becoming something. But what is that “something” education will help us become or hold? I think the “something” is important. It may not even be a single thing and, of course our student may become many different things.


I often said that each level of education should prepare people to enter the next level of education or go to work; students often I’ve had teachers reject “preparing for work” as the role of a school. The OECD says that the schools have a responsibility to prepare people to maintain, even, improve the community in which they live.


I’ve adopted a “life-roles” perspective of what education should do. Each of us is travelling life’s road; while on the road, we fill many life roles – learner, individual, consumer, producer, citizen and family member. Our education helps us to prepare for, cope with and contribute to events that we create or befall us along the way.
