Detailed commentary on Bloom's Taxonomy as revised by Lorin Anderson. Defines "levels" as points on a scale indicating amount, quantity, extent, or quality. Establishes Don as a student of Bloom's taxonomy. Explains six hierarchical levels of learning from lowest to highest: Remembering (recall), Understanding (comprehension/translation), Applying (use in new situations), Analyzing (separation into components), Evaluating (making judgments about value), and Creating (building new structures/meanings). Uses mountain climbing metaphor to illustrate progression. Emphasizes levels measure depth of learning, not subject matter complexity. - **Key People**: Bloom (educational theorist), Lorin Anderson (taxonomy reviser), Don Glendenning (student of taxonomy) - **Key Organizations**: Education 20/20 Inc. - **Tags**: EDUCATION, PROFESSIONAL - **Eulogy Relevance**: MEDIUM - Demonstrates Don's deep engagement with educational theory and adoption of established pedagogical frameworks in his work.

6 Creating:

4 Analyzing:
