A Learning Framework

Occasional paper No. 1 October 1, 2013


Introduction

Our future as individuals and as communities depends to a large degree on the quality of education of our citizens. Gaining a quality education, therefore, should be a major focus of every citizen and providing a quality education should be a major investment and major focus of government Among the obvious shortfalls at the moment are employability skills, independent information and advice, qualification mechanisms, and encouragement of independent learning.


In order to understand education and the education process, we first must understand learning. Whereas gaining an education involves learning, providing or improving education demands a number of support services; elements of the proposed framework are described in the paragraphs that follow.


1 Learner

So, who are our learners? Take a look in the mirror, then look around you. Look around you and you will see learners everywhere, our families and friends, neighbours and colleagues, and strangers we encounter as we go about our daily lives; all are learners.


But they are not all the same; learners differ from each other in a variety of ways including:

  • Age

  • Ambition

  • Interests

  • Health

  • Culture



While the differences bring richness but they also are factors to be taken into account when designing activities to help others learn.


2 Life Context

At birth, we find ourselves dumped somewhat unceremoniously onto the road of life; immediately, and for the rest of our lives, we engage in activities designed to cope with situations that we encounter, to create for ourselves a niche in which we feel comfortable, and even gain some skills to make the road of life easier for ourselves and those who follow.


Where one is on the road of life becomes an important factor in decisionmaking. One context for decision making is life roles; the Oregon Life roles model identifies the following six life roles:

  • Individual

  • family member,

  • consumer,

  • producer,

  • citizen and

  • learner

What one learns or needs to learn revolves around what one needs or thinks one needs to function in life’s role. But no two learners are at the exact same place on life’s road at the same time.

Learning Process

Every new experience is a learning experience, and all the experiences put together, or at least all the experiences that changed us, constitutes our education. So, learning is a process of changing behaviour. It is generally agreed that changes occur in three areas:

  • Attitude,

  • Skill, and

  • Knowledge


There is general agreement that all people do not learn in the same way or at the same pace; while learning needs and goals may be the same, our approach to learning is a very individual one and reflects our different experiences to date. Learning outcomes, themselves, however, are definable, observable, demonstrable and measureable.


Learner Support

So, learning is a very personal matter and is highly influenced by who we are and our previous experiences, how can we support another in their learning? First by ensuring a good state of wellness, physical, emotional and social, and secondly by providing or supporting:

  • instruction,

  • opportunity,

  • role models

  • encouragement

  • wellness,

  • information,

  • advice

  • recognition.


So why is this important?


We are influenced by our belief system. In fact, John Dewey once said that if he only could know one thing about a person, he would choose to know what the person believed. The extent to which you agree will determine the extent to which you will apply thie framework to the decisions you make about learners and learning.
