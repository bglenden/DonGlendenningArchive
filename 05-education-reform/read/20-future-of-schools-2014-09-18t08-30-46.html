Newsletter article (Open Education No. 20) by Editor Don Glendenning reflecting on the future of schools, prompted by remembering Patrick Watson's 1970 conference presentation about a community without schools. He argues that schools' traditional role as certifiers and knowledge providers is being challenged by social promotion, online learning, and employer reliance on assessment centers rather than diplomas.

The future of schools


One of my most memorable conference speakers was Patrick Watson who spoke at the PEITF annual conference in 1970, I believe. In his presentation, Watson described the dilemma of a community whose schools had mysteriously disappeared and their scramble to provide an education for their children.


While the chances of our schools disappearing are slim, the likelihood perhaps it’s time to have that conversation again especially in view of:

  • Increasing acceptance of social promotion calls into question the use of “high school graduation” as a criterion for hiring

  • Availability of on-line courses can reduce lecture time.

  • Employers are beginning to use reports from assessment centres as a supplement to or replacement for graduation diplomas.
