Don's conceptual paper on proficiency levels and assessment in education, arguing for separation of teaching, evaluation, and ranking functions (analogous to sports coaching vs. refereeing). Proposes an independent assessment service to certify proficiency in selected subjects, removing bias from schools. Reflects Don's innovative thinking about educational credentialing and assessment methodology influenced by Robert Paul Wolff's taxonomy of assessment purposes.

Proficiency levels in education

Educators: Mind your own business; Line of work; the business of teachers


Background

Rise of assessment:

  • No child Left Behind
  • International testing
  • Impact of information technology
  • Move to social promotion
  • Labour mobility – credentials
  • Gap





Current thinking

  • Learning path clearly shows that credentialing and Qualification separate from teaching
  • Qualification authority


Other models

  • Apprenticeship
  • International Baccalaureate
  • Music
  • Swimming
  • Canadian math Olympiad
  • GED
  • TOWES
  • Canadian Language levels
  • Qualification authorities



Conclusions:

  • Levels should be identified through analysis of subject or field
  • School documentation inadequate to task
  • School has other roles that to provide knowledge
  • Evaluation is separate from teaching


Proposal


Assessment service

  • Independent
  • Reduces bias
  • Improves consistency
  • Reduces teacher workload
  • Removes conflict of interest


Characteristics:

  • Independent
  • Laddering
  • Identify levels through analysis if field
  • Clients may be individuals or organizations
  • By application
  • Level independent of length of study
  • Awards a qualification not a credential
  • Results belong to the individual
