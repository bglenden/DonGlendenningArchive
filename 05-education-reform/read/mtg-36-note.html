Don's memo to board directors prior to April 4 meeting outlines organizational transition planning and governance structure work. Don proposes "reset" of Education 20/20 to clarify organizational purpose, increase community profile, and regularize operations. Indicates each director will receive a comprehensive package of organizational documents including promotional materials, company information, director list, vision statement, board regulations, bylaws, goal-based plans, and occasional papers. Proposes formal review and confirmation of: role of Secretary, role of Treasurer, role of Awards Committee, role of Souris Project Committee. Notes three-year director appointments with option for single renewal followed by one-year minimum break. Proposes annual executive elections with past president chairing nominating committee. Don expresses willingness to step down as Chair at any time but willing to serve additional months/year to ease transition and indicates desire to delegate operations if re-elected. Proposes setting Annual Meeting for mid-May and offers his cottage in Earnscliffe as potential retreat/planning location with BBQ/social option. Notes need to recruit replacement for Anne Miller given quorum issues. This memo reflects Don's sophisticated understanding of governance best practices and commitment to organizational stability.


Directors:

Just a reminder of a Directors’ meeting on April 4 at the Haviland Club starting at 3:30 pm.

In addition to the regular agenda, we have some issues about the future of Education 20/20 that we should attend to. I propose to use our discussion time on April 4 for that purpose.

  1. For our meeting, I will provide each Director with a package to include:

  • Promotional brochure

  • Company information sheet

  • List of current Directors

  • Vision Statement

  • Board Regulations (Approved in principle)

  1. I’d like to see us review, amend and confirm at least the:

  • Role of Secretary

  • Role of Treasurer

  • Role of Awards Committee

  • Role of Souris Project Committee

This should position us to fill the positions at our annual meeting – date to be determined

3 I think that we should also formalize terms and appointments of Directors. The regulations call for a three-year appointment of Directors who may be reappointed for a second term after which they must take a minimum one-year break. The executive is normally elected annually with a past President acting as a chair of a nominating committee. As for President, I’ve indicated a desire to step down as Chair any time, however, I will be happy to serve for a few months or a year longer if it will ease the transition process. If I am re-elected, I plan to delegate operations and ask others to report on their areas of responsibility at each meeting.

4 We need to set a date for Annual Meeting; perhaps around mid-May. I am pleased to offer our cottage in Earnscliffe as a place to meet, lay out plans for the coming year, even have a barbeque or glass of wine, if that is of interest.

5 I have not acted to replace Anne Miller but, given our earlier issues with quorum will move to do so shortly.

See you on April 4

Don
