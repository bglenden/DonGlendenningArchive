Framework paper defining education's role in developing citizen capacities. Discusses education as accumulated bit by bit through learning experiences, complicated by individual uniqueness in physical/mental/emotional/social makeup, prior experiences, and learning pace/style. Discusses clientele spanning infants to adults with various skill needs (attitudes, skills, knowledge). Education: Framework


Occasional Paper D-4-A March 28, 2015

Background:


It is widely recognized that education is a key factor in determining each citizen’s quality of life; in the same way, the collective education of our citizens will determine to a significant degree the quality of the community in which we live. Recognizing all of the above, our communities invest heavily in programs and facilities to help people, especially young people, to gain good education. Governments do this for two reasons, (1) to encourage and support citizens to develop their personal capabilities and (2) to ensure that our communities have the people needed to sustain themselves.


Living is a complex matter; to cope with the twists and turns, hills and hollows. During a lifetime we fill many roles that of an individual, family member, learner, citizen, consumer and producer (1). The preparation for life’s roles is called education which is accumulated bit by bit through learning experiences. Learning is complicated by the fact that each person is unique; unique in our basic physical, mental, emotional and social make up, unique in our combination of previous experiences, unique in our perceived futures, and unique also in terms of our pace and style of learning


Definitions:

The kind of education we provide depends to a large degree, on what we believe education to be, who it is for, and what we believe will be of most value to those being educated. Some definitions may be helpful:

  • Education – any experience that has a formative effect on the way we think, feel or act.

  • Learning - Learning is a process of engaging in experiences that have a formative effect on the way we feel, think and act

  • Purpose of education – help people to develop capacities needed to be full participating members of a community

  • Education system – a set of components, relationships and services put in place by a community to encourage and support the education of its citizens

  • A school system refers to the institutions, programs and services provided and encouraged by or with the approval of a community to provide formal education for its residents


Clientele:


Learners tell us that, from time to time, they need help in the form of education, feedback, practice, encouragement, counselling; while all learners need this kind of help they do not need it at the same time , in the same order or in the same amount. need just in time delivery to each learner

In order to organize educational services, it may be useful to consider age such as:

  • Infants aged 0-4

  • Early childhood ages 5-8

  • Youth perhaps another 5-7

  • Young adults

  • Adults


In terms of skills needed, one naturally thinks of:

  • Attitudes:

  • Skills:

  • Knowledge:


In terms of previous education, one thinks of such things as:

  • Gap filling focused on the experiences missed or inadequally learned earlier

  • Retooling to accommodate major changes in one’s life especially in the work world

  • Advancement or promotion within an organization which often brings with it the need for a different set of skills including supervision and management.

  • Leisure often provides blocks of time for new experience

  • Personal fulfilment means many things but is intended to focus on personal growth in reflection


Components:

Education being such a large and important sector, I thought it would be easy to find information on the element, components or functions; not so; while I found a number of articles dealing with health systems, articles about education systems were scarce and, while a number of references implied education items. Based on my reading, discussion and reflection I suggest that a system of education should include:

  • Independent information, counselling and advise for students, parents, employers, post secondary institutions and others who make dicisions about their own or someone elses education.

  • Intuitions, places with desks and chairs, for people who or need formal instruction

  • Community based services made available through the home and a variety of community organizations including NGO’s, libraries, Youth organizations, Access centres, Employer training, service clubs, NGO’s and Itinerant teachers

  • Certification and qualifications service for individuals or organizations who want or need their levels of performance.

  • Governance structure to provide leadership, planning, research, coordination and monitoring.

  • Resource materials such as textbooks, technological applications, devices and supplies.

  • Specialists in such areas as assessment, diagnosis,
