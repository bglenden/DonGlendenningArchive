This is a solicitation letter from Don Glendenning to Bernard Gillis at the Credit Union, seeking financial support for Education 20/20 Inc. initiatives. Don describes the organization's expanded activities including enhanced newsletters, social media, an education award with technology focus, stakeholder roundtables on skill preparation, and exploration of an independent qualification authority. The letter demonstrates Don's fundraising efforts and strategic vision for education reform.
Education Philosophy & Reform




November 1, 2015


Mr. Bernard Gillis


Dear .


It is a while since we last spoke about public education and, although the Credit Union made a contribution to our cause some time ago and you are on our mailing list, I’d like to take a moment to bring you up to date on our activities.


As for the state of education generally on PEI, I think it fair to say that conversation about education is more frequent, more widespread, and in a more urgent tone than when we had our last conversation on the subject. Since then, PEI has a new Minister and Deputy Minister of Education and, we are told to expect some announcements shortly about changes being planned. Obviously, we will be interested to see if the changes include any of the items proposed in our discussions and presentations or included in our Call for Action. (Attached)


While hope springs eternal for real change in education, history tells us that whatever is proposed is likely to fall far short of what is required and, even more so, is likely not to deal with root causes. From that vantage point, we are stepping up our activities and taking bolder steps to bring about change including:

  • Continuing and improving our monthly bilingual newsletter, expanding activities in the social media, continuing our education award but this time with a focus on the application of technology,

  • Bringing together a group of education officials, employers, institutional representatives , employers and the public at large to discuss what can be done to ensure that our young people have the skills needed to enter postsecondary institutions or employment. You will recognize that the convening of such a group by an independent body is a significant intrusion into domains that heretofore have been out of bounds except to the Miniater and his officials

  • In response to significant weaknesses in the setting of standards, the absence of relevant evaluative criteria and the conflict of role when those who teach also grade, Education 20/20 proposes to study the operation of an independent qualification authority, especially to assess basic skill levels.

  • Convening roundtables to explore the underlying beliefs and best practices of a quality education system.

  • It is likely that our future will include study centres; the exact nature of which will become clear in the year ahead.

  • And, we will continue our efforts to “update’ Islanders as to what constitutes an appropriate education and delivery system for the 21st century.
