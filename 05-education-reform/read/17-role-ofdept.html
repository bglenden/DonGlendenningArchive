Don challenges the concentration of educational authority in government, questioning whether a single body should control school attendance, curriculum, employment, assessment, and funding. He proposes separation of powers: government should encourage, support, and regulate; citizens should have choice; an independent body should certify learning; standards should be community-set. Reflects his vision for democratic educational governance.

PUBLIC EDUCATION

Trying to sort out what my problems are with public education; 

SHOULD WE ALLOW A SINGLE BODY (GOVERNMENT TO:

  • Require us to go to school
  • Decide what is to be learned
  • How long one has to learn it
  • Employs the people who teach it
  • Operates the schools that teach it
  • Declare how well you learned it
  • Use that information to select you for a job
  • Fund the costs


Seems to me that:

  • Government can encourage, support  and regulate
  • Citizens should have choice as to when and  where they learn
  • A different  body should determine/certify how much one has learned
  • Government can decide what  it will pay for
  • Standards should be set by the community (separate body)
  • Should be oversight by a community body
