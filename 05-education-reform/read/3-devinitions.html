Some Definitions

I am not sure just how our Department of Education defines education, but here is my usual response

Education: a process of engaging in experiences that result in learning information, skills, attitudes  and other attributes.

Education: a sector of the economy focused on providing services to people who want to learn

Educated: a level of education perceived to equip people to solve most problems of everyday life.

School: A facility designed, equipped and staffed to support the education process.

College: an educational institution providing higher education, specialized or vocational training

University: an educational institution designed for instruction and examination of students in various fields of learning

C

Purpose

Each person has their own reason for pursuing, improving, growing their education. For most people, improving education increases their likelihood of surviving and thriving in future years.  Communities also support education in the expectation that it will improve the community as a place to live. But governments and communities may also have expectations and purposes for supporting education. 

In my reflection, I adopted the “life roles” model used by the Oregon Department of Education. According to Oregon, we each fill six life roles: individual, family member, citizen, student, producer and consumer. Presumably education contributes to all but I have not found any evidence on the matter

Purposes for public education vary across the country. New Brunswick seems to includes “citizen neesa” as a benchmark. I am less sure about other provinces  including ours. I happen to believe in curriculum maps but I’ll leave that        for a separate letter.

About Education

Role of PEI Department of Education

The Department of Education describes its role as one of “developing and delivering curriculum”. My conversations with Islanders suggests that they expect more.

Broader roles include:

  • NB – Helping citizens gain attributes needed to be lifelong learners
  • Alberta – skills needed to be successful in a rapidly changing  economy and society
  • Anon – help people navigate life and contribute to society

The difference between “delivering curriculum” and developing “people” is huge.

Sincerely

Don Glendenning
