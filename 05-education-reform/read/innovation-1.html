A proposal to stimulate innovation in PEI's education system through an Innovators Network that would connect educators working on innovations, provide information about educational advances, and recognize and reward creative contributions. Don argues that education systems are bureaucratic and top-directed (inhibiting innovation) and proposes encouraging innovation through awards, public recognition, promotional campaigns, and competitions. He identifies PEI's historical tradition of innovation in agriculture, pharmaceuticals, and avionics, and proposes systematic support for educational innovators including electronic networking, newsletters, forums, and ongoing professional development rather than periodic initiatives.

Innovation-1



Background

It is widely recognized that the level and quality of education is key to our future as individuals, families, communities and countries. There is also a widely held view that the education system we now have is unlikely to provide the education our people need, especially our young people. The call for change is broad and comes from many voices, including educators, employers, and the public at large. .

There is no doubt that education is important, that improvement is needed, there is wide support for change, and necessary changes are not likely to happen unless the public demands it. Traditional efforts to bring about change have not worked. Some innovative, perhaps even bold, measures are required, but a better education system is worth it and Education First would like to play a leading role, perhaps even a bold one, in making change

Education systems are top directed and bureaucratic; such a climate is not supportive to front line innovation. In addition, educational innovators often work alone in schools that are miles apart. If we want to encourage innovation, this needs to change change the climate and offer more support.



Innovation

A quick scan of our early history reveals that Islanders have always been, and still are, innovators. New agricultural and seafood products, pharmaceuticals and avionics are but a few of the newer fields of endeavour. All sectors and businesses continue to reinvent themselves regularly, and must continue to do so, every sector, it seems axcept education. Every sector can benefit from innovation, Innovative ideas can come from many sources and innovation can be encouraged through offering incentives and awards. Educational improvement can benefit from similar measures.



Innovation generates recognition for the sector and the community, increases public information and engagement, Stimulates change, rewards creative thinking and helps build communities. Education First proposes to stimulate innovation in education through offering awards and providing public recognition through promotional campaigns and competitions, local, provincial, regional, national and international. Neither change nor innovation is a switch to be turned on and off and on as needed. Receptivity to change must be cultivated, it must become part of our culture – and we certainly aren’t there yet. Conservatism is entrenched, the system reminds one of the military or early industrial organizations. The authentic innovation is probably happening in spite of the system rather than because of it.





Innovators Network

Harvey Schacter, writing in the globe and Mail on May 6, 2012 identified the Five key elements key to successful change in any business:

  • Talent, because the skills needed for core and adjacent innovations are different from transformational efforts;

  • Offering that talent a chance to work separately from the core business so they can escape the gravitational pull of the corporate past;

  • Sustained, and often significant, funding;

  • Strong oversight of the innovation pipeline, so that sufficient innovation is in the works to handle future needs; and

  • Measurements to help managers effectively judge the initiatives that are taken to market.





Education proposes to take that step by encouraging and supporting front-line innovators through an Innovators Network that would:

  • Link innovators electronically with others of a like mind

  • Provide innovators with information about innovations elsewhere

  • Identify educational innovators

  • Link innovators electronically with others of a like mind

  • Produce and distribute a newsletter

  • Facilitate face-to-face meetings and fora

  • Recognize contributions of innovators

Tasks

  • Undertake a survey to identify people who self-identify as innovators and their areas of interest

  • Establish and support an electronic communications network

  • Publish a monthly newsletter

  • Organize a forum for innovators on PEI
