This is an extended draft/revision containing multiple versions of Don's call for conceptual clarity in education. The core argument: education and schooling are fundamentally different concepts (education is a service; school is a place). World Bank guidance supports this distinction. Don critiques the Education Act's elimination of an education definition in favor of a schooling definition, signaling institutional narrowing. He analyzes departmental literature, organizational structures, and terminology choices. The document includes repeated sections addressing departmental reorganization, the misalignment between government structure and educational services, and specific recommendations for defining key terms (education, school, student). His recommendation: the department should reestablish itself as "Department of Education" and publish clear definitions as "Step One" in education improvement.

Dear Editor

 CALL FOR CLARITY

Some time ago, I set out to explore public education with a view to increasing my knowledge and understandings. Education is complex; information plays a key role.  Learners have different interests, experiences, capabilities and needs; they also fill a variety of life roles and learn at their own pace. 

The chief cause of confusion, I find, comes from the tendency to use education and schooling interchangeably. In a report in 2018, the World Bank reminded us that education and going to school are not the same. We should listen to the world Bank. Education is a service; school is a place. Experiences in school are major contributors to one’s education but so too are experiences at home, in our communities and through travel. In other words,
