Newsletter article presenting systematic conclusions about PEI's education system limitations. Identifies structural problems including focus on schooling over education, institutional rather than lifelong learning, credential preference over qualification assessment, limited services for adult learners, efficiency over effectiveness focus, and teacher-centered rather than learner-centered support.
Education Philosophy & Reform

Open Education

28 May 20, 2015




Conclusions:

  1. Province focused on schooling rather than education; as a result, PEI:

  • Limits educational activities to instruction in institutions,

  • Favours credentials over qualifications

  • Limits services to independent, part time, employed and older citizens

  • Views knowledge growth as the chief criteria for school awards

  • Has significant gaps in educational services for its citizens especially independent information, counselling, learner management, qualifications,

  • Focused on efficiency not effectiveness.

  • Support services focused on teachers rather than learners
