Comprehensive opening remarks for a May 1, 2014 Education 20/20 event, establishing organizational mission and governance. Don Glendenning listed as one of six Directors (alongside Alice Taylor, Brenda Goodine, Bill Whelan, Tanya Craig, Owen Parkhouse). Organization described as small, independent, nationally incorporated, network-based (not membership-based), broadly inclusive ("big tent"), and financially supported by CFPEI, with sponsorship from R. Birt and Associates, Metro Credit Union, Maritime Electric, and anonymous donors. Core mission emphasizes holistic education concept, not just schools. Initial focus areas: information (website, newsletter), incentives (education awards), and engagement (two workshop series). Defines quality education as emphasizing learner health, safe environments, relevant content, community-linked outcomes, learner-focused support, timely/independent advice, and professional/autonomous teachers. Evening agenda included CFPEI presentation and youth survey findings by Steve McQuaid and Katherine MacDonald.

A quality education for every Canadian is one where: 

  • Learners are healthy, well-nourished and ready to participate;
  • Learning environments are safe and respectful; 
  • Content is relevant to living and working in a modern society;
  • Outcomes are linked to personal and community objectives;
  • Support is focused on learners and learning.
  • Information and advice are both timely and independent;
  • Teachers are professional, autonomous and accountable.
