Editor

PEI Education Needs an Overhaul

There is a growing credibility gap between education and the community it serves; education services on PEI are fragmented, cognitively based, bureaucratic, lack accountability and a self-correcting mechanism.  I do not, we cannot, blame the current government, the previous government or any single government that went before for the current status; we do, however, expecblame governments for not instituting improvement.  

While it is true that opportunities for improvement abound throughout the system, it is the writers view that much of the needed change is blocked by an outdated structure; to initiate the improvement process.  

  • Broaden our definition. PEI has a very narrow view of education – much narrower than that encountered on my arrival in PEI  50  years ago; narrower in terms of both offerings and availability.  Government sees its role is to deliver programs in French and English to young people.  There is no indication that learning is a developmental process nor mention of support for adult learners or encouraging independent and part time learners.  Yhe addition of Early childhood development by the previous  government and “life long learning” to the name of the Department implies that they are not legitimate parts   
  • Organize based on functions: I may be out of date on this issue but I was taught that in designing an organization, form follows function.  Typical function include funding, instruction,  education products, information an counselling, quality control. Aligning the organization with functions would, I suggest, improve both communication and efficiency.
  • Adopt PISA recommendarion
  • Public educators cite PISA and other rst results in support of educational improvement; yet the system ignores the findings of the OECD, the creator of PISA, when it poinys out tht PISA results are  better in schools that provide schools with a significant measure of autonomy in the ir day to day operations and hold schools accountable for school results. Significant measure of autonomy and accountability shifted to front line staff
  • I have never been a great believer in school document as a measure of what has been learned;  I prefer transcripts.  Most western countries, other than Canada and the USA. I am told, have developed qualification authorities to help translation of the myriad of school credit systems.
  • A system focused on learners and learning rather than institutions and programs.

Sincerely

 

Don Glendenning

A long time student of education.
