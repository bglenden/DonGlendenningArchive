Proposal for community study centres offering four progressive levels of support (supervised space, tutors, instruction, and counselling) to help youth and adults meet educational goals. Reflects Education 20/20's belief that schools struggle with diverse learning paces and styles, proposing locally-governed facilities with professional staff and volunteers to bridge the gap.


EDUCATION 20/20 INC.


Study Centre


Background:

  1. Educating is a process of helping people gain the attitudes, skills and knowledge needed to participate in the life of a community.

  2. Schools are time based learning institutions and have difficulty in accommodating different learning styles and paces.

  3. A local study centres will provide both space and support for young people with planning and acquiring the education needed to reach their individual education goals


Purpose - to provide youth of a community with space and help needed to meet educational goals:

  1. Level 1 centre -Supervised study space

  2. Level 2 centre – as above with tutors

  3. Level 3 centre – as above with availability of instruction/teaching

  4. Level 4 centre – as above with the addition of counselling for students and parents


Operation:

  1. A local “governing” body

  2. A safe and secure facility, supervised and welcoming with internet connections, and a quiet/discussion corner

  3. An on-site activities coordinator and site manager to set and maintains a positive learning climate.

  4. Coach and support

  5. Open to any Islander with priority to adults wishing to upgrade essential skills; of learning, available directly or by referral

  6. Security cleared professional staff available as needed supported by coaches and tutors



Budget:

  • Centre

  • Tutors – volunteer

  • Other staff – various arrangementsProvided by community

  • Misc supplies – local service club

  • Resource material – sponso

  • Donated by local group
