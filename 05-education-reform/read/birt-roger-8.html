Year-end letter from Don to Roger Birt reflecting on Education 20/20 Inc.'s advocacy efforts, frustrations with slow implementation of proposed changes, strategy for coming year emphasizing newsletter, awards, skills focus and research, and gratitude for Birt's sustained financial support.



December 28, 2015


Mr. Roger Birt, President

R. Birt and Associates

27 Brackley Point Road

Charlottetown, PE C1A 6Y1



Dear Mr. Birt:


It is year end and for me, it is a time for stocktaking and reflection. It’s been a good year personally; I’m still here and, although well up in my senior years, still reasonably healthy.


As for Education 20/20, we’ve helped keep the debate alive, we’ve met with MLA’s, Ministers, and senior officials and have reason to believe that many are supporting the changes we propose. Actual announcements continue to be disappointing. Our Call for Action, copy attached, is as good a summary of needed changes as any I have seen across the country. The problem is not a matter of knowing what to do or not to do; the problem is one of failure to act.


Our strategy for the coming year includes continuing our bilingual monthly newsletter and school award, focusing more directly on the issue of basic skills even to the point of doing research, a focus on standards, keeping in touch with decision-makers, and speaking in a louder voice.


On behalf of Education 20/20, I thank you for your financial support during the past five years and general encouragement. I’ve enjoyed our discussions and appreciate your availability when I drop by your office unannounced; please look after your health.


Warmest regards to you and Eleanor.


Sincerely



Don Glendenning

President
