Foundational document establishing Education 20/20 Inc. vision statement for "a quality education for every Canadian" and comprehensive educational philosophy. Details characteristics of quality education systems and processes, identifies top ten education and school issues, articulates system components needed (achievement emphasis, mutual respect, meaningful assessment, outcome-based approach), and outlines priorities including learner focus, information quality, public engagement, and student choice of schools.


About Education Our Views on Education

Occasional paper No. 04 July 14, 2014


Vision: A quality education for every Canadian.

Mission: To support and strengthen a culture of learning for all Canadians.

Mandate: To contribute to the education of Canadians by providing information, encouragement, incentives and support for learner-centered education for all.


Definitions:

  • Learners – we are all learners

  • Education – any experience that has a formative effect on the way we feel, think or act

  • Schooling – learning under the direction of teachers

  • Purpose of education – to help people gain the attitudes, skills, knowledge needed in life

  • Role of schools – to help people develop their full potential and to ensure that our community has the skills needed to grow and prosper

  • Learning – changing the way we think, feel or act

  • Teaching – helping people change the way they think, feel or act


A quality education system is one where:

  • Learners are healthy

  • Environments are safe and respectful

  • Content is relevant

  • Outcomes are linked to personal and community objectives

  • Support is focused on learners and learning.

  • Information and advice are timely and independent

  • Teachers are professional, autonomous and accountable

  • Standards are transparent


A quality education process is one that:

  • Emphasizes achievement not time served

  • Practices mutual respect among teachers, learners and learning stakeholders.

  • Includes meaningful assessment and feedback

  • Focuses on development of the whole child

  • Is outcomes based

  • Uses criterion referenced assessment

  • Encourages independent and lifelong learning

  • Helps learners prepare for qualifications and not just credentials

  • Where learners are engaged in the learning process

  • School, home and community are engaged in the learning process

Initial priorities:

  • A focus on learners and learning

  • Quality information for the community at large

  • Broad engagement in the educating process

  • Recognition of individuals, organizations and institutions for leadership

  • Unbundling

  • Expanding market practices

  • A stage based curriculum for Math, English and Science

  • External assessment of key skills

  • Priority in schools to the acquisition of basic/essential skills

  • Government to remove itself from direct operation of schools

  • Schools autonomy in day to day decision making

  • Stage based grouping especially for essential skills

  • Student choice of school attended

  • Investment in community-based educational services

  • Strengthened/Independent information and advisory service

  • Allow students to challenge all required courses for credit
