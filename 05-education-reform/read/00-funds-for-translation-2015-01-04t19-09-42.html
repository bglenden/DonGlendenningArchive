Funding request letter from Don Glendenning (President of Education 20/20 Inc.) seeking $1,000 to cover professional translation of the organization's bilingual newsletter over two years. The letter emphasizes the importance of independent information in driving educational change and describes Education 20/20's expanded communications efforts including website, newsletter, social media, and occasional papers.

Request for financial support- Pro-00

Dear

Our future as individuals and as a community depends in large measure on the quality of the education we make available to our citizens. To call attention to concerns about the quality of our education system and to harness the efforts of others with the same concerns, we established an organization in 2012, Education 20/20 Inc., as an independent, broad based, national, not-for-profit education organization, located in Charlottetown, PEI and governed by a volunteer Board of Directors. Attached is a brochure describing the organization, a list of activities undertaken during the past year as well as some notes about plans for the coming months; what the list does not show is the dozens of conversations with a diverse variety of backgrounds all on the subject of education


We are struck by the lack of information about education in the public domain; think for a moment of the media coverage of health issues, of Dr. Oz, Dr. Phil, Dr. xxx, and the other specialists, the monthly medical column in our local paper, local headlines about influenza, mumps and ebola. And of the dozens of health organizations from alzheimers to pain management undertaking activities in support of improved health. In education, it seems, we are limited to repetitious coverage of educational problems but seldom any in depth coverages of solutions.. While we realize that having information by itself does not guarantee change, without independent information the change is unlikely to survive – in other words information is the fuel for change.



About a year ago, we launched a newsletter for the purpose of (1) keeping the education debate alive, (2) provoking thought, (3) identifying relevant happenings elsewhere and (4) of course, keep the organization in the public view. At the moment we have a circulation of 200 which we will try to double in the coming year. We are also taking steps to increase our presence in the social media and launch a series of occasional papers.

With Canadian aspirations, we believe it important to publish our newsletter in both official languages. (Copies of our two most recent newsletters are enclosed) For a short while, we were fortunate to have the services of a professional translator as a volunteer, however, that is no longer the case. Our call for a volunteer, approached the University of Moncton who operates a training program for translators, and others solicitations were not successful either. I am pleased to report, however, that we now have the services of a trained and experienced translator for $35.00 per issue.



This letter comes as a request for financial assistance in the amount of $1,000 to cover the translation of our Newsletter for the next 2 years.


If you have questions or need further information about this proposal or Education 20/20 Inc, do not hesitate to contact me.


Sincerely


Don glendenning

President.
