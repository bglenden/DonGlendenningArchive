Open Education No. 2 (December 17, 2016), authored by Don Glendenning analyzing provincial assessment practices. He advocates for comprehensive assessment review beyond provincial exams, distinguishing between formative tests (identifying learning gaps for adjustment), summative tests (measuring achievement levels), and competitions (ranking students). Don argues schools should not conduct summative assessment or competitions, proposing instead independent qualification authorities like most developed nations employ. He acknowledges PEI's improved Pan-Canadian Assessment Program (PCAP) results while questioning whether standardized testing adequately measures readiness for employment or post-secondary education. The article welcomes translator Desmond Bernard and acknowledges reader correspondence, creating community dialogue.
