Dear Blake:

Making Math Relevant


For some time now, I’ve been exploring the issue of basic mathematics with representatives from the Department of Education, UPEI, Holland College, employers and anyone else who would listen.


We’ve made some progress. We have some agreement that the difference between any two people in terms of math proficiency, depends on their range of skills, levels of performance on each and the way their skills are clustered. Building on that belief, we now have a willingness to develop a master list of math skills which should dramatically improve communication throughout the system and become the basis for one or more pilot projects.


To move the project forward, Education 20/20 Inc. proposes to:

  • Undertake an analysis of mathematic competencies needed at the work entry level by Island employers

  • Identify criteria in assessing performance, and

  • Develop a common rating scale


The project involves convening a small group, say a dozen, representative Islanders and, with a trained facilitator, undertaking an analysis, developing a rating scale and striking profiles. On completion we will have a math map in a readable format consisting of perhaps 100 -150 math skills; the map will be made available to any interested Islander - employers, institutions, students, teachers, parents and certification bodies alike – then we’ll work with a school to conduct a pilot.


The cost of preparing the analysis and support materials is $12,500; we invite you to be a supporter. Contributions are tax deductible.


Thanks in advance for your consideration; I’ll be in touch early next week to explain the project and answer any questions.


Sincerely


Don Glendenning

President 1-7-1
