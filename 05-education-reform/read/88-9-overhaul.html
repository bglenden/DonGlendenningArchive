Article No. 88-9 (June 4, 2020) examines foundational beliefs about learners and education systems, beginning with a John Dewey quote about the importance of understanding what people believe. Don proposes key beliefs that should underpin PEI education: learners need individual advocacy, learning is personal and self-paced, education is central to community future, and change should be led by Islanders rather than outside experts. The piece contrasts beliefs about learners, education, and government responsibilities, emphasizing that improved education requires clarity about these fundamental principles before implementing systemic changes.

FIRST THERE ARE LEARNERS

PEI is home to some 150,000 citizens; education is central to their future and the future of our community. But it is not clear, at least to me, just what the Island is trying to do; or more precisely, what our government is trying to accomplish with and through education – and being clear is the first step in doing better or doing the best we know.


Our Department of Education expresses its goal as one of developing and delivering high quality programs to Island children; what about the rest of us?
