Windsor Foundation proposal for creating a mathematics curriculum map. Proposes consulting employers, postsecondary institutions, and community to identify desired skills for mathematics curriculum map. Seeks funding to develop competency-based curriculum map in mathematics, identify assessment level criteria, develop criterion-referenced rating scale transcending student ages/grades/boundaries, and establish assessment processes ensuring fair consistent standards. References 2013 CFPEI youth survey showing preparedness gaps, PISA results, and chamber of commerce presentations. Notes vulnerable youth lacking basic skills and duplication in postsecondary remedial services. Proposes starting with existing practical mathematics analysis from Holland College, using DACUM methodology pioneered by Holland College. Three-stage approach: develop master competencies list (curriculum map), develop externally-referenced assessment criteria aligned with outcomes-based education principles, establish externally-referenced benchmarks. Emphasizes separating teaching from assessment functions. The Windsor Foundation

Inquiry/Exploratory Grant Request


Creating a Mathematics Map


The proposer:

Education 20/20 Inc. is a federally incorporated, independent, broad based, national, not-for-profit education organization, located in Charlottetown, PEI and is governed by a volunteer Board of Directors: our purpose is to ensure a quality education for every Canadian. Education 20/20 Inc. is unique among educational organizations in Canada in that it is unaligned with any educational institutions, grade level, subject field or cause. The organization operates with ten Directors, a network of some fifty people, and a mailing list of about 300. Attached please find a list of activities carried out to date, activities planned for the coming year. Although most Directors have participated in externally funded projects in their other roles, this is the first such project for Education 20/20 Inc. and certainly our first request for funding from the Windsor Foundation.


Issue being addressed

This proposal addresses one of the major issues of the day in education, namely gap between basic skills learned by young people in the public school system and the skills needed to access postsecondary studies and employment; we propose to consult employers, postsecondary institutions and the public at large for the purpose of identifying the desired skills and to present our findings in the form of a curriculum map.


Purpose of request:

Education 20/20 Inc. seeks financial support to:

  • Develop a competency based curriculum map in the field of mathematics

  • Identify criteria for assessing levels

  • Develop a criterion referenced rating scale for math that transcend student ages, grades or institutional boundaries, and

  • Establish an assessment process that ensures that standards are applied fairly and consistently.


Relationship to Foundation Priorities


The project responds to both Windsor Foundation areas of interest:

  • Disadvantaged or vulnerable youth: The participants in this project are disadvantaged youth in that they lack some of the basic skills needed to gain entry to the next phase of life and, without which many are branded as failures. The deficiency is not necessarily the result of poor teaching, but may result from decisions taken by students themselves; on the other hand, we are also aware that the gap may result from, inflexible scheduling, lack of encouragement and mentoring, inadequate trsnscripts, and the list goes on.

  • Higher education initiatives: Although not as well documented, we’ve also been made aware of the many young people now in college and university who struggle with their studies because of low level of basic skills; the writer actually visited make-up classes as part of the information gathering process. While not part of this project, it should be noted that the duplication of services is a strain on taxpayer’s dollars.






PROJECT NARRATIVE

Background

In late 2013, In order to better understand the issues of youth, The Community Foundation of Prince Edward Island (CFPEI) undertook a survey of over 200 Island youth to identify issues that directly affect them; the report, Taking the Pulse of Island Youth, available on the Foundation’s website at www.cfpei.ca, provides a snapshot of the findings. Bright spots include a positive sense of community, emerging entrepreneurial spirit, volunteerism and new postsecondary offerings. The main concerns include a lack of jobs, surviving on low-paying jobs, educational deficiencies and drug and alcohol addictions. On the subject of education and learning, young people also expressed concerns about a “preparedness gaps with regard to readiness for post- secondary study and the workplace”. The survey results formed the basis for a series of workshops organized across the Island and discussions with educators and the public alike. It is our conclusion that “preparedness gaps” gap could be significantly reduced by the availability of a common or shared curriculum map, better defined levels of achievement and some changes in the assessment process.

We then searched the literature, examined curriculum materials from a variety of institutions and at various levels, reviewed achievement levels and assessment procedures for the OECD sponsored PISA assessment, Canada’s PCAP assessment, TOWES, OB program, NZQA standards and others seeking a foundation document, an analysis what has been or should be learned. As a base document, we propose to start with an analysis of practical math developed a number of years ago and made available to us by Holland College.

Proposal:

This proposal is intended to address the issue for one subject area, mathematics, and in three stages:

  1. The first step is to develop a master list of competencies, a “curriculum map”, which one may think of as a master list of competencies to serve all users; teachers may use the list to identify what is to be learned, students to track what has been learned, and curriculum developers as an analysis of the field, employers to identify the expectations of a new employee, and credentialing and qualifications bodies as a basis for their work. The analysis process will be based on DACUM (Developing A CUrriculuM), originally developed as part of a Nova Scotia NewStart project on Yarmouth, Nova Scotia but pioneered by Holland College in Charlottetown. (Of which, I am proud to say that the Project Director was the Founding President.)

  2. The second phase is that of developing an externally-referenced set of assessment criteria. (In contrast to the approach currently used by curriculum developers in the public school system throughout Canada in which they try to match outcomes to grade level; this approach is inconsistent with the founding principles of OBE as conceived by William Spady and later proponents of Outcomes Based Education. It is our considered view that an outcome is an outcome is an outcome regardless of the age of the learner; on the other hand, learners may achieve at different levels and one of the reasons for a different level may be the age of the learner.

  3. Stage two of the proposal is to identify criteria used to determine math level and to configure those criteria into a rating scale for public use. We are familiar with externally referenced criteria and rating scales but are not aware of any in the field of math.

  4. The third component is an externally referenced benchmark, something which is missing in school standards.




Deliverables:


  • A single sheet map of mathematics competencies, including a customized rating scale, developed in consultation with the community for the use of students, teachers, employers, counsellors other users.

  • Profiles on the mathematics map reflecting information gained by surveying a variety of users especially employers, postsecondary institutions, career counsellors and othersand the public at large.


Benefits

The result will be a one-sheet common/recognized map of math competencies available to students, teachers, employers, tutors, counsellors and other users as a basis for:

  • A common pool of competencies as a basis for curriculum planning and development

  • An aid to career counselling

  • Improving communication between and among users

  • Smoothing transitions from one level of education to another

  • As an aid for transition to employment.

  • An aid to self-study

  • A tool for employers

  • A tool for students to track progress towards their desired goal


Workplan:

  1. Establish Steering Committee

  2. Prepare analysis

  3. Develop rating scale - criterion referenced and reflective of Bloom’s taxonomy

  4. Identify profile(s)/levels

  5. Prepare final report


Timelines: The project will commence as soon as approved and be completed in the six months that follow.
