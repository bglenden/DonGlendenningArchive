Name 

Math skills map

Target Group 

Islanders seeking to improve their mathematics skills, and the people who help and hire them

Challenge/ Issue 

This proposal arises out of a series of discussion and information gathering with providers and users of mathematics in PEI during which it became obvious that there significant gaps in  math education. 


Mathematics is a key skill for all citizens; difference in levels can be attributed to the number of skills demonstrated, the level reached on each skill, the degree and extent of clustering by learners and the relevance of benchmarks. Each provider on PEI, it seems, has their own approach to determining skills to be taught, how to measure and how to report.  This proposal is focused on identifying and describing math skills needed by citizens in their everyday life including entry level employment.


Historically, “education content” has been determined by subject specialists when selecting, sequencing and teaching math courses; fundamentals come first with increasing levels of difficulty to follow, based on the view that, with fundamentals known, applications would naturally follow.  While math in that form is still needed, it appears that most citizens also need to increase their skill levels in wath applications, that is what people do with what they knoe.   

Description 

We propose to identify a map of math skills used by Islanders in everyday life. 

  • Convene a small group, perhaps 10 people, such as parents, teachers, employers. Community workers, etc. 
  • With the aid of a trained facilitator and, using a recognized analysis process, identify a map of math skills used in everyday life.  
  • Strike a profile of skills deemed appropriate for n Island citizen in the 21st century.
  • Strike a profile  of skills needed for work entry level    

Outcomes 

The project will deliver a basic map or master list of competencies used by typical Islanders in their day to day operation.  Copies will be made available at cost to any Islander wanting to improve his or her math skills; such a list will also be useful to teachers, tutors, counsellors, employers, credentialing and certification bodies and others.  It is also a communication tool between and among various providers ad users of math services.  

Capacity 

 We did not find an analyst in PEI experienced in leading a user-based skills analysis; We’ve found the capacity elsewhere willing to work with us on the project. (see costing below) With map in hand, however, the follow-up work can be done locally although not included as part of this proposal
