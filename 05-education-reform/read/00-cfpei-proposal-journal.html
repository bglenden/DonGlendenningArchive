Journalism Award


This application seeks financial support in the amount of $1,000 to establish an award for journalism students who write publicly about issues in education.


Education 20/20 Inc. is an independent, broad based, not-for-profit organization located in Charlottetown PEI but with national aspirations, and is governed by a volunteer Board of Directors. The organization came into being based on the view that education is a key factor in determining a person’s quality of life and that the collective education of our citizens will determine to a significant degree the quality of the communities in which we live. But we also recognize that there is a gap between what people want in the way of education and what institutions deliver, a gap that the educational system is either unlikely or unable to close by itself. Our mission is to help close that gap and to ensure a quality education for every Canadian.


Because we believe that Independent, recent and reliable Information is one of the keys to change, Education 20/20 maintains a Web site (www.education2020.ca), publishes a monthly bilingual newsletter, puts out occasional papers, and made presentations to both the English Language School Board and the PEI Standing Committee on Education, and is building a capacity in the social media. We recognize, however, that more needs to be done, especially in the area of public information.










As an entry point in the process of increasing the flow of information about education into the public domain; Education 20/20 Inc. proposes to:

  • Encourage journalism students to write about education through including them on our mailing lists and inviting them to public events.

  • Establish an award for students who write publicly about education

  • Limit the award in year one to students in journalism programs at Holland College and UPEI

  • Require those seeking the award to present three articles published in an Atlantic Canada newspaper during the previous 12 months

  • Provide an award in the amount of $1,000 to the winner.

  • Consult with both the industry and the institutions about the Award


I should point out that this initiative breaks new ground; there has been some discussion with the media and the institutions but much more needs to be done. If our request is approved, we will move immediately to establish a committee oversee implementation of the initiative


By means of this letter, we are request the Community Foundation to provide funding in the amount of $1,000 to establish this award.


I am enclosing information about our Education 20/20 and its activities, however, should you have questions or need further information, please do not hesitate to let me know.



Sincerely



D Glendenning

President
