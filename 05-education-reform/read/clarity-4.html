A CALL FOR CLARITY

As a frequent participant in conversations about education, I am struck by the absence of definitions, boundaries and documentation; communication and understanding suffer as a result. For example:

  • Department uses “Education and Lifelong Learning” in title; doesn’t  education include “lifelong learning”?
  • Department literature refers to “helping  young  in society”; shouldn’t Department serve all Is-landers?
  • Philosophy paper refers to helping young people to “secure a place” in society; not sure what that means or how one measures.  
  • Department refers to its goal as one of “developing and delivering programs” to young; Isn’t the role to “help people “gain skills, knowledge and attitudes needed to thrive in future years?
  • Recent report by World Bank cautioned developing countries about treating “education” and going to school as though they are one and the same”. Aren’t we doing that also?
  • Recently I sought the definition of” education” used in the Department only to learn that in had been dropped in a recent revision of the Education Act. Shouldn’t the Department have a definition that reflects its view?

While improvement can begin in any number of places, clarification and documentation are key steps in the change process.

Sincerely

Don Glendenning















We need to be able to conduct community surveys to confirm that our content meets community need; we should be able to analysis PISA results to compare with community practices.

Education is complex; information plays a key role. Those who teach, or other-wise serve learners, must be very clear in their communication. Lack of clarity is a significant issue for 


public education.  It must be addressed directly so that the concept of education won’t become diluted and potentially ineffective.
