Major draft opinion piece examining education transformation in knowledge age. Argues that traditional knowledge-transfer model is obsolete given technology; emphasizes need for problem-solving, personal skills, lifelong learning, and self-directed learning. Signed by Education 20/20 Inc., reflects Don's core belief that education system role must shift from information transfer to capability development for real-world application.

Better education not likely without a clearer mission


For the past two hundred years at least, education has been measured by the institution attended, subjects studied, amount and nature of knowledge gained, time spent in school and credentials received. Schools were places where teachers spent much of their time transferring information to students in an organized way; School were equipped with blackboards, books, maps and other sources of information. The amount of knowledge transferred was assessed, documented and published, with those who attended school being rewarded for their perseverance.


Thanks to technology, the availability and recency of knowledge has changed dramatically, it is available without filtering at the learners fingertips, and students are often more adept at finding information than their teachers. The new requirement is not the acquisition of knowledge but the ability to gather and us it to solve real problems. Furthermore, most parents also believe that the education measured by those criteria is no longer adequate preparation for the world in which their children will work and live. An Education system that:

  • Emphasizes achievement not time served

  • Recognizes that the essence of a quality education is mutual respect among teachers, learners and learning stakeholders.

  • Includes meaningful assessment and feedback

  • Focuses on learning needs rather than subjects to be taught

  • Holds teachers accountable for helping learners reach education goals

  • Is outcomes based

  • Uses criterion referenced assessment

  • Supports independent learning

  • Helps learners prepare for qualifications and not just credentials

  • Operates on a evidence-based model


Initial priorities:

  • A focus on learners and learning

  • Quality information for the community at large

  • Broad engagement in the educating process
