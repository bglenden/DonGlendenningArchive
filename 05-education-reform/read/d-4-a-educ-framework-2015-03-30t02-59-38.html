An occasional paper presenting Glendenning's framework for understanding education systems, identifying eight key components including information/counselling, institutions for formal instruction, community-based organizations, certification services, governance, resource materials, qualified staff, and defined school days. The paper also identifies several puzzling concepts in current education practice like age-based level assignment and social promotion.

About education

Demystifying Education


Occasional Paper D-4-A March 28, 2015

Background:


Every field has iIt is widely recognized that education is a key factor in determining each citizen’s quality of life; in the same way, the collective education of our citizens will determine to a significant degree the quality of the community in which we live.


The responsibility for a person’s education rests firsts with family and later with the individual himself or herself, but it is such an important issue and so complex that most homes and most individuals need help. Governments help, especially in education for the young, for two reasons, (1) to encourage and support citizens to develop their personal capabilities and (2) to ensure that our communities have the people needed to sustain themselves


It is important, I believe, to have a simple framework for making decisions; the notes that follow describe mine. Each of us should develop our own; the notes that follow are a brief description of mine. I present it, not for others to follow, but as an example of what I have found helpful. First, a few definitions:

  • Learner

  • Learning - Learning is a process of engaging in experiences that have a formative effect on the way we feel, think and act

  • Teacher

  • Teaching Role of teacher – to help students under their careto gain the attitudes, skills and knowledge they need to become functioning citizens of their community

  • Education – any experience that has a formative effect on the way we think, feel or act.

  • Purpose of education – help people to develop capacities needed to be full participating members of a community

  • Education system – a set of components, relationships and services put in place by a community to encourage and support the education of its citizens

  • A school system refers to the institutions, programs and services provided and encouraged by or with the approval of a community to provide formal education for its residents.


Components:

Given the size and significance of the sector, I thought it would be easy to find information on the elements, components or functions of an education system; not so; while I found a number of articles dealing with health systems, articles about education systems were scarce and, while a number of references implied education most dealt only with schooling. So here is my list:

  • Information, counselling and advice for students, parents, employers, postsecondary institutions and others who make decisions about their own or a family member’s education.

  • Institutions, places with desks and chairs, for people who want or need formal instruction

  • Community based organizations such as libraries, youth organizations, access centres, employer training, service clubs, NGO’s and Itinerant teachers that provide instruction, information, tutoring, experience in non-formal education

  • Certification and qualification service for individuals and organizations who want or need their levels of performance.

  • Governance structure to provide leadership, planning, research, coordination and monitoring.

  • Resource materials such as textbooks, technological applications, devices and supplies.

  • Qualified staff –to ensure that the human resources are available to provide services required.

  • School days per year – a clause in a labour-management agreement establishing the number of days per year that employees will be paid.


Concepts that puzzle me:

  • Education levels – practice of tying educational achievement to number of years of study.

  • Subject levels – practice of basing levels of achievement in a subject on the age learners.

  • Social promotion – the practice of promoting a student or awarding a credential to a person who has not met the posted requirements.

  • Outcome levels – practice of age based scale applied to education outcomes

  • Credentials – the high level that employers and the community assign to a credential

  • Unwillingness to spell the role of schools

  • Lack of consultation as to what should be taught


Although deliberate choices, none of the above are cast in stone. They reflect my thinking as I write. Maybe when I revisit the subject I will recast or retitle the components. The difficult items on the above list were institutions and community services; an alternative to institutions would be the term “instruction” could just as easily have been “informal educational services”
