EDUCATION 20/20 INC.

Meeting 36; April 4, 2018

Transition notes


It is time to push the reset button on Education 20/20, to review and reset its raison d’etre, raise its profile in the community and regularize its operations, and I propose to initiate the process at our meeting on April 4.   


  • First, each Director will be given a package of 20/20 documents that reflect our current corporate boundaries, including our:
  • Promotional brochure
  • Company information sheet
  • List of current Directors
  • Vision Statement
  • Board Regulations (Thanks to Bill)
  • Occasional papers
  • By-laws
  • Goal based plans – recommendations by a 20/20 committee; undated


  • Current activities:
  • Award: annual; $2,500; we may want to change the purpose another year
  • Newsletter: bilingual, monthly, currently searching for a translator; additional items also circulated
  • Occasional papers: two pages each; seven to date; copies in your package
  • Website:  Active; Bill helping with adjustments o make it more flexible
  • Facebook: mostly posts by Tom
  • Math transition: a number of asymmetrical meetings held with representatives of UPEI, HC, DEEEC and employers to discuss transition in math; From my perspective, summary of discussions circulated to Directors recently; some support for a separate body to establish and award individual levels of proficiency; meeting planned for next week; will propose a non-binding pilot to identify essential math skills in a format common to all.  Proposal for funding being drafted.
  • Souris project: Approved; required insurance which has since been arranged; project to be completed in 3 months from date of approval
  • Liaison: met with PC and Green Party leaders recently; meeting the Minister of Edu next week; had enquiries about participating in party political policy sessions; said no but am rethinking that position; comments? Had great discussion with Director of PSB, he would be pleased to meet with us; Intend to expand liaison activities; 
  • Annual meeting: need replacement for Anne Miller; executive is normally elected annually with a past President acting as a chair of a nominating committee. I’ve indicated a desire to step down as Chair any time, however, I will be happy to serve for the coming year if it will ease the transition process. Need to set a date for Annual Meeting; perhaps around mid-May. Bill has called an Executive meeting to discuss the issue. I am pleased to offer our cottage in Earnscliffe as a place to meet. 
  • STEAM – project on track
  • Continuing Education; In response to Directors’ suggestion, have been exploring the field of continuing education; believe that there is a gap; options include: an equivalent to Senior’s College, focus on adults and employment preparation, community schools or community hubs, information and counselling outside institutions, operate or provide information; for profit?;  provide information or run courses?


  • Other ideas:
  • Stakeholders lack current and relevant information about education   
  • Potential Workshop: “holistic” rating scale
  • Funds; Need to do some fundraising
  • Select a community and develop a community education plan-Souris?
  • Research
  • Modernize logo – proposal from Amber will be available at meeting
  • Public session on math (panel, round table or ?)
  • Polls and surveys – Education lexicon
