A Qualification Framework


Occasional Paper D-8 September 5, 2015

Labour mobility is a fact of life; our ancestors practiced it, so have most of us. The difference is that, jobs have become more complex, numbers have increased, the pace has quickened and people travel further afield. Labour mobility is an important contributor to our economy and refers to the movement of people from job to job, occupation to occupation and from one geographical location to another. Labour mobility may be hampered by geographic ties, absence of housing, unemployment benefits, licensing requirements, lack of portable qualifications, and lack of recognized educational levels; the latter, educational levels, is the subject of this paper.

To facilitate the movement of workers, both within and between countries, many countries have developed a qualification framework and a qualification authority to enforce it; Canada and the US are exceptions. Qualification authorities operate under government legislation but often have their own governing structure. While the nature of their work varies from country to county, qualification authorities usually perform direct assessments of individuals, set, prepare and mark examinations; identify levels of competence in an occupation or field, set standards and benchmarks; translate qualification documents; liaise with schools and training institutions on assessment practices, issue licenses and certificates and provide a recognized service for people with overseas qualifications.


Not surprising, people often confuse credentials and qualifications. Diplomas and degrees are credentials and are awarded by institutions to people who have successfully completed a program or course of studies.

Credentialling criteria are mostly knowledge based and are heavy on input measures, time, content studied, qualification of teachers; they also emphasize what has been studied rather than what has been learned. The reliability of credentials is limited, however, due to the large number of issuing organizations , reliance on teacher judgement and conflicting school policies such as social promotion. A qualification, on the other hand, is an actual measure of what the holder can do with what he or she knows. As an example, apprentices may take their formal training in high schools, colleges, industrial training programs or even the armed forces. Training is based on a common occupational analysis, agreed stages, on-job training and common exams, common analyses, both provincial and national examinations and an endorsement known as the Red Seal. Other Canadian examples of the separation of credentialing and qualification include the work of the Royal Canadian Conservatory of Music, Canadian Red Cross, Canadian Swimming.


Education 20/20 Inc. supports the establishment of a Canadian qualification framework and an independent body to run it. Such an authority would assess and certify a person’s level of performance on a task or set of tasks such as found in an occupation or field of study. For example, a qualifications authority would assume the grading function now handled by teachers but not the formative or dfiagnostic function. The qualifications authority would focus certification clearly on performance that matters support lifelong learning, and self-directed study and thereby allow schools to focus on formative assessment practices that serve to help students learn. A qualification authority would, in effect, serve two clients, organizations (i.e., employers) desiring an assessment of prospective employees and individuals wanting to establish the level and suitability of their qualifications. While qualification authorities we are familiar with, focus on approving courses, accrediting institutions, and analyzing current offerings; we believe in a qualification authority that assesses individuals directly, not one that massages the minutiae of hours, outcomes, teacher qualification of education providers. A qualification authority could also play a useful role in helping people identify gaps in their preparation for a given field of work.


Education 20/20 Inc. supports a qualification authority that is independent with its own governing structure, accepts clients on direct application or by referral and issues certificates as appropriate based on demonstrated performance. Additionally, the authority could (on request) provide applicants with information on learning gaps in certification requirements. A qualification authority would not, however, organize competitions, rank students, or grant credentials.


A review of the literature supports the value of a qualification framework. Benefits include easing the assessment burden on teachers and the school system, improving the consistency in reporting, serve as a n agency to evaluate foreign credentials, easing the mobility process, improving communications between and among education stakeholders, encouraging self- study and reducing the incidence of grade inflation.











Don Glendenning

Charlottetown, PEI
