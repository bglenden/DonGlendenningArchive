A vision void


The New York Times on Saturday, March 21, 2015 reported on a manifesto issued recently by a dozen school superintendents in Connecticut, a document the New York times referred to as a ‘clear and compelling vision for school reform.

The Superintendents posed four simple questions:

  • “What are the most worthy outcomes of our public education system?

  • Are we preparing our students for the world they will enter when they graduate?

  • Is our public education system positioned for continuous improvement, as opposed to ranking, sorting and punishing?

  • To what extent do our laws increase conformity at the expense of innovation?”

I can report that PEI has a Philosophy of Education; it was adopted in adopted in 1990. That’s twenty five years ago and much has changed since then in the environment in which schools operate. So, as a matter of principle, it needs to be updated. But the updating should also:

  • Consider the education of all Islanders not just the education of our children and youth.

  • Encompass educational experiences wherever they occur not just those organized in and by schools.

  • Include preparation for life not just “to their fullest capacities or postsecondary studies

  • View education as a developmental process not just a matter of gaining new knowledge.
