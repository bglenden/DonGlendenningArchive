Don references a New Zealand Herald article about schools as "storage lockers" for children while parents work, using it to examine schools' evolving role. He proposes that schools must address three mandates: (1) helping Islanders develop citizenship attitudes, skills and knowledge; (2) developing people for community needs; and (3) providing childcare "housed, happy and out of harm's way." Don calls for this multi-dimensional mandate to be explicitly included in future PEI educational philosophy discussions, acknowledging that custodial services now substantially shape how family life is organized.
Education Philosophy & Reform


January 6, 2015


Editor,

Charlottetown Guardian

Charlottetown, PEI


Dear Sir:

The changing role of schools


The New Zealand Herald on Monday, April 13, 2015 carried an article by Matt Heath on “School: a great storage locker for our children”.   While it was a light hearted look at how to cope with having your children home during vacation or storm days, it does raise the issue of schools providing care for children while parents work.  Without doubt, family activities are organized around the expectation that schools will care for children during most working days.


This leads to a three-part mandate for  schools of the future:

Help every Islander develop the attitudes, skills and knowledge needed for citizenship

Help develop the people we need to secure future of our community, and

Keep “kids housed, happy and out of harm's way all day” while parents work”


When the election dust settles and a philosophy of education for PEI is being written, I hope this issue will be included in the terms of reference. 

Sincerely,


Don Glendenning

A long-time student of education
