Early proposal for an education network model in Souris. Describes network functions paralleling the agency model but emphasizing flexibility: not-for-profit, independent, granting transcripts but not credentials, cooperating with all providers, serving any subjects and fields. Explores multiple potential host organizations including Town of Souris, Education 20/20, schools, charitable organizations, and provincial bodies. Documents alternative institutional approaches to achieving community education goals.

Education network

Souris/Eastern Kings, PEI

3-Souris;/2019/Service-1

Background:

Souris is an urban centre in eastern Prince Edward Island; it has a population in 2016 of 1,053; with additional 2745 people living in the immediate surrounding area. Together they make a community one of 3,798 people. The economy of the area is dominated by seasonal industries of fishing, aquaculture, agriculture and tourism. A recent study of the area revealed an interest and needs for additional continuing education services in the area.


Proposal: - to put in place a mechanism to encourage and support educational services for the Souris area.


Network will:

  • Encourage and support education services for residents of the area

  • Seek out, organize courses, facilitate and promote training

  • Promote, organize and advertise

  • Organize and host educational activities of interest to the community

  • Represent the community’s interests in matters of education

  • Liaise with other providers of educational services

  • Seek financial support
