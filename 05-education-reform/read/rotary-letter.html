Letter from Don Glendenning as President of Education 20/20 Inc. to Rotary Literacy Foundation (Kingsley Lewis) requesting funding for numeracy skills analysis. Don articulates the rationale: math skills cannot be improved by schools alone; a systematic analysis of numeracy competencies is needed for multiple stakeholders. References attached Canadian numeracy frameworks. Shows Don's approach to educational innovation through community partnerships.

Numeracy map

Attached is a request for financial assistance for a project in support improving numeracy levels of Islanders especially young people in transition to employment and/or further studies.


This proposal is based on the view that:

  • Improving skills in mathematics cannot be accomplished through changes in the public schools alone,

  • It is important to have a clear understanding of the skills at play

  • An analysis of the kind proposed will serve many users and not just public education – program planning, qualifications, position descriptions, job classification, curriculum development, levels of achievement, etc.

  • It is important not to confuse skills and levels

  • The attached proposal provides a transparent development process


Whereas we have a number of analyses of math skills, competencies or outcomes, we were unable to secure any analyses of numeracy skills; such an analysis would give PEI an opportunity to examine and experiment with a range of numeracy skills.


Attached is a Canadian document that makes the case for numeracy skills; I would be pleased to provide additional information


Sincerely



Don Glendenning
