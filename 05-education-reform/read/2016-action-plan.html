Work Plan

2016

Planning

April, 2016


Based on experience to date, we propose the following focus of activities for the coming year:


  1. Key skills- skills needed by all citizens regardless of the path of life pursued including – mathematics, communication (reading, writing, listening and speaking, science, self-management skills, a second language and a computer language.

  • Identify competencies considered necessary for every citizen

  • Continue survey efforts


  1. Achievement levels – establish achievement levels in key skills that are not related to age or grade of the learner and an assessment service for individuals wanting to use it.

  • Establish levels

  • Establish an independent testing service


  1. Supplementary support to learners of all ages including Information, assessment, counselling, supplementary instruction (short course, part time, night, seminars, workshops), surveys and learning management, assessment, instructional materials,

  • Pilot actual delivery of a service


  1. Information and representation on issues and matters that inform and influence the public and educational decision makers including a regular newsletter, Webinar, Social and networking, Public presentations ,Media releases, Speakers bureau, Occasional papers, Feedback

  • Review policy on website and social media

  • Review awards initiative

  • Conversations with specialists – curriculum, assessment, certification, report cards, etc


  1. Build capacity of Education 20/20 Inc. to enable it to accomplish its mission

  • Add directors

  • Review and update policies

  • Review and update structure,

  • Establish financial controls

  • Establish network

  • Engage volunteers

  1. Vacant
