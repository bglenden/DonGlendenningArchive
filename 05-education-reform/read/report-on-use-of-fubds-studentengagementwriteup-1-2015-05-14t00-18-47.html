Updated version of Kinkora Regional High School's award report from May 2015, documenting continued progress on student-engagement initiatives funded by the 2014 Education 20/20 award. Projects include cafeteria improvements with student artwork, free breakfast programs, and development of an outdoor learning space partnered with the Agriculture Diploma Program.

Kinkora Regional High School was thrilled to receive the inaugural 20/20 Education Award in 2014. This award recognized how the staff, parents and community work together to encourage student engagment in their education. Since winning the Student Engagement Award, students, staff, parents and the community have continued to support our students.

Shortly after receiving the award, the students completed a survey of improvements they would like to see at Kinkora. The number one area the students wanted to see improvement was the cafeteria. They made a number of great suggestions. A group of students worked with administration to select furniture and the entire school population voted on a paint color to paint a cozy corner. Then some students came in over a school break to paint the area. Currently, students are creating original pieces of artwork to display. The unveiling of this artwork will take place at the Annual Coffee House this Spring, when students present their various talents for the community to see.

At the same time that the students were planning to jazz up the cafeteria, we found out that the cafeteria service was going to be discontinued because of our low student population. However, with true Blazer spirit, staff, parents and students got together to find a solution. We currently provide free breakfast program daily, with smoothies on Mondays, Wednesdays and Fridays and healthy lunch options four out of five days a week for 20 to 30 students daily. There are also a number of students, as part of the Agriculture Diploma Program, who are growing fresh veggies to serve in the cafeteria. Because the staff and parents are volunteering, the cost is kept low and the focus is on providing healthy hot options that students can enjoy. The school also partnered with ADL to provide vending machines so the students can purchase milk daily.

The other major improvement that the students wanted was the creation of a green space/outdoor classroom. This project has taken a bit more planning, but we are finalizing the details now. Students in a tech. class helped create a draft of the outdoor space and we are hoping to have staff, students and parent volunteers create the space before the end of June. The goal is to create it as an extension of the classroom. Students in the Agriculture Diploma Program can then use this outdoor classroom for a number of agriculture related projects and for the growth of more vegetables to support our cafeteria, but many classes would be able to find curriculum links to bring education outdoors. The other goal is that the greater community will become involved. We are hoping community members can enjoy this space outside of school hours and hopefully help maintain it while students are not in school.

We would like to thank the founding members of Education 20/20 for supporting Kinkora Regional High and helping us make the school a more comfortable environment for the students to learn.

Sincerely,



Jaime Cole, Principal
