Brief paper proposing a math pilot program for PEI education reform. Don notes Alberta's curriculum overhaul shift from outcomes to competencies, terming this "adulting" of education. He advocates for competency-based frameworks more reflective of community language and life roles. The proposed pilot includes: competency-based structure, self-study platforms, qualification-based certification, independent learning managers, a math hotline, and annual conferences. Don recommends piloting at a small local scale to assess feasibility. This document shows his practical, pilot-based approach to implementing systemic reforms.

A Math Pilot

197


Recent news out of Alberta reveals a snag in their curriculum  overhaul;  among the changes, is a focus on essential skills.  . 


The switch from outcomes to competencies is consistent with what is happening elsewhere; competencies are more reflective of the language of the community and more relevant to life roles. “Adulting” of education is a new term to me; my examination  of math suggests increased numeracy, mote practice, increase application and more practice. 


Education on PEI could also benefit from an overhaul.  Instead of focusing on curriculum, however, perhaps we should choose a subjrct and  examine the transition I from level to level.  The idea of math as a pilot was proposed in some of my discussions.


It could prove instructive, for example, to cart themath learning path through the public school years. While the proposed pilot was not fleshed out, further exploration suggests  a  math pilot tto include:

  • A framework that accommodates transition 
  • Competencies rather curriculum based outcomes
  • A presentation platform that accommodates self study
  • Qualification based certification
  • Independent learning managers
  • A math hotline
  • Annual conference organized by ansd for the cath community
