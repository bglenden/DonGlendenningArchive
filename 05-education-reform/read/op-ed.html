Framework document outlining a comprehensive approach to organizing education services on PEI. Don articulates education as a lifelong, multi-role process spanning individual, family, citizen, student, consumer, and producer roles. Identifies eight core education functions (Information, Instruction, Feedback, Counselling, Funding, Products/Technology, Leadership/Governance) and critiques the current focus on age-based institutional delivery rather than service-based organization. References "Education 2020" initiative and the "Road of Life" framework.

FRAMEWORK FOR ORGANIZING 

EDUCATION SERVICES ON PEI


Some 150,000 people call PEI home; they differ dramatically from each other in terms of n age, interests, address, education, experience, aspirations, level of good fortune, physical, mental and emotional state. 


As they travel the road of life. a metaphor for the experiences one encounters between birth and death, each person fills a number of life roes, that of that of being an individual, a family member, a citizen, a student, a consumer and producer. They also encounter many obstacles including time, age, personal and family responsibilities, geography and funding, obstacles which may even be compounded and compromised by those we meet along the way: they also encounter obstcles that can be mastered by improved education – lack of skills, qualifications, and credentials. and of course he personal baggage of culture and our personal make up.


At any point they may find that that the skills they have and the skills they need will frequently differ.  The process of developing new skills, knowledge and attitudes is called learning and the discernable change that occur in us as a result of learning is called education. Education 20/20 convened a group of learners; they told us they needed, but not necessarily at the same time, Information, counselling, demonstrations, instruction, diagnosis,, learning plans, encouragement, etc.  


Provinces, as the level of government responsible for education, typically put together the following functions or services in support of the education of its citizens.

• Information: recent, independent, reliable, timel 

• Instruction: presentation, tutoring, demonstrations, debate, diagnosis, coaching and guided practice.

• Feedback:  effective, valid, transparent, relevant

• Counselling: planning, coaching, referral, advocacy

• Funding: access, space, time, funding, practice

• Products and technologies: texts, reference, self instructional material, MOOC’s and assistive technologies

• Leadership and Governance: Planning, research, advocacy, encouragement, quality assurance.

Under the British North America Act, education in Canada is a provincial responsibility, however, both levels of government are major investors; support is provided for two reasons,  firstly for the benefit of individual citizens and, secondly to ensure the availability of the skilled people needed to maintain  the future of the community in which citizens live.


 

The nature and availability of a quality education will, in large measure, determine the future of individual Canadians, Canadian communities and Canada itself; in view of its importance and given the concern about the state of public education, some thoughts  on a framework for making educational decisions may be in order.  The Road of Life is one such framework; it starts with the view that one’s education is preparation for the next stage on the road of life.  Having a framework for learning increases the chances of making relevant decisions and making those decisions more consistently.  It also provides common language and perspectives when collaborating with colleagues in the field.
