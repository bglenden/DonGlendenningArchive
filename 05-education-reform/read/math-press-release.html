   Math For Everyday Living

A dozen Islanders gathered recently to identify math skills used by Islanders in everyday life. Led by Meg Savory , an internationally recognized facilitator in the DACUM process, the group spent three days brainstorming citizen math skills.  Their list included some 84 skills under such headings as Estimating and calculating with whole numbers, Using measurement, using fractions and percentages, Spatial reasoning, patterns and relationships Interpreting statistics and financial literacy.

The analysis was initiated by Education 20/20 Inc. and, at least in part, arises out of concerns expressed by the community over the years including  the Greater Charlottetown Area Chamber of Commerce, the fact that many recent graduates are required to take basic educational upgrading in order to gain access to posisecondary studies, numeracy levels, and personal finances.   Similar documents have been found useful by students in tracking their studies year after year. 

Following completion of the analysis, a number of employers were invited to identify the skills they would like new employees to have. Copies are available from education 2020 Inc. Math
