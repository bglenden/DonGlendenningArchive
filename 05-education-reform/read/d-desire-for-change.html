Don identifies barriers to education change including politicization, absence of market forces, and scripted curricula. He proposes unbundling education services, removing the grading conflict, broadening community partnerships, and separating qualification authority from teaching. The letter reflects his core philosophy that education must evolve beyond traditional schooling.

February 3, 2011


Editor 

Guardian

Charlottetown, PEI


Dear Sir:

Conversations with educators


Over the past few months, I’ve been party to a number of public discussions as well as private conversations about education.  Three things became clear:

There is widespread support for change

There is substantial agreement as to the nature of desired changes

Most of the issues are school issues rather than education issues


Perhaps there are more changes taking place than we realize.  If so, informing the public would be useful, however, based on what I heard, major barriers to change include:

The politicization of education 

Absence of market forces.

The scripted curriculum. 


I believe that It is time to unbundle education services: 

The Department should get out of running schools and focus on education – policy, standards, qualifications, funding. 

Remove the conflict between teaching and sorting by establishing a separate qualification authority.

Broaden the focus from delivery of knowledge to development and include  arrangements with community organizations such as Junior Achievement and the 4H.  


Your views are always welcome at  HYPERLINK "mailto:standards2020@gmail.com" standards2020@gmail.com


Sincerely



Don Glendenning
