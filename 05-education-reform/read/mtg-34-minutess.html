Education 20/20

Board Meeting

November 23, 2017

The Haviland Club 

2 Haviland Street, Charlottetown, PE C1A 3S6

Time: 3:30 – 5:30 pm



Present: Don Glendenning, Amber Jadis, Katherine McQuaid, Tim McRoberts, Bill Whelan, Tess Miller, Ross Reid, Ronnie McIntosh 

Call to Order: Don welcomed the group with special acknowledgement of new Directors Tess Miller, Ross Reid, and Ronnie McIntosh. Don shared that Alice Taylor has withdrawn from the Board due to family and professional development commitments.

Approval of Agenda and Minutes: The minutes from the previous Board Meeting and an agenda were circulated.  Bill asked to add discussion on board members who would review submissions made to the Learning Partners Advisory Council to the agenda. A motion to accept the minutes, as circulated, was tabled by Katherine.  Amber seconded the motion.  Motion passed unanimously.

Open Discussion: Don began the discussion by reviewing a list of potential projects based on a previous meeting/workshop. Don shared this was important to him. He felt strongly about this as a part of his transition plan. The list was reviewed. Tim provided an overview of how the list was created. Ross indicated his interest in talking further about different forms of knowing. Don challenged the group to consider what we wanted to take on as a project. Amber discussed the idea of a public awareness campaign. Tess shared her thoughts on identifying linkages to the projects we undertake. How can we be timely? Enhance our optics? Partner with others? Bill provided an overview of the Learning Partners Advisory Council. Tess asked if there was a research or data collection piece we could undertake.

Discussion moved to training and learning. Can we be an advocate for the public so they know where to go for help? Katherine shared that these services have been in place for a long tome. Tim asked if others felt the public knew of these services. Could this be a research project? Bill shared that everyone has a role to play and education is connected to health and wellbeing.

Noting the rich conversation, Ross offered to submit his ideas regarding a definition of education. Amber shared that we could be thinking about a communications point person and utilizing various forms of technology. Amber will share more detail on this topic. Katherine talked about the importance of partnerships. Can we look at all the players out there and consider how we could partner with them?

Don thanked everyone for their contributions. 

New Directors: Don officially welcomed Tess Miller, Ross Reid, and Ronnie McIntosh to the Education 20/20 Board. Their nominations were unanimously approved through an email poll.

Wise Atlantic Proposal: A decision for this proposal should be made in December.

Website: Bill confirmed there is a donate button on our website. 

Awards Committee: Don will create a poster/press release for the Education 20/20 award. It was asked if communications for the award could be distributed through the Province to schools. Amber suggested this could also be accomplished through Home and School.

President’s Report: Don indicated his discussions in Souris are slowly moving forward. The newsletter is ready to go. The Christmas reception is booked for Wednesday, December 13 from 4:00 – 6:00 pm at the Haviland Club. 

Further discussion regarding research took place. Bill asked if it would be beneficial to identify some possible research questions. 

Learning Partners Advisory Council: Don and Ross will represent Education 20/20 regarding proposals received by the Learning Partners Advisory Council.

Adjournment: The meeting adjourned at approximately 5:30 pm.
