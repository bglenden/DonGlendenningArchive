A MATTER OF CLARITY

Education has a clarity problem. My conversations reveal concerns about clarity and visibility. Here are the clarity issues that come up most often.

For example:

  • We have a Department of Education and Early Years; before that we had “Education and Lifelong Learning”’ Doesn’t education include both of these?

  • The Department sees its role as one of “Developing and delivering high quality programs and curriculum”. I would have thought it to be something like “helping Islanders develop knowledge, skills and attitudes” needed to thrive in a democracy”.

  • Government departments are usually organized around “branches or services”; education appears to be organized around levels of schooling.

  • My search for a definition of education used by the Department revealed that the definition for “education” was dropped during a recent revision of the Education Act. So appears that we have a “school system” rather than an ”education system”.

  • The World Bank cautions countries about confusing education and schooling. I’ve scanned meeting agendas for the Provincial School Board, CAMET, CMEC and CEA and find many references that appear to confuse the two.

The Departmentt plays key role in Education in PEI. It is important, therefore that their definition be widely known. Given the above, I recommend that PEI develop and circulate a taxonomy of common education terms.

Sincerely

Don Glendenning
