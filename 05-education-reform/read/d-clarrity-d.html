Dear Editor:

A CALL FOR CLARITY

Some time ago, I set out to explore public education with a view to better understanding concerns of the local community. Education is complex; it is deeply embedded in our culture and information plays a key role.  Learners have different interests, experiences, capabilities and needs; they also fill a variety of life roles. Those who teach, or other-wise serve learners, must be very clear in their communication. Lack of clarity is a significant issue for public education 


First, I looked for a PEI definition, the kind of definition the Minister might use when speaking to retirees or the local Chamber of Commerce. I did not find one, however, the Education Act describes education as “the process of imparting or acquiring knowledge.”  I like it but find” knowledge” a bit narrow for 2023. In addition to knowledge, we need to acquire “skills, attitudes and other attributes” needed for the years ahead. We also need to share the definition widely.


Next comes the kind of help that learners need; here’s my short list: 

  • counselling, 
  • instruction,
  • learning products and technology. 
  • assessment,
  • And others 


Then comes an organization “to encourage, support and regulate the services.”  Turns out that, in spite of our rhetoric, Provinces operate school systems rather than education systems; developing an education system should be a priority.


Readers interested in public education may find the following of interest:

  • OECD,
  • World Bank, Report 208
  • Montreal Economic Institute
  • OECD – Education 2030

It is interesting to note recent changes in the senior Department management; restructuring is likely to bring about desire changes.


Sincerely

Don Glendenning
