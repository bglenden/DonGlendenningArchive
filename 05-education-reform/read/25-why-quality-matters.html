Defining Quality in Education


In recent years international agencies have released reports on /education. The fact that they exist speaks to the importance of education in world matters. reports recently by international agencies on that subject educational quality.




Why quality matters



Quality matters for many reasons and at many levels especially:

  • In a knowledge economy.

  • Movement of people/mobility of labour

  • Attracting International students

  • Preparing citizens to participate in emerging economy

  • Developing our own country

  • Significant investment by governments

  • value for money

  • Growing public demand/expectations

  • Protection of consumers

  • to attract students

  • secure revenues in competitive environments

  • contributes to economic growth

  • important for employment and social cohesion

  • increased students and employers’ expectations of tertiary education







Thos is one of a number of reports from UN agencies on matters of learning and education. Systems that embrace evidence based change are more likely to offer quality education to students



This paper encompasses a broad definition of quality and examines research related to learners, content, processes, environments and outcomes. The focus is developing each learner’s capacity act independently. The article recognizes that stakeholders often hold different views and each “judges the school system in terms of the final goals we set for our children our community, our country and ourselves”.


Definitions of quality will evolve as new research findings, information and understandings come to light.
