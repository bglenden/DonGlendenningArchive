Date: February 5, 2016


Holding schools accountable


The issue of school accountability is on the agenda of an Education 20/20 workshop on Tuesday evening and the public is invited to listen; Lizzie Yan, an M.Ed. student at UPEI, and Dr. Tess Miller, Associate Professor at UPEI report on the results of a recent survey of over 500 Islanders on one of the well-known measures of accountability, large scale assessment. The survey examined both knowledge and perceptions of Islanders especially as they relate to PISA, an international measure and PCAP, a Canadian one.


Large scale assessment serves a number of functions in an educational system including accountability, gate-keeping, instructional diagnosis and monitoring of student achievement; while conducted by educators, the results provide a useful snapshot for all Islanders. The presentation is being held on Tuesday evening, February 9 at 7:00 pm in the Southport Room of the Stratford Town Hall.
