Dear Editor:

A Question of Clarity

Education has a clarity problem; the literature refers to the problem as one of ”conceptual clarity”. For example, my search for a definition of “education” included the Department of Education, several school sites, CAMET, CMEC and the CEA. The only definition I actually found was on the Home and School web site. Seems to me that “education” should be defined and or described everywhere.

Furthermore, we have a habit of using “education and schooling” interchangeably. Both “education” and “schooling” are important but, as the World Bank points out, they are not the same.  I talk with a lot of Islanders about education, the public also seems to know the difference.

In fact, PEI operates a school system. Given the changes in education happening across Canada and around the world, a traditional school system is no longer adequate.  It would be nice if we could find a definition that encompass education at all levels and of all kinds. 

Sincerely

Don Glendenning
