Eight Year Study


During the period 1933-1941 (hence the name Eight Year Study) some 30 secondary schools were chosen to participate in a study of the pattern of courses taken by students in secondary school and student success in college. Colleges agreed to suspend their usual admission requirements. Each school organized its own programs based on known principles of learning and development, and schools focused their offerings around the general theme of “problems of living’.


The study found that there were no significant difference in scholastic achievement between students from experimental schools and students in schools offering programs in the traditional format. On the other hand, students from the 30 schools in the study did better in terms of most other aspects of college life. My experience in education supports these findings. While schooling is important, the myriad of requirements as to courses, grades, schedules and credentials impede rather than contribute to a quality education. Google “Eight Year Study” and decide for yourself

















Excellence Award


Nominations are now being accepted by Education 20/20 Inc. for the Excellence in Education Award. The award in the amount of $2,500 will recognize a school or learning centre in Prince Edward Island that best demonstrates engagement in its day to day activities. Anyone may nominate and the award is open to any school or learning center operating at the K-12 level; nominations close on May 25, 2015.
