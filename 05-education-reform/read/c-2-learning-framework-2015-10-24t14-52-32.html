About Education A Learning Framework

Occasional paper No. D July 14, 2014


Introduction

Gaining a quality education should be a major focus of every citizen and providing a quality education should be a major investment of governments. One gains an education through learning. In order to understand education and the education process, therefore, we first must understand the learner and learning process. The nature and extent of one’s learning is influenced by a number of factors.


Genetics

The ability to learn is impacted by genetics, people with certain genetic markers are more likely to finish high school or go to college than persons with other markers; genes also play a role in learning disabilities.


Learner’s past experience

So, who are our learners? Take a look in the mirror, and then look around you. You will see learners everywhere, ourselves, our families and friends, neighbours and colleagues, and strangers we encounter as we go about our daily lives; all are learners. Learners differ from each other in a variety of ways including:

  • Home experiences

  • School experience

  • Travel

  • Degree of wellness

  • Age

  • Ambition

  • Interests

  • Culture

  • Learning styles


Learner’s imagined future

From birth, we travel the road of life. It is a road with many twists and turns, hills and hollows but we are all cast in the same life roles* that of an:

  • Individual

  • family member,

  • consumer,

  • producer,

  • citizen and

  • learner


*Oregon Life Roles


Our education then becomes a series of learning experiences intended to support us in our various life roles. One could say that the purpose of education is to help one acquire the skills, knowledge and attitudes needed to function in, cope with, manage and even enjoy life’s journey.


Actual Learning Process

As a result of our prior learning, especially in the early years, we end up with a complex mix of attitudes, skills, and knowledge. But the futures we want may require a different mix of attitudes, skills and knowledge than the ones we now have. That’s called getting or improving one’s education, that’s why we continue to learn. Every new experience is a learning experience, and all the experiences put together, or at least all the experiences that changed us, constitutes our education. That makes both learning and education a process of changing behaviour, of changing:

  • Attitudes,

  • Skills, and

  • Knowledge


While learning needs and goals may be the same, we reach those goals in our own unique way.


Support needed

As we said, learning is a very personal matter and is highly influenced by our previous experiences. Support also needs to be customized. Our pace, style and even ability to learn may be influenced by our state of physical, emotional and social wellness. But the tools of educational change for learners include some combination of:

  • instruction,

  • opportunity,

  • role models

  • encouragement

  • information,

  • advice

  • Recognition.


Why is a framework important?


A framework helps us understand the complexity of learning; identifies the components of a learning strategy, increases the chances of making relevant decisions and making and making them more consistently. It also provides common language and perspectives when collaborating with colleagues in the field.
