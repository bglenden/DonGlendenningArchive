Dear Editor

Typical Education Services

Education is a service to people wanting to learn; surprisingly, the literature does not reveal just what those services are nor do education systems typically make them independently available. The list below was adapted from a list put together by the National Business Council:

  • Information – recent, accurate and independent information needed in the making of decisions about one’s own education or the education of others

  • Counselling – including diagnosis and assessment

  • Instruction - - help that includes presentations, demonstrations, practice, conversation, questioning and more.

  • Quality Assurance – to assure students their learning experiences meet community standards.

  • Learning products – books diagrams, models, photos brochures, manuals, maps, manuals, etc.

  • Technology – to provide access to educational resources, often at a distance.

  • Funding – Funding to a level and in a manner that is equitable for all Islanders.

  • Human resources – teachers, administrators, counsellors, advisors and technical specialists as needed.

  • Data – reporting student learning experiences including student progress


Given the importance and need for education in our futures, I’d like to see services be available to all citizens as part of an education system.


Sincerely


Don Glendenning
