Don critiques the PEI Department of Education's self-description as "developing and delivering curriculum." He presents broader formulations from other Canadian provinces: New Brunswick emphasizes "helping citizens gain attributes needed to be lifelong learners"; Alberta focuses on "skills needed for success in rapidly changing economy and society"; and an anonymous source describes helping "people navigate life and contribute to society." The difference between "delivering curriculum" and "helping people develop" is substantial, Don argues. His conversations with Islanders reveal expectations far exceeding curriculum delivery. He recommends the PEI Department review and expand its role statement to better align with citizens' educational expectations and international best practices.

May 31, 2024


Dear Editor:

Role of PEI Department of Education

The Department of Education describes its role as one of “developing and delivering curriculum”. My conversations with Islanders suggests that they expect more.

Broader roles include:

• NB – Helping citizens gain attributes needed to be lifelong learners

• Alberta – skills needed to be successful in a rapidly changing  economy and society

• Anon – help people navigate life and contribute to society

The difference between “delivering curriculum” and helping people” develop” is huge; the PEI statement should be reviewed.

Sincerely

Don Glendenning
