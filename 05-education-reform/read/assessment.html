No.0-1 Reframe Assessment



According to Robert Paul Wolff (A Discourse on Grading, 1969) there are three elements of student grading:

• Criticism or formative assessment, sometimes called analyses and feedback, is at the heart of teaching and learning and involves the give and take between teacher and learner about what happened or should happen during the learning process; assessment focuses on learning, teaching, outcomes, competencies, skills or even the acquisition of knowledge. This kind of assessment provides information for improving learning and teaching and is an interactive process between student and teacher designed to inform both learner and teacher how well their students are learning what teachers are teaching. The information is used by teachers to make changes in what will be taught next and when, where, how and even why. It is considered feedback to students, shared fully and probably confidentially, unique to each learner, course or incident based and sometimes anonymous and not connected to passing or grading.

• Evaluation or summative assessment is used to establish a person’s level of achievement or performance in a subject or field. Evaluation is criterion referenced, focused on establishing a level of achievement and may take into account more than course content and academic success depending on its purpose including discussion, cooperation, attendance, presentation, etc. Judgement?

• Ranking involves measuring how one person stacks up against others of the same age, number of years of study, in other countries; ranking serves a number of functions in an educational system including accountability, gate-keeping, instructional diagnosis and monitoring of student achievement.



Formative testing is obviously part of the teaching function and ranking is part of quality assurance function, but the who, what and where of summative testing is not so clear, at least not in the view of the writer.

There is no agreement as to what constitutes a level of achievement and how to measure it although seat time is the most common proxy, In recent discussions with a range of math education providers however, there seemed to be some agreement that math performance levels rests on three factors, number of skills and outcomes demonstrated, levels of performance on each and extent and nature and extent to which they are clustered in that learner’s repertoire.



The role of a teacher is to teach, to help students learn new skills and to increase levels of performance in existing skills, to gain new knowledge or change attitudes. As part of the instructional process, teachers gather information from all sources and, based on the findings, design new strategies to help learners with their learning.



It can be argued that that assessing students for the purpose of assigning levels of proficiency and ranking are not teaching functions, in fact, may not even be an education function, and represent a conflict of interest between teacher and student. For these reasons, most sectors and many educational organizations separate the teaching and the grading functions; apprenticeship, International baccalaureate program, qualification authorities, music and other are examples of the latter approach.

Linking achievement levels to ages of learners

The logic of using the same terminology to label both administrative and grading purposes escapes me; I’m sure that it must puzzle students and parents as well. The two should be separate. Have 12 levels of s subject if you must, although that seems excessive, but refer to them as levels not grades; only use Grade for administrative groupings – “My daughter is taking Level 9 Math”.

Background



Testing, standards, passing, assessment, grading, evaluation are all words associated with schools and education, and they are words that cause stress in various degrees to students, teachers, parents and the community – and the level of stress is rising







First some definitions:

• Testing - an objective and standardized method for estimating the extent of achievement based on a sample of desirable achievement

• Assessment - is a process of gathering information about a person’s performance on a task or

set of tasks for the purpose of initiating improvement

• Evaluation - an overall judgement of what has been learned

• Grading - the process of applying standardized measurements of varying levels of achievement in a course.

• Standards - a readily distinguishable level of performance, demonstrated through the making of a product or provision of a service, which is deemed to be worthy of recognition.
