Strategic paper proposing establishment of independent assessment services for certifying proficiency levels in academic subjects. Don distinguishes three assessment purposes (formative, evaluative, and ranking), arguing that schools should focus on formative feedback while independent bodies handle evaluation and certification, mirroring how sports separate coaching from refereeing roles.

Establishing proficiency levels in education

Faulty thinking

Background

  1. Public schools moving to social promotion

  2. Conflict between teaching and ranking and sorting

  3. Age-based grading is not defensible

  4. Credentials are losing their value

  5. Rise of international testing


Conclusions

  1. The following deserve further exploration:

  • Construction of a “math ladder” for describing  levels of math 

  • Current documentation by schools of a person’s actual math skills

  • Need for an independent math testing service

  • Description of math level(s) of local employers

  • Description of math needed to meet entrance requirements of postsecondary institutions

  • Establish a level of math expected for high school graduation



Proposal

To establish an independent assessment service to certify a person’s proficiency in selected subjects and fields.


Characteristics of Service

  • Identify levels on a field or subject area

  • Certify applicants level

  • Provide service to individuals and organizations

  • Provide assessment service to both providers and users

  • Independent of government

  • Open to individuals of any age

  • By application

  • Certificate not a credential

  • Results provided to person assessed and, on his request, to others

  • Partner with schools, colleges, private schools

  • Not limited to paper and pencil tests




Benefits:

  • Independent

  • Removes bias

  • Improves consistency

  • Reduces teacher workload


Student assessment


While searching for information on grades and grading, I came across an item by Robert Paul Wolff (Wolff: A discourse on Grading, 1969) in which he identified three purposes for assessing students:


Criticism or analysis, formative which is for the purpose of designing additional strategies for the purpose of helping a learner learn. Criticism prepares a teacher to provide meaningful feedback and is part of the ongoing give and take of the learning process.


Evaluation is designed to establish a person’s level in a subject or field and should be criterion referenced


Ranking intended to show how one stacks up against others; ranking results in students being winners or losers as for ranking, schools could take a lesson from sports with both coaches and referees. Ranking results from competitions; students should be encouraged to participate and teachers and students can use the results but teachers should play no part in the ranking process.


My theory is that the first kind , formative is definitely the work of schools and teachers but I feel equally strongly that ranking and grading is not a school function, perhaps not even an education function. Teachers will use the results of grading and ranking but actual ranking and grading their own students represents a conflict of interest. Sports teams have it right; they separate the functions of coaching and referring. Soooooo should schools.
