Letter critiquing PEI education terminology and organizational structure. Points out that Department of Education describes role narrowly as "developing and delivering curriculum" rather than helping people gain skills. Notes that PEI replaced "education" with "school" in legislation, limiting systemic scope. Urges Department to clarify and republish its mission.

Dear Editor:

A clarity issue

Education is a large and complex service to Islanders; it also appears to have a a clarity issue; some examples:

  • The Department of Education describes its role as one of “developing and delivering curriculum to Island youth”. While the Department   certainly does that, the statement misses the greater role of helping people gain skills, knowledge and other attributes needed thrive in the year ahead.  Preparing and delivering curriculum does not capture what the Department does nor expected to do.   
  • A second example is that of “helping young people to secure a place” in society. People fill a variety of life roles;” education” helps them to“develop skills, knowledgesttitudes  and other attributes to do so.  ‘Securing a meaningful place” doesn’t capture what schools do; neither is it sufficient to design a program or measure results. 
  • I learned recently that PEI replaced “education” in the Act with ‘’school”; seems we now have a school system rather than an “education system”. My rading of the literature suggests that education elsewhere is strengthening education offerings.
  • Most Government Departments seem to organize Departments around functions and Branches; we seem, instead, to hav organized around levels instead.


The above are based on a search of PEI education literature. Assuming they are accurate, I urge the Department therefore, to take steps now to and republish.


Sincerely


Don Glendenning
