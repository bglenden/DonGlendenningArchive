BASIC EDUCATIONAL BELIEFS

Consistent decision making requires a set of basic beliefs about learners and learning; this paper is based on the view that:

  1. Learning is an individual matter

  2. Although groups do not learn, individuals may learn in a group setting.

  3. Learning results in a change of behaviour.

  4. The role of a teacher is to help students change behaviour;

  5. People are educated whether or not they attend school

  6. Education should emphasize achievement not time spent or served

  7. Grading is not a teaching function and probably not an educational function.


TYPICAL INSTRUCTIONAL SUPPORT SERVICES:

  1. Governance and leadership

  2. Current and independent information

  3. Learning products and technology

  4. Quality assurance

  5. Instruction

  6. Human resources

  7. Funding


DEVELOPMENTS IN THE FIELD

The educational literature reveals a number of developments likely to impact the future of math instruction in PEI:

  • Format changes being adopted by the MPHEC.

  • General trend towards competency-based education

  • Emergence of dynamic/ holistic rating scales,

  • Independent multi purpose field and subject platforms as a basis for curriculum development.

  • Ungrading of schools.

  • Independent qualification authorities.

  • Disappearance of streaming

  • Education as a life long service.

  • Independent service providers


CONCLUSIONS

Based on the literature, the above noted discussions and the writer’s experience, the writer concludes that:

  1. The transition issue cannot be solved by changes to the public-school system alone

  2. Math instruction in PEI is fragmented

  3. Grading students is not an instructional or school function

  4. Cooperation among providers is hampered by the lack of agreed- upon definitions of a number of key concepts.

  5. Broadly speaking, education lacks an overarching statement of philosophy and direction;

  6. There is no formal mechanism for coordination among math providers

  7. Evaluation criteria for math should be the same for all levels of education.

  8. The way content is described differs from provider to provider.

  9. Improving transition requires cooperation of all math instruction providers.

  10. There is an interest among math providers in cooperating on a pilot project


Recommendations:

  1. Develop an over-arching statement of beliefs and mission on education in PEI as a basis of decision-making

  2. Replace Curriculum Based Outcomes with Competency Based outcomes.

  3. Adopt a common platform for curriculum development in mathematics to include field analysis, evaluation criteria and rating scale

  4. Prepare a lexicon of relevant education terms – outcomes, competencies, rating scale, etc.

  5. Identify key math profiles i.e. citizenship, employment entry and entry to post secondary institutions

  6. Develop a learning guide for each competency.

BENEFITS:

The benefits of adopting the above recommendations include:

  • An additional tool for employers when planning, hiring and promoting

  • A platform for negotiations between SkillsPEI and providers

  • A detailed math transcript for job seekers

  • Counselling tool for employers and institutions

  • Improved communication among providers and users

  • Increased relevance of what is taught

  • Clearer understanding of math and instruction process

  • A tool for the planning customized training

  • Reduces student confusion when changing schools

  • Reference tool for institutions when planning math courses

  • Guidance for workers/learners needing/wanting to upgrade smath skills

  • A reference for trade and professional organizations when organizing upgrading courses
