Detailed program notes for Education 20/20 Inc.'s "From a Learner's Perspective" workshop held October 22, 2014 at Stratford Town Hall. Don Glendenning welcomed participants, facilitated student panel discussions, and directed roundtable activities where participants mapped learner paths through the learning process. The event included flipchart documentation and post-event publication on the organization's website.
Education Philosophy & Reform

October 20, 2014

EDUCATION 20/20 INC

From a Learner’s Perspective

Southport Room, Stratford Town Hall

October 22, 2014 at 7:00 pm


1)   Objective:

  • Identify kinds of support valued by learners and chart the learning process 

 

3)  Program – 

  • Welcome – Don Glendenning
  • Student panel: Purpose: to hear firsthand from students as to the support they find useful: not looking for criticism of teachers but rather a list things that help;  Chair:  Tanya Crraig ; Panelists: two members confirmed but expecting two more;  brief comment by each plus discussion- perhaps 30 minutes in all.
  • Keywords: During panel, Mike Chapman will record key words on flip chart 
  • Discussion:  Don: brief recap of key words from flipchart lists; explain nature of discussion and development of flow chart; position flipchart so list of items can be seen during round table discussions,  ride a bicycle, write a letter, care for a pet; emphasize learner’s perspective.  
  • Roundtables: Directors sit at separate tables;  each Director facilitate or see that facilitator emerges; each table  “charts” a learner’s path through a learning cycle – how a learner incorporates the help into a learning journey; learner’s not teacher’s perspective , words such  as “receive instruction not provide instruction”; steps are drawn on large page and connected to make a flow-chart.  Not sure how long to allow for discussion but say 45-60 minutes
  • Invite participants to circulate and view other “charts”; charts can stay on flipchart stands or be posted on wall.
  • Reconvene:  Don will ask each Director for summary and comments
  • Thank all especially students (Extra Value Meal ticket at McDonalds0 for student panelists
  • Adjourn - Close session no later than 9:30; I think the building closes at 10:00


3)  Promotion: 

  • Included in two newsletters with mailing of nearly 200 including media 
  • Announcement on our website
  • Poster sent to education students at UPEI and HC
  • Our brochures on side table
  • Note sent to Guardian


5)  Follow-up:

  • Education 20/20 will provide bottled water
  • Come  a few minutes early to help arrange tables and chairs
  • Tim and Alice to bring additional flipchart stand
  • Tim to bring extra felt pens
  • Photos and story on will be sent to website
  • Will send photo and story to media
  • Repeat in other locations?
  • Volunteer photographer?
  • It would be nice to hear some suggestions as to topics for other workshops
