Everyday math skills to be mapped


Education 20/20 plans to invite Islanders to make a map of math skills used in everyday living. Although mathematics is a frequent subject of complaint, it appears that no one has actually surveyed Islanders to determine the math skills they use during the run of a day. When completed, the results will be made available to interested parties. The results may be of interest to teachers and curriculum developers but may also help when setting standards, with counselling and career planning, and even as a report of student progress.


This proposal arises out of an ongoing public concern, expressed in the media as well as in numerous conversations, about basic skill levels of young Islanders It appears to be a debate over pure math or its application. One wag compared it to a debate over how much a house painter needs to know about paint to be a productive painter.


The cost of developing a math map is estimated at $15,000; fundraising is w under way. An account has been opened at the Community Foundation in support of the project, the Foundation will provide charitable receipts for contributions of $20.00 or more. A detailed description is available on the Education 20//20 website www.education2020.ca.
