Outlines the Souris Education Innovation Fund, a program designed to encourage secondary school innovation through competitive grants. The document establishes the fund's goal, objectives (establish fund, identify activities, make awards, monitor results), and approach including local committee formation, proposal solicitation, and award announcements. Preference is given to collaborative, evidence-based projects addressing actual educational issues. Funding details include a five-page proposal requirement, with decisions managed by Education 20/20 Inc. and administrative support through the Community Foundation of PEI. The document demonstrates Don's systematic approach to educational funding and community engagement.
Education Philosophy & Reform



Souris Education Innovation Fund

Souris, PEI

BACKGROUND

Note about Souris

Education is important

Encourage innovation


GOAL: To encourage innovation in secondary school programming in Souris, PEI


OBJECTIVES:

  • Establish a fund

  • Identify activities that encourage innovation

  • Make awards

  • Monitor results


APPROACH:

  • Establish a fund

  • Establish local committee

  • Hold session on innovation for potential applicants

  • Receive proposals

  • Call for proposals

  • Interview proposers (optional)

  • Announce Award

  • project may be led by individuals and school

Community gro ups are invited to present their ideas on the use and organization of resources in their community to meet a specific educational need of community members. The call for proposals is open to any group, either already existing or coming together for the purpose of making a submission.


PREFERENCE WILL BE GIVEN TO PROJECTS:

  • Are collaborative

  • Address an actual education issue

  • Reflect and build even include existing resources

  • Be equitable

  • Anticipate continuing

  • Be based on best practices, leading edge thinking and research in the field of education


DOCUMENTATION:

  • Shall include but not be limited to a written format of five pages

  • Shall arise from the group and not be the result of a contract with professionals

  • Up to three letters of support


PROPOSALS

Documentation should address the following:

  • Description of an educational need

  • Educational principles on which submission is based.

  • Proposed activity

  • Likely future of the initiative


OTHER

  • Competition /opens/closes:

  • April 30, 2017

  • Costs, if any, are to be borne by the group making the proposal

  • Proposal may be sent to the address below or left at the office of the Community Foundation of Prince Edward Island, 119-121 Queen St. Charlottetown, PEI (Tel:902-892-3440)

  • Selection will be based on documentation received. Proposer may be invited

  • Final decisions will rest with Education 20/20 Inc.

  • Participants may be invited to present their proposals publicly

  • Education 20/20 may negotiate changes as part of the acceptance process
