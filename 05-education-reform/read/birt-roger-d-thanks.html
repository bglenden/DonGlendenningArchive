Comprehensive thank you and progress report dated June 1, 2016, to Roger Birt. Don thanks Birt for remembering initial conversation about young people's education and future, emphasizing alignment of purposes. Describes education system needing "outward looking, nimble organization" focused on developing mature adults. Reports increased education dialogue frequency, growing bilingual newsletter addressing identified niche, improved school awards program with planned expansion to Atlantic provinces, webinar under development, and emerging focus on educational standards. Notes Education 20/20 received charitable status from Revenue Canada this year. Credits Birt's support as essential to establishing independent organization working for education change.


June 1, 2016

Mr. Roger Birt, President

R. Birt and Associates

27 Brackley Point Road

Charlottetown, PE C1A 6Y1


Dear Mr. Birt:


This comes to thank you for your support for efforts to improve education on PEI. I remember well our first conversation, your concern about young people, about their education and their future; that’s been our mission as well.


For education to improve, it needs an outward looking, nimble organization focused on helping youngsters develop into mature adults. Are we making progress? I think so. The Premier’s commitment to consultation is a step on the right direction – but it is not enough. The enclosed Call for Action, lists seven urgent actions; as you will see, they are mostly organizational, but it’s the best list we’ve seen to date.


As for Education 20/20 Inc., we are pleased to report that we received charitable designation from Revenue Canada earlier this year. As for last year, there’s been an increase in the frequency, volume and focus in education dialogue; our monthly, bilingual newsletter is filling a niche; the school awards program is better focused and we hope will be expanded into neighbouring Provinces; we have a Webinar under development, and we are turning our attention to the issue of standards.


On behalf of Education 20/20 Inc., I thank you, Roger, for your encouragement and financial support; thanks to you, we’ve broken new ground in education through the establishment of an independent organization to work for change. It likely would not have happened without your long-term support and, of course, we’d be happy to have your support continue. I’ve enjoyed our discussions and appreciate your availability when I drop by your office unannounced; please look after your health.


Warmest regards to you and Eleanor.


Sincerely



Don Glendenning

President
