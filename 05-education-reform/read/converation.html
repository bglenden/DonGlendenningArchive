Teaching

People often need help with their learning; “teaching and educating” can be thought of as delivering that help. We usually think of delivering learners to a place of help but the reverse is also true. We can and should provide help to learners wherever they are.

That’s what an education system is. An education system is an organization that proviides help to learners. It organizes  support for learning of a kind and at a time convenient for learners. We did yhat during Covid; we need to cfinnd ways to ontinue it. ProviiConvening    learners in school is one alternative. 

Learning is a personal matter; only the learner can learn but that learning can be in any    field, any place and at any time. 

Although mention is frequently made of education systems in the education literature, my search f Departments Education in Canada only revealed school systems. 

Perhaps we should consider a switch from school based education to learner based education, especially in the senior grades. there is no doubt in my mind that such a move add additional depth to public education.

Education

Education is an umbrella term. An education system brings together services, products and people dedicated to helping others learn; leadership is provided by Departments of Education who promote, encourage, support and regulate education for the whole community – all ages, all fields and all purposes. Sometimes the numbers involved, the nature of the subject  and the cost, call for separate facilities called schools, which, like a rink or a hospital, is a space designed, equipped, staffed and operated to support the education process. 

The complexity of organized education is confusing; the emotionally laden content and long history doesn’t help.

A third item is about the matter of assessment and grading. The current grading system comes under substantial criticism as a measure of school success and a report on what has been learned. Should we consider others?

Given the importance of community participation in the education process, it may be time for PEI to stablish a forum for discussion of educational issues; they’ve done this in North Carolina; others simply have local education associations. The Conversation and the Hershberger institute are other approaches. Or perhaps CAMET or the CMEC could assume a leadership role in airing alternative and emerging ideas. Or perhaps we could create something new.
