Draft outline of multiple proposed initiatives under Education 20/20 umbrella: Awards program (school focus with community engagement), Information dissemination (public-oriented media and papers), Enabling Change framework (island-wide, 3-5 years), Leaders Club ("Forty Under Forty" emerging leaders with annual Charlottetown gathering and national expansion), and Credentials for Independent Learners (competency-based recognition for technical professionals). Emphasis on information, engagement, awards, and innovation support to drive systemic educational change. - **Key People**: None named - **Key Organizations**: Education 20/20 - **Tags**: PROFESSIONAL, EDUCATION - **Eulogy Relevance**: MEDIUM - Shows breadth of Glendenning's educational reform agenda and strategic thinking
Education Philosophy & Reform

Draft

November 4, 2012

PROPOSALS

Awards

  • School not individuals
  • Community Focus
  • With only one high school
  • Start with engagement
  • Award, cash and community event


Information:

  • Geared to public
  • Supports change and engagement
  • Media, electronic and occasional papers
  • Include education writers


Enabling Change:

  • a framework for encouraging change in public education
  • Island wide
  • 3-5 years
  • Focus on information, engagement, awards and support for innovation


Leaders: 

  • Young acknowledged leaders
  • Deemed to have made a difference
  • Potential for making difference
  • Leaders club
  • Forty under forty – have an age limit
  • An Island or maritime group but eventually a national group
  • Annual gathering in Charlottetown where each is expected to make a presentation
  • Visit other innovations in Canada
  • Take on a leadership role in an innovative project
  • Selected people may form a think tank
  • Initial work in 2013 with major meeting in 2014


Credentials for Independent learners: 

  • Recognized full or part credential –course, diploma or degree,
  • Challenge only
  • Competency based
  • Of interest for entrants from other countries
  • Pilot with technical people/skilled workers
  • Client oriented
  • May require a separate qualification authority
