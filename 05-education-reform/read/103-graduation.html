Opinion letter to the Charlottetown Guardian questioning the meaning and purpose of high school completion on PEI. Raises concerns about whether high school credentials reliably measure preparation for work and further studies, and suggests postsecondary institutions should have independent entrance requirements. Editor,

Charlottetown Guardian

Charlottetown, PEI  


Dear Sir: 

Giving meaning high school graduation


High school completion is considered an important first step in one’s formal education; its importance is indisputable but purpose(s) are unclear-. It could be to ensure that Islanders have skills, knowledge, attitudes and values needed for:

Citizenship

Entry into employment

Enter postsecondary studies

Thrive in the years ahead


Teacher(s) judgements

School attendance


Having, or not havfor citizenship

ing, a high school credential and often determines access to postsecondary institutions, employment, apprenticeship and even certain professional and technical institutions.


All of which points to the need for clarification as to:

Are high school results a dependable measure of preparation for work and further studies

Should postsecondary institutions have their own system for determining entrance requirements

Shouldn’t  public school system be required to accept back any of their students not accepted into postsecondary institutions 0r employment for academic reasons.  


There are a number of possible answers and solutions; this letter simply seeks clarification as to which solution prevails.


Sincerely, 



Don Glendenning
