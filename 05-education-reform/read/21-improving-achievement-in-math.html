Improving math levels


I’ve had a number of conversations recently about improving the level of math among high school students. Here are a few ideas that emerged:

  • Math ladder – establish levels of math that reflects the level of difficulty of the subject rather than the age level of the student and report level gained rather age level of student.


  • Common set of outcomes – Mathematics in the public school system is outcomes based. If the same applies to Holland College and UPEI, why don’t institutions agree on and work from a standard set of outcomes?


  • Establish a math testing center/service – similar to that provided by the Royal Conservatory of Music. This would allow teachers to teach, remove any suggestions of favouritism and reduce differences among schools


  • As an aid to study, provide each student with a set of outcomes – so that h/she becomes familiar with the language and can track his/her progress outcome by outcome.


  • Report achievement on each outcome – thereby increasing consistency, conveying a clearer picture to postsecondary institutions, alerting employers as to what has been learned.
