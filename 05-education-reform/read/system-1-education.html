A concise foundational document defining core education concepts. Don establishes precise terminology distinguishing learning (changing knowledge/beliefs/values), teaching (arranging experiences), education (sector of helping others learn), school (building with groups), and schooling (pattern of support). This establishes the linguistic foundation for his broader argument that PEI has a "school system" rather than an "education system." The document signals his intention to clarify definitional confusion as central to systemic reform.

Educ/System 4



PEI Needs an Education System-1

  • Learning is about changing what one knows, believes, values and is able to dp.
  • Teaching is about arranging experiences that help others learn
  • Education is a sector of the economy that includes the people and services of those who help others learn
  • Education is a specialty
  • School usually refers to a building equipped to handle groups of learners and teachers
  • Schooling refers to a pattern of offering support       to learners


PEI has a school system; it needs an education system would better serve its needs. Let me explain.
