Purpose Not Clear

Doc5/Hand

As I pen this letter, I am reminded of my favourite song in Mission Band as a youngster many years ago in the Miramichi. It included the words “Dare to have a purpose firm; dare to make it known.” Making the purpose of education “known”, is my challenge to public education.


Purpose is about direction, about what one hopes to achieve. Purpose provides the fundamental reason for establishing an organization and is the key element in developing a structure. Everything, it seems, flows from purpose. I write this letter, not because I disagree with current purposes of education but because the current purpose or purposes of education for PEI are not clear


Education is about helping people learn; It is said that every Islander fills six life roles, that of an individual, family member, citizen, student, consumer and producer. Any role could be considered “meaningful” and education is a factor in preparing for any and all. It seems to me that helping people prepare for any or all roles is a legitimate purpose although the content, context, emphasis or depth of study may vary. The current PEI purpose of providing ‘for the development of children so that each child may take a meaningful place in society” leaves most Islanders out of the education process and, furthermore, lacks sufficient detail to plan content or measure success.


Purpose is not a new education issue in PEI; Education 2020 Inc. published a “Call for Action” on education in April 2015. While not specifically addressing purpose, the paper recommended “a clear and compelling vision of education for all Islanders.” A statement of purpose could constitute the opening lines.


Clarifying our education purpose should be a priority; nothing this government will do in education is more important. Identifying purpose should engage the public and start yesterday.


Sincerely


Don Glendenning

D-22-02
