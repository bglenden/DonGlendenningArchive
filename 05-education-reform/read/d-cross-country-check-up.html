Don responds to a CBC Radio "Cross-Country Checkup" discussion of math skills, noting Quebec's strong PISA performance may relate to "student-friendly" math outcomes. He expresses frustration at the absence of national analysis comparing math competencies and emphasizes the need for a "qualification framework" like those used in other countries. He advocates for a separate qualification authority to certify achievement in math, similar to apprenticeship, Swimming Canada, Conservatory Canada, and the Canadian Mathematical Society models. The letter shows his comparative knowledge of international education systems and his consistent proposal for independent assessment authorities. His public engagement with national radio shows his thought leadership.
Education Philosophy & Reform


June 1, 2015


Editor,

Charlottetown Guardian

Charlottetown, PEI


Dear Sir:

Math skills


I found the discussion of math skills “Cross-country Checkup” most interesting:

With reference to the strong performance on PISA tests by Quebec, I wonder if it may be, at least in part, because of more “student friendly” math outcomes; readers who are interested may compare by Googling “Quebec/curriculum/math/grade nine”. 

I’ve been searching without success for a national analysis of math competencies; it could make a major contribution to standards and transferability of credit.  The logic of levels of math based on the age of the learner continues to escape me. 

Perhaps it’s time for a “qualification framework”; other countries have  them but the topic doesn’t even appear on Canadian radar.

I like the idea of a separate qualification authority to certify achievement similar to the model followed by apprenticeship, Swimming Canada, Conservatory Canada and the Canadian Mathematical Society.


I wonder what other listeners thought about the program.

Sincerely,


Don Glendenning

A long-time student of education
