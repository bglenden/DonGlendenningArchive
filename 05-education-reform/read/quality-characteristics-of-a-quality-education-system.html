Characteristics of a Quality Education System


In Newsletter No. 10, we reported on characteristics of a quality education published in a 2000 UNICEF report.  We’ve since reviewed some OECD and UNESCO document and have expanded the list.  A quality education system is one where:

  • Learners who are healthy, well-nourished and ready to participate
  • Environments that are safe and respectful 
  • Content that is relevant to functioning in a modern society
  • Outcomes are linked to personal and community objectives
  • Support is focused on learners and learning.
  • Information and advice are both timely and independent.
  • Teachers are professional, autonomous and accountable


To put them in perspective, these characteristics would apply  to a province, any province; the extent to which any or all would apply to a school district or to a school will depend upon how a province organizes its education support services..  


The strength of these characteristics, over others I have seen, is their focus on learners and learning.  Having a clear statement of this kind, I believe, certainly makes for more consistent decision making at all levels..
