This more ambitious draft addresses the coming election and education's place in public discourse. Don argues that government conflates setting rules with running schools, creating inflexibility, reduced innovation, and politicization of professional issues. He outlines options: an independent education board or government delegation of operations while retaining oversight. The document is notably fragmented and shows significant revision, suggesting Don was wrestling with comprehensive education system reform during election season. He emphasizes that consensus exists on needed changes but implementation remains blocked.
