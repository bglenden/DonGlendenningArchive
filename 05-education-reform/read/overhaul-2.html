SYSTEM OVERHAUL-2

4-11

Education is complex; information plays a key role. Learners have different interests, experiences, capabilities, needs and expecctations, and all will move on to a variety of life roles but life roles are also changing. Those who teach, or otherwise serve learners, must be very clear in their communication. Conceptual clarity heightens understanding of key words and concepts; lack of clarity is a significant issue for public education. Conceptual clarify is also a must in planning and assessing performance


An organization’s structure outlines how services are to be directed to achieve purposes, goals, roles and responsibilities. While organizations may take many forms, service organizations, like education, are usually local or decentralized giving members freedom to respond to individual and local demands .


Key Definitions:

  1. Education – a process of supporting others in the acquisition o knowledge, skills, attitudes and other attributes.

  2. Educated – the recognition of a person’s level of performance in day to day life experiences


First steps of reform should focus on:

  1. Terminology – a taxonomy of PEI terms and understanding

  2. Purpose(s)– in sufficient detail to direct activities and assess performance of participants.

  3. Focus - on task rather than schools

  4. Vision and plan of action - to include beliefs and guiding principles; its development should engage the community and the finished product should be widely circulated

  5. Organization -that reflects functions or services offered not ages served

  6. Accountabilities – at all levels that are clear, measurable and monitored

  7. Autonomy – front line workers in a service field need both competence and autonomy the OECD reports that PISA results are better from schools with autonomy in school day to serve individual differences

  8. Services – service should be defined, easily accessed and not limited to learners in schools.

  9. Individual profiles – should be a available to all who enter the system

  10. Adopt competencies as a basis for instruction
