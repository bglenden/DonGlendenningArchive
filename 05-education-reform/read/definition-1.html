A concise early letter establishing Don's core concern: the Department of Education no longer has a published definition of "education." The term was removed from the Education Act and replaced with "school." Don notes this linguistic shift appears strange and counterintuitive, particularly given education's importance to personal and community futures. He questions whether this change applies to all schools or only public schools, highlighting ambiguity in government communication. The letter succinctly frames definitional clarity as foundational to effective policy discussion and accountability.

Dear Editor:

Clarity

Definitions are crucial to the clarity of communication. 

Recently I went looking for the definition of “education” used by the Department of Education; couldn’t find it. (IT is not a personal strength)  Couldn’t even find the definition in the Education Act. 

On enquiry, I learned that the definition of “education” had been replaced in the Act by a definition of “school”. Not sure if this applies to all schools or only to public schools. 

Given the importance of education to both our personal and community future, it seems strange for the Deparment to be narrowing its offerings.

Sincerely

Don Glendenning
