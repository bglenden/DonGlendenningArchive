D Box Stores of Education

Don uses a retail metaphor to critique schools as "box stores of education" that, while efficient at accommodating large numbers of learners, lack ability to cater to individual needs. He notes that box schools employ missionary-like zeal to capture all learners and provide babysitting services. While acknowledging their role, he advocates for educational diversity including specialized schools (basic skills, sciences, arts) and alternative settings (home learning, workplace). He notes Nova Scotia's Junior Achievement credit system and advocates for similar credit for experiential learning. The metaphor demonstrates his ability to make complex arguments accessible and his belief that educational diversity and local control serve learners better than monopolistic systems.


July 3, 2012


Editor

Guardian

Charlottetown, PEI


Dear Sir:


Imagine a community with only box stores in the retail field. Gone are the shoe stores, dress shops, pizza parlours, corner groceries, country stores, fast food outlets and muffler shops. Having only box stores may be efficient but I would miss the specialty stores and personal service – and having all box stores would make for a much less interesting community.


Schools have become the box stores of education; people who work there have a missionary-like zeal to capture all learners. Box schools certainly can accommodate large number of learners for long periods of time but have a limited ability to cater to individual needs. The fact that box schools also provide babysitting is also an attractive option.


Box schools certainly have their place in education but also have their limitations. I’d like to see a greater variety of options. Why not schools specializing in basic skills, the sciences and the arts. Even encourage people to learn at home or in the workplace; while in Halifax recently, I learned that the Nova Scotia system now gives school credit for successful experience in Junior Achievement. Instead of trying to duplicate JA in schools why not give a school crdit for JA experience. . Given the new technologies, options to box schools become increasingly feasible and attractive.


Let’s make our community our education centre


Other examples where education would benefit from local control are welcome at standards2020@gmail.com


Sincerely


Don Glendenning

A long-time student of education
