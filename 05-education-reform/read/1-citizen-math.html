CITIZEN MATH

A group of PEI educators wondered about math skills used by citizens in everyday life; they searched the internet without success. Then then decided to ask fellow Islanders; the result is the pamphlet shown above.


The analysis produced 72 competencies in seven fields: Estimate and calculate with whole numbers, Use fractions, decimals, percentages, rations and rates, . Use spatial reasoning; Recognize and use /pPatterns and relationships; Interpret statistical Iiformation; Manage personal finance and Use measurement. It was developed by Education 2020 Inc. in consultation with panel of citizens and under the direction of a facilitator trained in the DACUM process. It is a list of skills practiced rather than a list of skills desired.


DACUM analysis is a Canadian achievement having emerged from a major research initiative funded by the Government of Canada in the 1960’s and 1970’s and pioneered in Holland College in the 1970’s and 1980’s. It differs from most analyses in that it gathers information directly from practitioners. Analyses of this kind serve many purposes including providing a basis for:

• Developing curriculum and training programs,

• Preparing position descriptions,

• Setting standards,

• Developing Teaching and learning plans and

• Recording student progress


An analysis is a neutral document in that it simply identifies skills performed. Users select competencies that best serve their purpose, how they will be used, level of performance expected or required and how performance will best be demonstrated. As part of the learning process, learners would be expected to demonstrate skills, self-rate and have ratings confirmed by an instructor. Education 20/20 Inc. acknowledges the anonymous contribution of an Island donor to the cost of the project and to panelists who participated in the analysis process.
