GOING TO SCHOOL 0R GETTING AN EDUCATION

Good’s Dictionary of Education defines education as “the aggregate of all the processes  by means of which a person develops abilities of positive value in   the society in which he lives.” While I might argue some specific words, this meaning generally reflects my view. 


The purpose of an education system, it seems, is to arrange experiences that help people  learn.  Typical education services include the provision of information, counselling, instruction, assessment, educational products and  human resources.  Although learning is a personal and individual experience, it is useful and convenient (and sometimes necessary) to bring people together to a place called “school” – a school serves a purpose similar to a hospital or shopping plaza. But learning that counts is not limited to learning in school.  Concern about this belief was expressed ai a 2018 report from the World Bank which chided Ministries as to what constitutes an education. 


Your Editor believes that the PEI view of education has changed from that of providing a service to individuals to that of “adding an education product” to one’s resume; products in the form of credits,  courses, certificates, majors or degrees.  It’s an exact reverse to developments in the US in which colleges and individuals may take “memberships” in institutions and access educational help as needed.  PEI should have a conversation about the emerging model.


To move forward, PEI needs:

  • A glossary of educational terms
  • A set of over-arching guidelines as a basis for decision making, and
  • A collaborative operational  framework 


POT POURRIA

Microcredentials

A recent Hechinger report carried an interesting item on microcredentials; these are short, specific courses or programs that lead to employment or upgrading of work qualifications.  The approach is a natural in times of uncertain employment and makes student debt less foreboding.

COMPETENCIES

“In summary, competencies define the applied skills and knowledge that enable people to successfully perform their work, while learning objectives  are specific to a course of instruction.  Both should be written in specific, measurable and behavioural terms. Competencies are generally written at a highr level than a learning  objective because they require more complex levels of performance.” - Santacatarina
