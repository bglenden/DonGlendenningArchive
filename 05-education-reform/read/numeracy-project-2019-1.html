A comprehensive project proposal outlining the theoretical foundation and methodology for the Numeracy Project. The document defines numeracy as application of mathematics across life domains, presents the Conference Board of Canada's assessment that Canada earns a "C" grade in numeracy, and proposes a structured approach to identifying essential numeracy competencies (estimating, counting, patterns, fractions, spatial reasoning, statistics, measurement, money management, time management, chance). The workplan includes six implementation tasks from initial analysis through adoption of assessment criteria and desired profiles for citizenship, employment, and postsecondary entry.


NUMERACY PROJECT

1-8-Num/Project-1

---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Purpose: To identify and describe a universal list of numeracy skills used in every day living as a foundation for curriculum development.


Background

Numeracy refers to the application of math; it is described as “having the confidence and skill to use numbers and mathematical approaches in all aspects of life - at work, in practical everyday activities at home and beyond, as consumers, in managing our finances, as parents helping our children learn, as patients making sense of health information, as citizens understanding the world about us.” A report by the PEI Department of Education on The Early Numeracy Intervention Program (ENIP) reports that numeracy “gives students the tools and the skills to solve problems logically, and to developmental flexibility, independence of thought, self-efficacy, and the ability to persevere” According to the Conference Board of Canada, “Over all, Canada earns a” C” grade on in adequate numeracy skills”


Growing support

The literature shows growing support for instruction in numeracy; some teachers teach it directly while others support numeracy through on-line courses. Most seem to consider numeracy as an additional offering and not a replacement course. The National Numeracy Organization (UK)proposes:

  1. Every teacher become a teacher of numeracy. That does not mean that every teacher becomes a maths teacher but that numeracy, just like literacy- is recognized as an intrinsic part of every subject across the school curriculum.

  2. A new measure of numeracy proficiency should be introduced for all young people at the age of 14. This would establish a national benchmark for the level of numeracy needed for further study in all subjects between the ages of 14-19

  3. An additional and universally respected qualification in numeracy (or core maths) is needed alongside GGCSE maths. Politicians and curriculum and qualifications regulators must give serious and urgent consideration and introduction of dual GCSSEs in maths and numeracy


Numeracy Map:

There appears o be no standard list of numeracies or even categories of categories, nor universal way of determining them; based on the writrs review however, it appears that math curriculum is built on an analysis of mathematics. Numeracies, on the other hand, stress applications and result from a structured survey of the population in need of help. Below is a typical list:

  • Estimating and calculating with whole numbers

  • Counting and grouping

  • Recognizing and using patterns and relationship

  • Using fractions, decimals, percentages, ratios and rates

  • Using spatial reasoning

  • Interpreting statistical information

  • Using measurement

  • Managing money

  • Managing time

  • Dealing with chance and uncertainty



Benefits of a separate list of Numeracies

Serves as a common base for users and providers of math services, course purchases, program planning, student career planning, interagency discussion, student tracking, employment and tutoring, student record keeping, employer hiring, setting entrance requirements. Industry surveys, employer associations, gap analysis, certification, staffing etc.
