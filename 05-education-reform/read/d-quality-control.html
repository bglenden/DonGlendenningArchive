Don uses restaurant quality control as analogy: customers vote with feet, regulators enforce standards, poor performers lose licenses. He contrasts this with education, a monopoly system where students and parents have no recourse for poor service, cannot access alternative suppliers without paying twice, and have limited choice. Schools are not client-centered or consumer-friendly, yet funding is identical regardless of quality. He calls for quality monitoring and consumer protection mechanisms in education comparable to other sectors. The piece (though containing some rough drafting) illustrates his consumer-choice orientation.
