Educational framework document titled "Elements of a Learning Support System" from Education Series. Presents comprehensive learning support model viewing every person as lifelong learner on "road of life" from birth to death. Outlines five life roles: individual, learner, producer, citizen, consumer, family member (Oregon Educational Act, 1990). Defines learning as behavior change across three domains: attitudes (affective), skills (psychomotor), and knowledge (cognitive). Identifies conditions needed: wellness (physical, mental, emotional, social) and opportunity (transportation, facilities, personal funds, advocate). Lists support elements: information (public health-style resources), professional advice (counseling, assessment, referral, advocacy), instruction (formal, tutoring, coaching), recognition (credit, credentials, feedback), and engagement (school culture, specialists, role models, funding). - **Key People**: Not specified - **Key Organizations**: Oregon Educational Act (1990 reference) - **Tags**: EDUCATION, PROFESSIONAL, HOLLAND_COLLEGE - **Eulogy Relevance**: HIGH - Demonstrates Donald's comprehensive, holistic approach to education supporting the whole person across lifespan, reflecting his educational philosophy and practical frameworks for educational support.
