In contrast to File 10's critique, Don outlines what constitutes a quality education system: current terminology, clear publicly displayed purpose, discussion of services, service-based organization, transition from grades to profiles, shift to learning guides, evaluated outcomes, focus on education rather than schools, public engagement, school autonomy, collaborative (non-silo) structures, accessible system information, education vs. school systems distinction, clear accountabilities, education associations/advisory committees, and increased public promotion. Dated October 13, 2023, this represents his vision for positive transformation.

CHARACTERISTICS

OF A

QUALITY EDUCATION SYSTEM


Education is intended to serve citizens and the community in which they live; characteristics of a quality education system include:

  1. A current taxonomy

  2. A clear and prominently displayed purpose

  3. A discussion on services

  4. Structue/Organize around services



  1. A switch to profiles from grades

  2. Switch to learning guides

  3. Evaluate each outcome



  1. A focus on education rather than schools

  2. Public engagement


  1. Autonomy of schools in their day to day operation

  2. Collaboration rather than silos

.

  1. Easy access to system information including related research


  1. An education system rather than just a school system

  2. Clear accountabilities

  3. An education association and/or advisory committees

  4. Increased public promotion
