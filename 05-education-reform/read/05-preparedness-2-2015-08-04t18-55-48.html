Preparedness-2

Occasional Paper No. 5 August 4, 2014



In order to better understand the issues of youth, in late 2013, The Community Foundation of Prince Edward Island (CFPEI) undertook a survey of over 200 Island youth on a number of issues that directly affect them; the report, Taking the Pulse of Island Youth, available on the Foundation’s website at www.cfpei.ca, provides a snapshot of the findings. Bright spots include a positive sense of community, emerging entrepreneurial spirit, volunteerism and new postsecondary offerings. The main concerns include a lack of jobs, surviving on low-paying jobs, educational deficiencies and drug and alcohol addictions. On the subject of education and learning, the study reports a high rate of completion of high school and postsecondary education, a high level of inclusivity and relatively affordable tuition costs; young people also expressed concerns about their readiness for the next stage of life including:


“preparedness gaps with regard to readiness for

post- secondary study and the workplace” – Vital Signs, CFPEI, 2013


Following the publication of the CFPEI Report, Education 20/20 organized four public discussions for the purpose of informing the community of the report and its findings, learning more about youth issues in our communities, exploring issues of education and learning, and to identify actions to be taken to close gaps. Discussions were lively and wide ranging, and carried on well after the formal session ended. The final report included a number of conclusions and recommendations of which the recommendation below triggered this report.


  1. Further study be undertaken to clarify the gaps in preparation for further study and for work. (Preparedness, Education 20/20 Inc., Occasional Paper No. 1)


Further study includes:

  • Search for master list of outcomes, skills or competencies

  • Assessment services available in PEI

  • Rating Scale

  • Benchmarks

  • Alternative assessment systems




Don Glendenning

Charlottetown, PEI
