Don identifies five inexpensive indicators of educational progress: updated philosophy of education, community participation in curriculum committees, departmental reorganization based on functional analysis, terminology change from grades to levels, and reinstatement of high school exemptions. March 28, 2016


Editor,

Charlottetown Guardian

Charlottetown, PEI


Dear Sir: 

Signs of an educational spring


I’ve been asked what I am looking for in the way of signs that education is improving; I know that the government is planning some major organizational changes but here are five simple, inexpensive and non-disruptive  changes that would indicate a change of thinking:

An updated statement of philosophy of education to clarify the focus especially around purpose and goals.

The appointment of community members to curriculum committees. 

A reorganization of the Department based on a functional analysis. 

Find a word, other than grade, to describe levels of student achievement.

Return of high school exemptions.


Any one of the above would be like the first robin in spring; three or four would indicate that a new season has arrived.


Sincerely,


Don Glendenning

A long-time student of education
