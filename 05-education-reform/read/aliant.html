Letter dated April 25, 2013, to Bruce Howatt, Vice President of Bell Aliant, describing Education 20/20 Inc. as a national not-for-profit organization dedicated to quality education. Solicits funding for an online course delivery workshop featuring visiting instructor Professor White-Hassler. The letter explains MOOCs (Multiple Open Online Courses) and their importance for educators, requests $350 sponsorship for the May 14 workshop. Demonstrates Don's forward-thinking approach to technology in education and his ability to secure guest experts for educational events.

Dear Mr. Howatt:


This letter is a request for funds in support of a workshop for educators and the public on the delivery of courses to students on line, but first some background about Education 20/20 Inc.

Education 20/20 Inc. is a national not-for-profit organization whose purpose is to encourage and support a quality education for every Canadian. We are governed by a volunteer Board , people who have demonstrated an interest in and knowledge of education but do not represent any institution or institutional point of view. Our purpose is to inform, educate, provoke discussion, encourage, and support quality education and lobby for change when the evidence warrants it. We operate independently, but cooperatively, with other organizations in a transparent manner based on sound business practices.

As a result of meetings and conversations with a large number of people throughout the Island, we have found a widespread interest in and desire for change on the part of the public and educators alike; there is also a realization that the changes that are needed are unlikely to take place without some external interventions. As you know, changing an existing organization is not easy but we believe that change is both possible and necessary; our focus will be on learning rather than teaching, because much of what is important in education occurs outside schools.

Activities to date include:

  • Publication of a newsletter, copy enclosed, directed to the public at large

  • An award to a school that best demonstrates the concept of engagement in its day to day activities

  • Discussions with educators about the improvement in the level of literacy of Islanders, especially levels of reading.

  • A series of workshops on innovation and best practices.


To be more specific, MOOC’s (Multiple Open On-line Courses) are now a fact of life; educators need to learn how to take advantage of them. To aid in understanding, we have organized a workshop on the delivery of courses on line. Our instructor, Professor White-Hassler who has many years of experience in computer based applications for teaching and administration in Arizona, Alaska and Connecticut. He has offered a number of courses on line, is currently living on PEI and delivering courses on line to students enrolled at Manchester Community College in Connecticut. In addition to his experience, I have had a number of conversations with Professor White-Hassler and am very impressed with his philosophy of education

We think that this course is a natural fit with your company and are seeking your financial support in amount of $350. The course will be offered in Charlottetown on May 14; an early response therefore would be appreciated. We do not have charitable status at the moment, however, the Community Foundation for Prince Edward Island is supporting our initiatives and can offer receipts acceptable for tax purposes. Contributions should be made to the Foundation a 119-121 Queen Street, Charlottetown, PEI, C1A 4B3.

I would be pleased to provide more information about Education 20/20 Inc. our findings about education, our activities in general or about the proposed course.

Thank you for your consideration

Sincerely



Donald E. Glendenning

President
