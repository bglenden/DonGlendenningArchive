July 7, 2015


Editor,

Charlottetown Guardian

Charlottetown, PEI


Dear Sir: 

Student staff ratios

The issue of student staff ratio keeps popping up.  It is an area of interest to me personally because I once turned down an offer because the Board-to-be tried to interfere with my proposed organization.  Student staff ratio may be useful to administrators when allocating funds but, as one report said, has little relevance as an indicator of the quality of service offered by a school.

My sense of the issue is that staffing decisions can best be made at the school level taking into account the strengths of existing staff members nature of the school and strengths of existing staff and biggest timewasters especially of professional staff.  The literature supports team teaching, focused staff development, with diagnosing, prescribing, and assessment of students assigned to specialist teachers.  Patterns may differ from school to school, not necessarily cheaper but dollars will likely be spend differently.

Many years ago my Professor of Higher Education, Dr. Gibson, told the class to “read Kant and hand in a paper on how you would organize a university”; the learning that resulted from the assignment has stood me in good stead ever since.

Sincerely,

Don Glendenning

A long-time student of education
