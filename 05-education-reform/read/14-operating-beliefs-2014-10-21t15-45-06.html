A brief position paper articulating Don's core operating beliefs about education systems: distinguishing education from schooling, criticizing schools' knowledge-focused approach, identifying systemic resistance to improvement, and asserting that credentials should be independent from teaching institutions.
Education Philosophy & Reform

Operating beliefs – education system

  • Education system and school system are not synonymous
  • We have a school system masquerading as an education system; 
  • Schools focus on the delivery of knowledge; 
  • The education system lacks incentives to respond positively for calls for change
  • The Importance of institutions (schools, colleges and universities) in acquiring an education is on the decline 
  • There are no apparent benefits for the education system to do a better job students to reach standards early or reach higher standards 
  • Credentials and qualifications should not be determined or awarded by those who teach
