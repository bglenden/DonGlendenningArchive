Don reflects on a community gathering of 35 people discussing education reform, noting recognition of needed changes, the persistence of issues, and rising public frustration. He suggests mathematics as a starting point for systemic reform, advocating for clarity on educational purpose (skill development, economic competitiveness, childcare), identification of required math competencies from multiple stakeholders, and structural changes to accommodate individual learning timelines. He emphasizes that the public possesses knowledge and capacity for reform without waiting for top-down mandates, calling for grassroots engagement and local ownership of solutions.

January 25, 2014
