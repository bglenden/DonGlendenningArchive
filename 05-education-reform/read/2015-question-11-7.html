QUESTION 11A -PURPOSES AND ACTIVITIES -7

For application to CRA for charitable status


APPROVED PURPOSES

The purposes of Education 20/20 Inc. as found in our documents of incorporation are as follows:

1 To promote education and educational opportunity for every Canadian

2 To exercise leadership in the improvement in all facets of education

3 To foster cooperation between and among all stakeholders

4 Undertake and support research and innovation

5 improve public understanding of the education system


As part of the process of preparing this application, we reviewed and revised the objectives as given in our incorporation documents to better reflect our activities and also to meet the requirements for registration as a charitable organization. The review and rewriting have benefitted the organization; based on our experience since our incorporation and future plans, we propose the following objectives:

Planned Webinars

B) ADVANCE EDUCATION THROUGH INCREASING THE AMOUNT AND FLOW OF INFORMATION AND IDEAS BETWEEN AND AMONG EDUCATION PARTNERS


It takes an informed and engaged community to educate a child; evidence shows that students perform better in schools where parents and the community work together; in PEI, during the last few months, the Department of Education has dramatically expanded the number of external credits. Education 20/20 would like to see cooperation between schools and other community organizations expanded even further.

In saying this, we are aware of, and have reviewed Policy CPS-022 (Effective date: September 2, 2003), and understand the limits placed on charitable organizations especially as they relate to lobbying and political activities


On September 25, 2014 Education 20/20 Inc. made a presentation to the English Language School Board on Prior Learning Assessment and Recognition during which we advocated for PLAR, Challenge for Credit and exemptions as a way of broadening studies, putting students in charge of at least some elements of their program, and increasing engagement. Work with employers:

About a year ago, the Community Foundation of Prince Edward Island conducted a survey of over 200 young people to better understand their issues. (www.cfpei.ca). On reviewing their report, Education 20/20 was struck by the “preparedness gap”, that is the preparedness of young people for further education and for work. Early in 2014, Education 20/20 Inc. organized workshops in six communities across the Island to publicize the findings, determine the extent of the concern, and identify actions, if any, that could or should be taken. Perhaps the most significant finding was the lack of communication about educational matters between providers and users of educational services. Survey results have informed the following activities:


To date, Education 20/20 Inc. has not been able to find well defined and broadly accepted achievement levels on school subjects. Furthermore, it appears that grade levels are proving inadequate as a measure of student achievement; teachers tell us that in any school grade and subject, levels of student achievement will vary significantly, 3-4 gade levels not uncommon but five to six not rare in any classroom. We have a committee exploring options as to steps that may help.


Working with employers and educators seeking alternative ways for employers to obtain the information they want about an applicant’s "essential skills". A mechanism for PEI is now emerging that will see the Holland College Assessment Centre and Workplace Learning PEI offering a testing service for employers and employees. The initiative is continuing. . Based on discussions to date with employers, UPEI and Holland College, the likely outcome will be a system, similar to that practiced in music, whereby the establishment of levels is handled by the Royal Canadian Conservatory of Music rather than local teachers of music; exploration continues.


One of the conclusions resulting from our preparedness workshops is the need for direct dialogue between education and the community it serves. To bridge the gap, we are organizing a series of discussions; each session will have a one page background paper, a presentation if appropriate, and followed by open discussion. The objective is to increase communications rather than to reach agreement. The pilot run of discussions will begin in early 2015 between 4:30 and 6:00 pm with a group not to exceed 20 people; additional groups will be organized if numbers warrant.


We will continue to engage employers and educators in projects and panels to increase communication; service clubs will be encouraged to invite educators, especially principals, as speakers, and will make a special attempt to produce occasional papers and media releases about items of interest to them.


Working with community-based education organizations


We are all familiar with schools, colleges and universities as providers of educational services; we are less familiar with, and often overlook, the many other providers of educational services including youth organizations, cadet programs, sports development programs, etc. More to the point, staff in these groups are seldom included in conferences, workshops or other educational activities. Education 20/20 Inc. views them as an important and growing segment of our education system and plans to (a) survey them as to their needs and interests and (b) actively recruit their participation in ongoing activities.




Working with the community at large


Parents play a pivotal role in the education of their children; children perform better when their parents and the community are engaged in the education process and yet, broadly speaking, the school system does not encourage deep engagement. To encourage engagement, Education 20/20 Inc. has instituted an award of Excellence to a school or learning centre that best demonstrates a high level of engagement in its day to day activities, especially those activities over which the school has control. Surveys of parents and other stakeholders are under discussion; one is a survey of interests of stakeholders as a basis for program planning with the other being a survey of stakeholder views on education issues with results publicly reported.

(C) ADVANCE EDUCATION BY PROVIDING ADULT EDUCATION COURSES, TRAINING PROGRAMS, ASSESSMENT, COUNSELLING AND TUTORING


There are a number of significant gaps in our instructional services:


Continuing Education gaps


Education is a lifelong process; most of our educational funding, however, goes to institutions and for the education of the young. But in rapidly changing times and internationalization of the world, continuing education becomes even more important. In addition, it appears that most part time courses now available are limited to courses being offered to others on a full time basis. Although technology has put knowledge building within easy reach of many adults, maintaining and progressing in the workplace may also require new skills and attitudes that can best be dealt with in group settings. In any case, our first step will be to undertake a survey to better define the gap. With that in hand, we can develop a plan of action.


Essential Skills


In terms of basic or essential skills, we have identified at least two groups of young people who need help. One group consists of people with high school graduation diplomas but insufficient skills to enter further studies or work. Education 20/20 plans to offer a tutoring program fashioned on the Sunshine tutoring program offered in Dartmouth Nova Scotia. Under this arrangement, sessions will be of two hours duration once a week in the late afternoon. Tutors will be volunteers; space has been offered by the PEI Association of Professional Engineers; costs will be minimal and will be met through donations from individuals and businesses. If demand warrants, we will grow this service into a full service study centre where youth can get customized help to meet their particular needs. That help may be as simple as counselling and advice but also may include diagnosis, access to resources, tutoring, counselling or referral to another source of help. Our longer term plan is to expand the service as needed and as resources allow including career information counselling and advice but also may include diagnosis, access to resources, tutoring, counselling or referral to another source of help. The services may be expanded to other communities


A second group may have the skills that employers need/want but do not have the documentation to prove. Education 20/20 Inc. is actively engaged with the business and education communities (Holland College, the University of Prince Edward Island, and community educational organizations) seeking a resolution to the problem. The resources and good will are available in the community; information and scheduling should solve the problem.

Emerging fields –

Our world is dominated by technology. Most computer users benefit from less than 10% of a program’s functionality. There are contemporary computer languages that are learnable by virtually anyone. Knowledge of a computer language not only helps us better use existing programs, but also enables us to start building some of our own tools, changing us from passive to active technology consumers. We want open this door to more people, especially young people. We are actively investigating introductory computer language courses on a part time basis available through fees or sponsorships at a cost of $100 per participant. Robotics and 3-D printing are also on our list.
