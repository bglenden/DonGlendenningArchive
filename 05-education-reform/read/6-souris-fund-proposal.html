This formal grant proposal for the Souris Education Innovation Fund establishes criteria and processes for awarding local educational grants. The initiative encourages innovation and best practices through local committee oversight, proposal review, and project monitoring. Preference is given to collaborative, community-issue-addressed, equitable projects reflecting leading-edge research. Documentation limited to three pages ensures accessibility for grassroots educators. The approach emphasizes community agency and shared decision-making, with Education 20/20 providing facilitation and resources. This document demonstrates Don's belief that educational improvement emerges from community-level innovation rather than top-down mandates.

Education Innovation Grant

Souris, PEI


GOAL: Encourage innovation and best practices in education in Souris, PEI

OBJECTIVES:

Working with Souris educators, Education 20/20 Inc. proposes to:

  • Secure funds

  • Identify activities that encourage innovation

  • Provide financial support

  • Monitor results


APPROACH:

  • Secure funds

  • Establish local committee

  • Promote educational innovation

  • Solicit proposals

  • Review proposals – (interview optional)

  • Work with proposer to further shape proposal - optional

  • Award grant

  • Receive report

PREFERENCE WILL BE GIVEN TO PROJECTS THAT:

  • Are collaborative

  • Address an actual education issue

  • Build on existing initiatives

  • Are equitable

  • Reflect best practices, leading edge thinking and research


DOCUMENTATION:

  • Shall include but not be limited to a written format of not more than three pages

  • Shall arise from the group and not be the result of a contract with professionals

  • Up to three letters of support


PROPOSALS

Documentation to include:

  • Description of an educational need

  • Educational principles on which submission is based.

  • Proposed activity

  • Likely future of the initiative

  • Details of the operation


OTHER

  • Competition /opens/closes:

  • Costs, if any, are to be borne by the proposer

  • Selection will be based on documentation received; proposer may be interviewed

  • Education 20/20 2ill have reasonable access to observe project operation

  • Decisions to grant rests with Education 20/20 Inc.

  • Participants may be invited to present their project publicly

  • Education 20/20 may negotiate changes as part of the acceptance process

  • Education 20/20 assumes no legal liability for the project

  • Proposal may be sent to the address below or left at the office of the Community Foundation of Prince Edward Island, 119-121 Queen St. Charlottetown, PEI (Tel:902-892-3440)
