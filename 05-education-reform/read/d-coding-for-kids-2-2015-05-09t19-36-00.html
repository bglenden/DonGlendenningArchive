Coding for kids


Britain launched a new computer curriculum as part of The Year of the Code, the Fredericton Library offers sessions on coding, the Ottawa Library offers opportunities for patrons to try out a variety of leading edge technologies, and the Squamish Nation in British Columbia is using coding as a tool to revitalize cultural languages and persons in carcerated in California are taught coding  as a tool to help them reneter th community


Coding is an important language for our young people to learn and learn now. According to Don Glendenning, President of Education 20/20 Inc. “Coding introduces people to a different way of thinking, it is a language of the age of technology”.  It’s not because everyone should or will become a programmer but it may be considered a tool for tomorrow.  There are many computer languages and they can be learned to different levels but even a few hours of instruction and hands on experience, say 25 to 50 hours can make a difference.


Schools are a natural home to host coding courses in that they have computers and support services; libraries are also natural hosts although are not always as well supplied with the technology.  Some schools have already been able to make coding available at little or no cost to students.
