Near-identical version of File 4 (St. Dunstan's letter), again requesting $15,000 from St. Dunstan's Foundation for the mathematics mapping project. Shows Don's methodical approach to institutional fundraising and his vision for education reform through collaborative, standards-based initiatives.
Education Philosophy & Reform

St. Dunstan’s Foundation


For some time now, we’ve been working with people from UPEI, Holland College, Department of Education and the community to consider ways of improving math levels; as a result of our discussions it is our view that the improvement needed requires cooperation among providers at all levels -public school, postsecondary and work entry.  Step one is the development of a common math map, a framework so to speak, for course planning, student tracking, counselling, instructional development, tutors and tutoring and reporting student progress.  We also think that a math map will dramatically Improve communication, engage the community, ensures relevance, Increase understanding of both the math and instruction process. We are focusing on math, not because of its importance but because of an interest and willingness of partners to collaborate.


A proposal for developing the framework is attached; the cost is estimated at $15,000.  This letter is                 a request for financial help.  The project has the capacity to improve the lives of all Islanders regardless of  their current age, location or educational goals.   


Attached you will find a proforma analysis, rating scale and a learning guide; the actual productsfor mathematics will arise from the study. I would be delighted to provide further information or meet with the Board to describe the project in more detail.


Sincerely


Don Glendenning

President
