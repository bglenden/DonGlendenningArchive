March 12, 2014

Education and community needs

The word ”culture” arose often in a workshop on Tuesday evening in Summerside; while not actually defined, it included the skills and attitudes needed to maintain a local economy, skills for living such as literacy, numeracy and personal finance, and the social skills needed to support a culture of care.  A second theme was that of the disconnect between communities and their schools, the lack of engagement, low attendance at school events, problem of voter turnout and limited communication.  And this is happening, it was pointed out, although it is recognized that students perform better when parents are engaged.  One specific suggestion was that CBC Compass create a regular slot for items about education.

The workshop opened with a presentation on the findings of a recent survey of over 200 Island youth between the ages of 15 and 25.  The survey, Taking the Pulse of Island Youth, dealt with youth concerns about health, education, employment and social well-being.  The presenters, led by Steve McQuaid, VP of the Community Foundation of Prince Edward Island and Katherine MacDonald, Researcher, provided details about the methodology and results.  

The workshop, chaired by Don Glendenning, was organized by Education 20/20, an independent group devoted to improving the quality of education for every citizen.  The next workshop is planned for Wellington on April 25.  Readers who are interested will find further information on the website, www.education2020.ca including an address for receiving the monthly newsletter.
