Education Philosophy & Reform


August 22, 2015


Editor,

Charlottetown Guardian

Charlottetown, PEI


Dear Sir:

BC Court Decision

During the recent sitting of the Legislature earlier this year, there was considerable discussion about class size and composition.

Readers may know that a recent a decision by the BC Court of Appeal removes some of the restrictions on class size and staffing patterns in BC schools; those changes alone, it seems, should reduce teacher stress and encourage innovation in schools, innovations like the one in Michigan where a school decided to operate without a principal and another I visited once in Arizona where school ranking dramatically improved by adding a half dozen teaching assistants.

BCTF is appealing the decision to the Supreme Court; if the decision is upheld I assume that it will apply to all provinces.

Sincerely,


Don Glendenning

A long-time student of education
