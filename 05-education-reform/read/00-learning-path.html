For immediate release

Date: April 19, 2015

Learning Path


Charting a learning path is not easy, but 22 Islanders thought it was important enough to give it a try. The group, consisting of parents, members of the business community, educators and others with an interest in the subject, met at the Haviland Club to exchange views on a draft “learning path” developed by Education 20/20 Inc. A learning path, as envisaged by Education 20/20, is based on the view that learning is a process and, like any process, can be shown in chart or graph form.


The process started some months ago with a question to learners about the kind of help they found useful when learning something new; their list included such items as information, encouragement, practice, feedback, self- study materials, instruction and self- analysis. The second question asked of students concerned the order in which help was needed. Since not all learners learn at the same pace and have different learning style, showing how to chart a path that provides help to students when they need it, posed a major issue. The process was drafted and presented to the group for discussion.


A learning path is an attempt to increase understanding through visualizing the steps taken by a learner, learning from a learner’s perspective so to speak; only when we understand the learning process, can we properly design an education system to support it. To quote one participant, it is an attempt “to model the process of how we learn”. While obviously of interest to those who teach, it may also be of interest to parents, group leaders or others who instruct or make decisions about our education system.


The result is a draft, although not a perfect one, and an interest in discussing the matter further; a couple of people have already provided follow-up comments. Education 20/20 will return to the drawing board and try to factor in such things as learning in the affective domain, learner interests, discovery learning. motivation and whether a single model can be drawn for learners of all ages. Education 20/20 plans to distribute the findings in a future newsletter and post the learning path on its website.
