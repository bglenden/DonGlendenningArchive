Don outlines fundamental principles about education: learning is personal, education results from experience-driven change in knowledge and skills, families and teachers are major contributors, provinces mandate schooling to boost learning. He identifies concerns about narrow curriculum focus, lack of learner choice, weak transcripts, and poor correlation between school curriculum and life needs.

Glendenning Notes

EDUCATION

  • Learning is a personal event; only a learner can learn
  • Learning results in changing one’s knowledge, skills, attitudes, values. etc. 
  • One’s education is the result of one’s learning
  • Our futures and those of our community will be determined largely by the nature and level of our education
  • Two of the major contributors to one’s education are the family and teachers.
  • Provinces/Communities require young people to attend school for a dozen years as a boost to their learning
  • Provinces cover costs of basic education
  • Basic education may be described as?
  • The Province considers basic education to be ?
  • The Province specifies the content it will pay for
  • Transcripts of what has been learned contain little detail
  • Students have little choice as to what they are taught
  • Writer has found little correlation between what is taught and what is needed in later life.
  • Appears to be little flexibility to respond to individual needs
