Written December 1, 2014, Don reflects on transformative changes in rural life (electricity, indoor plumbing, water systems) and argues education needs equivalent systemic changes. His priority list includes: adequate time, fair teacher compensation, criterion-referenced assessment, challenge-for-credit options, school autonomy, competency-based education, and teaching professionalization. The letter is partially incomplete but conveys his conviction that education transformation requires addressing multiple structural factors simultaneously.
Education Philosophy & Reform


December 1, 2014


Editor

Charlottetown Guardian

Charlottetown, PEI


Dear Sir:


Changes that make a difference


I remember the day when electricity arrived in the community where I grew up; lamps and lanterns were put away, soon we had water at the turn of the tap, next came indoor plumbing, all were changes that made a difference not only in our home but in the community at large, they were changes that made a difference. Education needs some changes that make a difference. On my short list of changes that make a difference, I would include:

  • Time

  • Paying teachers for what they do

  • Criterion referenced assessment

  • Challenge for credit

  • School autonomy

  • Competency based education

  • Professionalization of teaching
