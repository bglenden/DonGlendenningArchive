This letter challenges the fundamental practice of teachers ranking students, which Don views as a conflict of interest that distorts education's meaning. He questions why those who teach should award credentials, as this creates incentive misalignment. Don proposes a provocative interim measure—issuing credentials on "evaporating" paper valid only 10 days after graduation—to highlight the absurdity of static credentials. He argues that an independent agency should award all degrees and diplomas, separating teaching from credentialing functions.

January 6, 2015


Editor,

Charlottetown Guardian

Charlottetown, PEI
