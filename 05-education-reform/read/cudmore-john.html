Dear Mr. Cudmore:

Math on PEI


Although PEI math scores on National and international tests have risen, friends tell me that math has not improved on the shop floor; I’ve been wondering why.


All Islanders need math. they need to be able to measure, sort, calculate, group and estimate numbers, space, time, distance, etc. and they need to do it quickly, easily, accurately; and consistently. This approach to math is called numeracy and it involves the application of math to every-day lives. Numeracy is being adopted elsewhere; usually in addition to traditional math. Numeracy focuses on the math one uses rather than the math one knows.


Education 20/20 Inc. proposes to undertake a survey or analysis of math skills used by Islanders in every day life. Making such an analysis involves bringing together a small group of representative Islanders for a two- day of brainstorming session; we expect that the process will result in lost of 100 or more skills. It’s a proven technique, we used it regularly at Holland College but apparently there is no one on PEI now with the expertise. The cost of an analyses is $15,000.


We are looking for help cover the cost. The Community Foundation, 121 Queen Street, Charlottetown, PE, C1A 4B3 will accept donations on our behalf and issue charitable receipts.


I will be in touch in the next few days to answer any questions and see if you can help. Thanks in advance; the attached page provides additional detail.
