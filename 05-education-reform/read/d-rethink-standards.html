This letter presents a brief but significant argument that educational standards should be reconsidered. Don critiques the practice of labeling students by years studied and proposes starting fresh with standards development. He defines standards as distinguishable levels of performance worthy of recognition, independent of time spent in school. The letter suggests that Canada's approach to schooling standards lacks the flexibility and competency-based focus needed for true educational advancement. Though brief, it encapsulates his broader philosophy of performance-based rather than age-based assessment.
Education Philosophy & Reform


January 6, 2015


Editor,

Charlottetown Guardian

Charlottetown, PEI


Dear Sir:

Time to rethink standards

It is time to rethink the basis of schoolschool standards, not 

Same across Canada

Problem with labelling people by the number of years they studied

Start afresh

A standard is - a readily distinguishable level of performance, demonstrated through the making of a product or provision of a service, which is deemed to be worthy of recognition. 






Sincerely,


Don Glendenning

A long-time student of education
