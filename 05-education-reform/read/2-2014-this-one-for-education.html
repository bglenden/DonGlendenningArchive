Commemorating Confederation's 150th anniversary in 2014, Don proposes using this milestone to establish an educational legacy rather than merely celebrating the past. He views education as central to Canada's future and suggests creating an innovation fund, think tank, national center for public engagement, demonstration projects, or citizenship focus. The letter reflects Don's belief that education transcends institutional change to address fundamental national development. His vision positions education as the determinant of Canada's collective future.
Education Philosophy & Reform

June 13, 2012


Editor

Charlottetown Guardian

Charlottetown, PEI


Dear Editor:

2014: This one for education


In 1864 our political leaders met in Charlottetown to commence discussions on a scenario for political union; discussions went well and were continued, a few weeks later in Quebec City. The result was the forming of Canada. Should the event be celebrated? Absolutely!!! Ours is a remarkable country, and it is still evolving; we should celebrate that fact at every opportunity.


Should we have a party? I think we should; it doesn't have to be a fancy one, some entertainment, some music, a bit of food, some storytelling and reminiscing - and don't forget to invite some friends


Should we give ourselves a gift, a legacy to mark the event? Probably not necessary, but it would be nice; something about our future rather than our past. When I think of Canada's future, I think of education; I'm of the view that education will, to a large extent, be the determinant of our future – both individually and as a country. No, I'm not thinking of a new course or program, or even a new institution. Perhaps an innovation fund, a think tank, a national centre for public engagement; a few demonstration projects?  Or perhaps a focus on citizenship? Something to position us for the future?


l'm sure that the PEl20L4 would welcome your views. And, if you want to share your ideas with me, you may do so at standards2020@smail.com.


Sincerely




Don Glendenning
