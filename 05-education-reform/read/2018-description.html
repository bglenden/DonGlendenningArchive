Official award description for the 2018 Excellence in Education Award ($2,500). The award focused on collaboration as a strategy for improving education in PEI public schools. The document outlines that collaboration can take multiple forms—between teachers in the same school, between different schools, with colleges/universities, or with outside organizations. It details submission requirements (maximum 3 pages), application criteria, and contact information for Education 20/20 Inc. headquartered at 101 Kent Street, Charlottetown. This document demonstrates Don's vision for fostering collaborative educational approaches.



EXCELLENCE IN EDUCATION AWARD (2018)

$2,500

Competition closes: April 30, 2018



BACKGROUND

Education 20/20 Inc. is pleased to offer an Excellence in Education Award in the amount of $2,500 for the purpose of supporting and encouraging a quality education for Prince Edward Islanders,


The Award is intended to encourage collaboration as a strategy for improving education in PEI public schools. Collaboration may be thought of as two or more people working together to accomplish an objective or complete a piece of work. Collaboration is a well- known contributor to both teacher performance and student achievement, and is a key element in driving school improvement. Collaboration comes in many forms, between teachers working in the same school, among all teachers in a small school, between schools serving the same age group, between schools at different levels, collaboration with a college or university, or between a school with an individual or other organization. Experience with collaboration shows that the actual practice of collaboration changes as experience grows.


SUBMISSIONS

Submissions should address the underlying issue, the objective and the educational principles on which submission is based as well as the proposed activity, number of people involved and the time period over which the activity will take place. Also tell us how the money will be used and whether or not the activity is likely to continue.


APPLICANTS

Applicants should note that the competition is limited to Prince Edward Island; costs of the proposal. if any, are to be borne by the group presenters; decisions will be based on documentation received; Education20/20 Inc reserves the right not to make an award in any year; final decisions will rest with Education 20/20 Inc.; participants may be invited to present their proposals publicly. submissions shall include but not be limited to a written format not exceeding 3 pages, proposals shall arise from the group and not be the result of a contract with professionals; and selection will be based on documentation received.
