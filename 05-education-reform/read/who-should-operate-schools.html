Don argues against direct government operation of schools, proposing instead that while government has an important role in education, it should not operate schools. His concerns center on politicization, centralized decision-making that's distant from schools, institutional resistance to change, and government neglect of other essential educational responsibilities. This positions Don as favoring decentralized governance and greater school autonomy while maintaining government financial support and policy oversight.

October 14, 2014

Editor, Charlottetown Guardian

Charlottetown, PEI


Dear Sir:

Who should operate schools


There is an important role for government in education but, in my opinion, it is not in operating schools.  Here’s why I think that:

  • Politicization
  • Pushes decision too far up the line
  • Impervious to change
  • Leaves untended things that government should do



 



Sincerely


Don Glendenning

A long time student of education
