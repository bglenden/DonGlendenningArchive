Role of government in education 103

Time for an overhaul of education

Communities are of the view that education is important; in response, government has put in place an education system, an array of programs and services to encourage and support the education of its citizens .Governments do so for two reasons; (1) to support the education of its citizens and (2) because they need skilled people to ensure the viability of the commniity in which they live.   Education support typically comes in the form of funding, regulation, information,  counselling, instruction, quality assurance, education products. 

Communities suffer, however, because government narrowly defines education (using education and schooling interchangeably) , by labelling of  the portfolios as “Education and lifelong Learning” or,  as the case of the  former Government, “Education and Early Childhood Development”,   Government fails to recognize that education already  encompasses both. In addition, Government has a patchy record when it comes to responding to community needs,  part time and continuing education, services to small  communities.
