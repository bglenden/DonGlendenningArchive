This planning document outlines criteria and procedures for Education 20/20 to establish partnerships with other organizations. Don identifies three partnership inquiries (Handle With Care, after-school technology classes, and an arts organization) and proposes categorizing relationships along a spectrum (partner, affiliate, associate, network member, endorsement, sponsor, agent). The document establishes evaluation criteria based on shared philosophy, compatibility, values alignment, and mutual support.


Plan/HWC

January 17, 2015

EDUCATION 20\20 INC.


Notes on Guidelines for Partnering

Background:

At its regular meeting on January 14, 2014, Directors discussed establishing a relationship between Education 20/20 and Handle With Care (HWC) and asked that a paper be prepared for consideration by the Board at its next meeting. In the discussion that followed, it was suggested that the issue has at least two parts (1) a procedure for dealing with “partnering” issues, and (2) an actual recommendation on the relationship with HWC. Actually there have now been at least three expressions of interest in partnering with Education 20/20 Inc.

  1. Handle-With-Care (HWC) an “interactive program to help parents and caregivers, including those who work in early childhood settings, to promote the mental health of young children from birth to 6 years. The program began in 2002, is funded by Human Resources and Social Development Canada with PEI as a test site and Alice Taylor as the PEI partner.

  2. After school classes for young people in such subjects as computer languages, 3-D printing and robotics

  3. A new group thinking of establishing an organization to support a form of the arts not now available on PEI


Tom’s first question will be “Why do we need/want to form a relationship?” Possible answers include because we:

  1. Share common goals

  2. Strengthen our respective organizations

  3. Share costs


My search of the internet uncovered a number of levels of association

  1. Partner – an arrangement where parties agree to cooperate to advance their mutual interests

  2. Affiliate – officially attach or connect a subsidiary group or a person

  3. Associate-

  4. Network member

  5. Endorsement – giving one’s approval or support

  6. Sponsor

  7. Agent


Criteria to be applied in each case:

  1. Similarity of philosophy

  2. Compatibility

  3. Obligations/commitments

  4. Shared values

  5. Learner centered

  6. Access to promotion

  7. Each publicly support the work of the other
