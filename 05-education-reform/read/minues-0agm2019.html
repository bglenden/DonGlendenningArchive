Meeting minutes documenting Education 20/20's AGM held June 18, 2019, with Don Glendenning as President. The president's report highlighted the successful completion of the Souris Research Project and noted that the annual award was not given due to limited applicants. A significant agenda item involved Don's proposed research on "how much math do you need to know to fulfill the obligations of citizenship," advocating for practical mathematics education focusing on financial numeracy and everyday life applications. Directors were elected including Sharon Cameron as new President.

Education 20/20 AGM Meeting Minutes

Call to order

A meeting of Education 20/20 was held at Stratford Town Center on June 18, 2019,4:30-5:30 PM

Attendees

Attendees included Don Glendenning (President), Bill Whelan, Amber Jadis, Sharon Cameron, Ron Mcintosh, Katherine Macquaid, Ross Reid.

Members not in attendance

Tess Miller could not attend

Approval of Agenda

Agenda was presented and approved unanimously.

Approval of minutes

Bill Whelan moved to accept the minutes of the 5th Annual General Meeting. Ross seconded. The motion was carried.

Reports

President’s Report


  • Education 20/20 did not give out our annual award this year as there were only two applicants. 
  • The completion of the Souris Research Project was a highlight of the year. We gleaned much insight into issues of continuing education and community learning in Souris and identified some potential avenues for moving forward with the results through community partnerships.
  • The President’s report concluded.


  • 2018 Budget was approved.




2019 Budget Preview:


  • $15, 000.00 budgeted for survey on math skills used in everyday life- try to connect this with educational goals. Proposal in process. Focused on math for citizenship.
  • Upcoming budget approved.

New business

Discussion of proposed math skills research project:

  • Don posed the question how much math do you need to know to fulfill the obligations of citizenship? He proposed that we undertake an analysis of the required practical math skills of PEI citizens. 
  • First stage of the research will be focused on identifying broad range of categories of math skills such as; financial numeracy for banking, geometry for .
  • The study should be concerned with how citizens use math in day to day life
  • Some concern was raised about some potential issues with the survey. Can citizens accurately identify the categories of mathematical skills they use in daily life?
  • (Action) Bill suggested that we look at some different survey instruments to try and match this project with an appropriate survey method. A review of OCED survey instruments was recommended.

Nominations:

  • Katherine move to nominate: 
  • Sharon Cameron to the awards committee
  • Sharon Cameron to president of Education 20/20
  • Bill Whelan to Vice President of Education 20/20
  • Ross Reid was nominated to Secretary of Education 20/20 
  • Ross Reid Seconded the motion
  • The motion was carried for the slate of new directors

Announcements

On behalf of all the members of Education 20/20, Bill Whelan thanked Don Glendenning for his wisdom, advice, and advocacy in his role as President and founder of Education 20/20. 

We extend a warm welcome to Sharon Cameron in her new role as President of Education 20/20.

Ross Reid



Secretary
