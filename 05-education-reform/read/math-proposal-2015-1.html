Another version of the mathematics education proposal using the "Mount Mathematics" metaphor. Don advocates for establishing standardized competency levels aligned with PISA and OECD frameworks, promoting common standards and increased student mobility.
Education Philosophy & Reform


2015

Proposals


Mathematics



Educational issues:

  • Great energy expended in trying to decide in advance an appropriate elevation for students in each grade
  • Lacks logic
  • Open to interpretation by various users
  • Altitudes attained are open to interpretation
  • No national standard


Math:

  • Math as a mountain
  • It is a prerequisite for accessing opportunities in later life
  • Structure:   numeracy, elementary mathematics, algebra, geometry, trigonometry, calculus, set and function, complex numbers, logarithmec functions, and infinite series
  • Why climb Math Mountain: It is a prerequisite for accessing opportunities in later life
  • Cannot find listing of either outcomes or competencies
  • Complete analysis needed before



Proposal:

Develop a tool to educators


  • Undertake an analysis:
  • Levels as described by PISA
  • Provincial may offer other courses as well but they must have different identity
  • Similar to establishing levels in music and skating
  • Independent assessment service
  • Learners apply when ready
  • Establish benchmark


  • Establish levels
  • Levels – link with PCAP or OECD if possible
  • Similar to establishing levels in music and skating
  • Independent assessment service
  • Learners apply when ready


Advantages/benefits:

  • Common standard
  • Aid mobility
  • Much of groundwork already done
  • Reduce curriculum development workload


Project:

  • Establish committee
  • Undertake analysis
  • Establish levels
  • National consultation
  • Distribute widely
