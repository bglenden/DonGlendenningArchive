Dated September 4, 2013, this letter describes Education 20/20's Excellence in Education Award program in detail. Don explains that unlike the private sector, education rarely recognizes and rewards excellence in community engagement. The award would recognize schools demonstrating strong community-school partnerships, as research shows such engagement directly correlates with improved student achievement, teacher morale, and communication. The committee is chaired by Eileen Conway-Martin, guidelines are finalized, and a $2,500 cash award accompanies the recognition plaque. Don explicitly solicits Duffy's sponsorship, explaining the award's significance in breaking new ground in provincial education practice. This reflects Don's understanding of incentive structures and organizational change.
Education Philosophy & Reform


September 4, 2013



Dr. Regis Duffy

31 Maplewood Crescent

Charlottetown

PE C1A 7R5


Dear Regis:



Thanks for coffee and conversation today; I enjoyed the chance to get caught up and to brief you on our activities.

As we discussed, award programs are common in the private sector; not so in education. We’d like to change that by offering an Excellence in Education Award to a school that best demonstrates engagement in its day to day activities. Why engagement? Because schools are neither inclined nor required to engage with their communities in the education process; yet we know that when they do, student achievement improves, teacher morale rises and communication increases. We want to highlight engagement and reward schools for their efforts.


We have an Awards Committee chaired by Ms. Eileen Conway-Martin from our Board of Directors, guidelines are ready and a tentative schedule for implementation has been set. Attached is a brief description, however, I would be pleased to provide more information about the nomination and selection process if you are interested. In addition to a plaque, we propose a cash award of $2,500 with the money to be used by the school for any educational purpose. We are told that, given current school budgets, this would be a significant incentive.


Thank you for your sponsorship; we now move from having a proposal to having an actual project. Your cheque should be sent to the Community Foundation of Prince Edward Island, 119 Queen Street, Charlottetown, PE, C1A 4B3; the Foundation administers our funds and will issue a charitable receipt. Also, please let me know how the award should be named.


We are excited about this initiative, about breaking new ground in education and about your support.

Thank you.


Sincerely



Don Glendenning

President
