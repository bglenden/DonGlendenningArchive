Dear Editor:

Multi age grouping


I support the sentiments expressed by Marcella Bradley Byrne in this morning’s newspaper.


After reading the letter, I looked at a file I had put together in 2002.  On the second page of the first article I found the following two statements:


“It is possible to establish an effective learning environment in an elementary/middle level school when there are only a few children of the same age.”

… studies consistently show students in multi-age classes do as well as students in single –grade classes in academic achievement, study habits, social interaction, self-motivation, co-operation and attitudes toward school.”


The problem, as I see it, is that we have an inflexible, factory like system based on serving groups of students of the same age.  It assumes that groups learn; they don’t although individuals may learn in group settings.  There are alternatives but we seem reluctant to consider them.


What’s lacking is the will to organize in a way that supports individual learners and small schools.


Sincerely



Don Glendenning
