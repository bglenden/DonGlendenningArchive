Short letter to the editor proposing that PEI develop a comprehensive education system serving all Islanders, not just schoolchildren. References Education 2020 for learning models, competencies, and holistic rating scales. Volunteers Don's assistance with demonstration project funding—showing his ongoing commitment to education improvement.

Dear Editor: 

I’m thinking that PEI should develop an education system; a system of services open to all Islanders not just for young people in schools. Something like a health system that includes hospitals but not limited to care in hospitals. We have the knowledge and technology to do so; it just needs someone to make it happen.

Step one involves a defined learning model; readers are referred to Education 2020 for such a model developed a few years ago with the help of Island students. Step two involves a switch from outcomes to competencies to provide both the content,  context and learner tracking.  Again, I refer readers to an Education 2020 “math Map” to fill in the operational details. The third element is an holistic rating scale; one will also be found at Education 2020.

Ideally, the public system, the College and the University might collaborate in a selected area – literacy or numeracy comes to mind. ( I’d be glad to help with the search for funds for a demonstration project.)


Sincerely

Don Glendenning

31
