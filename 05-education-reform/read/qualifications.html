Credentials and qualifications


Public complaints about school standards arise, at least in part, because we confuse credentials and qualifications.


Diploma, according to Wikipedia, is of Greek origin meaning "folded paper", and is a document issued by an educational institution certifying that the holder has completed a certain programme of studies. A qualification, on the other hand, speaks to the issue of attitudes, skills and knowledge required to successfully perform in a role or at a task. In order to teach, one needs a credential, a university degree, and a qualification, a teaching licence, the latter attesting to one’s ability to teach. It is customary to separate the two functions except in schools.


We assess students for three reasons: (1) to determine how best to help them learn formative assessment, (2) to establish their level of achievement in a subject in field – summative investment, and (3) to rank them against others in a field.


I am of the view that teachers and formative assessment is an integral part of teaching, the assessment needed by a coach. I am also of the view that summative testing places a teacher in the role of referee with his or her own students, and constitutes a conflict of interest. In other words, teachers should not take a role ranking their own students or judging them for the purpose of prizes and awards.
