Comprehensive funding solicitation letter to Steven Loggie, Vice President of Maritime Electric, introducing Education 20/20's initiatives and philosophy. Don articulates the organization's three core strategies: independent information via monthly newsletters and website, engagement through island-wide workshops discussing education gaps, and recognition of innovative schools through awards and incentives. He details specific workshop dates (Stratford, Montague, Summerside, Wellington) and presents a detailed budget breakdown seeking $22,000 in support. The letter demonstrates Don's vision of addressing systemic education problems through public engagement and institutional change rather than reliance on the education system itself.

Dear Mr. Loggie:


Over the past few months, I’ve been working with a small group of Islanders who believe that education is important, that it needs some changes and that the key to change is an informed and engaged public. We are also of the view that the school system is unlikely to make the necessary changes by itself so, we’ve established an independent group, Education 20/20, and launched the following initiatives:


  • Independent information: Most of our information about education comes from the people who operate the system; to address the shortage, we currently produce a monthly electronic newsletter; in addition, our website has also just gone live and we plan to use it as an information hub for surveys, announcements, occasional papers, social media, etc.


  • Engagement: When families, schools and communities work together, student achievement improves. For this reason, we are hosting a series of discussions throughout the Island focused on concerns of young people about gaps in their education. The first workshop was held in the Stratford Town Hall on January 20, 2014; attendance was good and discussion was lively; it also went on long after the meeting closed. The workshop is being repeated in Montague on February 18, Summerside on March 11 and Wellington on March 25; in addition, workshops are planned for Souris and O’Leary; and we are preparing for a second series, this one likely on educational quality.


  • Recognition and incentives: The education sector lack incentives – students cannot finish early; there is little encouragement to innovate, principals have little autonomy to make local adjustments; each school is a cookie cutter of all other schools, and parents have little choice in choosing a school and none in choosing a teacher. As a way to foster greater engagement, and thanks to a generous donor, Education 20/20 will soon announce an educational award for a school or learning centre that best demonstrates engagement in its everyday activities; other incentives will follow.






Should you be interested, we would be delighted to share additional plans for the improvement of education.


Our group cannot make this happen alone; funds are needed firstly to continue the initiatives mentioned above, but also to support new initiatives such as user surveys, community discussions and additional incentives. Consequently, we are looking for partners and supporters; our short term goal for the next 18 months in support of these initiatives is $22,000 broken out as follows:


  • Gathering and disseminating information $15,000.

  • Promoting and awarding incentives $ 2,000

  • Workshops $ 2,500

  • Operational support $ 2,500

Total $22,000


We invite your support either for one of the initiatives listed above or our activities in general. On the other hand, should you have a different interest, we would be pleased to work with you on that as well. As background, I am enclosing a copy of our mission, a brief backgrounder on the current workshop series and a recent newsletter. Should you have questions or want further information about us and what we do, do not hesitate to let me know. I’ll be back in touch in a few days to see if you have any questions; I would also be pleased to stop by at a mutually convenient time just to discuss education.


The Community Foundation of Prince Edward Island supports our initiative and administers our funds. Contributions to Education 20/20 Inc., therefore, should be directed to the Foundation (Community Foundation of Prince Edward Island, 119 Queen Street, Charlottetown, PE C1A 4B3) who will issue charitable receipts.


Ensuring a quality education for every Canadian requires new thinking, new approaches and new partners. We have ideas but we need your support for ideas to become realities.


Sincerely



Don Glendenning

President
