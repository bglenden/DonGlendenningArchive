Don questions whether expensive diploma ceremonies represent genuine educational value or primarily serve as rites of passage with declining value. He argues that transcripts adequately convey educational achievement without ceremonial framing, and that structural problems in education delivery—particularly content delivery to captive audiences—have created unsustainable cost structures. He identifies the "cost disease" as affecting education like healthcare, with overhead consuming resources while qualified staff shortages emerge for complex cases. Don proposes business model transformation from content delivery to learner development.
