Education: a messy business


Education 20/20 will host a presentation at the Stratford Town Hall on Wednesday, November 25 at 6:30 pm. The speaker, Dave Cormier, believes that learning is a messy business and it will be worse in the future. In his presentation, he’ll tell us why he thinks that way.


Dave Cormier is an educational activist, researcher, online community advocate and the Project Lead for Student Relations Management at the University of Prince Edward Island. He has published on a variety of topics including open education, Rhizomatic Learning, MOOCs (Massive/Open Online Courses), and the impact of technology on the future of higher education.


Cormier believes in education, believes it should change and promotes that idea in his writing, in the social media and everywhere he travels; and he is telling this story every place he goes - USA, Spain. Fiji and India, to name but a few. He is very much focused on the future but, with all the changes happening around us and so much information at our fingertips, he wonders about the best way to prepare our kids for dealing with the complex challenges facing us?


Dave will share his thoughts and explore some of the reasons why we teach and learn the things that we do and how we might look at education as we move into an uncertain future. Education 20/20 spokesman, Don Glendenning believes that “The presentation is timely, especially given the changes both made and expected in PEI in the next few months.” The session is open to the public.
