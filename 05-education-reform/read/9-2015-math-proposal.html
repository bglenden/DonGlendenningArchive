Creative conceptual framework presenting "Mathematics as a Mountain" metaphor. Critiques current educational elevation system for grades as lacking logic, being subject to interpretation, and having no national standard. Proposes mountain-climbing framework emphasizing mathematics as prerequisite for later-life opportunities. Outlines mathematical structure: numeracy through infinite series. Advocates for complete field analysis before establishing levels. Proposes linkage with PISA or OECD levels and independent assessment service model similar to music and skating certification. Project plan: establish committee, undertake analysis, establish levels, conduct national consultation, distribute widely. Benefits include common standards, mobility support, and reduced curriculum workload. - **Key People**: None specifically named - **Key Organizations**: OECD, PISA, provincial education systems - **Tags**: PROFESSIONAL, EDUCATION, TECHNICAL - **Eulogy Relevance**: MEDIUM - Shows Don's creative metaphorical thinking and national-scope ambitions for educational reform.

1-3-8-9

Mount Mathematics

Educational issues:

  • Great energy expended in trying to decide in advance an appropriate elevation for students in each grade
  • Lacks logic
  • Open to interpretation by various users
  • Altitudes attained are open to interpretation
  • No national standard


Math:

  • Math as a mountain
  • It is a prerequisite for accessing opportunities in later life
  • Structure:   numeracy, elementary mathematics, algebra, geometry, trigonometry, calculus, set and function, complex numbers, logarithmec functions, and infinite series
  • Why climb Math Mountain: It is a prerequisite for accessing opportunities in later life
  • Cannot find listing of either outcomes or competencies
  • Complete analysis needed before



Proposal:

Develop a tool to educators


  • Undertake an analysis:
  • Levels as described by PISA
  • Provincial may offer other courses as well but they must have different identity
  • Similar to establishing levels in music and skating
  • Independent assessment service
  • Learners apply when ready
  • Establish benchmark


  • Establish levels
  • Levels – link with PCAP or OECD if possible
  • Similar to establishing levels in music and skating
  • Independent assessment service
  • Learners apply when ready


Advantages/benefits:

  • Common standard
  • Aid mobility
  • Much of groundwork already done
  • Reduce curriculum development workload


Project:

  • Establish committee
  • Undertake analysis
  • Establish levels
  • National consultation
  • Distribute widely
