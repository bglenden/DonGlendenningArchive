Dated July 8, 2015. High school focused survey questions addressing three main areas: ranking school roles (citizenship preparation, employment preparation, childcare), employer assessment of recent graduate preparedness with year-over-year comparison, and skill deficiency concerns (math, writing, reading, communications). Includes proposed newsletter questions about education value, exam exemptions, coding instruction, and external examination reinstatement. Represents multi-stakeholder approach to curriculum and workforce alignment. - **Tags:** high-school, survey-design, workforce-alignment, PEI-education, 2015 - **Eulogy Relevance:** HIGH - **Don Authored:** Likely (Education 20/20 project)

Possible Survey Questions


Survey 1

QUESTION 1


Rank the following high school roles in order of importance to you:  

  • Prepare young people for citizenship: very important, important, not important, not a school role
  • Prepare people employment: very important, important, not important, not a school role
  • Place to look after children while parents work: very important, important, not important, not a school role


ABOUT YOU:

  • Parent, Educator, Employer, Student, Retired

Survey 2


QUESTION 2:

How well served are you by recent high school students you employed during 2015:

  • well prepared, 
  • prepared, 
  • not prepared
  • No new employees in this category

How do this year’s newly hired graduates compare with those hired last year? 

  • better prepared, 
  • about the same,
  • not as well prepared
  • No opinion

Which skills are you most concerned about:

  • Math
  • Writing
  • Reading
  • Communications
  • Other (Please specify) 


ABOUT YOU:

  • Check the category that best represents your field: Construction, retailing, hi tech, manufacturing, tourism, primary industry, personal service, 

Survey 3


If we follow the example of the Guardian, possible questions in our Newsletter include:

  • Do you think that we get good value for our education dollar?
  • Should high school students be allowed exemptions from final exams?
  • Should introductory coding be available to all students?
  • Is it time to return to external exams for high school students?
