More random thoughts


  • On July 23, 2014 the Globe and Mail published an article by Thomas Hodd on the changing language of culture. It seems as though art is no longer about painting, dancing and storytelling but more about marketability, strategic planning and entrepreneurship.


  • Marion Brady - It took me about 15 years in the classroom … to accept that what mattered most wasn’t what I said but what kids did.


  • The Washington Post of July 10 reported on a lunch meeting organized by the President for four teachers; the teachers are reported to have told the President:

“1- There’s nothing wrong with the kids

2- Responsibility and delight can coexist

3- I’s not about good and bad teachers, it’s about good and bad teaching

4- If we want students to innovate, collaborate and solve real world problems, we need to make it possible for teachers to do the same things.”


  • If you ever wondered about complex and complicated and where schools fit in, read Vallerie Strauss in the Washington Post of August 8, 2014

Powerful Questions



I was influenced by the work of Neil Postman especially by “Teaching as a Subversive Activity”; One of his suggestions was that teachers place these questions beside their mirrors and read them every day:

  • What will I do today that will make a difference to a student?

  • What difference will it make?

  • How do I know?


Marion Brady, August 1, 2014 Washington Post; “Teachers teach, learners learn, and standardized tests monitor how well the process is going. The tests measure a quantity—the amount of information taught, minus the amount not learned or learned and forgotten. Subtraction yields a single, precise number convenient for sorting and labeling kids, teachers, schools, school systems, states, nations”.


Don Glendenning, Editor
