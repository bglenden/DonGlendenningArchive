Undated working draft establishing educational definitions and exploring skill training components. Don outlines distinctions between learning, education, teaching, and "good education," then discusses how educational offerings should serve two purposes: personal knowledge development and community benefit. References Oregon's "life roles" framework (individual, citizen, student, family member, consumer, employer) as a useful planning model. Raises concerns about curriculum context disconnection from learners' real circumstances and advocates for advisor roles analogous to doctors and lawyers.

Educational services are usually intended to serve two purposes, (1) individuals who want to increase the nature and level of their personal knowledge, skills and other personal attributes and (2) services that will result in a benefit to the community.  Although education of all kinds is encouraged, some states (Australia for one)  have different levels of financial support depending based on their value to the community. 

A number of years ago, the State of Oregon  undertook a major overhaul of it’s education system. It built its system on the belief that, during a normal lifetime, citizens fill six life roles that of an  individual, citizen, student, family member, consumer and employer.
