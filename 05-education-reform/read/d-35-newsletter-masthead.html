The basic skills gap


A few days ago, I was part of a discussion about students who graduate from high school and do not have skill levels needed to enter employment or further education; we were not long into the discussion before we realized that there is not much hard information available either about the nature of the gap or the number of people affected.


Participants were of the view that the role of schools is to help young people develop the skills of citizenship but skills of citizenship may be different from the skills needed to secure employment or for acceptance into college of university. I’ve continued my search for a master list of “citizenship skills” but the closest match may well be the Employability Skills as identified by the Conference Board of Canada; if this list is not adequate, we will likely have to undertake our own analysis. If any reader knows of such a list, please let me know


It seems that:

  • Having a master list of “citizenship skills” would be a useful in improving communication between those who teach and those who deal with students after graduation.

  • There is some confusion arising from the custom of using Grade” both as a method of grouping students and also as a level of achievement.

  • The school practice having teachers both teach and grade is out of step with stand practices in our society.


I’ll keep you posted on future developments.
