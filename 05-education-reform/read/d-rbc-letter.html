Solicitation letter from Don (President, Education 20/20 Inc.) to Mary McInnis, Manager of Royal Bank of Canada (Charlottetown), requesting $10,000 lead donation. Written early in Education 20/20's formation (Jan 2014), it articulates the organization's three-pillar approach: (1) information provision via newsletter and website hub, (2) community engagement through workshops (starting in Stratford Jan 20, with planned events in O'Leary, Summerside, Wellington, Souris), (3) incentives for innovation and engagement. Requests total $22,000 ($15,000 info, $2,000 incentives, $2,500 workshops, $2,500 ops). Describes young people's concerns about readiness for education/work. Conveys Don's educational philosophy: education is a community responsibility, not solely a school function.


January 24, 2014



Ms. Mary McInnis, Manager

Royal Bank of Canada

83 Queen Street

Charlottetown, PEI C1A 4A8



Dear Ms. McInnis:


Over the past few months, I’ve been working with a small group of Islanders who are of the view that education is important and is a community rather than a school issue. We are also of the view that changes are needed but schools are unlikely to make the necessary changes acting alone. The key to change in education is an informed and engaged public; the quality of education we want will only be achieved when the community becomes involved, and communities have to take the lead. Based on that view, Education 20/20 has launched three initiatives:


  • The provision of information: We’ve started to address the issue independent information through an informal monthly electronic newsletter, latest copy attached and we are about to launch a website to act as an information hub for surveys, announcements, occasional papers, social media, etc.


  • Increasing engagement: To this end, we’ve organized a series of workshops across the Island. The first, held in Stratford on January 20 focused on concerns of young people about gaps in their readiness for further education and work. The workshop was well-attended and the discussion was lively; it also went on long after the meeting closed. Dates have been confirmed for similar workshops in O’Leary, Summerside, Wellington and Souris and another series of workshops is soon to follow.


  • Recognition and incentives: The education sector lack incentives – students cannot finish early; there is little encouragement to innovate, there is little autonomy at the school level to make local adjustments; parents have little choice in choosing a school and none in choosing a teacher. As an incentive to greater engagement, and thanks to a generous donor, Education 20/20 will soon announce an educational award for a school or learning centre that best demonstrates engagement in its everyday activities; other incentives will follow.








Our group cannot make this happen alone; funds are needed firstly to continue the initiatives mentioned above, but also to support new initiatives such as user surveys, community discussions and additional incentives. Consequently, we are looking for partners and supporters; our short term goal for support of the above initiatives is $22,000 broken out as follows:


  • Gathering and disseminating information $15,000.

  • Promoting and awarding incentives $ 2,000

  • Workshops $ 2,500

  • Operational support $ 2,500

Total $22,000


We are pleased that the Community Foundation of Prince Edward Island supports our initiative and administers our funds. Contributions to Education 20/20 Inc., therefore, should be directed to the Foundation (Community Foundation of Prince Edward Island, 119 Queen Street, Charlottetown, PE C1A 4B3) who will issue charitable receipts.


We invite the Royal Bank of Canada be the lead supporter for the informative initiative with a donation of $10,000. As background, I am enclosing our mission statement, a brief description of Education 20/20 Inc. and a couple of brief background papers. Should you have questions or want further information about us and what we do, do not hesitate to let me know. We would also be delighted to meet with you and your colleagues just to share information and views.


Ensuring a quality education for every Canadian requires new thinking, new approaches and new partners. We have ideas but we need your support for ideas to become realities.


Sincerely



Don Glendenning

President
