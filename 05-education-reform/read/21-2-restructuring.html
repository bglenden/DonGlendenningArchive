RESTRUCTURING EDUCATION

21-2

Education is an umbrella word and includes all learning experiences wherever, whenever and however initiated, carried out or paid for.  Becoming more educated, results in changes in the way a person thinks, feels or acts.  Being educated has been described as a state or level of development that allows a person to “adequately cope with all demands of daily life”.


Education is a provincial responsibility; typical services include: governance and leadership, funding, instruction, human resources, products and technology, quality assurance and Information and counselling. Provinces generally group clients by age and direct deliver services to them.

Calls for change are not new.   I first heard about structural impediments in the education system a few years ago from a PEI trustee. Then in 2011, the OECD recommended more autonomy to schools for their day- to- day operation as a way to improve PISA test results. in 2018, the World Bank, the largest funder of educational services, spoke about improving an education system by delegating certain operational practices to schools and intermediary bodies.  Montreal Economic Institute reported that autonomy and accountability “jointly applied” are key factors in school achievement”. and just a few weeks ago, a friend forwarded information from UNESCO about the structure of educational services.  And that’s not to forget a “Call for Action” by Education 2020 Inc. in 2015, newspaper items and the many comments from retired educators and Island citizens at large.
