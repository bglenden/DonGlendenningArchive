Don proposes applying Canada's healthcare model to education funding. Rather than government providing all services, he suggests selective coverage: essential skills for all residents regardless of age (full cost), entry-level skills training for any occupation practiced in PEI, student/guardian counselling, qualification examinations on instructor recommendation, management training after five years full-time employment, while excluding advanced topics like calculus. This approach would shift focus from school roles to actual learning outcomes. Recent reading highlights emerging trends: learning pods being normalized, renewed interest in continuing education (especially post-pandemic layoffs), and importance of learning context. References Education 20/20's Citizen Math study revealing context matters in all learning—noting four numeracy contexts (citizen, personal, health, career). Observes schools organizing around specialized contexts (arts, sports, health, aviation, technology) and recalls Prince County Vocational High School and Provincial Vocational School serving this function.

Open Education

No. 92 September 25, 2020

Evaluation - aa overall judgement of what has been learned


WHAT PART OF EDUCATION

SHOULD GOVERNMENT

PAY FOR?

Canadians boast about their health care system; it covers the cost of many, but not all, health care services. Government encourages people to adopt healthy practices . Service is triggered by a person with a health concern contacting his or her family doctor who provides, or arranges for, particular services. I wonder how this would work in education?


Let me fabricate some clauses:

  • Essential skills – full cost for each resident regardless of age

  • Skills training to entry level for any occupation practised on PEI

  • Counselling sessions for student and guardian

  • Qualification examination on recommendation of instructor

  • Management training – available after five years of full-time employment

  • Calculus – not covered


This approach would certainly increase the focus on what is or has been learned rather than on the role of schools.



RECENT READING

Learning pods

According to the Washington Post (September 2, 2020) “Learning pods are here to stay”. Should they be part of the education system?


Mini courses

Interest in continuing education seems to be on the rise again, especially with layoffs resulting from the pandemic.


Learning context

One of the conclusions arising from the Education 20/20 “Citizen Math” study is the need to rethink the importance of context in the teaching/learning process. My reading suggests four separate contexts for numeracy - citizen, personal, health and career: furthermore, the literature suggests that context count in all learning. Some schools organize around the arts, sports, health services, aviation and technology. In some ways, Prince County Vocational High School and the Provincial Vocational school filled this role
