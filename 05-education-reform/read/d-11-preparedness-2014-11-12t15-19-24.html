Date: Glendenning, Don

Bridging the preparedness gap


Basic skills, especially in math and communications, are on everyone’s list of needs for young people finishing high school, whether going directly to work or taking further studies. But young people should also have good personal management skills and a high comfort level with technology regardless of their after school plans.


These were a few of the ideas put forth during a series of workshops organized across the Island by Education 20/20 earlier this year on the subject of preparedness. The idea for the workshops arose out of an earlier survey by the Community Foundation of Prince Edward Island of over 200 Island youth on issues that directly affect them. One of their issues dealt with a gap between the level of education gained and the level needed to enter postsecondary education or find employment. The study did not reveal the missing outcomes or levels of achievement.


As to narrowing the preparedness gap, participants suggested such things as greater student engagement, increased communication between parents and teachers, strengthening independent study habits, increased flexibility of programs to respond to individual interests and needs, and and the development of a “can do” atmosphere in schools. A report on discussions may be found on the organization’s website. (www.education2020.ca)
