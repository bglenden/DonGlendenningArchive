What learners find helpful


At the end of the day, it’s what each learner learns and not what a teacher teaches that really counts. Knowing the help learners want, and how to organize it is the subject of a workshop at the Stratford Town Hall on Wednesday evening, October 22, 2014.


The evening opens with a panel of students identifying the kind of help that they find useful. This will be followed by parents, teachers and others trying to sort out the best way to organize those support services; the result will be a flow chart of the learning process. The next step is to design a teaching strategy to match.


The workshop is being organized by Education 20/20 Inc. and is open to the public. Further information and may be found on the Education 20/20 website.www.education2020.ca
