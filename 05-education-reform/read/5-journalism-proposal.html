Don Glendenning proposes an award for journalism students at Holland College and UPEI to encourage writing about education issues. The award aims to increase the flow of independent information about education to the public, reflecting Education 20/20's mission to close gaps between educational aspiration and delivery.


Journalism Award


Education 20/20 Inc.

Education 20/20 Inc. is an independent, broad based, national, not-for-profit education organization, located in Charlottetown, PEI and is governed by a volunteer Board of Directors. It’s vision is a quality education for every Canadian and its mandate is to provide information, encouragement and support to ensure that quality is present. Initial activities include a monthly electronic newsletter in both official languages, public workshops and presentations, occasional papers, a school award and discussions with a wide range of education stakeholders


The Issue

Education is a key factor in determining a person’s quality of life and that the collective education of our citizens will determine to a significant degree the quality of the communities in which we live. But we also recognize that there is a gap between what people want in the way of education and what the system delivers, a gap that the educational system is either unlikely or unable to close by itself. Our mission is to help close that gap and to ensure a quality education for every Canadian.


We find information, the fuel of change, to be in short supply among stakeholders in the education

system yet we know that current, independent and reliable information increases the odds of good

decisions. For that reason, we instituted a number of our current activities, but more needs to be done

The proposal in the paragraphs that follow is an additional incremental step.


Proposal

To increas the flow of information about education into the public domain through the establishment of an award to a student In a journalism program at Holland College and the University of Prince Edward Island. The Award, in the amount of $000:

  • Is intended to encourage journalism students to write about education

  • through including them on our mailing lists and inviting them to public events.

  • Is for students who write publicly about education

  • Is limited to students in journalism programs at Holland College and UPEI

  • Requires those seeking the award to present three articles published in an Atlantic Canada newspaper during the previous 12 months
