Don's framework for conversation about PEI's education future. Written from personal experience with education and commitment to community contribution, this document establishes foundational definitions and life-role theory (individual, family member, citizen, student, producer, consumer) across which education needs arise. Don argues that Islanders recognize education's community importance, contrasts school systems with education systems, identifies key operating beliefs (learners at own pace, education as behavior-change, teachers as experience designers, ranking as non-educational), and catalogs typical services and branches. The document explicitly aims to facilitate community dialogue about transforming school systems into education systems.

AN EDUCATION SYSTEM FOR PEI

A framework for conversation


Some time ago, I decided to explore public education. I did so because of a long association with the field, an interest in the subject and an opportunity to contribute to my community. The notes that follow are an attempt to capture my findings and recommendations. The notes that follow result from conversations wih other Islanders, review of current literature and my own knowledge and experience. Some information may be wrong, some may be unclear; for both these reasons I welcome reader’s comments and observations.


The Community

PEI is a community of some 140,000 people on the East Coast of Canada; it is also an Island. Like any community, it has a range of interests and needs, and recognizes the importance of education to both.

Like most Canadians, Islanders recognize that the future of their community and its individual citizens is largely determined by the nature and level of education of its citizens. Let’s start with some definitions.


Education is a service to people of any age or interest. It is often associated or organized around the following life roles (Oregon Department of Education) that learners occupy:

  1. Individual

  2. Family member

  3. Citizen

  4. Student

  5. Producer

  6. Consumer


Definitions

  1. Learner

  4. Education - a process of helping others learn; more formally, education is the creation of expriences that result in ne information, understanding, skill or attitude

  5. Educated is a value judgement as to what a person knows, believes and is able to do. Levels of learning, levels of education and levels of schooling are often confused and benchmarks are practically non- existent. In addition,

  6. Schooling – a pattern or approach used to assist the learning process

  7. School – a room or building equipped to support the learning process
