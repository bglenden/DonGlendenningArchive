STRUCTURAL BARRIERS

TO CHANGE IN EDUCATION

Public education in Prince Edward Island needs an overhaul; the reference to structural issues first arose from school trustees during a series of community conversations held across PEI a number of years ago. As I understand it, the reference was to the way services are organized, freedom of employees to act and accountabilities for action taken


Te impact of educational structures was addressed by the OECD (… , 2011); The World Bank, (… 2018) and the Montreal Economic Institute (Bedard, 2015) to name but a few.


Structural barriers include:

  1. Edu-speak –

  2. Focus on schools from education chools/institutionssDepartment mandate - developing and delivering programs to youth is a narrow mandate; Department of Public Instruction may be a more fitting title.

  3. Education as product - the switch from service to education as a product; Education has dropped the emphasis in favour of delivering a product – a course, credit, grad, certificate, diploma, degree or other recognized credential; the literature suggests that the interest in and need for education “services” is returning.

  4. Accountability

  5. We limit autonomy for principals and professional staff in spite of the strong link between autonomy and student achievement.

The “fear factor” is the silent guest at every education conversation Educators are seen as silo dwellers during education dialogue; Health PEI has a Chief Health Officer; doesn’t education deserve the equivalent?


Some five yeas ago, Education 20/20 Inc. recommended that PEI develop a “clear and compelling” vision of education for Prince Edward Island’. It’s still needed and the best place, in fact the only place, to start.
