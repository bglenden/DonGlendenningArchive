KEY ISSUES

___________________________________________________________________________

It is widely recognized that education is a key factor in determining each citizen’s quality of life; in the same way, the collective education of our citizens determines, to a significant degree, the quality of the community in which we live. Education is complex, made so by the fact that each person is unique in so many ways - unique in basic physical, mental and emotional makeup, unique in the range and nature of previous experience, unique in perceived futures, and also unique in terms of learning pace, style and needs. Given these complexities, some system-wide issues are not surprising:

  1. Terminology – a common terminology improves communication throughout the organization. Nowhere is the error more prominent than the failure to differentiate between education and schooling.

  2. Purpose – purpose provides the fundamental reason for establishing an organization, a reason for which something is done or created. Preparation for citizenship, personal development, self- management and preparation for work are common purposes. PEI seems to have settled on helping Islanders “find a place”

  3. Education services – although education is a service, services are seldom identified. Services include: information, counselling, instruction, learning products and technology, quality assurance. governance, funding and human resources. Learners may need any or all services at any point in their education journey.

  4. Structure – “form follows function” is the standad whenstructuring any venture. Typically government department structures reflect branches, categories or services rendered. ; PEI seem to focus instead, on clients served.

  5. Autonomy - front line service providers (teachers) need both expertise and autonomy to respond to learner needs. The OECD tells us that PISA results are better for students in schools with autonomy for their day-to-day operation.

  6. Accountability - Mathew Bedard of the Montreal Economic Institute points out that “autonomy and accountability jointly applied” would cost practically nothing and allow education to better adapt to the diversity of student needs and aptitudes. .

  7. Collaboration – the literature tells us that education thrives on collaboration and communication; Paul Bennet speaks of the “muffled voices of teachers” who work alone in classrooms, in fact the profession itself, sems to operate as a silo in the community.


  1. Education system - given the impotrance, size and complexity of the subject, PEI should devlop an education system. Systems include services, standards; indormation,assessment, counselling. learning aids and technology.



  1. Transparency adds depth to education


These issues are not new; no doubt readers have others to propose.
