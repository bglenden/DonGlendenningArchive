Don examines the teacher's role in assessment and critiques the current system where government determines what's taught, employs teachers, and sets achievement standards. No. 78 July 20, 2019

We shouldn’t treat teachers as interchangeable widgets on a production line - Anon


STDENT ACHIEVEMENT LEVELS

A teacher’s role is to help another person learn. As part of the teaching process, teachers need to gather information about what a student already knows, what the student needs/wants to know and be able to do, and obstacles blocking progress. The process of diagnosing may include gathering information from the student as well as from others. Testing students to better understand what to do next is part of the teaching-learning process.


As I understand it, the difference between any two people in terms of their level of achievement depends on the skills learned, levels achieved on those skills and the particular applications or clustering of skills and competencies


It seems a bit incestuous, however for the Department Education to require attendance, determine what will be taught, train and employ teachers to teach it, determine achievement levels and who qualifies for each level. Teachers certainly must be familiar with standards in their subject but they shouldn’t be the one to set and aaward themset them.
