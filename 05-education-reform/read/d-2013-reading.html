Don identifies inadequate reading instruction as a critical PEI education problem, citing low literacy levels despite improvements. He proposes five specific changes: increase reading attention, update and specify outcomes, clarify assessment criteria and levels, distribute accountability clearly, and improve report card accuracy for reading performance. He suggests employers and community organizations (Chambers of Commerce, Rotary Clubs) take leadership roles in documenting literacy gaps and advocating improvement. The letter combines diagnostic analysis with concrete recommendations and stakeholder engagement strategies.
Education Philosophy & Reform

March 2, 2013


Editor, Charlottetown Guardian

Charlottetown, PEI


Dear Sir:

Reading


I am not a reading specialist but, given the frequency of comments about the literacy and reading levels of Islanders, I decided to examine the matter more closely.  The figures are readily available, and they aren’t good.  I understand that levels are improving in the school system but, overall, levels remain low.  


Based on what I’ve seen, heard and read, I have concluded that:

Reading does not get the attention it deserves, at least in the public school system

Outcomes need to be updated and made more specific

Assessment criteria and levels need to be more clearly defined.

Accountability for reading results is too diffused.

Report cards need to more accurately reflect reading levels.


The problem of reading levels is unlikely to be resolved without external interventions.  Since employers are on the receiving end of graduates, I wonder if Chambers of Commerce would assume the role of documenting their experience.  On the other hand, PER Rotary Clubs have given generous support to reading improvement and may be able to take a leadership role in bringing about improvement.


I’d be delighted to explore the matter further, and even more delighted to be proven wrong in the above conclusions.  Whether you think that my conclusions are right ot wrong, or some of each, I’d be delighted to hear from you at  HYPERLINK "mailto:standards2020@gmail.com" standards2020@gmail.com 


Sincerely



Don Glendenning
