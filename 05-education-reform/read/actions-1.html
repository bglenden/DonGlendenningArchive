Actions 2

The notes that follow are based on my review of the education literature, conversations with Islanders and my own experience; while a major overhaul may be warranted, I recommend that we should simply make, document and post changes as needed.

  1. Clarity: There is some confusion about branches, purpose, levels, “going to school and “getting or having an education”

  • Key definitions will improve communication

  • Need common meanings to key words

  • Government organizes most departments around branches or fields”; education seems to be be organized around levels.

  • Definition of “Education” replaced in current Education Act with a definition of school.

  • Purpose not clear; presumably to help Islanders develop knowledge, skills, attitudes and other attributes needed for future years.

  1. Structure should reflect functions or services

  • Functions/services not identified

  • Department organized around running schools rather than providing education services

  1. Autonomy

  • OECD reports that students from schools with autonomy in their day to day operation do better on PISA tests

  • Montreal Economic Institutes also recommends schools be given more autonomy and accountability

  1. Department role

  • Current role ( “develop and deliver curriculum” to youth) does not describe role

  • Recommended role “To encourage, support and regulate education services”

  • Education literature recommends that oversight and operations be separated. PEI divides responsibility between Department and Health PEI. Literature suggests schools e given autonomy for day to ay operation

  1. Documentation

  • Operation of schools appearswell documentd

  • Was unable to find Curriculum model, leaning model. assessment model, standards

  2. Standards – rating scale

  3. Access to Student controlled learning materials

  4. Defined education system including Services

  5. Accountability

  6. Flexibility
