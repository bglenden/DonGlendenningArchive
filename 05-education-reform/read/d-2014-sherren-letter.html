Don supports professional development while citing OECD research demonstrating students perform better when schools have operational autonomy in daily decision-making. He notes searching for information about principal roles yielded mostly qualification requirements but little about actual duties and responsibilities, leading him to conclude principals have minimal decision-making freedom. The letter reinforces his core argument that teacher and administrator empowerment through delegated authority is essential for educational improvement.

September 21, 2014


Editor, Charlottetown Guardian

Charlottetown, PEI


Dear Sir:

Sherren letter 


Joe Sherren has stimulated us again with his article about the importance of education and how to improve it. (Charlottetown Guardian; September 20, 2014; “Better schools  create better students”.)  An OECD study (School Accountability and Autonomy, Report No. 9) provides another reason for supporting professional development in schools in its finding that students perform better when schools have autonomy in their day to day operation.


After reading the letter, I searched the internet to understand just what principals do; I found quite a bit about qualifications required but little about duties and responsibilities; I am left with the impression, therefore, that when it comes to making decisions, principals have little freedom to act.


Sincerely


Don Glendenning

A long time student of education
