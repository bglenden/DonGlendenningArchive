Based on extensive conversations across PEI about education, Don identifies reading as the most critical educational concern parents raise. He critiques BC's appointment of a Superintendent of Reading as bureaucratic bloat rather than effective reform. Instead, he proposes making reading a distinct subject with assigned teachers, reporting on all levels, and developing common outcomes and assessment scales across all educators. He calls reading a "vital sign" of educational health and encourages public participation in education debate. This demonstrates his data-driven approach (gathering real concerns) and preference for structural clarity over administrative hierarchy.

August 25, 2012


Editor, The Guardian

Charlottetown, PEI


Dear Sir

Same conclusions but different actions


My recent meanderings and discussions included reviewing some PEI education studies and cogitating on a recent item in the Vancouver Sun.  In particular, I am intrigued by the different actions triggered by the same set of facts.  Let me illustrate:


  • The Report on School Governance, issued earlier this year, addressed the issue of governance, partnerships, community involvement in reviewing and refining goals, standards, policies, etc.; anything I’ve read or heard supports that view. One would expect, therefore, a high level advisory body broadly representative of the community served including parents, employers, justice officials, and other community leaders.  What the report recommends is a collaborative process with counsel coming primarily from people already in the education fold; people the Minister sees  and works with on a daily basis.
  • The second was a report on the K-12 school proposed or Souris  reported on the need for the Minister
  • The performance of students in British Columbia has been disappointing; the state of reading in PEI, although receiving considerable attention, is still of great concern to parents; I’ve encountered a number of parents and grandparents of children having difficulty in reading.  (Based on my conversations, however, I sometimes think that the real problem is with teachers having difficulty with teaching)   If the BC objective is to improve reading, instead of a Superintendent of Reading, one might expect such measures as  allocating more time to the subject, designating teachers of reading, better training of teachers, more indivudualized tutring, earlier detection.  
  •      


consider myself a life=long learnerlearned something about myself recentlyDuring the past few months I have discussed education with people from Tignish to Souris, many points in between, and even while visiting friends and family in New Brunswick.  There are many concerns about education, but the example raised most often is that of reading, sometimes known as “the golden skill”. I consider reading ability a vital sign of health for the educational journey. 


I am aware of efforts by Provinces, including our own, to address the issue and understand that levels are improving.  I was interested, therefore, to learn recently that British Columbia has appointed a Superintendent of Reading and, furthermore, that the National Reading Campaign is recommending that other provinces do the same.     While I applaud renewed focus on the subject, I doubt that adding to the bureaucracy will do much to help.


Let me offer a couple of different suggestions.  Without doubt, more support and encouragement should be offered to learners in and through the home.  As for the school system, since reading is such an essential skill,  I’d like to see it identified as a separate subject, teachers assigned to teach it, and performance in reading be included on report cards at all levels.  I’d also like to see a common set of reading outcomes or competencies and a common rating scale to be used by all who teach and tutor readers – a reading “hymnbook”..


Please do your part and enter the debate; it is a vital step towards reading Improvement and your comments are welcome at standards2020@gmail.com.  


Sincerely


Don Glendenning

A long-time student of education
