Dear Mr. Baksh:

Education 20/20 Inc. – file 3050233


This is a follow up to our application for registration as a charitable organization with Canada Revenue Agency; our original application was submitted on January 8, 2013 and, following receipt of your letter, was withdrawn on July 16, 2013. With a year of activities behind us, I feel that this application more accurately reflects our interests and intent.


  1. With regards to Question 11a, I should point out that we have developed a new set of objectives and realize that, if acceptable to you, they will have to be submitted to Industry Canada; activities are listed under proposed objectives as we feel they more accurately reflect the nature of the work that we do.



  1. During the past year, Education 20/20 Inc. contributed to the public debate on education by organizing public workshops in six Island communities. The issue was preparedness of youth for further education and for work. Education 20/20 provided experienced facilitators and a common set of questions - announcements, media releases and clippings and a copy of our report is enclosed.


  1. In recognition of the linkage between school achievement and community engagement, Education 20/20 initiated an education award with the first award being made to Kinkora Regional High School as part of their graduation exercises on June 20, 2014 – announcements and clippings enclosed



  1. Education 20/20 Inc. organized a number of workshops for educators and others with educational interests or activities – see attached documentation



  1. In recognition of our expertise, Education 20/20 Inc. has been invited to make a presentation to the PEI Legislative Committee on Learning and Technology – letter attached; we have also been given a spot on the agenda of the English Language School Board for their September meeting.



  1. In addition to continuing, and improving on last year’s activities, two new initiatives are underway:

  • A series of occasional papers on educational topics; this initiative serves two purposes, first to inform readers about issues and developments affecting education and, second, to encourage Islanders to write as a means of continuing their own personal and professional development.

  • Whereas our workshop series was intended primarily to engage parents and the public at large both developing and expressing their views on education, this year we plan to engage educators in discussions about such educational issues as standards, essential skills, credentials and challenging for credit.



  1. An initiative to improve the math skills of Island youth is under discussion. The first step is to identify a common set of outcomes or competencies and defining levels of achievement.



  1. As requested, I am enclosing a package of support documents.



I trust that the above will be helpful in the review process; if you need additional information please let me know. We also welcome any suggestions for changes in our incorporation documents to more accurately reflect our intent and your requirements.



Sincerely,



Don Glendenning

President
