Follow-up letter to Bernard Gillis updating him on Education 20/20 Inc. activities since October 2012. Don announces the name change from Education First Canada Inc. to Education 20/20 Inc., reflecting national aspirations and bilingual operations. He reports on completed early childhood education workshops, exploration of community engagement software, plans for school engagement awards, dialogue with educators about high school literacy, and efforts to gather and publish public education information. The letter demonstrates Don's commitment to building evidence-based advocacy and connecting stakeholders.

Dear Mr. Gillis:


This is a follow-up to my letter of October 17 and will update you on some of our activities.  First I should mention a change of name from Education First Canada Inc. to Education 20/20 Inc., the name under which we are incorporated.  We made the change because of our national aspirations and our intention to operate in both languages.  


Since I last wrote, Education 20/20 Inc.: 

  • Has delayed launching our newsletter until such time as we have a logo and website
  • Has organized a workshop on Early Childhood Education.  A panel, comprised of practitioners, shared their experiences and approaches that work; participants included other early childhood workers, parents, educators and members of the community at large.  We plan to offer workshops in other Island communities in the months ahead.
  • Has a committee looking into the matter of testing a new software package, developed in Canada for community engagement to assess whether or not it will have any application to our work.  This is being undertaken because of the evidence that there is a direct relationship between the level of engagement in a school and improved student achievement.
  • Plans to make an award to an Island school that best exemplifies a high level of engagement in its every day school activities; criteria, policies and procedures are now under development.
  • Has begun the process of engaging educators, especially decision makers, in a regular dialogue about educational issues, with reading skills of high school graduates being at the top of our list.
  • Is exploring ways to gather, produce and publish information about education of interest the public at large.


I trust that this information will be useful in your consideration of our proposal.  If you need additional information or would like to discuss the matter further, please do not hesitate to contact me.


With best wishes for the holiday season.


Sincerely



Don Glendenning

President
