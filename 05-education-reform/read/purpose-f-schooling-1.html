Education is a service; it is intended to help people change the way they think, feel and act, or even what they value. Education is also complex; people have different interests, experiences and needs; every Islander is said to fill six life roles, that of citizen, family member, individual, student, consumer and producer. The system offers a variety of programs, subjects, courses, grades, credits, certificates, diplomas and degree to that end.


Purpose provides the fundamental reason for establishing any organization, a reason for which something is done or created; it is inspirational and usually calls for action that results in a benefit to a community or society. Purpose is the key element in the structuring process and one from which all others follow. People are more likely to engage in their work when the mission and goals of the organization also matter to them—and when they feel like they can make a contribution to the bigger picture. Studies show that when leaders demonstrated clear purpose—vision, commitment to stakeholders and strong morals—their people were happier and more productive. To be meaningful, a purpose statement must be:

  • Easy to understand

  • Explain why the work is important

  • Contribute to alignment of activities

  • Manageable.
