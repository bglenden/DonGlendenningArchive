A Call For

“Conceptual Clarity”

No. 17 -B June 4, 2020

Quality:

Overhaul needed

Education services on PEI need an overhaul; I services are narrowly focused, cognitively based, bureaucratic, lack accountability, and focus on program delivery rather than on learner development – they also lack a self-correcting mechanism. I do not, we cannot, blame the current government, the previous government or any single government for the current status; Government has a responsibility however, for instituting corrective measures.


PEI has some 150,000 citizens; all are potential users of educational services. Each Islander fills six life roles, that of an individual, citizen, family member, learner, producer and consumer. Islanders need to be literate and numerate, to become independent learners, to take care of themselves and learn how to work and play together. Education’s business is to help Islanders make these changes but policy documents are focused on providing services to young people through schools. The public school system treats going to school and getting an education as one and the same. The World Bank cautions Ministries of Education about that practice.


The writer struggles with Department of Education documents; descriptions don’t seem to match what I hear on the street, documents obscure rather than enlighten, words and phrases seem to carry different meanings, definitions are seldom given, concepts I thought I understood now carried different meanings. Some smart entrepreneur has even developed and promotes a “jargon generator” for education writers.


Some of the more urgent others\ specific examples of my struggles include:


Definitions – education, learner, student, benchmark


Purpose of education - not clear if purpose is to raise levels of education, prepare people for employment, prepare people for citizenship in a democracy, simply provide Islanders with 12 years of instruction, all of the above or none of the above. A clear purpose, I suggest is needed to develop programs and measure success.


Education is an umbrella term, yet the Department describes its role as one of developing and delivering programs and curriculum to Island youth. Certainly programs are useful but the goal of the Department is greater than that - to help Islanders increase or improve their abilities, not only to cope but to thrive. in the years ahead.


Education Functions and Services - the literature suggests education functions usually include information, counselling, quality assurance, materials and technology, instruction. Sound organization practice suggests that “form follows function.” meaning that functions form the basis of the operating structure. I was not able to locate an organization chart for the Department, however, information at hand suggests that the Department might more aptly be considered a Department of Public Instruction.

Extent of Political embedment – the Department of Education has a major role to play in ensuring that Islanders receive the help they need to cope with the present and prepare for the future. Embedding the operation en-toto into an operating Government Department, has resulted in the emasculation of professional autonomy and accountability; I suggest a review of recommendations by the OECD ( ) and discussion with senior officials would be useful in any review. The recent restructuring of Health Services may provide ideas and experiences of interest and value.


Accountability- Islanders invest hundreds of millions of dollars in the education process. cannot find expectations or accountabilities in terms of achieving purposes





Nature of education – focus has narrowed from learning to passing, grading, and acquiring credentials; not sure how PEI defines


Standards –Hoe can we measure levels achieved without having clearly stated purposes?




Transparency – one hears of science based curriculum, learning, and other models but I haven’t found any


Information sharing
