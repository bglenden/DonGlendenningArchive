This working paper reflects Don Glendenning's analysis of education challenges on PEI based on organizational activities, director discussions, committee work, and personal experience. He identifies that the system needs overhaul with major issues in clarity, structure, communication, autonomy, and accountability. Don critiques education being treated as a product rather than service, notes the gap between community needs and system delivery, and concludes the current system cannot support needed changes. For Education 20/20, he proposes establishing a broad-based, independent professional organization representing all education providers. He challenges current directors about leadership commitment and outlines options: elect new officers, suspend operations, or wind up. A reminder about PEI's "fear factor" affecting professional expression indicates Don's awareness of political and bureaucratic constraints on reform.

FUTURE OF 2020-2B

Planning-

I reviewed and reflected upon our activities to date, our discussions, work of Tim McRoberts and his committee and my personal experience.  


About Education:

  • System needs overhaul
  • Major issues – clarity, structure, communication, autonomy and accountability 
  • Education is being treated as a product rather than a service
  • Gap between what Islanders want/need and what the system delivers.
  • Current system unlikely to allow or support required/needed/desired changes.

About Education 20/20:

  • Education could benefit from a broad based, independent professional organization with a focus on education rather than instruction. Such a move will involvea governing body that is representative of all education providers: public schools, private, industrial trainers, apprenticeship, full time. principals, counsellors, parents, Department staff, etc., etc., etc.
  • Are current directors prepared to take leadership role?
  • If answer is “yes”. Elect new officers
  • If answer is “no”; suspend operation, transfer to another group or wind up


  • REMINDER: Directors are reminded tht the “fear factor’ 

is alive and well on PEI
