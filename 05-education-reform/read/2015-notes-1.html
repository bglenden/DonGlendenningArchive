Education Philosophy & Reform


EDUCATION 20/20 INC.

January 9, 2015

Mathematics

NOTE: These notes arise from concerns about the level of math skills of Island youth.

BACKGROUND

Background


It is widely recognized that education is a key factor in determining one’s quality of life; in the same way, the collective education of our citizens will determine to a significant degree the future of our community and country. One of the most frequently mentioned concerns by Islanders is the low literacy levels among adults as reported via PISA results, recent CFPEI survey of Island youth, presentation to the Standing Committee by the Charlottetown Area Chamber of Commerce, a 2014 survey by survey by the PEI Home and School Federation and a series of workshops on Preparedness held throughout PEI by Education 20/20 Inc.


The most frequently mentioned concern among Islanders, and especially among employers, is the low level of basic skills, especially mathematics, communication and personal management skills. Following considerable discussion and reflection, Education 20/20 decided to explore the issue of basic math skills needed for to function in a modern economy.


Framework


The exploration will consider four elements:

  • Life roles which people fill: individual, citizen, student, producer, consumer and individual

  • Opportunities for learning math skills: self study, instruction, on-line-courses, tutoring, on-the-job,

  • Assessment either showing level or rank

  • Documentation in the form of transcript, credential, reference or qualification


Step One


Identify skills, competencies or outcomes employers expect and the level for each
