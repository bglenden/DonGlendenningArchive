Biographical notes on Education 20/20 Inc. Board of Directors (January 2015) including Don Glendenning's bio noting his experience at elementary, secondary, and post-secondary levels; founding presidency of Holland College; leadership of Glendenning Associates consulting firm; and past presidencies of Canadian Vocational Association and Community Foundation of PEI. Also profiles seven other accomplished educators and community leaders (Alice Taylor, Tim McRoberts, William Whelan, etc.) who served as directors, revealing Don's network and collaborative approach to education reform leadership.
Education Philosophy & Reform

January 20, 2015

EDUCATION 20/20 INC.

Current Directors 


Conway-Martin, Eileen - Conway-Martin has been involved in the areas of Counseling, Community Development, Program Evaluation and Education over the years. Along with a counseling degree, she holds a Masters in Education.  Eileen has worked with people of all ages within these endeavors.  Since coming to Prince Edward Island, she has enjoyed working with Aboriginal Organizations for over 30 years.  Today, Eileen is actively involved with "Handle with Care", a National Program for Youth/Family Mental Health.  In addition, she is assisting with the development of the "Lennox Island Sacred Garden", a community health/education initiative involving people of all ages. Finally, Eileen is a Board Member of Education 20 20, whose purpose is to create a quality education for every Canadian. 


Craig, Tanya, BA - Program Leader, Town of Stratford, PEI; developed Youth Can-Do for municipality, active in local home and school association and as a community volunteer. 


Glendenning, Donald E M, C.M. PhD - experience at elementary, secondary and post-secondary levels; Founding President of Holland College, President of Glendenning Associates, an educational consulting firm with  contracts in Canada and overseas, Past President of Canadian Vocational Association, Founding member and Past President of the Community Foundation of Prince Edward Island, Past Director of Institute of Public Administration of Canada, Past Director of the Association of Community Colleges of  Canada, Past Member of IRAC Advisory Committee. 


Goodine, Brenda R, MEd - Doctoral candidate, Early Childhood teacher and teacher educator; Learning Manager with  ECCE Program at Holland College; site research coordinator for Handle with Care Program; Education and Training Coordinator ECDA, early childhood coach, active in research and writing; active in ECDA, ECE teacher in Skiskia, Alberta.


McRoberts, Tim J, MEd, - Doctoral candidate; Program Manager and past curriculum consultant and instructor with Holland College, active conference speaker and workshop leader in Canada and abroad; active in research and writing; university lecturer; served on a variety of boards and committees; committed to examining new ways to promote learning for adults.


Taylor, Alice M, Diploma in Early Childhood and Diploma in Adult Education,  – early childhood teacher, teacher educator and consultant; retired as head of Early Childhood Education Department, Holland College; PEI partner in Handle with Care program (HWC); Past-President of PEI Early Childhood Development Association, co-author of The Dance of Bullying; served on a number of boards and committees; activist in support of quality education.


Whelan, Dr. William M, Ph.D., Professor of Physics at UPEI; contributor to medical journals, active volunteer having served as President of Canadian Red Cross Society (PEI Division) and member of National Board of Directors; President of the Prince Edward Island Home and School Federation; Co-chair, PEI Governance Commission; Past President of the PEI Home and School Federation; Chair of the PEI Science Fair and Co-chair of the Canada Wide Science Fair. 


White-Hassler, Thomas, MEd, -  retired Dean of Information and Resources  and Computer Science Professor at Manchester Community College, Conn; extensive experience in organizing and implementing technology support for education; worked with Navajo Tribe in Arizona  and as a system analyst with the Alaska Department of Education; instructor of on-line courses; active volunteer.
