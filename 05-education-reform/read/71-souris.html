WHAT WE LEARNED IN SOURIS

The Souris Study included a review of current offerings, capacity, interests and needs for further training as viewed by residents, employers and the community at large. The study was funded by the Department of Workforce and Advanced Learning through the Canada-PEI labour Market Agreements.


Among the things we learned:

  • Both employers and learners want more hands on or practical experience as part of their training programs.

  • The provision of information is an education function or service quite separate from instruction; Islanders lack current and independent information when it comes to making educational decisions.

  • The delivery of a quality education in Souris, and other Island communities, should be someone’s list of responsibilities.

  • Our inability or unwillingness to differentiate between education and instruction (the function of schools) delays the likelihood of improvement.

  • On- line courses are of interest, however, better internet connections are needed

  • The quality assurance feedback loop for education seems broken or missing.

The final report is available on the Education 20/20 website.
