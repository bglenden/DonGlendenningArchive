Published January 25, 2014, this analytical letter deconstructs the school system's structural characteristics and limitations. Don identifies three school functions: skill development for societal participation, workforce development, and childcare. He notes that schools control curriculum delivery for all students over 12 years regardless of progress, offer limited accountability mechanisms, and operate without market incentives for improvement. The letter reveals his systems analysis approach and conviction that structural reforms (not just professional development or technology upgrades) are necessary for meaningful change.

Dear Sir:

An insight into the operation of the school system 


To deal effectively with our math concerns, we  first need to deal with the following questions:

Why do we want our schools to do?  It seems that they have three functions: (1) to help students gain the skills they need to function in, contribute to and enjoy the society the live in (2) to help the Province and country develop the people it needs to participate in a global economy. (3) Provide care for young people while parents work.  

What math skills are needed? Ask students, Ask the community, Ask employers.  

We need a clear statement to this effect or a different

Through our agent,  students, our government, we establish and fund schools to provide specialized help to young people in gaining or improving skills needed throughout life. mathematics is included among the skills we fund.

Schools deliver instruction in mathematics to every student for 12 years; students are not allowed to finish early and receive credit; nor can they het help for a few extra days to get caught u. 

I’ve not found any evidence of minimum levels of performance required to receive a diploma.

Students, parents and taxpayers generally have very limited recourse if they are not satisfied with the results; they cannot change teachers, change schools, stop payment, 

The school system defies all normal market conditions; governments require students to attend, decides what will be taught, by whom, where and for how long; it licenses teachers; then employs them text books   As a result, there is no reason or incentive  to do better.

Withdraw services, seek another supplier, become more effective or efficient.

Because the government is also the employer, and governors are politicians, even small incident becomes a political issue and
