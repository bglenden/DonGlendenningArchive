May 1, 2014


Excellence in Education Award


The nomination process for a new education award has given Islanders an opportunity to demonstrate support for their favourite school or learning center; a dozen nominations came from across the Island and now await the judges’ decision as to who will walk away with the $2,500 award. Some nominations came directly from schools and learning centres, some came from parents obviously engaged in the education process and one came from an interested citizen as a result of an article in her local paper. Most nominations arrived during the past ten days and came via the Internet, our mailbox and drop-off locations, courtesy of the Community Foundation and J. Red Shoes, a shoe store in downtown Charlottetown.


The Excellence in Education Award was established by Education 20/20 Inc., thanks to an anonymous Island family, in recognition of the direct link between engagement and student achievement. According to the National Parent Teacher Association (PTA), student achievement improves, teacher morale rises and communication increases when schools and communities work together. Nominators were asked to build their case around the following five areas of engagement: student to student, student to teacher, student to learning materials, student to community partnerships, and parental involvement.

Education 20/20 Inc. is a new independent, broad-based, national, not-for-profit education organization, located in Charlottetown, PEI. In addition to an education award, Education 20/20 maintains a website at the link www.education2020.ca, publishes a monthly electronic newsletter and offers workshops on a variety of subjects.
