KEY ISSUES

___________________________________________________________________________


Key issues include:

  1. Terminology – A common terminology improves communication throughout an organizatione

  2. Purpose – purpose sets the stage for what we do and how we organize to do it. The PEI education literature refers to helping people find “a place” in society; we need to better define that “place”. Life roles have been described as that of an individual, citizen, a producer, a consumer, family member and student. All roles can benefit from education but all may not need the - same mix at the same time.

  3. Services – education is a service; sub-services include: information, counselling, instruction, products and technology, quality assurance. governance, funding and human resources . The publc should be infomed o services available and how to access them..

  4. Structure - ”Form follows function” is the standard when structuring any venture; one would expect, therefore, that the basic education structure would reflect services offered. Instead, th Department appears to be structured around clients served.

  5. Autnomy - Front line service providers (teachers) need both expertise and autonomy to respond to learner needs. The OECD tells us that PISA results are better for students in schools with autonomy for their day-to-day operation.

  6. Accountabilities - Mathew Bedard of the Montreal Economic Institute points out that “autonomy and accountability jointly applied” would cost practically nothing and allow education to better adapt to the diversity of student needs and aptitudes. Yet teachers and principals appear to have little freedom to do so.

  7. Collaboration – the literature tells us that education thrives on collaboration and communication; Paul Bennet speaks of the” muffled voices of teachers” who work alone in classrooms, in fact the profession itself, operates as a silo in the community.

  8. Education system – Although provinces generally speak o an education system, the writer’s review found only school systems.

  9. Fear factor


These issues are not new; no doubt readers have others to propose. All should be considered. They need to be dealt with soon, and should be dealt with in a public manner.
