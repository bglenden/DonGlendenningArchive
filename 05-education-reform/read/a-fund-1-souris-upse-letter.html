A formal fundraising letter from Don Glendenning to Karen Jackson, President of the PEI Union of Public Sector Employees, requesting a $1,500 lead donation to fund innovative educational initiatives in Souris. Don describes Education 20/20 Inc. (incorporated 2012, registered charity 2015) as a volunteer-governed organization. He highlights the organization's involvement with the Souris Garden Club and education-to-work transition issues, proposing the UPSE contribution be matched by Education 20/20 to demonstrate community partnership. The letter showcases Don's fundraising skills and ability to frame educational needs persuasively.

Karen Jackson, President

PEI  Union of Public Sector Employees:

4 Enman Crescent

Charlottetown. PEI

C1E 1E6


Dear Ms. Jackson:

Supporting Innovation in Souris


First let me commend you for your “UPSE Has a Heart” initiative, I never really doubted it but it is great to see it reaffirmed.


Education 20/20 Inc.  is an independent, broad based not-for-profit education group  that was incorporated in 2012 and received a charitable number in 2015 (822339-4).  It is headquartered in Charlottetown and is governed by a volunteer board. Activities include an annual education award, a monthly bilingual newsletter, workshops and public presentations, research and occasional papers and a variety low-key consultations with various individuals and organizations.  More detailed information may be found in the enclosed literature.

Innovation is needed everywhere and can start anywhere.  Quite by accident we became aware of the Souris Garden club, a largely volunteer initiative to introduce young people into gardening, food production and environmental matters. Since then, we’ve become involved in other educational issues especially issues around the transition of young people from school to work.  Offering financial support for innovative educational initiatives is simply another component. 


This letter is an invitation to the Union of Public Sector Employees to be the lead donor with a $1,500 grant to fund an innovative educational initiative in Souris; in return, Education 20/20 will raise a matching amount .  With funds in hand, Education will then call for proposals as per the attached description.  Should our fundraising exceed the matching amount, we will apply the surplus to a similar initiative in 2019.  Education 20/20 will not claim your contribution until we have a matching amount in our account.  By joining forces, we can double the likelihood that innovation will find take in Souris

We thank you in advance for your consideration and, should this application be successful, will give appropriate public recognition to the Union of Public Service Employees for your role in making it happen.


Sincerely


Don Glendenning

President
