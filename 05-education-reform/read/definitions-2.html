Guardian letter advocating for common definitions in education discourse. Don proposes the definition "any experience that has a formative effect on the way a person thinks, feels or acts" and calls on PEI's Department of Education to develop and publish clear definitions of key terms to improve institutional clarity and communication.


Dear Editor:

A Definition Needed

D-21-A

It would be helpful to have a common definition for education; they are useful in that they help us to communicate more clearly and to avoid misunderstanding: definitions are especially important in a subject as complex as education. . The Dictionary of Education (Good) defines definition as “the process of determining the definition or meaning of a word, idea or proposition in general and within a given context; the process of assigning meaning enhances both precision and clarity”.


The definition that best captures meaning for me is “any experience that has a formative effect on the way a person thinks feels or acts”. But there are many others:

  • The act o giving or receiving instruction

  • The level of development of a person’s thoughts, actions and feelings


I searched some of the local literature; thought that I might find some definitions on school sites. My search skills are not very highly developed so my efforts turned up blank. I even checked school websites, CAMET, CMEC, CEA ad Institutes Canada. While I would be interested in any definitions, my particular interest s with the Department in that their role is so crucial to all we do.


Sincerely


Don Glendenning
