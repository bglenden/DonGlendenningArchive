Welcome

You are reading Open Education, a monthly electronic newsletter published by Education 20/20 Inc.  Its purpose is to provide information, provoke debate and encourage discussion about educational matters of interest and concern. 

 Education 20/20 Inc.is:  

Education 20/20 Inc. is a new, independent, national, not-for-profit organization devoted to ensuring a quality education for every Canadian.  We are governed by a volunteer board drawn from the community at large, but individual members do not represent any other organization, institution or point of view.

Although the services of Education 20/20 Inc. are still under development, our intention to:

  • Increase the availability of public information
  • Recognize excellence
  • Encourage public engagement
  • Encourage innovation


We believe:

We believe that education begins at birth and continues through a lifetime, is the key to our success both as individuals and communities, and that investing in education pays high dividends.  At the individual level, the purpose of education is to develop a personal combination of knowledge, skills and attitudes that will best each of us in all life roles, that of “an individual, family member, citizen, learner, consumer and producer”.  But life and life roles are not static, therefore, one must keep in in mind the ever changing interests and needs of each person and the constantly changing society in which we live.    




While education is life-long, one can identify three distinct stages:

  • Early childhood, the first few years, led by the family as educator, sets the standard for our personal characteristics  such as self- confidence and interpersonal relationships.
  • Youth and early adulthood is a period of a rapid gain in both knowledge and skills, as well as strengthening both personal and interpersonal skills.
  • Adulthood where one fine-tunes, fills gaps or even sets off on new educational journeys.


Education System


We believe that an education system should:

  • Focus on needs and interests of individual learners
  • Recognize educational experiences gained outside schools
  • Include engagement of both learners, parents and the public at large.  
  • Encourage and recognize independent learning.
