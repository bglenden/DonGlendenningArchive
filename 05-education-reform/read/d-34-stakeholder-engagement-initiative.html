A comprehensive November 2013 proposal establishing Education 20/20 Inc.'s stakeholder engagement strategy. Don articulates a diagnosis that education systems are failing to prepare citizens for the future due to structural inflexibility and lack of market accountability. His three-pronged solution involves: information provision about best practices and needed changes, stakeholder engagement through conversations and debates, and creating incentives for organizations demonstrating best practices. Specific activities include monthly electronic newsletters, occasional papers, video webinars, media releases, blogs, public forums, speaker bureaus, and surveys. This vision demonstrates Don's belief that systems change requires simultaneously improving information access, fostering dialogue, and recognizing excellence.

Education 20/20 INC.

Stakeholder Engagement  Initiative

November 15, 2013


Purpose:  


To provide stakeholders with current information about informed opinion, research and best practices about education and to encourage them to become get involved in promoting and supporting a quality education for every Canadian.


Background

It is widely recognized that the level and quality of our education is key to our future including our futures as individuals, families, communities and countries.   But this view is tempered with a concern that the education currently available is not preparing nor likely to prepare our people, especially our young people, for the future.  The Conference Board of Canada in calling for greater emphasis on the fundamental skills of literacy and numeracy, the skills of self-management and the ability to work as a team member.  In addition, tomorrow’s citizens both high level technical and entrepreneurial skills.


Public Education is a line department of Provincial governments with instruction organized in a time  based subject centered batch process offering little in the way of support for innovative thinking and independent learning.  As a consequence, many Canadians leave the public school system without either adequate basic skills or an entrepreneurial spirit.  


The system must change but it is unlikely that it will make the desired changes without some outside help.   We say this based on the view that the education enterprise lacks the usual market measures that ensure change.   .  This need is becoming increasingly obvious. It is our view that, both in PEI and elsewhere, that shortfall is increasingly becoming recognized, brought on by a number of events including international competition, on-line-delivery of courses, student demand, large numbers of young people without sufficient basic skills, declining postsecondary enrolments, funding shortages, changing student demand and job skill requirements. To provide this kind of education will require a system that is flexible, transparent, customized with  accountable clearly identified standards



Change is fuelled by Information,  however, we know that having information itself doesn’t guarantee change – but information certainly supports it.  We feel that education sector, compared with other sectors, falls short in the nature and amount of information available to users and the public.  Think of the information, for example, that we receive day in and day out about cancer, diabetes, Alzheimers, the dangers of smoking as well as many other matters relating to our health services.  We need that kind of information about matters of education, information such as early detection of reading problems, helping with homework, standards or independent study.


Successful change will be built upon access to a variety of relevant information and community engagement.  In addition, providing the public with relevant information, this focus  will support the public in its quest for greater participation in the education process.  Little choice, does not encourage independent study, time based, batch process


Enter Education 20/20 Inc., a new independent national organization with the mission of ensuring a quality education for every Canadian.  (Vision statement attached) Against this background, a number of Islanders, seasoned in both the providing and using educational services, have come together  to form Education 20/20 Inc. a national, not for profit, independent  organization committed to providing a quality education for every Canadian.  (See attached mission statement)  We’ve taken a look at the state of education and levers of change, we’ve decided to focus our activities activities on the general public represented by parents, employers and students and to initiate a three-pronged effort to bring about change.

Strategy

The major components of our strategy will be:

  • The provision of information – To gather, store, access, produce and disseminate information geared to the public at large with special attention to especially those with children still in the public school system focused on best practices and desired system change.   While the availability of relevant information does not guarantee change, it is certainly a contributing factor.
  • Engagement  of stakeholders– To provoke, organize and support opportunities to involve the public, including parents, employers and students in conversations, dialogues, debates and exchanges in a wide range of educational matters including philosophy, standards, curriculum, flexibility.  
  • Incentives – To create incentives and awards for individuals and organizations that demonstrate best practices 

Proposal – to dramatically increase the range and nature of information about education available stakeholders


Activities -

  • Electronic newsletter – one page newsletter published monthly
  • Occasional papers – two page guest papers
  • Develop Videos/webinars – 3-4 minute video guest  videos
  • Media releases – aiming for a quarter page monthly
  • Blog – commence when web is operational
  • Public events – panel, forum, speech, workshop and round tables monthly as a means of informing the public, gathering media coverage and supporting education leaders and innovators.
  • Speaker’s bureau – to assist community groups seeking speakers on education topics.  
  • Surveys – series of questionnaire surveya seeking views as to needs and priorities for use in informing educators and encouraging change.
