Solving A Math Problem

D-5-B Occasional paper


The PEI Curriculum Guide for Grade 9 mathematics includes a goal for students to “become mathematically literate adults”. It seems like an achievable goal, at least for most students but, if math test scores and public comment are used as the measure, the goal remains a challenge rather than an accomplishment. I’ll not rehash the figures but simply point out that the very persistence of criticism demands attention.


I realize that every person who comes through a school door is unique, and unique in a variety of ways. At the same time I should point out that the school system has a considerable range of resources at its disposal, if and when it decides to solve the problem. Chief among their variables:


  • Time on task counts – if schools were really interested in teaching rather than instructing, they would find a way to extent teaching periods, days or years to those who need it.

  • Age based grading - teachers know that students in any subject and in any classroom are at different levels – and teachers and students alike are frustrated by it. This is especially so for basic education. One school I viaited had all students taking math at their own level at 9:00 each morning with students staying with their peers for the remainder of the day

  • Alignment – is the process of ensuring that the various elements of are work together; what’s missing in the literature, is any reference to alignment between what is taught and ehat is needed or desired by the community being served. Perhaps the best example of the alignment is the lack of community members on curriculum committees.

  • Educational triage – a form of diagnosing who is in most need of help and with what priority. Based on the stories I hear teachers are more likely to place students in a lower level of course than find help for study in the current level

  • Assessment – Ranking and grading students is not a teaching function; it’s time for an independent testing service for those who need or want it.
