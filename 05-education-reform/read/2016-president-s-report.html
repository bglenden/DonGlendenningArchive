Glendenning's comprehensive president's report for 2016 detailing the organization's diverse activities. Education 20/20 published "Open Education," a monthly bilingual newsletter; maintained active social media presence; hosted educational workshops including presentations on "Literacy for the 21st Century" and large-scale assessment research; awarded $2,500 to Grace Christian School for technology innovation; held a regional workshop with the Canadian Education Association; and achieved official charitable status from the Canada Revenue Agency. Glendenning personally met with the provincial Minister of Education and Premier, led presentations to the Progressive Conservative Party, and facilitated community events. The report demonstrates Glendenning's extensive advocacy network and commitment to advancing educational policy on PEI.

EDUCATION 20/20 INC.

President’s Report

For the Year ending December 31, 2016


I am pleased to present the following report of activities for the year ending December 31, 2016.


  • Newsletter: Open Education, a one-page electronic newsletter published monthly in both official languages  
  • Social media: Facebook activity mostly thanks to Tom White-Hassler
  • Workshops:
  • Public presentation on “Literacy for the 21st Century” by Barbara MacNutt
  • Public presentation by Lizzie Yan, an M.Ed. Student at UPEI and Professor Tess Miller a piece of research on “Public Perceptions of Large Scale Assessment”.  
  • Education Award: An award in the amount of $2,500 to Grace Christian School for the manner in which it applies technology throughout the school operation.
  • Selected presentations and meetings: 
  • Meetings: Tom White-Hassler and I met with the Minister of Education: I met with the Premier; Bill Whelan and I made a presentation the regular monthly meeting of the Progressive Conservative Party; met with Bethany MacLeod. PEITF; occasional communication with Peter Bevan Baker’s office.
  • Made a presentation to Tom and Jane White Hassler to recognize Tom’s contributions to Education 20/20 prior to his return to the USA
  • Presented a Certificate of Appreciation to recognize Roger Birt’s financial contribution over a five-year period. 
  • Christmas reception at Haviland Club for members of Education 20/20 network.
  • Beyond Measure – showing of a film on Sunday, October 16, 2016 at City Cinema.
  • Regional Workshop - participated in a Regional workshop in Halifax hosted by the Canadian Education Association; CEA is trying to establish a network of educators across the country.
  • Canada Revenue Agency officially conferred charitable status on Education 20/20. 
  • Preparedness Gap – an ongoing exploration of perceived skill gap between what students learned during their public school yes and the requirements of employers and Postsecondary institutions.
  • Thanks: Regis Duffy for sponsorship of an annual school award, Credit Union and RBC Dominion Securities, Roger Birt for financial support over a five-year period, and Louise Carota for art work and technical service on a Power Point presentation. 
  • Thanks also to CFPEI for ongoing support, Rachel Bellaire for translation, other donors both named and anonymous, Directors and volunteers.



Respectfully submitted

Don Glendenning, President
