Dated June 3, 2019, this letter addresses Hon. Brad Trevors, newly appointed Minister of Education and Life Long Learning. Don congratulates him on his appointment and calls it "the most important portfolio in the Province." The letter updates the Minister on Education 20/20's activities in Souris, including community consultations on continuing education and a research study funded through the Labour Market Development Agreement. Don advocates strongly for continuing education, referencing Holland College's success in 1986-87 when it served over 3,000 night school students. He proposes that institutions encourage part-time education, suggests merging Senior's College and Community Schools into an island-wide continuing education service, and requests a meeting to explore the issue further.


June 3, 2019

Hon, Brad Trevors

Minister of Education and Life Long Learning

PO Box 2000

Charlottetown


Dear Minister:

Education in Souris


First my congratulations on your appointment as Minister of Education; in my view, it is the most important portfolio in the Province. It is an area of interest with me and I look forward to future discussions. But first to bring you up to date on our activities in Souris.


Several months ago. Education 20/20 Inc. met with residents of Souris to explore education issues of mutual interest. The focus soon centered on continuing education for the adult population both for their role in the workforce and also to meet individual interests and needs. A study, funded under the Labor Marked Development Agreement, was undertaken by MRSB and completed in late 2018. Later a presentation was made at a joint meeting of the Deputy Minsters of Education and Labor Force development, and Education 20/20 Inc. will present the results to Souris Town Council on Monday, June 10, 2019. Your office will have copies of the report and I am attaching a copy of our Newsletter describing the project.


As you may know, Minister, I am a believer and advocate for continuing education. (Holland College served over 3,000-night school students in 1986-87, most courses were self-supporting). Speaking generally, it is the view of Education 20/20 Inc, that:

  • Islanders of all ages should be encouraged upgrade and update their education

  • Institutions should be encouraged to offer education a part time basis

  • Continuing education is more than offering regular courses to part time students

  • Continuing education needs a voice at the decision-making table

  • Encouraging education does not necessarily mean financial support,

  • Perhaps Senior’s College and Community Schools could be merged and expanded to create an Island wide. continuing education service


This letter seeks an opportunity to meet to explore the issue more fully


Sincerely


Don Glendenning

President.
