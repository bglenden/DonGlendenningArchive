Editor-year-end-

Year end  ahead. New year

I don’t look for much improvement in  education during the coming year. Government seems satisfied to muddle through with existing philosophy, unclear or unstated principles, narrow focus on needs and services and devoting itself to running schools.  The good news is that (1) the public is becoming increasingly frustrated (and ready to support change) and (2) the current government seems open to both consultation and change.

I believe the following actions are foundational to better education:

  • A clear statement  of direction: (Glossary of terms, Underlying beliefs and/or principles and ervices to bee offered
  • Alternative Best way to deliver each service (Government, commission, education or school boards, through NGO’s, 
  • List of services to be paid for government pay for  (literacy, numeracy, literature, employment preparation, interpersonal relationships, ???)
