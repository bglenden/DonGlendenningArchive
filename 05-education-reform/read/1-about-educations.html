Dear Editor: 

About Education

Education is important; it is also complex. There are many ways to describe it; a common understanding is important. Here’s my take on the subject.

We don’t know very much when we are born but we must know a great deal to survive and even more to thrive; learning is the process a person goes through to close that gap. Teaching refers to helping others with the learning process.  Help with learning usually comes in the form of information, counselling, instruction, funding, resource people, products and technology and quality assurance.

The result of what you have learned is your education; it is a culmination of changes brought about through learning - changes in what you know, what you can do, attitudes, values and more. People demonstrates the nature and level of their education by what they know, can do, and how they act. A good education is sometimes described as one that enables a person to deal with the issues they encounter in their daily lives. Years of study is frequently used as a proxi. 

Over the years, schools and schooling have become a ”public shorthand” for education. The World Bank tells us that “going to school” and getting an education are not the same. I think using the words interchangeably is misleading, a main cause of miscommunication and downplays the importance of our efforts.. 

It behooves us all to choose our words carefully. 

Sincerely

Don Glendenning
