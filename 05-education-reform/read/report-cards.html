A background research document analyzing student report card systems, proposing that they should be linked to outcomes, criterion-referenced, and organized by subject difficulty levels rather than age/grades. Don draws from research and policy documents across provinces, advocating for transparency in reporting systems and parent satisfaction through meaningful teacher comments aligned with learning outcomes.

My notes

Reporting on

Student Progress in School


Note: Most of the notes that follow are lifted from documents in my possession, a number of scans of the internet, and my own notes and are intended as background to our discussion and development of a position paper.


  1. Context:

  • Education helps people prepare to cope with and flourish in the various roles we fill on the road of life.

  • Governments invest in schools to improve the quality of life of its citizens and also to help meet its economic, cultural and social objectives

  • The major contributor to a person’s education is the learning that occurs in the home

  • Report cards report on schooling and tell little about a student’s education.

  • Learning results in a change in behaviour, that is a change of attitude, change of skills and a change in knowledge.

  • Changes in behaviour can be described, taught, measured, observed and reported.


  1. Report cards:

  • Are periodic written statements provided to a student and/or parents on a the student’s performance on changes in students arising from instruction provided by schools

  • Identify planned outcomes in all domains for the reporting period

  • Report on changes since last report and current levels of achievement

  • Identify any obstacles likely to impact on future success and strategy to deal with them

  • Recommend actions the student can/should take to reach objectives

  • Recommend that parents can/should take to improve student performance



  1. Rating scales:

  • Should be linked to outcomes

  • Should be criterion referenced

  • Should be designed around levels of difficulty inherent to the subject matter

  • May include judgment

  • Includes description of levels

  • Should include benchmarks


  1. Teacher Comments:

  • Report card forms generally include place for comments

  • Parents expect teacher comment

  • Parents prefer teacher generated comments over canned ones

  • Should be linked to outcomes


  1. Issues:

  • Parent satisfaction

  • Information provided

  • Disconnect with outcome statements.

  • Lack of relevant criteria

  • Absence of information about actual or desired corrective action, if any


  1. Recommendations/Actions

  • Link achievement to outcomes

  • Review course outcomes found on Department website;

  • discuss outcomes with teacher

  • Talk with other parents

  • Adopt or develop an outcomes based system for reporting and rating


  1. Attachments:

  • PEI reading outcomes

  • PEI math outcomes

  1. References:

  • PEI website Outcomes

  • British Columbia Ministry of Education has a 45 page bulletin on Reporting Student Progress: Policy and Practice

  • Ontario has a number of report cards depending on whether a student attends public or Catholic schools, whether or not the school is semestered and the kind of program in which a person is enrolled

  • Chignecto-Central Regional School Board reports on (1) the Learner Profile, a snapshot of the student’s work habits and social development, (2) Attendance Record and (#) Achievement of Expected Learning Outcomes charts the progress and achievement in relation to the expected learning outcomes.
