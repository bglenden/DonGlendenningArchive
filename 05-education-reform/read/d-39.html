Open Education

39 April 20, 2016


If you want to change student learning then change the methods of assessment – Chuckett and Sutherland


…hands of both teacher and learner…


Elsewhere I reported on a visit to the Provincial Learning Materials Distribution Centre; while there, I picked up a copy of the curriculum guide for Grade 10 Math. The Guide was developed by the Western and Northern Canadian Protocol, a consortium of Western Provinces, and was adopted by a other Provinces including PEI. I enjoyed math in school and still feel that my math skills are quite adequate for good citizenship but I must confess that I really didn’t understand most of the outcomes in the guides.


When I checked some old notes, I was reminded that outcomes should be clear and unambiguous. I would say that the Math outcomes are anything but – at least to me. While I realize that guides are written for teachers and not for students, my guess is that some teachers may also benefit from more cleary written guides.


There are a number of ways to describe outcomes : (1) educational outcomes as William Spadey proposed, (2) curriculum outcomes as found generally in public zchools across the country, and (3) competencies used by sime colleges, universities and qualification authorities.


I started this column with the intention of recommending that students be given curriculum outcomes for subjects they study; based on my review, however, I believe that a new level of detail be added first.

Effectiveness or efficiency


A couple of items caught my eye recently. One was an announcement from Ontario about adding reading specialists to schools; the other was about converting 32 administrative positions to teaching positions in PEI. To my way of thinking, both are examples of increasing efficiency.

Keep an eye out for a presentation about PEI results on the Programme for the International Assessment of Adult Competencies (PIAAC). The presentation will provide information on where PEI stands compared to the rest of Canada, and, in particular, to other Atlantic provinces.
