For Immediate Release Oct. 23, 2013



A Framework for Learning



The learner and the “learning process” was the focus of discussion at a community workshop sponsored by Education 20/20 in Charlottetown, Monday evening. According to the spokesperson and President of this newly formed organization, Dr. Don Glendenning, this is where all educational planning begins. Because of this, participants were asked to initially recall their most memorable learning experiences and why it still had an impact on their lives. It the end, the workshop was designed to go under that activity of learning centres, schools, and adult programs, to reflect on what really makes the difference., i.e. connection to one’s reality, cultural sensitivity, and engagement within the community that the learner is a part of.


“A Framework for Learning” identified four elements to be taken into account: the learner, learning process, needs of the learner, and support systems in their lives, i.e. the family, community, and systems of learning . Presenters Alice Taylor, PEI partner of Handle with Care Program, Eileen Conway-Martin, Communications Director, Native Council of PEI, and Brenda Goodine, Learning Manager, Early Childhood Education, Holland College led participants through presentations and discussions of learner differences, the learning process, and learning support activities.


Education 20/20 Inc. is a broad based, national, not-for-profit education organization, located in Charlottetown, PEI and is governed by a volunteer Board of Directors. The focus of the organization is to ensure a quality education for every Canadian. Focus is place on true involvement and “engagement” of the learner, in partnership with their family and community
