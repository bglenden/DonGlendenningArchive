The Need for Clarity-3

_____________________________________________________________________

Public education has a clarity problem. In the interest of being inclusive, those who set, develop and interpret policy in education, seem to overlook the need by readers for detail.

Recently I had occasion to check the Department definition of “education”. Imagine my surprise to learn that the Department no longer has such a definition. It seems that “education” was replaced by “school” in a recent revision to the Act. Seems strange to have a Department of Education without a definition of “education”.

Guess that leaves us with a school system rather than an education system. It also ignores the cautionary note of the World Bank about the difference between “education and going to school”.

Secondly, the Department of Education sees its role as being “to develop and deliver high quality programs and curriculum”. “Delivering curriculum” fails to capture the dynamics of helping people develop the knowledge skills and attitudes needed to thrive as a citizen in a democracy.

Education is complex; our descriptions must be clear. Definitions of key words are called for. A taxonomy would be useful.

Sincerely

Don Glendenning May 24, 2024
