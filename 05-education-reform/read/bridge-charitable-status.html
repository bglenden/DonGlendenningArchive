Don writes to Richard Bridge, a barrister and solicitor, requesting pro bono legal assistance for Education 20/20 Inc. to gain charitable status with Revenue Canada. The organization had previously withdrawn its application in July 2013 due to questions about whether their educational objectives qualified as charitable. With a year of operations completed, they developed new objectives and supporting documentation, and now seek Bridge's expertise in navigating the charitable status process. Don demonstrates confidence in the organization's mission to improve education and its unique national scope.

Dear Mr. Bridge:


Education 20/20 Inc. is an independent, broad based, national, not-for-profit education organization, incorporated in 2012, located in Charlottetown, PEI and is governed by a volunteer Board of Directors; we feel that we are rather unique in that our focus is on a “quality education for every Canadian” regardless of the field of study, level, provider or purpose. We are of the view that education, especially of our young people, is key to their future – and to ours. We need to focus on education not just schools and engage the whole community both in the change process and the final delivery model. We believe that Education 20/20 Inc. has a role to play in bringing about such changes.

In early 2013, Education 20/20 Inc. applied for recognition as a charitable institution; in response, we were advised that Revenue Canada had significant questions about whether or not our objectives were “charitable”; on July, 2013, we withdrew our application.

With a year of operation behind us, we are now ready to re-apply. In preparation, we have developed a new set of objectives and supporting activities, completed a new application and gathered supporting documentation. In the process, your name was mentioned as a person with a reputation for helping organizations gain charitable status. This is a preliminary letter to alert you to our work and our need and to ask if you would consider helping Education 20/20 Inc. with this next step, and to do so pro bono.

I thank you for reading this letter; I will contact you by phone on Friday, September 5 to follow up; should you want, I can be reached at my cottage in PEI (902-651-2186).

Enjoy our remaining summer.









Sincerely





Don Glendenning

President
