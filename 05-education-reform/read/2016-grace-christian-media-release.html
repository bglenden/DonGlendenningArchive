Grace Christian Receives Excellence in Education Award


Grace Christian School was named this year’s winner of an Excellence in Education Award from Education 20/20 Inc. This award is presented annually to a school or learning center for the manner and extent of its use of technology to enhance learning and teaching. The use of technology begins early at Grace Christian School and is part of the school’s efforts to develop the leaders, thinkers, and innovators of the future.


“At GCS, we view technology as a tool that plays an integral role in shaping any learning environment. Students at GCS learn to be responsible, well-equipped, digital citizens”, said Principal Jason Biech.


Beginning with the youngest learners, students are provided with daily opportunities to enrich their learning using a variety of technological tools and resources, including Lego WeDo, Scratch, LittleBits, Apple TVs, digital microscopes, iPads, and a LeapFrog learning environment to name a few. In addition, the flipped classroom allows students to view material at home, allowing classtime to be devoted to inquiry-based projects, follow-up discussions, and lab work.


“It was not just the availability of technological tools that impressed the judges, but also the integration of technology into every aspect of the school’s life,” said Don Glendenning, president of Education 20/20.


Grace Christian School actively prepares students for 21st century global citizenship and was founded in Charlottetown in 1978 as a private/ Christian co-educational school. The school currently offers a liberal arts curriculum to over 150 students in grades K-12.


Grace Christian School was one of three schools nominated for the award. The award, established in 2014 by Education 20/20 Inc., through the generosity of an Island family, includes a cheque for $2,500 and is intended to call attention to important characteristics of effective schools.


This was the third year for the award. Earlier awards focused on engagement and were awarded to Kinkora Regional High School and Prince Street Elementary School. The award was open to all schools and learning centres on Prince Edward Island.
