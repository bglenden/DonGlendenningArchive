Fundraising letter from Don Glendenning soliciting $1,500 for five island-wide workshops on education reform. Don describes Education 20/20 Inc. as a volunteer-led, nationally incorporated organization with diverse stakeholder engagement. Highlights achievements including public consultations, workshops, newsletter publication, and website launch. Notes partnerships with the Community Foundation of PEI and upcoming initiatives focused on youth issues and educational engagement. Demonstrates Don's collaborative fundraising approach.

Dear


Without doubt, education is important, changes are needed, and there is wide support for change both inside and outside the system; on the other hand, proposed changes fail to deal with a number of underlying weaknesses such as autonomy, accountability and the mismatch between skills taught and skills needed. In addition, required changes are not likely to happen unless the public, you and I, demand it. Traditional efforts to bring about change have not worked. Some innovative, perhaps even bold, measures are required, but we think that a better education system is worth it.


During the past few months, I’ve been working with a small group of Islanders who share these views and are willing to devote time and energy to the change process. The group, Education 20/20 Inc. is a broadly based, nationally incorporated, not-for-profit education organization, located in Charlottetown, PEI. It is governed by a volunteer Board of Directors; people who have demonstrated an interest in and knowledge of education but are not representative of any educational constituency. To date, we have:

  • Held consultations with education officials, met with both Home and School Federations (English and French) the PEI Professional Engineers Association, been in touch with other organizations and held conversations with individuals and small groups from every walk of life.

  • Held two public workshops in Charlottetown

  • Published a monthly electronic newsletter and occasional papers, and have

  • A website about to be launched



In the New Year, we will be:

  • Teaming up with the Community Foundation of Prince Edward Island to organize public discussions about issues facing Island youth – many of which are related to education.

  • Announcing an award for an Island school, or family of schools, that best demonstrates engagement in its every-day activities.

  • Undertaking surveys of users of educational services.


Our group cannot make this happen alone and, while we are appreciative of general support received to date, at the moment we are seeking $1,500 to cover out of pocket costs for the five workshops throughout the Island.






While we are incorporated nationally we do not, as yet, have charitable status. Contributions, therefore, should be directed to Community Foundation of Prince Edward Island, 119 Queen Street, Charlottetown, PE C1A 4B3. The Foundation also administers our funds and issues charitable receipts.


The enclosed Vision statement and newsletter will provide background on both our beliefs and activities, and I would be pleased to provide additional information if needed.


I’ll be in touch in a few days seeking an appointment to talk further about education and to seek your support.


Sincerely



Don Glendenning

President
