Glendenning Notes

Clarity PEI -1


The World Bank was correct; it warned developing countries about confusing going to school with getting an education. We may be doing that too. While schools, colleges and universities are important contributors, one’s education includes learning of all kinds, in all places, at all times and not just when one is in school. 

My third point is a call for transparency.  Public educators are not comfortable talking about education. Although I’ve encountered the “fear factor” on a number of occasions I still do not understand it. A friend in another Province attributes it to MLA interference; a different source suggested at it reflected alack of condidence.

 Lack of clarity also distorts the education structure in that the Department is focused on running schools rather than providing education services.
