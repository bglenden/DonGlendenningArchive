Realignment needed


It’s not unusual at this time of year to wonder if we should take our cars for an alignment check. We may not realize it, but school systems may also need realignment and should be checked from time to time. A thorough inspection will cover a number of points but here are the four areas to check first:

  • Relevance of outcomes to life needs

  • Clearly assessment criteria

  • Outcomes studied form the basis of reporting to students and parents

  • Willingness to adjust time available to students if needed


School principals have been designated as instructional leaders in their schools and, presumably, have authority to adjust the above variables as needed.



Not all alignment problems arise from school variables under the control of the school but, If your child is having a problem with a subject, services for your child, I suggest the following (1) review the outcomes for the course or grade of concern to see if they match your child’s needs, (2) check the most recent report card to see if the subjects listed match the outcomes planned, (3) ask for a copy of the criteria used to check your child’s progress, and (4) talk with the principal to see if the school can arrange more study time, if needed.
