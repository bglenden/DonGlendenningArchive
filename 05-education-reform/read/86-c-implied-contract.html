IMPLIED CONTRACT

It is not clear to me just what the PEI Government is offering  (promising?) its citizens in the way of education services.   PEI has some 155,000 citizens living in a democracy; each brings own capacity for change, each brings prior learning and experience and each brings own expectations and vision. All are expected to fill all life roles, that of “an individual, a citizen, a family member, a learner, a consumer and a producer.”  All can benefit from education services; what is our educational commitment to each?


I’m not sure, but our education system appears to promise: 

  • 12 years of study rather than a level you will reach
  • 12 years of instruction in a public school of Government’s choice
  • 12 years of instruction in a range subjects determined by the education system.
  • Access to study some subjects of learner’s choosing 
  • A graduation certificate or diploma depending on achievement level attained.


 But should we specify a level?

  • Instruction in basic skills to a level needed to function as a citizen
  • Instruction leading to entry level employment?
  • Skills needed to function in a democracy?
  • Skills need to care for one’s self?
  • Skills of a life long learner?
  • Skills needed to function in a democracy


Some Islanders may also be provided with:

  • A work qualification?
  • A level that meets entry to post secondary institutions?


It’s also unclear if Government’s role is to: 

  • Simply inform Islanders of their  options?
  • Encourage people to participate in education offerings
  • Provide financial support?


I write to learn; during the past few months I’ve typed and scribbled notes on many subjects including the ones on this page.  I’ll now set about trying to sort out answers or at least what I believe. 


 I invite readers to join the conversation 




Don Glendenning, Editor
