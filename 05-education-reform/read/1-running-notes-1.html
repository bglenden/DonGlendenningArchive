Education Philosophy & Reform


EDUCATION 20/20 INC.

January 20, 2015

Mathematics Preparedness (1)


Notes by Don Glendenning


NOTE: These notes focus on concerns of Island youth about thei ”preparedness for employment” and will evolve as our thinking/planning evolves.

Background


It is widely recognized that education is a key factor in determining one’s quality of life; in the same way, the collective education of our citizens will determine to a significant degree the future of our community and country. One of the most frequently mentioned concerns by Islanders is the low literacy levels among adults as reported via PISA results, recent CFPEI survey of Island youth, presentation to the Standing Committee by the Charlottetown Area Chamber of Commerce, a 2014 survey by survey by the PEI Home and School Federation and a series of workshops on Preparedness held throughout PEI by Education 20/20 Inc. Based on the above an discussions with a number of individuals and organizations, Education 20/20 decided to explore the issue of basic math skills needed to function in a modern economy.


Meeting 1;

A working group (Duffy, Miller, White-Hassler, Greyborn and Glendenning) met on Friday, January 9, 2015 to explore the issue using the following framework:

  • Life roles which people fill: individual, citizen, student, producer, consumer and individual

  • Sources of help: self-study, instruction, on-line-courses, tutoring, on-the-job, experience

  • Assessment of preparedness: level and rank

  • Documentation: transcript, credential, reference and qualification


The group agreed that we needed more detailed information about the math content employers need; although not spelled out in detail, implicit in the discussion is the view that content will be identified in skills or competies.


Follow-up:

Following the meeting, I spoke with Lori Johnston, Director of Workplace Learning PEI, (www.workplacelearningpei.com) a provincially funded agency that provides the following services to Island businesses: Instructional Systems Design, On-line and Blended learning, Consulting, Employee Assessment, Training Needs Assessment, and Trainer Training. I believe that Workplace Learning PEI has much to offer to our search and Lori has agreed to make a presentation on their work, and to answer questions. Unless I hear otherwise, this will be the next step in our exploration process
