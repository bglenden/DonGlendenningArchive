September 4, 2013

EDUCATION 20/20 INC.


Communications strategy


Note: discussion points


Background:


The mission of Education 20/20 Inc. is to improve the quality of education for every Canadian; central to accomplishing its mission are the twin pillars of information and engagement; information being the fuel and engagement the cauldron of change. The nature and range of information about education available to the public does not reflect the significance of the subject; we propose to initiate some changes. Communication activities will focus on education, not just schooling, and the needs of learners and the learning process with our website as the hub.


Currently have:

  • Newsletter

  • Web site

  • Blog

  • Surveys


Guiding principles:

  • Encourage others to write

  • Inform: Gather, all mediainformed opeinion

  • Gather info: surveys,

  • Clientele: public. MLA, trustees, employers, not the professionals, students

  • Focused on best practices and desired system change...

  • Both official languages

  • CVA model – practitioners not bureaucrats

Components

  • Webpage, Blog, Twitter, announcements, links, donation channel, releases,

  • Newsletter – Open education?, electronic but printable, monthly to report on current activities of Education 20/20 but also events in education in the community at large.

  • Surveys of to gather information of interest to our target group

  • Occasional papers on subjects consistent with our mission and priorities

  • Presentations

  • Media releases, Brochures, Wikis that allow readers to participate in the actual development of articles, Posters, Guides, Booklets for new mothers –see CLIA, Instructional material




Operations:

  • Communications committee

  • Staff

  • Volunteers

  • Budget

  • Writers: students, teachers in training, paid journalists, retirees,


Issues:

  1. Audience

  2. Level

  3. Focus

  4. Sponsorships/advertisements

  5. Kind of info: curriculum, standards, basic skills

  6. Public newsletter like Senior’s magazine

Initial Steps
