Skills development starts at home


Skills development needs to be defined. Presumably, it means increasing our ability to do something but that “something” is unclear. Some people limit skills to those in the hi-tech field but most seem to agree that it includes mathematics and communication skills as well.


The foundation of a person’s skills lies in the home and the K-12 education system; some referred to these as skills for citizenship, meaning a level of skills needed to participate as citizens. Postsecondary institutions and employers may need those skills but at a higher level or they may need completely different ones. In any case, a common definition is important. Just who sets standards and measures achievement is seems a bit unclear.


Workplace Learning PEI. for example, provides each student with a list of competencies learned during their training program; students have it for their own record or to show to employers, if needed. Most developed countries, other than Canada and the USA, have qualification authorities; so, does the Province of Ontario. Some Islanders think that PEI should have a separate testing centre. The public workshop was organized by Education 2020 Inc.
