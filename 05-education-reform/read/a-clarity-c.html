Don analyzes conceptual clarity issues in PEI education, identifying eight areas needing reform: terminology, functions/services, education vs. schooling distinction, purpose, structure, autonomy, accountability, and self-correcting mechanisms. Drawing on World Bank and OECD research, he argues these are easily correctable structural problems that require systemic change to modernize education delivery.

Conceptual Clarity-C

Doc 5/Clarity

The literature speaks of the need for educational reform; the writer sees it as a call for clarity. Uncertainties include:

  1. Terminology – The education literature needs to be read carefully. No word has a simple meaning; every word seems to have multiple meanings or at least multiple shadings of the same meaning. In an endeavour as large and complex as education, It is likely that shades of meanings exist. A PEI lexicon would be very helpful.

  2. Functions and services - Education is a service; typical ducation functions or services include: information, counselling, instruction, products and technology, quality assurance. governance, funding and human resources. Learners may need one or all services at any point in their education journey. Services need to be clearly identified and available to all Island citizens not just to citizens enrolled in schools and programs. How services are organized and paid for is a separate issue..

  3. Education versus schooling – The World Bank, the largest funder of public education, cautions Ministries of Education about treating “education and going to school” as though they are one and the same. That belief I also encounter this view in my conversations and in the PEI education literature. A school, like a hospital is only a resource- rich location; education results from experiences gained while there.

  4. Purpose - sets the stage for what we do, how we organize and how we measure.Helping peole find a place in society is insufficient direction o develop programs or measure progress.

  5. Structure – education is a service; the current structures treat education as though it is a product.

  6. Autonomy – a service requires front line staff to have both a high level experience and autonomy to tailor services to client needs. The OECD tells us that student do better from schools with autonomy for their day to day operations.

  7. Accountability – accountability for student for student development is unclear.

  8. Self- correcting mechanism – lack of a self – correcting mechanism

  9. Government protection, fear factor,

  10. ???


My searches for the causes of the above point to the need for structural changes in the delivery system.



Note: All are easily corrected; why not?
