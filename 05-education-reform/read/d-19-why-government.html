Don questions whether government should directly operate schools, recounting a conversation where inadequate rationales emerged (historical precedent, provincial reach, patronage). He contends governments should provide governance, financing, leadership, and information while being better served to delegate school operations, pointing to successful alternatives in higher education, private schools, and charter schools where students perform adequately.

July 7, 2015


Editor,

Charlottetown Guardian

Charlottetown, PEI


Dear Sir: 

Why does government run our public schools?

Several years ago I was having coffee with friends on my cottage deck when the conversation turned to education.  Someone asked the question, “Why does government run our schools?”  There was a period of silence as we looked at each other.  As I recall three answers were ventured. “Government has always run out schools, government is the only organization with the needed provincial reach, and running schools gives government a sense of power (and patronage opportunities)”.  I’ve thought of that discussion a number of time, and the fact that we were not able to muster stronger arguments in support of government’s operational role.

Governments have a role, in fact an important role, in ensuring govrtnance, financing, leadership gathering and providing information, ensuring that education has the technology and products,  human resources.  The problem is that it is so busy running schools that never gets to the only tasks that it can do.

Perhaps delegate the operation of schools to municipalotoesThere a number of things that government should doGovernment

Colleges and universities seem to get along well under boards of governors, students from private schools seem to fare well, students in charter schools on average seem to do as well as those in public schools and most if not all examples of home schooling I encounter seems to work.  

In the meantime, our students continue to attend schools that no longer serve them well.

Sincerely,


Don Glendenning

A long-time student of education
