Education Philosophy & Reform

January 25, 2014


Editor, Charlottetown Guardian

Charlottetown, PEI


Dear Sir:

Basic skills – a chronic problem 


I participate in many discussions about education but one key subject that seldom if ever comes up is that of curriculum; yet, to a large extent, it drives what happens with your child every day in school.   It is obvious that substantial amounts of money and energy goes into curriculum development and it is, or can be, a major source of information.


Every person interested in education should take a look at the curriculum; detailed outlines for every subject and grade can be found on the PEI Department website.  Find a subject of interest or even one that your child is taking and print a copy. It will give you a better understanding of the  and what your child is studying.  Depending on the age of your child, give him or her a copy.  In fact, I think that copies of outcomes should be in the hands of every parent, and the older learners so that they can better track their progress.


You may find it useful if your child is having difficulty with a subject, or understanding the difference between grade levels in a subject, or even the difference between  math for apprentices versus math for university.  Or if you look at the subjects over several grades, it may help you understand how math  gets more difficult in later grades.


Taking a look at the curriculum is a great introduction to the school system on PEI. 


Sincerely


Don Glendenning

A long time student of education
