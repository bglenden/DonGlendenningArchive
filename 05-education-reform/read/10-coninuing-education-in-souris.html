January 2019 paper documenting Education 20/20's needs assessment for continuing education and workforce development in Souris, PEI (population 3,798 in surrounding area). Commissioned with Canada-PEI Labour Market agreement funding, the survey identified training needs and barriers including lack of local opportunities, commuting difficulties, internet access limitations, and awareness gaps. Employer challenges included shallow labor pools, specialized equipment training gaps, and IT skills shortages. Don recommends bridge-building measures: online access, classroom-and-hands-on programming, area-specific skills training, and barrier removal. The paper identifies systemic issues: lack of integrated planning, siloed educators, delivery gaps, and limited community voice in decision-making. This work exemplifies Don's commitment to rural community development and responsive education.

Continuing education in Souris


Occasional Paper No. 10 January 16, 2019

Souris is an urban centre in eastern Prince Edward Island; it has a population in 2016 of 1,053; with additional 2745 people living in the immediate surrounding area. Together they make a community one of 3,798 people. The economy of the area is dominated by seasonal industries of fishing, aquaculture, agriculture and tourism.


In late 2018, Education 20/20 Inc., with financial help from the Canada- PEI Labour Market agreement, commissioned a formal survey of needs and opportunities for training leading o employment. Information gathering included a survey of existing institutions, interviews with employers and employer organizations, interviews with community leaders and government officials and a survey of the public at large. A copy of the report by MRSB: Assessment of Continuing Education and Workforce Development Needs in the Souris Area of PEI is available at www.Education2020.ca).


Barriers to continuing education:

  • Lack of education and training opportunities

  • Inability to commute to other training locations

  • Timing of course offerings

  • Difficulty of accessing high speed internet services

  • Return on investment (Limited match between employment opportunities and training)

  • Lack of awareness of available training opportunities


As one would expect, training interests are wide ranging and include the fields of business, health, IT, various trades and occupations, and training in the French language Indigenous needs were noted but not separately identified.


Employers face the following challenges:

  • Shallow labour pool

  • Lack of training on specialized equipment coming into area

  • Lack of hands on training

  • Cost of travel o courses outside area

  • Lack of basic and advanced IT skills

  • Area specific matters such as a dwindling and aging population, relative isolation, seasonal industries, and the wage structure





As bridge-building measures, the study recommends:

  • Better access to online training

  • Programs offer both classroom and hands- on training

  • Offer area specific skills such as fisheries and agriculture

  • Barriers be addressed

  • Other considerations including the matching of supply and demand, greater emphasis on employability skills, dwindling population, career planning and funding

  • Offer on-line courses.


Underlying issues:

Based on the report and ensuing discussions, Souris appears to be a community :

  • That lends itself to a multi-year project to develop and test an education system to best serve inhabitants of small, rural communities in transition.

  • Seeking more training for its citizens.

  • Where citizens have little voice in decision-making about local educational matters

  • Without an integrated education plan for he area

  • Where educators appear to operate in silos

  • Facing gaps in the delivery system – information, postsecondary offerings, interest courses, credit courses

  • Offering limited hands-on and practical training experience.



Don Glendenning, Editor
