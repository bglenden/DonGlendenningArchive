In this October 2024 letter, Don reconceptualizes education as a service supporting the learning process through information, encouragement, funding, instruction, assessment, assignments, materials, and technology. He notes learners study at their own pace, anywhere and anytime—schools, home, work, transit, day or night. Independent learners are increasing. Traditional education has brought "learners to learning" through schools; Don advocates developing the reverse approach: bringing "learning to learners" wherever they are. This service-oriented perspective represents a fundamental reframing of education from institutional (school-based) to learner-centered (learning-based), reflecting his broader vision of systemic reform.

October 5, 2024

Dear Editor:

Restructuring Education

My conversations about education continue; the most recent conversation focused on structure.

Education is a service; it supports the learning process through information, encouragement, funding, instruction. assessment, assignments, learning materials and technology.

Learners learn at their own pace and they can learn anywhere; Some gather in schools; others learn at home, at work or even in transit. –any time of the day or night  -  and independent learners are on the increase. 

Education has a tradition of bringing learners to learning; it’s time io  further develop the reverse as well.

Sincerely

Don Glendenning
