A marketing/promotional brochure for "Education: A Framework for Reform" from Education 20/20 Inc., repeated three times in the document. The brochure presents the organization's title and contact information (101 Kent St., Charlottetown, PEI). The final section contains "A Call for Action" message noting the gap between Islander educational expectations and reality, followed by seven key reform recommendations. This brochure appears designed for public distribution to raise awareness about education policy issues and mobilize community support for systematic change.

A Call for Action


There is a gap between what Islanders have and what they want in the way of education.


  1. Develop a “clear and compelling” vision of education for all Islanders.

  2. Develop a learner centered service model that provides support in all domains to learners as, when and where needed.

  3. Assume a leadership role in creating a learning culture and providing a quality education for every Islander.

  4. Reimagine and restructure education services to accommodate new learning wherever, whenever and however offered and by whom.

  5. Establish a qualification body for the purpose certifying individual levels of achievement to persons wanting or needing such documentation.

  6. Establish a professional model of service delivery where professionals are directly accountable to clients for the nature, extent and quality of service provided

  7. Put in place a strategy to encourage and support innovation, such a strategy to include freedom to experiment, an innovation fund and an external decision-making committee.
