This comprehensive needs assessment report outlines Education 20/20's findings from a formal survey conducted in late 2018 in Souris (population 3,798). The document identifies critical barriers to continuing education including lack of local training opportunities, inability to commute to distant programs, limited internet access, and low awareness of available services. Employers face a shallow labour pool and lack of specialized training. The report recommends establishing a local agency to coordinate and support continuing education services, offering skill training modules, online courses, and counselling. Potential funders include SkillsPEI, the Department of Education, and private donors. The document demonstrates systematic needs analysis and comprehensive recommendations for rural community educational development.

Continuing Education in Souris

Souris/2019-B



Souris is an urban centre in eastern Prince Edward Island; Souris and the immediate area together make a community one of 3,798 people. The economy of the area is dominated by seasonal industries of fishing, aquaculture, agriculture and tourism.


In late 2018, Education 20/20 Inc., with financial help from the Canada- PEI Labour Market agreement, commissioned a formal survey of needs and opportunities for training leading to employment. Information gathering included a survey of existing institutions, interviews with employers and employer organizations, interviews with community leaders and government officials and a survey of the public at large. ). The study engaged 2,313 households, an on-line survey, the local media and placed posters in a number of popular locations. Business, employer and community groups were included. A copy of the report by MRSB: Assessment of Continuing Education and Workforce Development Needs in the Souris Area of PEI is available at www.Education2020.ca


The report identified a number of barriers to continuing education:

As one would expect, training interests are wide ranging and include the fields of business, health, IT, various trades and occupations, and training in the French language Indigenous needs were noted but not separately identified.

  • Lack of education and training opportunities

  • Inability to commute to other training locations

  • Timing of course offerings

  • Difficulty of accessing high speed internet services

  • Return on investment (Limited match between employment opportunities and training)

  • Lack of awareness of available training opportunities



Employers face the following challenges:

  • Shallow labour pool

  • Lack of training on specialized equipment coming into area

  • Lack of hands on training

  • Cost of travel o courses outside area

  • Lack of basic and advanced IT skills

  • Area specific matters such as a dwindling and aging population, relative isolation, seasonal industries, and the wage structure





Underlying issues:

Based on the report and ensuing discussions, Souris appears to be a community :

  • Seeking more training for its citizens.

  • Where citizens have little voice in decision-making about local educational matters

  • Without an integrated education plan for he area

  • Where educators appear to operate in silos

  • Facing gaps in the delivery system – information, postsecondary offerings, interest courses, credit courses

  • Offering limited hands-on and practical training experience.

  • That lends itself to a multi-year project to develop and test an education system to best serve inhabitants of small, rural communities in transition.


As bridge-building measures, the study recommends:

  • Better access to online training

  • Programs offer both classroom and hands- on training

  • Offer area specific skills such as fisheries and agriculture

  • Barriers be addressed

  • Other considerations including the matching of supply and demand, greater emphasis on employability skills, dwindling population, career planning and funding

  • Souris Regional has capacity and interest in extended day

  • Offer on-line courses.


Local agency

It is our considered view that a local agency should be recruited or established to lead a continuing education initiative in Souris. Leadership by the Town or a charitable agency would ensure consideration of local needs and could help with fund raising.

Such a centre would be expected to:

• Encourage and support education services for residents of the area

• Organize and host educational activities of interest to the community

• Represent the community’s interests in matters of education

• Liaise with all providers of educational services

• Seek financial support


Proposed Services:

  1. Skill training modules (practical and hands on) in the form of a series of 8-week work entry level modules such aas literacy, numeracy, cabinetry, metal fabrication, IT and retail service.

  2. Credit courses modules offered by postsecondary institutions and trade and business societies such as PEI Construction Association, Tourism Industry Association of Prince Edward Island, PEI Business Women’s Association and STEM.

  3. On-line courses through postsecondary institutions assist residents to identify and enroll in on-line courses consistent with their wishes,

  4. Information and Counselling available to residents generally but especially to residents with an inclination or need for continuing education

  5. Personal development - Self- funded (tuition and/or sponsorship) courses in any subject, for any age and at any level.


Benefits to the community:

  • Increases skill levels

  • Increase training offered in the community

  • Develops local capacity

  • Strengthens local social fabric

  • Add to towns amenities


Funding

Potential funders include:

  • SkillsPEI/Department of Workforce Development

  • Self supporting courses and services

  • Department of Education

  • Donors and sponsor

  • In kind services

  • Grants

  • Students in training


Conclusions:

With regard to the Souris project, it is the view of Education 20/20 that:

  • Continuing education is an important part of education

  • The importance of continuing is not recognized within current programming

  • Continuing education vacuum in Souris is not atypical of Island communities

  • Leadership in addressing needs rests with the Province and the community

  • Public and/or private sector may have a role to play

  • .Education 20/20 Inc. is willing to play a supporting role
