Editor,

Charlottetown Guardian

Charlottetown, PEI

Let-8

Dear Sir: 

Reaching our objective 


I believe that (1) a role of an education system to help citizens learn the skills needed to function in society (2) that most people can, with help, learn those skills while in the public school system and, (3) the Government of PEI is both responsible and accountable to see that it happens.  Furthermore, it is the writers view that tools and processes for improvement are available and affordable, and the Government of PEI has the autonomy needed to institute the necessary change.


In saying the above, the writer recognizes that the current Government inherited most, if not all, of the present structure and policies and, in the change process, must honour current legal commitments.


Using PISA test results as an illustration, (OECD , Pisa in Focus,2011) reported:

1) Where schools have greater autonomy over what is taught and how students are assessed, students tend to perform better. 


2.) In countries where schools account for their results by posting achievement data publicly, schools that enjoy greater autonomy in resource allocation tend to show better student performance than those with less autonomy.” 


Sincerely,



Don Glendenning

A long-time student of education
