Dear Editor:

A CALL FOR CLARITY

As a frequent participant in conversations about education, I am struck by the absence of definitions and boundaries; communication and understanding may suffer as a result.

Education is complex; information plays a key role Conceptual clarity is key for teaching success. Examples include:

  • Department of Education frequently adds “Lifelong Learning” or “Early Childhood Education” to title; aren’t they both part of education?

  • Many references to “ young in society”; doesn’t Department serve all -lslanders?

  • Philosophy paper refers to helping young people to “secure a place” in society; How do we design programs to prepare people for “a place”?

  • Department refers to its goal as one of “developing and delivering programs” to young;” This doesn’t seem to capture the role as visualized by most Islanders

  • Most literature emanating from Department focuses on school matters; the Department should focus on education.

  • I recently learned that the Department dropped the definition of ”education” when last revising the PEI Education Act. Does this mean that our focus is now on schooling rather than education?

Education carries layers of subjectivity.Conceptual clarity is im[optant and must be addressed directly so that the concept of education won’t become diluted.

Sincerely

Don Glendenning
