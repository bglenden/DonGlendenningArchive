Dated November 5, 2015, Don updates Mary McInnis, Manager of RBC Charlottetown, on Education 20/20's progress and solicits continued financial support. He notes that calls for education system change have become "more frequent, more widespread, and more urgent." While Education 20/20 doesn't claim credit for driving change, Don observes they are helping keep education in the public spotlight. He outlines recent activities: monthly bilingual newsletters, public presentations, workshops, website, media coverage, Excellence in Education Awards ($2,500 annually to innovative schools), meetings with MLAs and education officials, and the "Call for Action" document with seven recommendations. Don details future plans: a working group exploring gaps between K-12 education and postsecondary/employment readiness, and improved student achievement measurement. He requests ongoing financial support, suggesting $1,000 annually for three years, with contributions directed through the Community Foundation of PEI.


November 5, 2015

Ms. Mary McInnis, Manager

Royal Bank of Canada

83 Queen Street

Charlottetown, PEI C1A 4A8


Dear Ms. McInnis:


It is a while since we were last in touch about public education in PEI and, although you are on the mailing list for our Newsletter and announcements, I’d like to take a moment to bring you up to date on our activities – and invite your support.


There is no question that, in recent months calls for change in the education system have become more frequent, more widespread, and more urgent in their tone; while we do not take credit for the changes, at a minimum, our activities are helping to keep the issue before the public. We have yet to see changes that we proposed but hope springs eternal for real change in education, even though history tells us that proposed changes are likely to fall far short of what is required and, even more so, are likely not to deal with root causes of the education malaise.


Activities of Education 20/20 during the past few months include:

  • A monthly bilingual newsletter, public presentations and workshops, a website and some exposure in both print and social media

  • An Excellence in Education Award in the amount of $2,500 to a PEI school or early learning centre; the Award was won in 2014 by the Kinkora Regional High School and in 2015 by Prince Street Elementary School; the 2016 award has just been announced.

  • Meetings and conversations with Members of the Legislative Assembly, education officials both in Government and the English Language School Board, and other opinion makers

  • A Call for Action that identified seven steps to improving public education in PEI. (Attached)


In addition to the above, our plans for the coming year include:

  • A working group recently established to explore the gaps that our young people encounter when seeking entrance to postsecondary institutions or employment.

  • A better system for measuring student achievement, especially as it relates to basic skill levels.


We cannot do this alone; we need your help. This letter seeks your financial support for the work we do. Our costs have been minimal but real; our donor base is small but committed. $1,000 per year for the next three years would certainly give us stability in our ongoing activities; we also seeking project funds. We are pleased to be able to say that the Community Foundation of Prince Edward Island supports this educational initiative and administer our funds. Contributions should be directed to the Foundation (Community Foundation of Prince Edward Island, 119 Queen Street, Charlottetown, PE C1A 4B3) who will issue charitable receipts.

I am enclosing some information about Education 20/20 Inc. and our activities: if you require further information do not hesitate to let me know.


Sincerely



Don Glendenning

President
