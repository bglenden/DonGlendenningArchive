Restructuring called for


The education literature speaks of the need for educational reform. I agree; the first steps, I suggest, should be structural

  1. Purpose sets the stage for what we do and how we organize to do it. The PEI education literature refers to helping people find a place in society; we need to better define that “place”. Life roles have been described as that of a individual, citizen, a producer, a consumer, family member and student. All roles can benefit from education but all may not need the same mix at the same time. That’s part of what makes service complicated.

  2. Services - Education is a service. Typical education services include: information, counselling, instruction, products and technology, quality assurance. governance, funding and human resources. Services should be identified and promoted and not limited to learners in school.

  3. System – PEI has a school syseml Islanders deserve an education system and the difference needs to be made clear.

  4. Structure - ”Form follows function” is the standard when structuring any venture; one would expect, therefore, that the education structure would reflect services offered. Instead, the Department appears to be structured around clients served.

  5. Autonomy – as mentioned earlier, education is a service. Front line service providers (teachers) need both expertise and autonomy to respond to learner needs. The OECD tells us that PISA results are better for students in schools with autonomy for their day-to-day operation; the World Bank advises Ministries of Education to delegate. PEI should also delegate.

  6. Accountability - Mathew Bedard of the Montreal Economic Institute points out that “autonomy and accountability jointly applied” would cost practically nothing and allow education to better adapt to the diversity of student needs and aptitudes. Yet teachers and principals in PEI appear to have little freedom to adjust services to suit individual learner needs.

  7. Grading/Assessment – form of feedback

  8. Climate – collective mood or morale, shared perceptions of the group; how we are feeling

  9. Culture – how people feel about the organization, beliefs, values and assumptions that provide the identity and set the standards of behaviour; the way we do things; goes deeper


These structural issues are not new; no doubt readers have others to propose. All should be considered. They need to be dealt with soon, and should be dealt with in a public manner.
