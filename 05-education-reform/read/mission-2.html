MISSION

A “mission statement” is a short statement of why an organization exists, what its overall goal is, identifying the goal of its operations: what kind of product or service it provides, its primary customers or market, and its geographical region of operation. (Wikipedia) It should also include underlying principles. The purpose of a mission statement is to communicate the organisation's purpose and direction to its employees, customers, vendors, and other stakeholders. A mission statement also creates a sense of identity for its employees. Department documents identify the purpose of education on PEI as one of “helping students find a meaningful place in society”. This seems like a very narrow role as compared with sister Departments.

  • Family and Human Services – supports Islanders in need and helps them …

  • Health – “Responsible for the delivery of publicly funded Health services

  • Justice – “works to develop supports o reduce risks in the community …


The PEI Department gives its goal as being to “develop and deliver high quality programs and curriculum in English and French to Island children from birth to Grade 12.” Other Provinces describe their work as:

  • NB – “each student develop the attributes needed to be a lifelong learner, to achieve personal fulfillment, and ….””

  • Nfld – “a community that fosters quality care and learning …”

  • NS – “every child js confident and proud, maximizing their potential and contributing to a thriving society…,


COMMENT

  • Department name includes “education and lifelong learning.”; isn’t lifelong learning part of education?

  • Some definitions would be helpful

  • “Developing and delivering” is a very narrow role for the Department; moe like a role for a curriculum unit.

  • Focus on youth; who looks after the rest of us?

  • Department documents seem to support the view that getting an education and “going to school” are one and the same.

  • How about “encourage, support and regulate education and education services”
