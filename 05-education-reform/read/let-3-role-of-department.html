Dear Editor:

PEI Education Mandate


It is widely recognized that our future as individuals and as a community depends to a large measure on the nature and level of education of our citizens. In saying this, I am using education as an umbrella word and thereby including all learning experiences wherever, whenever and however initiated, carried out and paid for- not just experiences provided by or experienced in school


A check on websites shows that most Provinces describe their roles as one of helping young people prepare to function as citizens in a democracy, to gain skills, knowledge and other attributes needed for employment or to prepare young people to advance into postsecondary studies; some Provinces focus on all three. PEI, on the other hand, limits itself to “developing and delivering programs in French and English to Island Children …”. I do not know what that means but it sounds as though the Department’s interest is in simply having youngsters go to school.  


My own view is simple; the Province should:

  • Encourage all citizens to continue their education
  • Regulate the “education service” sector, and
  • Identify services and sectors deserving support.


Put simply, the future of leaving education to schools is not good enough.


Sincerely


Don Glendenning
