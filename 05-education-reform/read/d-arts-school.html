Don supports expanded arts education and proposes a specialized arts school for PEI students pursuing arts careers. He suggests a charter school model might offer greater flexibility than traditional public systems. The brief letter supports Ian Saunders' advocacy for music and extends it to arts generally, reflecting Don's belief that diverse student interests and career paths require specialized institutional options alongside general education.
Education Philosophy & Reform

August 8, 2010


Editor 

Guardian

Charlottetown, PEI


Dear Sir:


School for the Arts


I support the comments on music in the schools by Ian Saunders’ in the Guardian of August 6, 2010.  What has been said about music, also applies to the arts generally.  


In addition to strengthening arts for all students, PEI should have a school specializing in the arts for young people planning a future in the field.  The public system is a logical body to organize such a school but may not have the flexibility to support it.  A charter school has a greater chance of success.




Sincerely


Don Glendenning
