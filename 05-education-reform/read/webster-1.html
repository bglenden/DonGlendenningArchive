A letter (date approximately November 2015) from Don to the Community Foundation of PEI regarding film screening of "Most Likely to Succeed." Don reports that Education 20/20 Inc. has observed increasing calls for education system change in recent months and notes the organization's visibility in keeping education issues public, though systemic change remains elusive. He outlines Education 20/20's activities including monthly newsletters, public presentations, workshops, website, media exposure, and the Excellence in Education Award (won by Kinkora Regional High School in 2014, Prince Street Elementary in 2015). Don proposes screening "Most Likely to Succeed" on February 7, 2016, at City Cinema and seeks $775 CAD to cover screening license, rental, and shipping costs.

Most Likely to Succeed


Education 20/20 Inc. is a broad based, national, not-for-profit education organization, located in Charlottetown, PEI and is governed by a volunteer Board of Directors, people who have demonstrated an interest in and knowledge of education but are not representative of any educational constituency. Our mandate is to support and strengthen a culture of learning and to ensure a quality education for every Canadian.


There is no question that, in recent months calls for change in the education system have become more frequent, more widespread, and more urgent in their tone; while we do not take credit for the changes, at a minimum, our activities are helping to keep the issue before the public. We have yet to see changes that we proposed but hope springs eternal for real change in education, even though history tells us that proposed changes are likely to fall far short of what is required and, even more so, are likely not to deal with root causes of the education malaise.


Activities of Education 20/20 during the past few months include:

  • A monthly bilingual newsletter, public presentations and workshops, a website and some exposure in both print and social media

  • An Excellence in Education Award in the amount of $2,500 to a PEI school or early learning centre; the Award was won in 2014 by the Kinkora Regional High School and in 2015 by Prince Street Elementary School; the 2016 award has just been announced.

  • Meetings and conversations with Members of the Legislative Assembly, education officials both in Government and the English Language School Board, and other opinion makers

  • A Call for Action that identified seven steps to improving public education in PEI. (Attached)


In recent weeks both the New York Times and the Washington Post carried reviews of a new film, Most Likely to Succeed, (clippings enclosed); we believe that people in PEI would not only be interested in the film but, would benefit from the vision of education that the film promotes. We have a tentative booking at the City Cinema in Charlottetown for Sunday afternoon, February 7, 2016; the film is accompanied by discussion materials which we will use to moderate a discussion following the showing.


But there is a cost to showing the film on PEI:

  • Screening license $350 US

  • Rental of City Cinema $232 Cdn. including tax

  • Shipping $ 40


Estimated Total Cdn. $775 Cdn.


Although Education 20/20 Inc. is not a registered charity under the Canadian Revenue Agency, we are pleased to be able to say that the Community Foundation of Prince Edward Island supports this educational initiative and administer our funds. Contributions should be directed to the Foundation (Community Foundation of Prince Edward Island, 119 Queen Street, Charlottetown, PE C1A 4B3) who will issue charitable receipts.

I am enclosing some information about Education 20/20 Inc. and our activities. I would welcome an opportunity to meet, to brief you more fully on our activities with you and to discuss the screening in detail. If you require further information do not hesitate to let me know.


Sincerely
