Beliefs

No.101 June, 2014


Education 20/20 Vision

  • Emphasizes achievement not time served

  • Recognizes that the essence of a quality education is mutual respect among teachers, learners and learning stakeholders.

  • Includes meaningful assessment and feedback

  • Focuses on learning needs rather than subjects to be taught

  • Holds teachers accountable for helping learners reach education goals

  • Is outcomes based

  • Uses criterion referenced assessment

  • Supports independent learning

  • Helps learners prepare for qualifications and not just credentials

  • Operates on a evidence-based model


My Beliefs

  1. Education is the most important issue of the day; our future as individuals and as communities depend, to a large degree, on the nature and extent of our education

  2. With each significant improvement in education we reduce problems in health drugs, law enforcement, literacy, unemployment, – perhaps even reduce bullying

  3. Most people can learn skills, knowledge and attitudes needed to live and work in a modern economy.

  4. In education, achievement is the constant and time a variable

  5. Publicly funded education systems have a responsibility to help each person to develop their capabilities and to ensure that the community has the people it needs to ensure its future.

  6. The purpose of education is to help each person to develop skills needed to function better in their various life roles: individuals, family members, consumers, students, producers and citizens

  7. Learning a process of changing attitudes, skills and knowledge

  8. We are educated even if we never attend school

  9. Organized education has neither the will nor the capacity to bring about the changes required or desired by the public it serves.

  10. While governments have a major role in providing education operating schools is not one of them

  11. Using education and schooling interchangeably interferes with improvement

  12. Our current system rewards schooling not education

  13. Cost of speaking out is too high for most Islanders; cost of silence is even higher

  14. Learner centered

  15. Flexible

  16. Transparent

  17. User friendly

  18. Our rewad system Professional freedom

  19. Professional accountability

  20. Learner choice

  21. School autonomy

  22. Engagement

  23. Community focus

  24. Results oriented

  25. Criterion referenced

  26. Technology assisted

  27. Continuous flow
