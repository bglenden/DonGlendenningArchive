Letter to Charlottetown Guardian editor by Don Glendenning explaining factors that affect school quality, distinguishing between variables outside school systems control (community characteristics) and those within their control (time on task, curriculum quality, instructional methods, depth of instruction). Advocates for schools to adjust controllable variables for educational improvement.

January 25, 2014


Editor, Charlottetown Guardian

Charlottetown, PEI


Dear Sir:

Why schools differ


There are a number of reasons why the quality of education varies from system to system or from school to school.  Some arise from the characteristics of the community in which the school is situated and, therefore, are outside the control of the school system.  


Each school system has at least four variables it can play with:

Time on task 

Curriculum quality

Instructional methods 

Depth of instruction
