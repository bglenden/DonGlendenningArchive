Education 20/20 Inc. – file 3050233


This is a follow up to our application for registration as a charitable organization with Canada Revenue Agency:

  • Original application

  • Your response dated – May 30, 2013

  • Withdrawal of application, July 16, 2013

With your letter of May 30 in hand and a year of activities behind us, we are now reapplying for a charitable tax number:

  1. Enclosed is a new application; with regards to Question 11a, we are reporting activities as per objectives found in our incorporation documents and also under a revised set of objectives which we feel more accurately reflects the nature of the work that we do.



  1. During the past year, the education climate has changed in PEI with more public discussion, additional media coverage, and with issues and concerns being taken up by recognized community agencies like Chambers of Commerce. While Education 20/20 Inc. does not claim credit, it certainly has been a player in keeping the subject in the public domain.


  1. Education 20/20 Inc. contributed to the public debate on education by organizing public workshops in six Island communities. The issue was preparedness of youth for further education and for work. Education 20/20 provided experienced facilitators and a common set of questions - announcements, media releases and clippings and a copy of our report are enclosed.


  1. In recognition of the linkage between school achievement and community engagement, Education 20/20 initiated an education award with the first award being made to Kinkora Regional High School as part of their graduation exercises on June 20, 2014 – announcements and clippings enclosed



















  1. Education 20/20 Inc. organized a number of workshops for educators and others with educational interests or activities – see attached documentation



  1. In recognition of our expertise, Education 20/20 Inc. has been invited to make a presentation to the PEI Legislative Committee on Learning and Technology – letter attached; we have also been given a spot on the agenda of the English Language School Board for their September meeting.



  1. In addition to continuing, and improving on last year’s activities, two new initiatives are underway:

  • A series of occasional papers on educational topics; this initiative serves two purposes, first to inform readers about issues and developments affecting education and, second, to encourage Islanders to write as a means of continuing their own personal and professional development.

  • Whereas our workshop series was intended primarily to engage parents and the public at large both developing and expressing their views on education, this year we plan to engage educators in discussions about such educational issues as standards, credentials challenge for credit etc.



  1. An initiative to improve the math skills of Island youth under way. The first step is to identify a common set of outcomes or competencies and defining levels of achievement.



  1. As requested, I am enclosing a package of support documents.



I trust that the above will be helpful in the review process; if you need additional information please let me know. We also welcome any suggestions for changes in our incorporation documents to more accurately reflect our intent and your requirements.

Sincerely,



Don Glendenning

President
