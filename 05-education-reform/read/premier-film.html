Dated January 4, 2016, Don invites Premier Wade MacLauchlan to a screening of "Most Likely to Succeed," a documentary produced by education philanthropist Ted Dintersmith. The film premiered in the United States and received positive reviews from both the New York Times and Washington Post. Don describes the film's focus on project-based learning at High Tech High in San Diego and its argument for emphasizing relational skills over pure content delivery. The screening is scheduled for Sunday, January 17, 2016, from 2-4:00 pm at The City Cinema, Charlottetown. Don offers to provide two complimentary tickets at the door and extends the invitation as "our guest," suggesting this was part of Education 20/20's ongoing advocacy strategy. The letter demonstrates Don's commitment to introducing innovative educational approaches to decision-makers.




January 4, 2016

Premier Wade MacLauchlan

PO Box 2000

Charlottetown, PE

C1A 7N8


Dear Premier:


As you know, our group has been promoting changes in education. Recently, both the New York Times and Washington Post carried accounts of a new film about education being unrolled across the US; both papers gave the film a high rating.


The film, Most Likely to Succeed, was produced by education philanthropist, Ted Dintersmith, and shows a project based approach to education being followed by a ninth grade class at High Tech High in San Diego, California. It argues for less focus on content and more emphasis on relational skills with students given more latitude in what, when and how they learn.


The film will be shown at The City Cinema, 64 King Street, Charlottetown on Sunday, January 17, 2016 from 2-4:00 pm. We would welcome you as our guest (2 tickets at door) to be in the audience to view the film and participate in the discussion to follow.


Wishing you the compliments of the season and looking forward to seeing you January 17.


Sincerely




Don Glendenning

President
