Policy description addressing public concerns about education quality with emphasis on standards-setting conflict. Documents that public views education system as flawed due to educator-controlled standard setting (unlike other industries where external bodies establish standards), large-scale assessment results, economic competency concerns, system quality changes (grade inflation, social promotion, report card confusion), awareness of alternative approaches, teaching-grading function conflicts, and technology-aided instruction impact. Identifies nearly 2,000 PEI achievement evaluators creating inconsistency. Distinguishes between grade (age-based administrative grouping) and achievement (learning level), emphasizing confusion from dual-meaning usage. Reviews best practices (apprenticeships, International Baccalaureate, music/swimming, mathematical competitions, credential systems, language levels, qualification authorities). Specifies needed qualification authority characteristics including independence, bias reduction, consistency improvement, workload reduction, and conflict elimination.
