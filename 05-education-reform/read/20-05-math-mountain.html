Detailed proposal for "Math Mountain" (alternative name for Math Ladder) to help more students reach higher mathematics levels. Identifies school system deficiencies: ranking students over assessing levels, conflict between teaching and ranking roles, lack of identified math levels, rejection of difficulty-based grouping, and transcripts that don't identify achievement levels. Proposes undertaking competency/outcomes analysis, establishing levels (similar to music/skating ratings), developing rating scales and benchmarks, and creating independent math testing service. Service would be individual-based, voluntary, self-supporting, fee-for-service, and open to any age without school credit. Elements include information/advice, assessment, instruction, tutoring, clubs, and competitions. Includes conditions for participation, pilot project ideas, and funding approaches involving fundraising, Canadian Math Society, and local sponsors. Proposals


Math Ladder


Purpose: Create a pathway to help more students reach higher levels in mathematics


Why Math?

  1. Needed for day-to-day living and is often a prerequisite for accessing many opportunities in later life

  2. Has an identifiable structure namely numeracy, elementary mathematics, algebra, geometry, trigonometry, calculus, set and function, complex numbers, logarithmic functions, and infinite series

  3. Lacks a widely available and accepted analysis of outcomes or competencies

  4. Lack widely accepted agreement on levels


School system deficiencies:

  1. Schools favour system of ranking students over assessing levels

  2. Teaching and ranking constitute a conflict of roles

  3. Lacks system of identified math levels

  4. System rejects grouping by level of difficulty

  5. Transcripts do not identify levels


Proposal:

  1. Undertake an analysis:

  • Undertake a comprehensive analysis of outcomes/competencies

  • Provincial may offer other courses as well but they must have different identity

  • Similar to establishing levels in music and skating

  1. Establish levels

  • Levels –Similar to establishing levels in music and skating

  • Rating scale

  • Benchmarks


  1. Math Testing Service

  • Individual based

  • Voluntary

  • Self supporting

  • Fee for service basis

  • Independent

  • Open to individuals of any age

  • By request

  • Does not offer credit

  • Test takers will be informed of level attained

  • On request by test taker, a record of student level may be forwarded to employer or to school


Advantages/benefits:

  • Common standard

  • Aid mobility

  • Much of groundwork already done

  • Reduce curriculum development workload





Project:

  • Establish committee

  • Undertake analysis

  • Establish levels

  • National consultation

  • Distribute widely

Steps:

  • Identify math competencies

  • Identify qualification levels

  • Find or develop assessment instruments and procedures

  • Design qualification system

  • No structured courses

  • Games can motivate students to improve skills that are usually learned by rote.
