Proposal for facilitated training in the Handle with Care program, an evidence-based mental health and social-emotional wellbeing initiative for early childhood practitioners and parents. Education 20/20 Inc. proposed training qualified facilitators in PEI and the Maritime provinces, building on successful pilot results, with certification from the Hincks Dellcrest Centre.


Prog/HWC/1-C

Handle with Care ----Training for Facilitators


We would all agree that social and emotional wellbeing is a major to a healthy, happy life; one that enables us to to reach our potential and contribute to the greater good of every person or situation we encounter. Many children learn about social and emotional wellness through their life experiences. Unfortunately this is not the case for a growing number of others. Some children need a helping hand in order to gain a healthy self –concept, self- confident and self- worth. What we know is that every parent wants what is best for their children so there is no better place to start to promote this well-being(Mental Health) than with parents; the child’s first and most important teacher.


Handle with Care is a program designed to help parents and other caregivers promote the mental health of young children every day! It is useful for everyone who cares for or works with children; especially parents, grandparents and teachers, and consists of sets of interactive strategies that build on the strengths of adults in their lives. (Brochure available)

The Handle with Care Project began in 2002 as a project of the Canadian Mental Health Association and Hincks Dellcrest Centre in Toronto; with Phase 1 provided a summary of recent literature and a bilingual resource booklet for child care practitioners. Phase 2 involved a national initiative to train childcare practitioners while Phase 3 was a controlled study in three Ontario communities. Finally, Phase 4 was a national pilot project, with funding from the Public Health Agency of Canada and involved 439 participants in four sites across Canada namely Yukon, Manitoba, Bruce County in Ontario and PEI. Alice Taylor was HWC team leader for the work in PEI. The results were extremely positive and the program now is being rolled out across the country. A copy of the evaluation is available as a separate document.


The program is based on four building blocks: Trust and Attachment, Building and Enhancing Self- Esteem, Expressing Emotions and Building Relationships with Others. Each block builds upon the one before and contains the following components: Key message, Research, Activities, Bringing it Home and Bringing it together, Other resources and a Summary Handout. The key to a successful program is trained facilitators. We are fortunate, indeed, on PEI to have two trained and experienced people, Alice Taylor and Eileen Conway Martin, approved by the Hincks Dellcrest Centre, to deliver this program to facilitators and parents.


We, in Education 20/20 Inc. believe that Handle With Care is an important contributor to education and, furthermore, expect it, or an equivalent, to become a component, not only in the training of teachers and leaders in Early Childhood Education but for all parents and would be parents on PEI. We expect that its growth will include incorporation into leadership training programs for most youth groups and funding will flow from a number of departments, including Education, Health, Child and Family Services, and Justice , to name a few. To prepare for its wider adoption, Education 20/20 Inc. proposes to start now with the training of facilitators, first in PEI and second for our sister Maritime Provinces.


Facilitator training, under the Handle With Care program will be led by two experienced trainers, in a process of 16 hours spread over two days. Participants in the program must be at least 18 years of age, have formal early childhood, teaching and/or social work education and experience. Experience dealing with children and families, personally and professionally is a great asset.


Participants will be limited to 18 in any single group and are expected to pay such out of pocket expenses as accommodation, travel, meals and a Facilitator’s Handbook ($80.00). On successful completion, participants will be recognized with a Certificate by Education 20/20 Inc. and one from Hincks Dellcrest as trained HWC facilitators.
