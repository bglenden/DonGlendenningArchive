Definitions are useful in any field; they help us to think through and communicate more clearly, to avoid misunderstanding especially when the subject itself carries layers of subjectivity.


The education literature describes education as follows:

  • Good - the aggregate of all the processes by means of which a person develops abilities, attitudes and other forms of behaviour of positive value in the society in which he lives

  • Good – the social process by which people are subjected to the influence of a selected and controlled environment so that they may attain social competence and optimum individual development

  • OECD – organic and sustained communication to bring about learning

  • UNESCO – a set of practices and outcomes that are defined qualitatively

  • Anon - any experience that has a formative effect on the way a person thinks feels or acts.

  • Ontario – both the act of teaching knowledge to others and the act of receiving knowledge from someone else.

  • World Bank – while I did not encounter a definition in my reading, World Bank documents warns countries that “going to school and getting an education” are not the same.

Education is complex. Learners have different interests, experiences, capabilities and needs. Learners also fill a variety of life roles. Those who teach, or otherwise serve learners, must be very clear in their communication. Conceptual clarity heightens our understanding of key words and concepts; lack of clarity should be addressed directly so that the concept of education won’t become diluted and potentially ineffective. Lack of clarity may arise from an idea itself, from the person promoting the idea or from the person receiving the idea; Lack of clarity could also signal conflicting ideas and level of discomfort or disagreement from within the group itself; some say the willingness to define is related to self confidence.


Education is multifaceted; it can happen any place and at any time – when one is alone as well as when people gather together. lt may be as simple as a bit of information or as complex as understanding the laws of the universe.

• Learning is about change by a person in what they know, believe, value or are able to do.

• Teaching is about arranging experiences for others. experiences that change what they know, believe, value or are able to do.

• One’s level of education is a judgement call

• One’s education is the result of experiences that have had a measurable effect upon what one believes, knows, feels, and able to do.

• Well educated means that the person can deal satisfactorily with most issues that arise in everyday life.
