Newsletter critiquing PEI's Department of Education as primarily serving public instruction rather than broader education goals. Don argues for expanded departmental support including tutoring, online courses, qualifications frameworks, part-time learning opportunities, and educational support for disenfranchised youth, proposing the department delegate school operations to focus on systemic education leadership.

Open Education

No. 30 July 18, 2015


To provide a quality education we need a quality education system - Unknown


Let’s call a spade a spade


Our Department of Education and Early Childhood Development is a bit of a sham; it is really only a Department of Public Instruction. By and large, it shows little interest except in schools and, whilen schools are a part if education, they are far from being all thee is to education..


We all could benefit from more or better education; we need it everywhere in our lives, at home, at work, for our leisure hours, for better community service and in our relationships with others. A true Department of Education would support range of services including:

  • Instruction in schools

  • Independent study through tutoring, access to on-line courses, self instructional materials and study centres.

  • Help for people wanting to obtain or upgrade qualifications. Credentials are fast losing their usefulness; Canada is one of the few countries in the world without a formal qualifications framework

  • Support and encourage study on a part time basis.

  • Educational opportunities for disenfranchised youth.

  • Access and advice to people making educational choices/ decisions.Schools have guidance counsellors but little help is availableoutside school for individuals, including parents who are involved in making decisions about their or their children’s education


As it now stands, Departments do the easy things; they focus on providing instruction to the young; I’d like to see the Department delegate the function of public instruction in favour of leading an education initiative.
