Expectations of school

The role and expectations of public schools are changing and the changes are causing some confusion. Most people agree that the role of schools is to help students to “develop their full potential”, however, full potential is seldom defined.


Although schools offer a wide range of educational experiences, the emphasis is mostly on the acquisition of knowledge, especially the kind of knowledge associated with further study, and even more especially the kind of knowledge favoured by universities. Evidence of this focus includes the emphasis on numbers of scholarships received, number of acceptances into university programs, lack of reference to outcomes on report cards, to name the most obvious ones.


Last winter, Education 20/20 conducted a series of workshops throughout Prince Edward Island. Listening to the comments of participants, it became clear to me that Islanders want schools to do more than develop knowledge; a recent article summarized it succinctly when it described the role of schools as that of helping young people prepare for “work, further education and citizenship”.


There’s need for a brief, clear statement as to the role of schools; Something like the following would go a long way towards satisfying workshop participants last winter.


The role of a school is to

help each student to

develop skills, knowledge and attitudes

required to

cope with, enjoy and contribute to a full life.”
