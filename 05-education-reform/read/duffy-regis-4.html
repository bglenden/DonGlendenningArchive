In this December 28, 2015 reflection, Don provides a status update on Education 20/20's progress and personal introspection. Despite age-related health concerns, Don reports that Education 20/20 has sustained debate on education, engaged with elected officials, and gained support from many decision-makers, though actual policy announcements remain disappointing. He expresses frustration that knowing what needs to be done is not the problem—implementation is. Education 20/20's 2016 strategy includes continuing the bilingual newsletter, maintaining the school awards, focusing on basic skills content and standards, and amplifying advocacy efforts. This letter demonstrates Don's perseverance despite limited tangible policy victories and his sustained commitment even as he ages.

Education 20/20 Inc. Award

It is year end and for me, it is a time for stocktaking and reflection. It’s been a good year personally; I’m still here and, although well up in my senior years, still reasonably healthy.


As for Education 20/20, we’ve helped keep the debate alive, we’ve met with MLA’s, Ministers, and senior officials and have reason to believe that many are supporting the changes we propose, however, actual announcements continue to be disappointing. Our Call for Action, copy attached, is as good a summary of needed changes as any I have seen across the country. The problem is not a matter of knowing what to do or not to do; the problem is one of failure to act.


Our strategy for the coming year includes continuing our bilingual monthly newsletter and school award, focusing more directly on the issue of basic skills in terms of both content and level, keeping in touch with decision-makers, and speaking in a louder voice.


On behalf of Education 20/20, I thank you for both your encouragement and financial support.


Warm personal regards.




Don Glendenning
