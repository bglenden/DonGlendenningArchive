Typical Education Services

4-11-8



Education is a service with a focus on creating circumstances or experiences that will help people learn; surprisingly, the literature seldom mentions them. The literature suggests the following typical forms of help:

  • Information – recent, accurate and independent information needed by Islanders in the making of decisions about their own education or the education of others

  • Counselling – including diagnosis and assessment

  • An organization with vision and responsibility to put in place and monitor educational services

  • Instruction -help that includes diagnosis, counselling, presentations, practice, conversation, questioning , modelling,

  • Quality Assurance – to assure students their learning experiences meet community standards.

  • Learning products – books, manuals, MOOCs, models, exercises, games and others.

  • Technology - access to learning resources

  • Funding - Funding to a level and in a manner that is equitable for all Islanders.

  • Specialists - Teachers, administrators, counsellors, advisors and technical specialists as needed.

  • Data – designed to inform about learner progress.


The above services should be available as part if an education system; unfortunately they are integrated into the school system and, therefore, lost to most Islanders.
