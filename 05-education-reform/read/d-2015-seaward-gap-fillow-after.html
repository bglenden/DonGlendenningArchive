Bridging the Preparedness Gap

The Community Foundation of Prince Edward Island, in a survey of young Islanders in late 2013, found “preparedness gaps with regard to readiness for post- secondary study and the workplace” (Vital Signs, CFPEI, 2013). Gaps may result from students dropping out before they graduate, not taking or not passing required courses, lack of information about entrance requirements or changing requirements by postsecondary institutions. These findings were explored in six communities in workshops organized across the Island.

One source of help is the Adult Basic Education program offered by Holland College. The program, funded through the Department of Workforce Development and Higher Learning, is offered on a full and part-time basis in eight centres across the Island for who people lack high school graduation credits, want to upgrade existing school marks or, for people who are not already high school graduates, to obtain the PEI High School Graduation Certificate for Mature Students.

The public can learn more about the Holland College program at a presentation at the Stratford Town Hall on Wednesday, September 30 at 4:30 pm. The presentation will be led by Gerry Seaward, a long time adult educator and currently Program Manager of Adult and Community Education, Prince of Wales Campus, Holland College. Students with an interest in bridging such a gap in their own lives are especially welcome.
