Education spectrum (1)


Every once in a while in both our personal and professional lives we need to stop and take stock of what we are doing and, even more important, where we are going. Different people call it different things, as I write I’m thinking of it as “ visioning”. Let’s start by envisioning an ideal education for someone we know, perhaps our child, a grandchild or even a neighbourhood youngster. In fact it may be helpful to consider two or three youngsters at the same time but not, I would say as a group.


My own thoughts race ahead as I write; I pause to formulate a definition and decide that educating is “helping another person learn a variety of things that will help him or her in life” – one teaches math but one educates for life. Educating is helping people change behaviour, sometimes it is new information, sometimes it’s a new skill and sometimes it involves changing of attitudes.


That the education process starts in the home is well understood and is backed up by research. So our education services should include providing help in the home and for the child not yet of school age; that’s tricky because each home is different, but so is each child. The help needed will depend on both the youngster and the home and may come in the form of home visits, sessions for parents or parents and children, books or other resources, or just old fashioned tips that work. It doesn’t literally have to be in the home, it nay e a group process in the local child care facility or even for parents over coffee in someone’s home.
