December 25, 2015 fundraising letter from Don Glendenning to John Robinson requesting $100 sponsorship for "Most Likely to Succeed," another education documentary. References positive reviews in New York Times and Washington Post. Film features High Tech High in San Diego using project-based learning with student autonomy and trust. Don explains film shows alternative model where success is not measured by standardized tests and relational skills receive emphasis. Requests donation through Community Foundation of Prince Edward Island pending Education 20/20's charity status. Shows Don's pattern of securing multiple film screenings to advance education reform agenda.



December 25, 2015


Mr. John Robinson

Paoli’s Wharf

Charlottetown. PEI



Dear John:


As you know, our group has been promoting changes in education. Recently, both the New York Times and Washington Post carried accounts of a new film on education being unrolled across the US; both papers gave the film a high rating. In the words of the one reviewer:


“The film is a bold indictment of the entire K-12 educational system.”

- New York Times, October 16, 2015


The film, Most Likely to Succeed, was produced by education philanthropist, Ted Dintersmith, and shows a project based approach to education being followed at High Tech High in San Diego, California where, in the words of one release “content is taken off center stage, success is not measured by standardized tests, relational skills are emphasized, project based learning is key, students and teachers are given autonomy and trust, and students and teachers are allowed to find their own approach to learning.”


The film will be shown at The City Cinema, 64 King Street, Charlottetown on Sunday, January 17 from 2-4:00 pm. We’d like you to be in the audience to view the film and participate in the discussion to follow. Although we plan to charge an entrance fee of $5.00, there will still be a shortfall of several hundred dollars. This letter comes as a request for some financial assistance, say $100, to help offset that shortfall.


Our application to Revenue Canada is still pending, therefore, your cheque should be made out to the Community Foundation of Prince Edward Island, Suite 105, Queen Square Place, 119-121 Queen St., Charlottetown, C1A 4B3.


I thank you for your support and look forward to seeing you at the film.


Sincerely




Don Glendenning

President
