Dear Editor:

How Well Should a Graduate be Able to Read?


Over the past few months, people have engaged me in conversations (it isn't hard to do) about reading.

Parents, and grandparents, wonder why they should have to pay the cost of private tutoring; most,

however, complain about the low level of reading of recent graduates. ln fairness, I also hear positive

comments about improvements in reading instruction at the elementary level.


While reading is not my specialty, I browsed some literature and the internet to see how reading is

measured. I did not find a simple description but did find that the ability to read is usually described in

terms of fluency, range of material, comprehension, independence, speed, vocabulary and flow. What I

was really looking for was a simple statement to describe the level of reading expected of a high school

graduate. I didn't find one, however, so I created the following:


ln order to obtain o high school graduation diploma, a person must demonstrate the ability to

read range of printed materials with independence, fluency and ease


Having such a description would, I believe, clarify both expectations and communication among

stakeholders. I am sure that there are better descriptions and would welcome yours at

standards2020@gmail.com


Sincerely




Don Glendenning
