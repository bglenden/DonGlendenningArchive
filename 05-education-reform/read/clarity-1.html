Dear Editor:

A Need for Clarity

A recent Conversation, a daily independent online publication out of Toronto, carried an article on reforming public education thereby sparking this letter.

Public education has a clarity problem. In the interest of being inclusive, those who develop policy for education seem to overlook the need for specificity. For example:

  1. The PEI Administrative Handbook gives the purpose of education as being “to provide for the development of children so that each child may take a meaningful place in society”. Shouldn’the mandate include adults?

  2. Elsewhere, the PEI literature speaks of helping students find a “a meaningful place”. While I understand the intention of the statement, I find it insufficiently clear as a basis to design programs or measure results.

  3. The PEI education website refers to the role of the Department being “to develop and deliver high quality programs and curriculum in English and French to Island children”. I think this to be a major understatement of the Department’s role;

  4. Government departments usually organize around functions. Our Department seems to have organized around ages and levels.

  5. And then I learned recently that the Department no longer includes a definition of “education”; it has been replaced by “school”. This probably explains why we have a school system rather than an education system.

  1. Education is important; the nature and quality of our futures depend on it. The Department is an important player. I’d like to see the Department develop a full-service education system for Islanders of all ages.

Sincerely

Don Glendenning

October 17, 2024
