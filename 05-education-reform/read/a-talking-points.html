INTRODUCTION

It is widely recognized that education is a key factor in determining each citizen’s quality of life; in the same way, the collective education of our citizens will determine to a significant degree the quality of the communities in which we live. Government support is justified for two reasons, one to support personal growth and development for every citizen; the other is to ensure the common good. Educating is a complex service made so by the fact that each person is unique in many ways, unique in basic physical, mental, emotional make-up; unique in the range and nature of previous experience, unique in perceived futures, but also unique in terms of learning pace, style and needs.


The decision to draft this paper arises from discussion with a variety of stakeholders and the hope of adding clarity to our education endeavors, I’ve adopted the view of Oregon Education that each person fills six life roles – individual, family member, student, citizen, consumer and producer. Education can support support students for whichever role they choose.

EDUCATION

Education has several meanings:

  1. Services put in place by a community to encourage and support the education of its citizens

  2. A process of arranging experiences which result in others learning knowledge, skills, attitudes and other attributes.

  3. The state or level of a person’s ability to deal with daily living

  4. A sector of the economy.


Key terms:

  • Learner – a person who, for whatever reason, needs, wants or is trying to bring about change in his/her life – trying to change an attitude, gain new knowledge, or add skills. But learners are neither empty jars nor blank slates; each learner carries three pieces of luggage to every new learning situation (1) certain physical, mental and emotional conditions with which a learner is born, (b) prior learning resulting from home circumstances, formal study, travel, state of health, personal interests, culture in which learner operates and (3) aspirations and imagined future including where one will live, the work one will do and the lifestyle one will have, nature of the family, quality of life, etc. . Learners have different interests, experiences, capabilities and needs.

Learners also fill a variety of life roles, that of an individual, family member, student, citizen, consumer and producer

  • Learning- a process of engaging in experiences that have a formative effect on the way we think, feel and act. Learning is multidimensional; it can happen any place and any time, when one is alone as well as when people gather together, it may be satisfied by something as simple as a bit of information or as complex as understanding the laws of the universe. Although only the learner can learn, learning may occur in group setting

  • Education - one’s education is the result of experiences that have had a measurable effect on their lives state or level of development of a person’s thoughts, actions and feelings

  • Education System- a set of services put in place by a community to encourage and support the development of its people

  • Teaching - arranging experiences for others resulting in changes to what they know, believe, feel and are able to do.

  • School- a resource rich facility designed to support the education process

  • School system - schools in a district reporting to a single senior executive member

  • Educated means that friends believe that the person can deal satisfactorily with most issues that arise in their everyday lives.

AN EDUCATION SYSTEM

An education system is a set of services put in place by a community to encourage and support the education of its citizens. Given the numbers served and the range of experiences, capabilities and interests of learners, designing and operating an education system is no easy ask. It is recognized that education can take place most anywhere and at any time. In fact, the home is recognized as one of the greatest influences on a person’s education followed by the contribution of teachers. Educcation offerings seem to all into two categoriies (1) traditional subjectsoffered in schools snd (2) competencies reflected n changing life styles.

  • Choice

Functions or Services

Helping people improve their life roles usually involves the provision of:


Education is a service; a service is further defined as:

• an act of help or assistance

• an organized system of labour and material aids to supply the needs of the public

• The supply, installation or maintenance of goods carried out by a dealer

• The state of availability for use by the public

• Functions performed by a person or organization

Learners may need ant and all services at any point in time



Typical education services include:

• Information and Counselling - Recent and independent information needed by Islanders in the making of decisions about their own education or the education of others

• Governance and Leadership - in the form of a body and organization with vision and re-possibility to put in place and monitor educational services for the benefit of its citizens,

• Instruction - The provision of help to others in their learning that includes diagnosis, counselling, presentations, practice and access generally to resources

• Quality Assurance - A system that assures Islanders that their learning experiences, in-clouding providers of those services, meet community standards.

• Products and Technology - Access by Islanders to educational materials, technology and other resources to help learners develop skills needed to meet their responsibilities and objectives.

• Funding - Funding to a level and in a manner that is equitable for all Islanders.

• Human Resources - Teachers, administrators, counsellors, advisors. Assistants and other people who help other people learn


The above are also the chief functions of schools

Learners may need any or all services at any point in time.

PRIORIY ISSUES:

  1. Terminology- change focus from schools to education

  2. Purpose – the literature refers to helping people find a place in society; that place is a role and should be named or described – a citizen, a producer, a consumer or? Both content and assessment demand it.

  3. Services - Education is a service, typical education services include: information, counselling, instruction, products and technology, quality assurance. governance, funding and human resources. Learners may need one or all services at any point in their education journey and services should be front and center in our offerings. Services need to be clearly identified and available to all Island citizens not only to citizens enrolled in schools and programs. Whether or not all services are paid for by government is a separate issue.

  4. Structure -” Form follows function” is still the standard when structuring any venture; one would expect, therefore, that the education structure would reflect services provided. Instead, the Department appears to be structured around clients served.

  5. Autonomy – as mentioned earlier, education is a service. Front line service providers (our teachers) need both the expertise and autonomy to respond to learner needs.

  6. Accountability - The OECD tells us that PISA results are better for students in schools with autonomy for their day-to-day operation; the World Bank advises Ministries of Education to delegate. Mathew Bedard of the Montreal Economic Institute points out that “autonomy and accountability jointly applied” would cost practically nothing and allow education to better adapt to the diversity of student needs and aptitudes. Yet teachers and principals in PEI appear to have little freedom to tailor services to suit individual learner needs.

  7. In-service training for teachers

  8. Grades and grading, represents an attempt to apply standardized measurement to levels of achievement on the education journey. Grades also serve as a communication device between a provider and user of a service. The technology allows us to record and track student progress. Most developed countries have established qualification bodies to deal with the rationalization of credentials and qualifications; Canada should consider the same.

  9. Need for a PEI Education Association


These structural issues are not new; no doubt readers have others to propose. All should be considered. They need to be dealt with soon, and should be dealt with in a very public manner.
