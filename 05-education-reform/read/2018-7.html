Activity Update- 2018-7


  1. Welcome- to our newest Director, Sharon Cameron, a former teacher, former Deputy Minister of Workforce Development; currently Special Advisor on Social Policy in the Privy Council Office.

  2. Newsletter

  • Newsletter No. 66, published July 19, 1918

  • As you know, I try to distribute occasional items between newsletter mailings; unfortunately, most are from the US. There’s definitely a shortage of public information in Canadian media about leading edge education events.

  • Newsletter No. 67 now drafted, expect it shortly for review; ; working with Bill to streamline “Canada Helps” to enable Education 20/20 us to solicit donations and post more items.

  1. STEAM Project

  • Amber: “We presented 5 Grade 2 magformers workshops and 1 Grade 6 coding and Makey Makey workshop in June. The remaining 4 Grade 6 workshops and 5 Grade 9 workshops will be scheduled for September. We had an overwhelming expression of interest from teachers, many more than 5 classes per grade looking for the workshops; however, scheduling with end of year exams and activities was tricky. AtlanticWISE has given us until mid-October to complete the project. “

  1. Education Award - proposal for 2019 Award will be circulated shortly.

Attached is a draft handout to form basis of my views. Also sending a note to the Premier offering an update on our activities.

  5. Contacts – Had a conversations about “Essential Skills”: with Richard Gallant SkillsPEI : had a separate conversation with Lori Johnston and Mark Douglas of Workplace Training on the same subject. Didn’t realize how narrow the concept is or the extent to which “essential skills” is trademarked.

  6. Handouts – preparing graphs to be used as handouts for discussion, especially for expected discussions with political parties. (See attached on Educational Services” Will keep you informed.

  7. Public presentations – would welcome invitations to speak on the matter of education.
