About Reform-1

_____________________________________________________________________________________

Education is a Provincial matter. The role of a Province is to encourage, support and regulate the practice of education within its jurisdiction. There is a significant gap, however, between community expectations and education services actually delivered: this paper includes some suggestions for closing the gap.


DEFINITIONS

  • Belief – a principle, proposition or idea accepted as true

  • Educate – to broaden the range and increase levels of one’s knowledge, skills, attitudes and values.

  • Education - any experience that has a formative effect on the way a person thinks feels or acts.

  • Education system – services put in place by a community o encouragr and support the education of its people


UNDERLYING BELIEFS:

  • Citizens are responsible for their own education.

  • Role of government is to encourage, support, and regulate educational services

  • Grading is not an instructional function .

  • Learners need help in managing their learning

  • Groups do not learn although individuals may learn in a group setting

  • Time is a variable

  • Learning is a personal matter

  • Institutionalization of services


TYPICAL EDUCATIONAL SERVICES

  • Governance

  • Leadership

  • Instruction

  • Quality assurance

  • Information

  • Counselling

  • Human resources

  • Learning products and

  • Funding








ISSUES

  1. Focus cognitive studies provided to young people in schoool

  2. Underlying principles and beliefs not spelled out

  3. Service gaps in terms of geography, age and relevance,

  4. Organizational structure does not reflect functions perfprmed

  5. Autonomy for day to day operation lacking at school level

  6. Accountability for student progress not clear

  7. Services not triggered by learner need

  8. Staffing model credential based

  9. Institutionalization of services



ACTION/RECOMMENDATIONS/:

  1. Adopt a glossary of common terms

  2. Adopt dynamic rating scale

  3. Adopt competencies as a basis for programming

  4. Track student progress by competency and level

  5. Create a learning management function

  6. Develop and promote foundational principles

  7. Make grading a function independent of education

  8. Government limit its direct role to that leadership (encouragement, support and regulation)

  9. Define accountabilities

  10. Make model for teacher education a duplicate of that used throughout the system

  11. Adopt a standard curriculum platform
