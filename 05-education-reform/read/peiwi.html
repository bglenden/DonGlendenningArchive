Dated September 30, 2013, Don introduces Education 20/20 to Cynthia Mitchell, President of the PEI Women's Institute. He acknowledges his personal connection to the organization through his mother's active membership in the rural Women's Institute of his childhood. Don frames education as "the most important issue of the day" and expresses Education 20/20's belief that public and parental involvement are essential to achieving necessary educational change. He proposes a meeting to share activities, exchange views on educational issues, and explore collaboration opportunities. The letter demonstrates Don's respect for established community institutions and his ability to leverage personal history to build relationships. He offers to meet in late October or November at a mutually convenient time.
Education Philosophy & Reform



September 30, 2013



Cynthia Mitchell, President,

PEI Women’ institute

40 Enman Crescent, Room 105

Charlottetown PEI C1E 1E6.



Dear Ms. Mitchell:


By way of introduction, Education 20/20 Inc. is a new organization dedicated to improving the quality of education for all Canadians. We are of the view that education is important, in fact, it is the most important issue of the day, but changes are needed to ensure that the education we provide reflects tomorrow’s need. Furthermore, we believe that there is an important role for parents and the public in helping bring about desired changes.


My Mother was an active member of the Women’s Institute in the farming community where I grew up. I am aware therefore, of the work of Women’s institutes, especially in rural communities but also in the Province as a whole. Because of your role in building communities and because of the importance of education to that role, we would be interested in meeting with you and your Board, to bring you up to date on our activities, to exchange views on issues to be addressed, and even to explore ways in which we could work together ensuring that Islanders receive the education they need, and deserve.


I am enclosing a copy of our Vision statement and also of our latest newsletter; we have a website in the making but it is not yet operational. If you are interested in meeting, please let me know and we will arrange a mutually convenient time, say sometime in late October or November.


Looking forward to meeting with you


Sincerely



Don Glendenning

President
