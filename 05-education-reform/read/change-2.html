REAL CHANGE


Of course education has changed and will continue to change; we have modern facilities, rooms full of computers, a low student-teacher ratio, on line courses, teachers with advanced degrees and even embedded professional development days. Important as all they are, they have not brought the kind of changes we hoped for nor are they likely to do so. If anything, they may lock us more firmly into the our current system.


So what would real change look like?


Autonomy at the School level

As far as I am concerned, the evidence is in. each school or family of schools, should be given autonomy to make decisions about each child in their care including selection of teachers

and referral to specialists, allocation of relevant funds, and accountability for the achievement of each learner in their care.


External Qualification Authority

I’ve long held the view that schools, colleges and universities should convert their credentials to qualifications; I do not think that any more. Teaching means helping others learn; the awarding of credentials involves a sorting, should not be delivered by the same person nor institution. In fact, sorting is not an education function at all. It is not by accident that sports activities separate the coaching and referring functions.


Accountability

I am a believer in personal accountability and, as some may know, during my tenure at Holland College there were no decision-making committees, except at Board level. In the school system, however, it is quite the contrary. It is difficult to identify a person who can make a decision; not surprising, it is also difficult to get an answer.


Educators may be of the view that, as professionals, they do not need position descriptions; I disagree.
