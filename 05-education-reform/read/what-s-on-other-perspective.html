September 2016 announcement detailing Education 20/20's programs and community engagement activities. Highlights include "Literacy for the 21st Century" presentation comparing PEI adults' literacy to Canadian standards, the film screening of "Beyond Measure," an annual education award, social-emotional learning presentation, occasional papers on education policy, community projects, and ongoing dialogue about mathematics education gaps. The document solicits volunteers and financial support, indicating Education 20/20's grassroots community-building approach combining events, publications, and donor engagement to advance education reform on PEI.

Events and Activities

September 1, 2016



Open Education

A monthly bilingual electronic newsletter in both official languages and including both news of upcoming events and an opinion piece on an educational matter of current interest.


Literacy for the 21st Century

Wednesday, September 14, 2016, 9:00 a.m. Southport Room, Stratford Town Hall

A presentation by Barbara MacNutt, Manager, Literacy/Essential/ Skills, Department of Workforce and Advanced Learning on where PEI adults stand compared to the rest of Canada, and, in particular, compared to the other Atlantic Provinces. The presentation will include discussion about availability of literacy training, what more needs to be done, and, generally, why literacy is important to the future success of PEI.


Beyond Measure

City Cinema, Charlottetown, Sunday, October 16, 2016, 2:00 p.m.

A film based on a book of the same name by Margaret Heffernan; the film addresses the issue of an “overscheduled, over tested and underestimated generation.


Education Award

An annual award to a community organization in support of education; check our website for details



Social and Emotional well-being

A presentation, planned for October, on the importance of social & emotional learning, and its links to well-being. TBA


Learning Journey

A Powerpoint presentation to providing a framework for people making decisions about matters of education




Occasional papers

A monthly release to our network of articles and papers on current thinking and practice in education


Community projects

A new initiative in which we work with a donor and a community group in support of a local education project.


Math

Ongoing discussions with employers and institutions about the gap in mathematics between level of math demonstrated for high school completion and level of math required for citizenship.


We’d like to hear from you:

We’d like to hear your ideas about ways to improve the quality of education in PEI; send an Email, give us a call, invite us to speak to your group or talk with one of our Directors.
