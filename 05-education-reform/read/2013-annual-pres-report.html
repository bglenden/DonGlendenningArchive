Don Glendenning's President's Report for the year ending December 31, 2013, highlighting Education 20/20 Inc.'s expanding influence and activities. The organization continued broad consultation with stakeholders across PEI (parents, educators, employers, volunteers, government officials). Formal 2013 activities included planning sessions with external facilitators yielding a vision statement, launch of a monthly newsletter, and a major workshop on "Learners and Learning" at the Quality Inn, Charlottetown (October 21, 2013). Meetings were held with high-level representatives including the Premier, Minister of Education, Deputy Minister, PEI Teachers Union President, and professional associations (Engineers, Construction Association, Home and School Federation, Women's Institute). Glendenning emphasizes that the organization's leadership role demonstrates public interest in education improvement and confirms education's fundamental importance.

EDUCATION 20/20 INC.

President’s Report

For the Year ending December 31, 2013



I am pleased to present the following report of Education 20/20 Inc. for the year ending December 31, 2013.


Activities during the year focused on consultation with a wide variety of people whose interest stemmed from their roles as parents and grandparents, educators, employers, the volunteer sector; while the majority was from the Charlottetown area, there were also conversations with a significant number of individuals from throughout the Island including Alberton and Souris.   Some sessions were informal while others were structured to hear concerns and sand views on education.  All sessions were invitational and included discussions of literacy, mathematics and curriculum  Meetings were  held with representatives  of the Home and School Federation, Woment’ Institute, PEI Association of Professional Engineers, PEI Construction Association, President of the PEI Teachers Union, the Deputy Minister of Education, Minister, Premier and others.


Formal  activities during 2013 include:

  • A planning session  with an external facilitator from which we now have a vision statement
  • A monthly Newsletter
  • A workshop on learners and learning held at the Quality Inn, Charlottetown, on October 21, 2013 on thw aubject of “learners and Learning”


I am pleased to report that expenses were covered thanks to donations for our support to the Community Foundation of Prince Edward Island, Because of this arrangement, Education 20/20 neither received nor expended money in its own name.


In closing, I want to thank Directors for support, the Community Foundation of Prince Edward Island for its timely assistance and advice, the “Consultative Groups” for assistance in shaping the vision, and all who gave willingly of time and talent to convert a dream to a reality.


Lessons learned confirm that education is important, there is a growing interest among the public in improving education,  and that Education 20/20 Inc. can play a leadership role.


Respectfully submitted




Don Glendenning

President
