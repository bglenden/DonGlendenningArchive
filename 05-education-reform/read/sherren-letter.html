Don responds to columnist Joe Sherren's article on education improvement by reinforcing the importance of school autonomy with OECD evidence. He succinctly endorses Sherren's combination of "freedom to act and well trained principals" as "a winning combination." This brief letter demonstrates Don's ability to build on others' work and his consistent messaging about autonomy as a key success factor.

September 22, 2014


Editor, Charlottetown Guardian

Charlottetown, PEI


Dear Sir:

Sherren letter 


Joe Sherren has stimulated us again with another article about the importance of education and ways of improving it. (Charlottetown Guardian; September 20, 2014; “Better schools  create better students”).  An OECD study (School Accountability and Autonomy, Report No. 9) found that students perform better when schools have autonomy in their day to day operation.  That’s another reason for focusing on school improvement.


Freedom to act and well trained principals sounds like a winning combination.


Sincerely


Don Glendenning

A long time student of education
