Glendenning reports on his survey of 40 education leaders (Deputy Ministers, university presidents, college presidents, superintendents) asking whether current graduation requirements predict success in post-secondary education or employment. Only one respondent affirmed that course patterns matter significantly. He contrasts existing requirements with New Zealand's National Certificate in Employment Skills, which focuses on practical workplace competencies (reading, writing, listening, problem-solving, teamwork, cultural communication). He suggests PEI should adopt competency-based alternatives that better serve the 50% of students entering workforce rather than higher education.
