Don identifies three key roles for schools emerging from an Education 20/20 workshop: developing young people to their full potential, helping the Island develop needed human capital, and providing care while parents work. He notes that acceptance of the "care" function may have contributed to social promotion and declining diploma value. This foundational letter clarifies Don's belief that clarity about institutional roles must precede other reforms, reflecting his systematic approach to education policy.

February 14, 2014


Editor, Charlottetown Guardian

Charlottetown, PEI


Dear Sir:

The role of schools


The role of schools was the first issue raised in a recent workshop hosted by Education 20/20 in Stratford; from their reaction, it is obvious that many in the audience were unsure.  Here’s my take on the issue:


Most people would agree, I think, that one role of schools is to help young Islanders develop to their full potential.


The public generally expect schools to also help the Island community develop the people needed to secure its future.


There’s a publicly unstated view that providing safe and supportive care for children while parents work is also a role of schools..


Some would argue that the acceptance by schools of the function of “care” has led to social promotion and the declining value of school diplomas.   


Once we agree on roles, dealing with the next issues should be easy.


Sincerely



Don Glendenning

A long-time student of education
