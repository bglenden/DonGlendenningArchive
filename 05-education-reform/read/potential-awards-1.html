Changes to awards program


Education, as compared to other sectors, lacks appropriate economic incentives or awards for either the student, teacher, institution or community to provide a better product or service and do so ahead of schedule. There is increasing support, however, for the view that incentives and awards help to improve student results; an award is for having done something rather than a grant to help do something. Consequently, and in keeping with the focus on a quality education for every Canadian and with the generous financial support of Regis and Joan Duffy, Education 20/20 Inc. instituted an Excellence in Education Award. The first two awards went to schools that demonstrated a high degree of engagement with their communities; Award number 3 went to a school for the way it integrated technology into its everyday operation; the 2017 award will recognize a community group for its efforts to improve educational services for its people. As one who has been involved since day, I believe it fair to say that our purpose is to encourage changes that will benefit all citizens not just those a particular level, community or field of study.


Each year the Awards Committee reviews its previous experience and each year has made some adjustments to the focus and criteria. – it’s time to do so again. Without intending to limit the generation, below are notes on a few ideas that have come to our attention; they are intended to sprk our imagination:

  • Expand the program both regionally and nationally with a “final event” held each year in Charlottetown

  • An award for a non-teaching staff member

  • An award to a Journalism students for the best series of articles on education.

  • A school whose students do best on exams sponsored by the Canadian Mathematics Society

  • A school that allows challenge for credit and PLAR

  • Best student designed school web site

  • A school that goes the extra mile for children of immigrants.

  • To teachers in training or new teachers for a paper on education, publishing the best papers and event for a public presentation

  • Encouraging teachers to write for publication, either public media or professional journals.

  • Students choice award (similar to patient’s choice in the health field

  • Matching dollars for the Canada 150 Fund)


Please feel free to discuss with others
