PURPOSE

No. 3 Doc-5


Education is a service; it is intended to help people change the way they think, feel and act, or even how they operate or what they value. Education is also complex; people have different interests, experiences and needs; every Islander is said to fill six life roles, that of citizen, family member, individual, student, consumer and producer.


Purposes of the public education journey are not always clear. The literature provides the following examples:

  • Fielding - “The first priority of education is to to learn how to live in personal relation with other people”

  • Alberta – “. . . skills needed to be successful in a rapidly changing economy and society”

  • NB – “helping citizens gain attributes needed to be lifelong learners”

  • Anon – “Entrance to postsecondary studies

  • Anon – “Help people navigate life and contribute to society”

  • Anon – “Help people develop skills, knowledge, attitudes and other attributes needed by citizens in a democratic society.

  • Home and School – “Raise one’s general level of education”

  • OECD – “education is about the capacity to live in a multi-faceted world as an active and engaged citizen”

  • Nfld – not found

  • Nova Scotia – not found

  • Ontario – to provide students with the opportunity to realize their potential and develop into highly skilled, knowledgeable, caring citizens who zontribute to their society.

  • Quebec – mission is to “provide instruction, socialize and to provide qualifications”

My search for the purpose of education in PEI was somewhat inconclusive; I am told that there are other statements but I did not come across them in my search.

  • PEI School Administrative Handbook gives the purpose of the PEI Public Education system as being “to provide for the development of children so that each child may take a meaningful place in society

  • The Department of Education Website gives te goal as “to develop and deliver high quality programs and curriculum in English and French to Island children”.

  • A document on philosophy refers to ” helping students secure a place in society.”

  • Another statement refers to helping students prepare to “take a meaningful place in society”.


Concerns:

  1. The statement limits the Island’s educational interests to children; I believe that all Islanders should be encouraged to learn at any age.

  2. The statements are not sufficiently specific to plan programs or measure student progress.

  3. Proposed role of Department “encourage, support and regulate”
