A powerful statement of Don's core educational beliefs, including that most students can learn most content with support, teaching is simply helping others learn, families need education advisors, schools should not rank credentials, and government should fund but not operate education. He challenges educators to publish their own beliefs, encapsulating his lifelong philosophy.

January 6, 2015


Editor,

Charlottetown Guardian

Charlottetown, PEI


Dear Sir:

Things I believe   


This I believe about schools and schooling:

Most students can learn most things taught in public school although some may need a bit more time and help than others

The role of a teacher is simply to help others learn

Every family should have a family education advisor to provide information, advice and counsel on matters of education

Ranking or awarding credentials and qualifications are not school functions

Government has a role in providing a quality education but governments should not be in the business of running schools

Research does not support the current emphasis on courses and grades.


I challenge our educators to publish their beliefs.



Don Glendenning

A long-time student of education
