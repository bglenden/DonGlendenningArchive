Education Philosophy & Reform


January 6, 2015


Editor,

Charlottetown Guardian

Charlottetown, PEI


Dear Sir:

School alignment   


It’s not unusual at this time of year to wonder if we should take our cars for an alignment check.  We may not realize it, but school systems also struggle with alignment issues.  A complete school alignment will cover 8-10 points, but somme of the most pressing include:

 Alignment of outcomes with life needs

Alignment of schools assessment criteria with those in later life

Alignment of report cards subjects with outcome statements

Allocation of time needed by a student in a subject with time allotted by the school 


If you have concerns about your child’s progress, I suggest that start with the course outcomes, review them first with your child then take them to the parent teacher meeting.  Doing so will help focus the discussion.  In fact, sometimes copies of relevant outcomes are given to each student as part of the orientation process at the beginning of each new course.  If not, they are readily available on the erbsite of the Department of Education

Sincerely,


Don Glendenning

A long-time student of education
