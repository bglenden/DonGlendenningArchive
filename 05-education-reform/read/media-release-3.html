For release 9:00 AM

Date: June 4, 2018



Excellence Award goes to Bluefield High


Bluefield High School was named this year’s winner of an Excellence in Education Award offered by Education 20/20 Inc. The award recognized the high degree of collaboration

among teachers, students and the community at large in organizing an “Ag for Life” event, an event intended to celebrate the community and its producers,


The idea originated with Jason Campbell, an Ag teacher at Bluefield High, who, along with two fellow teachers, Emily Perry and Terry Nabuurs organized an “Ag for Life” day to celebrate the community and its producers. The project involved three classes( Agriculture, Hospitality and Culinary) twelve presentations followed by breakout sessions, 21 expo booths and an evening meal. Over 200 people sa for dinner prepared and served by the culinary student under the direction of Chef Jesse MacDonald from the Rodd Crowbush Hotel.


In announcing the Award, Dr. Glendenning, President of Education 20/20 thanked Dr. Regis and Joan Duffy for their continuing interest in and support for education in PEI. This award is presented annually by Education 20/20 to recognize excellence in some element of education, this time the educational benefits of collaboration. Past awards by Education 20/20 Inc have focused on engagement, technology and removing mystery from math.
