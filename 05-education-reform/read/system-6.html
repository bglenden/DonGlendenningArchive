Education System: a set of services put in place by a community to encourage. support and regulate the education of its citizens.


Our future as individuals and as a community will be largely determined by the nature and level of our education. The World Bank cautions countries about confusing going to school wih getting an education; I’m afraid that we’ve fallen into he same trap.


Education is a broad term and includes what has been learned anywhere, at any time whether through personal exploration or with the help of others. Although we refer to our “education system”, my exploration reveals most Provinces to have a school system instead; we do our students a disservice when we confuse the two.

KEY CONCEPTS

  1. Citizen

  2. Learner – a person interested in changing what they know, can do, believe

  3. Self directed /independent learner

  1. Learning

  • Learning is an individual matter;

  • Only the learner can learn,

  • Learning may occur in a group setting and sometimes may even require others to be part of the learning process.

  • Learning results in a change of behaviour

  • Everyone can learn the skills, knowledge attitudes and other attributes needed by citizens in a democracy

  • One’s education is the culmination of things learned


2 Education is a process of arranging experiences that result in learning; typical education services include:

  • Information

  • Counselling

  • A service delivery structure.

  • Instruction – organization and delivery of “content” by way of presentations, practice and exploration.

  • Quality Assurance – to assure Islanders that their learning experiences meet community standards.

  • Learning products and Technology - Access educational materials and technology

  • Funding - to a level and in a manner that is equitable for all Islanders.

  • Human Resources - Teachers, administrators, counsellors, advisors. assistants and others

ORGANIZING PRINCIPLES

  1. Organizations need clear mission statement

  2. Structure should reflect function or services to be provided with key functions having separate Directors

  3. Autonomy and accountabilities must be clearly identified.

  4. Open lines of communication should be maintained with all stakeholders

  5. Time is a variable, Learning lvel is a constant.

  6. Form follows function; functions not identified.

  7. Should focus on services not clients

  8. Grading not an educational function

  9. Relevance and context

  10. Support individual profiles


An organization’s structure outlines services to be provided and how they will be organized and directed in order to achieve purposes, goals, roles and responsibilities. Organizations may take many forms – functional, divisional, matrix, open boundary, virtual, learning, modular, vertical and others. Service organizations, like education, should be local or decentralized giving members freedom to respond to individual and local demands


Key education beliefs - principles, proposition or idea accepted as true

  1. Learning is a personal experience

  2. Most people can learn skills, knowledge and attitudes needed to live and work in a modern economy

  3. Given that each learner learns at own rate, time becomes a variable in the education process.

  4. The primary purpose of education is to help each person to develop skills, knowledge and other attributes needed to thrive as citizens in a democracy.

  5. Assessment criteria should reflect education criteria no just the aquisirion of information.

  6. ach learner should have an advocate

  7. Learning results in a change of behaviour.

  8. Changes in behaviour are demonstrable through our actions.

  9. The role of a teacher is to help students change behaviour;

  10. Teachers design experiences to bring about desired changes in learners.

  11. Teaching is a developmental process

  12. People are educated whether or not they attend school

  13. Grading is not an educational function

  14. Credentials and qualifications are not the same

  15. Awarding prizes is not an education function


• Our future as individuals and communities depend, to a large degree, on the nature and extent of education services available to each citizen.

• Governments invest in education for two reasons (1) to help each citizen develop personal capabilities and (2) to ensure that the community has skills needed to service the community.

• Organized education lacks a change mechanism

• Cost of speaking out is too high for most Islanders educators; cost of silence is even higher

• Each learner has own combination of traits base on genetics, prior learning and aspirations

• Learners are responsible for their own education; teachrs are accountable for ervices provided.

• Although groups do not learn, individuals may learn in a group setting.

• Learning results in a change of behaviour.

• Teachers teach subjects and infer that students have the skills that parents asked for.

• Report cards should report on what a student has learned instead of what he/she has studied.

• The school system lacks the traditional a self- correcting mechanism - choice

• Helps learners prepare for qualifications and not just credentials

• Role of a government is to encourage, support and regulate the delivery of education services

• Education system and school system are not synonymous

• Schools should focus on the development of learners not just the delivery of services

• The education system lacks incentives to respond positively for calls for change

• The Importance of institutions (schools, colleges and universities) in helping individuals acquire the education they want and need is on the decline

• There are no apparent benefits for the education system to improving the educational level of their students; neither is there a penalty for lack of improvement

• Should encourage and support independent learners
