In this reflective planning document, Don Glendenning shares his personal journey founding Education 20/20 about a decade prior, driven by concerns about public education turmoil. He describes his vision for a broad-based organization supporting quality education for Islanders of all ages. Don candidly discusses his underestimation of educational cloistering and his evolving concern about provincial educational quality despite organizational growth in his knowledge. He acknowledges his advancing age and suggests the need for organizational changes while reaffirming commitment to current projects. The document addresses foundational questions about Education 20/20's future direction, potential restructuring, and leadership succession.

Future of Education 20/20 Inc

Plan/2020-1


About a decade ago, I found myself becoming increasingly concerned about the ongoing turmoil in education, especially public education and, in consultation with others with similar concerns we founded Education 20/20 Inc. My vision, given the size of PEI, was a broad based group supporting a quality education for Islanders of all ages and levels. Although I was cautioned, I underestimated the extent of cloistering existing among educators. While Education 20/20 allowed a broad area of service, my personal interest was, and still is, improving the quality of education for all Islanders.


I am pleased to report considerable growth in my personal knowledge of the operation and structure of education; growth in my understanding, however, has actually heightened my concern about the quality of services the Province actually delivers; current organizational arrangements are unlikely to allow or bring about required improvements in educational services.


All this brings me to the matter of Education 20/20 Inc. while I plan to continue with the projects we currently have and will continue to do whatever I can for the good of the cause,

my age and personal interests and limits suggest changes in our current operation.
