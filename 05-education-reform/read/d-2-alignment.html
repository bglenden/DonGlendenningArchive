Newsletter article comparing vehicle alignment to education system alignment. Don argues that education systems need written philosophy statements and measurable goals to maintain consistency across the system, similar to how car manufacturers specify performance standards. The piece advocates for comprehensive framework alignment covering goals, outcomes, assessment, instruction, and scheduling to prevent systems from drifting.

Alignment


It’s not unusual at this time of year for your vehicle to need a front end alignment. Evidence that your car needs an alignment may appear in the form of your vehicle wandering, always pulling to one side or even an uncontrolled shimmy. But may be fixed by resetting your cars camber, caster and toe in back to the manufacturer’s specifications.

Education systems may also get out of alignment resulting in the system pulling to one side, wandering from fad to fad and even exhibiting an uncontrolled shimmy.


While education systems do not have a manufacturer’s specification, they do have, or should have, a written statement of beliefs and measurable goals. Such a statement sets the direction and provides a framework for individual decision making. A well written and well publicized philosopyy allows decisionmaking to be decentralized and delegate while ensuring consistency across the system.


If a philosophy of education exists for PEI, I have not been able to find it; the nearest I came to such a document was a statement approved in 1990 dealing with schools. A new The first step in realigning education, therefore, is to update and expand that statement from a statement about schooling to one about education.


With that in place, we can then proceed to check:

  • Goals

  • Outcomes -should reflect the outcomes that describe good citizenship.


  • Assessment – will be criterion referenced and apply to each outcome.


  • Instruction – will be developmental and not just the delivery of course content


  • Student reports – will show level of achievement on an outcome by outcome basis.


  • Scheduling – will be flexible to allow each person to reach his or her full potential.


  • Scoring rubrics – will be community based


  • Benchmarks – will be the same as for any citizen
