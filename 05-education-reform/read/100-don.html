Confusing acronyms and overused jargon in education -Willen


SOME CLOSING THOUGHTS

So, this is Newsletter 100; the end of the series. Not a particularly inspiring series but a great learning experience for the writer. Actually this my third newsletter-series in recent years. It’s a great learning tool; I commend it to you.


Writing, at least notetaking, is part of my learning style. I write to learn. It helps me identify major points. I have a flipchart standing at the open at all times and i pick away at alternative phrases. Later, I move to my typewriter to begin writing.


I was never a very good writer but my curriculum development experience, and DACUM in particular, reinforced my inclination to write in bullet form.


So, let me recap:

  • A learner is a person who is interested in changing skills, knowledge and/or attitudes

  • Learning is the process a learnere goes through in changing the way he/she thinks, feels or acts

  • Instruction is a set of experiences created by a person or persons to help another person change behaviour.




  • A school is a purpose-built facility that houses learning resources.

  • Learning is a personal experience

  • Only a learner can learn

  • To each learning situation, each learner brings what he/she was born with, prior learning and personal aspirations.

  • Communities support learning services because it is good for bot individual learners and the community

  • While teachers may diagnose to plan better teaching, grading of learners is not a teaching function.

  • The awarding of prizes is not a teaching function


Thank you foor your interest and your comments
