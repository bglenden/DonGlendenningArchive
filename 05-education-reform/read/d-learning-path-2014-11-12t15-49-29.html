A student’s learning path


It was a new experience for me. I’m talking about listening to young people talk about the kind of help they find useful in learning new things. Information came first; for some that meant the lecture but others preferred to try Google.


Not surprising, students neither want nor need the same thing at the same time, and that’s the dilemma for schools, and a frustration for teachers as well as students.


As part of an initiative by Education 20/20 to diagram the learning path, panel members were asked to list things that helped with their learning. Below are other ideas:

  • An overview of the subject

  • A context

  • Instruction in the form of a lecture or demonstration, or on line

  • assessment and feedback,

  • Early attempts to perform the skill such as drafting an outline of a report

  • Reflecting on their own performance.


Panelists, like classes themselves, exhibited a wide range of views on both the problem and its resolution. The suggestions will be presented in the form of a flow chart which may be of particular interests to parents.


The panel, organized by Education 20/20, was chaired by Tanya Craig and included Callen Cudmore, Ryan Rioux, Iain Cudmore and Stephanie Mayne. I’ll report back when the path is posted.
