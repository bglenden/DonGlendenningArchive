This June 11, 2016 letter to Wayne Hambly, President of Hambly's Home Furnishings, reflects Don's views on education transformation. He argues for shifting from an inward-looking, bureaucratic system to an outward-looking, nimble organization focused on developing responsible citizens. Don notes some progress through the Premier's commitment to consultation but emphasizes that seven additional items in the Education 20/20 "Call for Action" remain on the agenda. He describes Education 20/20's activities (information gathering, newsletter, workshops, award programs) and announces plans to increase public advocacy, lobby for alternative delivery and assessment models, and refocus the education award from schools to community-led initiatives. The letter solicits potential financial support without obligation.

Dear Wayne:

I enjoyed our recent conversation, although it was all too short.

To recap about education; for education to improve, we need to convert an inward-looking, bureaucratic delivery system to an outward looking, nimble organization focused on helping youngsters develop into responsible citizens. Are we making progress? I think so. The Premier’s commitment to consultation is a step in the right direction – but it is not enough. The enclosed Call for Action lists seven other items that we believe should be on the education agenda.

To date, Education 20/20 Inc. has focused on gathering information, a monthly bilingual newsletter, public workshops and presentations, a school awards program, and the inevitable meetings. While these will continue, we plan to step up our campaign for change, to be more public in our efforts, to lobby for other delivery and assessment models and to work on narrowing the gap between graduation requirements and what students need in their various life roles. We also plan to switch the focus of our Education Award from schools to community led education initiatives.

We all have many requests for financial support and there are many worthy causes. If you have an interest in any of our activities, I’d be delighted to provide further information. If that leads to financial support, we will be appreciative but, if not, I still look forward to future conversations.

Sincerely



Don Glendenning

President
