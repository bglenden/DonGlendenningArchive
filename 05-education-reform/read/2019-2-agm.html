Math skills to be mapped


Education 20/20 proposes to map the everyday math skills of Island residents. Although mathematics is a frequent subject of complaint, it appears that no one has actually surveyed Islanders to determine the math skills they use during the run of a day. When completed, the results will be made available to interested parties. The results may be of interest to teachers and curriculum developers but my also help when setting standards, with counselling and career planning, and even as a transcript of math skills learned. Fund raising for the initiative is now under way.


As part of his report, Dr. Glendenning, President of Education 20/20 reviewed activities carried out during the year; he noted especially the survey of continuing education needs in Souris. Souris residents would like access to on-line-course and more hands-on training. Glendenning suggested that continuing education is important but needs to be well advertised. It also should be given a higher priority when making educational plans.


As part of the annual meeting, officers was elected for the coming year including Sharon Cameron, President, Bill Whelan, Vice President, Ross Reid, Secretary and Don Glendenning as Treasure; members also thanked Dr. Glendenning for his services during the founding years of the organization.
