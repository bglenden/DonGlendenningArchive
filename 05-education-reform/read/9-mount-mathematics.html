A metaphorical and conceptual framework presenting mathematics as a "mountain" requiring structured progression. Don identifies educational issues (difficulty predetermining appropriate grade levels, lack of national standards, interpretation problems) and proposes treating mathematics as a prerequisite hierarchy from numeracy through calculus. The proposal involves undertaking comprehensive field analysis, establishing PISA-aligned levels, and creating independent assessment services similar to music and skating certifications. Benefits include common standards, improved mobility, reduced curriculum workload, and leveraging existing OECD research. The mountain metaphor emphasizes that learners must progress from base to summit at their own pace.
