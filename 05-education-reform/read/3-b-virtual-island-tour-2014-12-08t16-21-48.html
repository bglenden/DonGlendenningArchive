Detailed planning document for Education 20/20's "Virtual Tour through Island Education" (January 21, 2015) at Stratford Town Hall, facilitated by Don Glendenning and team. The 2-hour interactive program guides participants in small teams to examine PEI education documents, compare definitions of learning/teaching/education, evaluate the Department of Education's completeness, and discuss teacher/principal roles, lifelong learning, and the "preparedness gap" identified in the Community Foundation's Vital Signs report.

r PLANNING SHEET


TITLE: Virtual tour through Island education

PURPOSE: Familiarize participants with education documents and services available to Islanders

FORMAT: Following an initial overview, participants will break into teams to search out and report findings on pertinent information about issues, programs, organization, standards, curriculum, and other matters of interest or concern, following which the group will draw conclusions, identify unanswered questions and consider future action.

DATE: January 21, 2015

TIME: 7-9:00 pm

RESOURCES: Education 20/20 team

CLASS: Limited to 20 people; willing to repeat if numbers warrant

LOCATION: Southport Room, Stratford Ton Hall

COST: None

NOTES: WiFi Svailable; in order to participate, participants will need an IPad or smartphones


Revised scenario but still a draft::


1)  (5 minutes)The session will open with a few remarks explaining the mechanics of the evening.

 

2)  (15 minutes) Working definitions: Each table to come up with a working definition of learning and teaching, educating and schooling, education system and school system. Each table report on one of their definitions


3) (20 minutes) What was the message from the Charlottetown Chamber of Commerce make to the Standing Committee? What is your reaction to their presentation. They idenrified a need for a clear vision; does the statement of philosophy meet that need? If not, why not?

4) (20 minutes) What are the functions of a system of education:  are they all accounted for in our Department of Education? If not, what’s missing? If anything is missing, what difference does it make?

4) What is the role of a teacher in the PEI school  system

5) What is the role of a school principal?

How does PEI support lifelong learning? 

The CFPEI report, Vital Signs, identified a “preparedness gap” ; where do young Island graduate turn for help to narrow the gap?

What do the ELSB minutes say about the 20/20 presentation? What action should we take?

Give  an elevator speech about the new literacy tests, or other issue of interest.

As part of the wrap up, we should list any unanswered questions and seek ideas as to how to improve the process if offering another virtual tour.

 

I’d appreciate your help in framing the questions and scenarios, perhaps posing some of the questions on January 21, and facilitating discussion at one of the tables.

 

Look forward to your ideas
