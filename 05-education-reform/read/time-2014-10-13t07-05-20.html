A critical analysis of how education systems misallocate learning time compared to service industries, arguing that schools determine time available rather than responding to learner needs. Don contrasts educational practices with other professions that estimate time required and deliver accordingly, highlighting that education's time-based, batch-processing model prevents students from finishing early or taking additional time when needed. He advocates for challenge exams and prior learning assessment to allow students flexible time options.
Education Philosophy & Reform

Open Education 17 June 20, 2014


Options


I am aware that any grade and any subject includes students that are ahead and behind grade level; it has always been thus and will always be thus. How should schools handle this problem?

  • Let them struggle

  • Provide extra instruction

  • Recommend a lower level of the subject

  • Recommend that they take an upgrading course after they graduate

  • Allow them to drop the class

  • Provide each teacher with an aspirin allowance

  • Send the staff for extra training

  • Adjust the delivery system to reflect the reality of learning

  • Allow students to challeng for credit







  • Schools have yet to learn how to make time work for learners; this results in many students leaving school with low grades. Additional learning time could result in higher grades thereby opening new doors to students when they graduate. The inability to customize learning time is one of education’s greatest challenges.

  • Challenging for credit comes in a number of forms, prior learning assessment, exemptions and challenge exams; it is also an issue of time. Allowing students to challenge for credit provides an incentive to students and successful challenges may also release teacher resources to devote to other students. When Colonel Gray High School discontinued the practice of exemptions a few years ago, the media reported that a new plan was under development by the Department. I haven’t found the replacement plan yet.



  • Not surprising, the chief time wasters controlled by teachers occur in the classroom, not starting classes promptly, excessive use of films, discipline time, finishing before end of period and non-teaching duties


I had an interesting conversation recently about the way the school system allocates time as compared to other sectors. Education has yet to discover how to budget time to the advantage of learners.


Most service industries identify an outcome whether fixing your car, taking your case to court or treating an injury, estimate the time required book people and facilities, perform the service and send you home.


Educators do the reverse; they tell you how much time is available to help but seldom ask what you need or want; keep you in class for the time allocated whether or not you need it, in fact, if you leave early, you may be penalized through lower grades or even the loss of credit or diploma. On the other hand, schools cannot allow more time on task even if needed. Chances are thatif you need more time to finish you must wait until the next year and even theun it not possible to pick up where he left off but instead must start at the beginning a year later.


This could only happen in education; if you want to send a note, I’ll certainly make time for your comments


Sincerely



Don Glendenning

A long-time student of education

About time


I had an interesting conversation recently about the way the school system allocates time as compared to other sectors.


Most service industries identify an outcome, whether fixing your car, taking your case to court or treating an injury, estimating the time required and booking people and facilities.


Educators do the reverse; they tell you how much time is available to help and when it’s available but seldom ask just what you need or want. In schools, one never finishes earlier than scheduled, or even if you do, you must hang around for length of time you booked; in fact, if you leave early, you may be penalized through lower grades or even the loss of credit or diploma. Schools cannot give you more time than the allotted time because administration has scheduled another group of students. If a student runs short of time, it is not possible to pick up where he left off but instead must start at the beginning a year later. If the grade assigned does not let you qualify, that is unfortunate but that is life – besides that, the student comes from a home where there are few books and he didn’t try very hard.


This could only happen in education; if you want to send a note, I’ll certainly make time for your comments
