Establishes a School Innovation Grant program to increase implementation of best practices and innovation in PEI schools. The program includes objectives to secure funds, identify innovation activities, provide financial support, and monitor results. The three-page proposal process includes preference criteria for collaborative, evidence-based projects addressing actual educational needs. Management through Education 20/20 Inc. with final decisions resting with the organization. Administrative details include cost-bearing by proposers and a two percent administrative fee through CFPEI. This document reflects Don's systematic approach to embedding educational innovation within established institutional frameworks and accountability structures.

School innovation Grant

Souris, PEI

Pro-1-4-D-Fund

GOAL: Increase the implementation of innovation and best practices in PEI schools

OBJECTIVES:

Working with Souris school personnel. Education 20/20 Inc. proposes to:

  • Secure funds

  • Identify activities that encourage innovation

  • Provide financial support

  • Monitor results


APPROACH:

  • Secure funds

  • Establish local committee

  • Promote innovation in schools

  • Solicit proposals

  • Review proposals – (interview optional)

  • Work with proposer to ensure that proposal fits both school and 20/20 interests - optional

  • Award grant

  • Receive report

PREFERENCE WILL BE GIVEN TO PROJECTS THAT:

  • Are collaborative

  • Address an actual education issue

  • Build on existing initiatives

  • Are equitable

  • Reflect best practices, leading edge thinking and research


DOCUMENTATION:

  • Shall include but not be limited to a written format of not more than three pages

  • Shall arise from the group and not be the result of a contract with professionals

  • Up to three letters of support


PROPOSALS

Documentation to include:

  • Description of an educational need

  • Educational principles on which submission is based.

  • Proposed activity

  • Likely future of the initiative

  • Details of the operation


OTHER

  • Competition /opens/closes:

  • Costs, if any, are to be borne by the proposer

  • Proposal may be sent to the address below or left at the office of the Community Foundation of Prince Edward Island, 119-121 Queen St. Charlottetown, PEI (Tel:902-892-3440)

  • Selection will be based on documentation received; proposer may be interviewed

  • Education 20/20 2ill have reasonable access to observe project operation

  • Decisions to award rests with Education 20/20 Inc.

  • Participants may be invited to present their proposals publicly

  • Education 20/20 may negotiate changes as part of the acceptance process

  • Education 20/20 assumes no legal liability for the project
