QUALITY TEACHING

Three broad conclusions from research on student learning (Teachers Matter, OECD)

  • “The largest source of variation in student learning is attributable to differences in what students bring to school.”

  • “… of those variables which are potentially open to policy influence, factors to do with teachers and teaching are the mist important influences on student learning. In particular, the broad consensus is that “teacher quality” is the single most important school variable influencing student achievement.”

  • Relationship between measures of student performance and academic ability or subject matter knowledge, although positive, is of a lesser extent than expected. Other characteristics, such as the ability to convey ideas, to create effective learning environments and to be enthusiastic and creative are also rated s important.

CHANGING ROLES

Areas of broadened teacher responsibility (Teachers Matter, OECD)


At the individual level:

  • Initiating and managing learning processes

  • Responding effectively o yje learning needs of individual learners

  • Integrating formative and summative assessment


At the classroom level:

  • Teaching in multicultural classrooms

  • New cross-curriculum emphasis

  • Integrating students with special needs


At the school level:

  • Working and planning in teams

  • Evaluating and systematic improvement planning

  • ICT use in teaching and adm9nstration

  • Management and shared leadership


At the level of parents and wider community:

  • Providing professional advice to parents

  • Building community partnerships for learning
