Letter documenting multiple structural confusions in PEI education. Don notes: (1) the Department claims to offer "schooling" only, despite "education" being in its title; (2) the Department is organized by schooling levels rather than functions; (3) the Department's stated role omits "helping learners"; (4) common terminology is lacking; (5) PEI has a "school system" rather than an "education system." Restructuring Called For


I’ve been talking with friends about education; a few things confuse me. There have been a number of staff changes recently at senior levels of the Department; lack of clarity may have been the cause.

  • Although called a Department of Education, we seem only to offer “schooling” to young people; on enquiry, I was told that “education” was dropped from the Education Act a few years ago.
  • Government departments are usually organized around major functions; our Department of Education seems tu is organized around “levels of schooling”.
  • The Role of the Department is given as “developing and delivering curriculum”; it seems strange that the idea of “helping” learners has been left out.
  • Given the importance of education, the number of people involved, the size and diversity of the education community, and  the complexity of the field,  the development and distribution of a list of key words would be helpful.
  • The education literature calls for an education system; we seem only to have a school system. 

Th literature reports on a variety of changes in education occuring elsewhere. perhaps its time for an upgrade in PEI. 

Sincerely

Don Glendenning
