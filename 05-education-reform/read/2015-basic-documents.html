Don critiques PEI's Department of Education Philosophy document for being narrowly focused on schooling rather than education, fuzzy in language, and lacking measurable goals. He argues that important learning happens outside institutional settings and that education should encompass online courses, workplace training, and home-based learning. Don recommends that updating this foundational document should be "Job 1" for a new Premier, reflecting his belief that clarity of purpose precedes effective implementation. This letter showcases his demand for philosophical clarity and systemic coherence.
Education Philosophy & Reform

January 6, 2015


Editor,

Charlottetown Guardian

Charlottetown, PEI


Dear Sir:

Time to update basic education documents   


Every Islander who is interested in education should take a look at the “Philosophy of Education” found on the website of the Department of Education and Early Childhood Development. It’s a short document about purposes, basic principles, goals and the role of schools in society.  It’s a basic document and one that reflects the beliefs and values and of the psople who operate our schools.  The document also helps understand the basis for decision making; it’s a useful document but it needs to be updated, refreshed and broadened.


WI say this because:

It purports to be about education but is only about schooling.

It is a fuzzy document

Most goals are not measurable
