Open Education

No. 91 -3 August 20, 2020

When schools and ministries are managed well, learning happens-World Bank


STANDARD

“A readily distinguishable level of performance, demonstrated through the making of a product or provision of a service “


School grades aren’t what I thought they were. According to my internet searches, grades refer to the level of material studied or level at which the teacher teaches. Grade has little too do with how much math a student knows or how well a student performs. At le4ast that helps me to understand a comment by a teacher a number of years ago about a student operating at the Grade six level in a Grade 10 class.

if i’m correct, its’s unlikely that the person examining a transcript, unless they we educators also, would know whether the student had studied or was good at fractions, statistics or managing personal finances.


I’m used to seeing transcripts that show not only that the holder has studied numbers, patterns and relations, shapes and space, statistics and probability, measurement, geometry, algebra, etc. and just how well he/she performed on each.

It also supports the case for an independent standards authority.



RECENT READING


PROFILES

A recent report in the Australian media proposes that “high school students focus on building a learner profile that reflects the skills and knowledge acquired during their school years “ While learning profiles are not new, including outcomes mastered is a major change and would go a long way to solving the problem of transition to both work and further studies.

MICRO CREDENTIALS

This is a fairly new term and one that seems to be gaining support. It seems to be consistent with the work of William Spadey and Outcomes Based Education. It is unclear at the moment whether a micro credential would report standing on every outcome attempted.
