Letter to editor arguing for structural review of PEI's education system, distinguishing between a "school system" (youth-focused) and an "education system" (lifelong learning). Don cites the World Bank's warning about conflating schooling with education, and advocates for service-based organization addressing Islanders of all ages. Proposes that clarity on education purpose and service offerings is essential staff development and policy communication.

Dear Editor:

A System Review

Our education system needs a structural review; to be more precise; although we refer to our education system. it seems only to be a school system. 

The World Bank, the largest financial contributor to public education, cautions countries about confusing going to school with getting an education. I believe we do that in PEI. We have a system focused on serving youth in school settings. What we should have is an education system focused on needs of Islanders of any age, interest and capability  whether in school, thinking about returning to school or simply needing  or wanting to learn more.

Based on my conversations, a restructuring is in order and should start with a clear understanding of education, a clear purpose and list of services offered. Preparing such a document, would be great in-service staff development; distributing such a document would add much needed clarity.respond to

Sincerely
Don Glendenning


“Educating” is a service intended to help people gain or develop their knowledge, skills, attitudes, values and other attributes; typical services include information, counselling, learning products and technology, analysis, instruction, human resources and a tracking system.
