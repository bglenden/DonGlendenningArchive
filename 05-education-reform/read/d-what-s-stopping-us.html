Don asserts that PEI has excellent educators throughout the system (Department, school boards, colleges, universities, private schools) and asks what prevents progress. The letter then lists obstacles in fragmented form: unanswered questions, lack of debate, bureaucracy, fear, autonomy concerns, formulae, politicization, CEA (union), democracy deficit, and blurred vision. This letter reads as a provocative diagnosis suggesting the barriers to education improvement are political, cultural, and systemic rather than resource or expertise based. The list format emphasizes multiple intersecting obstacles and implies that addressing education challenges requires overcoming institutional inertia, political constraints, and organizational silos—not hiring better educators.


July 7, 2015
