A brief letter proposing a fundamental shift in educational focus from "faults in the teaching system" to "faults in the grading system." Don notes that grading practices originated at Yale in the late 1700s, suggesting their historical basis may not match contemporary educational needs. The letter advocates for redirecting reform energy toward assessment systems rather than solely targeting teacher improvement, reflecting his conviction that grading structures are the real barrier to educational progress.
