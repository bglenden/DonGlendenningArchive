Don calls for PEI to develop an updated philosophy of education to replace the 1990 version housed on the Department of Education website. He argues the existing philosophy focuses narrowly on youth and schooling rather than recognizing education as a lifelong process for all Islanders. Drawing on OECD research, Don emphasizes that schools must balance individual development with preparing citizens for community needs. He positions this philosophical update as an ideal priority for a new government, framing education policy as foundational to governance.
Education Philosophy & Reform

April 2, 2015


Editor,

Charlottetown Guardian

Charlottetown, PEI


Dear Sir:

Philosophy of Education for PEI


PEI needs an up-to-date philosophy of education; the one now posted on the Department of Education website is focused on the young and schooling, and was approved in 1990.  


We need a philosophy that recognize that education is important, that education is more than schooling, and to include Islanders of all ages.  Teachers and the public do not always share the same view as to the role of schools, and the OECD adds a twist by arguing that schools have a responsibility to provide the skills needed to ensure the future of our communities.  


What better place for a new government to start. 

Sincerely,


Don Glendenning

A long-time student of education
