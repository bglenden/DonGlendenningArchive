A 2012 biographical statement with full credentials (C.M. Ph.D designation). Comprehensive overview from childhood in Little Branch through teaching career, federal training specialist role, and 18-year tenure as Holland College founding president (1969-1987). Emphasizes the College's international reputation for innovative competency-based education. Details post-retirement consulting, contributions to Canadian Vocational Journal and Community Foundation publications. Lists honors: Order of Canada, Atlantic Canada Innovator Award finalist, UPEI honorary degree. Notes family: married to Carol Chesney of Peoria, Illinois; two children and two grandchildren.

Donald Ernest Malcolm Glendenning, C.M. Ph.D

62 Queen Elizabeth Drive

Charlottetown, PE  C1A 3A9



Donald Glendenning was born and raised in New Brunswick and taught at the elementary, secondary and postsecondary level.  He attended the Little Branch School, is a graduate of the Miramichi Rural High School, Teachers' College and the New Brunswick Institute of Technology (now the New Brunswick Community College) and studied at the University of London (England) on a Lord Beaverbrook Overseas Scholarship.  He holds a B.Sc. and MSc. from Bradley University and a Ph.D. from Indiana University.  Dr. Glendenning taught at the elementary, secondary and post-secondary level, and worked as a training specialist in Ottawa for a number of years with the Department of Labour and the Department of Manpower and Immigration.


In 1969 Dr. Glendenning moved to Prince Edward Island to become the Founding President of Holland College, a college of applied arts and technology.  In this capacity, he played a leadership role in the founding of the College and in implementing competency based education.  The College acquired an international reputation for its innovative approach to education.


In 1987 Dr. Glendenning retired from Holland College and operated a consulting firm with projects both in Canada and overseas.  For a number of years he was a regular contributor to the Canadian Vocational Journal, and edited and published The Family Chronicle and the Community Foundation News.  He also authored and co-authored a number of publications.  His current activities include being a Director of the Community Foundation of Prince Edward Island, genealogy, gardening and reading.  Dr. Glendenning is the recipient of a number of awards and honours including the Order of Canada, Atlantic Canada Innovator of the Year Award Finalist, and holds an honourary degree from the University of Prince Edward Island.  


Don is married to Carol Chesney, of Peoria, Illinois and they have two children, and two grandchildren
