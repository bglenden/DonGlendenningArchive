Collection of Don's personal recollections from St. Paul's Presbyterian Church in Black River, including memories of gas lamps, oyster suppers, and early automobiles. Don shares vivid details of childhood experiences attending services and traveling by horse and wagon to church. The document captures his appreciation for small-town community traditions and religious life.

MEMORIES


The writer has many fond memories of St. Paul’s Presbyterian Church and decided to share some as part of this record.  A request for recollections was sent to others but the list was short, except for relatives, because of the lack of current addresses.  The comments received are included in random order below.


I remember:


  • The hissing of the gas lamps.  There were three, two double lamps over the main aisle and a single lamp over the choir stand.  As dusk began to settle, Uncle Jim, or someone, would light the lamps.  The lamps used naptha gas and air was pumped in to increase the pressure thus causing the hissing.  (Don Glendenning)
  • Oyster suppers (Don Glendenning)
  • Gordon Adams’ red car, the first red car I ever say.  When someone asked him about it, Gordon said “It was the style in the States”.  (Don Glendenning)
