Donald E. M. Glendenning


Don Glendenning was born and raised in Little Branch, Black River, New Brunswick.  He attended the Little Branch School and is a graduate of the Miramichi Rural High School. 

For a number of years, he taught in New Brunswick, first at Kouchibouguac, then at the Miramichi Rural High School and the New Brunswick Institute of Technology.  After a number of years as a training specialist with the Federal government, in Ottawa, he moved to Prince Edward Island where he was Founding President of Holland College.  On leaving the College, he operated his own consulting firm taking on projects both in Canada and overseas.


His formal studies include Teachers’ College in Fredericton, NB, New Brunswick Technical Institute, the University of London (England), Bradley University and Indiana University where he received his Ph.D.  In 1986 he was inducted into the Order of Canada.  Over the years he was active in a number of National organizations including the Canadian Vocational Association, Association of Canadian Community Colleges, and the Institute of Public Administration of Canada.  


For many years, he was a frequent speaker at conferences, he contributed a regular column to the CVA Journal, edited a newsletter for the Community Foundation of Prince Edward Island, published the Family Chronicle, and recounted recollections about life in Black River.


He is currently active in The Community Foundation of Prince Edward Island, tracing his roots, reading and puttering around in his garden.  Lately, he has become a frequent writer of letters to the Editor in support of changes in public education.


Don married Carol Chesney from Peoria, Illinois and they have two children and two grandchildren.
