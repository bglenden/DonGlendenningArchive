A ROUND TRIP FROM LITTLE BRANCH


Recalling the Journey 
















by


Donald Ernest Malcolm Glendenning. C.M., Ph.D, LLD

1929 - 2












FOREWARD



On Sunday evening, September 8, 1929 at Little Branch, Northumberland County, New Brunswick, Mrs. Elmer Glendenning gave birth to a son, Donald Ernest Malcolm.  He was the ninth and last child in the family.


Don grew sup in the community, attended St. Pauls Presbyterian Church with his parents, attended the Little Branch School and the Miramichi Rural High School.  At age 16 he left home to pursue higher education and the following year entered the teaching profession where his career included being a teacher, administrator, college president and educational consultant.  During his lifetime, he was active in education at the local, provincial and national level and practiced his profession internationally.


He married Carol Louise Chesney of Peoria, Illinois on January 31, 1958 and together they lived, studied and worked in New Brunswick, Ontario, British Columbia, Indiana and Prince Edward Island.  They also raised a family of two, Brian and Janet.


After retirement, Don and Carol continued to live in Prince Edward Isalnd but traveled extensively especially during the winter season.  Hhe also enjoyed his cottage, his garden, farm and home.


In 2???, Don returned to his home community and now rests in the Black river Cemetery together with his parents, siblings, family and friends.


This is a record of his journey – an account rather than an autobiography.  It is intended to bring together a brief account of his journey for the information and benefit of his offspring.  While the bulk of the document was prepared by Don Glendenning, himself.  Some parts of the return journey were completed by xxxx
