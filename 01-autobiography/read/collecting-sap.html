Collecting Sap


We always tapped a few maple trees on the ridge when the nights were cool and the days were warm.  The sap was good to drink but had little if any taste.


I recall occasions of going to the Ridge with Ken early in the morning to empty the sap buckets.  Ken hitched the horse and we took milk cans, of the kind used to ship cream to the creamery and I recall once taking the barrel from the churn to bring the sap to the house.


On occasion, and maybe frequently, Mum would boil the sap to make maple syrup.  It takes a barrel, 45 gallons, of sap to make a gallon of syrup and it seemed to take forever to reduce sap to syrup.  It took even more boiling to reduce sap to maple sugar. Maybe it seemed like a long time because  because it was usually my job to keep the woodbox filled.
