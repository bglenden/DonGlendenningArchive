Specialized rating scale for mathematics education focusing on six higher-order cognitive dimensions: Evaluation (verify answers and select optimal results), Communication (represent information symbolically/graphically), Synthesis (generalize patterns to principles), Analysis (interpret mathematical models), Application (use multiple solution methods), and Criteria (apply mathematical reasoning). This framework elevates mathematics instruction beyond computation to encompass deeper conceptual understanding, verification skills, and problem-solving strategies. Drawing from Colorado higher education standards, it reflects sophisticated approaches to mathematics pedagogy emphasizing reasoning, communication, and metacognition central to 21st-century mathematics education.

RATING SCALE FOR MATH *



Evaluation

Able to estimate and verify answers to mathematical problems to determine reasonableness, compare alternatives and select optimal results; recognize that mathematical and statistical methods have limitations; evaluate the quality of evidence and reasoning; and draw an appropriate conclusion





6

Communication

Able to represent mathematical information symbolically, graphically numerically and verbally



5

Synthesis

Able to generalize from specific patterns and phenomena to more abstract principles and to proceed from abstract principles to specific applications



4

Analysis

Able to interpret and draw inferences from mathematical models such as formulas, graphs and tables.



3

Application

Able to use several methods, such as algebraic, geometric and statistical reasoning to solve problems



2

Criteria

Able to use mathematical methods, reasoning and strategies to investigate and solve problems.
