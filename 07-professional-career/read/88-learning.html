This undated piece (April 15, 2020, No. 88) presents Don's foundational beliefs about learners and education, again referencing Dewey's emphasis on understanding people's beliefs. Open Education

No. 88 April 15, 2020

John Dewey once said that if he could only know one thing about a person,

he would like to know what that person believed


KEY BELIEFS ABOUT LEARNERS


  1. Learning is a personal experience

  2. Most people can learn skills, knowledge and attitudes needed to live and work in a modern economy

  3. “Teaching time” is considered a variable

  4. The purpose of education is to help each person to develop skills needed to thrive as citizens.

  5. Assessment criteria should reflect those applied in the community served

  6. Every learner should have an advocate

  7. Learning results in a change of behaviour.

  8. Changes in behaviour are demonstrable through new skills, increased knowledge or changed attitudes.

  9. The role of a teacher is to help students change behaviour;

  10. Teachers design experiences to bring about desired changes in learners.

  11. Teaching is a developmental process

  12. People are educated whether or not they attend school

  13. Grading is not an educational function

  14. Credentials and qualifications are not the same

  15. Awarding prizes is not an education function

  16. Our future as individuals and communities depend, to a large degree, on the nature and extent of the education of its people










  1. Governments invest in education for two reasons (1) to help each citizen develop personal capabilities and (2) to ensure that the community has skills needed to service the community.

  2. Each learner has own combination of traits base on genetics, prion learning and aspirations

  3. Learners are responsible for their own education

  4. Every learner needs an advocate

  5. Report cards should report on what a student has learned instead of what he/she has studied.

  6. The school system lacks a self- correcting mechanism

  7. Instruction Helps learners prepare for qualifications and not just credentials

  8. Role of a government is to encourage, support and regulate the delivery of education services





Don Glendenning, Editor
