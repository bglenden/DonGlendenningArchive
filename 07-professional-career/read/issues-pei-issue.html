Exploratory notes documenting his systematic investigation of PEI public education issues. Identifies key problems: definitions unavailable, branch-level confusion, clarity deficits (education-schooling, branches-levels, learning-grade levels), unidentified/unavailable services, school-operations focus, documentation gaps (learner profiles, tracking systems, learning/curriculum models, assessment models, transcripts), unclear purpose, benchmarking needs, and autonomy limitations. Frames investigation as foundational for reform work. (Dated May 22, 2024)

EDUCATION ISSUES- PEI

Decided some time ago to explore current issues about public education. Here are my findings:

  • Definitions
  • Not widely available
  • Confuse Branch and level
  • Clarity: 
  • Confuse  education and schooling, 
  • branches and levels, 
  • learning levels and grade levels.
  • Services not identified  or independently available, 
  • Department structured to run schools rather than provide education services
  • Lack of documentation
  • Desired learner end profile/expectations,
  • tracking system
  • learning model, 
  • curriculum development model, 
  • assessment model and 
  • transcript.
  • Purpose- not sufficiently clear
  • Benchmark needed
  • School autonomy
