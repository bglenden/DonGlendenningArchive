Math on PEI


Although PEI math scores on National and international tests have risen, friends tell me that math has not improved on the shop floor; I’ve been wondering why.


Islanders need math; citizens may not need a high level but they need to be able to measure, sort, calculate, group and estimate numbers, space, time, distance, etc. and they need to do it quickly, easily, accurately; and consistently. This approach to math is called numeracy and it involves the application of math to every-day lives. Numeracy is being adopted elsewhere; usually in addition to traditional math.


But we need more, to begin with we need an analysis of math skills used by Islanders. Making an analysis involves bringing together a small group for a two- day of brainstorming session. It’s a proven technique, we used it regularly at Holland College but apparently there is no one on PEI now with the expertise. The cost of undertaking the analyses is $15,000.


While all donations are appreciated, we are approaching a few people who might contribute $1-2,000 each to help cover the cost. The Community Foundation, 121 Queen Street, Charlottetown, PE, C1A 4B3 will accept donations on our behalf and issue charitable receipts.


I appreciate your consideration and would be happy to explain further and answer any questions. Thanks in advance


Sincerely,



Don Glendenning
