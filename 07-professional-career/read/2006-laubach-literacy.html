Don's June 2006 speaking notes to Laubach Literacy Council in Charlottetown. Reflects on his teaching career starting in 1947 at age 17, his mother's love of reading, and the importance of literacy beyond economic impact. Discusses the distinction between education and schooling and commends volunteers in adult literacy.

LAUBACH LITERACY COUNCIL

CHARLOTTETOWN, PEI- JUNE 3, 2006?


Speaking Notes



Thanks for invitation  and for the kind words of welcome

I am delighted to be here; miss contact with educators


Welcome delegates from across Canada

Fortunate to travel to all Provinces

Each part this country is unique


Great country

Much to be thankful for

I am proud to be Canadian


After Carol invited me to be here this evening, I looked at the web page.  And browsed a number of articles.


Liked what I saw – not too much academic jargon



Commend you and the many volunteers for your efforts

Teachers in universities, colleges and schools system get considerable attention.

You are unsung heroes of education


Recognize some of the PEI names 


Graduated from Miramichi Rural High in 1946


Started teaching in a one room school in NB in 1947 at the age of 17

Consider myself to have been in education ever since.


I enjoy the informality of this sized group

First time I spoke to a group in the west

Honour of your visit – holding gathering in the auditorium

Obviously delighted

Mentioned it to my boss when I returned

Boss pointed out the origin of auditorium

Audio-to hear; Taurus – the bull






My mother although with limited schooling enjoyed reading 

In the country

No library

Take book she finished to church meeting and return with another.

All were well worn and cannot remember my mother having a new book


One Christmas, I believe it was in 1938, I received several books as gifts

Hard to get me out to do the chores; had my head in a book until they were all read



Importance of literacy


Those of us who read, take literacy for granted; you know the facts better than I

So I will not repeat them


Literacy is a crucial 

Key to coping and to a productive and satisfying life

Importance is far beyond the economic impact on our communities

The key to success in school and to a good education is literacy 


We confuse -  education and schooling

They are not the same

educators perpetuate a myth

Schooling is what you learn in school – the acquisition of knowledge

Education is the application of knowledge  what you do with what you know


Some of the best educated people I know have limited formal schooling


My wife commented recently about the daughter of a friend who was having difficulty finding permanent employment.


“She has a good education”

How do you know

Graduate of UPEI and a masters Degree from …


I understand, but how do you know that she has a good education?



We speak of our education system but I’m not sure we have one

We do, however, have a school system

I often think that Departments of Schooling’ is a more appropriate term than Department of Education


In health – Family doctors

Specialists

Pharmacies

Special clinics – screening

Blood pressure stations in drug stores

Home remedies

Walk-in clinics

Visiting nurses

Associations and interest groups

Newspaper columns

Health education campaigns

And of course we have hospitals



In education – But in education, everything is school based

Instruction

Parent interviews

Libraries 

Guidance counselors

Education specialists

Coaching


What do we have outside our schools

Book stores

No regular newspaper columns

Local libraries

Science fairs


Why not vocabulary clinics

Writing workshops

Test your listening skills


There is an incentive to staying well without going to the hospital


Not so in education


In fact there is a disincentive to learning outside the school  certification


Certification has become a barrier to a more open education system  

Even if you learn outside the school you are required to go to school to have it validated



Schools are intended as places to help people learn


Schools also sort pass and fail

Grade and don’t grade

Get a certificate or not

Get an A, or a B or a C


Sorting interferes with the learning process.


And it is a conflict of interest.



If ill and you get better that’s great

Hospitals do not issue certificates to say you are healthy

Your doctor doesn’t certify that you to be healthy

If you need a health certificate, you go to  a doctor other than your family doctor

And your doctor does not publicly rank you in terms of your health



You can add significantly to your education outside the school – and you may even perform better

But it doesn’t count

You need to have a certificate

Only schools issue education certificates.

So you are stymied


There is a conflict of interest when the institution that teaches 

also sorts or certifies.


I think that sorting decisions actually  interfere with learning 


If schools must sort, and I don’t think they should,

At least remove the teacher from the process

Make sorting a separate function.


Sure, Schools need to measure in order to provide better instruction

But Schools should not be in the sorting business.


If sorting is necessary, set up an agency to do it




Again, thank you for the invitation

And keep up the good work
