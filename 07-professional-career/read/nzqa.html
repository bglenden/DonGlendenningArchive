Don's notes on New Zealand's National Qualifications Framework development, examining why NZQA emerged: gaps in recognized qualifications, unclear linkages between qualifications, unclear training access, non-recognition of prior learning, inconsistent terminology, and international mobility needs. Key principles include simplification, open learning pathways, lifelong learning emphasis, and performance-based assessment. The framework contains eight levels covering age six upward, using Units of Learning that may count toward multiple qualifications. Units include title, credit rating, performance criteria, and accreditation processes. Schools are accredited emphasizing teaching quality management, monitored by national staff. Learners receive records showing achieved units and earned certificates/diplomas.

The development of a national qualifications framework in New Zealand arose from the fact that:


· Some areas of learning had no nationally recognized qualifications;

· There was no obvious linkage between many of the qualifications and others · People were unclear where to find training to meet desired qualifications;

· Existing qualifications frequently did not recognize knowledge and skills already gained;

· Titles anctJDi1innology of certificates and diplomas have not been used consistently; and · Natinally rciognized certificates should help New Zealanders if they wish to work overseas.


The key principles of the National Qualifications Framework are:


· It is desigsned to be easily understood and greatly simplifiesssssssssssssssssssssssss the range of career-related and academic qualifications that existed previously;

· It is designed to open pathways to earning. As learners progress they carry with them their previous learning which may be credited to a range of qualifications.

· It promotes lifelong learning. The primary focus is to meet the requirements of the learner. In doing so, no disticnction is drawn between general education and career-srelated learning.

· Assessment focuses on learner performance which is judged against published national standards. The use of clear standards in assessment allows flexibility in learning.
