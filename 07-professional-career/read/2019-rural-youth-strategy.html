Proposal for Employment Skills Centre targeting rural youth in Souris. Program elements include literacy, numeracy, self-management, relationships, IT, and fabrication technologies. Emphasizes practical, hands-on, employment-related instruction using on-the-job training and community-based, competency-based curriculum with performance-based rating scales. Three-person local board provides governance. Shows specialized focus on youth employment skills within broader education initiative. Demonstrates Don's attention to specific demographic needs.


Rural Education/Youth Strategy/Demonstration

Employment Skills Centre

Souris/Eastern Kings, PEI

Souris/EStrategy

Background:

Souris is an urban centre in Eastern Prince Edward Island; it has a population in 2016 of 1,053; with additional 2745 people living in the immediate surrounding area. Together they make a community one of 3,798 people. The economy of the area is dominated by seasonal industries of fishing, aquaculture, agriculture and tourism. A recent of the area revealed an interest and needs for additional continuing education services in the area. In response, Education 20/20 Inc proposes a local Education Services Agency to encourage and support education services for residents of the area
