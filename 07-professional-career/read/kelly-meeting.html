SOURIS PROPOSAL-1-C

Meeting with Ella Kelly, Skills PEI, Montague Office


  • Background;  I submitted a  paper, but not an actual proposal, to Skills PEI on December 11, 2017.  The idea being to assess continuing education needs especially for out of school young people.  I proposed surveys of current assets, employers, key informants and students. 

  • On Monday, I met with Ella Kelly, SkillsPEI in Montague to discuss the paper; there is an interest in such a project. Funding would come through the Labour Market Partnerships Program and, as far as I can see, cover all costs; I do not know if it is in the form of  contract or grant.


  • I have the application form to which we attach a proposal; we need to  sharpen objectives, traditional budget, deliverables, etc.
  • SkillsPEI interest is in adults transitioning to work but any study would have broader implications
  • We discussed a 3-4 month project but could be greater or longer depending on changes to the project
  • We couldn’t enter an agreement with Skills PEI and then contract out  to another agency 
  • Options:
  • Take contract ourselves – hire a director to direct and manage the work.  We would need admin support but that would/could be included in budget.
  • Another agency take the project and we provide technical advice.


  • On reflection:
  • Perhaps we should consider both “training and Technology Needs for Souris Youth”. I say this because “technology” arose during our first meeting in Souris and, second, There I some evidence that “upgrading is improved when linked to practical technology.
  • I also see the possibility of a “DACUM—like” analysis of skills being useful
  • In addition to a small committee to drive the project, perhaps we should think of a larger consultative council in Souris that could meet a couple of times.
