A comprehensive statement of Don's educational philosophy articulated through 24 key operating beliefs. These beliefs reflect his conviction that learning is personal, that teachers should facilitate behavioral change, that education and schooling are distinct, and that governments should encourage and support (not operate) education systems. This document captures the core of Don's lifelong educational thinking.

KEY OPERATING BELIEFS


Belief - a principle, proposition or idea accepted as true


Key beliefs:

  1. Learning is a personal experience

  2. Most people can learn skills, knowledge and attitudes needed to live and work in a modern economy

  3. Learning rates vary

  4. Education is a process of changing behaviour.

  5. Assessment criteria should reflect those used in the community served

  6. Changes in behaviour are demonstrable through new skills, increased knowledge or changed attitudes.

  7. The role of a teacher is to help students change behaviour;

  8. Teachers design experiences to bring about changes in learners.

  9. Teaching is a developmental process

  10. People are educated whether or not they attend school

  11. Grading is a school function, not an education function

  12. Credentials and qualifications are not the same

  13. Our future as individuals and communities depend, to a large degree, on the nature and extent of education services available to each citizen.

  14. Governments invest in education for two reasons (1) to help each citizen develop personal capabilities and (2) to ensure that the community has skills needed to service the community.

  15. Each learner has own combination of traits based on genetics, prior learning and aspirations

  16. Learners are responsible for their own education

  17. Although groups do not learn, individuals may learn in a group setting.

  18. Learning results in a change of behaviour.

  19. Teachers teach subjects and infer that students have the skills that parents asked for.

  20. Report cards should report on what a student has learned

  21. The school system lacks a self- correcting mechanism (Choice)

  22. Role of a government in education is to encourage, support and regulate the delivery of education services

  23. Education system and school system are not synonymous

  24. Schools should focus on the development of learners not just the delivery of services

  25. Should encourage and support independent learning

  26. Ranking f students is not an education function
