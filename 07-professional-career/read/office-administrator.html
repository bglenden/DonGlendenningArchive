Office Administrator


For your consideration; unfortunately, I could not find my signed copy of her contract but whatever you say should focus on the description.



I would open with such positive words as


Working for a single person volunteer organization is not easy

Conflicting priorities

Little money

Operating on a shoe string

Time-consuming drop-ins

Imprecise or incomplete directions and details from directors

Many of us ask you to do things beyond the description

You have skills beyond the job requirements and probably more interesting.  


We judge you on your office skills,  that is our priority

You do many other things but our priorities  don’t get he priority we want.

Your priorities and our priorities are somewhat out of sync




You are 

Articulate

Present yourself well

Knowledgeable about CFPEI

Great resource

Take initiative

Peer to be more comfortable in the role

Put in efforts well beyond the call of duty


Position calls for good knowledge of office operation

You seem to get stalled by quirky computers, a balky copier,???

Could be more effective if you became more familiar with various office applications

Use help when offered but don’t see you initiating request for help

Not sure that you claim the office as your own or see it as a place to work.

Your legal training may get in the way of getting things done quickly







Planning and Scheduling


A lot of files and loose material around you, probably spend time looking for things

Filing every day would probably help

Handle pieces of paper too often.

Tend to give the telephone more attention than it deserves. – the phone controls you rather than you controlling the phone

Tend to say, I’ll do that later rather than doing it now

Office does not strike one as an efficient office – too cluttered

Long delays in completing tasks – last newsletter, letter to US citizens, reorganizing files


Improvements


Quite a few technical errors, mis-numbered documents, incorrect meeting dates, incomplete Board documentation

You’ve tidied up the office a bit

Seem more at ease when Directors come in


Summary


Need to:


Respond more timely to board priorities

Set realistic dates when a project will be completed.

Let Directors know when you cannot meet a deadline

Review your efficiency in handling work

Upgrade your computer skills
