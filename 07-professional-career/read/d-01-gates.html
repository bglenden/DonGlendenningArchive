This ambitious proposal to a major philanthropic funder (Gates Foundation implied) articulates a comprehensive vision of education system transformation. Don argues that education improvement requires three foundational changes: increased public engagement through unaligned organizations (like those prevalent in health), market-based delivery systems enabling school choice and teacher innovation, and continuous progress models replacing age-based cohort systems. He critiques the current system as bureaucratic, inflexible monopoly failing to accommodate individual learner needs or employ customer service principles. The proposal argues for early childhood as distinct from education, expanded life skills focus, and structural separation of instruction from assessment/credentialing. This document represents Don's most ambitious systemic reform articulation.

Potential Funder


Dear Sir:


This letter comes to introduce an idea which will exert a long-term positive impact on public education.  In saying this, I refer to the establishment of a mechanism that will increase both public information and public engagement in the education field; I am speaking of a public unaligned organization of the kind so prevalent in the field of health, but practically non-existent in education.


The nature and quality of our education will, to a large extent, determine our future as individuals, communities and countries and, while most countries and education systems, are instituting changes, the changes fail to deal with a number of underlying  weaknesses in the system.  I am of the view that the long-term improvement of education requires:

 

  • Increased public engagement


Even a cursory examination of education reveals that the system, especially when compared with health, is a cumbersome, bureaucratic, inflexible monopoly than can accommodate individual learner needs only with great difficulty; the concept of good customer relations is a foreign concept.   Sources of information are generally aligned with existing providers of educational services (Home and School, etc  ) rather than with  the sector as a whole.  Educators writing for the public are non-existent, at least in our community, documentaries deal with the operation of schools rather than with teaching and learning,   


  • A delivery system based on normal market principles


It can be demonstrated that competition or choice in a sector improves both products and service.  By choice, I refer to the ability to choose ones school, allowing schools to develop specialties such as the arts or sciences, allowing a significant ortion of the education budget “follow the student”, and even allowing teachers to open clinics outside a school.   In addition, teachers’ salaries are based on credentials rather than qualifications and do not acknowledge increased pay for quality teaching.

All of the above need urgent attention, however, I believe that more and better information and  greater engagement of our citizenry is the place to begin.  Consequently, this letter comes this letter comes on behalf of an emerging group of Islanders who want to change the dynamics of education by engaging the public in improving public education.  We propose to:


  • Increase the range and level of information and opinion available to the public through media releases, public presentations, columns, regular newsletters,  
  • Encourage and support open and regular public discussion groups in local communities on issues of concern to our citizenry and of importance for educational change. 


While the initiative is driven by volunteers, we recognize that an organization is required to provide the necessary support

Our focus is on improving education for Islanders of all ages regardless of when, where, or by whom training and education is provided.  It breaks new ground, certainly in PEI, and likely in Canada; as far as we can ascertain, most, if not all existing organizations, are aligned to some element of the school system.  That is not to suggest that they are biased but only that their focus is limited.  We recognize the likelihood, however, that the movement will spread.

In this initiative we are working closely with the Community Foundation of Prince Edward Island.  The Foundation is very supportive and has offered to receive and receipt donations and to provide at least a temporary administrative home.
