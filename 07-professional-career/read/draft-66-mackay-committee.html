Community Foundation News

No. , 2007


MacKay Fund Committee


The purpose of the David A MacKay Endowment Fund is to make grants to improve the cardiac emergency response system on PEI.


The Agreement provides for a committee to advise the Foundation on the criteria to be used in making grants. The first meeting was held on Friday, March 23. In attendance were: Heather MacDonald, Chair of the Foundation Grants Committee, Kay Lewis,



Selection will be based on its ability to meet and exceed the following criteria.

  1. Enhance cardiac response system on PEI

  2. Support accepted best practice in emergency cardiac care

  3. Support care at any point in the emergency cardiac care continuum from first response pre-hospital care to in hospital care

  4. Support emergency cardiac care in addition to what is provided by current operational budgets or funding sources

  5. May be a one time project to support initiatives such as education, training or equipment to enhance emergency cardiac care

  6. If it is a pilot project, a business case for sustainability of the initiative must be presented

  7. Preference will be given to development projects that are enduring and sustainable opportunities reflective of the intent of the endowment

  8. Proposal must contain an impact analysis as to benefits and potential risks, implementation plan and resources required.

  9. The significance of the impact as well as the number of people impacted will be considered in selection

  10. The Community Foundation and MacKay Endowment Fund must be acknowledged as the funding source in any promotional material on the project.


Proposals may be directed to:

The Grants Committee

CFPEI

Queens Square place, Suite 105
