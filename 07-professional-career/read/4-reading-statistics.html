READING STATISTICS


Reading: Magnitude of Problem


The magnitude of the problem

  • 42 percent of the adult population does not possess level 3 literacy – the level thought to be needed to take full advantage of opportunities present in the global economy.  
  • IALSS Study “Literacy rate did not change between 1994 and 2003
  • Globe and Mail Sept 15, 2009:children who aren’t reading well by the end of Grade 3 are at risk of dropping out or failing to graduate
  • Globe and Mail Sept 15, 2009: estimates that by the end of Grade 6, at lest 30 percent of Canadian students can’t read or write well enough to succeed in school
  • New York Times, March 4, 2011: “About three-quarters of the 17,500 freshmen at the community colleges this year have needed remedial instruction in reading, writing or math, and nearly a quarter of the freshmen have required such instruction in all three subjects. In the past five years, a subset of students deemed “triple low remedial” — with the most severe deficits in all three subjects — has doubled, to 1,000.”
  • Many articles in the Guardian
  • Many grandparents have told me that they have paid or are paying for private instruction in reading
  • That should not happen – unless a specialist says that the child cannot learn to read
