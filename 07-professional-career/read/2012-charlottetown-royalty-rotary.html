Charlottetown Royalty Rotary

Mentor Award

November 21, 2012

President Catherine, Rotarians, Friends All

Thank you, Norman, for your kind and generous words, and thanks to the Charlottetown Royalty Rotary Club for the honour you have bestowed upon me.

While my mother taught me many things, how to respond to this kind of generosity was not one of them.  All I know to say, therefore, is thank you, thank you, thank you.

While the award is given in my name, I would like to recognize the many people who enabled whatever I was able to do.  

  • First on the list is my wife, Carol, who tended our home and cared for our two children while I did my thing.
  • Then there were the many colleagues at Holland College, at the Community Foundation, and other organizations with whom I worked.  Among my list would be literally hundreds of Islanders who served in a variety of advisory roles in the development of Holland college programs.

In 1968, we were living in Ottawa when I came to the conclusion that it was time for a change.  Two opportunities came to my attention, one in the west coast and the other in the east, and both were to bring a new college on stream.  An offer came first from the west; it didn’t fit right, I turned it down.  Then an offer came from PEI.  I accepted.  My wife often remarked that she knew, when I returned from my interview in Charlottetown, if offered, that was the position I would accept.  The Island was welcoming and I was impressed with the College Planning Committee and their commitment to the challenge of providing an alternative form of education for the youth of PEI. 

At the time, we thought that we would be here for 3-5 years and then move along.  But the Island has a way of taking contro, and here we are some 45 years later

As I have said before, I am in awe of the generosity of Islanders, and as a past Rotarian, I know of at least some of your community activities.  I commend and thank you; our community is the richer for your many contributions.

Again, I thank the Charlottetown Royalty Rotary Club, for the Mentor award and through you, the people of Prince Edward Island for your welcome and support.
