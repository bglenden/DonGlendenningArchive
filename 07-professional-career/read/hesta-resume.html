Dr. Hesta  Arletta (Baker) MacDonald

206 Spring Park Road

Charlottetown, PEI

Born: March 28, 1924 in Falmouth, Nova Scotia


School: Falmouth Elementary

County Academy, Windsor

Provincial Normal College, Truro


School related: Valedictorian

Governor General’s Medal at Provincial Normal School

School teacher for two years


Married: Dr. Stewart MacDonald, a country doctor in Eldon, PEI; later Dr. MacDonald became an employee of Veterans Affairs Canada and the family moved to Charlottetown

Raised a family of four – Rebecca, Graeme, Alice and Kenneth


School Related: School Trustee, Eldon PEI 1958

First Provincial Education Conference, 1958

Committee to establish a Rural High School, Eldon

Home and School – various local and national offices

Chair, PEI committee on Child Abuse 

Committee on bridging to the Aboriginal Community on PEI

Brief to LeDain Commission

Taught reading to mentally retarded

Member of Educational Planning Unit, Department of Education

Member of First Unit School Board, Unit 3, Charlottetown, PEI

First Elected Chair, Holland College, Board of Governors


Community Related: Member of Charlottetown City Planning Board

Member of the Business and Professional Women’s’ Association

First Moderator of the Community Baptist Church

Lifetime Deacon of the Community Baptist Church

Member of Women’s Institute

Member of the Belfast Historical Society (Twice President)


Editor: 2006 - As I remember Them by Dr. Stewart MacDonald

2006 - Never a Dull Moment by Senator A.H. Johnston

2007 - By Land and by Air by Stewart Macdonald, DFC



Author: 2004 - Can I Tell You a Story? The Life of the Honourable Lorne Bonnell, MD, CM 

2005 - The Teddy Bear Coat – a children’s story

1973 - History of the PEI Home and School Association

Monthly article in Voice for Seniors since 1997


Awards: Governor General’s Award from PNS, Nova Scotia

Selkirk Award, Belfast Historical Society, 1989

Mentor Award, Charlottetown Royalty Rotary Club, 2001

Doctor of Laws, UPEI, 2003

First recipient of the Holland College Honourary Medal
