A Call For Restructuring

No. D-17 -C June 4, 2020

Quality:

Restructuring Called For


Our future as a Province and as citizens will be determined in a large measure by the nature and extent of the education of our citizens, yet the Province’s plan for education is unclear, at least to the writer.; let me explain.


PEI has some 160,000 citizens; The literature tells us that each person is unique and each person in a community needs to be literate and numerate, to become independent learners, to take care of themselves and learn how to work and play together. Education’s business is to help Islanders make these changes. On top of this, one must add the legal requirement, not for a level a level of education but that Islanders must attend school until they reach age 17 or 18.


Provinces recognize the importance of education and governments usually organize a department to deal with education matters. This is a complex mix of people, interests, strengths, personal and family views It is nature of services and the way in which they are organized that cause the writer’s confusion.


Name of Department: Education is an umbrella term; it includes education of all kinds and at all levels. Adding “lifelong Learning and Early Childhood Development” seem redundant.


Definitions: A glossary would be in useful.


Purpose: is about direction, what one hopes to achieve. The paper on philosophy published by the Department shows the purpose as one of providing “for the development of children so that each child may take a meaningful place in society.” New Brunswick states tts purpose more specifically as helping ”each student develop the attributes needed to be a life long learner, to achieve personal fulfilment and to contribute to a productive, just and democratic society. The degree of specificity ias important in thhhhhhhhhat directs the teaching process.


Goals: and purposes are interlinked the difference being that goals have a time frame.

“Educational services usually include information, counselling, quality assurance, materials and technology, Given the above, the current goal seems narrow indeed – and one wonders who provides other education services.


Youth under 17: Preparing to write this letter really brings home the narrowing of education services over the past 50 years; it also supports the World Bank concerns about some Ministries and Departments of Education confusing “going to school with getting an education. I find that to be the case in PEI









Structure: Structure:, Sound organization is based on the adage that “form follows function.” meaning that functions form the basis of the operating structure. That being the case one might expect to find a number of Directors reporting to the Deputy Minister:

  • Instruction

  • Information

  • Administration

  • Products and technology

  • counselling

  • , quality assurance,

  • materials and technology

  • Finance


While I do not consider myself an espert on organization, based on my experience the current ofganization will likely notbe able to deliver the education services need in the years ahead.


The Department of Education has a major role to play in ensuring that Islanders receive the help they need to cope with the present and prepare for the future. Embedding the operation en-toto into an operating Government Department, has resulted in the emasculation of professional autonomy and accountability; I suggest a review of recommendations by the OECD ( ) and discussion with senior officials would be useful in any review. The recent restructuring of Health Services may provide ideas and experiences of interest and value.






The writer struggles with Department of Education documents; descriptions don’t seem to match what I hear on the street, documents obscure rather than enlighten, words and phrases seem to carry different meanings, definitions are seldom given, concepts I thought I understood now carried different meanings. Some smart entrepreneur has even developed and promotes a “jargon generator” for education writers.
