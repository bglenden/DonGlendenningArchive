EDUCATION Issue No.C-3

20/20   A 21st CENTURY EDUCATION SYSTEM      _______________________________________________________________________It is called a Department of Education but it functions as a Department of Public Schooling; it is time to either change the name or change the function. Although I write this of Prince Edward Island, for the record, the same could be said about every Province across the country. 


Education is a broad term encompassing what we learn from many sources and in many situations.    While what we learn in school is an important part, it is not our education; we should call it what it is.  To some, this may appear to be only semantics but confusing the two limits our thinking about the services needed.


Think of our health system for example.  The hospital is only one service provider.  Depending on individual needs, one may seek help from a pharmacy, a doctor or a clinic.  We may also seek information or actual assistance from a health agency, and there are many.  Some services may even be delivered to our homes.  


Educational services, however, are delivered generally in or through schools, colleges and universities; that is the equivalent to operating a health care system through our hospitals.  It seems to me that education could benefit from some additional services that are not school based such as diagnosis, assessment, guidance and counseling, visiting teachers and specialized clinics for help with reading. I am sure that readers will think of many others.


While we must “do schooling” better, many of the shortcomings we attribute to schools would be better served by resources outside the school system..


While I commend the Minister for improvements implemented to date, I urge him to consider ways to broaden his portfolio from schooling to education.
