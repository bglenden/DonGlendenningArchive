Levels of education 22


Levels


.  The popular method is to measure one’s education by the number of years of formal study, but we all know that that is not a meaningful measure.  Some people add to that the list of ones credentials, high school graduation, Bachelors, Masters degree or Ph. D.  But the value of credentials is declining, perhaps because of the job market, the numbers of people with credentialsm and some would say because of declining standards (social promotion) on which they are granted.  Credentials, professional designations and other third party qualifications are also used as a measure of one’s education.  Readers, I am sure have their own criteria.


Determining  levels, I suggest, depends on a couple of things.  First  are the things a person knows or is able to do; the other is the level of each of ones skills


Leadership, communication skills, languages spoken


Here is an approach based on the DACUM model.  Discernable levels of education:

  • Exercise leadership in some aspects of one or more life roles
  • Functions well in most life roles with obvious special abilities in some
  • Functions adequately in life roles with minimum and occasional support from community
  • Able to function in most life roles with assistance and direction
  • Unable to function in life roles without assistance and direction
  • Unable to function in life roles
