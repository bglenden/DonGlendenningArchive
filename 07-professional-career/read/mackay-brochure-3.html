Professional Career & Consulting

What is The David A. MacKay Endowment?


The David A. Mackay Endowment Fund was established by Mr. MacKay’s family, colleagues and friends in recognition of his many accomplishments, his sterling character and his love for Prince Edward Island.  The Fund is a permanent endowment administered by the Community Foundation of Prince Edward Island.  The purpose of the David A. Mackay Fund is to make grants to improve the cardiac emergency response system on Prince Edward Island.




Who was David Arthur MacKay?



David Arthur MacKay, (1932-2000?) a son of Prince Edward Island was a  successful  businessman, an excellent judge of character, a builder who valued friendship and was a loyal friend to all.  He loved his family, loved life, loved PEI and loved a good story.

 

David had a full and varied career including starting dozens of different companies.  His greatest success and main passion was the establishment of MacKay and Hughes Ltd.which was listed on the Toronto Stock exchange and became the largest fresh potato distributor in North America.






 

What will the fund do?


The David A. MacKay Fund is one of a number of funds within the Community foundation of Prince Edward Island and is a non-profit, volunteer organization administered by a board of directors which oversees permanent endowments set up by members of the community.  The Foundation is a registered charity and all donations are tax deductible inn Canada and the USA


Grants may be made for  improving public awareness,  provide training (paramedics, nurses, doctors and the general public), purchasing equipment, etc. 





How can I help?


Individuals and organizations wishing to remember Mr. MacKay and support the David A. MacKay Endowment can make a direct donation to the Foundation.  Donations are tax deductible in both Canada and the USA.  A receipt to be used when filing your income tax returns will be issued for all donations. 














How Does It Work?


Each year the Foundation will determine the amount of money available for the purpose of making grants following which projects, designed to enhance the health system, will be solicited from both the private and public sector. 


The Foundation’s Granting Committee will base its granting decisions on evaluation criteria established from time to time by the David A MacKay Endowment Fund Advisory Committee which is appointed by the Foundation and will include a representative of the PEI Emergency Nurses Association or similar organization, a representative of the PEI Paramedic Association or similar organization, and a representative of the PEI Medical Society or similar organization.  The Committee may add others as non voting members including members of the MacKay Family involved in the establishment of the Fund. The primary role of the Committee will be to set up evaluation criteria to be used by the Foundation for the selection of projects to support.   



Fund Advisors


The establishment of the David A. MacKay Endowment was led by John Robinson, President of the Community Foundation and long-time friend and David Mackay and the Mackay Family, and with the help of Kay Lewis, a nationally recognized health care planner living on Prince Edward Island
