A Youth in Philanthropy (YIP) program document describing how young people aged 15-18 learn philanthropic decision-making and community leadership. Includes poignant rhetoric about "shoes" of community leaders and training youth to "fill your shoes tomorrow." This reflects Don's vision of succession planning and youth engagement in civic leadership on PEI.

Youth in Philanthropy



Shoes: How many pair do we need?


It’s time to talk about shoes, to be more precise, the shoes filled by our community leaders. Their shoes are large and well worn, some would even say old-fashioned, but they’ve served our community well for many a mile.


Who will step into them tomorrow?


Let’s help our young people prepare for the next step on the road to philanthropy. We offer youth the chance to practice philanthropy, to provide an opportunity today to learn more about their community, and to make philanthropic decisions. We want to train them today to fill your shoes tomorrow


Help today for tomorrow. Support the Youth in Philanthropy Fund


Better known as the Big Shoes Fund


These shoes are made for walking/working

The Community Foundation of Prince Edward Island is dedicated to improving the quality of life for all Islanders. We are a local organization with deep roots in the community, however we are part of a nationwide network, one of 168 in Canada.


With the establishment and management of funds gifted in perpetuity, The Foundation distributes the earnings for any charitable purpose including human services, arts and culture, education, environment, religion, and health and physical activity.
