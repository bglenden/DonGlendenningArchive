This proposal introduces the "math ladder" concept—a framework with clearly identified rungs representing known levels of mathematical achievement. Don argues that the practice of social promotion prevents meaningful comparison of skill levels across institutions and inhibits employers' ability to evaluate applicants effectively. The solution involves creating a mathematical ladder (comparable to music or skating certification levels) with independently assessed achievement levels. The ladder would establish common standards, aid labor mobility, and reduce curriculum development workload. Why math specifically: it has identifiable structure (numeracy through infinite series) but lacks widely accepted competency analyses and level agreements.
