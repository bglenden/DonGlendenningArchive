Date: October 27, 2004


To: Ken Gillis


From: Don Glendenning


Subject: IAHSP


I finally am back to focusing on IAHSP and a few days ago picked up packages from Katy and started to make my calls and follow-ups.  Triggered in part by Harry’s call and our discussion, I spent most of Wednesday morning with Katy reviewing a range of IAHSP matters but I would like to focus this memo on Fund Raising.  Since talking with Katy, I reviewed the tracking list and progress to date.


It appears that our FR activities were/are almost at a halt – and time is short.  Some notes are not dated and, therefore, I cannot tell just how much recent activity is taking place.  Having said that, however, I was more encouraged than I expected to be and I am confident that, with some extra effort, we could reach our $200,000 in the next few weeks.


I know from our recent conversation, Ken, that you are relying heavily on Katy to carry the bulk of the remaining fund raising.  Katy can play a role but the key to success, I believe, is your follow-up with key players and new contacts with a few others.  Katy can make some calls but is too new to the business and not well enough known by decision-makers to pull it off.  I know that this is a busy time for you with your civic responsibilities now compounded by family concerns in Cape Breton.


To cut to the chase, here is a proposed strategy:


You direct your efforts to tidying up two of the categories in Katy’s list – support promised no amount given and fall schedule

Katy activate Marion, Jeannette and I to do our follow-ups and finish our commitments.

Katy and I review, and likely add to, our list of individuals who deserve a visit and decide who can/will make the call.

Prepare for a letter writing campaign to folk who we will not visit but who, collectively, could put us over the top.  With Ron Irving’s help, we could probably put a list together fairly quickly but Katy may need help to gather addresses.  We would not mail letters now but we could be ready for a mail out campaign say next spring.

Find a volunteer, Ron Irving (?), to lead us in our approach to off-Island foundations

 Review our strategy for off-Island corporations.


While all of these need to be done, I suggest that we concentrate on the first three; we have a 5-6 week window of opportunity.  I would thinj that 20 hours each for you and I and fewer hours for Marion and Jeannette should make a major difference in our situation.  It will be great to have the matter behind us.


Just some ideas for your consideration.


cc: Katy
