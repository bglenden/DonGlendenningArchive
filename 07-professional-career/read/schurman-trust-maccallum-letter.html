Don Glendenning provides detailed legal commentary on a 1973 trust agreement for the Minor Hockey Council of Prince Edward Island and reviews proposed transfer to the Community Foundation. His analysis addresses compliance, permissibility, and recommendations for improving clarity in the new agreement.

12/06

Hi John,

Further to our exchange of messages yesterday, I confirm that I have
reviewed the original trust agreement which established the trust in
1973 ("the 1973 agreement"), and I have reviewed the draft document you
provided, which it proposed to transfer the entire trust to the
Community Foundation of Prince Edward Island.  You have requested that I
review and comment on same on your behalf alone as a trustee to this
trust fund.  My comments are as follows:

1.  It is permissible for the trustees to transfer the trust funds in
the manner proposed as section 4 of the 1973 agreement states, in part:

" ... the Trustees may in their absolute discretion, provided that all
Trustess agree, and with the consent of the
Lieutenant-Governor-in-Council, at any time or times, pay to or for the
use or benefit of the Minor Hockey Council of Prince Edward Island the
entire capital or such part or parts of the capital of the trust funds
as they deem advisable provided that such funds are used by the Minor
Hockey Council of Prince Edward Island for the purposes set out in
section 3 hereof......"

Section 3 of the 1973 agreement states the purpose the said trust funds
are to be used for:

" ... and shall pay the net income derived therefrom to the Minor
Hockey Council of Prince Edward Island to be used by the Council in its
discretion for improvement of the Minor Hockey Program on Prince Edward
Island covering all classifications up to and inlcuding juvenile
classification and provided such expenditures are within the aims and
objectives of the Prince Edward Island Minor Hockey Council."

Accordingly, the transfer is appropriate if the purpose is complied
with and continued.  This should thus be clear in the new agreement, and
isn't really (see proposed section 3). I would suggest adding some type
of preamble, or declaratory provision added (perhaps add on to proposed
section 3?) to the new agreement confirming that the trustees are now
desirous of transferring the capital of the trust fund to CFPEI, and
that the trustees have the authority to do so and are doing so pursuant
to section 4 of the 1973 agreement, and for the continued purpose set
out in section 3 of the 1973 agreement.

2.  The consent of the LGIC is required for this transfer.  I would
presume, John, that this means obtaining and EC Order, or decision at
least.  I'd also add a provision to the body of the new agreement
confirming this consent, as well as the consent of the MHCPEI, rathering
than simply having them both sign at the end of the document.

3. What happens to the capital if CFPEI is ever wound up?  Perhaps this
is covered off in the Statement of Investment Policies and Procedures,
to be attached as Schedule A, which I don't have a copy of.

4.  I'd like to see a release clause added whereby you, on behalf of
the Province, released from any and all future obligations and liability
with respect to the trust funds.  I'm not sure how the CFPEI would feel
about executing such a release, but the beneficiary, MHCPEI, should find
this acceptable since it is already consenting to the transfer.  Perhaps
the other two trustees would also be desirous of such a provision being
added as well.

5.  I'm not certain what the section 8 in the new agreement is
referencing but I assume is has already been determined that the MHCPEI
is a qualified recipient and thus is eligible to recieve the annual
grants?

6.  The new agreement needs witness lines for execution for all except
Government, and I'd put the date at the top if all parties are signing
at different times?

Those are my thoughts at this point John, please let me know if you
would like me to review your final document once agreed to by all other
parties.  Thanks, enjoy the day.

best regards,

Janice
