SCHOOL STANDARDS – 101


Exit Expectations – the Last Test


We’ve established schools as a resource for people who want to be better in the roles they will fill in adult life in their communities, that is better individuals, learners, producers, consumers, family members and citizens.  Schools provide a protective and supportive environment where learners are allowed and encouraged to improve existing skills and learn new ones.  It’s a tall order.  


Traditionally schools organize instruction around disciplines; students are required to gain credits in a variety of subjects in order to graduate from high school including language arts, mathematics, science, social studies, art, physical education and health, technology and some electives.  With aknowlede of a range of disciplines we infer that students will have the needed skills.  



An alternative, and a better way, is to identify key skills and, based on performance, to infer that they rest on a sound foundation.  The ability to communicate comes quickly to mind and, in the writer’s view, is key to success in all life roles.





Schools certainly can provide such help but schools cannot be everything to everybody.  Choices will be required.



The State of Michigan, on the other hand, states their high school graduation requirements as (State of Michigan on August 22, 1990):

 

Students will be proficient in listening, speaking, reading, writing, mathematics, and the use of technology. 

Students will apply knowledge, concepts, and processes acquired through the study of the arts, sciences, and social sciences. 

Students will demonstrate a healthy self-esteem. 

Students will select and use thinking skills, individually and in groups, to solve problems and to make decisions. 

Students will be able to find, evaluate and use information. 

Students will demonstrate respect and co-operation within a community of individual and cultural differences. 

Students will demonstrate personal, social, civic, and environmental responsibility. 

Students will be self-directed learners who are adaptable to change. 

Students will demonstrate a positive work ethic.
