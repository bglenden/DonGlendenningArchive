ABOUT LEARNERS


Learner – a person engaged in learning 


We are all learners, you’re a learner, I’m a learner, your family members are learners, as are parents and grandparents, your neighbours and your colleagues at work. 


So, we are all learners – but no two learners are exactly alike. Learners differ in a number of ways.



First, we differ because of what we call prior conditions


Prior Conditions:

  • Physical
  • Mental
  • Emotional
  • Social wellness


We carry some mixture of these conditions into our new learning situation. They likely impact on our new learning; in fact, or prior conditions may have to be dealt with before new learning can take place.

Prior learning:

  • Reacreational activities
  • Team  or alone
  • Camping
  • Drive a car
  • Play music


But we also carry the results of our previous experiences, experiences arising from our yesterday, and previous years – our prior learning.  In one sense it results from the culture or circumstances in which we grew up: siblings or no siblings, country or city, warm or cold climate, a western or eastern country  - and the list goes on.

Imagined future:

  • Ambition
  • Career choice
  • Interests
  • Choices
  • Values


A third difference, but one we seldom think of, is our imagined future, our daydreams, our ambitions, our interests – where we imagine ourselves to be, to be like or to be doing at some future time – whether it be a nurse, a poet, an astronaut or the Prime Minister.
