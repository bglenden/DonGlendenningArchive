Don's 2007 letter supporting nomination of Dr. Marlene Bryenton for Earth Day Hometown Heroes Award, praising her environmental stewardship and community foundation work—demonstrates Don's civic engagement and volunteer advocacy.





62 Queen Elizabeth Drive

Charlottetown, PEI, C1A 3A9

March 27, 2007



To Whom it May Concern:

Earth Day Hometown Heroes, 

111 Peter Street, Suite 503, Toronto,
Ontario, M5V 2H1. 


Earth Day Hometown Heroes Award


I am pleased to support the nomination of Dr. Marlene Bryenton for the Earth Day Hometown Heroes Award.


For a some time now, I have been aware of the personal efforts and drive of Dr. Bryenton in supporting many community projects in Prince Edward Island, one of which was to upgrade and maintain one of our city parks – the Joseph A.Ghiz Memorial Park.


While President of the Community Foundation of Prince Edward Island, I was approached by Dr. Bryenton who wanted to set up a fund to ensure the future of the Park.  This was unusual; most of us work on projects and when our terms are finished we move to another.  Not Dr. Bryenton.  She wanted to ensure the sustainability of the Park that graces one of the entrances to the city.  In addition, it is a move, albeit a small one, towards the improvement of our environment.


Not being a person of means, Dr. Bryenton organized a great variety of events and activities to raise funds; her objective was to establishment an endowment of $25,000 in 3 years.  She has now surpassed the $15,000 mark and I know that she will reach her final  goal as well.


During the past two years I have observed her in operation; she brings a focus, dedication, commitment, level of energy and passion not common among volunteers.


I am pleased to support her nomination. 


Sincerely




Donald Glendenning  C.M.
