A persuasive fundraising letter dated February 20, 2019, from Don Glendenning to Robert Irving, President of Cavendish Farms, requesting financial support for the Numeracy Project. Don articulates the problem: while PEI math test scores have risen nationally, practical numeracy skills on the shop floor remain inadequate. He proposes a $15,300 project involving a two-day brainstorming session with external facilitators to identify numeracy skills needed for everyday Island living. The letter demonstrates Don's strategic thinking about applied mathematics education and his networking within the business community.


February 20, 2019

Mr. Robert Irving, President

Cavendish Farms

100 Midland Drive

Dieppe. N.B. E1A 6X4


Dear Mr. Irving:

Mathematics on PEI


Although PEI math scores on National and international tests have risen, friends tell me that math has not improved on the shop floor; I’ve been wondering why.


Islanders need math; citizens may not need a high level but they need to be able to measure, sort, calculate, group and estimate numbers, space, time distance, etc. and they need to do it quickly, easily, accurately; and consistently. This approach to math is called numeracy and it involves the application of math to every-day lives. Numeracy is being adopted elsewhere; usually in addition to traditional math.


But we need more; to begin with we need to identify math skills used by Islanders in their every- day lives. Making an analysis involves bringing together a small group for a two- day of brainstorming session. It’s a proven technique, we used it regularly at Holland College but apparently there is no one on PEI now with the expertise. We’ll have to bring someone in at a cost $15,300. The making of an analysis is described in the attached paper.


We need help with the project. Although we are a registered charitable organization, we have opened an account with The Community Foundation of Prince Edward Island, 121 Queen Street, Charlottetown, PE, C1A 4B3, the Foundation will accept donations on our behalf and issue charitable receipts; in fact the product may also be of interest to your HR people.


I appreciate your consideration and would be happy to answer any questions. Thanks in advance


Sincerely,



Don Glendenning

President
