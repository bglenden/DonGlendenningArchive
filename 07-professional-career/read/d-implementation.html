Detailed implementation paper (March 28, 2015) translating seven policy recommendations into actionable steps. A Call- implementation


Occasional Paper D-1-C March 28, 2015

1) Recommendation - Develop a “clear and compelling” vision for education on PEI.


Rationale.

  • Comprehensive statement does not currently exist

  • Current philosophy only applies to schools

  • One size fits all

  • Adults and preschool children not included

  • Statement need to give overall direction

  • Needs to reflect current educational thinking


Implementation

  • Recognize that education is intended to increase ones capacity in one’s life roles

  • Prepare a new document

  • Engage the public

  • Small working group

  • For public use

  • Distribute widely

  • Not a study or commission

2) Recommendation - Develop a learner centered service model that provides support in all domains

to learners as, when and where needed.


Rationale

  • Currently a compliance model

  • Current system is time based

  • System is knowledge based; Education is developmental

  • Outcomes should be learner centered

  • Lacks criterion referenced assessment,

  • Schools should do formative evaluation only

  • Flexible scheduling,

  • Greater autonomy and accountability for teachers.

  • Stage based levels

  • Current model does not address learner needs


Implementation

  • Allow challenge for credit

  • Write exams at any time

  • Replace age based grading with staged based grading

  • Define teacher roles

  • Credit for independent learning

  • Math for all at the beginning of each day


3) Recommendation - Government assume a leadership role in creating a learning culture

and providing a quality education for every Islander.

Rationale

  • currently lacking, and education suffers as a result.

  • Operating and planning role in conflict

  • The role to include planning, setting priorities, funding, monitoring, quality control, ensuring the availability of professional resources.

  • An increased role in delivering support to families with pre- school children, continuing education classes and even in providing information and services to children and their parents

  • Politicization

  • Referee/coach model


Implementing

  • Pilot projects

  • Alternative paths

  • Management review


4) Government delegate the role of providing instruction


Rationale

  • School operation politicized

  • Bureaucratic

  • Decision making only at high levels

  • Teachers lack autonomy to make professional decisions

  • Position descriptions


Implementation

  • Delegate to School boards, schools or municipalities

  • Expand instructural authority to community agencies, professionals in private practice and learners themselves.

  • Expand role of libraries as study centres

  • Consider community based essential skills centres


5) Recommendation 5 - Establish a qualification body for the purpose certifying individual levels

of achievement to persons wanting or needing such documentation.


Rationale

  • Ranking and establishment of levels is not a teaching function;

  • When a teachers rank their students they are putting their ability to teach at risk;

  • Assigning student grades, selecting students for prizes and awards and awarding diplomas and degrees is a sorting process and should not be part of a teacher’s role.

  • Apprenticeship with certification through a Provincial Department of Labour and music through the Royal Canadian Music Conservatory are examples of a preferred arrangement.

  • Establish levels of achievement in each field not related to age of learner

  • Disconnect entrance requirements from school studies


Implementation

  • Start with essential skills

  • Atlantic qualification authority

  • Competency based




Recommendation 6 - Establish a professional model of service delivery where professionals are directly accountable to clients for the nature, extent and quality of service provided.


Rationale

  • Teachers should be able to dignose, help and manage

  • Teachers need a close professional-client relationship withstudents

  • Teachers need professional autonomy

  • Teachers should be accountable for student progress

  • Allow teachers to set up specialized clinics

  • Arizona exchanged 7 teaching positions for 14 teaching ssistants and results improved dramatically

  • School without a principal in Michigan

  • English school – all students study math at 9:00 am


7) Establish an innovation Strategy


  • Rationale

  • Incentivize

  • Innovation tends to be administrative

  • Incentive to continually approve

  • Autonomy is a form of incentive


Implementation

  • Set up a separate innovation fund

  • External granting committee

  • Experts in residence

  • Winning

  • Schools chart own course

  • Seek proposals from schools

  • Student choice



Top Ten

  1. Update philosophy

  2. Challenge for credit

  3. Independent qualification body/separate assessment service

  4. Focus on basic skills/essential skills/

  5. Provide tutoring service

  6. Community based study centres

  7. Innovation fund

  8. Independent achievement levels
