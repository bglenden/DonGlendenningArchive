Dear Mrs. Gallant:


First let me apologize for the delay in responding.  Although retired, I seemed to have been caught up in a whirlwind of activities lately and am just now responding to my mail.  Although reading is not my specialty, I believe good reading skills are required in all the things we do.  I know that schools are working to improve the levels of reading and I am told that levels are rising, but more needs to be done and done now.  


We know generally that students benefit from a close relationship between the home and the school and this is especially true, I believe with reading.  Students need encouragement and can benefit from conversations about what subjects of interest.  Perhaps picking up the occasional sports or hot rod magazine, depending on his interests, may also encourage him.


Since there is no one teacher of reading, I suggest that you start through a discussion with his teachers.  Some schools have special reading programs for students like your grandson; you should also have a discussion with the Principal.  You should feel as free to talk to a teacher or principal as you would with your doctor about a health problem.


I would also recommend that you talk with the PEI Literacy Alliance. They are located at 161 St. Peters road, the old Ellis Brothers Shopping Centre. (Tel: 902-368-3620).  They may have some ideas and be able to put you in touch with a volunteer tutor.


I commend you for your interest in your grandson’s school studies.  Our education system would benefit if more people like yourself took an active interest in what schools do.


Again, thanks for your letter.  I only wish that I could be more helpful.  Let me know how you make out.


Regards


Don Glendenning
