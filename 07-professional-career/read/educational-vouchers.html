Educational vouchers



The idea of educational vouchers is consistent with my philosophy; having said that, I have no experience with a voucher system.  The idea of the learner/client appearing at a school and plunking down money for services to be provided is intriguing.  The concept of the same student, getting up from their seat, walking to the principal’s office before departing a school with a refund in hand is even more interesting.  


The voucher idea has been around for my entire educational career but seemed to rise in prominence in the 1970s and 80s.  I consider them still in the experimental stages.  Do they improve education?  The jury is still out.


Education vouchers involve a system whereby learners, or their parents, provides a voucher to a Scholl for part or all of the cost of providing an education.  Instead of a school receiving a g rant as part of a funding formula, the learner receives a voucher for all or part of the cost of his schooling.  The learner   chooses a school and gives the school a voucher.  The learner is a consumer, a consumer with the ability to pay.  The idea being that schools must compete for learners, that competition is good for other sectors why not for e education.  The end result is improved accountability and improved education.


A major weakness that I have observed is that vouchers have been limited to poorly performing students.  This provides a negative image of the initiative.  Like placing less academically included students in vocational education.  A true test, I believe, should provide vouchers for all.
