Expanded assessment service proposal dated November 30, 2014, building on earlier concepts. Strengthens the business case by referencing broader concerns about literacy, social promotion impacts on institutional comparability, and value of recognized assessment services for labor mobility. Clarifies conclusions regarding the need for third-party independent assessment, identifies math as the priority pilot subject, and establishes assessment priorities (employer needs, postsecondary entrance, high school graduation). Recommends establishing a working group drawn from service users. Proposals similar service characteristics and work plan to earlier version but with refined scope.
Professional Career & Consulting


Nov. 30, 2014

Assessment

EDUCATION 20/20 INC.

Assessment Service-3

Background

  1. Concerns about literacy levels of Islanders have been widely reported: PISA results, CFPEI survey, Charlottetown Area chamber, Home and School, Education 20/20 workshops, Employers concerns, PEI Literacy Alliance

  2. Of particular concern are the recent graduates whose level of basic skills prevents them from entering post-secondary studies or securing employment.

  3. The practice of social promotion puts make it difficult to compare levels across institutions

  4. School report cards lack adequate description of levels of achievement

  5. Recognized assessment service would facilitate labour mobility


Conclusions

  1. There is a need for a third-party independent assessment service on PEI

  2. While it could be useful in many subjects, discussion to date indicates that math has considerable support as a pilot initiative.

  3. Assessment priorities are:

  • Establishing a level of math that meets employer’s needs

  • Establish a level of math to meet entrance requirements of postsecondary institutions

  • Establish a level of math for high school graduation

  1. At least some of the resources already exist on Prince Edward Island to provide an assessment service (Holland College and Workplace Learning PEI)

  2. The following needs further exploration:

  • Reducing actual or perceived gap identified in the “Vital Signs” report

  • Construction of a public “math ladder” or yardstick for describing  levels of math 

  • Better documentation as to the range and level of a person’s actual math skills

  • Establishment of a math testing service

  • Establishment of a separate math testing service


Proposal

Convene a working group to explore the matter of an independent assessment service for certifying a person’s proficiency in the skills of everyday living, with an initial focus on mathematics. Working group to be drawn primarily from users of the service and include postsecondary institutions, employers, public school system and public at large.


Characteristics of Service

  • A number of levels

  • Fee for service

  • Independent

  • Open to individuals

  • Any age

  • By application

  • No school credit offered

  • Certificate not a credential

  • Results provided to person being tested

  • Copy of results forwarded to employer or educational institution on request of student


Work plan

  • Review the literature

  • Adopt or develop a system of describing math levels

  • Find or develop an instrument that measures levels
