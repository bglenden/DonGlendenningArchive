Comprehensive commentary on "Learning for Life" framework, using metaphor of life as a road with twists, hills, and rough patches. Education prepares people to navigate life roles (Individual, Family member, Student, Consumer, Producer, Citizen). Framework discusses learner differences, types of educational support (instruction, feedback, mentoring, etc.), and documentation needs. Notably references his son Brian and daughter Janet as learners with different interests, capacities, and needs—personal application of educational philosophy to family.

Notes for 

Learning for life


Visual-1 – a few people of different ages and ethnicity


Some time ago, each of us was dumped unceremoniously onto life’s road, and we’ve been travelling  that road ever since; the road itself has many twists and turns, hills and hollows, and some  rough patches as well.  


The preparation for life’s roles is called education and the better prepared we are, the better we are able to cope with all that we encounter, or create.  


To provide a framework for making decisions about education, Education 20/20 Inc. is  developing a series of webinars, short clips on a variety of subjects available at a person’s convenience.  Each presentation is in outline form and of 3-4 minutes duration; each may be viewed alone or with others. The framework is intended to:

  • help our understanding. 
  • improve the consistency of our decisions, and  
  • insure that elements are left out by design not by accident.


Because our journey along life’s road is a long one and education is complex, a few definitions may be in order:


Visual 2 - as above with a few question marks; text below will pop out


Definitions:

  • Education – any experience that has a formative effect on the way we think, feel or act.
  • Purpose of education – to help people gain the attitudes, skills, knowledge wanted or  needed in later life
  • Learning – changing the way we think, feel or act

Visual 3 – two individuals on road of life


There are many ways to build an education framework, we’ve adopted one that revolves around the learner and the learning process, here we see two learners, we’ll call one Brian and the other Janet


  • Different interests
  • Different capacities, 
  • different experiences
  • Different plans
  • Different stages 
  • Different expectations


Visual-4 – life roles emerge


As we travel life’s road, we find ourselves playing a variety of life roles begin to  emerge; often we are playing a number of roles at the same time.  We are using the term as described by the Department of Education in the State of Oregon:

  • Individual
  • Family member
  • Learner
  • Consumer
  • Citizen
  • Producer


Ot is likely that Brian and Janet will require the same along the way but not likely the same help in the same order and at the same time


Third visual  - help appears either one item at a time or perhaps as background available at any time

Help may include:

  • Information
  • Instruction
  • Feedback
  • Practice
  • Independent study
  • Reflection
  • Recognition
  • Resource materials
  • Encouragement
  • Modelling
  • Mentoring
  • Demonstrations


Visual 6 – 


In addition to education. Individuals will likely need documentation as to what they have learned; sometimes documentation provides a barrier on the road of life, especially in relation to the role of producer. 

  • Resume or portfolio (self generated)
  • Transcript issued by a teaching organization
  • Credential (diploma or degree) issued by an institution
  • Qualification issued by an authorized body
  • Letter of reference provided by a person known to individual



We hope that your experience with this series will contribute in some small way to making better decisions about education, whatever life roles you are currently experiencing.


Consider the following questions:

  • What part of one’s education should the taxpayer pay for?
  • Who should Is the award qualifications?
  • Where should the content come from?
  • Where do parents fit into this model?
