A chronological documentation of DACUM implementation at Holland College from March 1970 to 1976. Records pivotal events: first DACUM analysis for Electronics in 1970, rapid expansion across Secretarial Arts and Commercial Design, full institution adoption by September 1970, national dissemination through conferences, and integration with vocational/trade education programs. Documents Holland College's pioneering role in DACUM adoption and international influence, with facilitator Bob Adams and later Lawrence Coffin introducing methodology to Ohio educational research centers.
Professional Career & Consulting

March, 1970 First DACUM analysis (Electronics) prepared at Holland College with Bob Adams as facilitator.  Analyses quickly followed for Secretarial Arts and Commercial Design.  


July 2, 1970 Students in Secretarial Arts became the first to enrol under the DACUM model.  This included the use of the occupational analysis, modular approach to curriculum development, and student assessment and management.


Sept. 2, 1970 All new and returning students enrolled under DACUM model.  


1971 Article appeared in CV Journal about Holland College including its commitment to DACUM model.


1971 Holland College staff acted as resource people and presenters at meetings, workshops and conferences in other provinces. 


1973 Visit by staff from the Ohio Centre for Research in Vocational Education.


1974 DACUM introduced to staff of the Ohio Centre for Research in Vocational Education by Lawrence Coffin from Holland College.


1976 Holland College assumed responsibility for all trade, occupational, apprenticeship and adult education on PEI and began converting trade and occupational programs to DACUM model.


1976 Holland College instituted a DACUM based training program for its instructional staff.
