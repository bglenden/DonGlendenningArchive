Letter from Don Glendenning to the Wilmots proposing alternative healthcare workforce initiatives for Souris Hospital. Outlines five options with pragmatic assessments: (1) active recruitment by hospital to high school students; (2) Nurse Practitioner upgrading (unlikely provincial funding); (3) relocation allowance triggered by vacancies; (4) scholarships for third/fourth-year nursing students with employment commitments (chosen path); (5) LPN-to-RN upgrading (requires government/union approval). Don's letter demonstrates strategic thinking and realistic assessment of implementation feasibility. Offers to discuss further with Terry Campbell (Souris Hospital) or himself.

Wilmot


Greetings from Prince Edward Island!


A beautiful crisp clear morning with temperature hovering at freezing as of noon.


Further to our discussions with the folks in Souris, I am delighted to convey a brief proposal showing some alternative initiatives in support of health care in the Souris Hospital.   While all are possible, we feel, some have a greater likelihood of coming to fruition in a reasonable time frame.  Some comments:


The Souris Hospital intends to take a more active role in recruitment generally and promotion to students still in high school.  

The Souris area certainly could benefit from an additional Nurse Practitioner and the cost of upgrading may well be manageable.  Once trained, however, the Province would have to create a new position and provide on-going funding.  In our view, it is highly unlikely that the Province would provide this measure of financial support.

A relocation allowance is a definite option but action can only be taken when a vacancy occurs.  When a vacancy occurs, the Hospital must first advertise on PEI,  if not successful recruitment can occur elsewhere in Canada.  To have the money available when a vacancy occurs would be ideal, but its actual expenditure must be triggered by an actual vacancy.

Scholarships for students in their third and/or fourth years could easily be added to students now in training on condition that they accept employment in Souris at the completion of their training.  This, in effect, would be a top-up to an existing scholarship arrangement and presents no hiring complications.

Upgrading of an LPN to an RN is possible, and I would say desirable, however, approval would be required from both the government and the appropriate union.


The attached proposal is for your consideration.  Either Terry (: 902-687-7150) or I (902-892-5859) would be delighted to provide further information or discuss the idea further.


Warmest regards


Don
