GRADUATION REQUIREMENTS (PEI)

  • At least 20 credits, including 12 from the following subjects:

  • three (3) English credits, one of which must be a Grade 12 course (600 level)

  • two (2) math credits (post-secondary institutions may require three or more math credits)

  • two (2) science credits

  • two (2) social studies credits, one of which must include Canadian content as listed in the Senior High Program of Studies or a social studies credit approved by the Director of the English Innovation, Education and Programs as a compulsory course credit

  • one (1) physical education credit, which must be PED401A

  • one (1) career education and personal development credit, which must be CEO or CARF

  • one (1) credit identified as a creativity or innovation course, or one (1) French language credit

  • five (5) credits must be from courses designated in the Senior High Program of Studies and List of Authorized Materials as Grade 12 courses (600 or 800 level courses)

EDUCATION SERVICES


  • Information - Recent and independent information needed by Islanders in the making of de-cisions about their own education or the education of others

  • Counselling – help to people making decision about their education needs

  • Governance and Leadership - A body with vision and responsibility to put in place and moni-tor educational services for the benefit of its citizens,

  • Instruction - The provision of help to others in their learning that includes diagnosis, coun-selling, presentations, practice and access generally to resources

  • Quality Assurance - A system that assures Islanders that their learning experiences, including providers of those services, meet community standards.

  • Products and Technology - Access by Islanders to educational materials, technology and other resources to help learners develop skills needed to meet their responsibilities and ob-jectives.

  • Funding - Funding to a level and in a manner that is equitable for all Islanders.

  • Human Resources = Teachers, administrators, counsellors, advisors. assistants and others who help people learn
