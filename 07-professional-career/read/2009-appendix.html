Guidelines appendix for the Catherine Callbeck Legacy Fund establishing how grants should be directed. The appendix defines literacy broadly to include reading, writing, oral communications, numeracy, problem-solving, and computer skills. Importantly, it focuses funding on adults who have left the formal education system or haven't yet entered it, supports specific initiatives rather than ongoing operational costs, encourages partnerships and team-based approaches, and explicitly excludes research funding. These guidelines reflect Don's pragmatic philosophy of targeted, action-oriented literacy improvement.

APPENDIX B


COMMUNITY FOUNDATION OF PRINCE EDWARD ISLAND


The Honourable Catherine Callbeck Legacy Fund



Further to Clause 3 in the agreement to which this attached, the following constitute further guidelines for the making of grants:


For purposes of this agreement, literacy is broadly meant to include reading, writing, oral communications, numeracy, problem solving and computer skills.

The focus of granting is on people who have left the public system (elementary, secondary and postsecondary) or have not yet entered it.

Grants may be made for any relevant activity including the granting of bursaries.

The fund is not intended for the purpose of funding research.

Grants normally will be made in support of an initiative and not simply to support ongoing operational costs of an organization

When feasible, partnerships and teams will be harnessed.
