The best classroom in the world is at the foot of an elderly person - Anon


Performance based rating

My morning paper informs me that parents in Calgary are not very happy with report cards brought home by their children. Current report cards provide a mark or grade for each outcome; parents want to return to number grades.



My experience suggests that performance based rating is good for education and especially for formative assessment it adds a new dime It engages learners in the process, both causes and allows students to self-rate, increases conversation about progress towards goals and is an under-valued contribution to the teaching function.



Dynamic rating scales, have been around for a while, most recently as Holistic rating scales, first appeared in the 1960’s, perhaps as a part of the outcomes movement, were practiced sparingly but now seem to be on an upward trend.

















Construction workers

The need for construction workers brings back memories of the early days of Holland College. The Board of the College wrestled with the issue of using its resources to support Islanders versus developing programs in support of local employment opportunities; it favoured programs for Islanders whether their futures rested on PEI or “away”.



Public education has an academic or cognitive bias; the importance of application is not valued. But for Federal funding, Provinces would offer little in the way practical and applied learning.



For the past 100 years the Government has provided leadership for instruction in practical education spruing up in all province. Support which gave rise to vocational high schools, trade and technical institutes, and our community college system. Now, it seems, the Federal only gives money – and career preparation for work hs virtually disappeared – especially among our youth.



Don Glendenning, Editor
