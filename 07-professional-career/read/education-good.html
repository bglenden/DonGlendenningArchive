Philosophical paper examining characteristics of quality education. Identifies three purposes: human fulfillment, employment preparation, and social progress. Emphasizes quality education provides range of skills enabling function across life roles. Discusses characteristics including whole-child development, value-based learning, proficiency standards, and knowledge retention. Notes quality education teaches thinking skills, meets high standards, and provides job-specific knowledge. Critiques current assessment practices focusing mainly on cognitive outcomes measurable through pen-and-paper tests while neglecting broader social and life skills development goals. Emphasizes alignment between broad learning aspirations and assessment mechanisms.
