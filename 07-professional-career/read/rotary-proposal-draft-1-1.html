'Building Island Communities'

1. Background and Assumptions 

The Community Foundation of PEI is committed to improving the quality of life in Prince Edward Island – in all its communities. Based on our activities to date, we  believes that there are many Islanders living (or have lived) in communities all across the Island who have the capacity and are interested in leaving  a legacy to their community to support a worthy cause or service. However, there is often not a broad-based 'community-owned' method or vehicle available to them to achieve their desire to do this. While it is acknowledged that there are opportunities to make donations or leave legacies to things like the regional hospital, these institutions are not really community-owned anymore as they are managed and funded by the government for the most part.   and the remaining local charitable organizations do not have or are not perceived to have  the capacity to handle endowments in perpetuity.


Over the past year, the Foundation has begun to develop a new approach to assisting Island communities through a fund called the Community Fund. The Community Fund is intended to provide a formal mechanism for Islanders to contribute to the well being of their community through a financial donation or a legacy fund. Having such a fund allows the community to support local services and initiatives (e.g. the high school band, local artists, health support services, etc).


Our Foundation has already helped the Eastern Kings area set up such a fund, and has begun similar discussions with interested parties in both the Charlottetown and Summerside areas. The Foundation has a vision of establishing a Community Fund in all areas across the Island.

The Foundation has a vision of establishing a Community Fund in at least 10 Island communities over the next three years (2010-2012).

Overarching  Goal:

The overarching project goal is to provide Island communities with a community-owned vehicle or mechanism whereby local citizens or patrons can financially contribute to and support the well-being of their community, and to be able to do this in perpetuity if that is their desire. With the knowledge that such contributions/funds are professionally managed in erpetuity.     

Specific Objective:  

to set up a Community Fund in ten Island communities by 2012.

3. The Target Beneficiaries 

The target beneficiaries for this Community Fund initiative would be the ten Island communities; more specifically causes and/or concerns within these communities would be addressed and supported (e.g. youth leadership, needs of certain disadvantaged groups, other areas or causes that are important to a community)  and given the flexibility to grow or change to reflect future  changing community needs.

4. Project Activities and Timelines 

The process of establishing such funds will involve a series of developmental steps with each community and will be initially time consuming; hence, there will be staff time and other developmental costs associated with the process.  The key developmental steps with each community will include: 


identifying and engaging 3-4 community citizens to explore the idea of setting up a Community Fund (could involve 3-4 initial meetings).

if citizens are interested - help them establish a more formal 'committee' to oversee the establishment of the fund.

work with this formal 'committee' to:

develop the local 'terms of reference' regarding their Community Fund; e.g. specific purpose of fund, how it will be structured, how people can contribute to it, etc.

develop and 'sign off' on the formal agreement with the Community Foundation of PEI to manage the Community Fund. 

develop a 'matching' fund development structure whereby the community would commit to raising a certain amount of funds annually and this amount would be matched by the Rotary Club of Charlottetown Royalty.   

develop a local strategy to promote the fund within community itself, and to those former community members who may be 'living away' but are still interested in their 'home community'.  

identify causes and/or concerns within the community that the Fund could be used to assist in the future (e.g. youth leadership, disadvantaged groups, other areas or causes that are important to a community but are often overlooked or under-supported). 

develop a local communication strategy that provides a regular update to the community generally as to how the Fund is doing growth-wise, how it is helping enrich and support local community causes, etc.


5. Proposed Time Frame 

As noted above, the Community Foundation views this initiative to be a three year endeavor, beginning in 2010 and ending in 2012; the Foundation's goal would be to assist at least three communities establish Community Funds during each year of the project.

7. Foundation Staff 

The Foundation currently employs two staff persons on a part time basis; a General Manager who provides leadership and direction to the work of the Foundation (twenty hours per week), and an Administrative Support person (twelve hours a week). Their resumes are attached to this proposal.   

In order for the Community Foundation to fully undertake this project, additional staff time and resources are required as per the above budget in Section 6 above. 

8. Plan for Measuring Results  

The Community Foundation will establish a project review/evaluation plan that will be designed around the following elements: 

identification of key project success indicators (e.g. number of communities engaged, number of Funds established, amount of Fund, etc)

identification of data sources regarding success indicators

collection of data re success indicators

Regularly reporting of results (bi-annually) to Project Sponsor
