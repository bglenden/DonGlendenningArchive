Transition/1

TRANSITION


“A student’s ability to transition from secondary school to post-secondary education and the labour market is a key measure of the education system’s success for students” – CMEC, July 2016


The above statement by the Council of Ministers of Education Canada (CMEC) highlights a concern that is shared by many educators, employers, certification agencies and the community at large. The issue will not resolve itself and cannot be resolved by educators alone, but resolve it we must - and soon. Although not a simple issue, there appears to be some agreement that the performance level of a person in a field of study or employment is determined by:

  1. The nature and range of skills that a person can demonstrate,

  2. The level of performance at which each skill can be demonstrated,

  3. Degree of clustering, and

  4. Relevance to the planned role


Typical examples of the transition dilemma include a person who:

  1. Graduated from high school but lacks specific course(s) required for entrance to program of choice.

  2. Has high school credits for specific course(s) required for entrance to postsecondary studies but lacks a high school graduation diploma.

  3. Graduates from high school and later decides to pursue postsecondary studies but does not meet postsecondary entrance requirements.

  4. Graduates from high school but lacks essential skills to enter employment

  5. Graduates from high school, secures employment but lacks essential skills to maintain or progress in employment

  6. Does not graduate from high school and, therefore, is rejected for employment or further study even though he/she may have the required skills.


Likely contributing factors include:

  1. Communication between and among the parties involved

  2. Entrance requirements to postsecondary institutions

  3. Assessment practices

  4. Reporting practices

  5. Benchmarks

  6. Conflicting views on role of educational institutions

  7. Credentials versus qualifications

  8. Role of education and schooling
