May 2020 paper on reorganizing education services structure. Argues current system reflects "schooling not education." Proposes function-based management with six key service components: (1) Governance & Leadership (planning, liaison, advocacy, quality control); (2) Instruction (teaching, demonstration, coaching, diagnosis); (3) Information & Counselling (assessment, referral, advocacy); (4) Quality Assurance (credentials, qualifications); (5) Funding (capital, operating); (6) Learning Products (texts, technologies, MOOCs). Represents mature articulation of systems thinking about educational delivery.



STRUCTURING EDUCATION SERVICES

D 20 Restructuring May 2020

Education System – Services put in place by a community to encourage and support

the education of its citizens.


It’s time to realign our education system; it reflects schooling not education.

Structure should reflect services offered




  • Governance & Leadership – Leadership, Planning, Liaison, Support, Research’ Advocacy and Quality control

  • Instruction - Teaching/learning model, Demonstration, Practice, Diagnosis, Instructional materials, Liaison; Coaching, Record keeping and Guided practice

  • Info & Counselling – Information, Counselling , Assessment, Diagnosis’ Referral and Advocacy

  • Quality Assurance – Credential, Qualification and Proficiency levels.

  • Funding – Capital and Operating

  • Learning Products - Texts and references, Self-instructional materials, Assistive technologies, Internet, Software, MOOCs and Technology
