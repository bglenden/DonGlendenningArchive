A Proposal for funding to undertake an assessment of

Continuing Education and Workforce Development Needs

In the Souris area

Souris/6

Goal: To complete an assessment of continuing education and workforce development needs of adults in the Souris Area of Prince Edward Island


Souris

Souris is a year-round port in Eastern PEI with a population of 1,053 (Statistics Canada, 2016 Census), a decline of 10% from the 2011 Census. Compared to PEI as a whole, Souris has a smaller proportion of residents under age 15 (13.8% vs. 15.9%) and age 15 to 64 (57.6% vs. 64.7%) and a higher proportion of residents age 65 and older (28.6% vs. 19.4%). Souris also had a much lower median total household income ($42,368) compared to PEI as a whole ($61,163). Wealth generating activities are focused around tourism, farming, marine services, fishing and related food processing, and the normal trades and services of any small community. Compared to the PEI population generally, Souris had a higher percentage of residents with high school education only and a higher percentage without any postsecondary educational certification as of the 2011 Census.


Education 20/20 Consultations

Education 20/20 Inc. is a not-for-profit organization devoted to ensuring a quality education for all Canadians, including residents of Souris. Education 20/20 Inc. has met a number of times in Souris to explore the nature and extent of current education services and how, if at all, Education 20/20 could be helpful. Discussion was wide ranging, but quickly focused on needs and interests of out-of-school youth, both employed and seeking work. Consultations included Education 20/20 and residents including employers, service providers, key informants, PEI Community Economic Development Agency and representatives of SkillsPEI. We have also been in touch with several Island agencies who offer educational services that may be of interest to the area.


Continuing Education

Educational services in Souris are provided by a variety of people, institutions and organizations including Eastern Kings Early Learning Academy, Souris Regional School (K-12), Ecole La Belle Cloche (Francophone K-12) Holland College, Harmony Training Projects, Harbourview Training Centre, and an office of Career Development Services. Continuing education is also available from many sources online, but local uptake is unknown. We know that this list is not complete and plan, funding permitting, to develop a complete list of continuing education assets.


Need

Continuing education is a critical element of community, workforce, and economic development and is one factor that supports rural population recruitment and retention. While there are several providers of continuing educational services in Eastern Kings County, there is no comprehensive assessment of their offerings or the continuing education needs of residents. There doesn’t appear to be any community input into overall continuing education priorities for the region. There is no local body for planning or coordinating educational services available or needed in the area. Finally, supports to adults in the area for educational planning are limited primarily to any advice provided via career counselling services


Goal: To complete an assessment of continuing education and workforce development needs of adults in the Souris area and to recommend a strategy for delivery of recommended services


Objectives

  1. Develop an inventory of existing continuing education organizations and their services (assets)

  2. Assess the continuing education needs and interests of adults who have completed or left public school relevant to community, workforce, and economic development (all ages 18 and above)

  3. Determine the degree of match and identify gaps between available assets and continuing education needs

  4. Identify the capacity and willingness of educational organizations to meet the identified continuing education needs

  5. Determine the best strategy for service organization and delivery

  6. Develop a strategy to monitor the delivery and outcomes of continuing education in terms of community, workforce, and economic development

Approach

  • Establish a project steering committee comprised of Education 20/20 and interested government, business, community organizations and residents

  • Review available existing documentation and statistics pertaining to population statistics

  • Develop interview guides and survey instruments

  • Interview key education organizations as to services offered, willingness to add other services, and suggestions for improvement.

  • Interview key informants (government, community, business, career development) as to as to continuing education needs and suggestions for improvement

  • Interview employer, employer organizations and employee organizations as to current and projected needs for continuing education in the Souris area

  • Conduct survey of Souris area adults as to continuing education experience, interests and needs existing in the area

  • Conduct an environmental scan of innovative approaches to continuing education in rural areas

  • Develop a plan to monitor and influence future continuing education services

  • Analyze data collected and assess the march and gaps between needs and availability, and identify a strategy as to how those needs may be met.

  • Develop a plan for meeting community needs and monitoring and structure final report including sources of support.

  • Develop a report outlining methods, findings, and incorporating Steering Committee input

  • Prepare and present final report for continuing education services

  • Share results of the survey with relevant organizations, individuals and the community at large via presentations, articles, releases, on-line-posts. Etc.


Deliverables: (All reports in electronic form only)

  • Data collection instruments

  • Ongoing materials/messages to engage organizations and individuals in the project

  • Environmental scan data

  • Draft and final project report


Timelines: Four months from date of approval
