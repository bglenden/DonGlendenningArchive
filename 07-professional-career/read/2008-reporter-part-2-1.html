Summer Islanders


Summer Islanders are planning a summer lecture series again and a social event. As well, they have established an Summer Islanders PEI Legacy Fund from which grants will be made for activities of particular interest to summer islanders.


Agricultural Heritage Newsletter


The Agricultural Heritage initiative has entered a new phase with the publication of The Agricultural Heritage Newsletter, in electronic form only, Charles Coles who is recently retired from the PEI Department of Agriculture. The focus of the initiative is on private collectors of early agricultural equipment. Anyone interested in being on the mailing list should Email Charles at charlescoles@eastlink.ca or visit our website at www.cfpei..ca


Souris Health Initiative


Last summer, while on Prince Edward Island, Louise and James Wilmot indicated an interest in providing a scholarship for nurses in training on condition that they commit to a period of employment at the Souris Hospital on completion of training. The matter has been the subject of discussions with the Souris Hospital, Souris Hospital Foundation and the Department of Health. Details should be available shortly. This activity illustrates a key role of community foundations, that of bringing together people for the purpose of solving community problems.


CPR Anytime™


Grants from the David A. MacKay Endowment Fund were made to the Heart and Stroke Foundation of Prince Edward Island to support a new initiative,


For most of us, learning to save a life wouldn’t qualify as a typical weekend activity. However, 30 residents of the Northport area did just that on Saturday, November 24th, by participating in a free CPR Anytime™ event sponsored by the Community Foundation of Prince Edward Island and the Heart. In a recent poll, Canadians who haven’t been trained in cardiopulmonary resuscitation (CPR) listed a ‘lack of time’ as the main barrier to learning this lifesaving skill. The Heart and Stroke Foundation of Canada has responded to this concern with their recent launch of CPR Anytime™ Family & Friends™, a new 22-minute self-directed CPR training course.


Helping Islanders Stay Warm


A n American Summer Islander has shown both concern and generosity by making a donation to help Islanders struggling with high heating bills. The donor, who wishes to remain anonymous, made the donation to the Community Foundation of Prince Edward Island (CFPEI) with the request that the money be given immediately to the Salvation Army home heating fuel fund. The Foundation can issue charitable receipts for Income Tax purposes in the US as well as in Canada.
