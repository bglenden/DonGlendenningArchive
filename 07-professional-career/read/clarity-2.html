EDUCATION: A CALL FOR CLARITY-2

About 4-11

_____________________________________________________________________________


Education is complex, it is also compulsory (at least school attendance is compulsory) Learners have different interests, capabilities, experiences and needs; every Islander is said to fill six life roles that of a citizen, family member, individual, student, consumer and producer;


As a frequent participant in conversations about education, I am struck by the absence of definitions and boundaries; communication and understanding may suffer as a result. Conceptual clarity is a must to understand the people we serve, the services we provide, learner needs, product, processes and values especially when the subject carries layers of subjectivity. The Dictionary of Education (Good) defines definition as “the process of determining the definition or meaning of a word, idea or proposition in general and within a given context; the process of assigning meaning enhances both precision and clarity.”


PEI Documents

While PEI educators must have working definitions, I find it off that none have come to my attention. It appears likely that local educators struggle with the 21st century versions. This document was triggered in part by a World Bank Report (2019) in which Ministries of Education are cautioned about using “going to school” and getting an education” interchangeably.Tthat practice also exists in Prince Edward Island.; Ive encountered it in conversation and when reading official documents. I suspect that the practice is not limited to PEI.


The education literature describes education as follows

  • Good - the aggregate of all the processes by means of which a person develops abilities, attitudes and other forms of behaviour of positive value in the society in which he lives

  • Good – the social process by which people are subjected to the influence of a selected and controlled environment so that they may attain social competence and optimum individual development

  • OECD – organic and sustained communication to bring about learning

  • UNESCO – a set of practices and outcomes that are defined qualitatively

  • Anon - any experience that has a formative effect on the way a person thinks feels or acts.

  • Ontario – both the act of teaching knowledge to others and the act of receiving knowledge from someone else.

  • PEI - definition not found

Prince Edward Island expects that all students who graduate from high school will be able to:

  • Respond with critical awareness to various forms of the arts, and be able to express themselves through the arts.

  • Assess social, cultural, economic and environmental interdependence in a local and global context.

  • Use thee listening, viewing, speaking and writing modes of language(s) and mathematical and scientific concepts and symbols to think, learn and communicate effectively.

  • Continue to learn and pursue an active, healthy lifestyle.

  • Use the strategies and processes needed to a wide variety o probles , including those requiring language, mathematical and scientific concepts.

  • Use a variety of technologies, demonstrate an understanding of a variety of technological applications, and apply appropriate technologies for solving problems.
