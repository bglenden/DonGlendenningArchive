Early Childhood


Early childhood usually encompasses the first 6-8 years of life and is a period of rapid growth in all domains, learning, social, emotional, cognitive, language, and physical. Early childhood experiences set the stage for what each person becomes in later life


Early Childhood Development

It should be pointed out that many of the services to young children already exist but are usually provided by separate agencies and often unfortunately operating independently of each other and sometimes under the rubric of preschool programming. Yet, experts in the field tell us that, children at this age, experience learning is holisticly. Early childhood development encompasses all aspects or components of development, psychological, economic, physical and social well-being, and it does so through a family lens. To be successful, early childhood development must both engage and empower all family members and service providers. The end result is individuals contributing to and enjoying this great country we call Canada.

Benefits


Effective early development programs provide many benefits to both the individual and the community. A review of the literature provides compelling evidence that investment in programs and services for children in the early years pays particular dividends in terms of health, education, literacy and the economy.


Delivery


The concept of early childhood development promotes/envisages an the coordination of services, but not a single provider, at the community level; it also includes significant support for parents and other family members.
