BACKGROUND


Education is a lifelong process involving participation in experiences that are intended to have a formative effect on the way a person feels, thinks and acts (attitudes, skills and knowledge). Major contributors to one’s education include one’s self, one’s home and extended family, schools, communities at large and one’s places of work. I

It is widely recognized that education is a key factor in determining one’s quality of life; in the same way, the collective education of our citizens will determine to a significant degree the future of our community and country.

Education is a Provincial matter; one of its key function being to provide instruction. Providing instruction, of course, leads to the issue of what fields, functions or subjects should be offered through instruction.

Historically, education focused on the three R’s – ‘Readin, Ritin and ‘Rithmetic. Over time, offerings w ere broadened to include history, literature, geography, science. Even later, came the addition of manual training and home economics and other practical courses.

The mid-20th century brought significant efforts to rethink, some would say modernize education probably signalled by the re-examination through the “Eight Year Study” in the US but hastened by the post war industrialization. A wcidenceI point to New Brunswick’s rural high schools, expansion of apprenticeship, comprehensive high schools, the Federal Technical and Vocationaal Assistance of the 1960’s, programmed instruction, instructional objectives, educational outcomes of William Spadey and DACUM. All of this was subsumed under the banner of outcomes based education.


Most educators seem to agree that curriculum development should commence with clearly defined outcomes

Starting with outcomes changes the focus of education from content to learners and organizes teaching and learning around outcomes, and measures change based on performance regardless of when the learning took place.


Today, there is wide support for outcomes based education in the sense of designing support programs based on the desired end. Outcomes based education seems to be the generic term with curriculum ased Outcomes and Competency based Education as the operating versions.

Conclusions:

  • Competencies better understood and more versatile.

  • Consistent with skill training

  • Lends itself to increased accountability

  • Reduces ambiguity


Recommendations:

  • Adopt competencies (CBE) for all domains as a basis for curriculum development in the PEI education system

  • Develop master list of competencies for each outcome (Communication, personal skills, mathematics, career development, etc.)
