Planning framework document establishing Education First's operational approach. Defines education broadly as a lifelong process affecting quality of life and community/national future. Articulates vision for learner-centered, flexible, transparent, and accountable education systems. Outlines enabling strategies including information provision, community engagement, innovation support, research encouragement, and organizational development. Represents Don's comprehensive approach to educational advocacy.

EDUCATION FIRST CANADA

Planning framework

Goal:  a quality education for every Canadian



Education:

  • An important factor in determining one’s quality of life
  • Will determine to a significant degree the future of our community and country.
  • A life-long process of engaging in experiences that have a formative effect on the way one feels, thinks and acts, and is usually expressed in terms of a person’s attitude, knowledge and skill.
  • Must provide for qualifications as well as credentials.
  • Benefits from meaningful public engagement.
  • Change must be immediate, significant and continuous, but planned.  
  • Is greater than schooling
  • Changes to the school system should always, within available resources, be made with individual learner in mind

Issues:

  • Autonomy
  • Accountability
  • Focus
  • Flexibility
  • Learner centered 
  • Access
  • Standards
  • Transparency
  • User friendly
  • Responsiveness 


























Enabling change includes:

  • Providing all stakeholders with recent, relevant, consistent and readily available information about education.  
  • Engaging the Community in the decision making process.  
  • Encouraging and supporting innovation
  • Recognizing leadership and innovative practices of people, organizations and institutions 
  • Encouraging and supporting research
  • Develop an organizational capacity for the task



















First steps:

  • A (printable) electronic newsletter
  • Occasional papers
  • Pilot a public participation activity using community engagement software.
  • Local discussion groups
  • Conversations with educators
  • A “best practice” award
  • Support for innovation
