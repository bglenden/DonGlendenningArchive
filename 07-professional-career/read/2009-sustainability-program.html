PROPOSED SUSTAINABILITY PROGRAM


BACKGROUND


The Community Foundation of Prince Edward Island (CFPEI) was incorporated in 1993 and has a Revenue Canada charitable tax number (89001 1190 RR 001) and a charitable tax number for the USA. ( 30-0050801).  CFPEI is one of 140 community foundations in Canada.  It has a Provincial mandate and is governed by 12 Directors from across the Province who are representative of the Island community.  The Foundation is committed to enhancing the quality of life in Prince Edward Island and operates with maximum transparency and public accountability.  


Community foundations provide an infrastructure for people with many different charitable interests to make long-term contributions to their communities through the establishment of endowments.  Foundations solicit and accept charitable gifts for the purpose of establishing endowments, pool the funds for investment and management purposes, and make grants, which reflect the wishes of the donors.  A second major focus is to provide leadership and support to other Non-Governmental Organizations including charitable organizations.  It is the latter issue being addressed by this proposal.


Need


In 2001, the Community Foundation of Prince Edward Island conducted a survey of some 40 community groups and organizations with a view to learning more about their organizational needs.  In response to the survey, organizations identified the following major issues:


$ Fiscal sustainability

$ Increasing Community Profile

$ Volunteer Recruitment and Retention

$ Board Development

$ Training and Development


Participants in the survey suggested that the Community Foundation of Prince Edward Island could be helpful by:


$ Providing grants to assist organizations with capacity building initiatives

$ Offering or arranging training and development opportunities

$ Assisting with board/organizational development activities

$ Direct provision of technical assistance

$ Initiating and coordinating activities to increasing profile and visibility, and

$ Building community capacity.


Community versus Institutional Capacity Building


Strengthening the capacity of an institution results in stronger, more focussed and more sustainable institution.  Community capacity building, on the other hand, focuses on the needs of a community and working with all organizations in their collectivity to address community needs and issues.  


PROPOSED PROGRAM


Concept


The Foundation proposes to support capacity building within the charitable sector in Prince Edward Island through the provision of customized support and to increase the capacity of communities through ensuring that the support at the community level is coordinated for the benefit of the community.  The Foundation proposes to do this through a private sector led initiative over a 5-year period.


Purpose:


The purpose of the project is to increase the capacity of communities through strengthening its charitable and non-governmental organizations.


Components


Sectoral support will be customized and be a judicious mix of:


Coaching provided by an HRD specialist with significant business, management and voluntary sector experience and critical analysis skills to work directly with organizations.


Customized technical assistance provided by a team of consultants on a fee for service basis in such matters as governance, financial management, proposal preparation, project management, fundraising and the use of technology.


Training as identified by the HRD specialist and the consulting team to include financial planning and record keeping, business plans, proposal preparation, preparation of public information releases, data base development, etc.


Information and networking activities and opportunities through workshops, retreats, an electronic clearinghouse, newsletters, distance learning, organizational profiling and recognition, public information and advocacy.


Special purpose grants based on recommendations of consulting team for such things as purchase of a piece of hardware or software, an evaluation of services provided, a development audit, or a business plan.
