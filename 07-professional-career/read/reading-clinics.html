READING

Over my lifetime, including my recent discussions, no subject comes up more often than the subject of reading and especially the level of reading demonstrated by our graduates.  It has been a concern for years, I recall even during my first year of teaching, discussions with experienced teachers about approaches to teaching the subject.  Some 60 years later, it still sparks controversy.  Given the above, I believe it is worthy of at least one letter to the editor.

Let me hasten to add that reading is not a specialty of mine but I believe it to be an essential skill, that most people can learn to read at a reasonable level, and that we know how to solve the problem.  In so doing, we may have to think outside the box.
