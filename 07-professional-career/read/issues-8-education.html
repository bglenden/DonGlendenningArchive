(125 words):** This condensed summary presents eight key issues Don Glendenning identifies in the education system: (1) education and schooling used interchangeably; (2) absent taxonomy for educational concepts; (3) education functions/services unidentified; (4) system relies on schools rather than comprehensive education system; (5) insufficient school autonomy despite OECD evidence supporting it; (6) unclear accountabilities; (7) limited services for independent learners; (8) assessment dependent on written tests; inadequate achievement reporting; system lacks benchmarks. This brief outline captures Don's core concerns in structured format, suggesting preparation for policy discussions or public communication. The emphasis on missing frameworks, inadequate reporting, and system-wide communication gaps aligns with his central argument that educational clarity and purpose are foundational.

ISSUES IN EDUCATION-8

About 4-11


An education system is a set of services put in place by a community to encourage and support the education of its citizens; given the numbers served and the wide range of prior experience, capabilities and interests of learners, designing and operating an education system is no easy task. These complexities have given rise to a number of issues: 


Education and schooling used interchangeably. 

Didn’t encounter a taxonomy

Functions/Services not identified

No description of education  system, seem to rely on school system

Autonomy – OECD reports students from schools with autonomy in  their day to day operation had better PISA results. 

Accountabilities not clear.

Limited access to services by independent learners

Assessment seems heavily dependent on written tests

Limited reporting on learner achievement

System lacks benchmarks

Need for conversation/engagement/collaboration/promotion
