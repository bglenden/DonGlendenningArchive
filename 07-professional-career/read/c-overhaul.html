Overhaul needed

Education services need an overhaul; they are narrowly focused, cognitively based, bureaucratic, lack accountability and focus on program delivery rather than on learner development – they also lack a self-correcting mechanism.


I do not, we cannot, blame the current government, the previous government or any single government that went before for deficiencies, however, each current government has a responsibility for instituting corrective measures.


We believe that education is central to the future of our citizens and our community. In 2020. citizens need to be literate and numerate and independent learners, be able to take care of themselves and get along with others, be both wise consumers and competent producers, and function as members of a democratic society. Government’s role is to encourage support education for all its citizens and regulate its ongoing supporting activities. A recent World Bank Report ( see below) reports that the two most important factors in a child’s education are what the child ”brings from home” and his/her teacher.


PEI has some 150,000 citizens; all are potential users of educational services. Their

need’ s, interests and capabilities are many and varied.


Educators need to engage with, engage in and collaborate with the community they serve on matters of education. The current conversation, however, seems politically filtered and often bogged down on matters of



administrivia (school upgrading, credits, grades, noon meals, technology, etc.) at the expense of changes that help Islanders gain knowledge, skills, attitudes and values needed to help them to thrive in later life.
