C) ADVANCE EDUCATION THROUGH STRENGTHENING LINKAGES BETWEEN EDUCATION PARTNERS


Employers


About a year ago, the Community Foundation of Prince Edward Island conducted a survey of over 200 young people to better understand their issues. (www.CFPEI.ca). On reviewing their report, Education 20/20 was struck by the “preparedness gap”, that is the preparedness of young people for further education and for work. Early in 2014, Education 20/20 Inc. organized workshops in six communities across the Island to publicize the findings, determine the extent of the concern, and identify actions, if any, that could or should be taken. Perhaps the most significant finding was the lack of communication about educational matters between providers and users of educational services. (A copy of the report is attached). Survey results have informed two program activities:


  1. A small group consisting of employers, college and universities and private citizens has to address the preparedness issue and, in particular the development of a math ladder and , possible alternatives to high school marks as a measure of math levels of job applicants. system to better define levels of achievement; at the moment, the focus of assessment and reporting is on ranking. We will be proposing a system similar to that practiced in music whereby the establishment of levels is handled by the Royal Canadian Conservatory of Music rather than local teachers of music

  2. Hosting a series of discussions between educators and the public they serve. Each session will have a one page background paper, a presentation if appropriate afollowed by open discussion. The objective is to increase communications rather than to reach agreement. The pilot run of discussions will begin in early 2015 between 4:30 and 6:00 pm with a group not to exceed 20 people.

  3. Including nd employers to increase communication such as bringing them together on projects and education workshop panels; we will also contact service clubs encouraging them to invite educators, especially principals, as speakers, and will make a special attempt to produce occasional papers and media releases about items of interest to them.
