Our future as individuals and as a community will be determined, in large measure, by the nature and level of education demonstrated by our citizens. Education is an umbrella concept and includes all experiences that have had an impact on the way we think, feel and act wherever those experiences occur and however they are triggered. The key to a person’s education, the literature tells us, are early experiences triggered in and through the home and family, followed by the influence of teachers


Education in Canada is a provincial responsibility; the role of a Province is to promote, support and regulate services; in the case of PEI, the Province is the chief deliverer. Although education services are not separately identified they appear to be mainly delivered through schools, colleges and universities and limited to students who are enrolled in the various institutions. thus, giving rise to a fragmented system. As a result, the writer argues, PEI is serviced by a school system rather than an education system. This paper reports on the writer’s exploration of the subject.


DEFINITIONS:

Some key definitions:

  • Learner – a person who, for whatever reason, needs, wants or is trying to bring about change in his/her life – trying to change an attitude, gain new knowledge, or add skills. But learners are neither empty jars nor blank slates; each learner carries three pieces of luggage to every new learning situation (1) certain physical, mental and emotional conditions with which a learner is born, (b) prior learning resulting from home circumstances, formal study, travel, state of health, personal interests, culture in which learner operates and (3) aspirations and imagined future including where one will live, the work one will do and the lifestyle one will have, nature of the family, quality of life, etc. . Learners have different interests, experiences, capabilities and needs. Learners also fill a variety of life roles, that of an individual, family member, student, citizen, consumer and producer

  • Learning- a process of engaging in experiences that have a formative effect on the way we think, feel and act. Learning is multidimensional; it can happen any place and any time, when one is alone as well as when people gather together, it may be satisfied by something as simple as a bit of information or as complex as understanding the laws of the universe. Although only the learner can learn, learning may occur in group setting

  • Education-one’s education is the result of experiences that have had a measurable effect on their lives state or level of development of a person’s thoughts, actions and feelings

  • Education System- a set of services put in place by a community to encourage and support the development of its people

  • Teaching - arranging experiences for others resulting in changes to what they know, believe, feel and are able to do.

  • School- a resource rich facility designed to support the education process

  • School system- all schools in a district reporting to a single senior executive member

  • Educated means that the person can deal satisfactorily with most issues that arise in their everyday lives.

  • Life roles –based on Oregon Education finding that each person fills six life roles – individual, family member, student, citizen, consumer and producer. Education can support any and all

  • The purpose of the British Columbia school system is to enable learners to develop their

  • individual potential and to acquire the knowledge, skills, and attitudes needed to contribute to a

  • healthy society and a prosperous and sustainable economy.

Purpose –

Purpose provides the fundamental reason for establishing any organization, a reason for which something is done or created; it is inspirational and usually calls for action that results in a local benefit to a community or society. Purpose is the key element in the structuring process and one from which all others follow. People will be most engaged in their work when the mission and goals of the organization also matter to them—and when they feel like they can make a contribution to the bigger picture. Studies show that when leaders demonstrated clear purpose—vision, commitment to stakeholders and strong morals—their people were happier and more productive. To be meaningful, a purpose statement must be:

• Easy to understand

• Explain why the work is important

• Contribute to alignment of activities

• Manageable.

Accountabilities

According to Anderson (See below) there are three main types of accountability – compliance, adherence to professional norms and results. Start with clarity and set expectations; be goal focused, hire the right people, Communicate with transparency. team and individual productivity, builds trust and collaboration, helps organization perform.
