A reflective column recounting an anecdote where Don was asked about his education at a social group. When he provided a comprehensive answer about farm upbringing, teaching training, and industrial arts education, his questioner dismissed it, wanting only formal school information. The piece illustrates Don's philosophical point about the limited, conventional understanding of "education" versus his holistic, lifelong view encompassing practical experience and skill development.

About Education


Where did I get my education?

I was asked not long ago where I got my education. I had joined a new group and expected to be a regular so I assumed that they wanted a bit of information for their records or in case someone needed notes to introduce me.  I was happy to comply. My questioner handed me a form to complete.

There were the usual questions, name, address, telephone number, Email address, where I was born, name of spouse, etc., etc, etc. not too difficult. Then came a space labelled “Education”.

Now that’s a question that invokes views. so I decided to elaborate in my answer. I mentioned growing up on a mixed farm which meant some involvement  with animals, vegetables and general  farm practices and, o course winter work in the woods.   I also mentioned that we each had chores to do and they varied  depending on the time of year.  I mention that I had traineed as a eacher, and separately as an industrial arts teacher,  but did not say for how long.I thought that I had given a reasonable view of my educational background.


Soon my friend returned to the table, picked up the paper and walked away; two minutes later he was back.  With a curt “I just want to know what school you went to”.
