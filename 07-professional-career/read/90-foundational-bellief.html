Article No. 90 (February 20, 2020) outlines foundational beliefs (learning is behavioral change, observable and measurable; education is gestalt of formative experiences; teaching creates behavioral-change strategies; groups don't learn but individuals learn in groups; most people can learn modern-economy skills). The piece references OECD research on teacher quality, noting that "the largest source of variation in student learning is attributable to differences in what students bring to school" and that teacher factors are more important than school variables influencing achievement. Don emphasizes that teacher quality matters more than academic knowledge alone—conveying ideas effectively, creating learning environments, enthusiasm, and creativity are equally important. This piece synthesizes research with his educational philosophy.

Open Education

No. 90 February 20, 2020

When schools and ministries are managed well, learning happens-World Bank


FOUNDATIONAL BELIEFS

  • Learning results in a change of behaviour

  • Changes in behaviour /are observable

  • Changes of behaviour are measurable

  • Education is the gestalt of personal experiences that had a formative effect on the way a person thinks, feels or acts

  • Teaching is the function of creating strategies and experiences that result in leaning

  • Teacher is a person, usually paid, who creates situations the cause others to change behaviour.

  • Groups do not learn but individuals may learn in a group setting

  • Most people can learn skills, knowledge and attitudes needed to live and work in a modern economy.

  • Assessment criteria should reflect those in use in the community being served

  • Governments invest in education to (1) help citizens develop personal capabilities and (2) to ensure that the community has the services needed to make it a place people want to live.


QUALITY TEACHING

Three broad conclusions from research on student learning (Teachers Matter, OECD)

  • “The largest source of variation in student learning is attributable to differences in what students bring to school.”

  • “… of those variables which are potentially open tp policy influence, factors to do with teachrs and eaching are the mist important influences on student learning. In particular, the broad consensus is that “teachr quality” is the single most important school variable influencing student achievement.”

  • Relationship between measures of student performance and academic ability or subject matter knowledge, although positive, is of a lesser extent than expected. Other characteristics, such as the ability to convey ideas, to create effective learning environments and to be enthisisstic and creative are also rated s important.
