Opinion piece on Souris education survey findings highlighting lack of community voice in educational planning. Out-of-school youth wanted information about local courses but appeared disconnected from education system planning. Glendenning argues education and community development progress separately without coordinated planning. Raises possibility of separating educational information from institutional offerings in information age. This incomplete draft reflects his concern for rural communities and community participation in education system development. Shows his attention to social justice dimensions of education.
