A February 2, 2009 memo proposing comprehensive review of information management systems including contact management, website, office records, and content organization. Don identifies software selection criteria emphasizing remote accessibility, security, backup systems, automatic correspondence generation, and expandability. He recommends surveying sister foundations, establishing written policies, developing RFP terms, and considering open-source and Google Apps solutions. This document demonstrates Don's understanding that organizational effectiveness depends on robust information infrastructure and his willingness to invest in technological solutions supporting institutional operations.

COMMUNITY FOUNDATION OF PRINCE EDWARD ISLAND


SD- 2009- 

Date: February 2, 2009

Prepared by: Don Glendenning


To: John, Donnie, Jeniene, Marla


Subject:  Information Management


I believe that we need to review our entire information management system including:

Contact Management

Website

Office records

Content (Policies, procedures, agreements, etc)

Human Resource needs


Hosting options:

Client (In house)

Remote

Web based meaning on our own computer but accessible remotely


General comments

Should try to find a commercial program and modify to suit needs

Identifying needs is usually the major problem; finding software, even free software, is usually not a problem

Human resource needs include someone to lead the conversion and people to enter data


Software selection critiera:

Remote accessibility

24/7 accessibility

Secure

Back-up system

Automatically spawn correspondence

Expandable


Next steps:

In house identification of needs

Survey of sister foundations; a standard set of questions would help

Prepare written policies and procedures

Prepare terms of reference for an RFP

Google Apps a good program

Automatically spawning letters may be a costly option; depends on volume

Open source programs are usually light in documentation
