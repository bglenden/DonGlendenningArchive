Re-setting our  direction

An education system is an array of programs and services put in place by a community to encourage and support the education of its citizens. Governments provide support for two reasons; (1) to support the education of jndividual citizens and (2) because communities need skilled people to ensure their viability.   Typical education services include funding, regulation, information,  counselling, instruction, quality assurance, education products. 

But there is a gap, some say a widening gap, between services wanted (needed?) by the community and services provided by the education  system – problems of structure, mandate and mission, government practices, bureaucracy, autonomy and accountability  - and that gap is interfering with both quality of service. 

The situation calls for action; step one, I suggest, is an overview of what is intended – a public process resulting in a public document setting out of basic beliefs an underlying principles on which our education system will operate.  The initiation of such a document falls naturally to Government; the development of the framework, however, should involve public consultation and independent generation. 

As I write, I am reminded of a quotation in a book, Setting Instructional Objective, popular sixty years ago.  In making the case for cler objectives, the author asks  “If you don’t know where you’re going, how will you know when you get there?” It’s time, I suggest, for education in PEI to review and restate where “it’s going”

Sincerely


Don Glendenning

A long-time student of education
