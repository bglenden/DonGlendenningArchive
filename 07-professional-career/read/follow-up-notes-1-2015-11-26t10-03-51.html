Preparedness-2

Meeting Notes

November 25, 2015


Participants:

Amanda Beazley, PEI Literacy Alliance, Executive Director

Derek McEwen – Department of Education,

Don Glendenning, Education 20/20 Inc.,

Elizabeth Costa-Department of Education,

Jeniene Peake, community member

Uqing Gong, UPEI

Roger Birt, Employer

Shelly Bradley – Holland College.

Steve McQuaid, President, CFPEI

Terri MacAdam, ELSB

Tom Davies, CFPEI Director 

Tom White-Hassler, Education 20/20 Inc.

Notes:

  1. The chair welcomed those present, confirmed the purpose to be an “exploration of the “preparedness gap” as identified in the CFPEI youth survey in 2013.

  2. Concerning the nature of the gap, those present were not aware of any analysis or study of employer needs except the following four skills identified in the presentation to Government by the Charlottetown Chamber of Commerce; inability to perform face-to-face customer interactions, inability to read cursive writing, lack of math skills required for measuring and cutting in construction, and inability to count change without a cash register. Don reported that he had searched without success for a master list of outcomes or competencies from which schools, colleges and universities, and employers, could use when communicating about the issue.

  3. With regards to numbers represented, Steve recall the results to be in the order of 20 percent, however, no one was aware of any formal study of the issue.

  4. There was considerable discussion about pressures placed on teachers arising out of student differences, student interests, socio-economic and related factors home conditions and limited financial resources. This brought fourth comments about the importance of students being able to connect their learning to their needs; at the same time, there are pressures being placed on the system in support of greater customizing of service to suit individual student needs.

  5. Preparation for citizenship seemed to be accepted as a goal of education; preparation for employment or further education seemed less clear

  6. Mention was made of the pressures on the system arising from issues of class size and composition;(Although not mentioned during the discussion, the matter of whether or not class size and composition is now before the supreme Court –Don)

  7. In response to a comment about ensuring that our young people are well prepared for the future, Derek described recent initiatives to strengthen support for career education

  8. Tom spoke about the need for first hand information and perceptions of the community as a basis for planning and described such an initiative that he had been part of in Alaska.

  9. Next meeting at the call of the chair.

  10. Attendees commented on the usefulness of the exchange and a willingness to meet again; this was attested to by the fact that the conversation continued well after the meeting closed.
