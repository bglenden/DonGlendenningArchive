Math 2015-1

January 4, 2015

Perceived gap in math skills


We spoke about education and, I think, I mentioned an interest in taking a closer look at a perceived gap between the basic skills young people have and the skills expected or required by postsecondary institutions, employers and the public at large.


There are a variety of possible reasons including:

  • Employers needs have not been clearly identified

  • Postsecondary needs have not been clearly identified

  • Insufficient communication between employers and schools

  • Schools may not be teaching what employers and institutions think they are teaching

  • Problems with assessment

  • Insufficient documentation

I believe that the issue is worthy of further exploration and am inviting you to an initial exploration of the issue as it relates to math with a view to identifying what, if anything, can or should be done. Coming to this meeting does not commit you to any further participation.

I know that you are busy but let me propose a meeting for noon on Friday, January 9, 2015 in the Board Room of the PEI Association of Engineers. 135 Water Street, Charlottetown; I’ll arrange for coffee. I’m hoping for 4-5 people; I’ll share the names once I know who will be attending.

Please let me know if you are interested in being part of the discussion, and if you can attend on Friday.

Regards and Happy New Year



Don Glendenning
