A detailed proposal identifying weaknesses in the current reading support system and proposing comprehensive improvements. Don identifies systematic gaps: lack of early detection, poorly defined outcomes, inflexible delivery, and inadequate assessment. His improvement strategy is holistic, including emphasis on reading as a separate subject, extended learning hours through summer and night classes, reading specialists and clinics, technology integration, and community engagement. He proposes learning passports, benchmark systems, community reading discussions, and awards to incentivize improvement. The document showcases Don's capacity for systems thinking, his understanding of learner-centered design, and his belief in combining professional expertise with community participation.
