About Education

A Personal Framework for change

(An evolving draft)


Background:

Some 140,000 Islanders are currently travelling life’s road, a road of varying lengths and for most, a road with many twists and turns. While on that road, each engages in a number of life roles including that of an individual, family member, learner, citizen, consumer and producer. The preparation for life’s roles is called education and the better prepared we are, the better we are able to cope with all that we encounter or create. That learning is complicated by the fact that each person is unique; unique in our basic physical, mental, emotional and social make up, unique in our combination of previous experiences, unique in our perceived futures for ourselves, but unique also in terms of our pace and style of learning. Our education consists of what we have learned or perhaps, what we have retained from our learning experiences, or even what we are able to do as a result of what we have learned


It is widely recognized that education is a key factor in determining each citizen’s quality of life; in the same way, the collective education of our citizens will determine to a significant degree the quality of the community in which we live. Recognizing all of the above, our communities invest heavily in programs and facilities; the state has two interests. One is to encourage and support a high level of personal growth and development for every citizen; the other is to ensure that our communities have the skills needed to provide a quality of life that our citizens need and expect.


There is a gap between what Islanders have and what they want in the way of education. This gap, the writer believes, arises primarily from our sloppiness with the language. Let me explain. Educators and the public at large tend to use schooling and educationa interchangeablyThey do not do so with agriculture and farming, health and hospitals, justice and law enforcement, religion and eorship nor sports and recreation. Educators I speak know the difference but even they fail to differentiate. It may well have something to do with the lack of public information and participation by the public at large in the education sector, lack of a large and well identified private sector component, Perhaps a few definitions are in order:


Definitions:

  1. Learners – a person engaged in changing behaviour

  2. Education – any experience that has a formative effect on the way we feel, think or act

  3. Schooling – learning under the direction of teachers

  4. Purpose of education – to help people gain the attitudes, skills, knowledge of citizenship wanted or needed in later life

  5. Role of schools – to help people develop their full potential as citizns of this country and to ensure that our community has the skills needed to grow and prosper

  6. Learning – changing the way we think, feel or act

  7. Teaching – helping people change the way they think, feel or act

  8. Education system – a set of components, relationships and services put in place by a community to encourage and support the education of its citizens

  9. A school system refers to the institutions, programs and services provided and encouraged by or with the approval of a community through its government to provide formal education for its residents


Operating beliefs - education:

  1. Each person carries his/her own combination of traits based on genetics, culture, health, emotional make-up, previous experiences and imagined future.

  2. We are educated whether or not we go to school

  3. The home is the major contributor to one’s education

  4. Parents want their children to be educated not just schooled;

  5. Each person is responsible for his/her own education; they just need a bit of help from time to time

  6. Educating is a developmental activity; schools emphasize the transfer of knowledge

  7. Education system and school system are not synonymous

  8. Most educators see education through a school lens

  9. Schools focus on the delivery of knowledge;

  10. The education system lacks incentives to respond positively to calls for change

  11. The Importance of institutions (schools, colleges and universities) in acquiring an education is on the decline

  12. There are no apparent benefits for the education system to do a better job or students to reach standards early or reach higher standards

  13. Awarding credentials and qualifications is not an educational function; o do so creates a conflict of interest
