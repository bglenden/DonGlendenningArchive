May 2006 one-hour workshop proposal for Financial Advisors Association of Canada titled "Charitable Options." Don Glendenning designed curriculum to help financial planners customize charitable gifts reflecting client wishes and resources. Topics include donor demographics, characteristics of giving, types of charitable organizations, fund structures, donor interests/capacity, and complex issues in charitable giving. Demonstrates Don's expertise in charitable planning and his ability to translate foundation knowledge into professional education for financial community.

Proposed Course Outline 

For Presentation to

The Financial Advisors Association of Canada


Title: Charitable Options


Background: Many donors want to and are capable of making significant and long term contributions to the charitable sector.  Some have a vision of where they want to make an impact and are open to creative opportunities consistent with their interests; this is especially true of younger donors.    Others simply wish to give back to their community and let others decide on the priorities.  Potential donors can best be served by being aware of the options available to them and professional advisors are a key component of the decision-making process.


This course is designed to assist financial planners to customize a charitable gift that reflects a client’s wishes and financial resources.


Objective: To familiarize financial advisors with options available to donors who want to make gifts of a charitable nature.


Format: A one-hour workshop using presentations, handouts and case studies.


Leader: Donald Glendenning, CM, PhD
