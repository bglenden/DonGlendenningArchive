Don's eulogy/memorial remarks for Walter S. (Bud) McMurtry (1922–2001), a vocational education pioneer in PEI and Holland College associate. Documents Bud's career trajectory, his role in establishing the Atlantic Police Academy, and his personal qualities. Shows Don's perspective on education leadership and institutional history.


Memorial Service

Walter S. (Bud) McMurtry (1922-2001)

January 6, 2002


Gathered to remember and pay respects to Bud McMurtry


I first met Bud in Ottawa at a National meeting in 1962,  He was articulate, knowledgeable and represented the Island well around the Conference table.


PEI became a founding member of a regional teacher training program.


Some things are easy to visualize:

A young lad growing up in Nova Scotia

A student 

A husband

A father

A stint in the Navy

Arrival on Prince Edward Island to work with the Department of Agriculture


For a number of years Bud was Mr. Vocational Education in PEI

Bud was a jack of all trades

Director of Vocational Education

Larger Provinces had specialists in each area but Bud was responsible for everything - vocational High school education, training of technicians, apprenticeship, training of unemployed , vocational teacher training

The face of education on PEI changed under his leadership

Construction of PVI and PCVHS

A college of applied arts and technology was established



In 1969 he came to Holland college 

As Program Planning Coordinator he spearheaded the establishment of the Atlantic Police Academy.  He worked with Police Chiefs and others involved in law enforcement to keep the initiative moving.

Chief Dan Allen, then in Chatham, was promoting police training.  We thought that we should talk with him.  Bud arranged with Someone, bill Rix, I think, to bring him over for the day

Later he was an instructor in the Academy - a role from which he retired


Bud was a good communicator distinguished looking

articulate

a good listener

liked to talk but did not boast of his accomplishmnts


Bud and Myrtle were active in their church



Active in scouting and rose through the ranks to be Provincial Ccommissioner



Had a love affair with the sea.

Had a boat

Spent many hours on his boat, or perhaps more accurately, around his boat.  

Seems to me that he spent more time cleaning, painting and repairing it than in actually sailing.


He loved to moor off shore, turn off his CB and just enjoy the water 

and being out of reach of the office


In the early years Bud taught courses in basic instructional techniques.

Students that that course was one of the best they ever had 

His courses are still remembered


In talking with colleagues in recent days, these were the kind of phrases they used:


Articulate

An excellent instructor

So often in uniform – choir, navy, police academy uniform, scouts

Craftsman

Love of the sea

Very active

Very involved


Bud McMurtry understood and believed in vocational education

He knew and practiced its basic principles

Many Islanders profited from the results of his good works 


The Island is richer because Bud was here
