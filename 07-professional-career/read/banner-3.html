CITIZEN MATH

This document provides a list of math competencies practiced by Island citizens in everyday life. It was developed by Education 2020 Inc. in consultation with panel of citizen under the direction of a facilitator trained in the DACUM process.  It is a list of skills practiced rather than a list of skills desired.   


The DACUM analysis is a Canadian achievement having emerged from a major research initiative funded by the Government of Canada in the 1960’s and 1970’s; It differs from most analyses in that it gathers information directly from practitioners.  Analyses of this kind serve many purposes including providing a basis for:

  • Constructing curriculum and training programs, 
  • Preparing position descriptions, 
  • Setting standards, 
  • Developing Teaching and learning plans and
  • Recording student progress


An analysis is a neutral document in that it simply identifies skills performed. Users select competencies that best serve their purpose, how they will be used, level of performance expected or required.  As part of the learning process, learners would be expected to

 demonstrate skills, self-rate and have ratings confirmed by an instructor.

MATH SKILLS USED ON A REGULAR BAIS BY ISLAND CITIZENS




 


1.Estimate and Calculate with Whole Numbers

  • Recognize whole numbers
  • Count whole numbers
  • Add whole numbers
  • Subtract whole numbers
  • Multiply whole numbers
  • Divide whole numbers
  • Round off whole numbers






2. Use Fractions, Decimals, Percentages, Rations and Rates

  • Add with decimals to second decimal point
  • Subtract with decimals to second decimal point
  • Multiply with decimals to second decimal point
  • Divide with decimals to second decimal point
  • Round to the nearest whole number
  • Calculate ratio
  • Compare percentages/interest
  • Apportion items










3. Use Spatial reasoning

  • Sort items
  • Sort by numeric value
  • Identify geometric shapes
  • Identify physical boundaries
  • Identify space requirements
  • Estimate spatial capacity
  • Allocate visual space
  • Lay out patterns
  • Optimize material usage
  • Read maps
  • Identify compass directions
  • Interpret diagram
  • Divide angles
  • Construct angles


4.  Recognize and Use Patterns and Relationships

  • Memorize numbers
  • Identify seasons
  • Identify deficits
  • Identify Surplus
  • Recognize anomalies in a given pattern 
  • Solve written problems
  • Monitor health statistics












5. Interpret Statistical Information 

  • Gather data
  • Validate data
  • Compare data
  • Identify trends
  • Read graphs
  • Write formulas
  • Analyze data
  • Summarize data
  • Read statistical models
  • Construct graphs
  • Read tables
  • Construct tables


6. Manage Personal Finance

  • Create a budget
  • Recognize currency units
  • Analyze financial risk
  • Identify unit cost
  • Recognize exchange rates
  • Calculate exchange rates
  • Compare dollar value
  • Calculate dollar value
  • Make change
  • Estimate costs
  • Calculate sales tax
  • Recognize relationships between revenue and expenditures
  • Calculate interest rates










7. Use Measurement 

  • Measure temperature in metric and imperial units
  • Measure energy
  • Measure length in metric and imperial units
  • Measure distance in metric and imperial units
  • Calculate distance in metric and imperial units
  • Measure circumference
  • Measure diameter
  • Calculate area in metric and imperial units
  • Weigh objects in metric and imperial units
  • Estimate weight in metric and imperial units
  • Measure volume in metric a and imperial units 
  • Estimate volume 
  • Read a clock
  • Budget time 
  • Estimate time
  • Calculate time zone differences
  • Translate 12 to 24-hour clock
  • Create a time table 
  • Convert imperial/metric units of measure 
  • Read a calendar
