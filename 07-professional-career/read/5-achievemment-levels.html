Strategic problem-solution document identifying gaps between school-provided education and community/employer needs. Frames three possible purposes: better define the gap, narrow the gap, or provide alternative instruction. Raises critical questions about education's purpose on PEI, schooling objectives, subject-specific goals, and school responsibility to employers and postsecondary institutions. Identifies potential interventions: common skill/competency analysis, recognized achievement levels, improved reporting, and independent assessment bodies. Distinguishes between communication interventions (stakeholder engagement), gap-narrowing strategies (partner engagement, employer testing, independent qualification bodies), and research approaches (meeting with ministers, undertaking research). Represents Don's systematic thinking about educational system change across multiple intervention points.

Proficiency Levels


PROBLEM: A gap between the nature and level of education provided by school and the nature and the nature and level needed by our citizens

PURPOSE: 1- Better define the gap for those who need/want to know, or

2. Narrow the gap for the benefit of employers and postsecondary institutions, or

3. Provide an alternative source of instruction for people experiencing the gap

4. Qualification service

ISSUES:

  1. Purpose of Education on PEI – not clear


  1. Purpose of schools and schooling on PEI – not clear



  1. Purpose for which subjects are taught (For the sake of clarity, I suggest that we limit our discussion to a single subject – Math?)



  1. Do schools have a responsibility to teach math required by its citizens? By postsecondary institutions? By employers? Other?


  1. Potential interventions

  • Common analysis of skills, outcomes or competencies for each subject (curriculum map) leaving users to make own selection

  • Establish recognized levels of achievement, a la qualification levels in most countries

  • Improve reporting

  • Establish an independent assessment body.


  1. Potential Interventions

  • Increase communication around the issue

  • Engage one or more partners in narrowing the gap

  • Employers and institutions ignore school grades and do own testing.

  • Establish an independent testing body


  1. Strategies

  • Increase communication among stakeholders

  • Engage partners

  • Urge employers and institutions to do own testing

  • Establish an independent qualification body

  • Meet with appropriate Ministers and officials

  • Undertake research

  1. References

  2. Vacant
