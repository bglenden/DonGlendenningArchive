Philosophical exploration of education as concept, encompassing personal development across the lifespan. Defines education as aggregate of experiences that measurably change how people think, feel, believe, and act. Explains that education is multifaceted, occurring anywhere and anytime, and depends on self, family, community, travel, and schools. Includes definitions from international sources including OECD, UNESCO, World Bank, and provincial ministries. Warns again that World Bank identifies "going to school equals education" as problematic in developing countries and PEI. Provides recommendations for PEI to establish common terminology, define education comprehensively, and implement system-wide clarification efforts for meaningful community conversation.

EDUCATION

About 4-16

Life is complex. Citizens come in all shapes and sizes; they have different interests, experiences, capabilities, purposes and needs. It is said that every citizen fills six life roles, that of an individual, family member, citizen, student, consumer and producer. Young citizens have much to learn in order to navigate and thrive in the life that unfolds; older people learn for the same reason but are often just able to start their learning from different place.


Education is an umbrella word; our education includes all the experiences that have changed us; our education is demonstrated by our actions; Our education depends, to a large degree, on our experiences at home, in our community, through travel, interactions with others, personal exploration and experiences that occur in schools. A good education has been described as one that provides us with the knowledge, skills. attitudes and other attributes needed to deal with circumstances that confront us; but there are other definitions as well. An educational experience may include changing wht you know, how you feel, what you value or what you an do and sometimes aa single experience will change all at the same time.

It is generally said that:

• Learning is a personal matter

• Each learner learns at own rate

• Groups don’t learn but individuals may learn in a group setting





An education system consists of programs and services put in place to organize and deliver education service. Typical tools of change include information, instruction; diagnosis, counselling, quality control, products and technology but we seem o group them together qs schools.

Schools, like hospitals and spas are purpose-built facilities that house the people and services needed to support education. Having a well-equipped school is important but it is the service that impacts one’s education, not the school.

Our Department of Education is sending mixed messages about its educational interests, services and plans.

• It seems to treat education and school attendance as though they are one and the same

• It gives it’s role as one of “developing and delivering programs” thereby missing the importance of the change process

• Purpose of efforts are unclear

• Places emphasis on teaching traditional subjects

In fact, most public articles about education seem only to deal with school matters. Until we recognize the difference and put education on our agenda, we are not likely to get the education that Islanders want and need. Although called a Department of Education, its actions are mostly limited to running schools, to the teaching function and to the young. The goal, as found on the website. of “developing and delivering programs and curriculum” is more suitable for a curriculum division than for a Department of Education.

I urge the Department to review, clarify and restate its mission, goals, objectives and guiding principles

Sincerely

Don Glendenning

The education literature describes education as follows:

  • Good - the aggregate of all the processes by means of which a person develops abilities, attitudes and other forms of behaviour of positive value in the society in which he lives

  • Good – the social process by which people are subjected to the influence of a selected and controlled environment so that they may attain social competence and optimum individual development

  • OECD – organic and sustained communication to bring about learning

  • UNESCO – a set of practices and outcomes that are defined qualitatively

  • Anon - any experience that has a formative effect on the way a person thinks feels or acts.

  • Ontario – both the act of teaching knowledge to others and the act of receiving knowledge from someone else.

  • World Bank – While the World Bank does not define education it warns countries that “going to school and getting an education” are not the same.

  • Ministries of Education often equate going to school with getting an education. (See World Bank Report of 2018)
