Dated October 14, 2008, this document refines the literacy fund concept with specific grant parameters and priority areas. COMMUNITY FOUNDATION OF PRINCE EDWARD ISLAND


SD- 2008- 29B

Date: October 14, 2008

Prepared by: Don Glendenning


Proposed Fund


A permanent endowment the income from which will be used to support the extension, expansion and improvement of literacy education on Prince Edward Island.


Fund Guidelines broad enough to include but not limited to:

  • Available for multi-year projects

  • Operate through call for proposals

  • Grants could be to individuals, individual agencies, partnership and collaborative projects

  • Multi-year projects should include annual evaluation

  • Could consider demonstration projects,

  • Grants could be for planning, training, preparation or purchase of materials. In-service education, capacity building, evaluation,

  • Some current priority areas include eastern and western regions of province, workplace literacy activities especially in private industry, First nations projects and literacy assessment for adults who wann to take skilled training

  • Cooperation with libraries, book stores, credit union, etc

  • Not especially interested in grants for research


Impact:

  • CFPEI would become a significant player in the field of literacy on PEI

  • CFPEI would be in a position to “add value” to literacy training


If Fund becomes a reality, suggest:

  • Invitation to MacNutt and O’Bryan to make presentation to Board on state of literacy on PEI

  • Coordinate our activities with other funders – perhaps a meeting to discuss
