Memo to Education 20/20 directors from May 2018 reviewing Education Award program and planning for 2019. Notes previous award themes (engagement, technology, mathematics demystification, collaboration), suggests reviewing committee terms and exploring community wellness as potential theme, and proposes grant model alternative.

Memo to Directors:

May 29. 2018

Education ward-2019

The 2018 Award was presented today to Jason Campbell, Emily Perry and Terry Nabuurs of Bluefield High School; we should now begin planning for 2019


The purpose of the award varies  from year to year.  The first two awards recognized engagement, the third recognized the application of technology throughout a school and last one recognized the wonders of mathematics, a program to demystify mathematics. The 2018 Award was for collaboration and was presented to Bluefield High School.

Recommendations:

  • Terms of Reference for the Committee be reviewed and reconfirmed
  • The theme for 2018 has potential again for 2019; In discussion at Bluefield High today, however, the idea of community wellness” came up today as an alternative theme.
  • Before deciding next year’s theme, it might be interesting to solicit comments from previous award winners  
  • Although we spoke of an award to the Duffy’s , no award has been presented to date,
  • We have always given the award to something already accomplished; the idea of a small grant to help a school “do” something should not be ruled out.  If this were the case, proposers could even make a presentation to Education 20/20 bout their plans
