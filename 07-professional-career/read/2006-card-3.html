Christmas 2006 family update letter from Don and Carol describing retirement activities, winter travel to Florida, autumn trip to New Mexico visiting family, and noting Holland College's honor in naming Glendenning Hall after him during his 18-year tenure. Personal reflections on family, travel, and his legacy at the college.


From our home to your home!


Greetings.  All are well.  A few notes about our family.   


We spent February in North Clearwater Beach, Florida; we like the north 

beach because there are fewer high rises and no through traffic – although 

that seems to change each year.  We were near but not on the beach and 

close to public transportation, a library, convenience stores, restaurants, etc.  

It fits our needs and style.


In October we flew to Denver and, after visiting the city, drove south 

stopping in Taos and Santa Fe before reaching Brian and Marie in Socorro.  

We had a great visit; first time ever in the Southwest USA during warm 

weather.  Brian and Marie have a lovely large home and, given the good 

weather, we were comfortable to limit ourselves to day trips.  Brian and 

Marie took some time off - and Brian and I actually built a house for the 

family cat.


Our trip also included a family gathering in Santa Fe in October – sort of 

a belated birthday party for Carol.   We had a great evening; Janet was 

New Mexico for a visit so we overlapped with her for a family dinner.  

Our granddaughter, Lisa, finished university and is working and Laura

finishes this year.





Earlier this year, Holland College, where I worked for 18 years, opened

 a new 96-bed residence and honoured me by naming it Glendenning Hall.


Carol and I keep well and busy.  We read, travel and garden; Carol loves

 her bridge and I still do some genealogy.


May the blessings of the season be with you in the New Year.


Warmest wishes.
