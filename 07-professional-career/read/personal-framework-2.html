Personal evolving framework outlining Glendenning's comprehensive education philosophy. Defines key terms (learners, education, schooling, learning, teaching, education systems). States operating beliefs emphasizing individual uniqueness, home's role, personal responsibility for education, developmental nature of learning, and declining institutional importance. Identifies education system characteristics needed: accessible, affordable, flexible, relevant, accountable, transparent, focused on helping individuals change. Lists key intervention points and critical gaps. Concludes that fundamental education problems are organizational rather than educational, with recommended key interventions including clear mission, professional autonomy, learner-centered models, professionalized teaching, independent assessment, and counseling services. This represents Glendenning's mature synthesis of decades of educational thought.

About education

Personal notes

(An evolving draft)

Definitions:

  1. Learners – a person engaged in changing behaviour

  2. Education – any experience that has a formative effect on the way we feel, think or act

  3. Schooling – learning under the direction of teacher(s)

  4. Purpose of education – to help people gain the attitudes, skills, knowledge of citizenship wanted or needed in later life

  5. Role of schools – to help people develop the skills of citizenship and to ensure that our community has the skills needed to grow and prosper

  6. Learning – changing the way we think, feel or act

  7. Teaching – helping people change the way they think, feel or act

  8. Education system – a set of components, relationships and services put in place by a community to encourage and support the education of its citizens

  9. A school system - institutions, programs and services provided and encouraged by or with the approval of a community through its government to provide formal education for its residents


Operating beliefs - education:

  1. Each person is unique as a result of genetics, prior experience and aspirations.

  2. We are educated whether or not we go to school

  3. The home is the major contributor to one’s education

  4. Parents want their children to be educated not just schooled;

  5. Each person is responsible for his/her own education

  6. Educating is a developmental process.

  7. The Importance of institutions (schools, colleges and universities) in acquiring an education is on the decline

  8. Education systems lack incentives to improve instruction, increase number served or save time

  9. Neither establishing nor awarding levels of achievement are educational functions

  10. The objective is to deliver educational services is, as and when needed
