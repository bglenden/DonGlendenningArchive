Don Glendenning's account of Anne Kaptein, a Berlin-born travel writer and WWII survivor who lived in Charlottetown. Don persuaded Tim Kaptein to donate her manuscript 'Those Tragic Years' to the Confederation Centre Library. Illustrates Don's community engagement and support for cultural/historical preservation.
Professional Career & Consulting



Anne Kaptein, a travel writer lived in Charlottetown with her husband, Tim.  She grew up in Berlin and was a nurse-in-training during  World War 11.  A copy of her story about those early years now resides in the Confederation Centre Library.  


The book came to the attention of Don Glendenning, a friend of the Kapteins who persuaded Mr. Kaptein to give his only spare copy to the Library


Those Tragic Years tells about  the rise of Hitler, the tension with family member who supported the Nazis and the fear that a slip of the tongue could result in their being reported to the secret police.  Anne also tells of the early bombing of Berlin and the relocation of the school.


After the War, Anne married Tim Kaptein, from Holland  they and lived in Charlottetown until her death.  Tim has since moved to Niagara Falls, Ontario to be near his family.
