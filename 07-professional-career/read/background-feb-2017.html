Meeting notes from February 8, 2017 discussion facilitated by Don Glendenning as Chair. Documents attendance by Tamara Hubley-Little and Katherine McQuaid. Discussion covers wide-ranging topics including communication among educational stakeholders, institutional entrance requirements, cost of re-teaching high school subjects at postsecondary level, variance in standards between schools, achievement levels, coordination with other provinces, and CMEC initiatives on competencies. Don's conclusions outline the need for inclusive groups spanning educators, employers, registrars, and certification bodies; proposes graphing the transition process; identifies major transition points (Grade 10 entry, college/university entrance, work entry); emphasizes advanced notice requirements. Meeting adjourned with continuation at Chair's call.

TRANSITION 

Notes from discussion of February 8, 2017


Note: Due to weather and out of Province travel, a number of invitees were not able to attend.


Present:  

  • Tamara Hubley-Little
  • Katherine McQuaid
  • Don Glendenning


Notes:

The conversation was wide-ranging and included the following: 

  • Communication among interested parties,
  • Institutional and program entrance requirements, 
  • Cost to the system of re-teaching high school subjects at adult level, 
  • Variance in standards from school to school; 
  • Achievement/performance levels,
  • Coordination of content with other Provinces,
  • CMEC initiative in developing common competencies and reporting progress.


Conclusions of the Chair:

  • Group should include educators, employers- registrars/admissions officers and certification bodies
  • It would be useful to graph the transition process from school entry to work entry
  • Major transition points entry to Grade 10, entrance to college and university, entrance to work from high school or postsecondary.
  • Need to provide more advanced notice

 

Next meeting:

  • At the call of the Chair


Don Glendenning

Chair
