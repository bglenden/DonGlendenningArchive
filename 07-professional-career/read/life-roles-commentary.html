A commentary on the learning framework incorporating Maslow's hierarchy of basic needs and the Oregon Educational model's concept of real and imagined life roles. The document identifies six life role categories (individual, family member, citizen, learner, consumer, producer) and explores how people develop imagined futures in areas such as family, career, lifestyle, community, and income. This framework positions learning as driven by both fundamental human needs and aspirational life goals, demonstrating Don's sophisticated understanding of motivation and human development as central to educational reform.
