Don concludes that education involves community effort with primary responsibility resting on individuals and families. Recommends establishing performance levels, independent achievement assessment services, student choice in courses, and separating credentials from teaching.


About education

July 29, 2014

Conclusions and recommendations

Definitions:

  1. Education – any experience that has a formative effect on the way we feel, think or act

  2. Schooling – learning under the direction of teachers


Conclusions-Education:

  1. We are all on the road of life where most fill roles of individuals, family member, citizen, learner, consumer and producer.

  2. Each person carries his/her own combination of traits based on genetics, culture, health, emotional make-up, previous experiences and imagined future.

  3. Education involves development and change in attitudes, skills and knowledge.

  4. Education and schooling are not the same; we are educated whether or not we go to school

  5. The role of government is to ensure a quality education for every Islander

  6. Of all sectors, education is the least open to market forces; there are no apparent benefits to any stakeholder for producing the same quality in a shorter period of time or producing a better quality.

  7. It takes a community to educate a citizen; while the primary responsibility for education rests with one’s self, the major contributor is the home.


Conclusions-Schooling:

  1. Schools continue to focus on delivery of knowledge;

  2. Parents want their children to be educated not just schooled;

  3. The Importance of institutions (schools, colleges and universities) in acquiring an education is on the decline

  4. The role of schools and teachers is to help people change;

  5. credentials and qualifications should not be determined or awarded by those who teach.

  6. We have a school system masquerading as an education system;

  7. The education system lack incentives required to make necessary changes

  8. Teaching is a development process with the role of teachers being to help students learn skills required on the road of life

Recommendations:

  1. Develop performance levels for math, reading and writing

  2. Establish an independent service to establish student achievement levels

  3. Allow students to challenge all required courses for credit

  4. Provide each school the autonomy needed to make decisions about day to day operations including the selection and assignment of staff

  5. Allow students to attend any school in Province where space allows

  6. Switch from outcomes to competencies at learner level

  7. Increase investment in other community educational agencies

  8. Government to remove itself from direct operation of schools

  9. Ban the practice of schools awarding diplomas
