Substantive letter from Don to Ken Gillis (August 22, 2006) providing detailed feedback on IAHSP wind-up planning. Don emphasizes the importance of project timelines, requests greater detail on participant status and remaining completion requirements, and identifies needed wind-up activities including final reports and thank-you letters. He notes that John Robinson had been informed of the December 31, 2006 deadline and suggests this requires update if timeline changes. The letter demonstrates Don's systematic project management approach and accountability to stakeholders.
Professional Career & Consulting

Date: August 22, 2006


To: Ken Gillis


From: Don Glendenning


Subject: IAHSP 


Sorry for the delay in responding; thanks for the draft paper on the windup of the IAHSP Program.  


Inclusion of the timelines is important because they point out some of the restrictions to finishing on time that were not of our making.  


Katy’s document mentions the likely status of each participant as of December 31.  I’d like to see more detail.  Specifically, what will remain for each participant to complete beyond the end of the contract?  The list should include their final reports.


As Katy points out, there are also project wind-up activities – the settlement of accounts, the preparation of a final report, thank you letters and a wind-up of files.  These also need to be identified in some detail.


Although Katy titles her draft “a report of possibilities”, in fact, the paper only offers one phase-out scenario and that deals only with project administration.  Assuming that this paper is for discussion purposes by the Board, I’d be inclined to deal first with wind-up activities of participants.  With that clear in our mind, we can then turn our attention to winding up the project.


You will recall that John Robinson met with the IAHSP Board some time ago to discuss our phase-out plans.  At that time, he was told that the project would be wound up by December 31, 2006.  If this is no longer the case, John should be alerted so that discussions can commence with DOCH.


I hope these comments will be helpful.


Regards


Don
