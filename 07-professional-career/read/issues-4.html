About 4-11

May 23, 2023

It is widely recognized that education is a key factor in determining each citizen’s quality of life; in the same way, the collective education of our citizens will determine to a significant degree the quality of the communities in which we live. Government support is justified for two reasons, one to support personal growth and development for every citizen; the other is to ensure the common good. Educating is a complex matter made so by the fact that each person is unique in many ways, unique in basic physical, mental, emotional make-up; unique in the range and nature of previous experience, unique in perceived futures, but also unique in terms of learning pace, style and needs.Given the numbers served and the wide range of prior experience, capabilities and interests of learners, designing and operating an education system is no easy task. These complexities have given rise to the following structural issues: 


Terminology – Educators are creative people; they try to accommodate a number of concepts with a single term. Learning excellence as an example. On the other hand, the writer seldom finds definitions in educational writings nd certainly  is left with the impression that going to school, in all its configurations, naturally leads to a good education. Confusing education and schooling


Purpose – Purpose provides the fundamental reason for establishing an organization, a reason for which some-thing is done or created; it is also the key element in the structuring process and one from which all others follow. To be meaningful, a purpose statement must be easy to understand, explain why the work is important, contribute to the alignment of activities, and be manageable. 

Structure – Educ is a service. Typically, the structure of organizations is based on the dictum “form follows function”. The Department seems to have ignored this rule.  As a result, some services have been left out. “form follows function” is the usual basis for structuring an organization; that’s why purpose is so important. Preparing for employment, furthering ones academic studies, knowledge and skills of citizenship are frequent purposes. Departments of education need leadership in th evarious functions  function a citizens or just to be self-sufficient in their future years. The struc-ture of the Australian De-partment of Education re-flects a number of live roles – childhood development, cit-izenship studies, employ-ment preparation  the educa-tion task.  Context

Education has entered a new area; our “education system” needs to be updated.  fact, I  would argue that we no longer have an edu-caton sytem but a number of school systems instead.  Let’s en-gage Islanders in the change pro-cess. If you do this, and it is open to the public, count me in.

Autonomy – the Organization for Economic Co-operation and Development did a study of PISA results from 13 countries and found that  students from schools with autonomy in  their day to day operation had better PISA results. 

Accountability – According to Anderson (See below) there are three main types of accountability – compliance, adherence to professional norms and results. Organizational accountability is about defining an organization’s mission, values and goals as well as everyone’s role in working towards them; holding yourself, and everyone else,  responsible for accomplishing goals, completing assignments and making decisions that deliver on expectations;  people resist and fail to support what they don’t understand; culture of accountability. Start with clarity and set expectations; be goal focused, hire the right people, Communicate with transparency. team and individual productivity, builds trust and collaboration, helps organization perform . Martin Bedard, Montreal Economic Institute, tells us that when teachers and principals have greater autonomy and accountability, students generally achieve better grades, even when standards living are considered.”  Autonomy, it seems, results in higher levels of satisfaction among teachers, better serves students with different learning styles, the article also points out that the high level of pedagogical autonomy in Quebec increased autonomy and accountability is sometimes credited with higher levels of contributes to their success in national and international tests.
