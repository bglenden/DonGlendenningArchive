Don follows up with Mary Jean Irving, CEO of Master Packaging Inc., on his education initiative. He apologizes for delays caused by family matters (a death in the New Brunswick branch) and updates her on ongoing efforts: consultations with education officials, an active reading improvement group, scheduled public discussion sessions, an upcoming visit from the Deputy Minister of Education, and plans for school awards and workshops. The letter demonstrates Don's commitment to persistence despite personal challenges and his ability to build momentum for his reform agenda.

Dear Ms. Irving:


This is a follow-up to my letter of February 1 about an initiative to bring about change in education.  In that letter, I indicated that I would be back to you shortly; unfortunately the call got delayed due to a spate of bad weather, a death in the New Brunswick branch of the family and a trip south.  


I am pleased to report, however, that our efforts continue:

  • Consultation with officials is on-going
  • Our group on reading met last week and will meet again on May 2
  • Two public discussion sessions are scheduled for April
  • The Deputy Minister of Education will speak to our group in late April
  • We have plans for a school award and public workshops for later in the year.
  • We are also exploring our role in the matter of skill training.


I would welcome an opportunity to brief you on my findings and views on education, share future plans, answer your questions, but also solicit your support; I’ll be in touch in a few days seeking an appointment.


Sincerely



Don Glendenning

President
