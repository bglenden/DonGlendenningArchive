Don convened colleagues in 1991 to identify critical secondary education issues. The group recognized education as vital to economic prosperity, human development, and international competitiveness. Don identifies critical issues: computer technology implementation, teacher role transformation, accountability systems, educational leadership, serving disadvantaged students, and inter-level coordination. Recommended demonstration projects include developing lifetime achievement records, improving communication skills, establishing open learning systems, challenging credits, autonomous agencies for 15+ students without certificates, and comparative teacher studies. Co-authored by Hall, Thomas, Glendenning, Baker, and Malmberg.
