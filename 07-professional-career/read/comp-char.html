Reference document outlining ten characteristics of a competency in standards context. Competencies must be action-oriented, preceded by "A person is able to...", derived from occupational analysis, verifiable through performance, observable, readily understood, expert-driven, and assessed against criteria. Core framework used throughout Don's standards work.

CHARACTERISTICS OF A COMPETENCY




1 Can be preceded by “A person is able to ...


2 Derived from an analysis of a field, discipline, or occupation


3 Verifiable through performance


4 Begins with an action word


5 Readily understood by relevant community


6 Expert driven


7 Performance of a competency is observable


8 Action oriented


9 Reflects the application of knowledge


10 Assessed against performance criteria
