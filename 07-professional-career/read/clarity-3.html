EDUCATION: A CALL FOR CLARITY-3

Draft letter

About 4-11

_____________________________________________________________________________


Education is complex, it is also compulsory (at least school attendance is compulsory). Learners have different interests, capabilities, experiences and needs; every Islander is said to fill six life roles that of a citizen, family member, individual, student, consumer and producer; education can bolster all these roles. but may require different approaches. Purpose is key to determining what is taught and how success is measured.


Some examples

  1. Alberta Education is “preparing citizens of tomorrow”

  2. New Brunswick is helping citizens gain “attributes needed to be a lifelong learner”

  3. Anon – “Preparing for life, work and citizenship.”

  4. PEI is helping young people “find a place in society”


I can visualize undertaking a community survey to identify knowledge and skills used by citizens in every day work; I can also visualize assessing a student’s level of preparation to be a citizen. I must confess, however, that I would find it difficult to select content to prepare a student to “secure a place”.


Education is important to both individual Islanders and to the community in which we work. A clear purpose would make a positive contribution to our education efforts.

Sincerely

Do Glendenning



Neither is an adequate base for building an education system or measuring student progress.


PEI needs an updated statement as o what it means bt education and what it hopes to gain by supporting it; only then can we decide what to teach, when, where and how. Clarity is a first step in improvement.


Sincerely


Education is complex. Learners have different interests, capabilities, experiences and needs; They also fill a number of life roles, that of an Individual, family member, citizen, student, consumer and producer; education is needed or may help with any and all.

Education is also an umbrella word; a person’s education includes all the experiences that have changed a life and is demonstrated by our actions; Our education depends, to a large degree, on our experiences at home, in our community, through travel, interactions with others, personal exploration and roles we play in life. A good education has been described as one that provides a person with the knowledge, skills. attitudes and other attributes needed to deal with circumstances that confront us in our- every day lives; but there are other definitions as well

An education system consists of programs and services put in place to organize and deliver education services; typical tools of change include information, instruction; diagnosis, counselling, quality control, products and technology.

Schools are an important component of any education system; they focus on instruction. Attendance is required by law. Like a hospital or spa, schools are purpose-built facilities that house the people and services needed to support education. Having a well-equipped school is important but it is the service that impacts one’s education.

It is said that education should help us develop the attitudes. skills, knowledge and other attributes that will allow us to thrive in later life but, to borrow a phrase from a sister Province, should each child leave school “having mastered the tools to learn”?

Sincerely


Don Glendenning
