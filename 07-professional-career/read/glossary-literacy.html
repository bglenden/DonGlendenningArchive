Literacy Notes


Glossary


Literacy is defined as the ability to use printed information at home, at work, and in the community to achieve one’s goals and develop ones knowledge and potential. On the other hand, the HRD uses the term, essential skills which includes reading text, using documents, using numbers, writing, speaking, listening, working with others, thinking and using a computer. There is considerable overlap but also some differences. For the purpose of these notes, I will focus on literacy since that appears to be the term most widely used in the public domain when talking about the subject.


Traditional descriptions identify the following components or kinds of literacy:

  • Prose - information contained in text such as newspapers, magazines, textbooks and tourist literature.

  • Document – information in the form of maps, charts and employment forms,


Literacy Skills may be demonstrated through one or a combination of the following skills:

  • Speaking – the ability to obtain, provide and exchange information through speech and gesture

  • Writing – ability to provide information in the written form

  • Demonstrating – ability to convey ideas through gesture

  • Computing – ability to us computers to gather, manipulate and present information

  • Listening – ability to gather information through listening

  • Reading – ability to gather information through reading

  • Problem solving – the ability to analyze information related to a given situation and generate appropriate responses

  • Manipulating data – the ability to find and manipulate numbers in relation to such tasks as keeping household records, placing an order, checking a grocery bill and tipping for a pizza delivery.
