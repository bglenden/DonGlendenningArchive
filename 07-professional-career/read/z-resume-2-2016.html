Some notes about


Donald Glendenning



Don Glendenning moved to Prince Edward Island in 1969 to become the founding President of Holland College.  For a number of years he had taught in the elementary and secondary schools of New Brunswick, been head of a teacher training program for vocational teachers also in New Brunswick, and worked as a training specialist with the Federal Government in Ottawa. 


He was born in Little Branch, a farming community near Chatham on the Miramichi in New Brunswick. He attended the Little Branch School, the Miramichi Rural High School, Teachers' College and the New Brunswick Institute of Technology (now the New Brunswick Community College); he also studied a at the University of London (England) and Bradley University and Indiana University in the USA


In 1987 Dr. Glendenning retired from Holland College and operated a consulting firm undertaking with projects both in Canada and overseas.  He is a founding member and Past President of the Community Foundation of Prince Edward Island, is an amateur genealogist and enjoys gardening, reading and puttering around.  He has edited and published a number of newsletters, is the recipient of a number of awards and honours including the Order of Canada, Queen’s Jubilee Medal, Atlantic Canada Innovator of the Year Award Finalist and holds an honourary degree from the University of Prince Edward Island.  He served on the executive of a number of local and national organizations and has also has a number of publications to his credit


Since 2012 Dr. Glendenning has taken a leadership role in education reform through the establishment of Education 20/20 Inc., a national, independent educational organization committed to ensuring a quality education for every Canadian.  He and his wife, Carol, live in Charlottetown and have two children and two grandchildren.
