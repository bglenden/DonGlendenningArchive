SOME NOTES ON SPEAKING

By

Don Glendenning


About this paper:

This paper is intended as background to discussions about the improvement of communication skills among Islanders of all ages.  Our review of the literature, discussion with people both inside and outside the formal education system and our own beliefs support the view that communication skills are important, that they can be learned, and can be improved through formal instruction.  For the purpose of this initiative, we view the key components as reading, writing, speaking and listening; while usually taught in combination, for the purposes of clarity, each will be dealt with separately.  This is a draft document and expected to continue in that form as we learn more and more about the subject and improvements that are likely to arise are in place and working effectively. 


Speaking:

  • The act of conveying information in spoken language.


Background:

Good listening skills result in better communication skills overall, for listeners and for speakers. Evaluating listening skills requires knowledge of what constitutes good listening--attention given to the speaker, active listening by note-taking, avoiding external distraction, body language and asking follow-up questions.
