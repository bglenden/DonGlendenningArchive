A comprehensive three-stage strategy for establishing non-aligned public community groups focused on reading improvement. Stage One emphasizes public engagement through discussion papers, workshops, and media outreach. Stage Two includes establishing a federal charter society with membership, identifying reading as a separate subject in teacher training and certification, and creating qualification authorities for reading assessment. Stage Three involves creating training programs, publications, and community services like reading round tables and library tutoring. Don's proposal encompasses teacher professional development, system redesign through report cards and benchmarks, community information campaigns, and research documentation. This represents his most ambitious reading reform vision, integrating multiple intervention levels from policy to grassroots community action.
