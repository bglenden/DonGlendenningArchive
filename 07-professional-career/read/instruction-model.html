(155 words):** Don Glendenning presents a competency-based instructional model emphasizing practical skill development aligned with field practice. The model includes identifying work-relevant competencies, establishing performance standards, creating advisory committees from served communities, using dynamic rating scales, competency-by-competency progress reporting, learner profiles, self-evaluation, and community engagement. Key features include basing programs and assessments on demonstrated performance rather than time served, providing individualized tutoring as needed, and diagnosing learner progress through discussion and learning plans. This model reflects Don's belief that education should focus on demonstrated competency rather than seat time, addressing individual learner needs, and maintaining strong community connections—themes recurring throughout his reform proposals.

INSTRUCTION MODEL

4/Binder 5

Instructional models are underlying principles, guidelines or beliefs which guide instruction. based;

  • Base content on competencies practised by target population

  • Strike profiles as appropriate

  • Establish performance levels required for recognition

  • Ensure conceptual clarity

  • Appoint an advisory committee from community being served

  • Adopt a dynamic rating scale

  • Report student progress on a competency by competency basis

  • Provide each student with a master list of competencies as a basis for personal planning and tracking

  • Require students to self rate first

  • Adopt a learning context

  • Engage/collaborate with community

  • Base assessment on performance only

  • Provide a descriptor for each competency

  • Include application/practice for each competency

  • Report/document progress at competency level.
