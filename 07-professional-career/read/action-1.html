PEI EDUCATION

Action 1


I have been in education all my life; recently I have been exploring public education; not many days pass without my having a conversation about the subject.


Education is a large, multifaceted, people centered set of services. That in itself complicates communication but understanding is “fuzzified” further as per the following:

  1. There is a tendency to use education and school/schooling interchangeably; World Bank called attention to it in their 2018 report. It is causing problems on PEI as well.

  2. Seems to me a Depatrment of Education needs a definition of education. My search of Department literature did not turn up a definition of Education. In fact, I was informed that a definition of education was left out of the PEI Schools AcL during a recent revision. A working definition is needed for clarity and should be developed and distributed generously throughout the system.

  3. The Department describes its role as one of “developng and delivering high quality programs and curriculum.” While the Department does this, I suggest that the the Department’s role is much, much greater, that of helping Islanders gain new information insights and skills.

  4. Government structure Departments around branches or fields of work; In the case of education, however, structuring is based on levels of schooling – a major diversion from its real role.

  5. “Form follows function “is an organizational maxim. The function of public education, I suggest, is to help Islanders gain knowledge, attitudes, skills and other attributes needed to thrive in future years. Instead, The Department of Education seems to be organized around the running of schools


The proposed changes should simply be introduced as part of the daily business of the Department.


Sincerely

Don Glendenning.



Feb 18/24
