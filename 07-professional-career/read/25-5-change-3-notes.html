Enabling Change


Widespread  call  for change in education , not just a single or random occurance or event but a constant condition  - organizations need to embrace change and keep themselves ready at all time Reasons are many including Outside regulations, economics, increase choice, or client demands.  In the case of education, widespread call for change from both wirhin and outside the profession and include:


  • Focus on schooling rather than education
  • Diffused and obscured accountability
  • Lack of public engagement
  • Focus on time  rather than achievement
  • Degree of learner centeredness
  • Encouraging innovation
  • System lacks market mechanisms
  • Support for independent learners
  • Little attention to skills
  • Widespread support for system change
  • Inability of system to change itself
  • System is bureaucratic and top directed
  • Help for parents and their infants 
  • Easy access to certification/credit for prior and self-directed learning
  • Support for learners who are not students
  • Over emphasis on credentials


Critical elements

  • Change agent
  • Action system
  • Innovation proposal
  • Action plan


Steps

  • Start with an idea
  • Identify problems to address
  • Develop a  clear vision of the future alternative. 
  • Establish primary goals
  • Raise awareness of alternatives
  • Muster support including people of influence
  • Develop action plan
  • Identify assets available and needed
  • Implement plan
  • Review risk
  • Evaluate progress

These organizations have cultures that foster innovation and collaboration and accept risk and failure. The leaders must help employees understand why change is necessary, create an environment of open communication and build teams with complementary competencies and strengths.

To become a change-ready organization, Musselwhite and Plouffe said leaders should assess their organization’s:

  • Raise awareness of alternatives - A company’s ability to redefine itself as necessary. … Good change awareness practices include scanning the environment for opportunities, focusing on emerging trends and planning for the future.”
  • Engagement of others “A company’s ability to engage people in pending changes. … An organization with good change agility has the capacity to stretch when necessary and quickly shift resources to the place they make the most difference.”
  • Change Reaction: A company’s “ability to appropriately analyze problems, assess risks and mange the reactions of employees. This internal focus ensures your company can sustain the day-to-day business while reacting in a timely and appropriate manner to self-initiated and market-dictated change.”
  • Develop Change Mechanisms: A company’s “ability to integrate a change into existing systems, accountability for results and reward systems that reinforce desired change behaviors. This contextual focus is critical to the ability to implement desired change with no interruption to daily operation.”
  • Provide rewards compensate people in creative ways for delivering new values

Education First - L'education avant tout !

Framework for Educational Change


Education - any and all experiences that have a formative effect on a person’s attitude, knowledge or skill.  


Quality - in education is measured by how well one is able to function well in one’s various life roles


Life Roles* – during a lifetime, each person normally fills the role of individual, family member, learner, citizen, producer and consumer.


Schools – a major, but not the only, provider of educational services 


Issues:

  • Focus on schooling rather than education
  • Diffused and obscured accountability
  • Lack of public engagement
  • Focus on time  rather than achievement
  • Degree of learner centeredness
  • Encouraging innovation
  • System lacks market mechanisms
  • Support for independent learners
  • Little attention to skills
  • Widespread support for system change
  • Inability of system to change itself
  • System is bureaucratic and top directed
  • Help for parents and their infants 
  • Easy access to certification/credit for prior and self-directed learning
  • Support for learners who are not students
  • Over emphasis on credentials

Enabling Change:

  • Public engagement 
  • External qualification authority
  • Realigned roles and responsibilities/accountabilities
  • Modernized delivery model
  • Increased market forces
  • Rewards for improvement

*Oregon Education Act, 1991
