Dear Sir: 

Assessing education for employment


We up our education for many reasons – we want to increase or income, change jobs, be more mobile, understand our origins or surroundings, etc. etc. etc. many reasons are simply to meet our own circumstance and needs some reasons reflect the wishes or requirements of other; further education and education for paid employment fall under the latter.


Employment preparatory courses us usually use employment-based criteria when performance, such as:

• Quality

• Quantity

• Problem solving

• Leadership, and

• Dependability

Some employment-based rating scales in-clude both assistance and supervision; rat-ing on academic subjects tends to be based on written tests. And, therefore, are less applicable to employment.


Don Glendenning

A long-time student of education
