Arts workshop


When IAHSP ended, there was an outstanding payment from the Royal Bank of $3,000 on its commitment.  Because the Bank does not contribute to endowments, I suggested that, instead, we accept the contribution  and use it for purposes similar to that for which it was originally intended.  Royal Bank accepted the proposal.  


Since then, I have had discussions with DOCH and the Province about an Arts Workshop in the fall of 2008  My plan is to use the $3,000 as seed money and lever some additional funds including some overhead for CFPEI.  I am in touch with participants about a follow-up activity..


Ideas put forward included:


Adding Value; continuing; extending; consolidating

Linking arts and business; fundraising, marketing studies, donor base development, volunteer management, strategic planning, governance, expertise  development in specific areas such as touring and financial management

Supporting long term sustainability; 

capacity building covers governance, management practices, financial sufficiency, development of new and diversified audiences

Components: Development, capacity building, networking, governance, management practices, 

Arts/industry partnership, matching of need and expertise/ loan/assignment of business people to arts organizations

Feasibility study of  a Professional Partners Program for the Arts – short term placements of professionals from business to arts organizations.

IAHSP:  very much a work in progress; goal setting and monitoring, quarterly financial review, training for ED; Information exchange among organizations; 


Also hahd some discussions with Arsenault, MacDearmid, Langille about the federal matching grant for endowment building but decided not to pursue it further.


Now focused on a workshop in late 2008 for participants in the IAHSP program.  Sent enquiry to see if there is any intertest;  five emails sent, only Scott macauley responded


One thing I suggested at a previous workshop was for IAHSP Program organizations to come together in producing a pamphlet that would be circulated in the waiting areas and offices of Allied Professionals(doctors, lawyers, accountants, financial planners).

 

The pamphlet might be called…Leave An Artistic Legacy...and each organization would have their own dedicated panel describing their mission, reason for being, registered charitable number(if any) board members, web site, etc. 

 

This document would plant the seed in readers to consider leaving a legacy in their wills, through life insurance policies, stocks or properties etc. during their lives or in their estates.

 

The Community Foundation could be the catalyst bringing all of these independent groups under one umbrella or assist in marketing the organizations as separate 

Independent entities.
