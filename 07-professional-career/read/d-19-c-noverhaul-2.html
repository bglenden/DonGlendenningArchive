Restructuring Called For

No. D-17 -C June 4, 2020

Quality:

Restructuring Called For


Our future as a Province and as citizens will be determined in a large measure by the nature and extent of the education of our citizens, yet the Province’s plan for education is unclear, at least to the writer.; let me explain.


PEI has some 160,000 citizens; The literature tells us that each person is unique and each person in a community Islanders need to be literate and numerate, to become independent learners, to take care of themselves and learn how to work and play together. Education’s business is to help Islanders make changes in their lives.


Provinces recognize the importance of education and governments usually organize a department to deal with education matters. On top of this, one must add the legal requirement, not for a level a level of education but that Islanders must attend school until they reach age 17 or 18.


This is a complex mix of people, interests, strengths, personal and family views It is nature of services and the way in which they are organized that cause the writer’s confusion.


Name of Department: Education is an umbrella term; it includes education of all kinds and at all levels. Adding “lifelong Learning and Early Childhood Development” seem redundant.


Definitions: A glossary would be in useful.


Purpose: is about direction, what one hopes to achieve. The paper on philosophy published by the Department shows the purpose as one of providing “for the development of children so that each child may take a meaningful place in society.” New Brunswick states iits purpose as helping ”each student develop the attributes needed to be a life long learner, to achieve personal fulfilment and to contribute to a productive, just and democratic society.


Goals: and purposes are interlinked the difference being that goals have a time frame.

“Educational services usually include information, counselling, quality assurance, materials and technology, Given the abobe, Educational services usually include information, counselling, quality assurance, materials and technology, Given yjr above, the current goal seems narrow indeed. And again, it fails to serve the bulk of Islanders.


Youth under 17: Preparing to write this letter really brings home the narrowing of education mandate over the past 50 years; it also supports the World Bank concerns about ssome Ministries and Departments of Education confusing “going to school with getting an education.


I first learned about structural impediments from a trustee a number of years ago; I really did not understand it until I drafted this letter. Only when these are dealt with, can steps to improvement begin.













Definitions: The World Bank pointed out recently that some “Ministries of Education” confuse “going to school” with getting an education”; I’ve encountered this on PEI. In fact, my impression is that the misunderstanding is widespread within the education system.


















Structure: Sound organization practice suggests that “form follows function.” meaning that functions form the basis of the operating structure. I was not able to locate an organization chart for the Department, however, information at hand suggests that the current organization buries some of the important function elsewhere in the bureaucracy. to the extent that Department might more aptly be considered a Department of Public Instruction.


The Department of Education has a major role to play in ensuring that Islanders receive the help they need to cope with the present and prepare for the future. Embedding the operation en-toto into an operating Government Department, has resulted in the emasculation of professional autonomy and accountability; I suggest a review of recommendations by the OECD ( ) and discussion with senior officials would be useful in any review. The recent restructuring of Health Services may provide ideas and experiences of interest and value.






























































Education is an umbrella term, yet the Department describes its role as one of developing and delivering programs and curriculum to Island youth. Certainly programs are useful but the goal of the Department is greater than that - to help Islanders increase or improve their abilities, not only to cope but to thrive. in the years ahead.

The writer struggles with Department of Education documents; descriptions don’t seem to match what I hear on the street, documents obscure rather than enlighten, words and phrases seem to carry different meanings, definitions are seldom given, concepts I thought I understood now carried different meanings. Some smart entrepreneur has even developed and promotes a “jargon generator” for education writers.

Education services on PEI need an overhaul; I services are narrowly focused, cognitively based, bureaucratic, lack accountability, and focus on program delivery rather than on learner development – they also lack a self-correcting mechanism. I do not, we cannot, blame the current government, the previous government or any single government for the current status; Government has a responsibility however, for instituting corrective measures.
