EDUCATION FIRST CANADA

Background Notes


Education:

  • An important factor in determining one’s quality of life
  • Education of our citizens collectively will determine to a significant degree the future of our community and country.  
  • Education is a life-long process of engaging in experiences that have a formative effect on the way one feels, thinks and acts, and is usually expressed in terms of a person’s attitude, knowledge and skill.
  • Our education system is focused on schooling  
  • Credentials and qualifications are not synonamous
  • The public wants meaningful engagements in bringing about changes in education  
  • Change must be immediate, significant and continuous, but planned.  
  • Most criticism of education focuses on the operation of the school system
  • Changes to the school system should always, within available resources, be made with individual learner in mind

Our vision:

  • Learner centered 
  • Flexible
  • Transparent
  • User friendly
  • Focused on learning
  • Responsive  
  • Accountable


Enabling change includes:

  • Providing relevant information.  
  • Engaging the Community   
  • Encouraging and supporting best practices   
  • Recognizing leadership of people, organizations and institutions.  
  • Encouraging and supporting research.









Focus:

  • Public
  • Providers – schools, home, post secondary institutions
  • Levels – ECD, ECE, elementary, secondary, post secondary, continuing 
  • Purposes – further education, life, citizenship, individual development
  • Stakeholders – students, learners, taxpayer, parents, 
  • Process


First Steps:

  • A (printable) electronic newsletter
  • Occasional papers
  • Pilot a public participation activity using community engagement software. 
  • Structured local discussion groups
  • Conversations with educators
  • One or more school based awards for best practices
  • Leadership activity


  • Gathering and disseminating information
  • Focus: report cards, reading, basic skills, preparing for employment
  • Clientele: Public, parents, teachers, administrators, 
  • Media: local media, electronic or print, 
  • Format:  newsletter, blog, press releases, opinion pieces, guest articles, workshops 


  • Notes:
  • Our goal is to foster/support improvements in the system
  • Initiatives are our raison d’etre
  • Needed to help with fund raising
  • Try to involve UPEI education students in out initiatives, new teachers
  • Resources needed to mount initiatives
  • National focus may reduce concerns of Island teachers
  • As we shape ideas, it may be useful to consult broader group
  • Call for papers/nominations versus self-nomination
  • Invite others to join working groups
  • Even replicating the discussion of last night would have value.
