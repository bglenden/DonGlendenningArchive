Proposal for developing an integrated platform for essential skills curriculum and training delivery. Identifies eight core essential skills (literacy, numeracy, science, self-management, information technology, personal relationships, community awareness, emotional awareness) and proposes DACUM-style analysis. Reflects Don's educational philosophy regarding foundational competencies needed for work entry-level positions across PEI.


Essential skills

About-S



Purpose:

Develop an integrated platform for developing curriculum and delivering training in essential skills


Background

We are regularly reminded of the need for and state of basic skills for every day living, the foundation for learning all others. Essential skills provide the foundation needed for work, learning and life and enable us to evolve/thrive in our various life roles


Not surprising essential skills seem to have emerged from the three R’s, “Readin’, ‘Ritin’ and ‘Rithmetic; they’ve gone through a number of iterations and are now found under a variety of umbrellas. For the purpose of this paper, essential skills are considered to be:

  • Literacy

  • Numeracy

  • Everyday Science

  • Self Management

  • Information technology

  • Personal relationships

  • Community awareness

  • Emotional Awareness


Proposal:

  • Develop a DACUM-style analysis of essential skills deemed appropriate for the work-entry leve

  • Develop an accompanying dynamic/holistic rating scale for student and learner use.


Applications:

  • Development of learning guides

  • Tracking of student progress

  • Self rating document

  • Basis for training purchases

  • Aid to employee selection process

  • Career planning

  • Student Record of achievement

  • Levels of achievement


Phase 2 – test model


Based on the literature, discussions with math educators and the writer’s experience, the writer concludes that, with regard to mathematics:

  1. Instruction in PEI is fragmented

  2. There is no formal mechanism for coordination among math providers

  3. Evaluation criteria should be the same at all levels

  4. Instruction would benefit from a common curriculum format.

  5. Improvement requires cooperation of all math instruction providers.

  6. Current instructional model getting out of date – alignment, curriculum map and rating

  7. There is a willingness, if not an interest, among math providers in in cooperating on a pilot project


Applications of analysis:

  • Curriculum development

  • Student tracking

  • Counselling

  • Guide for tutors and tutoring

  • Record of achievement

  • Course planning

  • Hiring new staff


Characteristics map:

  • Easily adapted to IT

  • Engages the community

  • Uses community language

  • Counselling tool

  • Minimal cost


Benefits:

  • All parties working from same document

  • Engages community

  • Ensures relevance / engages
