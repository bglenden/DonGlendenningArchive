ABOUT THE PROPOSER


Donald E. M. Glendenning



Donald Glendenning was born in New Brunswick, graduated from Miramichi Rural High School, Teachers College, New Brunswick Technical Institute, completed a year of study at London University (England), holds a Bachelor’s and Master’s Degree from Bradley University  and a PhD. from Indiana University.


His experience includes teaching at the elementary, secondary and post secondary levels, a high school principalship, a technical training specialist with the Government of Canada, 


Dr Glendenning was the Founding President of Holland College and, under his direction for 18 years, Holland College was a recognized leader in outcomes based education, performance based assessment, flexibility and responsiveness to both learner and community needs.  In addition to the usual college level programs, Dr. Glendenning brought into being the Atlantic Police Academy, Culinary Institute of Canada and a School of visual Arts. 


Upon leaving Holland College, he established his own consulting firm and carried on projects both at home and overseas.  Canadian projects were many and varied the largest being the development of a competency based model for secondary education.  His overseas work included projects in Kenya, Swaziland, India, Pakistan and the ASEAN Region.


Dr. Glendenning was an active player on the national and local scene, has written  and spoken extensively.  Most recently, he played leadership role in the establishment of the Community Foundation of Prince Edward Island and still serves as a Director.  In the latter case, he led a partnership with the Federal and Provincial Governments and the private sector to implement a stabilization initiative in the arts.
