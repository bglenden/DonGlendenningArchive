Working document examining the theoretical basis for proficiency level assessment, referencing Robert Paul Wolff's classification of assessment, evaluation, and ranking as distinct functions. Don proposes models like apprenticeships and international certifications where teaching and qualification are separated. He advocates for an independent assessment service with clear characteristics including laddering, field-based level identification, and individual ownership of results.

Proficiency levels in education

Educators: Mind your own business; Line of work; the business of teachers


Background

Rise of assessment:

  • No child Left Behind
  • International testing
  • Impact of information technology
  • Move to social promotion
  • Labour mobility – credentials
  • Gap


Robert Paul Wolf (Wolf: A discourse on Grading, 1969) identified three purposes for

school assessment:

  • Analysis or criticism (formative) designing additional strategies in support of learning.  analysis prepares a teacher to provide meaningful feedback and is part of the ongoing give and take of the learning process
  • Evaluation- to establish a person’s level in a subject or field, criterion referenced
  • Ranking – how one stacks up against others


Assessment versus evaluation

Assessment change in learning teaching and outcomes, interactive between teacher; intended to improve learning “information is learner-centered, course based frequently anonymous and not graded”   Duke University


Evaluation focuses on grades and may reflect classroom components other than course content and mastery level – duke Edu




Current thinking

  • Learning path clearly shows that credentialing and Qualification separate from teaching
  • Qualification authority

Conclusions:

  • Levels should be identified through analysis of subject or field
  • School documentation inadequate to task
  • School has other roles that to provide knowledge
  • Evaluation is separate from teaching


Proposal


Assessment service

  • Independent
  • Reduces bias
  • Improves consistency
  • Reduces teacher workload
  • Removes conflict of interest


Characteristics:

  • Independent
  • Laddering
  • Identify levels through analysis if field
  • Clients may be individuals or organizations
  • By application
  • Level independent of length of study
  • Awards a qualification not a credential
  • Results belong to the individual
