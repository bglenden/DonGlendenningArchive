An expanded exploration of education system missions globally, including statements from Maine, Bangor, Alberta, and other jurisdictions. Glendenning documents that PEI's education mission "captures neither the spirit, breadth, significance nor urgency of the education task." He identifies systemic problems: the department operates in "public instruction" mode rather than educational development, lacks engagement with 21st-century competencies, ignores soft skills, and shows no interest in broader Islander education. His conclusion: PEI's government is unclear about what it aims to accomplish through education. He proposes a new mission: "Help Islanders develop knowledge, skills and other attributes needed for life in 21st century" and recommends mission statement development as a valuable staff development activity.
