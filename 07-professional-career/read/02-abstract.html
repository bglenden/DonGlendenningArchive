Abstract of Don's manuscript on DACUM methodology, tracing its Canadian origins in the 1960s NewStart Corporations and its evolution into an internationally recognized competency-based education framework. Positions DACUM as significant Canadian contribution to education philosophy.

ABSTRACT


DACUM is an acronym for either Developing A CUrriculuM or Designing A CUrriculuM.  It is a Canadian term and a Canadian development.  The acronym is well-known in competency based education and curriculum development.  Its impact is international.  DACUM is widely practised, highly respected and easily identified.  Surprisingly, however, we know little of its origin.  Although coined only 25 years ago, the origin and development trail in Canada are somewhat obscure.   


During the 1960's, federal and provincial governments provided massive funding to establish and expand vocational training facilities and programs.  During this period a number of centres, NewStart Corporations, were established to demonstrate new and innovative ways of assisting communities with their social and economic development.


Within the framework of the NewStart Corporations considerable attention was focused on innovative approaches to training as a component of individual and community development.  Out of the NewStart activity came the concept of DACUM.


There are three common views of what constitutes DACUM, namely (1) a way of presenting curriculum, (2) a performance based analysis of a field or occupation, and (3) a model for developing and delivering competency based education.  The first and second views have been implemented widely.  The latter, full implementation, is not widely practised.  Most users of DACUM share one major characteristic - the entire analysis is presented in a single sheet visual format.


As a result of the review involved in preparing this document, the writer is satisfied that DACUM can easily be recognized as a significant Canadian contribution to education, especially the philosophy of education that builds a system on a foundation of competencies, outcomes or results.
