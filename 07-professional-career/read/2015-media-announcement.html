For immediate release


Open for nominations


Nominations are now being accepted by Education 20/20 Inc. for the Excellence in Education Award. The award in the amount of $2,500, donated anonymously in 2014 will recognize a school or learning centre in Prince Edward Island that best demonstrates engagement in its day to day activities. Anyone may nominate and the the award is open to any school or learning center operating at the K-12 level


Last year’s call for nominations, the first for the award, resulted in 11 nominations resulted in made last ear to kinkora Regional High School

The Award is based on the view that student achievement improves, teacher morale rises and communication increases when schools and communities work together. The focus of the award is on engagement in the daily operation of the school and only those items over which the school has substantial control such as: communication with family, culture, community; frequency of community based events and parent/community involvement in curriculum development and planning


Education 20/20 Inc. believes in the importance of engagement and the value of recognition, and has instituted the awards program as a mechanism to call the attention of the public to important characteristics of effective schools and to provide some financial support to schools for their efforts to implement them. For the purpose of the award, the following five areas of engagement will be considered: student to student engagement, student to teacher engagement, student to learning materials, student to community partnerships, and parental involvement.


An Awards Committee will be established to review nominations and recommend a recipient; decisions will be made solely on the basis of information presented. Anyone may nominate a school or learning centre, further information and nomination papers are may be downloaded from the Education 20/20 website, www.education2020.ca. Nominations close on March 29, 2014 with the award being given in early May.


Education 20/20 Inc. is a broad based, independent national, not-for-profit education organization, located in Charlottetown, PEI with an interest in improving the quality of education for every Islander.
