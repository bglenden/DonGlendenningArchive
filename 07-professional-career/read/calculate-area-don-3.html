Calculate area in metric and imperial units


Skill Overview: 

The metric system was developed about 200 years ago. It is a system based on multiples or submultiples of ten. Today it is estimated that 80% of the world trade is conducted using the metric system and that over 95% of the world's population uses the metric system. 

You as a person involved in the work field will be in the situation where you have to use both the English measurement system and the metric measurement system. You will also need to be adept at calculating both measurement systems.


Related Skills: 

  • None


Tools/Equipment/Materials: 

  • Measuring devises such as: scales, containers, tape measures, etc...

Skill Performance Objectives: 

Before you begin, conduct a self-assessment by reviewing each element of the Performance Objectives to establish your level of competence within the skill.  Compile documentation for PLAR (Prior Learning Assessment and Recognition) if applicable. 


1.   Measure weight and mass using the English and the metric systems 

Performance Standards: 

    • Demonstrate the use of the terminology 
    • Demonstrate use of the metric system of measures 
    • Demonstrate use of English measures 
    • Demonstrate ability to convert from one to the other 
    • Solve business problems with metric and English measurements
    • Demonstrate an understanding of the metric and English terms used for length and area.


2.   Measure distance and lengths using both measurement systems 

Performance Standards: 

    • Demonstrate ability to measure using metric system
    • Demonstrate ability to measure using English system
    • Convert metric system measurement to English measurement
    • Convert English system measurement to metric measurement


3.   Measure area using both measurement systems 

Conditions:

    • Using both measurement systems


Performance Standards: 

    • Measure lengths
    • Calculate
    • Area


Skill Learning Activities: 


Before you Begin: 

  • Review all the suggested Learning Activities in this Skill.
  • Select a variety of Learning Activities that will help you develop competency in this Skill.
  • Plan and organize an approach to carrying out the selected Learning Activities.
  • Please feel free to suggest additional Learning Activities to your Learning Manager.


Learning Activities: 

1.    Review related resources and define the following terms: 

  • Metric measurement system
  • English measurement
  • Meter
  • Length
  • Area
  • Kilo
  • Hecto
  • Deca
  • Base Unit
  • Deci
  • Centi
  • Milli
  • Imperial measurements

2.    Read the available resources and demonstrate your knowledge and competence in using the English measurement system. IE. Inches, feet, yards, miles, etc


3.    Read available resources and demonstrate your knowledge and competence in using the metric system.  IE meters and the derivatives there off…


4.    Using your knowledge and competency in both systems practice your skills by using the tools available to measure rooms,and /or areas using the following units of measure: 

  • Distance, metric and English
  • Lengths, metric and English
  • Area, metric and English


5.    Using your knowledge and competency in both systems practice your skills by converting the following: 

  • Distance, metric to English and vis versa
  • Lengths, metric to English and vis versa
  • Area, metric to English and vis versa


6.    Review an appropriate resource from a resource library or supervisor/manager and complete sufficient exercise and/or problems to demonstrate your competency on the English and metric systems.  


7.    Review your resources and/or work and make a list of the metric terms used for length, width, height, and area, and relate the metric and imperial measurement to these.
