Open Education

No. 100 December 21, 2020

It came upon a midnight clear


A SEASONAL MEMORY

I left home for Teachers’ College at age 17 on January 2, 1947; I started teaching in September still in New Brunswick and still at the age of 17. I had not planned to be a teacher but teaching and nursing were inexpensive transition routes in my community for young high school graduates. Besides that, my mother thought that study beyond high school would be good even if I never taught.


On that January morning as I packed for Fredericton, my mother handed me a little plaque for my bedside table. I still have it; it reads:


I shall pass this way but once

Any good thing therefore that I can do or kindness that I may show

Let me do it now

Let me not defer it nor neglect it

For I shall not pass this way again


While I may not have always followed the advice, it has been a factor in my decision-making ever since.
