Enumeration of standard education services provided by provincial education systems. Identifies eight core services: information provision, counseling, governance and leadership, instruction, quality assurance, products and technology, funding, and human resources. Each service includes brief explanation of function within education ecosystem. Recognizes education as provincial responsibility with services appropriately customized to regional contexts. Presents foundational framework for understanding what comprehensive education systems should provide beyond schooling.

Typical Education Services

4-16



Education is a provincial responsibility; roles may be defined in a variety of ways. Typical services include:

  • Information - Recent and independent information needed by Islanders in the making of decisions about their own education or the education of others

  • Counselling – help to people making decision about their education needs

  • Governance and Leadership - A body with vision and responsibility to put in place and monitor educational services for the benefit of its citizens,

  • Instruction - The provision of help to others in their learning that includes diagnosis, counselling, presentations, practice and access generally to resources

  • Quality Assurance - A system that assures Islanders that their learning experiences, including providers of those services, meet community standards.

  • Products and Technology - Access by Islanders to educational materials, technology and other resources to help learners develop skills needed to meet their responsibilities and objectives.

  • Funding - Funding to a level and in a manner that is equitable for all Islanders.

  • Human Resources = Teachers, administrators, counsellors, advisors. assistants and others who help people learn
