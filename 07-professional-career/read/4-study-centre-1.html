Comprehensive proposal for community study centers designed to support youth educational achievement. The proposal outlines a phased implementation: Phase 1 (supervised study space), Phase 2 (with tutors), Phase 3 (with instruction/teaching), Phase 4 (plus counselling). Centers would operate under a local governing committee, provide safe facilities with internet, employ coordinators and managers, offer professional staff and coaching, and provide supportive services. The educational philosophy emphasizes helping youth acquire skills for community participation. Budget categories include space rental, tutors, resource staff, supplies, and materials. This reflects Don's systems thinking about educational support.

Background:

  1. Educating is a process of helping people gain the skills needed to participate in the life of a community and includes attitudes, skills and knowledge.

  2. Schools are time based learning institutions and have difficulty in accommodating different learning styles.

  3. A local study centres will provide both space and help for young people with planning and acquiring the education needed to reach their individual education goals



Purpose - to provide youth of a community with space and help needed to meet educational goals:

  1. Phase 1 -Supervised study space

  2. Phase 2 – as above with tutors

  3. Phase 3 – as above with availability of instruction/teaching

  4. Phase 4 – as above with the addition of counselling for students and parents



Operation:

  1. A local “governing” committee

  2. A safe and secure facility, supervised and welcoming with internet connections, and a quiet/discussion corner

  3. An activities coordinator and site manager who, with the aid of a user committee, sets and maintains a positive learning climate.

  4. Services in support of learning, available directly or by referral

  5. Security cleared professional staff available as needed supported by coaches and tutors
