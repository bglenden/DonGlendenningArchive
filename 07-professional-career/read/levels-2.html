This document focuses specifically on achievement levels in literacy and numeracy, chosen because they are foundational to modern society, unique in their educational roles, and critical budget priorities. Don cites European Commission research showing that increasing national average educational attainment by one year increases productivity by 6.2% immediately and 3.1% long-term. The paper outlines core beliefs: people differ in conditions of birth, prior learning, and aspirations; achievement levels involve range and level of demonstrated skills; sorting is not a school function; outcomes derive from subject examination rather than learner characteristics; and levels are non-competitive status indicators. Current issues include teacher-sorting conflicts, poorly measurable outcomes, absent benchmarks, complicated assessment systems, grade-level confusion, social promotion, and inadequate report cards.

About/3-A-2


Life is a journey with education being what we’ve learned along the way; it is intended to help us cope with, enjoy and even smooth our journey on the road of life. There are calls for improvement in a number of areas of education, this paper deals only with the matter of measurement and reporting on achievement, only with levels of achievement in literacy and numeracy. I choose literacy and numeracy for three reasons, (1) a reasonable level of literacy and numeracy is required to participate in a modern society, (2) literacy and numeracy are somewhat unique in their roles in education , (3) developing literacy and numeracy skills should be a first call against the education dollar and (4) concerns about literacy and numeracy levels tend to be the first to surface in most discussions about education.
