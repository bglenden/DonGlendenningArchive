LEARNING TOMORROW

It is often said that it takes forty years to bring about change in education; I think it takes longer. I’m not sure why but it is likely due in large measure to the person centeredness of the task. But look ahead we must.

Following a recent conversation, I did a quick scan to identify likely changes in the field. In very few minutes my list reached thirty seven items. It was not a very scientific action but served to clarify my focus.

Here are some I like:

  • Increased school autonomy

  • Personal profiles to replace grades

  • Learning guides to replace lesson plans

  • Self rating as part of the assessment process

  • Learning maps to engage learners in the planning process

  • Outcome based learner tracking

  • Better documentation


The list of other possibilities is equally long an probably equally valid
