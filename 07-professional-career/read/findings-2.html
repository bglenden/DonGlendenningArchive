PEI EDUCATION FINDINGS -2

Working list

For some time now have been exploring public education; here are my findings:

FINDINGS:

  1. There is low level but widespread concern about the state of public education

  2. Public concerns are mostly about essential skills, employment, communications and math

  3. Structural problems arise from issues of clarity

  4. The system is unlikely to change itself

  5. PEI has a school system; it needs an education system .

  6. Important and useful changes can be made without incurring either great expense of disruption to the system. (Next right thing)

  7. Did not find a provincial education plan

  8. Schools need greater autnomy and accountability.

  9. Operating systems need better documentation

  10. There is little public conversation, engagement or collaboration in policy setting and direction

  11. System lacks a self-correcting mechanism (Choice)

  12. Lacks definition of education (Replaced aby school during revision of Act)

  13. Confuse education and going to school

  14. Criteria for structuring Department of Education different from other Departments (levels rather than fields)

  15. Stated role of Department does not reflect what Dept does or should do

  16. System lacks clear purpose

  17. Criteria lacking for selecting content

  18. Accountabilities not clear

  19. Confuses levels and branches

  20. Levels of education should not be based on age of learners

  21. Fear factor

  22. Titles should reflect roles – Souris; no one tasked with helping Sourians gain education they need/want

  23. Unable to find documentation of accountabilities

  24. Lack supporting community groups – mathm vocational, reading clubs,

  25. PEITF advisory groups ?

  26. System lacks public documentation – curriculum development, standards, learning model, definitions,

  27. Lacks National standards, research reviews, Feb 24/24
