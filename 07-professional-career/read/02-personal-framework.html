Extensive 2016 document presenting Don's comprehensive personal educational philosophy evolved over years of professional experience. Addresses education as most important societal issue and spans multiple sections: quality education characteristics (accessible, affordable, flexible, relevant, accountable, transparent), systemic gaps (lacks philosophy, suffers from politicization/bureaucracy), barriers to change, organizational problems (top-down, formula-driven, out of touch with clients), and proposed solutions. Includes Don's core beliefs about learner-centered delivery, distinguishing education from schooling, mathematics as prerequisite for opportunity, and need for external qualification authorities. Proposes priority changes from simple (exemptions/challenge for credit) to structural (depoliticizing education, establishing independent assessment bodies). Major theme: education system is production-oriented rather than development-oriented and requires fundamental restructuring.
