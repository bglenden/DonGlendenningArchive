Revised speaking notes for Premier conversation emphasizing system deficiencies, absence of self-correcting mechanisms, and proposed solutions including educational think tank and school autonomy. Demonstrates Don's systemic approach to education improvement.

NOTES FOR 

CONVERSATION PREMIER

Date


Why am I here:

Education is most important issue of the day

What the Premier, your Minister and Minister of Education say is important

What I say reflects  what I am hearing

There is a fear among educators about making their vires public

I am happy to tell you what I am telling others


My comments:

Could say about any Province

Faults are in the system that we created

No self-correcting mechanism 

In education, decisions are bureaucratic


Conclusions: 

System is not capable of changing itself

Because government runs schools, every decision becomes a political one

Only sector where no one benefits from an improvement in productivity

We do not have educational standards

No excuse for levels of preparedness of our young people.


Ideas

Challenge for credit

External qualification authority

Educational think tank

Public advisory committee

Apply fog index

School autonomy

Choice as an agent of change


Action by Premier:

Prince Edward Island has a long and proud history in/of education and many have made their mark elsewhere in Canada and, indeed, internationally

The world is changing around us; all sectors need to change to survive and even change dramatically to  thrive; 

Our entrepreneurs compete internationally, so should our schools

I recognize the importance of education; in fact, I consider myself to be an education Premier.

Asking the Minister to put in place a broadly consultative process to develop a vision statement for education for the next 50 years.
