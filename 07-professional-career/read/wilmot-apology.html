An apology letter from Don Glendenning to a donor (recipient name Wilmot, February 2007). Similar to the Upson letter, Don apologizes for failing to enclose a handwritten note with Foundation information following a visit. The letter shows Don's high personal standards for donor relations and his characteristic transparency about administrative lapses. References Norman and planning for a July 4, 2007 reception.
Professional Career & Consulting



Wilmot


Greetings from Prince Edward Island.  We have had a marvelous fall and winter so far.  Here it is almost the first of February and we are still without snow.  I trust that you and James are well.


I write to offer an apology and an explanation.


Following our visit with you and James at your summer home in Fairfield, I prepared a hand written note to enclose with information about the Foundation.  Just yesterday, I realized that it had been placed in your file instead.  I am deeply embarrassed.


For your information I am enclosing my original note and a package of information about the Foundation.  


I have also made Norman aware of what happened; he sends his regards.  


I am pleased to report that the Committee has already begun planning for the reception on July 4, 2007 – this time at a different venue.  We look forward to seeing you at that time if not before.


Warmest regards to both.


Sincerely




Don Glendenning
