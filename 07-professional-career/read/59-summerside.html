YEAR-END MUSINGS


INSPIRE CENTRE

Congratulations to Summerside for a successful launch of their Inspire Centre; while most of us talk change, Summerside simply opened “an education centre” dedicated to helping residents find the help they need to further their education. Kudos for your foresight and dedication.


DEFINITIONS (Again)

Thanks to readers who commented on definitions; from your feedback I sense that it was a timely topic. Defining learning as a “change of behaviour” was new to some, one reader wondered if there was a definition of learning that implies a higher plane. That is certainly possible but I haven’t found supporting evidence yet. Readers also question whether people have to be paid to be teachers; you convinced me and I’ve now amended my glossary. As to the question on “practice”, I believe that practice is part of instruction but probably also a part of formative assessment.


I was reflecting further on the definition of school included with my last Newsletter. While a school is building where students and teachers meet, schooling does not really convey an adequate image of what happens there. One of the functions of education is that of instruction. Perhaps then, an appropriate definition for school would be a “centre designed for the provision of instructional services”; another, and broader definition would be “a centre for the provision of educational services”. The first reflects their current role’ the latter addresses the need.




SOMETHING NEW

Our website this month has a new feature; it poses a question to readers about whether, in your opinion, “PEI schools have a responsibility for helping “students prepare for entry into the workforce!” Click www.education2020.ca and register your vote.


STEAM

We are delighted to report sponsorship by WISE ATLANTIC in the amount of $3,000 to create, promote and deliver in-school workshops in science, technology, engineering and math. Workshops will be delivered by coSolved Consulting.


YOUNG BIRDS

The Charlottetown Guardian of December 5, 2017 carried a letter wondering why young students aren’t allowed to learn at their own pace. I want to reassure the letter writer that she is not being discriminated against; older students are not allowed to progress at their own pace either.

and best wishes
