Education and schooling



We often confuse schooling and education; we do not similarly confuse health and hospitalization, religion with church attendance or citizenship with court appearances.  Seldom do we conclude that a person is healthy because they spent time in a hospital or are religious because they attend church, mosque or synagogue regularly.  


Good’s Dictionary of Education defines education  as “the aggregate of all processes by means of which a person develops abilities, attitudes and other forms of behaviour of positive value in the society in which he lives.”  Mine is simpler than that; I define education as any experience that has a formative effect on the way a person thinks feels or acts.  A functional analysis would show schooling, or more likely “instruction”, as one of the functions of an education system


Schooling, on the other hand is described in Good’s dictionary as “individual training or education received in an educational institution, military or civilian.”;  again I define schooling that part of one’s education resulting from experiences planned and organized for a learner while in an educational institution.  This may be an appropriste time to pause and verbalize your own definition


The website of the PEI Department of Education, Early Learning and Culture includes a Philosophy of Education; a quick read tells us that the Department’s focus is on children but only those children who are in or preparing for; the structure of the Department reflects the same view.  Based on  their own documentation, a more accurate title would be Department of Public Instruction.  Department documents would leave the impression that the other 100,000 Islanders, those who are not children, don’t need or warrant an educational service.  



Although not as familiar with New Brunswick and Nova Scotia Education systems, web documents suggests that the focus of those Departments also to be youngsters in school. In  taking this position, let me hasten to say that I am aware the all three Provinces provide support to other educational activities through other Government Departments but none seem to have a philosophy of education as a whole.


Readers may well ask why the fuss is and whether or not it makes any difference; I believe that separating  the two:

  • Is required to put schooling in context
  • Results in clearer communication among stakeholders.
  • Expands our view of learning opportunities
  • Would give rise to independent and lifelong learning
  • Reinforce that education is limited to the study of traditional disciplines,
  • De-emphasize the importance of personal skills such as self-reliance, problem solving, entrepreneurship and creativity.
  • Blurs accountability
