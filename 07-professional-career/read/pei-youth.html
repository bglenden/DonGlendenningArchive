Youth Statistics-PEI

Average unemployment rates (CFPEI, Vital signs, 2013)

  • 25-29 years 12.5%

  • 20-24 years 21%

  • 15-19 years 20%


Top five concerns of youth on PEI (CFPEI, Vital signs, 2013)

  1. Lack of jobs and meaningful employment

  2. Surviving on low paying jobs

  3. Education has not prepared them well enough

  4. Drugs, alcohol and addictions

  5. A prevailing poor attitude towards youth


Education and learning

  • Over 91% of young people aged 20-24 have completed high school

  • Two thirds of Islanders aged 25-29 have some form of postsecondary education

  • PEI has the second highest provincial rate of postsecondary attainment at 47%

  • In 2009 PISA tests, PEI performed at the lowest rate in Canada inreading, mathematics and science

  • Students with learning disabilities are well supported in PEI

  • PEI has the lowest and slowest rising tuition rate in the Maritimes


Important questions to be explored:

  • Are we preparing youth as best we can through their early education and learning for transitions to post secondary study, work, living independently and managing confidently?

  • What are some of the ways to maximize their preparedness?

  • Are we providing our young people with the information and supports needed to ‘sort through’ the decisionmaking processes related to future study, career, and work choices?

  • How well prepared are young people after graduation? How can we address the disconnects or gaps in workplace preparedness that are evident after post-secondary graduation?

  • What are the barriers, real or perceived, that prevent some young people from pursuing post secondary study?

  • How do we capitalize on the skills and contributions that young people have to offer employers? What are the ways to better engage youth early on in meaningful work experience

  • What can different stakeholder (employers, communities, youth, government) bring to the table to address issues around persistent youth unemployment and underemployment?

  • How can we maximize and maintain the interest and engagement that young people show in their volunteerism and community involvement?

  • How can we ensure that all youth have access to the services and support programs they need? (food aid, housing, addictions and mental health supports, financing education, etc)

  • How can youth be given a greater voice in shaping their future? Are formal processes and structures needed?

Ideas for helping preparedness:

  • Career counselling

  • Cooperative education

  • Work placements

  • Adapting curriculum to focus on life skills, critical thinking, and problem solving
