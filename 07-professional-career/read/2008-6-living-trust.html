A comprehensive February 5, 2008 proposal for the Living Memorial Trust environmental initiative. Don documents partnership with the Keuper Family to establish tree-planting sites across PEI, with donations supporting both immediate planting and permanent endowments. His detailed plan includes specific funding levels ($25 minimum donation, $100 for endowment, $250 for plaques), site selection criteria, partnership structures, and promotional strategies. This demonstrates Don's passion for environmental stewardship, community partnerships, and creative fundraising that connects emotional values (memorials, special occasions) with conservation goals.

COMMUNITY FOUNDATION OF PRINCE EDWARD ISLAND


SD- 2008-6

Date: February 5, 2008

Prepared by: Don Glendenning


To: Executive


Subject: Living Trust


The Living Memorial Trust was established on the initiative of the Keuper Family; its purpose is to protect, expand and improve the “greening’ of public spaces in PEI.  The inaugural planting took place in 2007 through the planting of a cluster of three oak trees on each side of the Wood Islands entrance to Trails PEI.


Jeniene Peake, Donnie and I constitute a small committee to move the initiative forward including the development of guidelines for future plantings and actual planting in 2008.  Our plan is to turn the initiative over to the Granting Committee before yearend..


Our current thinking is as follows:

To quickly designate one site per county for planting trees; each site will have two areas one for general planting and another for named trees.  Eventually there may be 10, 20 or even more such sites on PEI  We will use these sites as part of our promotion but planting will likely not occur in all sites in any year.

We see Trails PEI as natural locations especially for initial plantings.  Based on last years experience and our discussion, ideally we would plant a cluster of three oak trees on each side of a trail entrance and somewhere close by have a spot for both general planting of unnamed trees and another for the planting of named trees.

Native species will be planted, likely with the trees at the entrance being Island Oak.  This should make for easy identification of entrances.

It is our hope that we can continue the partnership of last year, Transportation and Public Works, Trails PEI, Forestry, McPhail Woods, John Keuper and others in the initiative..

Planting at designated sites will be under the direction of CFPEI; CFPEI will identify the amount of money  available each year, decide which site or sites to be planted and issue a contract for the trees and the planting

That initial public promotion will focus on undesignated donations, friendship trees, memorial trees, partnerships.

We propose a minimum donation $25.00 in support of planting trees at a site designated by CFPEI.  

A donation of $100 will be added to the endowment with the income used in perpetuity for the planting of trees at sites designated by CFPEI.

For $250, a  “plaqued’ tree for $250 at  site  designated by CFPEI.

Customized plantings (large trees, clusters, outside designated areas, non-natiive species may be arranged for an extra charge.

We may have some interest in partnerships (municipalities or community organizations), under which we assist in the planning and make a grant towards the cost.but the organization will manage the planting.  In addition, we would collect and receipt donations.

We plan some publicity about the initiative and seeking donations.  We believe that there is significant potential in trees for special occasions, retirement, recognizing community leaders, graduations, Mothers’ day, birthdays, memorials, etc.  this year, however, it is likely that most donation will go immediately to the planting of trees in order to jump start the initiative.

Although the Board has not yet made a determination of funds available from the endowment for 2008, our plan is, as a minimum, use those dollars for planting at one site this year.  Ideally, we would like to plant a cluster at an entrance and plant both named and un-named trees on a new site.

We are in the process of identifying sites requiring little or no site preparation.  As soon as that happens, we will:

Negotiate approval to publicize

Set a date for planting/public ceremony

Announce the initiative solicit donations

Make a Island-wide, and perhaps a local, for donations

Because the initiative  is fairly labour intensive, we propose a $5.00 charge against each donation for operating purposes, promotion, printing, etc.

to the extent possible, we will encourage contributions through our web site (this reduces our administration)

Each designated site will have coordinates so that donors can locate it via the intenet..
