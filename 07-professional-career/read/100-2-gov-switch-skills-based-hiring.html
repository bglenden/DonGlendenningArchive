Is grade inflation the culprit?

According to data provided by the Harvard Business school, “Grade inflation is imperiling jobs in the manufacturing, retail, accommodation, food service, health and social assistance’, This startling statistic is found in an article by Lawrence  Lanahan on skill-based hiring.  But this is grade inflation by employers not by schools, colleges and universities.  Seems that many employers have policies calling for new workers to have degrees even though the nature of the work itself cannot support that view.  The resulting  grade inflation has a substantial upward impact on the cost of doing business.
