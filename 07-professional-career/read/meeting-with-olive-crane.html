EDUCATIONAL POLICY ISSUES

July 15, 2010


Note: 1  "Relate the school to life, and all studies are of 

    necessity correlated." – John Dewey 

2  Change will not come voluntarily and from within



INTERNAL/GENERAL 


      1 Mission:

Ultimate role of schools is to prepare students to be successful in life roles 

Life roles: individual, family member, learner, citizen, producer and consumer.

Concentrate on producer, learner and citizen

Each level should prepare students for the next level or for work 

Next level of school not work gets most attention

Many of the skills learned in school are transferable among roles


Organization

Need to switch focus from institution to learner 

Teachers/ student relationship should be more like doctor/patient

System does not allow teachers to act as professionals, manage time, prescribe, implement, sign student document, 

Lack of choice; choose every service except your child’s  teacher,

Providing choice would solve much or quality problem

Standard should be set, time is the variable

Content should come from community not disciplines

School environment at odds with real life

Standards are content based not life based; like placing an emphasis on the medicine rather than the health of the patient

Report cards provide little useful information

Rating scales need community based benchmarks

Content only measurable by application

Quality










EXTERNAL


Standards

Sorting is not an educational function

Separate qualification authority

Challenge for credit

Conflict of interest when teachers both teach and award credit

Community based benchmarks

Defined rating scales; What’s an A?


Accountability

What are schools accountable for

Who are schools accountable to

Who monitors them on their accountabilities

What kind of public accountability should we expect

Why aren’t employers demanding more?

Funding:  Increase percentage of funds following students

Access

Does everyone who should actually enroll

Long programs reduce access

Over emphasis on 4 year degrees

With today’s technology, can programs be condensed or spread over longer periods

 What is the process of dealing with a drop out

Make it easier for learners to move in and out of education as needed

Affordability

Society cannot afford to maintain the current model

Rewarding teachers for what they know and how long they knew it does not make sense.

Fund the various functions separately: instruction, research, community sports, recreation, 

Don’t be so quick to bail out colleges and universities

Why shouldn’t a school feel the financial impact of each student who drops out

How much of my tax dollar actually goes to education?  Transportation? Sports?











Intervention points:


Improve reporting – Ontario report card needs more work, sharpen outcomes (from curriculum to life based) add skills probably have to eave subjects as well for now.

Magnet school – arts-charter school (run outside the system); Canada’s smartest school

Select communication skills to pilot (equivalent of a DACUM chart for junior and senior high school, put in the hands of teacher, learner and parent)) 

PLA – by schools but with an external option; Atlantic Qualification Authority

Challenge for credit






Personal:


Develop a clear model in your own mind – life roles, schools helps build skills, learning is an individual matter, teachers are change agents, schools are businesses,  who should sort?

Scan the NYT, Educ headlines – choice, cheating, grade inflation, increasing allocation of dollars to non-teaching services, 

Change won’t come without external pressures; pick your intervention points
