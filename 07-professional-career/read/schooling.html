This document provides comprehensive analysis of schooling system weaknesses in PEI. It defines schooling as education occurring in schools under teacher direction, discusses components and state control characteristics (monopolies, limited participation, minimal choice). Lists system weaknesses (outdated delivery, inadequate accountability, silos between providers), service gaps (early childhood, specialist access), and identifies opportunities for external interventions including grants for innovation, teacher preparation, public information campaigns, and establishing a "Better Education Fund" for systemic improvement.



June 25, 2011


Schooling



Schooling is that part of one’s education that occurs in a school under the direction of a teacher or teachers.


Components


The purpose of schools is to help young people gain skills, knowledge and attitudes thought to be required in normal life roles. There are two divergent schools of thought preparing for productive employment and growth and fulfillment.


Characteristics:

  • What is taught in school, the manner in which it is taught, and the recognition of what has been learned is also determined by the state.

  • State run

  • Monopolies

  • Limited public participation

  • Very limited choice


Conclusions:

  • We have a school system not an education system

  • There is widespread concern about the quality of public school education

  • Changes are needed

  • Most of the frustration appears to be related to the delivery model

  • The system is not able to change itself; outside intervention is required

  • There is widespread support for change both inside and outside the system.

  • Important and useful changes can be made without incurring either great expense of disruption to the system.

  • There is little exchange of information among service providers (silos)

Service Gaps

  • Infants and early childhood

  • Little or no access to specialists

  • Access to credit for self directed learning

  • Public information

System Weaknesses

  • Focus on preparation for postsecondary education

  • Diffused and obscured accountability

  • Poorly defined standards

  • Little opportunity for public input

  • Delivery model in conflict with current educational philosophy

  • State monopoly/closed system

  • Segmented/silos

  • Lacks diagnosis capability

  • Limited coordination among providers

  • Out dated delivery system

Opportunities for external interventions

  • Initiate local and/or provincial study/discussion/user groups

  • Grants in support for selective innovations

  • Public information campaign

  • Lecture/workshop/conferences

  • Support expanded non-school options – libraries

  • Establish a “Better Education Fund” to support innovation

  • Teacher preparation

  • Referral system for learners needing help

  • Encourage non-aligned associations

  • Help for parents of young children
