Don's letter following up on Order of Canada nomination for Dr. Hesta MacDonald, providing references and elaborating on her education and community contributions. Shows Don's role advocating for recognition of peers.





62 Queen Elizabeth Drive

Charlottetown, PE  C1A 3A9


April 22, 2008


Director

Chancellery of Canadian Orders

Rideau Hall

Ottawa, Ontario K1A 0A1


Dear Sir or Madam:


Dr. Hesta (Baker) MacDonald


This is a follow-up to a letter of nomination of Dr. Hesta MacDonald for the Order of Canada.


Below are names of possible contacts; I did not contact people on the list at this time although a few may have been contacted at the time of the original nomination..  One of the problems in providing references for Seniors is that many peers are dead, have declining mental faculties or have moved elsewhere to be with family.  Anyway, for your consideration:


Dr. Ronald J. Baker, CM,  2-13957 70th Ave, Surrey, BC V3W 0A3 – was the Founding President of the University of Prince Edward Island and served on the Holland College Board when Dr. MacDonald was Chair.

Mr. Alan Buchanan,   Belfast, PEI, C)A 1A0 former MLA, community activist and entertainer would be familiar with Dr. MacDonald’s work with the Belfast Historical Society

Mr. Henry Purdy, CM, RCA , 65 St. Peter’s Road, Charlottetown, PE C1A 5N2, well known Island Artist and Arts Educator illustrated some if not all of the books Dr. MacDonald authored and edited.

Mr. Tom C. Wilkinson, 94 Highland Ave, Charlottetown, PE C1A 2H6 a retired school principal

Dr. Karl Winter, Dunedin, New Haven, PE C0A 1H0 retired Agricultur Sscientist, Past President of the PEI Home and School Association and would have worked with Dr. MacDonald  during the late 1960’s and 1970’s

Mary Ross, 57 Fitzroy St. Charlottetown PE, C1A 1R4






Dr. Wade MacLaughlin, President of the University of Prince Edward Island,  550 University Avenue, Charlottetown, PE C1A 4P3  where Dr. MacDonald was a recent major donor

Ardeth Smith, Editor, Voice for Seniors, York Point, PE C0A 1H0, the magazine for which Dr. Macdonald has published a monthly article for some 18 years.

Rev. Ross A. Howard, 32 Greensview Drive, Charlottetown, PE C1A 6C3 who should be knowledgeable about Dr. Macdonald’s leading role in the establishment of the Community Baptist Church

Hon. Alexander B. Campbell, Kensington, RR#6, Stanley Bridge, PEI, C0A 1E0  former Premier of Prince Edward Island who would know of Dr. MacDonald’s work in the evolution of the School system in the late 1960’s and 1970’s

Mr. Fred Hyndman, President, Hyndman and Co. Ltd, 57 Queen St., Charlottetown, PE C1A  4A5, a member of the Holland College Planning committee and a member of the first Board of Governors

Betty Howatt, Tryon, PEI , C0B 1A0, community activist, farmer, frequent guest on radio and former member of the Holland College Board of Governors

Frank Zakem, 30 Emerald Drive, Charlottetown, PE C1A 2Y6 former Mayor of Charlottetown would be in a position to know Dr. MacDonald’s experience in serving on City committees.  Mr. Zakem, a local amateur historian himself,  is also a key member of a Holland College History Committee 


If you require further information, please do not hesitate to contact me.


Sincerely




Donald Glendenning, C.M.
