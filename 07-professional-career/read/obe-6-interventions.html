Brief outline of key OBE interventions required for successful implementation. Lists essential elements including clear outcome statements in hands of both teacher and learner, demonstrated achievement as basis for progress, varied instructional strategies, flexible time and assistance for individual learning styles, and competency-based reporting. Identifies common problems: confusion between outcomes and levels, lack of clear differentiation in level descriptions, and confusion of outcome identification with performance identification and instructional organization. These are practical operational notes.

OBE

Interventions



  1. A clear and unambiguous statement of outcomes

  • A list of outcomes in the hands of both teacher and learner to monitor needs and progress

  • Learner progress is based on demonstrated achievement

  • A variety of instructional strategies and assessment tools

  • Time and assistance adjusted to support individual learning style

  • Reported progress on each outcome

    • Consult with community when identifying outcomes
