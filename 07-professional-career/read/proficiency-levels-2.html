Ladders of educational achievement

Phase One – Constructing the Ladder

A Background:


Standards for most products and services are usually established by or in consultation with users or the government acting on their behalf; not so in the public school system where those who teach, not only set the standards but measure and determine the level reached by students served. In the views of the writer this not only constitutes a conflict of interest but casts doubt on the quality of the product. The issue is further clouded because:

  • Outcomes, which are the foundation of standards, are based on outcomes of uncertain origin and from an unclear process

  • Absence of “externally referenced criteria” to the assessment process

  • Erosion of public confidence in public school standards

  • Results of large scale assessment (PISA, PCAP, ??)

  • On the contribution of education to economic development

  • Trend by employers to explore alternative selection criteria

  • Remedial activities by postsecondary institutions

  • School centered and, therefore, local in application

  • Confusing practice of assigning two meanings to “grade levels” to two dissimilar functions, age based grouping and achievement

  • Rise of technology-aided instruction and assessment


The following are examples of strategies that avoid the above negative characteristics:

  • Apprenticeship where schools train and Departments of Labour certify,

  • The Canadian Language authority,

  • International Baccalaureate Program

  • Canadian Conservatory of Music.

  • Many countries have Qualification Authorities

  • French language program

B Proposal:

  • To develop a laddered approach to recognizing student achievement that is independent of the age of the learner

Steps

  • Secure funding

  • Establish steering committee

  • Undertake literature search

  • Prepare analysis

  • Develop rating scale

  • Establish levels

  • Establish application profiles


Deliverables:

  • An analysis of the field of mathematics

  • A rating scale

  • A minimum of 5 levels

  • A minimum of 3 applications – chemical technology, actuarial science and business

  • A proposal for exploring an independent assessment centre/service



Impact/benefits:

  • Prevents grade inflation

  • Removes uncertainty about levels

  • Increases personal mobility

  • Reduces teacher workload

  • Provides basis for curriculum development


Recommendation:

  • That Education 20/20 Inc. convene a committee to explore the matter of laddered and competency based achievement levels for mathematics
