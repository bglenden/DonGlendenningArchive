A refined proposal document (dated July 15, 2015) further developing the mathematics proficiency assessment system. This version distills key findings: current education lacks recognized subject analysis and separates teaching from assessment functions. Don proposes a systematic approach using mathematics as a pilot: analyze the field, identify proficiency levels, produce assessment models, and establish independent assessment bodies. Services would be independent of schools, employers, and government; non-competitive; and confidential. The document cites successful existing models (Apprenticeship Red Seal, Red Cross Swim Program, Royal Canadian Conservatory of Music) demonstrating that subject-specific proficiency certification is viable and beneficial.


1-3-8-8

July 15, 2015

EDUCATION 20/20 INC.


Math Proficiency Levels- 3


Education, like life, is a journey and like any journey needs recognizable checkpoints that inform us of progress towards our goals. There is increasing evidence that the current system of grade levels and diplomas have lost much of their value as measures of a student’s school achievement.


We find that:

  • A recognized analysis of school subjects appears not to exist

  • Establishing levels in a school subjects is not a school function

  • Certifying levels of achievement in fields of study is not a school function

  • Well-described levels of achievement are an aid to learning

  • Age should not be a criterion for assigning achievement levels

  • The nature and extent of formal schooling should not be a criterion for assigning achievement levels.


Conclusions:

  • There is a need for nationally recognized levels of proficiency in school subjects

  • The teaching and assessment functions should be separated


Proposal

Using mathematics as the focus, to:

  • Prepare an analysis of the field of math

  • Identify a number of levels of proficiency in math

  • Find or produce an assessment model

  • Establish an independent body to carry out individual assessments.


Characteristics of Service

  • Independent of teaching institutions, employers and government

  • Certify individual’s proficiency level

  • Open to individuals regardless of age or prior formal education

  • Not a competition; establishes a person’s level not rank

  • Confidential service


Benefits:

  • Lets students know where they stand

  • Reduces chances of bias

  • Improves consistency

  • Reduces teacher workload

  • Supports independent learning

  • Increases opportunities for innovative learning and teaching


Existing models:

  • The Apprenticeship Red Seal program allows qualified tradespeople to practice their trade anywhere in Canada

  • The Canadian Red Cross Swim Program allows sswimmers to work or continue training anywhere in Canada.

  • The Royal Canadian Conservatory of Music offers examinations in music that ARE RECOGNIzed across the country.

  • Competitive sports separate the function of coaching and referring.
