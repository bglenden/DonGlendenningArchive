A letter critiquing educators' neglect of "skill development" in favor of knowledge focus. Don observes that teacher compensation and student grades emphasize knowledge acquisition (lowest level of Bloom's taxonomy) rather than ability to apply knowledge. He defines skill development as "an educational process of increasing a person's ability to do something" and argues it applies across all programs and subjects. The letter advocates for higher achievement standards and greater precision in educational terminology to increase accountability.

February 12. 2017


Editor,

Charlottetown Guardian

Charlottetown, PEI


Dear Sir: 

 

Skill Development

Educators seldom talk about skill development; it seems at odds with their culture.  An educator’s life seems to revolve around knowledge; teacher pay scales are  based on their level of knowledge rather than their skill or complexity of task.  Student grades are based on tests of knowledge, one of the lowest levels of learning.  Published grading requirements re waivd   through ”social promotion”.  No wonder high school diplomas have lost much of their value as a screening device for postsecondary institutions or employment.

In my understanding, undertaking a skill development program could include increasing one’s level in public speaking, mathematics, coding, or teaching.
