A foundational document outlining thirteen guiding principles for educational reform. Key principles include: education's role is coaching and support; learning is demonstrated through doing; testing serves diagnosis and measurement; standards must be clearly defined and life-based rather than discipline-based; learners should challenge for credit anytime; assessment reflects performance only; and previous learning receives credit. These principles reveal Don's learner-centered, pragmatic approach emphasizing flexibility, clear communication, and practical demonstration over traditional institutional hierarchies.

Some Guiding Principles


Role of education is provide assistance to learners – coaching, demonstrations, practice, information, counsel, advice, direction, explanation and 

We demonstrate learning through doing

Testing is an important part of the education process – diagnose weaknesses, measure performance, establish levels, basis for learning plans

Sorting learners is not an educational responsibility

Standard must be clearly defined and be available to all stakeholders

Standards shall be life based not discipline based – life roles, identified by people from community

Learners should be able to challenge for credit any time

A student’s level will be determined only on performance

Credit will be given for previous learning

Testing for learning is an integral part of the learning process

Standards in the sense of a technical specification and standard as an approach to testing, and standard definition, standards for data exchange, labour mobility, coordination
