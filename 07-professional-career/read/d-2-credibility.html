Brief occasional paper examining credibility problems in education research and practice. References philosopher John Wilson's observation that educational studies lack coherent discussion, psychologists noting teacher credibility's strong effect on student learning (trust, competence, dynamism, immediacy), and Joel Levin's work on education research credibility gaps. Notes that education has a quality problem and that PEI Education Act defines school narrowly as "structured learning environment through which education program is offered."
