20/20 JOURNALISM AWARD


BACKGROUND

It is widely recognized that education is a key factor in determining each citizen’s quality of life; in the same way, the collective education of our citizens will determine to a significant degree the quality of the community in which we live. One of the keys to developing and maintaining a quality education system is providing stakeholders with current and independent information. In this regard, Education 20/20 Inc. is of the view that the public is underserved with information in general and investigative reports in particular.


PURPOSE: To encourage journalists to investigate and publicize matters that increase public understanding of education.


AMOUNT: Two Awards at $400 each


CRITERIA: 
The criteria for judging entries are:

  • Accuracy,

  • Originality,

  • Effectiveness in explaining issues to the public,

  • informational value, and

  • Insight.


ELIGIBILITY & RULES:

  • Any student enrolled in the journalism program at Holland College or UPEI on the date of the submission

  • A series of 3 stories, reports or postings

  • Published or broadcast in Canada in print form, posted on a Canadian-based website

  • One entry per person

  • Must have been published during the eligibility period.

  • Editorials or columns that only express opinions are not eligible.

  • Judges decisions are final


SUBMISSION TO INCLUDE:

  • Completed transmission form

  • A copy of the series, three stories, reports or postings. (PDF, USB key or DVD)

  • A summary of the story or article (60 words)

  • A brief description of how the entry contributes to the understanding of education

  • A biographical sketch of writer/reporter/producer(s)

  • An official clipping/transcript showing publication or broadcast date

  • Completed entries must be submitted on or before November 15, 2021

  • Electronic submissions preferred.
