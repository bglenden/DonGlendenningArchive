Critical analysis of mission statements across Canadian education departments, including PEI, New Brunswick, Newfoundland, Nova Scotia, and comparison with Maine and Alberta. Reveals that PEI's mission focus on "developing and delivering high quality programs and curriculum" is narrowly operational rather than transformational. Don argues this reflects incomplete understanding of education's role and suggests PEI confuses school administration with education leadership. Contrasts provincial statements with broader definitions encompassing lifelong learning, democratic citizenship, and personal fulfillment. Concludes with recommendation that PEI reconsider and restate its education mission with greater clarity and ambition.

MISSION UNCERTAIN-1

About 4-16


Education is about helping people change their ways - the way they think, feel and act and what even what they value. It is complex in that education is a personal matter yet people have different interests, capabilities, experiences and needs. They also fill a number of life roles and fill a variety of life roles: individual, family member, student, citizen, producer and consumer; roles which are important today but will be even more important tomorrow.


Education documents send mixed messages


Typical PEI Department mission statements:

• Family and Human Services – supports Islanders in need and helps them …

• Health – “Responsible for the delivery of publicly funded Health services

• Justice – “works to develop supports to reduce risks in the community …

• PEI - Education – “helping students find a meaningful place in society”.

Departments of Education describe their roles as follows:

  • NB – “each student develop the attributes needed to be a lifelong learner, to achieve personal fulfillment, and ….””

  • Nfld – “a community that fosters quality care and learning …”

  • NS – “every child is confident and proud, maximizing their potential and contributing to a thriving society…,”

  • Maine – Promote the best learning opportunities for all Maine students

  • Bangor Department of Education – provide educational opportunities that inspire students and adults to grow every day, to thrive over time, and to spire continuously for excellence a leaners and people

  • Alberta –simultaneously prepare the citizens of tomorrow while equipping our students with the knowledge and skills they need too be successful in a rapidly changing economy and society”.

  • Anon – “encourage, support and regulate education services”

  • Anon – knowledge, atttiudes and skills need to thrive in a democratic society

  • PEI - “develop and deliver high quality programs and curriculum in English and French to Island children from birth to Grade 12.”

Comment:

  • PEI education documents capture neither the spirit, breadth, significance nor urgency of the education task

  • Developing and delivering” is a very narrow role for the Department; more like a role for a curriculum unit.

  • Reflects a “public instruction” role rather than an “education role”.

  • Very passive.

  • Limited to youth; isn’t the Department interested in ducation of all Islanders?

  • Department title includes continuing education; shouldn’t something be said about that as well?

  • Department documents seem to support the view that getting an education and “going to school” are one and the same; see Education Report. (World Bank, 2019)

  • Focus on traditional subjects rather than needs to thrive in 21st century

  • Discussion would benefit from a glossary of terms; may already exist but I have not encountered it

  • Recent “study centres” were placed with Finance rather than Education; may reflect view that Department not interested in broader role

  • Nothing about “soft skills”


CONCLUSION

  • What Government is trying to accomplish through education is not clear.

  • Government only interested in education of young people in school

  • Lacks sufficient detail on which to build an education system.

  • Limits education to experiences in school

  • Proposed purpose “Help Islanders develop knowledge, skills and other attributes needed for life in 21st century”

  • Developing a new statement would be a great staff development activity

  • Scanned a few high school websites but found nothing about mission.
