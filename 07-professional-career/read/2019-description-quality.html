Official award description for the 2019 cycle emphasizing educational quality. Defines quality using UNICEF framework across content (relevant curricula, basic skills, literacy, numeracy), processes (child-centered teaching, assessment, well-managed classrooms), and outcomes (knowledge, skills, attitudes aligned with national education goals). The document shows Don's commitment to evidence-based, internationally recognized definitions of educational quality and his systematic approach to recognizing schools making quality improvements.

EDUCATION AWARD (2019)

$2,500

Competition closes: December 31, 2018


BACKGROUND

Education 20/20 Inc. is pleased to offer an Award in the amount of $2,500 to an Island school, wishing to offer or expand the quality of its offerings (UNICEF, Defining Quality-2000) through adjustments to:

  1. Content that is reflected in relevant curricula and materials for the acquisition of basic skills, especially in the areas of literacy, numeracy and skills for life, and knowledge in such areas as gender, health, nutrition, HIV/AIDS prevention and peace.

  2. Processes through which trained teachers use child-centred teaching approaches in well-managed classrooms and schools and skilful assessment to facilitate learning and reduce disparities

  3. Outcomes that encompass knowledge, skills and attitudes, and are linked to national goals for

education and positive participation in society.


SUBMISSIONS

Submissions should address one or more of the above characteristics of a quality education by providing details as to purpose, clientele, staff involvement, activities, participants, time allocation and time span, and any other information supportive of the proposal. the objective and the educational principles on which submission is based as well as the proposed activity, number of people involved and the time period over which the activity will take place. Also tell us how the money will be used and whether or not the activity is likely to continue.


APPLICANTS

Applicants should note that the competition is limited to Prince Edward Island; costs of the proposal. if any, are to be borne by presenters; decisions will be based on documentation received; Education20/20 Inc reserves the right not to make an award in any year; final decisions will rest with Education 20/20 Inc.; participants may be invited to present their proposals. submissions shall include but not be limited to a written format not exceeding 3 pages, proposals shall arise from the group and not be the result of a contract with professionals; and selection will be based on documentation received. The successful applicant is expected to file a report with Education 20/20 at the close of the proect.
