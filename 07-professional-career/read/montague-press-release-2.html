Post-workshop press release (February 19, 2014) reporting on Montague's February 18 workshop. Key issues identified: lack of basic skills (literacy, numeracy, citizenship) and disconnect between schooling and real life. The release critiques structural issues: government funding creates political short-termism over sound policy. This demonstrates how Don's workshops were catalyzing critical community analysis of systemic barriers.


February 19, 2014

Preparedness on the hotseat

The lack of basic skills, literacy, numeracy and citizenship, were the lead issue raised by participants in a workshop in Montague on Tuesday evening; the relevance of schooling to real life was a second.

The workshop, organized by Education 20/20 first heard a presentation on the findings of a recent survey of over 200 Island youth between the ages of 15 and 25.  The survey, Taking the Pulse of Island Youth, dealt with youth concerns about health, education, employment and social well-being.  The presentation, led by Steve McQuaid, VP of the Community Foundation of Prince Edward Island and Katherine MacDonald, Researcher, provided details about the methodology and findings.  

Among the other discussion points was the view that some of the desired changes are structural and not educational, a question as to whether or not it is the role of a school to teach life skills, the declining value of school diplomas, and the difficulty of developing a culture of learning. Since schools are entirely funded and more or less operated by Government, improvement initiatives often reflect short term political considerations over sound principles of public policy”. 

 Similar workshops are planned for other Island communities in the coming weeks.  People interested in future activities of Education 20/20, or to be placed on the mailing list for the monthly newsletter, should check the website, www.education2020.ca
