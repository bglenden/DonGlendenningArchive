Meeting agenda at Haviland Club introducing participant beliefs about educational assessment. Establishes shared understanding: value in continuing discussions; performance distinction criteria (number of skills, achievement levels, skill clustering); outcomes and competencies are not age-related; proficiency may reflect learner age; Bloom's taxonomy provides foundation for proficiency levels; outcomes should state in learner-direct terms. Proposes continued discussion on common math skills list, grade versus achievement levels, objective-based assessment criteria, report card design, independent grading bodies, and other matters. Demonstrates Don's facilitation approach through establishing explicit shared beliefs before detailed discussion.

NOTICE

Meeting on Math Transition

Tuesday, October 10, 2017 at 3:00 pm

Upstairs Board Room, Haviland Club

  1. Haviland Street


  1. This will confirm my notice of September 7, 2017; please note new location

  2. Based on previous discussion, participants seem to share the following beliefs:

  • There is value in continuing the discussions

  • The significant criteria for distinguishing levels of achievement in a subject or field of study are (1) number of skills demonstrated, (2) level of achievement on each skill demonstrated and (3) the extent and nature of skill clustering

  • Outcomes and competencies are not age related

  • A Learner’s proficiency level may, at least in part, be a reflection of a learner’s age

  • Bloom’s Taxonomy provides an appropriate basis for establishing levels of proficiency in any outcome or competency

  • Education outcomes should be stated in terms that apply directly to learners


  1. Discussion on the following to be continued:

  • A common or master list of math skills

  • Grade levels and achievement levels are seldom the same

  • Assessment criteria may reflect an objective but not an age

  • Student report cards: achievement of progress?

  • Independent body to assign levels
