Talking points document from February 12, 2017 outlining key concepts for discussing proficiency levels in education. It defines proficiency as determined by two elements: what one can do and how well one can do it. The document identifies employers' inability to rely on high school grades as measures of competency and references Wolff's assessment framework. It contrasts assessment (learner-centered, interactive, formative) with evaluation (grades-focused). Proficiency 1-3

February 12, 2017


Proficiency levels in education

(Talking points)


Proficiency:

  • Determined by two elements (What one can do and (2) how well one can do it


Problem:

  • Employers can no longer rely on high school grades as a measure of proficiency in math and communication skills


A discourse on Grading by Robert Paul Wolf, 1969

  • Analysis or criticism (formative) designing additional strategies in support of learning. analysis prepares a teacher to provide meaningful feedback and is part of the ongoing give and take of the learning process

  • Evaluation- to establish a person’s level in a subject or field, criterion referenced

  • Ranking – how one stacks up against others


Assessment versus evaluation (Duke University)

  • Assessment change in learning teaching and outcomes, interactive between teacher; intended to improve learning “information is learner-centered, course based frequently anonymous and not graded”

  • Evaluation focuses on grades and may reflect classroom components other than course content and mastery level


Glendenning:

  • Diplomas and degrees are not an adequate basis for denoting levels of proficiency

  • The practice of using grade to indicate age and proficiency is confusing

  • Establishing and awarding grades is neither a school nor education function

  • Employment mobility demands recognized qualifications

  • Levels of proficiency should be externally determined

  • Most developed countries have qualification authorities

  • Social promotion appears to be on the increase







Other models:

  • Theoretical Examination

  • Practical Exam

  • Above plus component based on schoolwork

  • Rate individual competencies


Examples:

  • Apprenticeship

  • International Baccalaurate

  • Music

  • Swilmming

  • Canadian Math Olymiad

  • GED

  • TOWES
    Canadian Language Levels

  • Qualification authorities



Proposal:

  • Develop an independent assessment service for individuals who need or want to know their proficiency level in math and communication skills.
