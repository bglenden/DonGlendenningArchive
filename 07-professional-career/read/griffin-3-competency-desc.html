RECOGNIZE WHOLE NUMBERS

Competency description


RATIONALE

Recognizing and using whole numbers is an important part in the life of a citizen. Numbers are all around us: on road signs, in money transactions, in grocery stores and gas stations, sports events and even on tires. A number represents a value of an item, or a count/collection of items. Numbers assist us in making sense of and communicating in our world.


RECOGNIZING WHOLE NUMBES INCLUDES:

Upon development of this skill, you will be able to:

  • Use whole numbers to represent an amount of items

  • Estimate a count of items

  • Explain the difference between numbers and their meaning

  • Reading numbers

  • Interpret place value


TYPICAL ACTIVITIES

The following are typical examples of this sklll.

  1. Counting objects

  2. Manipulate magnetic numbers on a board

  3. Digits…periods

  4. Writing numbers

  5. Rounding numbers

  6. Interpreting place value

  7. Play bingo


PERFORMANCE ASSESSMENT

To demonstrate your competency with this skill you may be asked to:

  1. Take an inventory

  2. Read signs that include numbers

  3. Represent the value of a number with a picture of items

  4. Read identification numbers

  5. Count of a group of items

  6. Round numbers up and down

  7. Place numbers in order to demonstrate a sense of lower and higher numbers

  8. Identify numbers that are not whole numbers

  9. Write numbers

  10. Track documents

  11. Read postal codes

  12. Play bridge
