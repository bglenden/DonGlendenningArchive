A proposal to engage young Islanders in documenting significant community events and decisions from the past 150 years through interviews with seniors and examination of local records. Objectives include increasing community knowledge and pride, building intergenerational relationships, recognizing community builders, and strengthening communication skills. Don proposes organizing groups in fourteen communities with priority for 14-year-olds (honoring the year 2014). Implementation includes a part-time coordinator and local organizers with honoraria, community group funding for materials and events, and a province-wide gathering to share findings. Reports would be placed in libraries and schools, with originals in provincial archives. This initiative combines educational benefit, intergenerational connection, and community history preservation.

EDUCATION 20/20 INC.

Proposal for Funding


Subject:  Community Engagement Through History


Background:



Proposal: To engage young Islander in learning about significant events in their local community during the past 150 years.

Objectives:

  • Increase level of knowledge an understanding of people about the community in which they live
  • Increase the level of pride in community
  • Build positive relationships among community members
  • Build relationships between generations
  • Provide a record to a community of its accomplishments
  • Help communities recognize their own community builders
  • Strengthen communication skills among participants.

Concept:

Engage Island communities in an activity that focuses on their history through a process of identifying key decisions, activities and events in the life of their own community. Information will be gathered mainly from two sources, newspaper reports of the day, records of community organizations, and memories of senior citizens.  


Workplan:

Communities will be identified, groups will be organized and information gathering begun in the fall of 2013; Community shaping events will identified and priorized, senior citizens interviewed, and reports drafted by Easter.  Reports will be released and presentations made to the community, including local groups and seniors’ residence, in May.  Reports will be finalized in June and reports released.  Organizers of local events will be encouraged to incorporate findings into their other community celebrations during the summer.  An opportunity will be sought for a province wide gathering in Charlottetown where participants can display their reports and meet an interested public.


Deliverables:

Copies of reports will be placed in local school libraries, local branches of the public library.  While much of the background information will be found in the public domain, it is likely that some will come from family scrapbooks, therefore copies of reports, interview reports and “original” material will be sent to the Provincial archives.






Organization:

To recognize our 2014 celebrations, we are proposing to give priority to 14 year-olds and organize groups in fourteen communities.  Actual organizational  details may vary from community to community in that some existing communities my handle local arrangements where  a new group may need to be organized in others.  There will be a part-time paid project coordinator and a local organizer who will be given a small honourarium; others involved are expected to volunteer Whatever the arrangement, each local community group will be provided with given some funds to support local project costs such as the purchase of project material and preparation of reports.
