Documents the historical context: post-WWII industrial expansion relying on skilled European immigrants, 1960 Technical and Vocational Training Assistance Act funding, mid-1960s Canada NewStart Corporations demonstration projects, and Robert Adams' development of the DACUM (Developing a Curriculum) competency analysis model in Yarmouth, Nova Scotia. CANADA 150

DACUM: A CANADIAN ACHIEVEMENT



Background:

The decade of industrial expansion immediately after WW2 was only possible because of the influx of skilled immigrants from Western Europe. By the late 1950’s it became evident that Canada could no longer depend on this source of supply. Furthermore, large numbers of Canadians remained unemployed even though Canada suffered from a shortage of skilled workers. The Technical and Vocational Training Assistance Act (1960) provided massive funding to remedy the problem


In the mid 1960’s, TVTA administrators joined with officials working on the issue of high unemployment and slow economic growth and pooled their efforts in support of a series of demonstration projects. These projects, called Canada NewStart Corporations were funded by the Federal Government but given a large degree of independence as to their clientele and focus of their research.


One of the NewStart Corporations was established in Yarmouth, Nova Scotia with Tom Jones as Director and Robert Adams as Director of Occupational Training. Given Adams experience in occupational and functional analyses, it was not surprising that analysis was a cornerstone of programming in his projects. The foundation of Adams work was a competency analysis of the occupation for which training was being provided with competencies being drawn from skilled practitioners under the leadership of a trained facilitator. This resulted in a single-sheet presentation of competencies which could be used by a variety of users. Adams went further and outlined a training model based on the DACUM model. The model was adopted and adapted to become the STEP model of Holland College effective September 1, 1970


Purpose: To recognize the DACUM analysis process as a Canadian achievement, to promote the model and to recognize Robert Edwin Adams, its developer.
