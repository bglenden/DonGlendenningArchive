School Autonomy


Simply stated, there is a direct relationship between school performance and school autonomy.  In the words of the OECD Report, Pisa in Focus, 2011:

  • “In countries where schools have greater autonomy over what is taught and how students 

are assessed, students tend to perform better. 

  • In countries where schools account for their results by posting achievement data publicly, schools that enjoy greater autonomy in resource allocation tend to show better student performance than those with less autonomy. 
  • In countries where there are no such accountability arrangements, schools with greater autonomy in resource allocation tend to perform worse.” 

In support of the concept


Ideally, a school should have autonomy on how to resolve all learning matters as they pertain to students In practical terms, autonomy at the school level means giving each school the freedom to:

  • recruit, hire, assign and supervise any and all instructional staff whether full-time, part time, teachers, teacher aides or volunteers
  • Set their own calendar  
  • Adjust the school day
  • Allocate funds within a budget to reflect the needs and priorities of students and the community.
  • decisions about curricula and assessment, course offerings and textbooks. 


But autonomy and accountability needs to be shared with teachers as well.  Teachers should have freedom to:

  • Make decisions as to how best serve each individual
  • Assign grades at their sole discretion 


Students should have freedom to:

  • Select any school; other Provinces allow it.  
  • Challenge any course for credit

PEI principals

The role of school principals is described in The PEI Schools Act, however, the degree of freedom to act is not clear. It appears that their scope of practice is quite limited The following are clearly spelled out:

  • educational leadership in the school,
  • ensures that educational programs as described are carried out, 
  • Evaluation of teachers
  • Maintain discipline
  • Foster cooperation within the school
  • Maintain order
  • Consult teachers
  • Attend to health, comfort and safety of students
  • Carry out such other duties as the Superintendent, School Board or minister may assign
