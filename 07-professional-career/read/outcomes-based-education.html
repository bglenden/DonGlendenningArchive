20/20         OUTCOMES BASED EDUCATION                                                                                         _______________________________________________________________________Let me say up front that I am a believer in outcomes or competency based education.  I am familiar with it, having both studied it and worked in a CBE system. Sound educational planning demands it.  Clear, unambiguous and measurable outcomes are the place to start.  


My experience was with competency based education; you can imagine my delight listened to William Spady.  We were talking the same language.  Granted competencies were more specific but I feel that the detail is needed. A tighter specification is needed to properly plan activities and interventions, hence my preference for the term competency.   



A competency is a statement of observable behaviour resulting from the application of knowledge to the solution of a problem or situation.  It follows then that competency based education is an approach to organizing educational programs and activities that clearly identifies what is to be learned and various levels of achievement. 


I browsed the internet recently to update myself on outcomes based education.  Imagine my disappointment to learn that, although many still confess to be using an OBE model, in fact the emphasis has switched to curriculum based objectives.  


My later browsing of the objectives as found in the PEI curriculum outlines confirms the general trend.  Atlantic Provinces Learning outcomes are, in my view, nneither competencies of educational outcomes.  They are, as the literature said, curriculum outcomes.


In issue No. 1, I mentioned the six Oregon Life Roles: individual, learner, producer, consumer, family member and citizen.  It is my view that program planners and curriculum developers should, therefore, look to those sources to identify in some detail the nature, range and depth of skills expected in each role.  It seems to me that those identifying outcomes went o books instead.  


I believe that going to text books is an important part of education but that is a second step not the first step.  Text books are useful in helping you organize   


This was borne out in my review of  browsed some for the PEI language arts outcomes recently and find that than many outcomes do not pass the test, or at least my grading of that test.   Outcomes are central to the teaching/learning process and  should determine the what, when and where of a teacher’s daily, monthly and yearly teaching plans. Those same outcomes should form the basis for reporting student progress.  Some redesign, new materials and fresh signage is called for.



Most educators in the public school system reject competency based education in favour of  the Spady model of based on educational outcomes.   In fact, however, they operate on neither educational outcomes or competencies but rather a hybrid that I view as curriculum objectives.



Responsibility for the above repairs rests clearly with the Department of Education and, I should point out, can be carried out at minimal cost and interference with the ongoing operation of the system.
