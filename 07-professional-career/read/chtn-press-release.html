January 21, 2014

Preparedness on the hotseat

Courage was an unexpected word in the discussion on education Monday evening in the Stratford Town Hall.  It came up independently at a number of round tables; at one it was used to describe a quality needed by young people as they transition from school to work, at another the word was used to describe community members who take a stand for education, while the third application was to school leaders who have the courage, not only think outside the box, but to act on that thinking.

The workshop, organized by Education 20/20 and chaired by Don Glendenning, first heard a presentation on the findings of a recent survey of over 200 Island youth between the ages of 15 and 25.  The survey, Taking the Pulse of Island Youth, dealt with youth concerns about health, education, employment and social well-being.  The presentation, led by Steve McQuaid, VP of the Community Foundation of Prince Edward Island and Katherine MacDonald, Researcher, provided details about the methodology and results.  

The group then turned their attention to one of the findings, the preparedness gap, and how it could be narrowed.  Ideas were many and included the need for action not further study, the need to review and perhaps redefine the role of schools, and the declining value of high school diplomas resulting from social promotion.   Similar workshops are planned for other Island communities in the coming weeks.  People interested in future activities of Education 20/20, or to be placed on the mailing list for the monthly newsletter, should send an email to pei@caneducation2020@.com
