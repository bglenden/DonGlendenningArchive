Committee meeting notes documenting discussion with Peter Russell and Jeniene Peake about awards program modifications. Lists brainstormed ideas for new award categories and grant structures. Additional concepts include converting the award to a grant program, holding fundraisers to increase awareness, and implementing residential workshops modeled on ACCC professional development. Shows iterative planning process and collaborative committee approach.

Changes to awards program


  1. Present: Peter Russell, Jeniene Peake, Don Glendenning

  2. Promotion of 2017 Award : Posted on website, monthly mailout of Newsletter, notice circulated, item in Guardian in mid-December

  3. Discussion and brainstorming around original list

  • Award for teachers

  • Award/prize for teachers in training

  • Leadership workshop: in 3-day weekend, one week, patterned after those offered by College

  • An award for a non-teaching staff member

  • An award to a Journalism students for the best series of articles on education.

  • A school whose students do best on exams sponsored by the Canadian Mathematics Society

  • A school that allows challenge for credit and PLAR

  • Best student designed school web site

  • A school that goes the extra mile for children of immigrants.

  • To teachers in training or new teachers for a paper on education, publishing the best papers and event for a public presentation

  • Encouraging teachers to write for publication, either public media or professional journals.

  • Students choice award (similar to patient’s choice in the health field

  • Matching dollars for the Canada 150 Fund)


  1. Additional ideas:

  • Switch from an award to a grant

  • A grant to a teacher or preferably 2 or more teachers working together

  • Call to public for ideas

  • A fundraiser for funds to make a grant would heighten awareness of our activities

  • Residential workshop (a la ACCC model) – local or regional, in cooperation with University?;

  • Award to a teacher-in-training for …

  • Prizes
