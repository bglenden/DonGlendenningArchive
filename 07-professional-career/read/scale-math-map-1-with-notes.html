Math Rating Scale-1

Thanks to those who gathered on Wednesday to explore the issue of rating math skills. The focus was on assessing a person’s skill level not whether these skills are important or should be taught.

  1. Discussion opened with a listing of potential criteria; we seemed to reach agreement quickly on accuracy, speed and degree of assistance. Other criteria included fluency, supervision, creativity, strategy, etc. It is interesting to note that Ontario refers to its new test strategy, for teachers as a Math Proficiency Test.

  2. Although I had read widely about math instruction I had not encountered the term “Math facts”. The term resonates with me and probably best describes the criticism raised most often in the community at large. Tess used the term as follows: “ Can perform this task, can apply math facts accurately, respond to real world problems involving mathematics”. To quote Tess again “An absence of math facts hinders the accuracy of responding to a problem”

  3. Agreed that the “0” should be dropped

  4. We then selected one of the models on the table and revised it as shown below.

  5. On further reflection, I propose that we:

  • Discuss and sharpen the list of criteria; definitions below may be helpful.

  • Consider the issue of adaptability, creativity, problem solving, leadership, etc

  • Share views as to how “learners” work their way up the scale; are all criteria applied equally at each step?

  1. Some definitions

  • Assessment - is a process of gathering information about a person’s performance on a task or set of tasks for the purpose of initiating improvement.

  • Benchmark - is a level of performance which is widely recognized by practitioners and the Public at large and which serves as the reference against which others are evaluated.

  • Criteria are indicators used to judge the extent or level of performance.

  • Evaluation –an overall judgement of what has been learned

  • Quality (NZ) - a customer’s perception of the value of a supplier’s work output

  • Rating – assessing performance against criteria

  • Rating Scale - a scale which attaches a numerical value to discernable levels of performance competency

  1. Standard - a readily distinguishable level of performance, demonstrated through the making of a product or provision of a service, which is deemed to be worthy of recognition
