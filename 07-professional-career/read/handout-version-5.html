This is an evolved version of the educational framework, retitled "A Learning Framework" instead of "An Education Framework." It incorporates similar foundational content but with refined organization. It presents Don's comprehensive philosophy on learning, including key definitions, quality standards based on UNICEF criteria, and PEI-specific needs. The framework expands into detailed sections on typical education functions (leadership, instruction, care, qualifications, transportation, products/technology) and includes additional material on learning levels (primary through tertiary), life roles (individual, family member, citizen, student, producer, consumer), and learner needs. The document reflects Don's commitment to comprehensive, holistic education system design that addresses learner needs across all life domains.

QUALITY

A quality education system is one where:*

  • Learners are healthy
  • Environments are safe and respectful
  • Content is relevant 
  • Outcomes are linked to personal and community objectives
  • Support is focused on learners and learning.
  • Information and advice is timely and independent
  • Teachers are professional, autonomous and accountable

PEI NEEDS


  • A “clear and compelling” vision of education for all Islanders.  We believe that having such a statement is required, especially for a service as large and complex as education


  • A learner centered service model that provides support in all domains to learners if, as, when and where needed.  Doing so would involve student centered outcomes, criterion referenced assessment, flexible scheduling, challenge for credit arrangements, and greater au-tonomy and accountability for teachers. 


  • Leadership in the creation of a learning culture and providing a quality education for every Islander.  Government should focus on matters of planning, setting priorities, funding, monitoring, and quality control, ensuring the availability of professional resources, home sup-port for preschool children, information and continuing education. 


  • Autonomy for schools in their day to day decision-making


  • An independent qualification body for the purpose certifying individual levels of achievement for persons wanting or needing such documentation.   


  • A service delivery model where professionals are directly accountable to clients for the nature, extent and quality of service provided


  • Put in place an externally controlled  strategy to encourage and support innovation.







BELIEFS


John Dewey once said that if he could only know one thing about a person he would want to know the person’s beliefs; we also believe that beliefs are important and include the following which helped shape our views of education:

  • All people can learn but some may need more time than others.
  • Learning is an individual matter but may occur in a group setting.
  • The most important part of one’s education is the part that occurs in the first six years of life.
  • The purpose of education is to help a person gain attitudes, skills and knowledge that will serve them well for the remainder of their journey.
  • The responsibility for one’s education rests with one’s self.
  • Our future as a community depends, in a large measure, on the nature and level of education of our citizens. 
  • Government’s role is (1) to encourage education among its citizens, (2) to ensure equity in its support and (3) to ensure the future of the community it serves.
  • An education system should be designed to deliver educational services to the extent possible if, as and when needed.









THE LEARNER


A learner is a person who, for whatever reason, is trying to bring about change in his/her life - trying to change an attitude, gain new knowledge or add to one’s skills.  But learners are neither empty jars nor blank slates; each learner carries three pieces of luggage to every new learning situation:

• Certain physical, mental and emotional conditions with which the learn was born.  

• Prior learning, or education, resulting from formal study, home circumstances, travel, state of health, personal interests, culture in which they operate and learning styles

• Aspirations and  imagined future including where one will live, the work one will do and the lifestyle one will have, the nature of the family, quality of life, etc..


We are all on the road of life; the road is a metaphor for the experiences .one encounters between birth and death. Differences vary but commonly include:    

• Six life roles or themes that engage us, that of an individual, a family member, a citizen, a student, a consumer and producer.

• Natural obstacles, restrictions and hurdles such as time, age, personal and family responsibilities, geography, finance, 

  • Obstacles resulting from or even compounded and compromised by people we meet along the way.

• Man-made obstacles that allow or prohibit access to learning opportunities such as an entrance requirement, a qualification, an age level, physical characteristics, level of education, level of wealth, temperament or a credential.

• Personal baggage unique to each of us.

Framework 5


Education has several meanings:

1. A set of services put in place by a community to encourage and support the education of its citizens,

2. A sector of the economy having to do with the, promotion supply and regulation of education services, 

3. A process of creating experiences which result in others learning skills, knowledge, attitudes and other attributes, and 

4. The state or level of a person’s ability to deal with daily living 


Key beliefs include:

1. Learning is an individual matter

2. Only the learner can learn

3. Learning may occur in a group setting and sometimes requires other people as part of the process

4. Learning results in a change of behaviour

5. Everyone can learn the skills, knowledge attitudes and other attributes needed by citizens in a democracy

6. Learning pace the same for most learners; difference in rate mostly dependent on prior learning


Assumptions

1. Most people can learn knowledge, skills and attitudes of every day life


Typical Education Services as identified by the Business Roundtable:

1. Information - recent and independent information needed in the making of decisions about their own education or the education of others

2. Counselling – help to people in making decision about their education interests and needs. 

3. Instruction - The provision of focused help to others in their learning endeavors including diagnosis, counselling, presentations, practice and access generally to a range of other resources 

4. Quality assurance - a system that assures the community that their learning experiences, including experiences with service providers, meet community standards.

5. Products and Technology - Access by citizens to educational materials, technology and other resources to help learners develop attitudes skills, knowledge and other at-tributes needed to meet their needs and objectives.

6. Funding -funding to a level and in a manner that is equitable for all Islanders.

7. Human resources - Teachers, administrators, counsellors, advisors. assistants, leaning managers and others with the knowledge and experience needed to help others learn.
