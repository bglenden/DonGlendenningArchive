Early planning minutes from November 15, 2006 Agricultural Heritage Project meeting with Dr. Tom Hall, Willie Dunbar, and Weldon Rennie. Participants discussed preserving antique PEI farm equipment, especially pieces manufactured pre-WWII. Plans were made to convene interested residents in Prince County and eventually Kings and Queens counties. The initiative aimed to identify, catalog, and preserve this aspect of Island heritage through community-sourced equipment inventories and potential museum exhibitions.



11/16/2006


COMMUNITY FOUNDATION OF PRINCE EDWARDISLAND


Agricultural Heritage Project


Minutes


Date: November 15, 2006

Time: 10:00 am

Location: St. Eleanor’s Recreation Centre


Present : Dr. Tom Hall

Willie Dunbar (853-2955)

Weldon Rennie (853-2667, 853-7948)

Don Glendenning


Purpose: to explore mutual interests relative to the preservation of antique farm equipment


Notes: Rennie and Dunbar interested in developing one or more small museums in Western PEI probably in conjunction with agricultural exhibitions


Agreed that it would be useful to convene groups of interested in the subject to share information and explore potential areas of cooperation


Agreed to convene a meeting soon in Summerside for interested people in Prince County.  Agenda to include brief presentations by representatives of Museums in Prince County, general discussion, future activities if any, and a list of equipment,  especially equipment manufactured on PEI prior to WW2.  Meetings in in other counties will be arranged later.



Note:  Please note that we are arranging the above noted meeting at the St. Eleanor’s Community Centre for Monday, November 27 starting at 10:00 a.m.  Lunch will be provided and the meeting is expected to finish at 1:00 pm
