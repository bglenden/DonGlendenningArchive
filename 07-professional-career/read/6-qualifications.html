One of Don's most important conceptual contributions, this document meticulously distinguishes between credentials (diplomas and degrees signifying program completion) and qualifications (actual demonstrated competencies and ability to perform). Don argues that schools have become confused about their role, conflating the awarding of credentials with certifying qualifications. He proposes establishing an independent "Qualification Authority" (AQUA) to assess and certify actual competencies separately from educational institutions. This would reduce bias, enable lifelong learning, and allow educators to focus on teaching rather than sorting. The AQUA would operate on fee-for-service basis with advisory panels and serve all Atlantic Canada, potentially based on PEI. The document includes extensive discussion of how this confusion hampers both education and employment.

Introduction



Canadians are great travelers, many have passports. Passports are issued by one’s home country, provide basic information about the bearer and, if examined, provide some insight into the exciting places travelled. Many Canadians also hold visas, but they differ from passports. Visas, on the other hand, are issued by a receiving country, involve a more detailed level of scrutiny by the receiving country, specify the purpose of the visit and provide some detail as to activities to be carried on; often travelers require both a passport and visa. Seems to me that similar statements could be said about credentials and qualification

and are welcomed all over the world.


Credentials

Definition, characteristics, origin, who needs, how used

Diploma, according to Wikipedia, is of Greek origin meaning "folded paper", and is a document issued by an educational institution indicating that the holder has completed a certain programme of studies; degrees are also granted by educational institutions on a similar basis. Degrees and diplomas are considered credentials in that they may provide entrance into an arena but are not an indicator of how well one will perform once there.


Qualifications

Definition, origin, characteristics, who needs, how used

A qualification, on the other hand, speaks to the issue of attitudes, skills and knowledge required to successfully perform in a role or at a task. Employment advertisements often identify both a credential, such as a high school diploma, and a set of qualifications usually including training and or experience. In order to teach, one also needs a credential, a university degree, and a qualification, a teaching licence, the latter attesting to one’s ability to teach.

Schools are teaching institutions; their role is to help learners learn. Each learner decides what he or she will learn, although others, including teachers, influence that decision. As part of the teaching process, teachers assess what learners have learned and, depending on the results, design and implement new teaching strategies. Unfortunately and increasingly, the emphasis in school has switched from helping learners learn to grooming them the education “race”for a race, and even selecting winners. The purposes are incompatible and should be separated. The answer is to separate the functions of teaching and sorting.



An alternative Options - Qualification authority

In spite of my earlier comments, I believe that both learners and the public should have and would benefit from the certification of skills such as literacy and numeracy. Perhaps such a service could be provided through the apprenticeship division or perhaps certification could be provided through existing program infrastructure.



Another option is to establish a new body, a qualification authority, whose sole purpose is to evaluate and certify a person’s level of performance on a task or set of tasks, competencies, outcomes. Such an authority should be independent of government and educational institutions, have its own governing structure, operate on a fee for service basis, accept clients on direct application or by referral, issue certificates based on demonstrated performance, and provide applicants with information on gaps to be filled to meet certification requirements.

It would, in effect, have two clients, the organizations desiring an assessment service and individuals wanting to aconfirm their qualifications. A qualification authority could also play a useful role in helping people who take on line courses convert them to a qualification – or even the assessment of foreign credentials.


If establishing a qualification authority, it would make sense to develop a service capable of serving all Atlantic Canada; it would make an interesting new business venture and could be located on PEI.


The acquiring of an education and the certification of qualifications are two separate functions although the two are closely linked and frequently one is thought to be the other. The fact that the delivery of both functions is, to a large degree, provided or driven by educational institutions often works to the individual’s disadvantage in terms of both education and certification. While separate certification bodies exist, they appear to place undue emphasis on results of study and too little emphasis on what has been learned outside the formal education system.


Concept


The idea behind this proposal is to establish an independent, private sector driven body with authority to assess and certify a person’s level of performance on a task or set of tasks such as found in an occupation or field.


Characteristics


AQUA will:


  1. Be an independent organization with its own governing structure

  2. Operate on a fee for service basis

  3. Accept clients on direct application or by referral

  4. Issue certificates based on demonstrated performance

  5. On request, provide applicants with information on gaps to be filled to meet certification requirements.

  6. Make extensive use of advisory/working panels.


Benefits/Advantages


It is anticipated that the availability of AQUA will:


  1. Remove barriers to certification encountered in the current approach

  2. Focus certification more clearly on performance

  3. Encourage more self study

  4. Allow educational providers to focus on the teaching function

  5. Allow a certification body to focus on certification without regard to teaching.

  6. Removes length of learning time as a component of certification

Implementation Steps

Credential or Qualification?

While sorting stuff in my basement recently, I came across a number of diplomas, degrees and certificates. Although I remember getting them, I cannot remember ever using even one. So, one wonders what purpose or purposes they serve.

At one time I thought the diplomas and degrees could be redesigned in order to attest to qualifications but I’ve changed my mind; I now realize that credentials tell what has been studied, qualifications tell what has been learned and, as a result, what the holder can do. Combining both functions in one document won’t work, frustrates the process and the people and, in the end, is doomed to fail.
