Outline of Don's "Road of Life" educational model incorporating the Oregon Educational Act's six life roles (individual, learner, producer, citizen, consumer, family member), life stages from birth to death, and Bloom's Taxonomy of learning levels. Establishes foundational concepts for conditions of learning and support structures essential to his broader educational philosophy.

About Learners and Learning

Road of Life


Learner – every person is a learner on the road of life



Life Stages:

  1. Early childhood

  2. Childhood

  3. Young adult/youth

  4. Adult

  5. Mature adult

  6. Senior


Life roles*

  1. Individual

  2. Learner

  3. Producer

  4. Citizen

  5. Consumer

  6. Family member

*Oregon Educational Act, 1990


To learn is to change behaviour,

  1. Attitudes –affective - act in a way that is different from before

  2. Skills – psychomotor - able to perform existing skills better or to perform new skills

  3. Knowledge – cognitive - has additional or new knowledge


What we learn – we select


Why learn – Insert Maslow’s needsto satisfy a need

To gain the skills, knowledge and attitudes required to cope with,

contribute to and enjoy life



Importance:

  1. Must know

  2. Should know

  3. Nice to know


Characteristics

  1. Own action although may occur in group setting

  2. Own decision

  3. Own pace

  4. Own style

  5. Own reasons


Levels -Bloom's Taxonomy - visual

  1. Evaluation

  2. Synthesis

  3. Analysis

  4. Application

  5. Comprehension

  6. Understanding!

  7. Awareness


Changes arising from learning are:

  1. Definable

  2. Observable

  3. Demonstrable

  4. Measurable


Conditions for learning:

  1. Motivation

  2. Engagement

  3. Readiness

  4. Opportunity


Support for learning:

  1. Information

  2. Guidance

  3. Encouragement

  4. Demonstration

  5. Model/mentor

  6. Recognition


Education - all the learning that has a continuing formative effect on a person
