EDUCATION

Doc 5 clarity

Education has a clarity problem and it is interfering with our ability to upgrade the educational level of our citizens. And it is not limited to Prince Edward Island

Take the matter of education. Many people believe that going to school and getting or having an education are one and the same. In fact, the length of time in school is frequently used as a measure uof one’s education. I don’t believe it; neither does the World Bank. But the belief continues. 

Education is an umbrella term; it is the result of our life experiences, first in the home, then in school and finally in life itself. Being educated means different things to different people. It is suggested that a good education is one that prepare you to make everyday life decisions.  Definitions are hard to come by in the education literature; I’ve googled a number of Provinces without success.  I’ve also searched other provinces, CMEC, CAMET and the CEA. Whether or not  I agree is not an issue, the fact that definitions are missing from provincial literature is another.

The practice of dealing with education under a school umbrella is diverts or detracts from the business at hand; Iif done deliberately would be considere a questionable practice.
