Press release

April 3, 2014

Education award sparks interest

 

Sometime in late May an Island school or learning centre will receive a cheque for $2,500, and the money can be spent for whatever the school decides; the award is given because engagement and student performance go hand in hand.   Education 20/20 has identified five kinds of engagement, student to student, student to teacher, student to learning material, student to community, and parent involvement; and there are likely others.  Anyone may nominate; nominations close on April 26 with the award being made in late May.


Engagement comes in many forms like the Future Local Forms for Grade 9 French students, the Junior Achievement in Kinkora; providing translation service for newcomers, communication between home and school, cross cultural events and the use of technology. 


Schools may demonstrate engagement in a number of ways, in a activities of a single class, students and teachers  in different classes and ages working together; students undertaking projects out in the community or members of the community working with students and teachers working together in a school; the range of activities reflects the interests and skills in the community.  


This award is made possible through the generosity of an Island family who also believes in the value of engagement.  Further information and nomination forms are available at www.education2020.ca. But act soon.  Nominations close on April 26 and the school or learning centre could have a $2,500 cheque by June 1.  


Contact: Education 20/20 Inc.
