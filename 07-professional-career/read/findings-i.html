Most comprehensive findings document integrating research, complaints, gaps, and conclusions. Research themes include autonomy-achievement relationships (OECD PISA), education-schooling confusion, clarity deficits, fear factors, structural misalignment, bureaucratic rather than field-directed decision-making, politicization, self-correction mechanism absence. Extensive "Current System" characterization lists monopoly, institution-centeredness, obscured accountability, time-based structure, credential-driven focus, "educationese" language, licensing based on credentials, and batch-process limitations. Detailed Gaps and Deficiencies inventory addresses early childhood education, specialist access, prior learning certification, information/advice access, basic skills support, independent learning encouragement, public engagement opportunities. Complaints section documents communications skills emphasis, rising costs, accountability deficits, time-over-achievement focus. Conclusions emphasize widespread quality concerns, structural urgency, system change resistance, internal/external support availability, achievable improvements without disruption, component coordination gaps.
