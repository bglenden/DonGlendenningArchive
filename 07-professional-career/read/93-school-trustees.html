Evaluation - aa overall judgement of what has been learned


School trustees

I was pleased to read in recent days that the matter of school trustees has moved higher on the government agenda; whether or not this will have a positive impact on education will depend on what trustees believe about education, their mandate as a trustee and degree of autonomy.


Gap

Readers may remember that Education 2020 Inc. carried out a study of continuing education in Souris a few months ago. The study revealed an interest in on-line courses, more hands on or practical training, increased employability training, career planning and prrhaps even self-supporting courses.


What surprised me was, not the interest in further training but apparent lack of a person, channel or mechanism to deal with it. This need is handled in other Provinces in a variety of ways:

  • Allowing public school

  • An umbrella organization

  • Private sector


So I’m wondering if, instead of school trustees, perhaps communities need an “education Board or Committee” to advise on or be responsible for ensuring that each community receives the education services that each community needs.


Don Glendenning, Editor
