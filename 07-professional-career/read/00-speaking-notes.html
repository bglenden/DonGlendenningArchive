Don's personal talking points on education dated April 2016, articulating his core beliefs about learner-centered delivery, government's role in ensuring education quality and fairness, and systemic problems with the current OBE model. These notes reflect his decades of education reform advocacy and position that education systems require external intervention to improve.

PERSONAL TALKING POINTS  



Why I speak out:

Education is most important issue of the day

Cost of speaking out too high for most Islanders

It is my profession


This I believe:

Learners centered delivery

Education – intended to help us on the road of life

Role of Department is to ensure quality and fairness of services available; not to actually operate schools 

Grading students is not a school function

Reward people for what they do not for what they know

Role of schools is to help people learn

Current OBE model is not adequate for the task

Standards

Government does not have the capacity or will to change itself


General:

Education issues are not unique to PEI

While problems not created by current government, current government has an opportunity and  responsibility to improve it

School system needs a self-correcting mechanism 

System is not capable of changing itself

Because government runs schools, every decision becomes a political one

Only sector where no one benefits from an improvement in productivity

Current focus is in delivery of service and not on development of participants

Significant gap in expectations between what public wants and what system delivers

Lacks accountability for what they do


Education:

We are all on the road of life where most fill roles of individuals, family member, citizen, learner, consumer and producer.

Each person carries his/her own combination of traits based on genetics, culture, health, emotional make-up, previous experiences and imagined future. 

Education involves development and change in attitudes, skills and knowledge. 

Education and schooling are not the same; we are educated whether or not we go to school

The role of government is to ensure a quality education for every Islander 

Of all sectors, education is the least open to market forces; there are no apparent benefits to those who operate the system for students reaching standards early or reaching higher standards in the available time 

It takes a community to educate a citizen; while the primary responsibility for education rests with one’s self, the major contributor is the home.

Schooling:

Parents want their children to be educated not just schooled; 

The Importance of institutions (schools, colleges and universities) in acquiring an education is on the decline 

The role of schools and teachers is to help people change; 

Credentials and qualifications should neither be determined or awarded by those who teach. 

We have a school system masquerading as an education system; 

The education system lacks incentives to respond positively for calls for change

Teaching is a development process with the role of teachers being to help students learn skills required on the road of life.

The ability of schools is limited,  not only by bureaucracy and inertia, but also by expectations and demands of the community

Improvement in education is directly proportion to their level of autonomy and accountability

Mist students are capabe of graduating but it may take some people longer than others
