An expanded version of the credentialing distinction, providing detailed definitions of credentials as documents issued by officially designated bodies following assessment, indicating capability to perform specified actions. Certification is reframed as a sorting process separate from education, carried out by independent bodies, attesting to ability to perform services, potentially subject to renewal. Don emphasizes certification is not itself part of any learning model. This refined articulation demonstrates his conviction that fundamental system reform requires clarity about institutional roles and separation of educational delivery from credentialing and certification functions.

SORTING-CREDENTIALING


Credential - 

Credential 

  • Document
  • Usually issued by an educational institution
  • Attesting to participation
  • Of a person in a program of studies
  • A piece of paper
  • Provided by an institution or body officially designated for the purpose
  • Following an assessment
  • Indicating that the holder is capable of performing a specified range of actions

Certification:

  • A sorting process 
  • Not an educational function
  • Different from teaching
  • Carried out by an independent body
  • Based on assessment
  • Attest to the ability of a person to perform a service
  • May be valid for a stated period of time/may require renewable
  • Awarded by an independent body
  • Not in itself a le is a statement of what a person can do and how well.
  • Certification is not part of any learning model
