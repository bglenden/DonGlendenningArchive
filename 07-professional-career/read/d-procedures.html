Details the award development and evaluation process. Documents the systematic steps: formulating the idea, establishing the committee, calling for nominations, acknowledging submissions, individual member reviews, and collaborative discussion/confirmation. Outlines five evaluation elements reflecting school control: student-to-student engagement, student-to-teacher engagement, student-to-learning material engagement, student-to-community partnerships, and parental involvement. Reports 11 nominations received from across PEI at all educational levels. Expresses satisfaction with submission quality and documents this as a "great day for education" celebrating community accomplishment and the principle that "it takes a community to raise a child, a community to educate a child."

EDUCATION 20/ 20 INC.

Education Excellence Award

Process and findings

Process

  • Formulated an idea

  • Established committee

  • Called for nominations

  • Acknowledged each nomination

  • Each committee member reviewed documentation

  • Met to discuss and confirm


Elements:

  • Day to day

  • Over which school has control


  • student to student engagement,

  • student to teacher engagement,

  • student to learning materials,

  • student to community partnerships, and

  • parental involvement.


Criteria

  • Communication with family, culture and community

  • Frequwncy of community based events

  • Parent/community involvement in curriculum development and planning




Numbers/ announcements:

  • Eleven nominations

  • Across Island

  • All levels – high school, elementary, Early childhood

  • Some comprehensive and some for specific acts or events



General comments about nominations:

  • Delighted with the number of submissions

  • high level of engagement

  • Quality of nomination documentation

  • Insight into good things that are happening


Great day for education

  • Recognize and celebrate a community accomplishment

  • Not just a student, a class or a club for winning a prize but a school that values engagement and created conditions that increase the likelihood of achievement for all

  • Takes a community to raise a child, A community to educate a child
