Sitting by Nellie

Altered

Although we never used the term, it’s probably accurate to say that much of my early education was gained from “sitting by Nellie”.  No. I’m not talking about a classmate but rather about growing up on a mixed farm on the banks of the Miramichi in New Brunswick and absorbing farm skills, and others, by working beside my older brothers. Sitting by Nellie is not a Miramichi term; it actually arises from the days of child labour in the cotton mills in England where one learned by watching “Nellie” as she tended her loom.  The good part of the experience was the absorption of the plant culture; the downside was that Nellie often lacked the competencies that plant owners hoped would be emulated.

Today’s work placements for students, I suggest, are an attempt to capture some of the advantages gained from sitting by Nellie.  Too short mostly to absorb the employment culture, they do inject some reality into a student’s educational experience.  

My thanks to all the “Nellies” who helped me in my education career.
