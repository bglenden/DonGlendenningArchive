August 2, 2006 letter from Don Glendenning to Simon Compton seeking financial support for an agricultural equipment display project at Orwell. Don references their shared interest in preserving Island-made equipment and their previous site visits together, requesting contributions to the museum project.







August 2, 2006


Mr. Simon Compton

223 Sundance Cove

Stratford, PE

C1B 1L9


Dear Simon:


We’ve talked on occasion about a common interest in finding, retrieving, restoring and housing agricultural equipment made in Prince Edward Island.  One of the major problems, shared by all museums, is that of storage space.


The Prince Edward Island Museum and Heritage Foundation already has a number of pieces of Island-made agricultural equipment and is interested in developing a special display if suitable space were available.   The Community Foundation has been assisting them in their quest and attached is a brief description of the project.


Given our discussions, this letter comes to ask if you and/or other Compton family members, has an interest in contributing financial support to such a project?


You and I visited the site at Orwell and viewed the building a year or so ago.  I would be delighted to tour the site again with you and/or members of your family, and to meet with Museum Staff, if that would be helpful in the decision-making process.


I will contact you again in a couple of weeks to see if you have any questions.


Sincerely



Don Glendenning

Director
