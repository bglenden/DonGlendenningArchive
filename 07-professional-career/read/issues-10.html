About-Issues-10


While there is always room for improvement in any organization, some issue are fundamental, system wide have special significance in that they affect the whole organization, influence every student, and permeate multiple levels of the organization. Issues are topics, problems or concerns of importance to groups of people; they are the big questions. Issues are not simple problems for which definitive answers can easily be found. On the contrary, they are complex and of significance. Once understood, the simpler questions can be dealt with and progress resumed. It’s not always easy to describe an issue in precise terms; sometimes we confuse issues with symptoms and incidents. An issue may be current, that is a problem of today; emerging and potential issues, on the other hand, focus on those threats and opportunities that are like to impact on life tomorrow. But the field is messy. Issues arise in a variety of ways but often become of concern when they impact on personal interests, activities or events, or are related in some way to concerns of friends or family.

AUTONOMY

Simply stated, there is a direct relationship between school performance and school autonomy. An OECD Report in 2010 reported that “In countries where schools have greater autonomy over what is taught and how students are assessed, students tend to perform better.” Autonomy, include such things as allocation of resources, hiring and dismissing teachers, formulating budgets, decisions about curricula and assessment, course offerings and textbooks. In practical terms, But autonomy and accountability needs to be shared with teacher as well. Teachers should have freedom to:
