(115 words):** Don Glendenning outlines potential mathematics education projects designed to align curriculum with practical application and competency-based assessment. Projects include: correlating PISA test content with actual community mathematics use; analyzing course content relevance to community application; developing performance-based rating scales; identifying competencies across grade levels; providing competency lists to students; adopting New Brunswick's Grade 5 citizenship mathematics standards; questioning ranking systems; implementing self-rating practices; developing learning platforms; and establishing comprehensive learning competency frameworks. These projects reflect Don's commitment to making mathematics education relevant, practical, and competency-focused rather than grade-focused. They align with his systemic reform vision of education serving practical life and work needs.
