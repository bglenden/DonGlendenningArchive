Invitation letter to Hon. Richard Brown, Minister of Workforce and Advanced Learning, to attend a screening of the documentary film "Most Likely to Succeed" produced by education philanthropist Ted Dintersmith. Don notes positive reviews from the New York Times and Washington Post. The film showcases project-based learning at High Tech High in San Diego, California, emphasizing relational skills development and student autonomy over content-focused instruction. The screening is scheduled for Sunday, January 17, 2016 at The City Cinema in Charlottetown, with discussion to follow. Don extends a complimentary invitation as his guest.




January 4, 2016

Hon. Richard Brown, Minister

Workforce and Advanced Learning

PO Box 2000

Charlottetown, PE

C1A 7N8


Dear Minister:


As you know, our group has been promoting changes in education. Recently, both the New York Times and Washington Post carried accounts of a new film about education being unrolled across the US; both papers gave the film a high rating.


The film, Most Likely to Succeed, was produced by education philanthropist, Ted Dintersmith, and shows a project based approach to education being followed by a ninth grade class at High Tech High in San Diego, California. It argues for less focus on content and more emphasis on relational skills with students given more latitude in what, when and how they learn.


The film will be shown at The City Cinema, 64 King Street, Charlottetown on Sunday, January 17, 2016 from 2-4:00 pm. We would welcome you as our guest (ticket at door) to be in the audience to view the film and participate in the discussion to follow.


Wishing you the compliments of the season and looking forward to seeing you January 17.


Sincerely




Don Glendenning

President
