Don's 2003 letter nominating Dr. Hesta MacDonald for honorary degree at UPEI, detailing her pivotal role in Holland College governance during his presidency and her ongoing community leadership in education and civic affairs.






April 10, 2003



Dr. Karen Smythe, Registrar,

University of Prince Edward Island

550 University Avenue

Charlottetown PE

C1A 4P3


Dear Dr. Smythe:


I wish to propose and support the nomination of Mrs. Hesta MacDonald for an Honourary Degree at the University of Prince Edward Island.


When I arrived on Prince Edward Island some thirty-four years ago, I soon learned of the community involvement of Hesta MacDonald – and that work has continued unabated.  A few months later she was appointed to the Holland College Board of Governors and elected Chair.   Holland College was a new institution, serving a previously unserved population and doing so by a performance based and learner-centered delivery model.  The Board of Governors was key to shaping this new institution, it’s offerings, locations and delivery model.  Hesta was a leader in shaping the College that emerged.  During her tenure on the Board, she provided encouragement, advice and direction to the work of this new institution.   Our association and friendship has continued ever since.


For a number of years Hesta served as the Information Officer at Holland College and prepared and edited a number of our publications.  As Information Officer, I found her to be a good ambassador throughout our multi-campus operation.  She was also able to identify areas needing improvement and bring these to the attention of the appropriate people.


As a mother and housewife, she was interested in her local school and later in the system of education for the Island in general.  Hesta promoted, and was an active member of, the PEI Home and School Association and was, I believe, its founding President.  She later went on to serve on the national body.  While she had an interest in all aspects of education, she maintained a special interest is the area of abused children.







Her most recent endeavour, at least the ones of which I am aware, is the presidency of the Belfast Historical Society.  Not surprising, under Hesta’s direction, the Society has launched a yearlong series of events to mark the 200th anniversary of the landing of the Selkirk Settlers.  At least one event is planned for each month.  This is a mammoth undertaking for a small organization but I know that it will be a first class event.  It would not have happened without Hesta’s leadership.


Hesta is a writer and a natural storyteller, and has engendered similar interests in family members.  She is a regular contributor to the Senior’s Magazine and edits the Community Reporter for the Community Foundation of Prince Edward Island.  She has written about her family, her community and her youth.  She not only reports on events, she also interprets and draws meaningful conclusions.  


Hesta is a low-key person, but with broad experience and great self-confidence.  She knows her own capabilities.  Her comments are thoughtful, measured, and direct.  She knows her own mind and is not afraid to express her view or have a minority opinion.  She is not a person to seek the limelight or a high public profile.  Her objective is to get the job done.


Hesta’s church and family play a very important role in her life.  She believes in, engenders and supports strong family values.  Family bonds are strong.  She played, and continues to play, a leadership role in her church including the building of a new sanctuary.  She also served on the senior church council at an international level.


I have no hesitation is supporting the nomination of Hesta MacDonald.  It is a well-deserved honour, she will wear it well and be a good ambassador for the University.


Sincerely



Don Glendenning C.M.

President Emeritus

Holland College
