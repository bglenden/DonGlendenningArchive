Education


No. 01 June 1, 2014

C-@


Education - Any and all experiences that have a formative effect

on a person’s attitude, knowledge or skill.


Background


We often confuse schooling and education; we do not similarly confuse health and hospitalization, religion with church attendance or citizenship with court appearances. Seldom do we conclude that a person is healthy because they spent time in a hospital or are religious because they attend church, mosque or synagogue regularly. Why, therefore, do we continue to speak of education when we mean schooling?


To make my point, I was asked recently about my education. I proceeded to tell about the work habits learned on the farm, authors of influence, the impact of a university course in philosophy, backpacking in Europe, a consulting project in Nairobi, etc. all of which had an impact on me. It turned out, however, that all my questioner wanted to know was where I went to school.


Blurring the differences between schooling and education is likely to:

  • Discourage lifelong learning

  • Create dependence rather than promote self-reliance

  • Limit learning to what is available in school or organized by teachers

  • Reinforce the notion that education is limited to the study of traditional disciplines,

  • De-emphasize the importance of personal skills such as self-reliance, problem solving, entrepreneurship and creativity.

  • Blur accountability



Education


As for definitions, Good’s Dictionary of Education defines education as “the aggregate

of all processes by means of which a person develops abilities, attitudes and other forms of behaviour of positive value in the society in which he lives.” A persons education is often described in terms of such skills as creativity, citizenship, social and civic responsibility, communication, respect and cooperation, self-reliance, problem solving, analysis, planning, etc. I happen to like the definition shown at the top of the page.


Schooling


Schooling, on the other hand is described as “individual training or education received in an educational institution, military or civilian.” My working definition describes schooling as that part of one’s education resulting from experiences planned and organized for learners while in school.


Schooling is often described by the name of the institution(s) attended, the field or fields of study, the number of years of study, or by the credential awarded.


Purpose of education


The purpose of education is to help us learn; to learn what? Learn things that will help them function better in our current and future life roles? And what are life roles? I’ve adopted the life roles model developed in Oregon as part of their educational reform in 1990. I find it convenient, easy to understand and simple.



According to the Oregon model, in the course of a lifetime, most people fill roles of:

  • An individual

  • A family member

  • A learner

  • A citizen

  • A consumer

  • A producer.


Obviously, some of what w learn is generally applicable to any and all live rolescommunication skills for example..Based on this model, the purpose of our education, including our schooling, is to help us prepare for these life roles. Others, especially some highly technical skills may be primarily, even wholly used in ones role as a producer.


Role of home, family and community


Education starts at home. The first few years of life and the influence of home and family are most important in shaping who we are and what be become in later life. What we learn while still at home is part of our education but not our schooling. The early years not only result in our learning, they shape our readiness for further learning.


There is increasing recognition of the importance of learning provided by our homes and communities. In parallel with this, is increasing recognition that the delivery of knowledge, which has been the forte of schools In fact is increasingly being picked up by the availability fof information generally but also by the many on-line-courses available at no charge.


Home and community based learning is now increasingly possible and should be supported. New dollars should be invested there rather than in schools but that is a subject for a separate paper.


Responsibility for education


While I recognize the hurdles, many virtually insurmountable, that individuals face in their efforts to improve their education, I believe that, Ultimately, we are all responsible for our level of education While only learners can own learn, and hence, develop their own gestalt, others certainly not only help but play a major role. Family members are our first and most important teachers. To that list we must add teachers, friends and even strangers
