An invitation signed by Don Glendenning for Education 20/20's second conversation series, focused on "DACUM: A Canadian Achievement" (February 12, 2015). The invitation acknowledges DACUM's origins with Robert E. Adams during the 1960s and notes that the discussion would be led by Don Glendenning, Lawrence Coffin, Alice Taylor, and Gaylene Smith, all with extensive DACUM experience. This reflects Don's deep expertise in competency-based curriculum development methodology and his leadership in promoting Canadian educational innovations across PEI.


On behalf of Education 20/20 Inc.

I am pleased to invite you to a presentation

on


DACUM: A CANADIAN ACHIEVEMENT


The event is informal and will provide opportunities to meet and talk

to others who share an interest in education. 

The session, the second of a series of conversations with Island educators,

will be held at the


Haviland Club from 4-6:00 pm

Thursday, February 12, 2015


DACUM (Developing a Curriculum) is a competency based approach to curriculum development; it was the brainchild of Robert E. Adams, Director of Training at the Nova Scotia NewStart Corporation, Yarmouth, Nova Scotia during the late 1960’s. The presentation and discussion will be led by Dr. Don Glendenning, Lawrence Coffin, Alice Taylor and Gaylene Smith, all with extensive experience in using the model.




I look forward to seeing you on January 12.

Don Glendenning, President


Space is limited so an early RSVP is requested

Email: pei@education2020.ca







Education 20/20 inc. is an independent, broad based, national, not-for-profit education organization located in Charlottetown; its mission is to support a quality education for every Canadian.
