Duplicate of the education minister version, this letter invites Doug Currie to the January 17 film screening at City Cinema with follow-up discussion. The identical letter sent to both roles reflects Don's systematic approach to policy engagement and his use of cultural programming as a vehicle for substantive education dialogue with government leadership.



January 11, 2016


Hon. Doug Currie,

Minister of Education, Early Learning and Culture

PO Box 2000, Charlottetown,

PE C1A 7N8


Dear Minister:


First let me congratulate you on your appointment as Minister of Education, Early Learning and Culture; you will understand, I am sure, when I say that it the most important folio of our government.


I also want to impress upon you the importance of education, especially the education of our young people; I do so for the sake of our young people who deserve the best we can offer, but I also do so because of the economic and social benefits a good education brings to our community. PEI has the wherewithal to become a world leader in providing education for its people; to do so will require leadership and some bold moves on the part of Government.


I am enclosing some information about Education 20/20 Inc., an independent, broad based, national, not-for-profit education organization devoted to ensuring a quality education for every Canadian. Among other things, Education 20/20 maintains a website, publishes a monthly bilingual newsletter, offers awards to innovative schools, convenes workshops and discussions and publishes occasional papers. In particular, I call your attention to “A Call for Action” which identifies seven priority recommendations for the improvement of education in this Province.


On Sunday, January 17, our organization is hosting a film screening at the City Cinema – see attachment. We would be delighted to have you in the audience as our guest. The film will be followed by a discussion led by bill Whelan and Ron MacDonald, Dean, Faculty of Education at UPEI. I will hold a seat for you but, should you not be able to attend, I would appreciate hearing from you so that I can release the seat to someone on the waiting list.


As you know, I have an ongoing interest in education and would welcome an opportunity to meet and discuss in detail the education issues facing PEI and what can be done to serve our community better.


Sincerely



Don Glendenning, C.M.

President
