Issues in education

Occasional paper No. 2 October 20, 2013


Issues are topics, problems or concerns of importance to groups of people; they are the big questions. Issues are not simple problems for which definitive answers can easily be found. On the contrary, they are complex and of significance. Once understood, the simpler questions can be dealt with and progress resumed. It’s not always easy to describe an issue in precise terms; sometimes we confuse issues with symptoms and incidents. An issue may be current, that is a problem of today; emerging and potential issues, on the other hand, focus on those threats and opportunities that are like to impact on life tomorrow. But the field is messy. Issues arise in a variety of ways but often become of concern when they impact on personal interests, activities or events, or are related in some way to concerns of friends or family. When trying to sort through issues, it is often useful to begin one’s analysis with the basic questions of who, what, when, where and why. It may also be useful to browse or skim broadly in the field, such as browsing the internet, to get a feel for the issues. In the case of education, while the organization is mine
