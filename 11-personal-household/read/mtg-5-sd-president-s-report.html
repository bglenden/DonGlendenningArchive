  • Requested a meeting with Imelda Arsenault and colleagues and identified reading and challenge for credit as two areas of interest; no response as of time of preparing this report.  Obviously, I would be delighted if you can attend.  I expect that Tom Hall will be interested in attending.  It would be good to have a small group to meet  on a regular basis.  Need discussion.
  • Had a brief conversation with Barb MacNutt and Audrey Penner about a meeting to discuss reading.  Would like to discuss how we handle meetings of this kind
  • Need to make plans for our annual meeting
  • I had asked Bill Whelan and Maureen Kerr for comments about the “engagement software.  Their responses were:
  • Maureen: Merry Christmas! I did have a look and it appears similar to a project management software program I'm familiar with called Basecamp. I'd say it would be useful once you started to scale your organization and have to project manage more than a handful of people. I'd be happy to go through it with you in person if you'd like.
  • I reviewed ThoughtStream and I think it appears to be an excellent way
    to gather, organize and prioritize information from individuals in an
    organization. The interactive site is user friendly and easy to use. I
    really like the capability to group thoughts into major areas as part of
    a convergence exercise, and then allow the group members to prioritize
    ideas. The key utility of this software is to provide for a more focused
    and efficient discussion when the group gets together face-to-face (eg
    all the pre-work up have been completed in ThoughtStream). Hence the
    group can focus on solutions/strategies moving forward for the top
    ideas. And the price is very reasonable.
