Official description of Education 20/20 Inc.'s Excellence in Education Award (2015), established with Island family funding to recognize schools demonstrating high engagement. Award emphasizes family-school-community partnerships, citing research showing parental involvement improves student achievement and school morale. Five engagement areas evaluated: student-to-student, student-to-teacher, student-to-instruction, school-community partnerships, and parent involvement in education.

Excellence in Education Award - 2015


BACKGROUND


Our future as individuals and as communities depends to a large degree on the quality of our education. Gaining a quality education, therefore, should be a major focus of every citizen, providing a quality education should be the focus of governments, and providing a quality education the objective of every teacher and school. An award is for having done something done rather than a grant to do something.


Thanks to the generosity of an Island family, Education 20/20 Inc. has established an awards program as a mechanism to call attention by the public to important characteristics of effective schools and to provide some recognition to schools for their efforts to implement them. The focus of the award is on engagement for, as the National Parent Teacher Association points out that “when families, schools, and communities work together student achievement improves, teacher morale rises and communication increases”. Study after study show the dramatic impact parental involvement makes to a child's success in school. In fact, engaging families in their children’s education improves school achievement, attendance, behaviour and graduation rates. The focus will be on the daily operation of the school and on those items over which the school has substantial control.


ELEMENTS OF ENGAGEMENT

Education is interested in a broad engagement across the institution including:

  • Student engagement with peers showing based on respect, cultural competency, caring, courtesy, tact, honour, cooperation and collaboration.



  • Learning environment should be welcoming and one that is safe and comfortable physically and emotionally, based on the assumptions of respect, cultural competency, trust, care, optimism and intentionality. School encourages integration of learning across courses and invites students to discuss “big questions” that matter beyond the classroom. Teacher encourages critical inquiry, writing, research, collaborative learning and other skills that develop students' intellectual and practical skills. Teacher ensures he/she is on leading edge of current teaching practices and technology so as to better prepare students' for 21st century learning



  • Student to instruction: student engage actively in learning and helps students make meaning of the information for application in the real world. Some examples would include collaborative projects, i.e. learning to work and solve real problems in groups, by sharpening their own understand through listening seriously to insights of others especially those from different backgrounds and cultures. Students could also be encouraged to record example of their best work through a portfolio or video and discuss their learning with other classmates and teachers.



  • School Community Partnerships: Experiential learning with community partners is an instructional strategy and can be made part of a course. This involves direct experience with issues they are studying in the curriculum. The opportunity to learn, analyze information, and be a participant in community setting also reinforces the idea of good citizenship, i.e. giving back to the community. Other examples of student/community partnership may include fundraising for various community projects and involvement in diverse community service.



  • Parent involvement in their children’s education: Parents and teachers are partners in the challenge of helping children reach their potential and like any partnership, communication is key. Partnership activities will likely include parent teacher discussions, newsletters, participation in events,

DOCUMENTATION


As background, nominees should provide brief description about what the school is trying to achieve and how engagement supports their objectives. perhaps something already distributed to parents. Along with the general description, should be not more than a couple of pages of evidence for each category.



NOMINATION PROCESS


Education 20/20 called for nominations, nominations are welcomed from any interested person with the actual selection being made by a nomination committee based on documentation
