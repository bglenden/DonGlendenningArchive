Glendenning analyzes organizational structures of Canadian education bodies (CAMET, CMEC) and provides critical comments on PEI education specifically. He identifies systemic problems: functions inadequately identified, major services not represented at organizational top levels, operations based on client institutions rather than service delivery, unclear roles and accountability, and confusion between "going to school" and "getting an education." He recommends restructuring based on functions, writing clear role descriptions, and delegating decision-making downward. He notes that OECD research shows better educational outcomes in schools with operational autonomy. This assessment likely drove his broader reform agenda.
