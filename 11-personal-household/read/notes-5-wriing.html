Working notes on reading assessment and rating scales, outlining key performance criteria (range, comprehension, fluency, independence, speed, vocabulary) and proposing a 6-level proficiency scale applicable from high school through adult education. The framework reflects Don's systematic approach to defining measurable competencies independent of age or grade level.

Key Performance Criteria:

  • Range of material
  • Comprehension
  • Fluency
  • Independence
  • Speed
  • Vocabulary


Levels

Rating scales normally have at least a half dozen levels.  To complete a scale for reading, we need to add a brief description of other levels of reading ability, that is a way to describe readers  who read at higher or lower levels of proficiency.  While the above was created with high school graduation in mind, the same rating scale can be applied to adult readers at any level whether they be in college, university of adult classes.


Performance Levels (discernible):

  • 6- Read a wide variety of printed materials with fluency and ease
  • 5-
  • 4-Read, without assistance, a range of printed materials with independence, fluency and ease
  • 3- 
  • 2-
  • 1-
  • 0- does not recognize any words on a printed page.
