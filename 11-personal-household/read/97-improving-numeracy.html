Don distinguishes between education as a service to learners versus education as a product focused on credentials. He argues that educational achievement should be demonstrated through practical competencies (writing, repairs, household management) rather than abstract credentials, and criticizes the prevalence of course numbers, credits, and grades in departmental communications. He advocates for educational reporting that reflects real-world competencies learners have developed, drawing implicit contrast with the current system's focus on administrative credentials and course structures rather than demonstrated capability.
