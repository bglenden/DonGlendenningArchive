Opportunities and barriers:

Souris weighs in on training needs


Souris residents had an opportunity Wednesday evening to hear first hand the results of a study about training needs and opportunities in their community. Like many other small communities, Souris faces challenges of a declining population and a seasonal workforce. Employers are faced with a shrinking labour pool and available people frequently lack needed skills, especially skills needed to deal with specialized equipment.


Training institutions in the area are running at or near capacity and do not have the resources to increase offerings; Holland College will be offering upgrading classes including courses on line. Forty percent of respondents reported exploring training opportunities in the past three years. When asked about training need to improve their own employment situation, respondents mentioned a wide range of practical courses in the heath, small business, computer studies and trades such as carpentry. When asked their preferred delivery method, 59 percent were interested in receiving instruction on line, 40% through night school and 38% via correspondence or self study.


Strategies for closing the gap include courses on line, more information about training options and availability, hands on practical training, how to operate specialized equipment, extending the school day at Souris High,


The study included a review of current offerings and capacity, interests and needs for further training as viewed by residents, employers and the community at large. The study was conducted for Education 20/20 by MRSB and was funded by the Department of Workforce and Advanced Learning through the Canada-PEI labour Market Agreements. The study comes at a crucial time in that PEI is in the midst of population growth and a strong economy; future employment will require a flexible and skilled workforce. The final report is available on the Education 2020 website..


Education 20/20 Inc. is an independent, broad-based national, charitable organization located in Prince Edward Island with a focus on ensuring a quality education for every Canadian.
