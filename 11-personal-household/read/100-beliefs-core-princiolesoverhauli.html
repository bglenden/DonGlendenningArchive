Includes extensive notes on systemic problems and recommendations.

Core Beliefs

Education, in fact every organization, needs a set of core beliefs to guide decision-making, and the list should be written and widely quoted.  Such a document can take many forms – a philosophy, mission, mandate or guiding principles,  A properly crafted statement becomes a reference statement for the organization, improves communication, increases consistency, supports decentralization, is a development tool,  assures the public that the organization is well run – a set of beliefs is a basic document from which all decisions flow.  

Such a document may well exist but I have not been able to find it.  Yes, I know there is a ”philosophy of education” that applies to public schooling, but it is out of date and conveniently fuzzy; mandate letters tend to deal with priorities not foundational beliefs, as do Ministerial Directives.  I browsed a number of school websites as well as those of the University and Holland College. None of my searches revealed the kind of document that  I feel is necessary. It should be brief, a page or two at most; Holland College, based its early development a dozen or so foundational statements.

While the process requires the input of educators, I’m not sure that it should be led by an educator; I view education as a service to Islanders, this document is a public stafement as to the principles on which the system will be built.  Could the committee be com[psed of MLA”s?




 



I am not suggesting a Newfoundland or Nova Scotia style study; we should prepare the plan ourselves with outside help if needed.
