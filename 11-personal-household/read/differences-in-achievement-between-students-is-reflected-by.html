Brief note on tracking student achievement differences through range/nature of skills and performance levels. Proposes electronic record-keeping system showing learner progress with dated skill-level changes. Demonstrates Don's systems-thinking approach to education assessment and his focus on observable, measurable learning outcomes.
Personal & Household

Differences in achievement between students is reflected by:

  • The range and nature of skills or competencies each person is able to perform.
  • Level of performance on each skill.   


I visualize the record keeping as a list of outcomes in a column down the left side of a page, although, it fact, the record will be generated electronically.  In the space to the right of each outcome, a teacher records and dates each change of level and such other information as may be germane to the learning process.  A glance at the page quickly shows the unfolding picture of a learner’s progress.  One can easily monitor by comparing current skills and levels with skills and levels at an earlier date. In term of monitoring and reporting progress, one literally “sees” growth happen.
