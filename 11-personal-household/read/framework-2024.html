DEFINITIONS

  • Learner – a person who is in the process of changing the way they think, feel or act

  • Learning – any experience that has a formative effect the way a person thinks, feels or acts

  • Student – a person who is enrolled in a school or training institution

  • Education – a person’s education is a state of development that allows them to adequately cope with all demands of daily life (Anon)

  • Quality education: “…one that provides all learners with capabilities they require to become economically productive, develop sustainable livelihoods, contribute to peaceful and democratic societies and enhance individual well-being…” (New Zealand Education Authority)

  • Education system – services put together by a community to encourage and support the education of its citizens

  • Credential – usually refers to academic qualifications by a school, college, or university

  • Qualification – a document attesting to the fact that the holder has the attitude, skills and knowledge required to successfully perform in a role or task

LEARNERS AND LEARNING


A learner is a person who, for whatever reason, is trying to bring about change in their life - trying to change an attitude, gain new knowledge or add to skills. But learners are neither empty jars nor blank slates; each learner carries three pieces of luggage to every new learning situation:

  • Certain physical, mental and emotional conditions with which a learner is born;

  • Prior learning resulting from formal study, home circumstances, travel, state of health, personal interests, culture in which they operate and learning style;

  • Aspirations and imagined future including where one will live, the work one will do and the lifestyle one will have, the nature of the family, quality of life, etc.



Most of us need help in at least some of our new learning experiences; students tell us that they sometimes need:

  • Information: recent, independent, reliable, timely

  • Presentation: demonstrations, discussion and debate

  • Feedback: effective, valid, transparent, relevant

  • Counselling: planning, coaching,

  • Opportunity: access, time, funding, transportation

  • Products and technologies: textbooks, references, self-instructional material and assistive technologies


STUDENT ASSESSMENT


Robert Paul Wolff (Wolff: A discourse on Grading, 1969) identifies

three purposes for assessing students:

  • Criticism or analysis for the purpose of designing additional strategies to help a learner learn; criticism is part of the ongoing give and take of the learning process.

  • Evaluation is designed to establish a person’s level in a subject or field and should be criterion referenced.

  • Ranking shows how a person stacks up against others; ranking results in students being winners or losers. Students should be encouraged to participate and teachers and students can use the results of competitions but teachers should play no part in the ranking process.


BENCHMARK

A benchmark is a level of performance which is widely recognized by practitioners and the public at large and serves as a reference against which others are judged. A benchmark may be a parent, local businessman or other personality.





LEVELS OF LEARNING

The Revised Bloom’s Taxonomy recognizes six levels of learning:

  1. Creating: Builds a structure or pattern from diverse elements. Put parts together to form a whole, with emphasis on creating a new meaning or structure

  2. Evaluating: Make judgments about the value of ideas or materials

  3. Analyzing: Separates material or concepts into component parts so that its organizational structure may be understood. Distinguishes between facts and inferences.

  4. Applying: Use a concept in a new situation or unprompted use of an abstraction. Applies what was learned in the classroom into novel situations in the work place.

  5. Understanding: Comprehending the meaning, translation, interpolation, and interpretation of instructions and problems. State a problem in one's own words.

  6. Remembering: Recalling previous learned information.





REPORTING STUDENT PROGRESS

Some states issue report cards on schools as well as students. With the introduction of ungraded schools, student progress can be reported on an outcome-by-outcome basis.

  • The purpose of education is to help a person gain attitudes, skills and knowledge that will serve them well for the remainder of their life’s journey.

QUALITY EDUCATION

UNICEF, The International Working Group on Education, Florence, Italy, 2000 tells us that a quality education system is one where:

  • Learners are healthy

  • Environments are safe and respectful

  • Content is relevant

  • Outcomes are linked to personal and community objectives

  • Support is focused on learners and learning

  • Information and advice is timely and independent

  • Teachers are professional, autonomous and accountable


OECD, (PISA in Focus, 2011) studied PISA results in 13 countries and concluded that “in countries where schools have greater autonomy” over what is taught and how students are assessed, students tend to perform better”.


MEI, (2015) an Educational Think Tank in Montreal, concluded that “accountability and autonomy jointly applied are key factors in school achievement and that changes to accountability and autonomy would cost government nothing, would entail better academic results and allow education to better adapt to the diversity of student needs and aptitudes.


WORLD BANK-Education Crisis. 2019 Successful education reforms require good policy design, strong political commitment, and effective implementation capacity.

TYPICAL EDUCATION SERVICES

As identified by the Business Roundtable:

  1. Information - recent and independent information needed in the making of decisions about their own education or the education of others

  2. Counselling – help to people in making decision about their education interests and needs.

  3. Instruction - The provision of focused help to others in their learning endeavors including diagnosis, counselling, presentations, practice and access generally to a range of other resources

  4. Quality assurance - a system that assures the community that their learning experiences, includingexperiences with service providers, meet community standards.

  5. Products and Technology - Access by citizens to educational materials, technology and other resources to help learners develop attitudes skills, knowledge and other at-tributes needed to meet their needs and objectives.

  6. Funding -funding to a level and in a manner that is equitable for all Islanders.

  7. Human resources - Teachers, administrators, counsellors, advisors. assistants, leaning managers and others with the knowledge and experience needed to help others learn.

MEANING

Education has several meanings:

1. A set of services put in place by a community to encourage and support the education of its citizens,

2. A sector of the economy having to do with the, promotion supply and regulation of education services,

3. A process of creating experiences which result in others learning skills, knowledge, attitudes and other attributes, and

4. The state or level of a person’s ability to deal with daily living
