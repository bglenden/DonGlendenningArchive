MAJOR CHANGES COMING

I usually scan a number of newspapers and reports each week in search of what’s happening in education. Last week brought surprises in the form of governments adopting policies that shape what institutions offer and the quality students receive

My position is simple; our education starts the day we are born and continues for the rest of our life. Citizens play or fill a number of life roles – individual, citizen, family member, learner, consumer and producer, Education can allow and help us thrive in all roles. The role of government is to promote, encourage, support and regulate education services.


My reference to change was reported in my last newsletter. Both Australia and the Province of Ontario introduced legislation to make grants to postsecondary institutions that reflect the importance of certain studies to the community served. In other worse, programs of greater need or benefit to the community will be cheaper to encourage increased numbers – economic interest rather than personal interests, so to speak.




This morning’s Sydney Herald reports that schools in Australia will have targets based on whether students are studying, working or unemployed five years after they leave school. student growth, phonics, attendance, wellbeing, Aboriginal education and post-school pathways. Education Minister Sarah Mitchell said everyone in the education system needed to shoulder responsibility for student success.

  1. the need for well defined goals and objectives

  2. increased communication between providers and users of educational services and

  3. a sharper focus on quality for every student.


At the same time, we need to be extremely cautious that we don’t simply feed the standards race.



Don Glendenning, Editor
