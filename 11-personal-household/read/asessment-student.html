Layman's guide to student assessment for the PEI education agenda. Defines testing, assessment, evaluation, grading, standards, qualifications, and credentials. Background

The matter of assessment is back on the PEI education agenda, a stress raising signals to students, teachers, parents and the community alike. It’s likely that taxpayers will need a ready reference for some assessment terms. We hope this description helps; it can also be found at www.education2020.ca


First some definitions:

  • Testing - an objective and standardized method for estimating the extent of achievement based on a sample of desirable achievement

  • Assessment - is a process of gathering information about a person’s performance on a task or

set of tasks for the purpose of initiating improvement

  • Evaluation - an overall judgement of what has been learned

  • Grading - the process of applying standardized measurements of varying levels of achievement in a course.

  • Standards - a readily distinguishable level of performance, demonstrated through the making of a product or provision of a service, which is deemed to be worthy of recognition.

  • Qualifications – a document attesting to the fact that a person has the attitudes, skills and knowledge required to successfully peorm in a role or task.

  • Credentials – a document issued by an educational institution indicating that the holder has completed a certain program of studies

  • Benchmark - level of performance which is widely recognized by practitioners and the public at large as a reference against which others are judged

  • Provincial assessments -

  • Rating scale/grid – a scale which attaches a numerical value to each discernable level of performance on a competency

  • Benchmark – a level of performance which is widely recognized by practitioners and the public at large and which serves as a reference against which others are evaluated


Purposes

Generally agreed that student assessment serves three purposes:

  • Formative assessment, sometimes called analyses and feedback, is at the heart of teaching and learning and involves the give and take between teacher and learner about what happened or should happen during the learning process; assessment focuses on learning, teaching, outcomes, competencies, skills or even the acquisition of knowledge. This kind of assessment provides information for improving learning and teaching and is an interactive process between student and teacher designed to inform both learner and teacher how well their students are learning what teachers are teaching. The information is used by teachers to make changes in what will be taught next and when, where, how and even why. It is considered feedback to students, shared fully and probably confidentially, unique to each learner, course or incident based and sometimes anonymous and not even connected to passing or grading.



Evaluation or summative assessment is used to establish a person’s level or grade in a subject or field; it is a regulatory function responding to society’s demand for minimum levels of performance in certain life roles. Evaluation should be criterion referenced, focused on establishing a level of achievement and may take into account more than course content and academic success depending on its purpose including discussion, cooperation, attendance, presentation, etc. Such levels are usually referred to as qualifications, certification or licensing; qualification bodies are usually set up by or under government regulation and operated as independent bodies. Education seems to be an exception; teachers not only teach but also grade. It’s not allowed in other spheres; we shouldn’t allow it in education either. We need an independent body to establish levels, test students and award certificates. Having an independent testing service would improve consistency of results, reduce teacher workload, reduce chances of bias, minimize cheating, and improve system flexibility and support independent study. Education levels are a form of summative assessment in which all may pass or fail. For these reasons, most sectors and many educational organizations separate the teaching and the grading functions; apprenticeship, International baccalaureate program, qualification authorities and are examples of the latter approach. Education really confuses the issue when it links age and grade levels in a single word


  • Ranking involves measuring how one person stacks up against others of the same age, number of years of study, in other countries; ranking serves a number of functions in an educational system including accountability, gate-keeping, instructional diagnosis and monitoring of student achievement. In the view of the writer, ranking is certainly not a school function and may not be an educational function either.

Conclusion

Summative assessment nor ranking provide useful information teachers and students but should not be considered teaching or school functions; steps should be taken immediately to transfer these responsibilities to an independent body. It is likely that establishing levels can be limited to a small number of core skills. Evaluation should be criterion referenced, focused on establishing a level of achievement and may take into account more than course content and academic success depending on its purpose including discussion, cooperation, attendance, presentation, etc
