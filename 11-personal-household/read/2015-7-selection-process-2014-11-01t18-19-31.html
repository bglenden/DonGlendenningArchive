Detailed selection process for Education 20/20's Engagement Award (2014-15). Committee members review nomination packages using scoring grids, prepare comments, identify top candidates by numerical scoring, meet under board-member chairmanship to ensure inclusive discussion, decide by consensus or executive override, and recommend recipient to Board for final approval. Emphasizes transparent, collaborative decision-making.

Education 20/20 Selection Process for the “Engagement Award”

EDUCATION 20/20 INC.

Excellence in Education

Awards Program – 2014-15


1. Selection Committee members receive the completed nomination packages.  A member of the Selection Committee notifies the nominees and asks if they wish their name to stand.  Following these confirmations, the list of nominees and respective packages are dispersed to members of the Selection Committee.   Members review nominations and prepare comments for discussion at the selection committee meeting. Each committee member should come to the meeting prepared with a list of who they feel are the two strongest candidates for the award.  Selection of the strongest will be made based on a numerical scoring and comments. (See attached grid.)


2. The selection committee meeting will be chaired by a board member of Education 20/20 Association. The purpose of the chair is to ensure all committee members have opportunity to bring forward their ideas and that no one person dominates the decision making process.


3. Decisions will be made by consensus. In the event that the committee is unable to reach consensus, the final decision will be made by the Education 20/20 executive member on the selection committee.


4. The committee will propose one potential recipient to the Education 20/20 Board of Directors, for the Board’s final decision, with documentation provided.
