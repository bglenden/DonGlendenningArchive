Robert Paul Wolf (Wolf: A discourse on Grading, 1969) identified three purposes for

school assessment:

  • Analysis or criticism (formative) designing additional strategies in support of learning.  analysis prepares a teacher to provide meaningful feedback and is part of the ongoing give and take of the learning process
  • Evaluation- to establish a person’s level in a subject or field, criterion referenced
  • Ranking – how one stacks up against others


Assessment versus evaluation

Assessment change in learning teaching and outcomes, interactive between teacher; intended to improve learning “information is learner-centered, course based frequently anonymous and not graded”   Duke University


Evaluation focuses on grades and may reflect classroom components other than course content and mastery level – duke Edu
