Detailed analysis of Colonel Gray High School's exam exemption policy (effective September 2011), including qualification criteria (attendance, marks ranging from 65-80% depending on absences, completed coursework, behavioral standards).   1. Colonel Gray Exemption Policy (Revised Effective September 2011)

To qualify for an exemption students must meet the following criteria:

Students must be enrolled in and complete four 110 hour credit courses in a semester.

2. To qualify for an exemption students must meet either of the following mark and

attendance criteria below in all four courses:

a. no more than 6 absences and a minimum grade of 65% or

b. no more than 12 absences and a minimum grade of 80%.

• An absence has been defined by the Student Records Management Steering Committee

as anytime a student misses more than 50% of the scheduled class time.

• The following absences are considered excused in determining exempt status. All other

absences will be used to determine the total number of days absent during the

semester.

AC – Absence Curricular – Activities directly associated with the

curriculum where the full class is expected to participate.

AA – Absence Approved School - absences approved by admin, meetings

with counselors, youth workers, etc. In rare cases, an absence may be

coded as AA by the administration to allow a student to participate in an

event when the student has been absent due to a high level of

involvement in school based extra-curricular activities.

3. Students must have completed all course work and assessments as assigned by their

four subject teachers.

4. Student behavior must meet the expectations of their teachers, school (Code of

Conduct), school board (Caring Places to Learn Policy, etc.), and the School Act.

Administration reserves the right to veto an exemption if the behavior of the student is not acceptable.

5. Students must write a least one exam each semester

6. Students may exempt a maximum of 2 exams per semester



  1. The Colonel Gray Development Committee took up the matter of school attendance and engagement at their school during the spring and summer of 2006. The assumption was that having students at school increased their chances of being taught and doing well on school studies. They proposed “an Examination Exemption Policy” presumably to the Department and the program was implemented in 2007.

  1. In 2010, I believe, the Province contracted with UPEI (Tim Goddard, Dean of Education) to do an evaluation of the Program. Goddard and his team conduct quite an intensive review ans recommended that the program be continued. The report included the following five recommendations:

  • Academic benchmarks be revised

  • Categories of acceptable absences be extended

  • A minimum number of examinations be maintained

  • English and math not be exempted, and

  • Mid-term examinations be implemented

  1. PEI The Senior High Working Group (2013) made the case for increased flexibility in allowing students to gain credit in a variety of ways including community based learning, community service, cooperative education, service learning, distance education and challenging for credit.
