Undated 10 Key definitions Glendenning provides foundational definitions of core educational concepts. Learner: a person initiating change in… ▸ more
Glendenning provides foundational definitions of core educational concepts. Learner: a person initiating change in their life, carrying three pieces of "luggage" (inherent conditions, prior learning, future aspirations), filling diverse life roles (individual, family member, student, citizen, consumer, producer). Learning: multidimensional experience with formative effects on thoughts, feelings, and actions, occurring anywhere/anytime. Education: accumulated experiences with measurable effects on person's development. Education System: community services supporting people's development. Teaching: arranging experiences creating changes in others' knowledge, beliefs, and capabilities. These definitions distinguish education from schooling and position learners as active agents with complex backgrounds and multiple life roles, reflecting his learner-centered philosophy.

Undated 500 WHAT an A Don questions what "A" grades actually signify, noting schools apply such labels despite no evidence they meaningfully… ▸ more
Don questions what "A" grades actually signify, noting schools apply such labels despite no evidence they meaningfully describe student capability. He contrasts educational grading with medical assessment: doctors use specific measurements (weight, blood pressure, PSA levels) without averaging across categories to inform clinical judgment, while teachers average unrelated subjects' grades. Don sought written explanations of what constitutes an "A" from schools with minimal success, finding transcripts unhelpful. He argues an "A" should clearly define performance—does it mean writing clearly, having superior vocabulary, speaking logically? If so, say so; if not, what does it mean? He provides examples: a plumber who solves problems independently, a writer using creative expression with proper grammar, a technician understanding deep processes. People deserve to know what grade labels mean, and employers need such clarity for hiring decisions.
