A year-end reflection written December 30, 1986, meditating on sunlight, shadows, and the passage of time. The poem contemplates life's ups and downs, the search for perspective and understanding as the year ends, and hope for renewal in the coming year.
Creative Writing

Untitled



Year end,

 Sunlight, 

Shadows, 

Reflections, 

New Year. 


Sunlight glitters 

On new fallen snow 

On the window sill.

In the yard, 

On a neighbour's roof 

Across the harbour 

Disappearing over the horizon. 



Sunshine of Christmas 

Family 

Friends 

Health 

Accomplishments 

Aglow at year end. 



Shadows accompany 

Days at bright sunshine 

Some playing lightly 

On Life's unfolding 

Others unfathomable 

Forbidding, 

Foreboding, 

Darkest of shadows 

Impenetratable 

Leaving a permanent scar. 


Year end reflections 

Fleeting 

Encompassing 

Presenting a montage of life. 

Heights not imagined 

Depths unimaginable 

Life's roller coaster 

Included them all. 


In the peace of the season 

A sense of perspective 

Usually found. 

Instead, still eludes me 

Haunting, disturbing 

Will it ever be found? 


Though 

Understanding eludes me 

Hope springs eternal 

The advant of a New Year 

Beckons me on


Dec 30, 1986.
