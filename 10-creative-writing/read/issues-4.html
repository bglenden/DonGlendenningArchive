(150 words):** This document analyzes why education systems are slow to adapt to changing conditions, comparing education to other institutional sectors (health, justice, religion, hospitality). Don identifies five characteristic categories affecting change resistance: autonomy, clarity, accountability, structure, and culture. He then catalogs contemporary issues across K-12 and higher education: higher education faces student debt, completion rates, mental health, accreditation questions; K-12 confronts funding, safety, discipline, technology, charter school debates, standardized testing, teacher salaries, bullying, poverty, class size. Structural barriers include unclear purpose, limited autonomy, undefined accountability, inadequate reward systems, and risk-averse culture. Additional issues span health/wellness, equity, governance relationships, and resource adequacy. This document reveals Don's systematic thinking about institutional change barriers and his comprehensive understanding of contemporary education challenges at multiple system levels.
