Guidelines for Occasional Papers

Background:


It is widely recognized that the level and quality of education is key to our future as individuals, families, communities and countries. There is also a widely held view that the education system we now have is unlikely to provide the kind of education our people need, especially our young people. There is a widespread call for change from many quarters, including educators, employers, and the public at large. The field is broad, interests are diverse and widespread and resources are scarce. At the same time, the need for information is both urgent and widespread.

Information is the fuel of change and, while the availability of relevant information does not guarantee change, it certainly supports it. Education First is of the view that providing the public with relevant information, in addition to being useful in its own right, will support the public in its quest for greater participation in the education process. One approach is to develop and distribute to the public a series of occasional papers on matters of interest and concern

Proposal

To gather and disseminate information geared to the public at large especially those with children still in the education system. Education first proposes to do this through a series of occasional papers focused on topics gleaned from our conversations with educators and the public alike. The primary focus will be on topics that, in the view of Education First, contribute to a quality education for every Canadian.

Characteristics

  • Each issue is a stand-alone issue

  • Limited to two sides of a standard page

  • A standard format will be developed.

  • Guest authors accepted

  • Each paper is a stand alone


Initial topics

  • Outcomes and competencies

  • Standards

  • Independent learners

Approaches

  • Work with Journalism students, teachers in training, paid journalists, retirees


Administration

  • Committee to include public members

  • Preference for Island writers

  • Part of an initiative to develop Island expertise

  • While new writers will be welcome standards will be set
