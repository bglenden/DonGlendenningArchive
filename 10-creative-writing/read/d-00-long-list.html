This extensive brainstorming document catalogs dozens of educational initiatives under six strategic areas: Focus on Teaching, Challenge for Credit, Grade Improvement, Library-Based Services, Provincial Clinic, and Comprehensive Reform. Don synthesizes diverse concepts from educational research (including references to Pathways to Education from Globe and Mail). Proposals range from teacher competency identification to early childhood development to qualification authorities to summer programs. The "First Steps Plan" outlines ambitious systemic improvements including early childhood education reform, school performance focus, independent learning encouragement, and qualification authority establishment. This document reveals Don's comprehensive educational vision and capacity for synthesizing complex ideas into actionable frameworks.

6 ESTABLISH EXTERNAL QUALIFICATION AUTHORITY

An external assessment authority, not part of the public education system, to provide a formal assessment of what has been learned.  The authority would not issue credentials or qualifications as such but would, for a fee, provide individuals, institutions of higher education, certification and licensing bodies with unbiased results of a student’s performance.  This will both allow and encourage individuals of any age to study on their own and provide a simple and readily accessible system for receiving formal recognition.  The emphasis should be on what the individual knows and can do; whether or not that qualifies for acceptance into postsecondary education or employment should be determined by the receiving organization.  t
