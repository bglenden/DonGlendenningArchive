Short reflective poem dated October 22, 1981 in Vancouver, invoking Will Rogers's adage that "strangers are friends we haven't met." Celebrates the experience of moving to a new community and discovering that strangers become neighbors, friends, teachers become counselors, and even landlords are not villains. Reflects Don's adaptability and ability to build community wherever he relocated during his career transitions.
Creative Writing

New Friends



Will rogers was right

Strangers are friends we haven’t met


How interesting again

To be catabulted

Into a new community

At first so new and strange

Perhaps


The stranger next door

Is a neighbour

Is a daaughter’s school mate

The teacher is a councilor

The landlord is not a villan

Even the telephone voice

Is no longer unknown


Yesterday we met strangers

Today, they are friends



Vancouver, oct 22, 1981
