Narrative poem depicting a young daughter's morning routine and demands for parental attention. Captures the chaotic energy of childhood—requesting snuggles, watching cartoons, teasing the cat, and the cascading needs from early morning through school departure. Conveys parental patience, affection, and the organized chaos of raising a child, likely inspired by Don's daughter Janet.

My Little Girl

Daddy, 

Where are you?

I just want to know

Will you snuggle with me?

I don’t want you to go


From daybreak 

To sun down

And later than that

Watching cartoons

And teasing the cat


A knock on the door

At seven o’clock

Wakens us all

Noise in the bathroom

Feet in the hall


Quiet again 

For a few minutes more

Except for the footsteps

And the rustle of paeer

And a knock on the door


Mommy, I’m hungry

What time is it, Mom

No use pretending

Night is now ending

“Ok, then I’ll come


Her room is in shables

Hher clothes on the floor

Paper and books

A half  dozen coat hooks

A “trap” at the door


Clothes on at last

Teeth brushed

Hair combed

Amid all the commotion

Just ry to stay cool





A last minute rush

Then out of the door

Quiet at last

What’s that at the door?


I need my crayons

(Or pencil or paper)

I pity poor teacher

Who puts up with such capers


The scene is repeated

Day after day

Questions and questions

What does one say.
