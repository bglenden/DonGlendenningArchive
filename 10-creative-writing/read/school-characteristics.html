School, characteristics

Home › Creative Writing › School, characteristics
A refined critique from February 2014 explicitly comparing school system characteristics against current educational thinking. Don identifies key discrepancies: client-centeredness versus content delivery, achievement versus time spent, teaching versus instructing, spiral versus linear curriculum, and coaches versus referee models for teachers. The document reinforces absence of market mechanisms creating perverse incentives and critiques the monopoly structure preventing outsourcing, credit for external learning, or independent assessment. Non-educational activities (ranking, transportation, fundraising, competitive sports, day care) are identified as distraction from core educational mission. This refined version demonstrates Don's evolving sophistication in diagnosing systemic problems and the conceptual frameworks needed for reform.

Gaps:

  • Autonomy

Non-educational activities
