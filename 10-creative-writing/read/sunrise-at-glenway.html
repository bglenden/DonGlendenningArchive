A meditative poem celebrating the dawn over Orwell Bay as seen from Glenway (the summer cottage). Don captures the beauty of the landscape—water, silos, geese—and expresses gratitude for natural blessings and the privilege of owning property with such views. The poem reflects his love of the cottage property and his poetic sensibility, using natural imagery to express contentment and spiritual appreciation.

Sunrise at Glenway



The flag flutters idly in the breezes

The water ripples gently in the bay

The silos point their domes toward the heavens

As morning dawns again on Orwell Bay


The sky begins to brighten to the eastward

Shaping silhouettes along the way

The canopy above begins to brighten

As dawn returns again to Orwell Bay


The stars hhve disappeared at glenway

The moon has disappeared in light of day

The morn is about to break in all its glory

As I contemplate the  beauty of the day


My deck becomes a front row seat to beauty

As God puts his Glory on display

The orb appears afar on the horizon

With the promise of another splendid day


Windows light on neighbours and their sleeping

Some geese to the southward wend their way

An omen that winter is approaching

And we must soon depart Glenway


The wonder of the morn now spreads before me

The sun, the sky, the land all on display

I thank my god for many, many favours

Including days o’erlooking Orwell Bay
