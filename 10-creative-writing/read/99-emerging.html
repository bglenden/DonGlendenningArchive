Don outlines his vision of emerging changes in PEI education, including individual learner profiles, outcomes-based report cards, a Board of Education with both appointed and elected members to oversee all educational services, family education advisers, a Chief Education Officer role, program advisory committees including employers and community, mini-courses with short-term credentials, education clinics for literacy/numeracy support, expanded library services, and area directors of educational services. This document captures his comprehensive proposal for structural educational reform emphasizing professional autonomy, community engagement, and lifelong learning opportunities beyond traditional schooling.

Pedagogical autonomy and accountability:

a recipe for improving academic results - Bedard


COMING TO A SCHOOL NEAR YOU

Changes to watch for:

  1. Individual learner profiles for secondary and post secondary students.

  2. Outcomes rather than subjects appearing on report cards.

  3. A PEI Board of Education – to provide oversight to educational services available to Islanders not just young people in schools. It will likely include a combination of appointed and elected members

  4. Family education advisers – to help learners find the education they want and need; such a change may a re-examination of the staffing pattern for schools

  5. Chief education officer – in my early years, New Brunswick had both, a Deputy Minister and Chief Superintendent. I expect a similar development on PEI, perhaps in role similar to the chief medical officer

  6. Program advisory committees for each subject will meet annually and include teachers, employers parents and others.

  7. Mini courses and/or credentials specific courses of short (1-3 weeks) duration for older students and community members.



  1. Education clinic/centre for such services as numeracy, literacy; self selection or referral by advisors

  2. Access to and help with educational products either through expanded role of libraries instructor help. Education products may converge in a form similar to pharmacies with some help available on site.

  3. Teachers sign off on student achievement levels.

  4. Area Dirctors of educational services

  5. Information, promotion and counselling
