(185 words):** This comprehensive document identifies nine interconnected structural issues in education systems, with emphasis on PEI. Don argues that education—complex and individualized—requires clear definitions and systemic alignment. Issues identified: (1) Clarity—lack of definition and shared understanding, perpetuating "educationese" jargon; (2) Purpose—PEI's stated purposes lack necessary detail for program development and assessment; (3) Education versus schooling—fundamental definitional confusion confuses roles and increases costs; (4) Structure—failing to organize around identified functions, particularly administrative and instructional services; (5) Autonomy and accountability—insufficient educator autonomy limiting responsiveness to diverse learner needs, despite OECD research showing autonomy improves outcomes; (6) Personalization—calendar-driven delivery ignores individual learner needs and interests; (7) Government relationship—political operation of education systems introduces inefficiency and instability; (8) Quality assurance—tension between teaching and grading roles; (9) Human and social capital—isolation of teachers prevents collaborative culture. Don advocates for systemic conversation and structural reorganization (dated June 5, 2023).

ISSUES IN EDUCATION -3

About 4-11

March 3, 2022

It is widely recognized that education is a key factor in determining each citizen’s quality of life; in the same way, the collective education of our citizens will determine to a significant degree the quality of the communities in which we live. Government support is justified for two reasons, one to suppot personal growth and development for every citizen; the other is to ensure the common good.


Educating is a complex matter made so by the fact that each person is unique in many ways, unique in basic physical, mental, emotional make-up; unique in the range and nature of previous experience, unique in perceived futures, but also unique in terms of learning pace, style and needs.


An education system is a set of services put in place by a community to encourage and support the education of its citizen; typical functions or services include leadership and governance, Information and counselling, funding, products and technologies, instruction, human re-sources and quality assurance. Given the numbers served and the wide range of prior experience, capabilities and interests of learners, designing and operating an education system is no easy task.


These complexities have given rise to the following issues:

Conceptual clarity heightens our understanding of key words and concepts; lack of clarity is a significant issue for public education. It must be addressed directly so that the concept of education won’t become di-luted and potentially ineffective. Lack of clarity may arise form an idea itself, from the person promoting the idea or from the person receiving the idea; Lack of clarity could also signal conflicting ideas and levels of discomfort or disagreement from within the education family group itself and some say “clarity” is also related to self confidence. Conceptual clarify is also a must in planning and assessing performance. Education has a reputation for generat-ing “educationese”; in fact, one can purchase an education “jargon generator”.

Mission/Vision

Government Departments generally describe their role as one of “encouraging and supporting their sectors”. The Department of Agriculture and Fisheries, for example, describes its role as that of “advancing Prince Edward Island primary industries and its approach to food development, production and sales”. Our Department of Education, on the other hand, seems to have adopted or been assigned a more limited role, that of “developing and delivering high quality programs and curriculum in English and French to Island children from birth to Grade 12” – a narrow mandate for such a major field of service


Province needs public statement of over-arching beliefs and guidelines that set a framework for planning, developing and implementing educational services. Given the number of Government Departments, institutions and agencies delivering educational services including private schools, such a document would inform the public, set the tone, increase consistency and reduce red tape throughout the system.

Education is a service industry, it delivers help to individuals who want to change their behaviour. Provincial governments pay for some services because they know that higher levels of education are good for the community as a whole. Because of this, front line service proviiders need to be well trained and have a large measure of freedom to respond to individual learner needs.
