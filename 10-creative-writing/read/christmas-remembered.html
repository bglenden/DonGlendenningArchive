Nostalgic poem dated December 1973 reflecting on childhood Christmas memories—Santa's arrival, the red tractor gift, waiting for Santa, and the transition to adulthood when Don realized he would be filling stockings for others. Captures the bittersweet passage from childhood wonder to adult responsibility, revealing personal vulnerability and affection for family traditions.

Christmas Remembered

Christmas is over

Festivities ceased

Eating as normal

Then ond of the feast


Turmoil and gladness

Reel through the mind

Yet we remember

Past years, other times


Early to bed

Listen for Nick

And if we hear him

We’ll run downstairs quick

How I remember

The little red tractor


Notes from St. Nicholas

Written in haste

Try to decipher

Read them we must

Eyes cannot follow

Is there a Santa?

Dare one say no?

We’d better not gamble

Our stockings we show


Another Christmas season

Friends gather round

Candy and apples

No longer a Santa

How can that be
