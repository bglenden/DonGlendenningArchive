Philosophical and conceptual analysis contrasting education with schooling, drawing on World Bank perspectives. Argues that education is an umbrella concept encompassing learning processes that occur anywhere, anytime, through multiple mechanisms, while schools are specialized institutions for formal learning. Notes that schools' focus on traditional subjects and cultural studies can cause education itself to suffer. Outlines diverse support mechanisms for learning beyond traditional classroom instruction.
