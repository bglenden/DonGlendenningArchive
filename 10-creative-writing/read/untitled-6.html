Untitled


Beside the fire I sit

And pen this note

While the household sleeps

Ot, at least, rests


The evening shortened

By the stroke of

Some lawmakers pen

Provides a lengthened 

Period for

Relaxation and reflection


Life also

Is shortened

By powers beyond us

‘tis strange 

To reflect upon

Who knows what regulations

Are  being struck for me


Events

In kaleidoscopic fashion

Impinge and infringe

Upon us

Unclear and uncertain

In their machinations

They push

Pull

Lift and Scatter

The familiar things about us


Youur words 

Give rise to thoughts

About life

That lie buried

‘Neath the pressures

Caused by telephone

  Messages

Meetings

Memoranda

Letters

Appointments

Reports and

Travel

Even this hour

At the close of a perfect

Autumn day

Is not enough 

To clear the mind

Of yesterday

Let alone to focus on tomorrow


My words

Do not flow as yours

Bouncing

Glancing

Dancing

From here to there and back

Leaving me in wonderment

About your thoughts

Your experiences 

And You



My background 

So traditional

From farm

To school

Repeated

Ad innfinitum

(Or so it seems)

Untill my present occupation


Nearly two decades have passed

Since this chapter begn

A page unmarked

Upon which I

And many others have boldly marked

Painted 

Drawn

Scratched and 

Eg\ngraved


The chapter’s almost writ

Soon

A new page will appear

Who knows

(Except the final author)

The marks and design

That will emerge thereon


I read once

Some wise advice

About tomorrow

Which said that one must (should?) 

Plan ech day 

As though one lives forever

But live each day

As though one dies tomorrow


Good advice, I guess,

But like most advice

Honoured in the breech


The fire flickers

Time passes

My last piece of paper

No doubt

A sign to draw

This disjointed note to an end


The process we’ve engaged in

Stirs me to consider

Writing – a thought long buried.  In fact,

I searched my bookcase recently

And found some words

Penned on various occasions

Perhaps, soon

I will have the 

Courage to read them


I am aware 

That the mask

Has slipped a little


Oct 26, 1986
