An untitled poem by Don about childhood evening routines—playtime chaos, mealtime chatter, bedtime resistance, and a daughter seeking her father's comfort and presence. The piece captures the tender domestic moments of parenting with vivid sensory details and emotional connection.

Untitled



Evening excitement

Friends in to play

Dolls and doll clothing

Toys and paper

Not ut away


Supper and chatter

Noise never ending

Tears

Temper tantrums

To get her own way

Bed time at last

Reluctantly going

“I need something to eat’

“My nose needs blowing”


In bed with her playthings

And not yet asleep

You quietly sneak out

Not making a peep


“Daddy, where are you?

I just want to know

Will you snuggle with me

I just want to know


Undated
