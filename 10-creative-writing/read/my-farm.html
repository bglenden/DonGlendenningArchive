Poem from October 18, 1995, describing Don's farm property—hilly, wet, wooded, and "a treasure" despite its challenges. He uses a tractor and ploughs as "toys" for pleasure rather than livelihood. Details infrastructure improvements (bridge, swamped road) and small satisfactions that evoke youth memories of rainy roofs, brooks, and summer woods. The property provides peace and serenity, suggesting a retreat from professional life.

My Farm


I say I have a farm

But not really

It was a farm

And is now a piece of farm property


It’s hilly

Its wet

Its wooded and yet

It is a  treasure to me


A tractor and ploughs

Are really just toys

But they give me pleasure

In so many ways


A bridge has been built

A road has ben swamped

Access to all corners secured


The thins I  recall 

Are really quite small

But give me hours of pleasure


The sun and the wind

And sometimes the rain

Recall the days of my youth

The rain on the roof

A bite by the rrook

The smell of the woods in the summer


The expectations of spring

The fullness of summer

The beauty of autumn

Give way to the blanket of winter

There is no logic I know

As to why I there go

But eace and serenity follow


October 18, 1995
