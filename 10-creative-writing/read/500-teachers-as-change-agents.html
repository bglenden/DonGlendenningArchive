Teachers as Change Agents



Teachers are change agents.  They help learners gain a set of behaviours needed to participate in adult community.  Content is the easy part.


To learn is to change; to teach is to help others change.  In educational jargon, changes may be in the cognitive, affective or psychomotor domains.  More simply put, a person who has learned has new or changed attitudes, skills or information or some combination thereof.  


Learning is an individual matter.  Only the learner can learn.    The stimulus for learning may be internal or external and the actual learning may take place in a group setting or when the individual is alone.  A teacher does not teach a class but a teacher may teach individuals at the same time in a group setting.  


The role of a teacher is to stimulate learning – to create a set of circumstances so that a learner learns the desired behaviour. To help a learner to learn is to help bring about changes in the learner.  Teachers need a variety of techniques in their tool kit – and they will likely require all of them.  Tools include setting an example, providing information, asking questions, setting time lines, assigning projects or home work, organizing field trips and assessing performance.  The trick for each teacher is to create a learning environment for each learner that will result in each learner having a favorable experience, even in a group setting.  Not easy but it is the crux of good teaching.


Teachers use subjects as a medium for teaching; most any subject serves as a medium through which to teach communication skills; perhaps the school environment is the best medium for teaching citizenship.  Most subjects can be used as a medium for teaching a number of skills.  Teachers use a variety of methods to both motivate and teach students assigned to their care such as having students take tests and exams, prepares course papers, participates in discussions and group activities, field trips.


One cannot measure English, Literature or chemistry one can, however, measure one ability to communicate in a language, ones ability to solve problems, discuss great authors, summarize events, ability to analyze a situation and draw conclusions, etc.  This is what people do in real life.
