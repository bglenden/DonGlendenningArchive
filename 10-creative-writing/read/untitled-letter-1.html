A personal letter in verse form dated December 13, 1987, apologizing for not responding sooner to a friend's letter while noting that the friend's words are being published through a prestigious university (Cambridge, implied by "Located on the Cam"). Don expresses both personal reflection on his changing life circumstances and a broader philosophical pondering about identity and material trappings. The tone is warm, introspective, and shows Don in his post-Holland College consulting phase.

Untitled



Upon my desk, they sat

Opened, read, and contents noted

But still unanswered

For ere so long

My conscience is much troubled


So with the spirit of the season

I sit this morn

To pen to you 

A few rambling thoughts


First let me assure you that

My breach of etiquette

Has naught to do with you

Nor anything you penned

But simply my unorganized

And disorganized

Or unfocused

(Or less focused)

Style of living


Enough said’ Let us move on

I note on re-reading

That the source of learning

Or at least its instrument

Located on the Cam

Will expose your words to wider readership

My congratulation 

And good wishes


I am sure you will have noted

My change in stationery

And how I still require

Some Trappings to provide

Some comfort and security

That those without

Find within

Perhaps soon

I will have the courage

To find out who I am 

Without such things


In many ways

I have reverted to 

A much more basic cycle

Where work and home

Are less discrete

And more gestalt

Like yesteryear

But with new technology


It works

I work

Travel

And do things I did before

But to a different end

And with less urgency



My note is short

But it will let you know, dear friend

That you still form part of my experience

And wish you well

In your existence

Whatever the season 

And the New year brings


Dec 13, 1987
