Vivid descriptive poem dated June 21, 1987, capturing observations from a PEI country road. Uses synesthetic imagery to describe birds, wildflowers (lupins, dandelions, clover), butterflies, waters, and the vast open landscape. The poem demonstrates Don's deep appreciation for PEI's natural beauty and his observational precision, conveying the overwhelming sensory experience of rural contemplation.

From a Country Road




A panorama 

Of sights and sounds 

Unfolds

As I sit quietly

On a country road

In PEI


Blackbirds chucking

Sparrows twittering

Swallows chirping

And other birds

Not recognized

Adding to the 

Quiet cacophony

On a country road

In PEI


Pink and pur;ol lupins

Danndelion whiskers

Locust tree blossoms

Alsike and red clover

Greens of all values

Brown and grey

Where people inhabit

PALE BLUE SKY

Steel grey waters

The deeper blue of Nova Scotia

With frequent ferries 

Passing to and fro


Silently a yellow butterfly

And a white moth

Flutter by

Hover above the lupins

Then disappear

Into a collage

On a country road

In PEI


Shapes irregular

Triangles

Squares

Cylinders

Lines at all angles

Spheres

No special perspective

Except for

A country road 

In PEI



June 21, 1987
