Reading proficiency scale with five progressive levels plus a zero level, assessing literacy competency from non-recognition (Level 0) through complex technical reading (Level 5). Level 5 describes reading diverse complex materials with fluency and ease; Level 3 represents functional reading of newspapers with occasional assistance; Levels 2-1 describe narrower reading capacity and basic sign/instruction interpretation. Includes pedagogically significant note that Level 3 reading competency is required for active citizenship, establishing baseline literacy expectations for civic participation. This framework acknowledges literacy as essential to democratic engagement and community participation, reflecting Glendenning's commitment to education's civic purpose.
Creative Writing


Reading levels:

A PERSON:

5


CAN READ a wide variety of complex printed materials, including technical material, with fluency, ease and without assistance

4


CAN READ a range of printed materials with independence, fluency and ease

3


CAN READ a range of printed materials including the daily newspaper but needs occasional help

2


CAN READ a narrow range of text to find information

1


CAN READ and interpret signs and follow written instructions

0


CANNOT recognize any words on a written page.

Notes: Level three is required for active citizenship.
