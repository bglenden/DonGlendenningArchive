Reflective poem from January 1980 about realizing his son Brian has become a man at age 18. Catalogs the gradual changes—height, weight, voice, completing school, enrolling in university, driving. The moment of recognition comes in the bathroom when Don sees his son's electric razor (a birthday gift) actually being used, symbolizing the transformation from boyhood. Reveals Don's paternal observation and emotional awareness.
Creative Writing

He’s a Man


At age 18

A  son’s a man

You hardly notice

The changes


The height

The weight

The changing voice

The changes in his friends


School completed

Enrolled in university

Driving the car

All evidence

He’s a man


Today

It registered

In the bathroom

The electric razor

A gift for his birthday

Wass no longer just sitting there

It was plugged in


Its true

He’s a man


January 1980
