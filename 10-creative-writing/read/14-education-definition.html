This document compiles multiple authoritative definitions of education from diverse sources: general (any experience with formative effect), Good's definitions emphasizing positive social development, OECD (organic and sustained communication), and UNESCO (practices and outcomes). Don uses these definitions to establish a comprehensive conceptual foundation, positioning education as both personal development and social process. Defines "learner" as a person in the process of changing behavior.



Education

  • any experience that has a formative effect on the way a person thinks feels or acts.

  • Good - The aggregate of all the processes by means of which a person develops abilities, attitudes and other forms of behaviour of positive value in the society in which he lives

  • Good – the social process by which people are subjected to the influence of a selected and controlled environment sot that they may attain social competence and optimum individual development

  • OECD – organic and sustained communication to bring about learning

  • UNESCO – a set of practices and outcomes that are defined qualitatively


Learner – a person who is in the process of changing behaviour
