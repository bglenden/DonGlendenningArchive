Philosophical meditation on different types of "finality"—temporary (closing a door before a trip), bittersweet (saying goodbye to a friend), prolonged (family farewells), and permanent (death itself). Written in 2002, the poem contemplates how various endings punctuate human experience, with the final stanza addressing "the finality of finalities" (death). Reflects Don's sophisticated engagement with mortality and loss.

Finalities


There is a  finality

In the click of a door lock

As we follow our suitcases

To the waiting taxi


There is a finality

With the diminishing silhouette

Of the taxi

As we enter the airport


There is a finality

With the receding ground

As the aircraft lifts

From PEI’s red soil


……………………………………


There is a momentary finality

In the click of a doorlock

As we leave on a trip


There is a sweet finality

In the adieu of a friend

Departing a rendevous


There is a lingering finality 

In the good by 

Of departing fmily


There is a permanent  finality

With the departure of another

In the finality of finalities


April 10, 2002
