Early draft of a poem dated December 1973 about a camping experience far from traffic and civilization. Describes frost, stars, brookside sounds, and campfire dwindling into moonlight. Uses nature imagery to evoke memory creation and the fleeting quality of moments. This is a draft version with slight variations from the final version.
Creative Writing

A Midnight Sojourn



Far from the traffic

Stillness overwhelms one

Frost in the hollow

Why am I here

Stars twinkle

Brooks trickle

Gentle sounds all

As the campfire dwindles


Flickering shadows

Fade in the moonlight

Memories created

Long may they reigh


December 1973
