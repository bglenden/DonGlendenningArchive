Open Education No. 63 (April 18, 2018), authored by Don Glendenning questioning whether current school calendars can coexist with learning excellence. He notes individual learner pace and style differences conflict with group-based instruction organized in time blocks, complicating diploma awards and graduation ceremonies. Don proposes five alternatives: schools focus on learning, not grading; independent bodies award grades; learning time adjusts to individual pace; graduation requirements change; Canadian Qualification Authority established; graduation becomes "recognition." The article references an education award program and discusses information as an education service separate from instruction. Don's personal reflection on an eye doctor's "Cataract Information for Patients" pamphlet raises whether comparable patient/learner information services exist, and a concluding poem lament reflects on teaching effectiveness when students face social challenges.

RETHINKING GRADUATION


I’ve been pondering the dilemma of school graduation; can the current school calendar and “learning excellence” co-exist? It’s not a new issue; I was alerted to the dilemma on arrival on PEI fifty years ago, and it was part of a discussion about education within the past week 


Each of us has our own learning pace and style; groups don’t learn although individuals may learn in a group setting.  On the other hand, most of our instruction, which is the work of schools, is organized for groups in blocks of time. It complicates the awarding of diplomas and the staging of graduations.  


Some options:

  • Schools to get out of the grading business altogether and simply focus on helping others learn.
  • Establish an independent body to award grades; after all, grading is a regulatory not a teaching function
  • Set desired standards and adjust learning time to reflect individual learning styles. 
  • Change graduation requirements; (see Occasional Paper Number 2 at www.education2020.ca
  • Set up a Canadian Qualification Authority; most western countries, except Canada and the USA, have such a body.
  • Change graduation to “Recognition”

INFORMATION

I visited my eye doctor yesterday; as I was leaving his office, he handed me a pamphlet on “Cataract Information for Patients” published by the Department of Health.  It was both reassuring and informative.  Later, as I read through it, I wondered if that kind of service is available to learners.


The focus of the Department of Education is to deliver programs; the focus of the Department of Family Services is to help Islanders become more self reliant – an Interesting difference, to say the least.


POEM

I’m haunted by a poem of yesteryear; it’s about a teacher who just heard that a former student had been convicted of murder.  The teacher remembered him well - where he sat, his quiet manner, his long periods of gazing into space and out the school window.  The poem ended by the teacher saying something like “and I taught him the rhyming words of poetry and how to parse a sentence.  Help
