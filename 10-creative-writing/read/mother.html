Elegiac poem titled "Mother's Day" (dated 1964) honoring Don's mother, reflecting her warmth, encouragement, and unconditional support. The poem traces her qualities—welcoming smile, cherish words, comfort on her knee, and her faith in family members regardless of their path. The shift to past tense and mention of passing a store "I would purchase never more / For Mother" indicates this was written after her death, expressing continued grief and longing.

Mother’s Day


A special day is drawing near

A day when all from far and near

Look forward to a meeting dear

With Mother


A  welcome smile

A cheery word

Were treasures that were oft conferred

By mother


And young folk have a special place

On Mother’s knee, and their embrace

Brought extra gladness to the face

Of Mother


A conversation always led

Regardless of the path they tread

To praise for those the race wjho led

By Mother


And as for those who had not led

Who stumbled on the path they tread

The kindest words of them were said

By mother


But yesterday, I passe3d a store

With greeting cards displayed galore

Which I would purchase never more

For Mother


And as my eyes were dimmed with tears

And stabbing pain m,y heart still sears

I thought about those former years

With Mother


Ssome day when life its course has run

And the Master says to us “Well done”

We’ll meet again with our loved on

With Mother
