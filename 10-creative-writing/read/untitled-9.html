A reflective poem from December 1973 exploring themes of time, uncertainty, and human struggle. The verses touch on problems solved in the night, concern for the less fortunate, the relentless passage of minutes and hours, and the preciousness of time as a limited resource. The philosophical tone suggests contemplation during a significant period in Don's life, coinciding with his presidency at Holland College.

Untitled



Hyper

Perhaps

Morning approaches

Problems are solved

In the still of the night


Othes

Less fortunate

Drowning their sorrows

Unable to cope

With a rising tomorrow


Minuutes and hours

TRelentlessly passing

Decisions required

What will they be?


No use delaying

Sand has all fallen

Answers are given

Time will tell


Tim is important

Limited quantity

When will we learn

To treasure its worth


December 1973
