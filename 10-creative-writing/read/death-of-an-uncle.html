Poem dated December 1973 capturing the shock of a late-night phone call announcing an uncle's death. Traces the emotional journey from confusion to grief, with memories of family gatherings and laughter interrupted by mortality. Reflects on human finitude and divine will, displaying Don's contemplative approach to loss and family bonds.

Death of an Uncle


The hour is late

The telephone rings

Your hear jumps a beat

Is it bad news


You jump from the bed

Incessant, the ringing

Turn on the light

Children awake


Who can it  be?

At this time of night

Sickness or worse

You reach for it

Wondering


A voice so familiar

Is some consultation

But you can soon sense

There is something more


The family all well?

A qualified answer

Break the news gently

An uncle is gone


Conversation continues

Some sketchy details

Funeral on Tuesday

Will you be there?


Thoughts tumble backwards

To earlier days

And visits and laughter

And joyous occasions


You turn from the phone

Walk slowly apace

While sorrow and sadness

Mustt show on your face


We are so mortal

So finite

So minute

God in his wisdom

Plans for us all



December 1973
