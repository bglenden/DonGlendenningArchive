Celebration of "Glenway," Don's property overlooking Orwell Bay in PEI, written October 18, 1996. Describes the property as an oasis of natural beauty that attracts neighbors and friends seasonally, creating enduring memories and bonds. For youth, it was "a place of freedom, friends and more." The poem reveals Don's deep attachment to this place as a family gathering space and reflects his ability to create meaningful shared experiences.

Glenway



It may sound high falutin’

Or that’s what some folks ssay

But its beauty is for certain

Overlooking Orwell Bay


I’ve seen it in the winter

I’ve stayed there in the fall

Spring brings hope eternal

But summer’s best of all


For summer brings us nneighbours

And froemds from near and far

Who share this bit of heaven

Oof land and sea and star  /star


It has gentle, natural beauty

As beautiful as  it can get

My memories are etched forever

With the  beauty of our sunsets


The immigrants to glenway

Share its beauty and their lives

For such a short short  season

But with stronn, compelling ties


For youth it was s pecial place

Of  freedom, friends and more

Memories they carry always

Of a place on Orwell’s shore


And as we sit at eventide

Absorbing sound and sight

The beauty of the sunset

Gives way to glorius night


In a world so filled with turmoil

How fortunate are we

For the experience of glenway

Our oasis by the sea



October 18, 1996
