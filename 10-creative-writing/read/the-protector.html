A short poem from Vancouver (October 1981) about an umbrella, using it as a metaphor for protection and irony (when the umbrella is up, people are down indoors). Don displays his ability to find meaning and humor in everyday objects through wordplay and observation about the relationship between weather and human activity.

The Protector



Finished

For the day

It stands in a corner


Neither open 

Nor furled

Point set firmly

It leans against the wall


Its usefuleness is attested to

By the puddle of water

Around it on the floor


Will duty call today?

Tomorrow?

Tis too soon to tell

Staying or going 

Will depend

On the weather


When its up

People are down

When its down

People are up


That’s one of the strange things

About an umbrella


Vancouver, October 22, 1981
