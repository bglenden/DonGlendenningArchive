A nostalgic poem from December 1973 reflecting on an old clock that once stood in a country kitchen during Don's boyhood. The poem traces the clock's journey through different homes and its witness to joyful and sad occasions, using it as a metaphor for the passage of time and memory. This piece reveals Don's sentimental connection to childhood and objects of domestic life.

The Clock


It stands

Unused to adulation

And in its tranquility

Ticks away the time


A memory of other times

Of boyhood recollection



Its advent 

On a winter night

Accompanied by excitement


For years i

It marked the time

In a country kitchen


It held a high position

Surrounded by matches

A key or two

And two stalwart oil lamps


It marked occasions

Both joyous and sad

Time passed quickly 

People assed on

It found a new home


It stands again

Proud of its history

Unused to its settoing

And in its tranquility

Memories abound


December 1973
