Satirical poem written in Ottawa in 1966 capturing the chaos of television viewing—rapid scene changes, emotional whiplash from dramas to news to commercials. References current events (riots in Africa, Hess), describes typical evening escapism, and concludes with fatigue before the workday begins. Reflects Don's observational wit and critique of media consumption.

An Evening of Television


The day was a rough one

The evening was free

Unload worry and CareRelax in your chair

And watch some TV


The room was in shambles

The fight was terrific

Slamming and banging

And also haranguing

The scene was horrific


Love in the making

You cannot mistake it

Billing and Cooing

Internally stewing

Oh how can we take it


Again the scene changes

Producing domestics

Father, mother

Sister, Brother

A little less hectic


An oft changing pattern

Keeps unfolding before us

Cities and plains

Subways and trains

Sometimes they bore us


Late full-lenth movies

Sales and promotions

Detergernnts and cars

And young movie stars

Upset our emotions


Riots in Africa

A flashback on Hess

Living and dying

Laughing and Crying

The world’s in a mess





A drink and a snack

And you hurry right back

A channel to change

From city to range

With a tumble-down shack


You yawn and you doze

The hour is late

An evening misspent

Emotions to vent

Work starts at sharp eight


Ottawa, 1966
