Poignant poem about the death of Don's sister Elinor (one of his 8 siblings). The narrative begins with a missed phone call where Elinor had called about a hall fire, followed weeks later by news of her illness and death from the flu. Don reflects on moving Elinor to a location twenty years earlier and witnessing the hearse carry her away from that same spot. The poem conveys deep sibling affection and demonstrates Don's emotional sensitivity to family losses.

February Memories

My mind wanders back

Tonight as I sit

In my own living room

And I think back a bit


To one year ago

In the heart of the winter

Death entered the circle

And left with my sister


When I came home one night

I learned in our hall

I was a  few minutes too llalte

For her telephone call


She called to find out

About family and all

And to tell us the news

Of a bad fire at the hall


I did not phone back

‘twas too late at night

In another few days

I would sit down and write


Another week passed

Or perhaps it was two

Again the phone rang

Elinor, down with the flu


It was much worse than that

As I soon was to hear

Within a few days

Her life was no more


Her family, her friends

They passed by her bier

Recalling good times

With a person so dear


Soon it was over

We ech went our way

With our private memories

Of that sad, sad day


I stand in the yyard

See the hearse drive away

The loss of that sister

Hit home on that day


In fact I had moved her

With all that they’d got

Some twenty years earlier

To that very spot


On leaving for home

Dhe stood at the door

Or out in the yard

And waved as we left


But today she was leaving

And I was still there

I stared unbelieving

In the crisp morning air


Oft in the evening

My mind wanders back

To the many occasions

She gave me a snack


And we talked

And we laughed

As we talked of old times

Of family and friends

Of earlier times


But now  they are memories

To treasure and hold

They shall remain with me

To the days  when Im old
