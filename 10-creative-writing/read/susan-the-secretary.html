A children's story written by Don in which his young daughter Janet visits her father's office (likely as President of Holland College) and learns about office work by observing secretary Susan. The narrative shows Don's professional role and family life, using the encounter to teach his daughter about secretarial duties and office procedures. The story reflects his ability to use his leadership position as a teaching opportunity for his child.

SUSAN THE SECRETARY 


Janet looked out the window "Here's Daddy", she said 


As a car turned into the driveway. 


She ran to him 


When he came into the house. 


Daddy said, 


"Sorry I'm late tonight, 


But my secretary was sick today." 


"What's a secretary?" Janet asked. 


II A secretary", Daddy said 


"Is a person

"Well", said Daddy, 


"You come to my office tomorrow And you will see

Next morning, 


Janet was up early.
