Email from Barry R. MacKenzie (Sept 12-15, 2006) to Don's genealogy group providing newspaper death records for William Watling (died Oct 11, 1895, age 75, Miramichi resident 65 years, arrived 1830) and Mary McNaughton Watling (died Nov 6, 1871, age 47, of consumption). Includes census data and discussion of John Watling's marriage to Jessie Carruthers (1849), their children, and their movements between New Brunswick and Massachusetts. Demonstrates Don's role organizing collaborative genealogical research.

Hello all,
 
Some answers (at least I think they are) to Don's good questions.
 
Firstly, as per the deaths of William and Mary:

 

Date : October 11, 1895
County : Saint John
Place : Saint John
Newspaper : The Daily Sun
Chatham (North. Co.) Oct. 10 - For 65 years William WATLING was a resident of Miramichi. He came to this country from England with his parents in 1830 and was 75 years of age at the time of his death. He was laid away in the Black River burial ground. Four years ago he removed from Black River to Black Brook where he resided up to the time of his death with W.S. BABKIRK.

 

Date : November 6, 1871
County : York
Place : Fredericton
Newspaper : Colonial Farmer
d. Black River (North. Co.) 9th ult., of consumption, Mary McNaughton WATTLING w/o William WATTLING, age 47, left husband, nine children.

 

SO, it appears that the couple is buried in BRB.

 

Secondly, as per John Watling.




        Jubal and Susan did have a son named John. He was baptized in 1823 in England. My understanding in the past was that he and another sister, Susannah, must not have come to Canada.

        Your John Watling married Jessie Carruthers in Napan in 1849. From Gleaner.

        In the 1851 Census, John is listed with his wife Jessie in the Parish of Chatham, with a 1 year old Janet N Watling, listed as daughter. The census says he came in 1845. Our Watlings came in June 1830 (census).

        In 1861, they have a Jennet (11), Robert (8), Susan J. (5), and Mary A. (2).

        In 1865-66 and 1867-68 Directories, John is listed as living on McCallum St in Newcastle, a mason by trade.

        Neither John nor Jessie pops up in the 1871 Census. However, Robert , Jennet, and William (where did Mary go?) appear in Newcastle. Don’t know who they were living with, as I don't have the full census, just a list of Watlings in it (from Carman Williston).

        In December 1871, Robert died at Newton, Mass, aged 18.

        On 10 Nov 1875, Jennet married Frank E. Crafts of Reading, Mass, at Dover, Mass. From Union Advocate.

        In 1881, I can't find any of them. Same for 1891 and 1901.

 

This is what I have discovered. It doesn't seem to me like these folks are connected to our Jubal and William or their other three siblings. But here's hoping that a lively debate will ensue!

 

Wishing you all a good night and happy searching though your own files!

 

Barry,
