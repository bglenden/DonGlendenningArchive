Hi Don,

A bit more about William Glendinning son of John Glendinning and Mary Rae.

The entry in your father's family bible ties in very nicely with other information I have, to confirm it is the same person.

In 1855 a daughter Jane Glendinning was born on 9 Sep 1855 to William Glendinning and Grace Marshall.  The place of birth was given as Little Skellyholm, St Mungo Parish, Dumfriesshire.  The birth record noted that this couple had one previous child, a son who was still living.

In the 1851 census for St Mungo is the family of William Glendinning aged 32 a farm labourer born Middlebie, his wife Grace aged 36 born Hoddam, and a son John aged 11 a farm labourer born St Mungo.  So that would make John born about 1839 or 40.  In fact the OPR has a baptism for this John on 23 Feb 1841 at Middlebie.  The two facts could be consistent as he might have been born in St Mungo the previous year and baptised when he was a few months old, back in his father's old church.

The bible entry for William pointed me towards the Statutory Index of deaths and this confirms William died in St Mungo in 1874 aged 56.  I do not have the full record of this.

But I do have the full record of his daughter Jane as she died in Langholm and this town is of particular relevance for my family so I have most of the Glendinning records for that town.  You can see her record at http://www.glendinning.name/ancestry/records/dcert839.htm#1876_41  You will see she was unmarried and both her parents were deceased.  Her father William was listed as a Grocer.

I think that is all I have of this family.  Hope it is of interest.

Ian

hello all from Halifax  Nov 19, 2005



I am being lazy this Saturday evening and while there is work to do I don’t 

feel like it. So I’ll write instead.


It was a sunny day here in the east today but it started off quite cool.  

The fountain in the pond froze and I had to take the nozzle off and melt the 

ice – so winter is coming.


Christy and I did not do anything really exciting all day.  She worked on 

school stuff and I went shopping to collect things I needed for chores.  A 

quick replacement of the motion sensor light outside the back door led to 

the discovery that there was no electrical box behind it,  a wire came 

through the wall and the fixture was screwed directly to the siding. So I 

planned my expedition – was very efficient shopping. My chain saw now has a 

new blade. The light is back up with a proper electrical box.  The leaking 

sink in the bathroom is not only fixed but has a new set of taps too. The 

propane tank is filled. The oil and filters to change the oil in both cars 

has been bought but not installed. Neither are the snow tires. Oh well, 

Tomorrow is another day.


While I was in the grocery store I met someone from Black River.  Janet 

MacLean (nee Jenkins) has a daughter living in Bedford who has just gone 

back to work – so grandma came to baby sit and has been here for a couple 

months. Normally she lives across the road from my Aunt Mildred’s in Black 

River in the house Jim Adams built after the war.  Janet is retired from 

teaching. Her husband, Bob MacLean, was killed when his tanker left the road 

it must be over 20 years ago.  Bob was the son of Myron MacLean of Black 

River.


I was talking to mother in Black River tonight. She and Mabel (Cameron) 

Gardiner were over to the Black River Hall this afternoon where friends and 

relatives were gathering to celebrate Fraser MacRae’s 80th birthday. She saw 

a lot of people including Fraser’s sisters, Helen Patterson and Mary 

Jamison. Helen was telling stories about Fraser as a boy.  She mentioned 

Elbie and Shirely Donnelly, Cameron and Nan MacDonald, Norman and Jean 

Glendenning, Jimmie MacKenzie’s daughter, Pat, who has bought the Alex Major 

house just across the road from mothers.


I am going up to Black River on Tuesday.  Mother has an eye appointment to 

get fitted with glasses now that 6 weeks has passed since her cataract 

surgery.  She has been functioning without glasses since the surgery except 

for dollar store magnifying glasses for reading.


This week has kept this family busy.. I was in Ottawa Tuesday and Wednesday 

for Law of the Sea meetings and Marion went to Hamilton on Wednesday, on the 

first leg of her fall tour. She goes on to Calgary on Tuesday and then to 

Vancouver on Wednesday and then Ottawa on December 1 for the Gloucester Band 

concert on Dec. 4 and gets home Dec 5.  Christy has confirmed that she is 

returning to Acadia after Christmas so was sorting out accommodation and 

courses.  Throw in an ISO9001 Maintenance audit and marking 22 knowledge 

exams from a competition and it was a full week.


Well, I must sign off - Marion tells me it was snowing in Hamilton all day 

yesterday so I hope it comes this way slowly – very slowly, so I can get a 

few outside things done tomorrow.


Dick
