A heartfelt Christmas letter from Don and Carol reflecting on their year at age 86, including travel to Cuba and Victoria BC, summer cottage life, family visits including grandchildren, and continued good health and community involvement. The letter reveals Don's appreciation for family, travel, gardening, and maintaining deep connections across a lifetime.

Christmas Greetings

from the Glendennings

Christmas 2015



Cuba 2015


Dear


Time to reminisce and write a Christmas note to family and friends.


We’ve had another good year. We are still together, able to live in our own home, have reasonably good health and drive ourselves to most places – except after dark. Yes, we have our share of aches and pains, newspaper print is smaller than it once was, messages travel a bit slower to our brains and limbs, stairs have gotten steeper and most nights appear foggy. But we are here and functioning so what more could we ask?


I’ve always enjoyed puttering around and doing the chores but last year I decided to hire someone to shovel our walk; it was a good decision in that Charlottetown set a record – 16 feet of snow. And I’ve always cut my own Christmas tree but his year decided to purchase one. It is a nice little tree but not nearly as nice as the ones I brought home in past years.





Thanks to Janet, we spent a week in Havana last February. Janet was a great guide and


made sure that we got around. Although Havana is run down, one can still imagine what it must have been like in its hey day


In late April we spent a couple of weeks in Victoria, BC; weather was great, we were just one block back from the harbour, and we had great accommodation at a reasonable rate. Although we had rented a car, we could walk to the museum art gallery and a number of good restaurants.


While in Victoria, our grand daughter and her husband, Lisa and Kevin came one weekend for a visit: While they were there, we got around the city a bit and had a visit to Bouchart Gardens.


It was also great to visit with Laine Warden, who we hadn’t seen for 40 years. Laine and Bill were friends from our Ottawa days. Their oldest child, Tommy and our son Brian are the same age. Unfortunately Bill died a couple of years ago.


We spent our summer at the cottageoverlooking Orwell Bay. It is a peaceful spot and unites us with a group of summer friends. Don has a small vegetable garden –






potatoes, peas, beans, carrots, beets, tomatoes and a few squash; he says that he had his poorest garden ever but we are still enjoying our home-grown squash,


Carol manages our flower beds in the summer and is active in both bridge snd book club during the resy of the year.


The highlight of our summer was time at the cottage and visits from family. Janet actually made three visits this summer including her school reunion, “cousins” gathering and a regular visit. Brian was here in August but Marie and our granddaughters didn’t make it this year.


To friends and family everywhere, we send greetings, warm wishes for good health and peace in our world.. You are much in our thoughts and prayers as we write this note.






Merry Christmas


and


Happy New Year
