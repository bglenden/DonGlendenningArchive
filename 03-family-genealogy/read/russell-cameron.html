Moncton Times clipping reporting death of D. Cameron Russell, age 72, of Point au Carr, Northumberland County, died on arrival at Hotel Dieu Hospital Chatham. Born Point au Carr, son of Ernest and Burgess Cameron Russell; worked as farmer, fisherman, carpenter. Member St. Paul's Presbyterian Church Black River (elder, active in church activities). Miramichi Highland Society member. Wife was former Venetia G. (Don's sister). Survived by wife, one daughter (Donna), two sons (Leonard PEI, Glenn Newcastle), two sisters, one brother, and four grandchildren. Important family connection.

A clipping, believed to come from the Moncton times reports:

"D.C. Russell POINT AU CARR (Special) - The death of D. Cameron Russell of Point au Carr, Northumberland County, occurred on arrival Wednesday at Hotel Dieu Hospital, Chatham.  He was 72.

Born at Point au Carr, he was a son of the late Ernest and Burgess (Cameron) Russell, and was a farmer, fisherman and carpenter.

He was a member of St. Paul's Presbyterian church, Black river, and an elder of the church.  He took an active part in all church activities throughout his life.

He was a member of the Miramichi Highland Society.

He is survived by his wife, the former Venetia G; a daughter, Mrs. Vernon Goodfellow, (Donna) of South Esk; two sons, Leonard of St. Eleanors, PEI., and Glenn Russell of Newcastle; two sisters, Mrs. Gordon MacDiarmid (Betty) of Centre Napan and Mrs. Hugh Berry (Aldine) of Averill Park, N.Y.; ,a brother, Howard Russell of Point au Carr, and by four grandchildren.

The body is resting at his home at Point au Carr.

Services will be held at 3:30 p.m. Saturday at St. Paul's Presbyterian Church, Black River, with interment in the church cemetery."
