Correspondence (Nov 2011) between Don and St. John's Cemetery administrator regarding restoration of his sister Margaret Virginia's gravestone in Hampton, Virginia, buried 1918. Shows family care and connection to diaspora.

November 12, 2011 


My father and mother, Elmer and Jane Glendenning, lived in Hampton, Virginia during the construction of the Langley Field and, in fact, had a daughter, Margaret Virginia buried in St. John’s Episcopal Cemetery. A number of family members, including myself, have visited the cemetery over the years. 

My remaining siblings are wondering if the stone still exists, what kind of shape it is in and if it could benefit from restoration.

Is it possible to arrange for someone to look at it and provide an estimate of the cost of cleaning and/or restoration.

Thanks is advance for any assistance that you can provide.

Sincerely

Don Glendenning



November 14, 2011

Dear Mr. Glendenning, My name is David Bishop and I am the administrator of
St. John's Cemetery. I will look into this matter for you and report back
to you within a week or so. (I doubt if I'll be over at the cemetery sooner
than this coming Sunday.) / David Bishop


November 20, 2011
