HARLEY NOTES

January 1, 2008


I had a number of queries recently about my Harley connections and information about the Harley Family.  I used the queries as an excuse to review my notes and files.  Here are a few notes that do not seem to fit into the actual family tree but may be of interest:


My connection to the Harleys is through my Great Grandmother, Margaret Harley who married John Glendinning

While all evidence points to my Great Grandmother being a daughter of William and Ann (Bidders) Harley, I do not have, nor have I seen actual proof of, the relationship.  

Margaret’s siblings are William, John, Elizabeth and Mary Anne

The Spring 2004 Issue of Generations, Journal of NB Genealogical Society, carried an item from the obituary of John Harley taken from the Union Advocate of Sept 29, 1875.  I can send it to you if you would like to see it.

According to my information, our Harleys came to Canada in the following order:  William in 1820, Mary Ann in 1822 and John in 1823; I do not know the dates of arrival of Elizabeth and Margaret.

Books of interest:  Louise Manny  “Ships of the Miramichi” and Margaret Hunter “Pioneer Settlers of the Bay Chaleur” in 1978; I have not seen the first one but have a copy of the second.

Burton Glendenning, a descendent of Margaret Harley and John Glendinning, was NB Archivist and wrote a book, perhaps a thesis for a degree, on the Burchills of the Miramichi.  I have not seen the book.

I note the possibility that Hurley and Harley may be the same family; I did not encounter that but understand that they have the same roots.  Interesting to note in Heather’s list of Hurleys, that a John Hurley married Elizabeth Burchill in 1841.  Our John Harley and George Burchill were partners in shipbuilding on the Miramichi; they probably came from the same place.

As some of you know, I publish a Family Chronicle” ( HYPERLINK "http://www.glendenning.net/don" www.glendenning.net/don)  This started as a way to pass along family information to my siblings – I am the youngest of nine.  Later I began to include information about the community where I was raised, Black River Bridge, New Brunswick, about 15 miles east of what was Chatham and now is Miramichi City.  Some time ago  I decided to devote an issue of the family Chronicle to each of my siblings.  The one on myself is Issue No. 18, May 6, 2006.

Harley information may be found in the following Family Chronicles: 1, 4, 125, 134 and 136 ( HYPERLINK "http://www.glendenning.net/don" www.glendenning.net/don)


In preparing these notes, I simply reviewed the notes I have in my file.  I know that there are others who have dome much more than I  but am glad to share anything I have that others may find useful.


Don Glendenning












Unfortunately, I have mot been as meticulous as I should have been and, therefore, have not recorded my sources
