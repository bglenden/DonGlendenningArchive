B Who were our early settlers?


  1. First nations

  2. French fishermen came ashore at mouth of Miramichi in the 1500s; fur-trading settlement on Bay du Vin Island by mid – 1660’s; Gardiners Point had settlement of at least 15 families by 1750’s; French settlers at Hardwicke about the same time; most French settlers driven out by 1758; no evidence that either aboriginal or French settlers along the Black River.


  1. Scotland – some major strains; in mid-1700’ss clan system was destroyed, clans were broken up, could not wear tartans or carry arms leases were terminated and estates were converted to sheep farms to take advantage of soaring mutton prices, people left in droves for North Amertica where settlers could own their own land and it was free or cheap.




  1. American Revolution – 1776; people who had fought on side of the British and wanted to remain part of British North America; known as United Empire Loyalists; came by the boat to Nova Scotia which included what is now New Brunswick; mostly during period !785-1795; first permanent settlers; fought on side of British, after American revolution, many were well educated, had been successful leaders in their community, business men, government officials; dramatic reduction in the size of the military.


  1. Many Loyalists settled in Canada just north of the St Lawrence River. As you would expect, most landed at Halifax or St. John; most were given grants in in central Nova Scotia or Southern New Brunswick; they moved further afield, many, many moving further up the Saint John River and into the Naashwaak River, from the headwaters of the Nashwaak to the headwaters of the Miramichi was a short portage; Allie MacDougall reports that a number of families came to Black River from Nashwaak including Neil McGraw, Dougald Campbell, James Gunn. John Bailey Williston first settled in Nova Scotia in 1784, four years later he resettled in Bay du Vin;


  1. Although Loyalists came here from the USA, many had Scottish roots. Allie MacDougal mentions the 78 Highlanders and the 42 Regiment. The 78th eventual became part of the Seaforth Highlanders and the 42nd Regiment was part of the Black Watch Regiment.


  1. Even when settlers arrived in the Miramichi, they often settled in one spot and then moved around. Alexander MacDonald, it is said, first lived in Point Aux Carr. Allie’s book also talks about settlers trading lots. Many settlers Scots landed in Pictou and then moved to PEI and New Brunswick


  1. Others came to other smaller ports, some directly to the Miramichi itself – Harleys, Watlings, Allens,
