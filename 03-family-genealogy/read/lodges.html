Presents facsimile of Hadattah Lodge No. 117 (Independent Order of Odd Fellows) certificate dated October 21, 1901 in Portland, Maine admitting "Bro. Elmer A. Glendenning" to membership. Includes handwritten inscription from Masonic textbook: "I was made a Master Mason the last Saturday night in 1911 at Ocoee Lodge No. 212, Benton, Tennessee, E.A.Glendenning." Notes Masonic book presentation from James Cortey of Knoxville, Tennessee. Jane returned to NB for Walter's birth September 1913; both returned to NB 1914. Fraternal organization involvement indicates Elmer's community engagement and social standing. Evidence of multiple organizations across geographic relocations.

Lodges


While looking through papers recently, I came across a certificate from Hadattah Lodge No. 117, Independent Order of Odd Fellows which reads s follows:


Portland, Me. , Oct 21st, 1901

This Certifies that Bro. Elmer A. Glendenning

Was this evening admitted to membership in this lodge.

John Steward  N.G

Henry G. Milliken Sec’y


In a Masonic Text Book, in Dad’s handwriting, is the following inscription:


I was made a Master Mason the last Saturday night in 1911 at Ocoee Lodge No. 212, Benton, Tennessee, E.A.Glendenning


And in another Masonic Book in Dad’s handwriting:


Presented to E. A. Glendenning

By James Cortey of

Knoxville, Tennessee


Dad and Mum set up housekeeping in Parksville, Tennessee on April 11, 1911 and their first child, Grace was born there. Dad worked on the construction of Ocoee No. 1, a dam at Parksville.  Later they moved a few miles up river where a second dam, Ocoee #2,  was built.  Mum returned to NB for Walter’s birth in 1913.  Dad may or may not have accompanied her but they were both in NB in 1914.
