Herald Advocate obituary from February 24, 1967, reporting death of Guy W. Glendenning, age 77 (born Canobie, NB). Canadian WWI veteran; moved to Bowling Green, Florida in 1930. Active Mason (Past Master of Bowling Green Lodge 121, Scottish Rite Mason) and member of Wauchula Elks Lodge. Survived by widow Hazel Stultz Glendenning, son Robert, daughter Cornelia, brother Ray, and four grandchildren. Family connection document.

The Herald Advocate of February 24, 1967 carried the following:

 

Guy W. Glendenning, 77, Died February 8 after an extended illness.

 

He was born in Canobie, New Brunswick, Canada and was a Canadian Veteran of World War l.  He came to Bowling Green in 1930 from Peru where he was affiliated with the Masonic Lodge in 1921, serving as Worshipful Master a few years later.

 

He was Past Master of Bowling Green Lodge 121 and Past Patron of  Bowling Green Chapter 23, Order of the Eastern Star, a Scottish Rite Mason, and a member of the Wauchula Elks Lodge.

 

Survivors are his widow, Hazel Stultz Glendenning; a son Robert Glendenning of Chesterton, Md.; a daughter, Cornelia Farrell, Huntington Station, Long Island, N. Y.; one brother, Ray Glendenning of Canobie, New Brunswick, Canada and four grandchildren.

 

Funeral services were held February 13 at the Bowling Green Methodist Church with the Rev. E. J. Halter officiating.  Burial was in the Bowling Green Cemetery.
