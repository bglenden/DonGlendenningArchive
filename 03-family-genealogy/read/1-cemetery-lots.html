Don's 2012 letter to cemetery administrator regarding cemetery lot arrangements in Black River, discussing family burial plans and urn capacity. Personal correspondence documenting his connection to his birthplace.

62 Queen Elizabeth Drive

Charlottetown, PE

C1A 3A9


August 10. 2012


Lee Burnham

30 North Black River Road

Black River Bridge 

N.B. E1N 5S4


Dear Lee:

Black River Cemetery Lots


Many years ago, 1977 to be precise, while visiting my sister, Grace, in Boston, she expressed an interest in being buried in Black River.  Shortly thereafter, I “purchased” two 10’x 10’ lots, #143 for Grace and #142 for myself.  


My wife and I plan to be cremated with the ashes buried in urns in our lots.  It was our intention, when a stone is erected, to show the names of our two children.  Recently, however, our son and daughter have raised the idea of also having their ashes, and that of their spouses, interred in the Black River cemetery as well.  This letter comes to seek clarification as to limits, if any, on the actual number of urns that can be buried on our lot  and the size and wording of headstones  and the.  Do you require that all names be registered at this time?


As a matter of interest, what is the cost of opening and closing a lot for burial?


If you need any further information, do not hesitate to let me know.


Regards


Don Glendenning
