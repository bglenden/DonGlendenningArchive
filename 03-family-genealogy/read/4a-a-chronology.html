Mum and Dad


As reported in the last Newsletter, Mum and Dad operated the Post Office at Little Branch from 1914 to 1916.  Because of this new information, I decided to review some of their early dates. 


June 29 1875 Dad was born presumably in Canobie, NB

Feb. 28 1890 Mum was born presumably in Upper Black River above MacBeath=s Bridge.

? Dad went to Portland where he and some of his brothers ran a cartage business.  They also lumbered in New Hampshire and likely in Maine.  

Jan. 24 1908 Mum landed in Portland by train the day the City Hall burned.

Feb. 14 1908 Walter Glendenning, Dad=s father, died while visiting Portland

Jul 18   1910 Dad was in Wausau, Wis. Working on a coffer-dam.

Sept.18 1910 Dad autographed Mum=s album giving his address as Wausau, Wis. (Perhaps he brought it to her as a gift.)

Oct. 18 1910 Mum and Dad were married at the Watling home in Little Branch, NB.



Dec. 1910 Friends autographed Mum=s album in Bonny Eagle (Portland) Maine over Christmas period.

Feb 11, 1911 ACommenced housekeeping, Parksville, Tenn@.  Dad worked on the construction of a dam, the first of three on the Ocoee River. (Ocoee #1)

Sept. 23 1911 Grace born in Parksville, Tenn.

Dec. 1911 Dam completed in Parksville

May 1912 Work commenced on diversion dam and flume line up stream from Parksville.

June 12 1912 Living at Goforth Creek, about 10 miles up river from Parksville. (Ocoee #2) It was here that they had to travel across the suspension bridge.

Sept. 12, 1912 AE. Cochran came to board@ at Goforth Creek.  It was expected that people with houses would help provide accommodation for other workmen.

Sept. 23 1913 Walter born back in NB

Oct. 1913 Second dam completed

July 21, 1914 Dad bought the Jessie MacDonald property at Little Branch where Norman Glendenning now lives.

Nov. 1, 1914 Dad became Postmaster at Little Branch, NB.

Feb. 15 1915 Grandfather Watling died.








May 14, 1915 Venetia born in Nb: Grandmother Glendenning died in Portland where she had gone to live with her daughter, Venetia Doring.

Aug.01 1916 Resigned from Post Office

Jan. 8 1917 Lillian born in New Haven, Conn.  I have no information about Dad=s work there.

Jan 13, 1918 Margaret Virginia died in Hampton, Virginia.

Feb. 27 1918 Photo taken of Grace, Walter, Venetia and Lillian taken in Mussel (Muscle?) Shoals, Alabama.  The Wilson Dam was being built at that time.

Dec. 14, 1919 John born in NB

June 11, 1924 Bought the Finno property

? Bought property from Dan MacLean where Walter later built.

? Bought property from Bob MacLean with understanding that he could continue to live in the house.  Later, John built on this property.

June 27 1929 Bought property from Jane MacDougall (The AMac@ property)

Sept. 27 1934 Grandmother Watling died

May 18 1938 Dad died at home

May 21, 1938 Dad was buried in the Black River Cemetery.

Mar. 24 1964 Mum died in Miramichi Hospital.

Mar. 28 1964 Mum buried in Black River Cemetery.

North Shore Regiment


Recently I found a History of the North Shore Regiment by Will. R. Bird (Brunswick Press, 1963).  I found it fascinating especially the part dealing with WW2.  Certainly Archie MacNaughton received high praise as an officer and as a person.


I was intrigued, also, by the following references:

The Chatham Gleaner of July 14, 1843 made reference to Aa disgraceful scene at Black River after the drill and muster there@

A rifle competition on August 17, 1868 with competitors from Black River and Chatham.

The Northumberland County militia reorganized in 1869.  No. 4 Company was from Black river under Capt. Jeremiah Ullock, Lt. Hugh Cameron and Ensign Robert MacNaughton.
