Planning document for "Black River Beautification Awards" program to promote roadside beautification in the Black River community (Northumberland County, NB). Describes community boundaries, volunteer committee structure, beautification criteria (landscaping, painting, etc.), and annual $500 grant from the foundation. Lists issues for consideration including cash prizes, promotion timing, and eligible use of funds. Shows Don's involvement in community beautification initiatives related to his Black River heritage.



Black River Beautification Awards

___________________________________________________________



  • The community of Black River wishes to promote the beautification of roadside properties within its boundaries. 

 

  • For the purpose of the Award, Black River is described as the community in Northumberland county, New Brunswick situated on both sides of the of Black River and its tributaries between the former Victoria Bridge and the headwaters of the River.


  • To oversee the making of the Award, a volunteer committee will be struck by the community for the purpose establishing the criteria and amounts of the award, and to select or approve the recipients.


  • Beautification is intended primarily for those parts of a property visible from the road and may include planting, trimming and shaping flowers, shrubs, gardens, painting of structures, gardens, gates, fences, hedges, benches 


  • The Award, given annually, will be given to a resident or residents of the community in recognition of efforts to improve the visual impact of their property


  • Each year the Foundation will make a grant, currently in the amount of $500, for the above mentioned purpose. 


  • Revenue Canada requires that the grant be made to a qualified donee.  


Issues:

  • Cash prize(s), banner, trophy
  • Cost of promotion
  • Announced before labour Day weekend
  • What would the money actually be used for?
