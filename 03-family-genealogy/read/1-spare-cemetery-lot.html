Don's 2013 letter regarding Black River Cemetery lot arrangements with family member Barbara, discussing lot ownership and potential redistribution. Documents his family connections and commitment to Black River as burial place.

62 Queen Elizabeth Drive

Charlottetown

PEI  C1A 3A9, Canada

May 21, 2013


Dear Barbara: 


Good to talk with you last Thursday but sorry to be the bearer of bad news; this seems to happen all too often as we get older. 


We spoke about the lot 143, where your mother is buried, in the Black River Cemetery.  As you know, I bought my lots in 1973. I kept lot 142 for myself, and gave lot 143 to your mother; Norman owns lot144 and 145 on the other side of where your mother is buried.


As we agreed during our telephone conversation, I’ll have the other half of lot 143 changed back into my name.  Since a number of our relatives own lots in the vicinity, I will check to see who, if any of the family is interested.  If no family member wants it, I’ll make it available to someone else.


I’m putting this on paper, and sending a copy to Neil Watling who is a member of the                      Black River Cemetery Committee, just to they can keep their records straight.


As you know, we were over to Verne’s funeral and I was pleased to see the lot had been marked for my convenience; I am planning to have a stone erected later this year.


Say hello to all the family.





Cc: Neil Watling
