"Memorial Obituary

William MacKenzie

The death of William MacKenzie of Lower Napan, occurred at Victoria Public Hospital, Fredericton on Friday, March 29. Mr. MacKenzie had been in failing health for some time, and had undergone surgery and treatment for several weeks at the hospital.

He was in his 80th year having been born in "The Hardwoods", MacKenzie Settlement back of Little Branch, Black River on July 31, 1877, the only son of the late John MacKenzie and Elizabeth MacKnight, the latter familiarly and affectionately known as "Auntie Betsy".

When quite a young man, he left home and friends and went to the Klondyke fields during the early days of the gold rush. There he faced many hardships, but being of a rugged constitution, he withstood privations and after some years, returned to his home, bought a farm at Lower Napan and has since resided there.

Besides his widow, who was formerly Elmira Loggie of Point Aux Carr, he leaves four sons and three daughters, John of Chatham; Gordon of Moncton; Byron and James of Lower Napan; Mrs. Robert Harvey, (Lena) of Fredericton; Mina an RN on the staff of the Victoria Public Hospital at Fredericton and Katherine of Toronto. Also several grandchildren.

Mr. MacKenzie was well known throughout a large area. He was keenly interested in agricultural work and in the betterment of community life, a good neighbour and always ready to render assistance to anyone who met unfortunate circumstances. He will long be remembered by those who knew him.

His funeral, which was one of the largest ever seen in the community was held on Sunday, Mar. 31, from Carmel United Church at Lower Napan. Rev. T. Constable conducted the service and internment was in St. Stephenís cemetery, Black River Bridge.

The pallbearers were: Herbert Fowlie, William Loggie, Fred Murdock, Walter Cameron, Herbert MacKnight and David Kelly.

Floral tributes were numerous and very beautiful.
