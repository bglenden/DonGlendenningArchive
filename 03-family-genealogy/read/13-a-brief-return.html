REESTABLISHING ROOTS IN NEW BRUNSW3ICK



Back to New Brunswick



We do not know exactly when Mum and Dad returned to New Brunswick but here are a few facts: 


Elmer and Jane set up housekeeping in Parksville, Tennessee on February 11, 1911.

Their first child, Grace, was born in Parksville, Tennessee on September 23, 1911.

Jessie MacDonald died in April, 1912.

A number of entries in Mum’s autograph album, the last on November 13, 1912, show they were still in Parksville at that time.  

Entries in Mum's autograph album by her brother, Ernest, and another from "Hughy", her cousin, “Mac” MacDonald,  dated November 13, 1912, indicates that either she and/or they were about to leave Parksville.

The Parksville dam was completed in December of 1912; a new dam was built up the Ocooe  River near Goforth Creek in 1913.

The second dam had the flume line which we know that Dad worked on.

A reference in Mum’s record book shows them living at Goforth Creek in 1913.

Mum was back in New Brunswick when Walter was born in on September 29, 1913.

Dad purchased the Jessie MacDonald property on July 21, 1914. 

Elmer Glendenning was listed by Canada Post as being Postmaster at Little Branch from November 1, 1914 to August 1, 1916.  

Venetia was born in New Brunswick on May 15, 1915.  

Lillian was born in North Haven, Connecticut on January 8, 1917.


My scenario is that Dad and mother moved from Parksville to Goforth Creek at the end of 1912.  In the summer of 1913, Mum returned with the children to Little Branch.  Dad may or may not have accompanied her but he came sometime that fall or winter.  They stayed at Little Branch until late 1916 and then moved to North Hampton, Connecticut where Lillian was born on January 8, 1917.
