Stephen John Dick

January 18, 1895-October 1, 1918





Stephen John Dick, was born in Black River Bridge, Northumberland County, New Brunswick. He was the youngest child of Alexander and Selina (Weaver) Dick .His siblings were Elizabeth Agnes, Barbara Victoria and John William


Gnr. Stephen John Dick, #522059, 1st Brigade, Canadian Field Artillery, buried in Haynecourt British Cemetery, about 6 miles northwest of Cambrai, France, age 23.


The Painting


Barbara Victoria, Stephen’s sister painted a portrait in oil and shows Stephen Dick, from mid torso up in a suit or perhaps a university robe; the painting is approximately 24x 32 inches; while I haven’t seen it, I am told that it is in very good condition considering its age.  The painting is signed by the artist, Barbara, Stephen’s sister; I do not know if the painting is dated

The Artist


The artist, Stephen’s sister, Barbara Victoria, married Charles Albert Parker and lived in Athabaska, Alberta. Among her paintings was one of her brother, Stephen, painted just before he went overseas. The painting passed down through the family and was donated to the Irvine Museum, Irvine, Alberta, by Carolyn Haggart, daughter of Eldon Dick and Opal Fraser, and a Grand Niece of Stephen Dick.
