BURIAL


To be cremated

Lot 142 in Black River Community Cemetery.  It is actually between lots owned by David Taylor and Grace Smith.  It is large enough for two normal burials, however, since we are being cremated.  There is actually room for the whole family.  I checked with the cemetery folk and there is no limit on the number of urns. 

Based on discussions with Marie and Brian, we should have a stone large enough for all our names regardless of where we die or are buried.  It is perfectly acceptable to scatter my ashes the Branch River near the Little Branch Bridge or on Orwell Bay off the cottage if you prefer.

RECEPTION - 

After the funeral, assuming it is a normal one, it is customary to invite friends somewhere for a reception.  An option, would be to have something at the church.  Shouldn't be elaborate just a cup of tea and a sandwich before people head for home.  Usually only a few close friends and people from out of town come anyway.  For my sister, Lillian, there was a viewing in the morning prior to the service for folks from away, in my case, folks from off Island.  Then lunch was provided and people visited until the service began.  That would work for folks from off-Island.  I wouldn’t be surprised if Holland College would cater and charge very little.


MEMORIAL SERVICE - Another option depending on the circumstances is to have a memorial service instead of a normal funeral.  This seems to be happening more and more.  A memorial service tends to be lighter and often friends participate. (It would give you a chance to tell some of my old jokes !!!!)
