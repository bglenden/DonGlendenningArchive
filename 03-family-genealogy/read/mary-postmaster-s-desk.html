Don writes to Mary about an antique postmaster's desk from Hexham, New Brunswick with family/community connections. He traces the desk's history through Bob and Ira MacDougall and suggests it be preserved as local history, offering to help contact the NB Archives. This anecdote reveals Don's attention to family and community heritage.
Family History & Genealogy

April11, 2009






Happy Easter


Great to talk with you the other night and to know that you are on the mend.  It won’t be long now until you’ll be thinking of opening your cottage although the snow in the forecast is not very spring-like.


I’ve been intending to mention the old postmasters desk in your garage but forgot each time I visited so decided to send this note.  Since I am mailing this to your Black River address, I will also send a copy to Jeannie in case you are still in Moncton.


One time I was with John when he stopped at Ira MacDougall’s place in Loggieville to purchase something.  Did Ira sell farm equipment of supplies?  While looking around waiting for John, I spotted an old desk in the shed.   When I asked Ira about it, it turned out to be, as I recall, the old postmaster’s desk used in Hexham by Bob and Bioney – and maybe others before them. (Bob and Ira were brothers, I think) 


As we drove home, we talked about it and the fact that it was a genuine antique.  I was not particularly interested in it myself but thought it should be saved.  On a return visit John got the desk from Ira, I am not sure if he paid for it or Ira simply gave it to him.


The last time I saw the desk, it was along the side of the garage next the road and was used for storing nuts and bolts.


I thought that I should let you know, Mary, so that it can be kept.  While it does not have a family connection, it is part of our Black River history.  I am sure that the NB Archives would be interested if your family isn’t.  I searched the internet, and am enclosing what I found about Hexham post offices.


Just thought that you should know


Regards to all the family
