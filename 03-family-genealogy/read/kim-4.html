Email thread between Don Glendenning and Kim Nielsen (January 2008) regarding the Harley family genealogy. Discusses Thomas Harley's presence in Toronto and York area in 1834, his position as Constable, and potential connections to the Harley relatives Don is researching. Kim mentions Loyalist connections (Barnes family) and attempts to establish family relationships. Shows Don's collaborative genealogical efforts and his interest in connecting various branches of ancestor families across different regions and time periods.

Feb 15th - Report #1 which was received and read viz constable Thomas Harley which the standing committee on offices beg to submit to the favourable consideration of the council.

Feb - 27 was turned down by common council for a full time job by a vote of 10 to 6. 

Anyway this goes on but did you not mention you had relatives in York about the same time 1834 or so.  I tell you I'm expecting a new computer in a couple of days and I have been busy printing all my genealogy and it's all in a pile.  But I thought I remembered someone saying they had relatives in that area.  I also remember something of the Barnes family being Loyalists and I thought the Harley's might be also but this was something I haven't checked into yet.  Maybe that's how Thomas met Margaret.
