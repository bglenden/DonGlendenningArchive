JOHN GLENDINNING

1807-1877


John Glendinning, 1807-1877, the author’s Great Grandfather, was born in Callister Hall, Dumfriesshire, Scotland; Callister Hall is still occupied but not by Glendinnings. .


He came to Canada in 1830, married Margaret Harley, who came to New Brunswick for Ireland, in 1833 and settled on a piece of property at Salmon Beach on the Baie de Chaleur. John farmed and Margaret taught school. The family later relocated to Canobie. John and Margaret had five children: Mary Ann, John, Margaret, Elizabeth Jain and Walter.,


The Glendinning/Glendenning name is not as rare as first thought. A number of families, mostly Glendenning rather than Glendinning, came to New Brunswick in the early 1800’s and all seem to have come from Dumfriesshire. They settled near Bathurst, Saint John, Rexton, Sussex, Woodstock and in the Port Elgin area. Although I expect a family linkage exists, I have not found one to date


The author’s lineage includes:


  • John Glendinning, Dumfriesshire, Scotland………..1755-1830

  • John Glendinning (married to Mary Rae)………….1772- Unkn

  • John Glendinning (married Margaret Harley) 1807-1877

  • Walter Glendenning (married Elizabeth Peters)… .1845-1908

  • Elmer Glendenning (married Jane Watling)……….1875-1938

  • Donald Glendenning (married Carol Chesney)… 1929-

JUBAL WATLING

Abt 1790-1845


The first Jubal Watling encountered was the author’s Great Great Grandfather. There were three Watling ancestors named Jubal; the first, at least the first in my records was born in Brockdish, Norfolk, England about 1790 but, after marriage lived in Suffolk, England. Jubal married Susannah (Susan) Leech and they had nine children: Their children included: Emily, Mary Ann, Emily, William, Jubal, Susannah, John, John and George, several of whom die

d young in England. Jubal’s wife, Susanna, also died in England and was buried on May 5, 1829 in Syleham. The family moved to Canada in 1830, perhaps via Pictou, Nova Scoria but ended up in Prince Edward Island. .


On July 14, 1831, Jubal Watling and Louisa, a widow, were married in St. Paul Anglican Church, Charlottetown and the marriage record says that Jubal and his wife were "lately" in Norfolk County, England. Jubal and Louisa also had at least one child, William Charles. After Jubal died, Louisa married a William Smith.


Jubal’s sons, William and Jubal moved to the Miramichi in the mid 1800’s to work in shipbuilding; they both married MacNaughtons and owned land at the Meadows in Black River.


The author’s lineage includes:


  • Jubal (married to Susannah Leech) 1790-1840’s

  • Jubal (married to Christina MacNaughton) 1821-1900

  • Jubal (married to Grace Macdonald) 1846-1915

  • Jane Elizabeth (married to Elmer Glendenning) 1890- 1964

  • Donald (married to Carol Chesney) 1929-

ALEXANDER MACDONALD

1775-1843


Alexander MacDonald was the author’s Great Great Grandfather and was a native of the Parish of Assynt in Rosshire, Scotland. That area of northwest Scotland is hilly and rocky with many inlets and streams with little land suitable for cultivation; the county also includes many islands


He married Grace MacAskill also of Assynt, Scotland (I believe a relative of the giant MacAskill of Cape Breton); family lore suggests Alexander and Grace were married in Cape Breton.. Their children included: Kenneth, Annabel, Hugh, Anne, Isabella , Margaret, John, Mary, and Jessie.


The original MacDonald home, I believe, was close to the river in the field just above where Norman Glendenning now lives. In those days, travel was by boat and the land at that site sloped gently to the River. Both Bill Fowlie and my sister, Lillian, remembered the remnants of an old house in that area. In a taped interview with Aunt Janet Godfrey, she recalled an old barn in that location also.

  • John (married Elspeth Jardine)

  • Grace (married Jubal Watling)

  • Jane(married (Elmer Glendenning) 1890-1964

  • Donald (married Carol Chesney) 1929-

JOHN PETERS

1817-1904


John Peters, the author’s great Grandfather, is thought to have come to Canobie, New Brunswick from County Cork in Ireland, but Peters is also a Scottish name as well as the Anglicized name of Petrie.


John married Jane Sealey and they had 13 children: Elizabeth, Richard, William, Mary Ann, Jane, Susan, Albertina, Norman Bedford, Laura, Robert, Evelyn, Esther and Amanda. After Jane died, John married Sarah Ann McKnollan and they had four children: Isabelle, Bedford, Ella and Lesly.


The author’s lineage includes:

WILLIAM HARLEY

1750-1830


William Harley, the author’s Great Great Grandfather was a schoolmaster in Courtmacsherry, a small town near Cork, County Cork, Ireland. He married Ann Bidders and they had five children, all of whom immigrated to Canada:

  • William was a government surveyor. He married near Fredericton and subsequently moved to Upper Canada.

  • Elizabeth married William Knowles and lived in the Bathurst area

  • Mary Ann married John Henry of Newcastl; after her husband died in 1870, Mary Ann moved to Portland, Maine.

  • John and George Burchill were partners in a shipyard on Beaubear’s Island and is credited with having built 62 ships on the Miramichi.

  • Margaret, a teacher, married my Grandfather, John Glendinning.
