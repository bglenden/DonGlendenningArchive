Obituary of Peter Kelly (died December 17, age 95), native of Thomastown, Kilkenny, Ireland, who arrived in Canada in 1828. Educated teacher and "fine Penman" who educated many Black River and surrounding area residents. Noted for sincere practical piety and Presbyterian faith. Maintained hale and hearty lifestyle until near death. Document includes verse tribute and was submitted by Judy Watling (great-granddaughter). Historical community record with tangential family connection.

Peter Kelly


OBITUARY:- The late Mr. Peter Kelly who died at Black River on December 17th in his 95th year was a native of Thomastown, Kilkenny, Ireland.   Mr. Kelly arrived in this country in 1828.  Having a good education, he engaged in teaching for several years.  He was a fine Penman and well versed in the common branches of learning, and a considerable number of the inhabitants of Black River and the surrounding district are indebted to him for the education they received.  Mr. Kelly was a man of great intelligence.  His piety was sincere and practical, he was a …….Presbyterian church.  His memory will be held in respect and regard, for many a day.  He was hale and hearty till within a brief period of his death.  His life was truly a useful one, and it may be said of him in the (words?) of the lines:

How blest the righteous, which …

When sinks a weary soul to rest

How mildly beam the closing eyes

How gently heaves the expiring breast.


Sent to me by Judy Watling

A great Granddaughter
