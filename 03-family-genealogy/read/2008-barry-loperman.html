Email exchange (January 30-31, 2008) between Don Glendenning and Barry MacKenzie regarding Alexander Watling and Mary Ann Lopemen. Don inquires about Alexander Watling of Chatham (born September 10, 1889), while Barry provides census analysis from 1851-1881 identifying this Alexander as likely the son of William Watling and Mary MacNaughton. Demonstrates Don's systematic genealogical research methods and collaborative correspondence with specialists.

Hello Don!

 

I am afraid that you are overestimating my level of Watling expertise! However, in this case, I believe that I may have figured out who the Alexander who married Mary Ann Lopeman was. The Census of 1881 shows Alex Sr.(26), Mary Ann (23), and two children: Mary M. (3) and William (2m). Alex Jr. was born later, as we know. The family does not appear in the 1891 census.

 

It seems that this Alex Sr. fits the age description for Alexander, a son of William Watling and Mary MacNaughton. Alex (Wm) was 6 in 1851, 15 in 1871, and no longer at home in 1881. The Alex in question is the only Alex Watling in the county in the 1881 census. Let me know if you have other information as to the spouse/progeny of William and Mary's Alex. If not, I don't know where else this Alex Sr. would have come from. I think he's our man!

 

Barry
