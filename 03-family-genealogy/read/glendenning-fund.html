Documentation of the Glendenning Family Fund established by the family of Elmer and Jane Watling Glendenning (Don's parents) for charitable purposes related to Black River Bridge, New Brunswick (Don's birthplace). Lists grants (2002-2008): history prizes, library books for Miramichi Rural School, and planned cemetery bench. Notes include contacts (Janet Glendenning, Loisanne Gregan, Donna Goodfellow) and discussion of future Miramichi Foundation transfer. Demonstrates family philanthropy rooted in ancestral community.

Background


Established by the family of Elmer and Jane (Watling) Glendenning, grants may be made for any charitable purpose related to the community of Black River Bridge, New Brunswick.  (Black River is a rural community stretching along both sides of the Black River which empties into the Miramichi near its mouth)  Grants to date:


2002 A first and second prize for a history related to Black River to Barry

MacKenzie and ?

2005 A grant of $200 to Miramichi Rural School for the purchase of library

 Books

2006 A second grant to the Miramichi rural School also for the purchase of

library books

2007 A third grant to the Miramichi Rural School for the purchase of library books

2008 Plans are to provide a bench for ate Black River Cemetery.  


Some of the committee members know that there is talk of establishing a community foundation in the Miramichi area but I have heard nothing lately.  If a Miramichi Foundation is established, the idea was/is to  transfer the glendenning endowment from PEI to the Miramichi.



May 8, 2008


Some support for planting trees/shrubs/flowers at Black River community hall but word is that hall and grounds are undergoing reconstruction. (Miramichi Recreation Council of Black River & Bay du Vin -- I believe the president is either Hilary or Jonathan Guimond.)

 
The nature of this kind of fund makes for slow decision-making; some day it will likely be converted to a scholarship; if so, it should not be limited to a student attending university
