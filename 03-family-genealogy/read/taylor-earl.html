Newspaper obituary for Earl Taylor (age 53, died by accident at Black River), husband of Elinor Glendenning. Third-party document. Important family document: Earl was David Taylor's father and married into the Glendenning family. Lists multiple children including daughter Sandra (Mrs. Eugene Shea) and details of extended family connections in the Miramichi area.

A clipping, believed to be from the Moncton times, reports the following:


Funeral For Victim of Road Mishap

BLACK RIVER (Special) - The funeral of a black river man who died in hospital Tuesday of injuries received when hit by a car, will be held today at 2:30 p.m. from St. Pau's Presbyterian Church here.

Earl Taylor, 53, was struck by a car in front of his home just after he steeped from a friend's vehicle, Newcastle RCMO said.  The mishap occurred at 6:15 pm. and Mr. Taylor died in Miramichi Hospital, Newcastle, three hours later.

Driver of the vehicle that struck Mr. Taylor was Willard MacDougall, also of Black river. Coroner Dr. J. P. Losier has ordered an autopsy and inquest unnecessary.  Black River is about 8 miles east of Chatham.

Born at Napan Bay, Mr. Taylor was a son of the late Ernest and Roberta (McInnis) Taylor.  He was a tradesman's helper at CFB St. Magaret's.  He was a second World War veteran and attended St. Paul's Presbyterian Church.

He is survived by his wife, the former Elinor Glendenning; two daughters, gene Shea (Sandra) of Ferry Road and Mrs. Benson Harris (Ginny) of Simcoe, Ontario; five sons, Gerald of Beechwood, Robert Toronto, David of Moncton, Frank and Kenneth at home; six sisters, Mrs. Harold Ryan of Fredericton, Mrs. Harvey Williston of Chatham, Mrs. John Williston of Bay du vin, Mrs. George MacDonald of Black River, Mrs. Edward Roach and Mrs Wilfred Turbide, both of Seven Islands; six brothers, Ronald of Redbank, Linwood of Chatham, Bud and Leo, both of Seven Islands, Alvin of Napan Bay, and Melvin of Lincoln, and seven grandchildren.

The Body is resting at his hoe where prayers will be said at 11:00 am today.  The body will be taken to St. Paul's Presbyterian Church where the funeral will be held at 2:30 pm today.  Interment will be in the Black river cemetery.
