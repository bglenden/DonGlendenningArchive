Email exchange between Don Glendenning and Twila Buttimer regarding genealogical research about the Harley and Glendenning families. Buttimer assists Don in locating geographic place names and historical references in New Brunswick, particularly regarding Vernon, NB, and the Mary Ann Harley burial site. References a Master of Arts thesis on the Burchill mill (1850-1906) by Burt Glendenning at Concordia University. Shows Don's methodical genealogical research methods and collaboration with other family historians.

Hi Don,

 

I'm stumped, too.

 

Our NB Gazetteer shows only a Vernon Island in the Parish of Saint George in Charlotte County and a Vernons Mountain in the Parish of Simonds in Saint John County.  I have also checked Alan Rayburn's work "Geographical Place Names in New Brunswick" (1975) - because he often includes place names that are no longer used - but no luck.  There is a Mount Venning near the Miramichi River - but that is not Vernon.   Sorry!

 

For Burt Glendenning's thesis (it is a Master of Arts thesis completed at Concordia University in Montreal in 1978) on the Burchill mill (1850-1906), you probably could find it at the University of PEI Library.  It should be there on microfiche.  Burt was checking at home to see if he had an extra copy that he could send you -but so far has not been successful.

 

Thanks for the item on Mary Ann Harley which you sent along.  It is very interesting and I believe it is the item that I was looking for.

 

With best wishes, Twila
