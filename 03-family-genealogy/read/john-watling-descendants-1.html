Extended genealogical research on descendants of John L. Watling (1825-), Don's ancestor. Details his immigration from England, marriage to Jessie Carruthers (1849), and census records tracking the family through 1871. Demonstrates Don's genealogical methodology and family connections.

Descendants of John L. Watling



First Generation


1. John L. Watling  was born in Dec 1825 in Syleham, Suffolk, England. He was christened on 6 Mar 1825 in Syleham, Suffolk, England. He died before 1920 in probably Ayer Junction, Massachusetts. 


John was employed as  a plasterer/mason. 


It is presumed that John Watling died before the Watlings made the Atlantic crossing in June of 1830, as 

he does not appear in any New Brunswick records.  That is, unless he did not make the crossing or 

settled somewhere other than NB.

There is another possibility, however. A John Watling appears in the 1851 NB Census. That census 

shows him in Napan, an immigrant of 1845 and the husband of Jessie (Carruthers). Perhaps our John 

stayed in England for some years before he immigrated to Canada. Some members of the family believe 

this to be true. Below is what I know about the family of John and Jessie (Carruthers) Watling, to err on 

the side of caution.

 - John Watling married Jessie Carruthers at Napan in June 1849, as reported in the 26 June 1849 issue 

of The Gleaner and Northumberland Schediasma. Jessie was the daughter of James Sr. and Jane 

Carruthers.

 - In the 1851 Census, John is listed with his wife Jessie in Napan, with a 1 year old daughter, Janet N. 

Watling. 

 - In the 1861 Census, John and Jessie are listed in the Parish of Newcastle with: Jennet (aged 11), 

Robert (aged 8), Susan J. (aged 5), and Mary A. (aged 2). Also, a 16 year old Jennet Edge (presumably 

of the Black River Edges), was their servant.

 - In the 1865-66 and 1867-68 Hutchison Directories, John is listed as living on McCallum St. in 

Newcastle, a mason by trade. He does not appear in the 1871 Lovell Directory.

 - Neither John nor Jessie appears in the 1871 Census. However, Robert , Jennet, and a William appear 

in Newcastle. Robert was employed as a clerk, living with the family of John Fish. Jennet and William 

appear in the household of James Fowler. Susan and Mary Ann appear in the household of their aunt 

and uncle, Emily (Watling) and William Eddy in the Bathurst area. 

 -  A John Watling, aged 41, appears in the 1870 US Census as a boarder in Newton, Massachusetts.

 - On 5 December 1871, Robert Watling died at Newton, Massachusetts, aged 18, according to the 13 

December 1871 issue of the Union Advocate.

 - In 1873, John is listed as a mason in the Newton, Mass., directory. His daughter Janette is listed as a 

dressmaker.

 - On 10 Nov 1875, at Dover, Massachusetts, Jennet N. Watling married Frank E. Crafts of Reading, 

Massachusetts, according to the 15 December 1875 of the Union Advocate.

 - The 1880 US Census lists Jennet and Frank Craft living in Reading, Massachusetts, with a son, 

Clinton W. Craft (aged 3).

 - The Natick, Mass., Census for 1880 contains the following entry:

     John WATLING   Self   M   Male   W   53   ENG.   Mason And Builder   ENG.   ENG.  _     Mary 


WATLING   Wife   M   Female   W   37   NB   Keeps House   NB   NB  _     E. Gertrude WATLING   Dau   

S   Female   W   7   MA      ENG.   NB  _     Lizzie May WATLING   Dau   S   Female   W   4   MA      

ENG.   NB  _     William WATLING   Son   S   Male   W   17   NB   At Home   ENG.   NB  

 - Could this family be the same John Watling family? The ages of John and William correspond with 

earlier records. However, Mary must have been John's second wife. The couple appears again in the 

1900 Census (see below), which indicates they had been married 30 years at the time of the census, or 

about 1870 - the same year given for their immigration to the USA.

 - In 1884, John is listed as a Mason in the Milford, Mass., directory (address: South Natick).

 - In 1884-1885, he's listed as a Mason in the Middlesex County directory (address: South Natick).

 - In 1887, John L. Watling, mason, can be found in the St. Augustine, Florida, city directory - perhaps in 

Jacksonville?. The family was still living in St. Augustine in 1892, for John's three children were 

enumerated that year in a census of school-ages children.

 - John Watling is listed in Fitchburg, Mass., directories starting in 1898 (though he certainly could have 

been there much earlier). He appears there in directories or census records almost annually until 1911. 

In 1911, under his name in the same directory it says "removed to Ayers Junction."

 - The Fitchburg, Worcester County, Mass. Census for 1900 contains the following entry:

     John Watling 69 

     Mary Jane Watling 58 

     Lizzie May Watling 24 

     Herbert J Watling 19

 - The Fitchburg, Worcester County, Mass. Census for 1910 contains the following entry:

     John L Watling 81 

     John H Watling 27 

     Elizabeth M Watling 34

     (This is where I get the middle initial "L."

 - None of the family appear in any further NB records. If Susan or Mary lived to adulthood, they must 

have resided elsewhere.

2. Janet Nickolson Watling  (John L.) was born on 28 May 1852 in Napan, NB. She died after 1930 in probably Reading, Massachusetts. 


The 1900 Census suggests that Janet first came to the United States in 1867 at the age of 33. The 1910 

Census says she came in 1872. The 1920 Census suggests that she came in 1870 and was naturalized 

in 1875. The 1930 Census suggests she come only in 1880! The spelling of her name varied from 

Census to Census. I have used Janet.

9. John Herbert Watling  (John L.) was born on 25 Apr 1882 in Massachusetts. He died about 1930 in Florida. 


He was still living at home with his widowed father at the time of the 1910 census. The Fitchburg 

Directories for 1909 and 1910 list Herbert J. as a musician. The Fitchburg Directory for 1911 says she 

had "removed to Ayer Junction."

At the time he registered for the draft during the latter years of the First World War, he was married and 

living in Jacksonville, Duval County, Florida.

In 1920, John Herbert was living in Ortega, Duval County, Florida, and working as a marine machinist.

In 1930, J.H. Watling was an "inmate" in the Florida State Hospital in River Junction, Gadsden, Florida, 

the state's first mental institution; he may have suffered from mental illness, or have been stricken with 

tuberculosis or some other such illness. His wife was living in Jacksonville with her mother and daughter 

at the time, and listed herself as widowed. Perhpas John Herbert was dead when she was enumerated; 

I can't be sure.  In the 1929 Directory, there is a Pearl M. Whatland, stenographer - this must be the 

same woman. In the 1930-31 Jacksonville Directory, Pearl is listed as the widow of Herbert Watling. 

John must have died before this?

*****

In 1892, Herbert was enumerated, along with his sisters, in St. Augustine, Florida, in a census of school-

aged children in the area. Perhaps this is how John ended up in Florida - he returned to Florida 

sometime after 1910 and before the end of the Great War.

Herbert Watling 10 M_Lizzie Watling 16 F _Gurtrude Watling 19 F
