Moncton Times obituary of Walter J. Glendenning, age 84 (Don's eldest brother), who died June 16, 1998 at Moncton Hospital. Born Black River Bridge, son of Elmer and Jane Watling Glendenning. Retired from Modern Enterprises (30+ years), member St. Andrew's Presbyterian Church Moncton and past elder at St. Paul's Black River Bridge. WWII Canadian Army veteran. Married 58+ years to E. The Moncton Times carried the following (Clipping undated)


"Walter J. Glendenning Mr. Walter J. Glendenning, 84, of Beech St., Moncton and formerly of Black River Bridge, passed away at the Moncton Hospital on Tuesday, June 16, 1998 after a lengthy illness.

Born in Black River Bridge, NB, he was the eldest son of the late Elmer and Jane (Watling) Glendenning.  Walter was a retired employee of Modern Enterprises  with over 30 years service.  He was a member of St. Andrew's Presbyterian church, Moncton; a past elder of St. Paul's Presbyterian Church, Black River Bridge and was a veteran of world War ll, serving with the Canadian Army.

Walter is survived by his loving wife of over 58 years of marriage, E. Agnes (Gibson) Glendenning; two daughters, Lillian Murray (Mike). Carla Flewelling (Tom) of Moncton; one son, Stephen (Carolyn) of Brampton, ON; seven grandchildren, Kim, Stephen, Shelly, Kristy, Meghan, Sara and Michael; one great grandson, Stephen; two sisters, Venetia Russell of Point Aux Carr, NB, Lillian Weeks of Harvey Station, NB; three brothers, Kenneth (Verne) of Napan, NB, Norman (Jean) of Black river Bridge, NB, Donald (Carol) of Charlottetown, PEI; several nieces and nephews.  He was predeceased by a daughter, Betty Ann; three sisters, Grace, Elinor and Margaret Virginia; one brother, John.

Resting at Ferguson Knowles Funeral Home, 1657 Mountain Road, Moncton (858-1995) with visiting hours Thursday from 7 to 9 pm and and Friday from 2 to 4 pm and 7 to 9 pm.  Funeral services will be held at St. Andrew's Presbyterian Church, Park Street, Moncton, on Saturday, June 20 at 11 a.m. with Rev. George Anderson officiating followed by a service at St. Paul's Presbyterian Church, Black River Bridge son Saturday, June 20 at 4 pm.  Interment in the Black River Community Cemetery.

In Walter's memory, donations to the Heart and Stroke Foundation would be appreciated by the family."
