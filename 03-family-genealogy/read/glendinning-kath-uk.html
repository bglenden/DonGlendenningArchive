Letter from Don Glendenning to Kath Glendinning in the UK (July 15, 2009) sharing a newly discovered entry in the family Bible (c. 1830) that extends his ancestry by one generation. Proposes John Glendinning (1755-1830) as his Great Great Great Grandfather, and queries Kath's knowledge to support this interpretation. Shows Don's continued genealogical investigation into late life and his international family connections with Scottish relatives. Contact address: 62 Queen Elizabeth Drive, Charlottetown, PEI; email: don@glendenning.net.

July 15, 2009



62 Queen Elizabeth Drive

Charlottetown, PEI C1A 3A9

Canada


Dear Kath:


Some time ago, I had computer problems and, as a result, seem to have lost your Email address – hence this letter.


Recently, I found another entry in the family Bible in possession of my brother, Norman Glendenning – an entry, I should add, that had completely escaped me earlier.  The family Bible had belonged to my Great Grandfather, John Glendinning (1807 – 1877).  While quite faint in places, as best I can decipher, it reads:


“In Memory of John Gl(endinning?)

Who Died at Calester Hall (Parish of?) Middlbie, Dumfriesshire…

April the 14th, 1830 

Aged 75


At age 75 in 1830, this John would have been born in 1755 and could have been my Great Great Great Grandfather thus extending my family one generation earlier; if so the lineage would be:


John Glendinning     1755-1830

John Glendinning     1772-           m Mary Rae

John Glendinning     1807-1877   m Margaret Harley

Walter Glendenning  1845-1908  m Elizabeth Peters

Elmer Glendenning   1875-1938  m Jane Watling

Donald Glendenning 1929-          m Carol Chesney


I am wondering if you have any information that either supports or refutes my interpretation. 


My Email address is  HYPERLINK "mailto:don@glendenning.net" don@glendenning.net


Trust all is well with you.


Warmest regards


Don Glendenning
