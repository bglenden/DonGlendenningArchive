Christmas Greetings from the Island


A Winter Road on PEI

Circa 2008


Greetings from PEI:


As I write, I am looking into our back yard and thinking “What a beautiful morning”. The sun is shining, temperature about five degrees and hardly a cloud in the sky. Shortly, I will head to the country for a walk in the woods.


Just prior to taking my seat in front of the computer, however, I sat by the fire and re-lived memories while reading last year’s Christmas cards and notes. My Christmas card list includes one friend, Les Ingham from New Zealand, who I have kept in touch but not seen since 1956.


The year included a Christmas cruise at the end of 2010, a hip replacement in April for Carol, visits from Brian, Janet, a visit from granddaughter Laura and Cory, attendance at the annual Glendenning Family Reunion (the 34th consecutive ones), and a visit in October with Brian and Marie in New Mexico with the added joy of having Granddaughter, Lisa, come home while we


were there. While in the West, we also travelled to Sonoma, California to visit the wine country.


There are no significant changes in our lives since I last wrote except that Janet and Mike are now married and living in Ottawa where Janet keeps an eye on how our taxes are spent and Mike is looking forward to his first full winter of shoveling Canadian snow, Laura is working and paying taxes in Maryland, Lisa continues her studies in Seattle while Kevin ensures that our Amazon accounts remain secure, Brian continues to improve our knowledge of the universe while Marie keeps the NRAO computers running. I still plant a garden, go to my woodlot, and do a bit of volunteer work. My Holland College Recollections, are finished and I have no intention of publishing them but they can be found on the Glendenning website.


Carol had a full recovery from her hip operation, is back in her bridge routine, enjoys her IPod (thanks to Brian and Marie) and generally keeps on the go. Her flower garden this year was one of the best ever, while my vegetable was one of the poorest. Our only confirmed travel plans at the moment is for three weeks on Treasure Island in Florida in early March.


The photo on the left is of my not-so-secret hide-away, 78 acres of mostly woodland with a beautiful brook that runs year round.


Warmest wishes to all








Christmas 2011
