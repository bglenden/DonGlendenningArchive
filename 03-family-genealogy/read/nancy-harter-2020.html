A warm family letter from Don to his niece Nancy Harter, recounting family news, Carol's recent health challenges (pneumonia), updates on their children and grandchildren, and reflecting on shared memories. The letter shows Don's family bonds and concern for younger generations, including his first great-grandchild Astrid.

October 11, 2020


Dear Nancy:


Thanks for your newsy note.

Great to hear from you

Thought of sending an Email but not sure that I have your correct address – (katybug1997@att.net - ?)  Let me know if this is correct, please let me know and we’ll keep in touch via Email.  Mine is more complicated – dglend@pei.sympatico.ca


We are doing ok; Carol was had pneumonia with complications last summer; almost did not make it. Is home now; can look after herself but tires easily and rests a lot.  She is on air, not oxygen, at night. Able to be out but needs a steadying hand. We were able to spend time at the cottage this summer. Have any great memories of your Mum and dad, in fact of the family and remember  your visits both to New Brunswick and Christmas in Ottawa. A lot of water over the dam or under the bridge since then


Our offspring  are well; Janet lives in Ottawa; we talk with her every Sunday.  Brian lives in New Mexico.  Granddaughter Laura lives in Maryland; Granddaughter Lisa is married and lives in Seattle.  Our big news is our first Great granddaughter Astrid born, I think, on April 28.  In fact, Brian and Marie are visiting Lisa in Seattle these days.


Hard to believe that your Mum and Dad are gone and at a relatively young age.  Yes, I remember Howard;  Carol  and I visited Howard once in that beautiful Senior’s home.  Carol and I had dinner with you and Dennis in Peoria; thought that you were living somewhere near Henry.


Your Dad was a man of faith, I remember how important his church was in his life and his daily practice.  Thanks for the book by CS Lewis; I look forward to spending time with him this winter.

Also glad to hear about your children and family.

 

I have quite a bit of information about Carol’s side of the family.  If interested, I would be glad to share.  I did send a copy to the Henry Library a number of years ago. 


Nancy, you’ll never know how much I appreciate your letter. Thank you

All the best to you and your family

Love


Uncle Don
