Don's 2022 Christmas letter reflecting on nostalgia for his mother and the importance of Christmas in his family's history. He recalls his parents' moves through Wisconsin, Texas, Alabama, Tennessee, New Hampshire, Connecticut, Maine during their early married years. Don reports having a light case of COVID while Carol remained unaffected. He recounts Brian's retirement and new travels (including Egypt and Jordan), Janet's visits to Washington and Middle East, and family time at the Orwell Bay cottage. Don mentions PEI's September hurricane, which left them without electricity for seven days and damaged trees on their property. He remains engaged with retired friends' coffees, education work, and family tree projects. Handwritten by Don.

Christmas Greetings

 from Carol and Don

Christmas 2022

_____________________________________________________________________________________________

.GREETINGS ACROSS THE MILES:


‘Tis that time of year again. 


We are looking forward to Christmas 2022. Beautiful fall weather, long evenings, occasionally a lighted fireplace and great memories of family and friends. What a great time of year.


 I also think of my Mother and how she treasured this time of year and the Christmas cards and notes.  Dad and Mum spent the first 10 years of married life moving from worksite to worksite in Wisconsin, Texas, Alabama, Tennessee, New Hampshire, Connecticut, Maine and perhaps a few others. Christmas ws a special time for her.


Another good year.  We are still here, still together, still in our own home and mostly managing on our own with the help of local restaurants and meal suppliers .I had a light case of Covid but Carol has escaped so far.


Carol is doing fine, she looks after herself around the house but needs to rest quite a bit. She no longer tends her garden but we managed to spend July and August  at our cottage.  


Brian and Marie, Janet and Mike spent   time with us at the cottage this summer; it is so peaceful atching the sunsets and the tranquil Orwell Bay. It is such a eautiful setting with the red cliffs on all sides of us. Five of the originals still summer there, plus a number of their offspring.  How fortunate we are!


PEI suffered a devasting hurricane in September.  We were without electricity for seven days; we also lost some trees in our back yard. Fortunately no one was hurt.  Janet came home for a week to tend out on us,


Children. grandchildren and great Granddaughter keep well but live too far away. We are thankful that all keep well and Marie keeps us  up to date with photos.


Think I reportrd last year on Brian’s retirement. He and Marie bought a camper trailer and travel in Western USA. They just returned from a trip yo Egypt and Jordan. Janet and Mike are regular  visitors to Washington and Janet managed a visit a couple in the Middle East that she first met  while teaching in Korea.


Doesn’t take much tokeep me busy. Still have regular coffees with old friends and retirees, I keep busy with my education and family tree stuff. If I do anything further, I will update some family information.


Trust that you folks are well and continue ti escape the Covid.  It has been so good over the years to have a great network of friends and relatives for support.



To friends and family, wherever you are, although we may not actually touch base often, you are much in our thoughts and prayers as we write this note.  May this Christmas season bring peace and joy to you, to your families and all who you hold dear.


Merry Christmas


and


Happy New Year
