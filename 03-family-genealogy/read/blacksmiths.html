Brief note, likely from a correspondent, providing information about blacksmith shops in Black River. The writer mentions Jack Dick's blacksmith shop and a building beside John W's house that was formerly a store. This appears to be a response to research inquiry sent to Don, showing his collaborative research methods with community members.


Sorry for the tardiness in replying. I can't remember any other establishments other than the

ones you mentioned, but of course my recollections date from our arrival in Black River in 1945.

I remember Jack Dick's blacksmith shop. I should mention , and perhaps you recall, there was

a small building right beside the road in front of John W's house. They always referred to it

as the "shop", and I understand it was a store at one time. I don't know what years it operated,

but certainly pre- 1945.
