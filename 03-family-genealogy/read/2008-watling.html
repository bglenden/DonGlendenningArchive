Email correspondence (January 27, 2008) between Don Glendenning and members of the Watling Genealogy Yahoo group. Don inquires about Jubal Watling and Sysan Leach who married November 14, 1814 in Suffolk, England, and their five children who immigrated to New Brunswick. Response from group members (Anne, Elaine) offers baptism records from Brockdish, Norfolk (1790) and marriage information. Demonstrates Don's active participation in genealogical research networks and his efforts to extend family history documentation backward in time.

Hi Don, welcome to the Group.

We recently looked into this and I found a baptism, in Brockdish, Norfolk, that might be Jubal. Or not.

Baptism 6th Jul 1790 J. Watling, son of Samuel Harper Watling & Susanna

    As you can see there is only an initial, but the date seems to fit and so far it's the nearest I've come across. A visit to the Record Office might reveal more but we have no one who can do that, at the moment.

    Anne  has a marriage for Samuel HARPER to Susanah WATLING, in Brockdish, 6th July 1790 (same day). Co-incidence or is it the same couple who jumped the gun?

    Elaine

If my information is correct, I trace my ancestry from Jubal Watling and Sysan Leach who married at Metfield, Suffolk County, Englannd on November14, 1814; as far asa I know, they did not leave England.

 

According to my information, five children came to New Brunswick, Canada:

 

Jubal who marriied Chriistina MacNaughton

William who marriied Mary MacNaughton

Emma who married William Eddy

Mary Ann who married William Maxwell

George who apparently moved to the USA

 

With the exception of George, all settled in the same community, just south of Chatham, New Brunswick

 

I am happy to share information about those who came to Canada but am really interested in extending my information into earlier years.

 

Don
