Letter from Don Glendenning to Mrs. K. Glendinning, Low Hesket, Carlisle, Cumbria, UK (August 15, 2007). Don responds to a genealogical inquiry passed through the Dumfries and Galloway Family History Society. Outlines his direct descent from John Glendinning (b. 1807 at Callister Hall) through John's emigration to New Brunswick (1830), marriage to Margaret Harley (1833), and death in 1877 at Canobie. References his informal family newsletter and website (www.glendenning.net/don). Demonstrates Don's engagement with international genealogical communities and his role as a family historian sharing research across continents.

Dear Mrs.  Glendinning:


Your request for information, #3515, in the Dumfries and Galloway Family History Society was passed to me by a Ian Glendinning.


I am a descendant of John Glendinning and Mary Rae through their son, John, who was my great Grandfather.


John, my Great Grandfather, was born at Callister Hall in 1807, came to New Brunswick, Canada in 1830, married Margaret Harley in 1833 and died in 1877 in Canobie, New Brunswick.


Some years ago, I commenced an informal newsletter as a vehicle to pass along family information to my siblings.  I am enclosing the first issue for your information.  Since then my son, Brian, established a website and all issues of the newsletter are now found there although not all deal with family information.,   HYPERLINK "http://www.glendenning.net/don" www.glendenning.net/don.  I also may be reached by Email at  HYPERLINK "mailto:don@glendenning.net" don@glendenning.net


If you are interested, I would be delighted to share such information as I have about my branch of the family.  I am assuming that you have a family connection to John Glendinning and Mary Rae and, therefore, I would be interested in learning more about other branches of the family tree.


Sincerely



Donald Glendenning

August 15, 2007
