The Northern Light of November 13, 1958 includes the following on page 8 "Leonard Glendenning Paid Final Tribute

The death of Leonard Glendenning occurred  at the home of Weston Breckenridge in Bass River Sunday evening after a lengthy illness. He was 77.

Born in Canobie, he moved to the United States and resided in Palermo, Maine until about ten years ago when he returned to take up residence in Salmon Beach.

He is survived by his wife, the former Alma Jagoe of New Bandon; two daughters, (Verna) Mrs. Parker O'Brien of Newton, Mass; (Millie) Mrs. George Parker of Illinois; two sons, Myron of Palermo; Stedman of Hampton Heights; one sister, Mrs. Venetia Doring of Daytona Beach, Florida; two brothers, Raymond of Canobie and Guy of Florida.

A private service was held at the home of Clinton Glendenning on St. Andrew Street, Bathurst at 10:00 am Wednesday from where the funeral was held at 3:00 pm, to the Pentecostal Tabernacle where the service was conducted by Rev. S. Irvine, Rev. E. R. Woodside and Pastor Leslie Mann.  Burial was in the Sand Hill cemetery.  Pall bearers were John Glendenning, Walter Glendenning, Guy Glendenning, Sterne Glendenning, Ira Jagoe and Hazen Jagoe.

A large number of floral tributes and messages of sympathy were received by the family testifying to the high regard in which Mr. Glendenning was held."
