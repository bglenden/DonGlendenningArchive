Holland College & Institutional Legacy

November 24, 2014


Editor, 

Charlottetown Guardian

Charlottetown, PEI


Dear Sir:

Another option


For many years employers used high school graduation as a measure when hiring new employees.  Now, however, employers tell me that a graduation diploma doesn’t provide the assurance it once did, especially as a measure of basic communication and math skills; perhaps it never actually did.  


In view of this, it’s time for an independent assessment service or centre where people, young or old, could have their skills assessed.  Different employers may have different requirements, but the testing service, I am sure, could easily accommodate any differences.  If a person meets an employer’s requirement, problem solved; if a person doesn’t meet requirements, presumably s/he can return to school and further study


Tests of basic skills are already available on PEI at Holland College and Workplace Learning PEI, and likely there are others.  In fact, I am told that some employers in the West require Islanders to take tests on PEI before heading there to work.  I am not suggesting a massive new testing program but, for people who need to know their skill levels, a separate testing service deserves consideration.



Sincerely




Don Glendenning

A long time student of education
