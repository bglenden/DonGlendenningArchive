Official minutes from the November 25, 2015 stakeholder meeting chaired by Don Glendenning, attended by representatives from Holland College, UPEI, the Department of Education, employers, literacy advocates, and community members. Key discussion points: confirmation that education's purpose includes preparing citizens; acknowledgment that the "preparedness gap" is poorly defined, with employers identifying specific deficits in customer service, reading cursive, mathematics, and numeracy; Don's observation that no master list of required mathematics and communication skills exists; recognition that both academic and soft skills gaps exist; estimated 15-20% of young people experience the gap; discussion of systemic constraints (teacher training, class size/composition, socioeconomic factors). The meeting demonstrates Don's facilitation skills and systematic problem-framing approach.

Notes:

  1. The chair welcomed those present, confirmed the purpose to be an “exploration of the “preparedness gap” as identified in the CFPEI youth survey in 2013; during his remarks, the chair noted that those invited were, by and large, people he had spoken with about the subject but it is likely that other people would be interested and should be added to the list.

  2. Those present seemed to agree that a goal of education is to prepare young people for citizenship.

  3. In summary:

  • The gap is not well defined; the GCACC presentation to Government identified the following problem areas, inability to perform face-to-face customer interactions, inability to read cursive writing, lack of math skills required for measuring and cutting in construction, and inability to count

  • Don mentioned that he had searched for master list of mathematics and communication skills but neither was found; such a list, he suggested, could be useful to schools, colleges, universities, and employers when communicating about what students know and are able to do.

  • It is likely that the shortfall includes both academic and soft skills; brief mention was made of Employability Skills which includes both.

  • We do not have estimates of the number of people experiencing the gap although the figure of 15-20 percent was floated.

  • Steve and Roger commented on the need for simplicity and clarity

  1. Tom White-Hassler briefly described a community survey on education that he had conducted while working in Alaska.

  2. It was pointed out that teachers are under pressure by the range of student differences, student interests, socio-economic background, home conditions and financial resources. This brought comments about the importance of students being able to connect their learning to their needs and resulting pressures on the system to customize services.

  3. The school system is also constrained by the labour contract, especially by the issues of class size and composition. (Editor: Recently a BC court ruled that neither class size nor class composition are negotiable; the ruling is being appealed to the Supreme Court of Canada.)

  4. At the end of the meeting, several participants expressed an interest it knowing about the roles of people at the table; we will make that item one on the agenda of our next meeting

  5. Attendees commented on the usefulness of the exchange and a willingness to meet again; this was attested to by the fact that the conversation continued well after the meeting closed.

  6. The Chair thanked all for their attendance and Roger Birt for the use if a meeting room as well as the coffee and cookies

  7. Meeting closed at 10:30 am

  8. Next meeting at the call of the chair.
