I was asked recently about the chances of improvement in public education. Based on the observations below, the chance of significant improvement is slim: 

  • The re-establishment of school boards is a positive move; the key to improvement will be reflected in the level of autonomy granted to boards and schools.  
  • Adding Lifelong Learning or Early Childhood Development  to the Department title suggests a hesitancy to tackle the education issue.
  • Delivering programs to youth misses the dynamics of the education process.  
  • A few days go, I sat with a small group of Islanders when a fairly routine matter arose that we thought should be discussed with officials in the Department; we did not know who to talk with; transparency remains to be dealt with. 
  • The department lacks a unifying set of beliefs, principle and goals and has yet to tackle the matter of identifying and describing what it is trying to do.
  • Human/social capital
  • We know how to do math better; the task force could be a lever to improvement.

The school machine is still chugging along; not sure yet if it will embrace change.

Sincerely


Don Glendenning

A long- time student of education
