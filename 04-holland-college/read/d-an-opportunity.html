Don positions PEI's move to a single school board as an opportunity to implement governance reform. He argues schools shouldn't be run by government, advocating instead for delegation to teachers as professionals (comparable to doctors and lawyers), school autonomy, and closer decision-maker-to-stakeholder relationships. He references Holland College's 1969 Board of Governors model as successful precedent for delegated governance. The letter demonstrates Don's belief that structural governance changes enable operational improvements and his institutional knowledge from college leadership.

July 18, 2012
