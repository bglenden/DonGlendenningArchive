Planning document describing small community-level projects ($2,000-5,000 range) for PEI, including book clubs for kids, education navigator programs, Handle With Care parent training (with kids' literacy components), and after-school STEM programs. References successful programs like Pathways to Education and Holland College STEP, reflecting Don's commitment to community-based educational innovation aligned with organizational advocacy.


Planning

 Small Projects


After our annual meeting concluded last Wednesday, we spoke about potential funding for small projects, the example was a possible project in Eastern PEI, perhaps the Souris Area, in the range of $2-5.000. Here is a summary of responses to date: 


  • Book Club for Kids

A reading club for kids and their parents; New York Times of July 24, 2016

http://www.nytimes.com/2016/07/24/opinion/sunday/the-right-way-to-bribe-your-kids-to-read.html?emc=edit_th_20160724&nl=todaysheadlines&nlid=55694578&_r=0); see also ‘”How to start a Book Club for Kids” (www.pbs.org)  

 

  • Education Navigator To provide young people, and their parents, with an advisor/navigator to help with the decision-making process about their educational future; one such model is that put in place recently at Westside High School in Vancouver.


  • Encouraging Literacy

A workshop for parents on “how literacy develops” in children;   


  • Handle With Care:
  • Conduct parent training programs, programs usually run for___ hours per week for  8 weeks; 
  • Add a “kids book club” component to run concurrently with the adult program we could encourage literacy through the reading club and for younger children a love of reading through stories and art of various kinds.
  • Three parent programs -east, central and west; If we had 3 pilot programs running for  parents and children simultaneously with ($2,500.00) funding for each would enable an honorarium for each facilitator as well as cover the cost of materials and supplies for each program .  Seek community support for snacks for parents and children.
  • Technology

An afterschool program to provide students in Grades program to provide students in the Grades 7-9 age group with opportunities to work with electronics, learn Scratch programming, learn and experiment with 3D design; students meet in the computer lab at a local school for 1.5 hours once a week for 6 week; students are also expected to tour the FabLab at new Engineering building at UPEI, and have their 3D designs painted.    1. It depends how many computers we have access to. 20 is a comfortable group size. 1.5 hour a week for 6 weeks. Normally, $2000, but I would say $2500 to help cover the cost of transportation to UPEI.

  • High school design course

A March Break style camp or after-school program for Grade 10-12 modelled on the UPEI 1st year design course.  Students could work with people in the community to design and prototype a device that would help them solve a problem and present their solutions to the public..  Support would be required through the design and prototype stages as well as access to the FabLab at UPEI, high school shops (welding, carpentry, etc) and possibly local businesses. 

  • Educationsl facilitator

 to work with a community in organizing educational activities to meet their needs?
