PEI Math Map

Math Map 25


Purpose:

Develop a ready-reference map of math competencies needed by Islanders for every day living.


Background

Mathematics is the study of the science of number, quantity and space. The Oregon Department of Education contends that math provides “a foundation for the study of science and technology as well as the interpretation of quantitative information in other subjects. Mathematics may be structured in a number of ways but structure does not appear to have a particular influence on the quality of instruction.


Math is an essential skill needed to some level by all citizens and can be learned to the citizen level by all or practically all Islanders. The public school math curriculum guide lists the goals of mathematics as being to prepare students to:

  • Solve problems

  • Communicate and reason mathematically

  • Appreciate the value of mathematics

  • Make connections between mathematics and its applications

  • Commit themselves to lifelong learning

  • Become mathematically literate adults using mathematics to contribute to society.


Furthermore, helping citizens acquire math skills is one of he key roles of our education system unfortunately, however, there is no agreement as to the nature and level of math needed and how it should be determined. As to proficiency levels, there appears to be some agreement that differences in math levels may be attributed to three factors:

  1. The number of skills that can be demonstrated

  2. Levels achieved on skills demonstrated, and

  3. Nature or degree of clustering of skills learned.


Current providers of math instruction in PEI include:

  • Public-school system

  • Postsecondary institutions

  • SkillsPEI through a purchase arrangement

  • Private schools offer instruction with content being aligned with the relevant provincial curriculum.

  • Other including self study, MOOC’s, employer- based training and trade and professional associations

Based on the literature, discussions with math educators and the writer’s own experience, the writer concludes that, with regard to mathematics:

  1. A common analysis of math competencies does not exist on PEI.

  2. Instruction would benefit from a common analysis or master list of competencies on which profiles can be plotted, programs developed and progress charted

  3. There is no formal mechanism for coordination among math providers nor between providers and “users”

  4. Evaluation criteria should be the same at all levels

  5. There is a willingness among math providers to cooperate on a pilot project


Proposal

To undertake an analysis of math competencies and plot a profile of math requirements at the PEI work entry level

Characteristics of a math map: Single sheet (fanfold or open?), Easily adapted to IT, engages the community, uses community language, minimal cost and Increases relevance.


Applications of map: planning, negotiating, student tracking, counselling, transcripts, record of achievement, curriculum development


Benefits: Improve communication, engages community, ensures relevance, increase understanding of math and instruction process quality control, eases transition and minimal cost.
