OVERHAUL-1

INTRODUCTION


Education is complex; information plays a key role. Learners have different interests, experiences, capabilities and needs; they also fill a variety of life roles. Those who teach, or otherwise serve learners, must be very clear in their communication. Conceptual clarity heightens our understanding of key words and concepts; lack of clarity is a significant issue for public education. Lack of clarity could signal conflicting ideas and levels of discomfort or disagreement from within the education family group itself; some say “clarity” is also related to self confidence. Conceptual clarify is also a must in planning and assessing performance


An organization’s structure outlines how services are to be directed to achieve purposes, goals, roles and responsibilities. While organizations may take many forms, service organizations, like education, are usually local or decentralized giving members freedom to respond to individual and local demands. In recent years, however, it appears to the writer that education is increasingly being considered a product rather than a service.


Learning is an individual matter; only the learner can learn, however, learning may occur in a group setting and sometimes may even require others or be part of the teaching-learning process.


The Dictionary of Education (Good) defines education as “the aggregate of all the processes by means of which a person develops abilities of positive value in the society in which he lives”. Given this, readers will understand, therefore, why I stuttered when asked by a senior Island educator where I got my education. While there are bound to be differences of opinion about meanings and understandings of words, I believe that the Department of Education has a special responsibility to share their meanings of frequently used words – especially those as “meaning laden” as found in education.


Typical education services include:

  • Information - Recent and independent information

  • Counselling – help to people making decision about their education needs

  • A governing structure - a body with vision, responsibility and resources.

  • Instruction - diagnosis, presentations, practice and access generally to resources

  • Quality Assurance – to assure Islanders that their learning experiences meet community standards.

  • Products and Technology - Access educational materials and technology

  • Funding - to a level and in a manner that is equitable for all Islanders.

  • Human Resources - Teachers, administrators, counsellors, advisors. assistants and others


Education services are delivered to citizens in a variety of ways including family members, friends, tutors, teachers, media, public and private institutions, created and directed by Provincial Departments of Education; but education also arises from activities undertaken by individuals and organizations npt part of the formal system. an education system outlines how services are to be directed to achieve purposes, goals, roles and responsibilities. While organizations may take many forms, service organizations, like education, are usual local or decentralized giving front-line members freedom to respond to individual and local demands. In recent years, however, it appears to the writer that education is increasingly being considered a product (graduation diploma, degree or other credential) - a change that may work to the disadvantage of quality.


It is the writer’s view that a number of structural issues interfere with the functioning of our education sector; chief among them are the following:

  4. Distinction between going to school or getting an education - A recent World Bank report and some comments about the differences between going to school and getting an education. As I understand it, schools focus on helping young people learn traditional subjects. Education, on the other hand, includes traditional subjects but adds a significant component on development of mental agility, character development, creativity, problem solving, personal development, analysis, self-assessment, discussion, etc. Schooling, is a part or stage of learning that takes place in facilities specifically designed for education purposes. Experiences in school are an important part of education but only a part. Holistic rating scales and self- assessment are education tweaks.

Autonomy and Accountabilities – Organizational accountability is about defining an organization’s mission, values and goals as well as everyone’s role in working towards them; holding yourself, and everyone else, responsible for accomplishing goals, completing assignments and making decisions that deliver on expectations; people resist and fail to support what they don’t understand; culture of accountability. Start with clarity and set expectations; be goal focused, hire the right people, Communicate with transparency. team and individual productivity, builds trust and collaboration, helps organization perform

not shown in available literature; successful services organizations devolve autonomy and accountabilities to front line workers, one of the hallmarks of Holland College among community colleges in Canada was the degree of autonomy in its day-to-day operation; the writer is of the view that a similar level of autonomy and accountability should be extended to public schools.


The OECD, who gives us PISA tests, agrees.

  • in countries where schools have greater autonomy over what is taught and how students are assessed, students tend to perform better.”

  • “… in countries were schools account for their results by posting achievement data publicly, schools that enjoy greater autonomy in resource location tend to show better student performance than those with less autonomy. In countries where there are no such accountability arrangements, schools with greater autonomy in resource allocation tend to perform worse.
