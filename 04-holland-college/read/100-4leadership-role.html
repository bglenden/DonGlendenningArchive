Editor



Core Beliefs

Education, in fact every organization, needs a set of core beliefs to guide decision-making, and the list should be written and widely quoted.  Such a document can take many forms – a philosophy, mission, mandate or guiding principles,  A properly crafted statement becomes a reference document for the organization, improves communication, increases consistency, supports management, is a development tool,  assures the public that the organization is well run – a set of beliefs is a basic document from which all decisions flow.  

Such a document may well exist but I have not been able to find it.  Yes, I know there is a ”philosophy of education” that applies to public schooling, but it is out of date and conveniently fuzzy; mandate letters tend to deal with priorities not foundational beliefs, as do Ministerial Directives.  Is background for this letter, I browsed a number of school websites including UPTI and Holland College. None of my searches revealed the kind of document that  I feel is necessary. It may be brief and may be part of a larger document.  Holland College, based its early development a dozen or so foundational statements.  The following  statements are indicative of those that may be included:
