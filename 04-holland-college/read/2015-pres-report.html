EDUCATION 20/20 INC.

President’s Report

For the Year ending December 31, 2016


I am pleased to present the following report of Education 20/20 Inc. for the year ending December 31, 2015.

  • Newsletter: Open Education, one-page electronic newsletter published monthly in both official languages  
  • Social media: blog, Facebook and twitter account thanks to Tom White-Hassler with assistance of Maureen Kerr
  • Workshops:
  • Public presentation on September 30, 2015 by Gerry Seaward on “Bridging the Gap”, the Adult Basic Education program at Holland College.
  • Public presentation on  November 25, 2015 by Dave Cormier on “Teaching and learning in a Complex World”
  • Occasional Papers published:  
  • Eight Year Study
  • A Call for Action
  • Learning Path
  • Award: An award in the amount of $2,500 to Prince Street Elementary School for demonstrating a high level of engagement in its day to day activities; award money used to refurbish the stage, including drapes, as part of their Values Education program
  • Presentations: 
  • Bill Whelan and the President met with the  Minister of Education, Hal Perry and Deputy Minister to brief them on the work of Education 20/20 am and discuss education issues generally August 6, 2015
  • Bill Whelan and the president made a presentation the regular monthly meeting of the Progressive Conservative Party; the presentation consisted of some opening comments and questions from the floor.
  • Tom White Hassler and the President met with Richard Brown, Minister of Brown on November 23, 2015.
  • Reception: at Haviland Club on Wednesday evening, December 10, 2014 with 30 people in attendance
  • Meetings and conversations with Political Party leaders, officials in Department of Education, community leaders, and others on matters of education.
  • New Directors: Directors expressed the desirability of a more inclusive board and specifically mentioned students, parents, active teacher; since the meeting, I have, both formally and informally, without success. Brought me to the realization that my age is a limiting factor as to knowing potential Directors.  Suggest others need to be involved in the process
  • Thanks: Especially to Roger Birt of R. Birt and Associates for a sustaining grant; Regis Duffy for sponsorship of a school award, CFPEI for ongoing support, Rachel Bellaire for translation, other donors both named and anonymous, Directors and volunteers.


Respectfully submitted

Don Glendenning, President
