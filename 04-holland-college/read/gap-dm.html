Basic Education

Letter Cameron and Wilson


In late 2013, The Community Foundation of Prince Edward Island (CFPEI) undertook a survey of over 200 Island youth to identify issues that directly affect them; the report, Taking the Pulse of Island Youth, available on the Foundation’s website at www.cfpei.ca, provides a snapshot of the findings. On the subject of education and learning, young people expressed concerns about their readiness for the next stage of life including the “preparedness gaps with regard to readiness for postsecondary study and the workplace”.


We have continued to explore the issue under a “basic skills” theme, meaning those skills required of any and all citizens, and considered foundational to all other learning. On further informal exploration, we find that:

  • Basic skills mean different things to different people; people tend to roll together academic upgrading, employability skills, literacy skills and others.

  • Low basic skill levels drag down the economy of PEI

  • The ABE program at Holland College appears to be a repeat of some of our high school courses

  • It appears that the Province is paying twice for the same service.

  • It is difficult to identify basic skills within the PEI curriculum framework

  • The practice of linking college and university selection to high school graduation grades and pattern of courses should be reviewed.

  • Basic skills appear to have little be priority on the education system.

  • A low level of basic skills may not only block entrance to postsecondary education but may also lower skill levels of people who graduate.
