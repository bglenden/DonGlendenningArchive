Don recounts visiting Philadelphia's "School Without Walls" in the 1970s, inspired by a Life magazine advertisement. The school located classes throughout the city in libraries, galleries, churches, and available spaces rather than in traditional buildings, with teachers' first task being to secure meeting space. Don reflects that while interesting then, schools without walls are now more feasible given internet information access, competency-based education, performance assessment, computers, and individual learner management systems. He proposes two implementation scenarios: apprenticeship programs using competency analyses with learning managers (unlike teachers, more akin to physicians) who identify needs, create learning plans, secure opportunities, and provide feedback. The learning manager contracts specialized instruction and arranges apprenticeship testing and placement with employers. Similar models could work for mathematics, communication, history, art, and music.
