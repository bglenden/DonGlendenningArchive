Self-Assessment

Blog/1//6/5/2022


Analysis and feedback are at the heart of teaching and learning; they provide a basis for discussion between teacher and learner as to what was intended, what happened and “why”.  It acts as a check on the teaching -learning process and signals as to readiness for the next step. I first encountered its significance as part of the DACUM process and saw it in action within the Holland College setting. Most recently I encountered it in articles on “holistic rating scales” and the teaching of mathematics. 


Self-assessment calls for a transparent teaching learning process with both assessment criteria and a rating scale known to all.  Typical criteria include how complete the task time, need for assistance if any, accuracy, speed but the development of a rating scale is a subject for another blog. 


Self-assessment works on the assumption that what one learns can be demonstrated. At an appropriate stage in the learning process. a student may select or a teacher may assign a project. Under the watchful eye of the teacher, a learner proceeds to perform the task or solve the puzzle
