Education Needs an Overhaul

It seems that most weeks produce a new set of educational problems if not issues – math tests for teachers in Ontario, curriculum overhaul in Alberta, math levels of nurses in Manitoba, grade inflation, shortage of construction workers in PEI, high cost of university study, impact of poverty on student performance, and the list goes on.  The gap between education services desired and education services provided continues to grow.

But the world we live in has changed dramatically; education products are not enough.  There is a dramatic increase in the need for education services – information, counselling, education products,  short term instruction and support.  Education institutions lack capacity to respond

What might tomorrows education service look like? Well. I see it as an educational pharmacy or hospital, or some combination thereof, where one may be referred or check in for educational support.  In writing this, I am reminded of  a motion approved by the Holland College Board of  Governers in, I think, August 1969 which gave the President to offer raining of any kind provided it was legal and funded.

Sincerely

 

Don Glendenning

A long- time student of education.
