A Primer


Occasional Paper D-4-A March 28, 2015



Background:


It is widely recognized that education is a key factor in determining each citizen’s quality of life; in the same way, the collective education of our citizens will determine to a significant degree the quality of the community in which we live.


The responsibility for a person’s education rests first with family and later with the individual himself or herself, but it is such an important issue and so complex that most homes and most individuals need help. Governments help, especially with education for the young, for two reasons, (1) to encourage and support citizens to develop their personal capabilities and (2) to ensure that our communities have the people needed to sustain themselves


It is important, I believe, to have a simple framework for making decisions. the notes that follow describe mine. Each of us should develop our own; the notes that follow are a brief description of mine. I present it, not for others to follow, but as an example of what I have found helpful. First, a few definitions:


Why a Primer:


When speaking of learners:

  • Education - any experience that has a formative effect on the way a person thinks, feels or acts.

  • Purpose – to develop one’s capacities to be full participating member of a community

  • Learner – a person who is in the process of changing behaviout

  • Learning - a process of engaging in experiences that have a formative effect on the way we feel, think and act

  • Teacher – a person who develops strategies to help another person change behaviour

  • Education/learning domains – Cognitive, affective and psychomotor


When speaking of an education system:

  • School teacher – a person who works in a school and is paid to help students learn

  • School system refers to the institutions, programs and services provided and encouraged by or with the approval of a community to provide formal education for its residents.

  • Education system – a set of components, relationships and services put in place by a community to encourage and support the education of its citizens

  • School -


Components of an Education System:

  • Information, counselling and advice for people who make decisions about their own or a family member’s education.

  • Institutions, places with desks and chairs, for people who want or need formal instruction

  • Community based organizations such as libraries, youth organizations, access centres, employer training, service clubs, NGO’s and Itinerant teachers that provide instruction, information, tutoring, experience in non-formal education

  • Certification and qualification service for individuals and organizations that want or need their levels of performance.

  • Governance structure to provide leadership, planning, research, coordination and monitoring.

  • Resource materials such as textbooks, technological applications, devices and supplies.

  • Qualified staff –to ensure that the human resources are available to provide services required.

  • School days per year – a clause in a labour-management agreement establishing the number of days per year that employees will be paid.

Role of a School:

  • Help young people to become good citizens

  • Provide a community with the skills needed

  • Provide safe and supportive care while parents work


Concepts that puzzle me:

  • Education levels – practice of tying educational achievement to number of years of study.

  • Subject levels – practice of basing levels of achievement in a subject on the age learners.

  • Social promotion – the practice of promoting a student or awarding a credential to a person who has not met the posted requirements.

  • Outcome levels – practice of age based scale applied to education outcomes

  • Credentials – the high level that employers and the community assign to a credential

  • Unwillingness to spell the role of schools

  • Lack of consultation as to what should be taught


Although deliberate choices, none of the above are cast in stone. They reflect my thinking as I write. Maybe when I revisit the subject I will recast or retitle the components. The difficult items on the above list were institutions and community services; an alternative to institutions would be the term “instruction” could just as easily have been “informal educational services”
