Open Education

38 March 25, 2016


“They are sick of pretend solutions that address the politics of our problems rather than

the problems themselves.” – McKinnon and Galston


A bold move in Australia


I am on the record as saying that Provincial Governments should get out of the business of operating schools. They have not shown, at least to my satisfaction that they are up to the task, but not only that, their involvement in the running of schools interferes with their ability to monitor and ensure quality and fairness. Although current governments did not create our education problems, government is the only body with the authority and mandate to solve them.


To be effective, education needs a clear statement of mission, mandate and goals; the existing PEI statement needs updating, especially of its principles and goals. A second concern is the lack of congruence between form and function; in the absence of a functional analysis, a better functioning organization is unlikely to emerge. A third concern is the absence of role descriptions, especially roles expected of teachers and principals; I’m of the view that everyone on a salaried position should have a job description; the confusion as to the role of principal makes the point. Hopefully, the departmental reorganization now under way will resolve the above issues.


Given my views, I was delighted to read, in the Sydney Morning Herald, a comment by Doug Whitby, Executive Director of Schools, Catholic Education, Diocese of Parramatta, Australia (http://www.smh.com.au/national/education/sydney-catholic-schools-looking-to-appoint-principals-from-other-industries-20160315-gnj601.html) that their future school leaders may be drawn from the private sector, and that the title of principal may well disappear.

Competency-based education


Competencies have been a long-standing characteristic of education in the field of health, and the trend continues with the recent announcement that Queen’s University is converting their medical education program to a competency-based program.
