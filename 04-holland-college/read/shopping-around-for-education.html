Shopping around for education


When I travel I am a regular reader of local papers whether at a restaurant where we stop for lunch or where we overnight, and I have a special interest in articles about education and schooling.  Concerns for quality appear almost daily in news items, editorials and letters to the editor.


Suggestions for improvement are almost universal, smaller classes, curriculum changes, better qualified teachers, longer school days or school years and more standardized testing.  The Charlottetown Guardian of March 31, 2005 referred to “shopping around” for early childhood education programs.  Then it struck me.  If shopping around is acceptable for early childhood education and for college and university studies, why not allow it in the public school system?


Stop and think for a moment, we can choose our church, dentist, lawyer, mechanic, mayor, shoe store and doctor but we have limited choice of schools our children attend and no choice in their teachers.  


I believe that the time has come to allow parents to “shop around” for the schooling of their children.  Implementation of the concept won’t be easy but it is possible – and desirable.

Don Glendenning
