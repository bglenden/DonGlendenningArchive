Don's critical analysis of governmental constraints on education reform. He identifies problems: government complexity, competing priorities, excessive management layers, power concentration, redundant checking, risk aversion, expertise gaps, and operational weaknesses. Proposes solutions: prioritizing limited reforms, reversing negative trends through hiring, increasing transparency, and empowering leaders. Notes government's lack of competitive pressure and reluctance to relinquish power. This document reveals Don's pragmatic understanding of bureaucratic constraints he navigated during Holland College leadership.
