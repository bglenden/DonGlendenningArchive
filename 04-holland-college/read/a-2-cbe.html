Competency Based Education

No. 2-1 August


Background

Traditionally schools focused on content to be learned, whether it be in the arts, literature, sciences, business or technology. For the purpose of teaching and learning, fields were divided into subjects, courses, units, lectures or lessons; assignments and assessment followed suit. One’s education, at least the part experienced in school, became the study of a series of discrete subjects seldom integrated into a coherent life related whole.


The work of Benjamin Bloom in in the mid-twentieth century, resulted in a focus on outcomes, the desired ultimate end product. One model, competency based education recast the model to focus more directly on competencies of learners, a change from what they know to what one is able to do with what one knows. The current decade has seen that movement coalesce around competency based education or CBE. Competency based education differs from other forms of outcomes in its focus on the learner, life roles we play with a rating scale to match. The references list below are indicative of this development.


Definitions

  • A competency is a statement of observable behaviour resulting from the application of knowledge to the solution of a problem or situation.

  • Competency based education (CBE) refers to a system of instruction, assessment, grading, and reporting based on students demonstrating that they have learned the knowledge and skills they are expected to learn as they progress through their education. 

Misconceptions:

  • Students study one skill at a time

  • Competency bsed Education and mastery learning are the same thing


Characteristics of CBE System:

  • High expectations for all students.

  • Help to be focused on each learner

  • Expectations for each learner is clearly identified;

  • Learner progress is based on demonstrated performance

  • A variety of instructional strategies and assessment tools

  • Time and assistance adjusted to support individual learning styles

  • Focus on output not input

  • Progress reported on each outcome

  • Competences are drawn from the community in which the learner will operate


Elements of a CBE system:/Steps

  • An analysis of occupation or field

  • Profiles

  • Assessment criteria

  • Levels

  • Benchmarks

  • Reporting


Benefits of CBE: -

  • Consistency - in course offerings across the educational system

  • Accountability - expectations for learning are clearly stated, and frequent assessment processes help both teacher and student identify progress toward meeting the outcomes

  • Accessibility - clearly defined outcomes enable learners to demonstrate achievement of those outcomes through prior learning assessment processes

  • Changed the focus of education from content to the learner

  • Accommodate individual learning styles including challenge for credit

  • Deeper levels of learning
