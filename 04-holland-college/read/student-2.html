A STUDENT’S  RESPONSIBILITY IS TO LEARN



Learning is something that we do; it is not something done to us.  We determine what we learn from each new encounter in life.  Granted that many times we do not learn what a teacher intended and even the wrong kind of learning is reinforced, nevertheless, as we mature we have a responsibility to sort out what is happening and take steps to learn that which will be most interesting or useful to us.  Blaming others for our unsuccessful learning has, or should have, its limits.


Learning new information and new skills is easy; even unlearning old information or skills is relatively easy.  It is the unlearning of old ways of thinking and acting, however, that is the most difficult - and this is especially true of the things that we learn in childhood.  A psychology professor of mine taught that our characteristics are set by the time we start school.  And it is this learning that is difficult to change, although I should add, not impossible.


In my teaching days, I likened learning to building our house.  There are many techniques and materials available to us.  We need to choose carefully which ones to use.


The story is told of a builder who decided to take a vacation.  Before doing so, he called his foreman and told him of his plans but asking him to continue with the latest house being planned.  The builder departed and construction began. 


In the absence of the builder, the foreman decided on some savings.  He reduced the size of the footings, used a lower quality concrete, put in less insulation, used lighter shingles, etc.  But the house got built.


When the builder returned, he called his foreman.  He said, “My wife and I decided to close our business.  Life has been good to us and we want to do some other things in our remaining years.  Over the years, you have been both a good worker and a good friend.  As a thank you, this latest house is yours.”


As students, we are building our own houses.  They are houses we will live in for the rest of our lives.  Yes, they can be renovated but it is so much easier to put in quality materials as you build.
