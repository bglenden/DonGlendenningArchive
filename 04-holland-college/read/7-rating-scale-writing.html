A six-point rating scale for evaluating written work, ranging from 1 (disorganized with many errors) to 6 (creatively written with acceptable standards). Emphasizes grammar, sentence structure, organization, and vocabulary. Scale level 5 includes precise vocabulary and some creativity, while level 4 focuses on organization with few errors. This practical assessment tool reflects educational standards of the 1990s and likely informed Holland College's writing competency framework.

RATING SCALE FOR WRITING

6 Creatively written while observing acceptable
standards of grammar, sentence structure and
organization. 

5 Very well organized showing precise and wide
vocabulary, good sentence structure and appropriate
grammar and some creativity. 

4 Organized with few errors in grammar and sentence
structure. 

3 Organized with acceptable sentence structure,
grammar and vocabulary. 

2 Generally organized but uses limited vocabulary and
has significant errors in grammar and sentence
structure. 

1 Disorganized and unfocused with many errors in
grammar and sentence structure accompanied by a
limited vocabulary. 

Information House
