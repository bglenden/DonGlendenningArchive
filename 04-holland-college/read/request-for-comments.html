I’m writing a brief history of St. Paul’s, Presbyterian Church in Black River and would like to hear from you.  Tell us something that you remember – a word or two or a few paragraphs.  Send by email, by mail or even phone.  Pass the request along to others.  Send one or a number; I’ll try to include them all.

MEMORIES


The writer has many fond memories of St. Paul’s Presbyterian Church and decided to share some of his memories as part of the record.  A request was sent to others for their recollections as well.  Comments received are presented in random order below.


I remember:


  • The hissing of the gas lamps.  There were three, two double lamps and one single lamp over the choir stand.  As dusk began to settle, Uncle Jim, or someone, would light the lamps.  They used naptha gas and were pumped up thus causing the hissing.  (Don Glendenning)
  • Oyster suppers (Don Glendenning)
  • Gordon Adams’ red car; the first  red car I ever say.  When someone asked him about it, Gordon said “It was the style in the States”.  (Don Glendenning)
