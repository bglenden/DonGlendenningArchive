Describes an "Invitational Lecture Series on Education" proposal to profile and recognize innovative individuals and organizations in PEI education. The initiative features no monetary awards but offers public recognition through presentations, membership in the PEI Educational Innovators Society, and documentation in occasional papers. Examples of innovators include Holland College advisors, Grace Christian School, and community learning centers.


Prog/Prop/5

Invitational Lecture Series

On

Education



Background:

  • Promotes innovation

  • Recognizes individuals

  • Builds a cadre of innovators on PEI


Proposal: To profile Island individuals and organizations who demonstrate innovation

practices in education.


Characteristics:

  • Recognition not an award

  • No monetary value

  • Innovators will be invited to make a public presentation

  • Those selected will automatically become members of the PEI Educational Innovators Society

  • Independent selection panel

  • Participants not ranked

  • Innovation to be documented in an occasional paper

  • Five selected each year


Selected Innovators will:

  • Make public presentation(s)

  • Have their innovation publicized

  • Be the subject of an occasional paper

  • Become member of the PEI Educational Innovation Society


Examples:

  • Education advisors role on the ABE program at Holland College

  • Computer application at Grace Christian School

  • Tammy Little

  • Summerside Rotary for Community Learning Centre

  • Maureen Arsenault at the Centre Goeland, Wellington
