Comprehensive proposal by Don Glendenning for establishing a CFPEI Education Improvement Fund to address systemic education challenges. Document analyzes criticisms of education (accountability, cost, relevance, standards, flexibility) and identifies priority areas for improvement. Proposes endowment-based grantmaking to support education reform projects while distinguishing foundation's role from direct project implementation. Details fund governance through advisory committee and competitive grant process. Demonstrates Don's deep commitment to education quality and strategic philanthropy, likely connecting to his 18 years as Holland College's first president.

Draft May 10, 2003





COMMUNITY FOUNDATION OF PRINCE EDWARD ISLAND


EDUCATION IMPROVEMENT FUND


BACKGROUND:


Education is under considerable scrutiny and pressure to reform. Much of the criticism revolves around accountability, cost, relevance, standards and flexibility.  Some of the criticism is valid; some is not.


Provincial governments across the country have responded in a number of ways including:


restructuring school boards

lengthening the school day

encouraging charter schools

increasing teacher qualifications

specifying learning outcomes

introducing external examinations

providing teacher aides, and

adding computers.


These changes will help but other changes are also needed.


It is generally recognized that the usual components of the education system are elementary, secondary and post secondary institutions, the Department of Education, School Boards, private schools and distance delivery arrangements.  Educational improvement may be impacted, however, by such external forces or bodies as teacher certification requirements, trade and professional associations, employers, labour groups, trustees, parent advisory groups and student councils.  All stakeholders must be considered as part of the improvement process.


Some of the desired improvements may require action by more than one component of the delivery system.  An additional or different change agent may be helpful.  An Educational Improvement Fund within the Community Foundation of Prince Edward Island will make a valuable contribution to the change process.


THE FOUNDATION


The Community foundation of Prince Edward Island is a general purpose, non-government, non-profit organization established under the laws of Prince Edward Island.  It is also registered as a charitable organization with Revenue Canada.  The foundation’s mission is to meet community needs by providing a vehicle for many different donors to exercise their varied charitable interests an a minimum of cost and a maximum convenience.  Gifts to the Foundation will be invested in a common pool with the earnings distributed in the form of grants to a wide variety of non-profit groups throughout Prince Edward Island.


PURPOSE


The purpose of the proposal is to seek funds to stimulate, encourage and support improvements in public education.  The object is to establish an Education Improvement Fund in the form of an endowment.  The fund will exist in perpetuity, only the earnings will be used to support educational improvement. 


CONCEPT


Our goal is to establish an educational improvement Fund for the purpose of encouraging and supporting improvements in public education in Prince Edward Island.  Once established, the availability of project funds will be announced and proposals will be invited.  The Foundation may initiate discussions with one or more organizations to stimulate proposals in areas of interest to the foundation or where it is thought beneficial to have a number of stakeholders involved.  An advisory committee will be convened to advise the foundation on such matters.  Details of project criteria, communications strategy and granting procedures are yet to be worked out.  The Foundation itself, will not undertake projects.  Project reports will be received by the foundation.



PRIORITIES


The Foundation has identified the following as priority areas for educational improvement:

Accountability in education is widely dispersed among teachers, principals, School Board staff and the Department of Education.  This may result in good teaching being inadequately recognized and rewarded and poor teaching condoned.

Costs continue to rise.  The delivery model allows little opportunity and few rewards for containing or reducing costs.

Relevance of some programming to later life is improving but still a challenge and is often sacrificed to the requirements of higher education.

Standards within the system bear little relationship to those encountered in life outside the education system.  Alternatives should be explored.

Flexibility has been lost in a system of rigid class schedules, a mammoth school transportation system, negotiated student-teacher ratios, regulated school days and years, and prescribed course outlines and school texts.  



The Foundation recognizes also that priorities for educational improvement will require periodic review and may be amended accordingly.  It is likely, furthermore, that educational improvement will benefit from partnerships within the education system as well as with agencies from outside.  Such partnerships can only be dealt with on a project by project basis.


FUND GUIDELINES


While many of the improvements in education may be undertaken inexpensively, some of the major interventions may require several years in order to make the necessary changes and to integrate them into the on-going education system.  


Some preliminary guidelines include:


grants are intended to impact on priorities identified above,

grants will not be made in support of an individual student,

applicants are expected to show how the results of the project will be incorporated into on-going activities,

grants will not be made for ongoing operating expenses,

grants will not be made to remove a deficit,

the decision to fund or not fund a project rests with the Foundation, 

the Foundation may seek outside advice when reviewing proposals,

the Foundation may encourage the submission of proposals which it feels will contribute to educational improvement, 

The foundation may work with an applicant to develop a proposal before a project is improved.


The goal of the Foundation is an Educational Improvement Fund in the form of an endowment.  The Fund will exist in perpetuity; only the income will be used to support educational improvement.
