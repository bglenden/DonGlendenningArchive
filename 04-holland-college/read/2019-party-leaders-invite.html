Gentlemen:

An invitation


We are pleased to invite you, as leader of your Party, along with leaders of other official Parties, to a conversation on the matter of public education on Prince Edward Island.  We extend this invitation because of the importance of the subject and the key role played by the Provincial Government in bringing about change.  


The conversation, we suggest, should focus on a framework for organizing and managing educational services in the 21st century.  While open to all suggestions, we are thinking of starting with a review of education functions or services and alternative approaches to organizing them.  We will also review suggestions for improving an education system emanating from the OECD, UNESCO and UNICEF. You may also be interested in a learning model developed in consultation with Island students, the results of a study of continuing education in Souris, and some exploratory work in mathematics education. Given the subject, it would seem appropriate, also, to also invite the Minister of Education. 


While you know me as Founding President of Holland College, my experience includes a teacher and principal in the public school system of New Brunswick, head of a teacher education program serving technical and vocational education for the Atlantic Provinces, a Training officer with the Federal Department of Labour, operator of my own consulting firm undertaking projects in Canada and abroad, and representing Canada at a couple of OECD meetings in Paris.


We know that you have full schedules and are happy to juggle dates and times until we find a time that best suits. In the meantime, however, let me suggest we meet from 3:30 – 5:00 pm on Wednesday, August 29 in the second floor Board Room of the Haviland Club, at 2 Haviland Street, Charlottetown. 


We look forward to your response and, hopefully, to a stimulating conversation. 


Sincerely



Don Glendenning
