This draft constitution for Glenway Estates Association Inc. was written by Don Glendenning to establish governance structure for a cottage development purchased in 1970 by the MacCuaig, Glendenning, and Galloway families. Don proposes amendments to clarify membership, director authority, and decision-making processes, advocating for greater consultative governance. The document demonstrates Don's continued civic engagement in community organizations and attention to proper governance principles even in retirement.

Draft 1


GLENWAY ESTATES ASSOCIATION INC.



BACKGROUND     


The land for the summer cottage development known as Glenway was purchased in 1970 by the MacCuaig, Glendenning and Galloway families with the first lot being sold and cottages erected by 1971.  During the first 25 years, road maintenance was provided by the original owners with contributions from lot owners.  By 2006, waterfront lots were individually owned and, in 2008, the centre section was subdivided into separate and individually owned lots.


By 2006, lot owners entered into discussions about establishing a Glenway owners association.  The organization was brought into being through a process of discussion/participation by all residents (at least all were invited), over a period of weeks if not months.  Those involved in the “birthing” process, and that included most residents, expressed an interest in retaining the consultative model in dealing with Association business.  A committee was struck, a constitution and by laws were approved and the Glenway Estates Association, Inc. became a legal entity.


In 2008, the Association was presented with a request that, although simple, was contentious.  As a result, much of the discussion was not about the issue itself but rather about how the issue was handled and who could vote. As a result, and on reflection, the writer is of the view that our current constitution and bylaws interfere with the consultative process rather than fosters it; furthermore, the writer believes a broader consultative process should be restored and reinforced.  To do this, changes in, or interpretations of, the constitution and by laws will be required.

Conceptually, in a membership-based non-profit organization a Board of Directors is a smaller unit of governance than the full membership. As such it can act on behalf of the full membership, but only in a temporary way as its actions must be ratified or negated by the full membership. Typically, the Board consists of the Officers of the Association and a relatively small number of at-large members.


    However, in the construction of our Board of Directors, we left the door open to anyone who was a member to self-select as Board members. To reflect the limited authority of the Board, it was determined that board membership would be tied to lot ownership, but with no one board member having more than one vote. As it stands any lot owner can declare to being a member of the Board of Directors; if 2 or more persons share ownership in one lot, their Board membership is 1/2 each. If the 2 own more than one lot, both owners can be Board members, if they so declare.

Decision Making


The current constitution assigns decision-making as follows:


Members – set and collect fees, elect the Board, 



Officers – President, vice-president, Secretary and Treasurer; duties identified in bylaws;  


Directors – manage the affairs of the association; elect officers; call meetings, exercise (7.02)  “all powers, duties and authority vested in or delegated to the Association, except those reserved to the members.”; maintain complete records; supervise all officers, agents and employees; choose officers; fill vacancies among directors until the next annual meeting; 


Annual meeting – confirm minutes; approve banking accounts and financial statements; deal with resolutions; confirm nominations of Directors; confirm capital expenditures; consider other business; 


Article 10.05 all capital expenditures shall be approved by the membership










PROPOSED CHANGES


The writer proposes that the Bylaws be amended to:


Give full authority to the membership to carry out the purposes and objects of the association:

Delegate such authority to the Directors as it deems appropriate


Amend 7:01 (ii) to read: 

To exercise for the Association all powers, duties and authority specifically delegated by the members including that of:


presenting a slate of directors/Officers to the annual meeting, 

presenting an agenda for the annual meeting ,

implementing any decisions taken at meetings of the Association, 

overseeing any committees created by the membership ( such as road maintenance, beach access maintenance, etc).

Approving a budget including annual fees

Recommending a plan of work for the new year

Exercising such duties, responsibilities as my be delegated to it by the membership of the Association 



Amend Article 10.03 as follows:
