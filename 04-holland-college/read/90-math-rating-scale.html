UNIVERSAL RATING SCALE

A few weeks ago, I circulated a Citizen Math Map; skills were identified by a panel of Islanders with the help of a skilled facilitator. Panelists were asked about skills used in everyday life; this is what they told us.  The list consists of some 74 skills under the following headings:

  • Estimate and Calculate with Whole Numbers
  • Use Measurement
  • Use Fractions, Decimals, Percentages Ratios and Rates
  • Use Spatial Reasoning
  • Recognize and use patterns and relationships
  • Interpret Statistical Information

In our review of the literature, we noted that rating scales generally were adopting criteria of a kind that applies to life generally. We convened   a second panel and they told us that they assess community skills based on: 

  • Accuracy or quality
  • Assistance needed 
  • Speed- time to complete task, 
  • Results were consistent across tasks attempted

Although still a work in progress, I decided to send it along at this time.  Our exploration reveals a spreading adoption of universal rating scales, similar to this, usually coupled with a learner self- assessment.
