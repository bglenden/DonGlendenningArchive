Don's tribute to Bob Adams, a Nova Scotia colleague who introduced Holland College to DACUM (Developing A Curriculum) methodology during its foundational year. Don recounts Holland College's initial traditional delivery model and deliberate search for better alternatives through institution visits and innovation research. He credits DACUM's simplicity, analytical rigor, single-page competency presentation, and rating system as transformative to the college's mission, and notes its international adoption, though sometimes diverging from Adams' original vision.
Holland College & Institutional Legacy


Dear Editor:


A friend of Holland College


Yesterday I learned that a former colleague had died; he was living in Dartmouth, Nova Scotia.  Few readers would have met him but many readers would have felt his impact.  You see, Bob Adams introduced Holland College to DACUM charts.  Employers may remember the three- day brainstorming sessions; students will likely remember the large sheet identifying the competencies of their chosen career.  


Although Holland College operated on a traditional delivery model during our first year, we actively searched for something better.  Step one was the development of a statement of beliefs; step 2 was a search for a delivery model to match.  We visited a number of institutions and gathered information about other innovative projects both in Canada and the US, and then one day, we visited the Nova NewStart Corporation in Yarmouth, Nova Scotia, talked with Bob Adams and observed the DACUM model in use.  


DACUM was a great match; it had many of the elements Holland College was looking for; in particular we were impressed with the simplicity of the model, the analysis process, the single page presentation, and the rating system.   Since then DACUM has gone international; unfortunately, not always as Bob envisaged it.

 

I write this note to express my sympathy to Bob’s wife, Coleen, and family, to recognize his contribution to Holland College and to call attention to DACUM, a great Canadian achievement.

 

Sincerely


Don Glendenning
