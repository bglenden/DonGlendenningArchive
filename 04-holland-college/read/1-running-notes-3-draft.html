EDUCATION 20/20 INC.

January 30, 2015

Mathematics Gap

NOTE: These running notes focus on concerns of Island youth about their preparedness for employment and will evolve as our thinking evolves.

Background

It is widely recognized that education is a key factor in determining one’s quality of life; in the same way, the collective education of our citizens will determine to a significant degree the future of our community and country. One of the most frequently mentioned concerns by Islanders is the low literacy levels among adults as reported via PISA results, recent CFPEI survey of Island youth, presentation to the Standing Committee by the Charlottetown Area Chamber of Commerce, a 2014 survey by survey by the PEI Home and School Federation and a series of workshops on Preparedness held throughout PEI by Education 20/20 Inc. The most frequently mentioned concern among Islanders, and especially among employers, is the low level of basic skills, especially mathematics, communication and personal management skills. Education 20/20 held a series of workshops across PEI on the results of the CFPEI survey; the Mathematics Gap initiative is a further step in our exploration of math skills needed to function in a modern economy. For the purpose of these notes I refer to math skills although others refer to numeracy, essential skills, math outcomes and math competencies.


Meeting 1;

A working group (Duffy, Miller, White-Hassler, Greyborn and Glendenning) met on Friday, January 9, 2015 to explore the issue. Our exploration was framed in terms of a labour market mismatch between skills of available labour and skill needed. While the issue, appears to be one of a training gap, it is equally possible that the issue may be one of the education mix, timely delivery, employers specifications or institutional documentation.

Recent activities:

Since the meeting I’ve:

  • Reviewed Essential skills documents

  • Gathered copies of PEI Specific Curriculum Math Outlines, one for Grade 9 and another for Grade 10;

  • Retrieved listing of Grade 9 math competencies from the Quebec Department of Education.

  • Transcribed a list of “Practical Mathematics Competencies” from an old Holland College document dated 1978; this, I believe, can be the basis of our employer survey

  • Met with Lori Johnston, Manager, Workplace Learning PEI, an organization that works directly with individuals and employers in assessing training needs, training workers and helping employers develop a capacity to offer training to their employees. Lori has agreed to make a presentation to our group probably during the second week of March.

  • Confirmed that neither the Charlottetown or Summerside Chamber has surveyed math needs of employers.

  • Identified a person with experience in identifying competencies with a view to finalizing an instrument for an employer survey
