We shouldn’t treat teachers as interchangeable widgets on a production line - Anon


A MISNOMER

Practically every day an article comes to my attention about the importance of math in our daily lives and the gap between what students learn and what they need; I also hear it regularly from grandparents and employers. Improvement in pan-Canadian and international tests do not seem to correlate with local views. The gap is referred to as a math problem; I see it as an education problem.


Math is a tool; all of us need to learn how to use it. I liken it to driving a car; we need to know a bit about cars but the real test is to integrate the automobile into our every day activities. So it is with math. Issues seem to be ones of:

  • Relevance to every day living

  • Transparency in reporting

  • Benchmarks

  • Fluency or practice

  • Lack of consultation and communication


If the public perception is not accurate, the Department should inform us of the facts; if perceptions are accurate, steps should be taken to improve instruction.
