Challenge for Credit


Learning takes place everywhere but credit for what has been learned is limited to what is studied in schools. Education 20/20 believes learning outside schools is also worthy of credit and urges schools to be more flexible in how credits are granted.


That was the nub of a presentation by Education 20/20 to the English Language School Board at its last regular meeting. As part of the presentation, the Board was asked to put in place a formal evaluation process whereby students can obtain course credit towards their high school diplomas based on a challenge process. If the recommendation is accepted, the granting of exemptions will be reinstated and students be allowed to challenge courses for credit whether or not they have taken the course.


Most schools, colleges and universities give credit for equivalent courses taken elsewhere. The latest examples in PEI are the granting of school credits for learning organized by the Dance Umbrella and the Royal Canadian Naval Reserve; that brings the number of external agencies providing up to eight. It’s a trend we can expect will continue.

PLAR (Prior Learning Assessment Recognition) is an approach to granting credit for learning accomplished outside school. People seeking PLAR credit prepare a portfolio that describes their learning experiences and the learning process; institutions assess the portfolio and determine credit. Most postsecondary institutions including Holland College, UPEI and College Acadie I.P.E offer credit through PLAR; Education 20/20 would like to see this service available to high school students as well


A copy of the presentation is available on the organization’s website , www.education2020.ca
