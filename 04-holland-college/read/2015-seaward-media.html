A bridge to new careers

People who lack high school graduation credits or want to upgrade existing school marks may do so through basic education courses offered in a number of centres across Prince Edward Island; the public can learn more about one such opportunity at a session being organized by Education 20/20 at the Stratford Town Hall on September30.

Gaps in readiness for post- secondary study and the workplace were revealed in a survey of young Islanders in late 2013 by The Community Foundation of Prince Edward Island. They arise from a number of causes, most frequently, it seems, from students dropping out of high school before they graduate, not taking or not passing courses demanded by postsecondary institutions, lack of information about entrance requirements or changing institutional requirements. Lack of preparedness may block otherwise promising careers.

One such program is funded by the Department of Workforce Development and Higher Learning and offered on a full and part-time basis by Holland College in several centres across the Island. Gerry Seaward, a long time adult educator and currently Program Manager of Adult and Community Education, Prince of Wales Campus, Holland College will tell about the program, who takes it, what’s involved and the results.

The session will be held in the Southport Room of the Stratford Town Hall on Wednesday, September 30 starting at 4:30 pm, and is open to the public. For more information, check the Education 2020 website, at www.education2020.ca
