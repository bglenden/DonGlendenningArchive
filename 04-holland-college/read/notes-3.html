DACUM: A CANADIAN ACHIEVEMENT

Notes-3


Discussion at Haviland Club on Wednesday, March 15, 2017: Larry, Jack, Mike and Don; Missed you, Deborah:


  • Don shared two letters from Lance Hogan, Eastern Illinois University and mentioned that he would be joining a conference call with CVA Exec on Thursday
  • Agreed that, Ideally, we would like to have both CVA and Hogan as part of our activities
  • Confirmed that HC first developed a set of guiding principles and then went looking for a model to support its thinking; included in our search was POET (Performance Oriented Electronics Training at CFB, Trenton?); Technical Education Research Centre, Cambridge University. (one analysis was Biomedical equipment; the other may have been mechanical technician); DACUM; A new college in Michigan – cannot recall name; the work in the Bedford Stuyvesant Borough of New York
  • Agreed that any program should include early years of analysis (Apprenticeship, instructional objectives, programmed learning); DACUM; current scene.
  • Possible participants, as resource persons or as participants include certification bodies, HR people, colleges, vocational instructors of high school programs, employers, 


Notes on conference call with CVA Executive on March 16:

  • Pierre Morin, Wayne Wilson, Anna Kays and one other
  • Wayne commented about how active/well received is the CVA website and monthly newsletter
  • I updated them on Bob’s passing, the book he coauthored, the interest of Eastern Illinois university, that Bob’s files had gone to the USA
  • Briefed them on our thinking; I mentioned the phases as early models, advent and impact of DACUM, and the international spread of the analyses process,
  • Their main questions were about the HC reaction, tentative date, Canada 150 money, whether this would end with a book or report.
  • They spoke about using their website and newsletter to promote
  • Spoke about my search of the internet for spots where the model is practiced; I assured them that we’d be happy to have CVA participate and would keep them informed.
  • I expect that Pierre will respond; I am not expecting CVA to propose a joint initiative but maybe offer something by way of support


Conclusions:

  • I expect that we will hear from Pierre promptly
  • Think that we should continue our planning on a small-scale program
  • Would welcome your views
  • Intend to try to capture the ideas generated and will be back to you soon
