Background

To some degree, every educational institution and training organization in PEI teaches math.; most base curriculum on a form of outcomes (Outcomes Based Education Curriculum Based Outcomes and Competency Based Education). All agencies seem to undertake the analysis process as though what they are doing is unique to them. I’ve looked at a number of outcomes and find that the differences are mostly one of level of specificity.


Furthermore, my exploration reveals that the differences in educational achievement rests primarily on two factors, (1) the range of competencies a person can demonstrate and (2) the level that each person can demonstrate on each competency. Based on this view, it should be possible to establish a comprehensive list of competencies that span all grade levels and age groups. Students either select or are assigned the competency to be learned; the difference between any two learners in any competency is only the level of achievement that can be demonstrated. A teacher’s role is to help learners select the competencies needed and to raise the learner’s level of achievement on each.


I have had a number of conversations about math with representatives of UPEI, Holland College, Department of Education, employers and community members and, while not saying that there is support for this specific proposal, there is interest in some quarters for further exploration. Based on the above, we believe that PEI should develop, and would benefit from, a master list of competencies and a rating scale to match. The idea is not new, seems to be widely practices in Ontario and elsewhere. Some countries try to accomplish through the same feature the establishment of “qualification authorities”,


Developing such a map, we believe, would assist in improving quality education, reducing costs, including communication, easing transition, securing an even better range of study resources and cast us in a leadership role in the field, guidance tool and tracking student progress


Proposal: That Education 20/20 take a lead role in developing a map of the competencies in the field of math and that we do so in a manner that supports Island needs.
