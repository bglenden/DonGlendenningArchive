October 1, 2018



To Whom it May Concern:


Howard (Howie) Jamieson


I am pleased to support the nomination of Howie Jamison for an Honourary Degree from the University of Prince Edward Island. 


While I am aware of his efforts in support of a single university, his leadership in student affairs at UPEI and his identification as a “comer” in his work with the PEI Government, I will focus my comments on the critical role he played in the shaping of Holland College.


In 1976 the Government of Prince Edward Island decided to transfer responsibility for adult education to Holland College; the transfer included the Provincial. Vocational Institute, Prince County Vocational High School, Marine Training Institute, the Handcraft Training Centre and all educational upgrading – staff, facilities, labour relations, programming and all such support services.  It was a substantial change in reporting lines, operating procedures and even philosophy. Howie’s; expertise, his knowledge of the education scene, his personal experience and skills were central to the transfer and redevelopment of a well-functioning organization.



Howard understood the task at hand and was trusted throughout the organization, He was open, honest, efficient, available and effective in his work.  In addition, he maintained strong linkages with the community and was respected for his personal capabilities and integrity.  In addition, he was aa great supporter of the University and an ambassador for the University in the Island community. An honourary degree to a recognee of your first graduation class would be a fitting way to mark a fiftieth anniversary.


It is without any hesitation that I commend Howard Jamison as a worthy candidate for this award.


Sincerely



Donald Glendenning, C.M.

President emeritus

Holland College
