Holland College & Institutional Legacy

Dear Editor:

School autonomy

I was asked recently about the lack of autonomy in our school system. 

As readers may know, among community colleges, Holland College was known for the degree of autonomy in operational matters.  That was a deliberate action taken by the College Planning Committee during the drafting of the Holland College Act. A similar level of autonomy should be given to public schools.

The OECD, who administers PISA tests, tells us that students from schools with autonomy in their day to day operations, do better on PISA tests.  Furthermore, a paper by Mathieu Bedard, Montreal Economic Institute also recommends increased school autonomy and accountability. (Bedard, Mathieu,  Montreal Economic Institute, 2015)

My only caution about such a move is to ensure that schools have access to current educational research in order to guide them in their decision making (but that is the subject of a separate letter)

Sincerely

Don Glendenning

Nov 26, 2023
