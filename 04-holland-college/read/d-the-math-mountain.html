Using mountain climbing as an extended metaphor, Don argues that mathematical learning should not be described or assessed by student age. Just as mountain climbers progress at individual rates with appropriate guides and base camps, math learners need varied pacing and support structures. He questions whether we can identify root causes of post-secondary rejection or employment barriers, suggesting deficiencies might stem from age-based rather than competency-based educational approaches. The letter invites reader perspectives on this fundamental educational design question.

July 10, 2014


Editor, The Guardian
