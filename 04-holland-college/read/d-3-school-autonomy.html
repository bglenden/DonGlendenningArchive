Don draws from his Holland College experience to argue that schools should have autonomy similar to what made community colleges successful. He cites OECD/PISA research showing that schools with greater autonomy over curriculum and assessment, combined with public accountability measures, achieve better student performance. This letter is brief but significant, applying his institutional knowledge to current policy debates and supporting evidence-based educational reform.

Dear Editor: 

AUTONOMY AND ACCOUNTABILITY


One of the hallmarks of Holland College among community colleges in Canada was its degree of autonomy in the day-to-day operation; the writer is of the view that a similar level of autonomy and accountability should be extended to public schools.  The OECD, who gives us PISA  agrees.

According to the OECD

  • In countries where schools have greater autonomy over what is taught and how students are assessed, students tend to perform better.”
  • in countries were schools account for their results by posting achievement data publicly, schools that enjoy greater autonomy in resource location tend to show better student performance than those with less autonomy. 
  • In countries where there are no such accountability arrangements, schools with greater autonomy in resource allocation tend to perform worse.       


Seems to me that items like this should appear n our education agenda.   



Sincerely

Don Glendenning
