Fundraising letter to Roger Birt requesting financial support ($1,000-$2,000) for a numeracy initiative. Despite rising PEI math test scores, Don notes that practical shop-floor math skills remain unchanged, indicating a gap between testing and real-world application. He proposes conducting a two-day brainstorming analysis of Islanders' actual math needs, drawing on his experience with similar techniques at Holland College. The analysis costs $15,000 and would inform development of numeracy programs addressing practical application of mathematics skills.


February 20, 2019

Mr. Roger Birt, President

R. Birt and Associates

27 Brackley Point Road

Charlottetown, PE C1A 6Y1

1-1-2


Dear

Math on PEI


Although PEI math scores on National and international tests have risen, friends tell me that math has not improved on the shop floor; I’ve been wondering why.


Islanders need math; citizens may not need a high level but they need to be able to measure, sort, calculate, group and estimate numbers, space, time distance, etc. and they need to do it quickly, easily, accurately; and consistently. This approach to math is called numeracy and it involves the application of math to every-day lives. Numeracy is being adopted elsewhere; usually in addition to traditional math.


But we need more, to begin with we need an analysis of math skills used by Islanders. Making an analysis involves bringing together a small group for a two- day of brainstorming session. It’s a proven technique, we used it regularly at Holland College but apparently there is no one on PEI now with the expertise. We’ll have to bring someone in at a cost $15,000. The making of an analysis is described in the attached paper.


I am approaching a few people who might contribute $1-2,000 each to help cover the cost. The Community Foundation, 121 Queen Street, Charlottetown, PE, C1A 4B3 will accept donations on our behalf and issue charitable receipts.


I appreciate your consideration and would be happy to explain further and answer any questions. Thanks in advance


Sincerely,



Don Glendenning

1-1-num
