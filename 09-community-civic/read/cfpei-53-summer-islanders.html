Published in October 2006, this newsletter describes CFPEI's initiative to establish a Summer Islanders Legacy Fund targeting seasonal residents from Canada and the USA who return annually to PEI. The foundation promoted this with a July 4 reception for US citizens and an August advertisement. The Summer Islanders Fund supports landscape, arts, heritage, genealogy, health, environment, and education initiatives. The newsletter outlines plans for 2007 including a second US Citizens' Day Reception (led by Garth Staples), a summer social, and summer lecture series. This reflects Don's vision for leveraging non-resident wealth to support PEI community development and his inclusive approach to philanthropic engagement.

Community Foundation News

No. 53 October 6, 2006

Summer Islanders Activities


In discussing our mission, to improve the quality of life on Prince Edward Island, we noted that PEI provides a summer home for many people whose full-time residence is elsewhere.


Summer Islanders are visitors who spend time on the Island on a regular basis and who think that PEI is a special corner of the world. They may come from any corner of the globe but our contacts so far have been from Canada and the USA. All share an interest in supporting initiatives that help to maintain the Island as a place to which Summer Islanders want to return year after year.


Our first initiative was a reception on July 4, 2006, for US citizens. The purpose was to welcome and thank them for their many contributions to our communities and events. The event was reported in Issue No. 48 and, for those who missed it, is available on our website.


In August CFPEI placed an advertisement in the local paper to alert summer Islanders to the initiatives being taken.


As a result, I am pleased to report that a Summer Islanders PEI Legacy Fund, within the Community Foundation of Prince Edward Island, is now in place and available to persons wishing to contribute. (The Foundation is a registered as a charitable organization in both Canada and the USA and can issue receipts acceptable to tax authorities in both countries). Grants from the earnings will be made on the advice of a Committee of Summer Islanders and may be used to support a wide variety of interests including the landscape, arts, heritage, genealogy, Health, environment and education


In addition, plans for the summer of 2007 include:

  • The second Annual US Citizens’ Day Reception on July 4. Last year’s committee under the able Chairmanship of Garth Staples has agreed to organize the event for next year and probably at a new venue.


  • A committee is now in place and busily planning a summer social in August. Date and venue to be announced, and


  • Another Committee is busily planning to organize summer lectures. Topics and venues yet to be determined.


.


Logistical support for all summer activities is being provided by the Foundation.
