February 6, 2014

Windsor Foundation


Over the past few months, I’ve been working with a small group of Islanders who believe that education is important, that it needs some changes and that the key to change is an informed and engaged public. We are also of the view that the school system is unlikely to make the necessary changes by itself so, we’ve established an independent group, Education 20/20. (Mission statement attached). Our initial strategy is three pronged, independent information, engagement and incentives with the objective of encouraging changes that will improve the system for all.


  • Independent information: Most of our information about education comes from the people who operate the system; to address the shortage and lack of independence, Education 20/20 Inc. currently produce a monthly electronic newsletter; in addition, our website has also just gone live and we plan to use it as an information hub for surveys, announcements, occasional papers, social media, etc. (www.education2020.ca)


  • Engagement: Having information is one thing but understanding and internalizing it is another. We’ve chosen as the first step to launch a series of workshops across PEI, the first on was held on January 20 in the Stratford Town Hall, was well attended and generated significant discussion during and following the workshop. Repeat workshops are scheduled for three other communities and planned for others. Our thinking is to encourage discussions on educational issues of all kinds in many Island communities. (As one participant pointed out, the very fact that an independent organization convened such a meeting is a breakthrough)


  • Awards and incentives: The education sector lack incentives – students cannot finish early; there is little encouragement to innovate, principals have little autonomy to make local adjustments; each school is a cookie cutter of all other schools, and parents have little choice in choosing a school and none in choosing a teacher. As an incentive to greater engagement, and thanks to a generous donor, Education 20/20 will soon announce an educational award for a school or learning centre that best demonstrates engagement in its everyday activities; our plans include both offering the award to schools in neighbouring provinces and identifying ways in which incentives may be used to encourage improvement in other areas such as literacy and mathematics.


We realize that we are not alone in having ideas about the improvement of education and would be delighted to explore with you other ideas for improving education for our citizens.




We cannot make this happen alone; funds are needed firstly to continue and improve the initiatives already under way but also to support new initiatives. While we welcome support in any amount and for and all initiatives, we feel that incentives can make a particular contribution to the improvement process. In saying this, I am thinking of such things as math clubs for students at all levels of education, professional development for teachers from all levels of education, system to work together, creativity workshops, student competitions, and generally support innovative thinking within our education system.





Consequently, we are looking for partners and supporters. While we will be grateful for any support, we would be e; our short term goal for the next 18 months in support of these initiatives is $22,000 broken out as follows:


  • Gathering and disseminating information $15,000.

  • Promoting and awarding incentives $ 2,000

  • Workshops $ 2,500

  • Operational support $ 2,500

Total $22,000








We invite your support either for one of the initiatives listed above or our activities in general. On the other hand, should you have a different interest, we would be pleased to work with you on that as well. As background, I am enclosing a copy of our mission, a brief backgrounder on the current workshop series and a recent newsletter. Should you have questions or want further information about us and what we do, do not hesitate to let me know. I’ll be back in touch in a few days to see if you have any questions; I would also be pleased to stop by at a mutually convenient time just to discuss education.


The Community Foundation of Prince Edward Island supports our initiative and administers our funds. Contributions to Education 20/20 Inc., therefore, should be directed to the Foundation (Community Foundation of Prince Edward Island, 119 Queen Street, Charlottetown, PE C1A 4B3) who will issue charitable receipts.


Ensuring a quality education for every Canadian requires new thinking, new approaches and new partners. We have ideas but we need your support for ideas to become realities.


Sincerely



Don Glendenning

President
