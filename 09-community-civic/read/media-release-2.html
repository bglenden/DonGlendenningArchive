Follow-up media release inviting applications for the 2017 Excellence in Education Award, expanding on the award's focus on community organizations. Recaps previous award winners and highlights the philosophy that education is a community endeavor. Includes Don's quote about community-led education initiatives, repositioning the award to recognize efforts beyond traditional school settings.


_____________________________________________________________________________


For immediate release



Applications now being received


Applications are now being received by Education 20/20 Inc. for an Excellence in Education award. The 2017 Award will recognize a community organization or agency for its efforts to improve the quality of education for its citizens.


Community groups are invited to send submissions, not over 3 pages in length; projects should be community led, address an actual community education issue, include activities that would be undertaken if resources allowed and be based on best educational practices. Details are available on the Education 20/20 website, www.education2020.ca; nominations close on April 30, 2017.


“It takes a community to educate a citizen,” says Don Glendenning, President of Education 20/20 Inc., “so we thought it time to recognize them for their efforts.” The first two awards focused on engagement and were awarded to Kinkora Regional High School (2014) and Prince Street Elementary School (2015). The 2016 award went to Grace Christian School for the manner and extent to which it demonstrated the application of technology in support of student learning.
