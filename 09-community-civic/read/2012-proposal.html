Formal proposal dated September 24, 2011 from Don Glendenning to CFPEI Board proposing discontinuation of traditional July 4 reception in favor of municipalities organizing their own celebrations. Proposes CFPEI initiate separate "summer islanders" reception on membership basis with potential fees, targeting Island leaders across sports, arts, business, and government sectors. References successful Upson reception as precedent. Rationale emphasizes cost efficiency, goodwill generation, tangible outcomes (one endowment, $15,000 in scholarships), and opportunity to highlight CFPEI's identity separately from July 4 celebrations.

COMMUNITY FOUN DATION OF PRINCE EDWARD ISLAND

Proposed 2012 Strategy



Date: Sept 24, 2011


To: Directors


From: Don Glendenning


Subject: Change of strategy – US Reception


Background:

We successfully hosted six receptions for US summer visitors on July 4 each year

Number varied from l40 to 150

Program usually consisted of music, national anthems and a speaker. The US consul from Halifax, or his representative, attended 3 receptions

The future of the initiative has been the subject of a number of discussions with in the foundation, with Committee members and attendees.



Proposal:

That the July 4 reception hosted by CFPEI be discontinued with municipalities be invited initiate appropriate July 4 events instead

CFPEI initiate a reception for summer Islanders, including US citizen

Attendance be on a membership basis, perhaps with a membership fee

Efforts be made to invite Island leaders in a variety of fields, sports, arts, business, government, etc. as part of the guest list.

Continue our efforts to organize similar events in other communities throughout the Island

The recent Upson reception is one example of such an event


Rationale

Low cost

Generates goodwill

Has resulted in one endowment and $15,000 flow through scholarships

Separating CFPEI event from July 4 celebration will allow us to feature CFPEI
