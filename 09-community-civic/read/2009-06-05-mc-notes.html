Speaking notes prepared by Don Glendenning for his role as Master of Ceremonies at the July 4, 2009 US Citizens Day Reception. Includes welcoming remarks, introduction of honorable guests including Judge Thomas Upson from Connecticut, background on Haviland Club architecture, recognition of Community Foundation directors, and acknowledgment of sponsors and volunteers.
Community Foundation & Civic Life

June 5, 2009


COMMUNITY FOUNDATION OF PRINCE EDWARD ISLAND

US Citizens’ Day Reception, July 4, 2009


Speaking Notes



Intro My name is Don Glendenning

Long time Director of the Community foundation of Prince Edward Island

Also with many close personal connections with your country 

through family, friends and a wife from Illinois

Pleased to be your master of ceremonies today


Welcome To the Fourth US Citizens’ Day Reception

Event organized for two reasons

to celebrate this historic date in your history

thank you for your contributions to our community

You are among friends 

Have a beverage and a snack

And mix and mingle

Enjoy the afternoon


Special welcome to - Long Island Youth Orchestra

Will be playing tonight at Park Royal United Church

We are going to hear from some members in a  few minutes

For more information, including information about tickets

See Joan MacFarlane -– stand and identify herself

I’ll introduce our other guests shortly


Haviland Club An historic building, best example of Italianate style of architecture on PEI

Built 1868-69, residence for Esther Lowden

US Consulate during 1890s 

Have a copy of a letter written from the Consulate on July 25, 1912

Later army and navy Club, United Officers Club Commend for efforts to maintain in such beautiful condition



National Anthems:


Michael van der Gaag to lead us in the national anthems

Accompanied by Glen MacEacheren 





CFPEI is pleased to be your host

Community foundation, one of 160 in Canada

You may be familiar with one near you

Show of hands for visitors who are associated with a community foundation back home

Charitable organization, focused on any an all needs of a community

Interested in the welfare of the Province whether it be education, the arts, heritage  

Our motto is “connecting people who care with causes that matter”


As I said earlier, we come together today

to celebrate this historic date in your history, and 

thank you for your many contributions to our community


You contribute to our community by being here

Many of you also support our theatres, attend our churches, visit art galleries, dine in our restaurants, contribute to special events.  

Some of you support the Community Foundation – and we thank you for that also.
