December 25, 2015 letter from Don Glendenning to Premier Wade MacLauchlan inviting him as guest to "Most Likely to Succeed" screening (January 17, 2016). Explains the film showcases High Tech High's project-based approach emphasizing relational skills over content mastery. Don positions the film as relevant to PEI's education system and extends formal invitation. Shows Don's willingness to engage government leadership directly in educational discourse, and his strategic choice to position film screenings as tools for civic dialogue with decision-makers.



December 25, 2015

Premier Wade MacLauchlan

PO Box 2000

Charlottetown, PE

C1A 7N8


Dear Premier:


As you know, our group has been promoting changes in education. Recently, both the New York Times and Washington Post carried accounts of a new film about education being unrolled across the US; both papers gave the film a high rating.


The film, Most Likely to Succeed, was produced by education philanthropist, Ted Dintersmith, and shows a project based approach to education being followed by a ninth grade class at High Tech High in San Diego, California. It argues for less focus on content and more emphasis on relational skills with students given more latitude in what, when and how they learn.


The film will be shown at The City Cinema, 64 King Street, Charlottetown on Sunday, January 17, 2016 from 2-4:00 pm. We would welcome you as our guest to be in the audience to view the film and participate in the discussion to follow.


Wishing you the compliments of the season and looking forward to seeing you January 17.


Sincerely




Don Glendenning

President
