Formal letter from Norman Carruthers, President of Community Foundation of PEI, to friends of Dr. Charles Keuper announcing the establishment of a Living Memorial Trust endowment. Explains the trust's purpose to honor Keuper's legacy of preserving PEI's natural beauty and heritage. Describes initial tree planting at Trails PEI entrance in Wood Islands where Keupers first settled. Includes donation information and enclosed brochure. Shows collaborative effort between Keuper family, Foundation, and Glendenning.

Dear


As a friend of Dr. Charles Keuper, you will know that he loved Prince Edward Island and worked diligently for the preservation of its beauty and heritage.


To continue his work and to honour his name, the Keuper family established a Living Memorial Trust at the Community Foundation of Prince Edward Island.  The Trust is in the form of an endowment and will provide grants in perpetuity for the enhancement of public spaces.


While funds may be used for any public space, The Foundation decided as a first priority, to plant trees at a number of entrances to Trails PEI, a network of walking trails based on the original railway lines.  Recent years has seen Trails PEI assume an increasing importance in the life of Islanders and visitors alike. 


To launch the Living Memorial Trust, and in recognition of the life of Dr. Keuper, a cluster of native red oak trees was planted at the Trail entrance in Wood Islands, the community in which the Keupers first settled when they moved to PEI.  Dr. Keuper’s son, John and his family, still live and carries on a horticultural business in the community.


We are delighted to share this information with you and to include a newspaper clipping of the official launch.  The Living Memorial Trust is also described in the enclosed brochure together with a donation form should you want to make a donation in his memory.  The Foundation has both a US and Canadian charitable tax number.


Should you desire more information about the Living Memorial Trust or the Community Foundation, do not hesitate to let us know.


Sincerely



Norman Carruthers

President
