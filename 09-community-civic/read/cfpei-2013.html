Letter dated August 23, 2013 from Don Glendenning to CFPEI President Tom Davies describing the Glendenning Family Fund (established in memory of Elmer and Jane Watling Glendenning of Black River Bridge, NB). Fund began with a $5,000 goal and has grown to approximately $17,000 with a new target of $25,000. When fund reaches $25,000, it will convert to the Glendenning Scholarship supporting postsecondary students from Black River Bridge. Eligible institutions: UNB, University de Moncton, Mount Allison, Saint Thomas, NB Community College (excluding private institutions). Reserves right to make grants for special projects upon family advisory committee approval (Don, Lois Anne Glendenning Gregan, and Janet Glendenning).


August 23, 2013


Tom Davies, President

Community Foundation of Prince Edward Island

Suite 105, 119-121 Queen St.

Charlottetown PE, C1A 4B3


Dear Mr. Davies

Glendenning Family Fund


As you know, the Glendenning Fund was set up a few years ago in memory of Elmer and Jane (Watling) Glendenning and to honour their contributions to their community, Black River Bridge, New Brunswick.  Since its inception, grants have been made for a variety purposes including books to Miramichi Rural School, to celebrate the anniversary of St. Paul’s Presbyterian Church, to commemorate the 200th anniversary of the first land grants in the community and an award to the Miramichi Historical Society for a history prize.


Our original goal was $5,000; as you know, the Fund now stands at around $17,000; our new target is $25,000.  When the fund reaches that goal, we propose to convert it to the Glendenning Scholarship in support of a person from the community pursuing further studies at the postsecondary level; at the moment, that would likely be the University of New Brunswick, University de Moncton, Mount Allison University, Saint Thomas University and the New Brunswick Community College,  but not a private institutions; in saying this, we recognize that other institutions may be established in the years ahead.  Notice should also be sent to the James M Hill Memorial High School, the high school that serves that area of New Brunswick.


While the above is our current plan, we do reserve the right, however, should a project of special interest arise, to use the income on occasion to make a grant for another purpose.  This change would be triggered by communication from Lois Anne (Glendenning) Gregan, Janet Glendenning and me who constitute a “family” advisory committee. 


We are delighted with what we’ve been able to do with earnings from the fund and thank you for your support.


Sincerely


Don Glendenning

Cc: LoisAnne (Glendenning) Gregan

Janet Glendenning
