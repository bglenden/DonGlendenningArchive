A formal proposal for a one-day Literacy Roundtable convened by the Community Foundation to bring together practitioners, providers, and stakeholders for dialogue and information sharing. The invitational event would focus on "the interface between learner and teacher" and accommodate diverse providers. Participants would present concerns in succession without debate, followed by thematic discussion of topics including diagnosis, teaching methodologies, assessment, and reporting. The proposal emphasizes non-attributable discussion to encourage candor, with expected deliverables including scheduled follow-up discussions, fact sheets, and potentially a "Society for the Promotion of Literacy." This reflects Don's belief in the power of structured dialogue to overcome institutional silos.

Educ-Proposal-2

 Subject: Literacy Roundtable


Background:


Evidence abounds as to the importance of literacy for both our individual and community welfare.  Becoming literate is a very personal experience.  In PEI, responsibility for literacy lies with a number of agencies and organizations.  A couple of years ago, the Foundation was in touch with a number of providers and, while there is a wealth of information and services available, there appears to be little coordination or exchange of at the front line level.


The Foundation:


Community foundations, and thee are 170 in Canada today, are relatively new and unique part of the third sector in that they have a community focus, are committed to improving the quality of life in their community, act as hosts to individuals and organizations wishing to establish a trust,   Foundations also act as conveners of groups for the purpose of sharing information or solving problems.  This proposal is an example of the latter function.


Proposal:


The Community Foundation of Prince Edward Island proposes to convene a roundtable, for people intimately involved in the delivery of literacy education, for the purpose sharing information but also to identify gaps, if any, where the Foundation may be of service.


The roundtable will:

Be invitational

Be of one day’s duration

Focus on the interface between learner and teacher

Accommodate a wide spectrum of providers 

Be free to participants


Format:


Following opening remarks, each participant will be expected to speak briefly on some aspect or concern about literacy; this will be followed by an equally brief opportunity for questions or clarifications but not for debate.


The second round of discussion will focus on items arising from the morning’s presentations.  Based on our earlier explorations, topics will likely include diagnosis, teaching/coaching, levels of literacy, rating scales, assessment and reporting.  A more precise agenda can only be put together after initial discussions.  


Roundtable guidelines:

Each person at the table is expected to participate in the discussion

While what is discussed may get wider coverage, participants agree not to attribute comments following the roundtable

Participants are encouraged to bring and share resource material
