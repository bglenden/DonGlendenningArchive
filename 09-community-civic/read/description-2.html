Proposal document dated October 8, 2014, recommending development of an independent assessment service for certifying mathematics proficiency with initial focus on entry-level employment. Outlines background concerns about literacy levels including PISA and CFPEI findings, problems with social promotion limiting credibility of high school diplomas, and inadequate school documentation of achievement levels. Proposes service characteristics including multiple levels, fee-for-service model, independence from schools, and certificates for proof of competency. Includes work plan (literature review, system development, instrument development, pilot testing) and key unresolved issues.


October 8, 2014

Assessment

EDUCATION 20/20 INC.

Assessment Service

Background

  1. Concerns about literacy levels of Islanders have been widely reported: PISA results, CFPEI survey, Charlottetown Area chamber, Home and School, Education 20/20 workshops, Employers concerns, PEI Literacy Alliance

  2. Of particular concern are the recent graduates whose level of basic skills prevent them from entering post-secondary studies or securing employment.

  3. The practice of social promotion puts into question the value of high school graduation as a screening instrument when hiring

  4. School report cards lack adequate description of levels of achievement

  5. Investigation to date indicates that math has considerable support for a pilot test.

  6. Intervention points include:

  • Reducing actual or perceived gap identified in the “Vital Signs” report

  • Construction of a public “math ladder” or yardstick for describing  levels of math 

  • Better documentation as to the range and level of a person’s actual math skills

  • Establishment of a math testing service

It’s time to take the next step.


Proposal

To develop an independent assessment service for certifying a person’s proficiency in the skills of everyday living with an initial focus on mathematics.


Characteristics of Service

  • A number of levels

  • Fee for service

  • Independent

  • Open to individuals

  • Any age

  • By application

  • No school credit offered

  • Certificate not a credential

  • Results provided to testee only

  • Copy of results forwarded to employer or educational institution on request of student


Work plan

  • Review the literature

  • Adopt or develop a system of describing math levels

  • Find or develop an instrument that measures levels

  • Pilot


Issues:

  • Math or arithmetic?

  • Specific math skills or facility in math?

  • Although the initial focus is on entry level employment, project could expand to additional levels?

  • Essential skills or basic skills?

  • Ownership: Private, NGO, Education 20/20, or Government
