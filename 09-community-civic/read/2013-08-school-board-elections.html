Supporting school board elections as engagement indicators, Don cites National Parent Teacher Association research confirming that family-school-community collaboration improves student achievement, teacher morale, and communication. Addressing concerns about low voter interest, Don suggests public apathy reflects perception of trustee powerlessness rather than disinterest. Empowering trustees with real decision-making authority would likely increase engagement. The letter positions community involvement as a policy lever rather than an isolated governance matter.

May 13, 2013


Editor, Charlottetown Guardian

Charlottetown, PEI


Dear Sir:

School board elections


I support the idea of school board elections because they are an indicator of school engagement, and students do better in schools where communities are involved.  The National Parent Teacher Association in the USA reports that “when families, schools, and communities work together,

Student achievement improves,

Teacher morale rises,

Communication increases” 


As for low voter interest and turnout, it may simply be a reflection of a public perception that trustees have little authority to make decisions that count; public apathy may be better countered by giving trustees real decision-making authority.   

Join the discussion and send me a note at  HYPERLINK "mailto:standards2020@gmail.com" standards2020@gmail.com 

Sincerely

Don Glendenning

A long-time student of education
