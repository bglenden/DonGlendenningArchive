Who are Community builders?


Community Builders are people who are interested in making Prince Edward Island an even better place to live – both for this generation and the next. Community builders want to give back to their community and want to do it now. Community builders are thinkers, planners, and doers in all walks of life. They realize that their successes resulted from help given by many people and, by establishing a legacy fund; they can say thanks.


What do Community Builders do?


Community Builders can be recognized by what they do, even though all community builders do not do the same thing. Some build quietly and out of sight, others build publicly; most have a passion whether for the environment, heritage, education, health, religion, or the general welfare of friends and neighbours. They are active in volunteering, contributing, leading, supporting and giving. They help out today but also think about tomorrow. They also set aside funds to support their passions even when they are no longer here to do so.


What is a Community Builder Fund?


A Community Builder Fund is an endowment the income from which is available in perpetuity to support good works and causes identified by the community builder. Endowments may be created at the time of death; however, increasingly community builders participate in both the planning and building of an endowment while they are still alive. In this way, they can participate in the design of their legacy and the decision-making about gifts. Community builders also may, if they so desire, may continue as advisors to their investments even after assigning ownership to CFPEI.

Community means so many things to so many people and the world to us. In all we do, and all we say, we are all for community.

We are part of Canada’s community foundation movement, represented by more than 160 community foundations from coast to coast.

Community vitality has been our purpose, promise and passion since 1921, when we began connecting donors to community needs and opportunities.

Together, we help Canadians invest in building strong and resilient places to live, work and play.

More benefits

Giving life insurance through your community foundation is one of the simplest ways to make a significant contribution to your community and establish your legacy of giving. You can make a gift when life insurance is no longer needed for personal financial wealth replacement. You may receive a number of tax benefits including reduced income taxes and estate taxes. And, if you choose to continue paying premiums through your community foundation, you will be entitled to tax credits.
