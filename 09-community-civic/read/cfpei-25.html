No. 25 May 18, 2005

A Superstore for Donors


A few days ago while waiting to have my car serviced, I noticed the message on the sign of a nearby realty office. It read “Realty Superstore”. It struck me that a community foundation is also a superstore – a “Superstore for Donors”


Superstores and one-stop shopping are common in North America. No longer do outlets limit themselves to one line of service or product. For example, Canadian Tire carries clothes, grocery stores have pharmacies, stationary stores carry computers, computer stores carry kitchen appliances, and service stations are convenience stores.


Charitable organizations generally cater to specific charitable interests, such as a church, a hospital, school, a special community project, etc. A charitable organization raises funds and makes grants only for that purpose.


Community foundations, on the other hand, are multi-purpose. They focus on the well being of a community whether the need, or challenge, be social, educational, environmental, religious or cultural. Donors no longer have to shop for a worthy cause; they simply articulate their charitable wishes and let a community foundation carry them out. And because of the breadth of community contacts and experience, community foundations can provide useful advice about both community needs and opportunities.



Through the Community Foundation of Prince Edward Island, a donor can:


  • Set up a scholarship for a student in high school, university, college or any other educational institution on PEI or elsewhere.

  • Set up an endowment to remember a family, family member or friend.

  • Honour a colleague or community volunteer.

  • Make a memorial contribution.

  • Establish a legacy now or through a will that continues support to a charity of the donor’s choice into the future.

  • Participate in the making of grants.

  • Tailor-make a fund that addresses the donor’s interests rather than contribute to a fund already established.

  • Enjoy the benefits of a family fund without the hassle of administration.

  • Make a contribution to an existing fund that supports the well being of the community and your charitable interests.


Contact the Foundation Office or any Director for further information.
