Getting help in Souris

Some time ago, I was waiting for friends at the Evergreen Restaurant in Souris when I was struck with the question as to where Souris residents obtain information and counselling on matters of education, We could ask the same question in any community.

Students can talk with teachers and counsellors but what about the people who are not in school? People may be thinking about furthering their education for many reasons: personal interest, self-development, job change or updating, to help children or grandchildren, to improve job performance, to improve quality of life in the community, and to help attract and retain businesses. Whatever the reason, where does a person or organization turn for information and advice?  

On returning home, I Googled “Souris development, Souris business development and Souris education” to see what I could find.  I used “development” as a keyword because educating is a developmental process but I also know that education is an important tool in educational development; I found schools, business development offices, career services and a few other things, but none struck me as being place where I could drop by for help.  The need for independent information and advice becomes even more important as we move more deeply into the 21st century

One solution, of course would be for the Department of Education to appoint  an area Coordinator to ensure that the people get the services they deserve; and some day when school boards find favour, to appoint a Board of Education rather than school trustees to ensure that all citizens are served not just youngsters in school



Sincerely,



Don Glendenning

A long-time student of education
