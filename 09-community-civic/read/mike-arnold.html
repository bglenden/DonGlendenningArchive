Vital Signs


This letter comes as a follow-up to a recent brief conversation about a new initiative, Vital Signs, being planned by the Community Foundation of Prince Edward Island. First a brief note about the Foundation.


The Community Foundation of Prince Edward Island (CFPEI) is an incorporated, non-profit organization that provides a 'user-friendly' vehicle for donors to create endowment funds to support charitable activities. Income generated from these investments is directed through grants and scholarships to support a wide range of diverse needs in Prince Edward Island communities. Community foundations, and there are nearly 200 in Canada today, are front and centre in their communities in the initiation of may programs for the benefit of their communities. One of these is a community check-up known as Vital Signs.


Vital Signs is a national program, trademarked and coordinated, by Community Foundations of Canada and is offered through local community foundations. All communities follow the same format, share a common release date, use a standard reporting format and participate in national as well as local publicity. Because check-ups are conducted on regular intervals, usually every year or two, trend lines are easy to establish. Data is gathered from local, regional and national sources and findings are presented in a reader friendly format to make them as accessible as possible


Vital Signs gathers information on the following ten core community indicators:

  • Getting started (immigrants),

  • Housing,

  • Environment,

  • Health,

  • Gap between rich and poor,

  • Work,

  • Learning,

  • Safety,

  • Arts and culture,

  • Belonging and

  • Leadership.




Benefits

Reports are intended to help communities make connections between issues and trends in the core areas of community life. The checkup increases awareness of our strengths and challenges, is a tool for community development; provides useful information when attracting individuals and organizations, supports individuals and organizations in their search for information, and informs the work of third sector organizations. From the Foundation’s perspective, the availability of the information will increase the effectiveness of our grant making, better inform our donors about issues and opportunities in the community and assist us in making connections between individuals and groups to address those issues. Rather than load down this letter, I refer you to the following websites for detail:

  • Community Foundations of Canada: www.cfc-fcc.ca

  • Vital Signs: www.vitalsignscanada.ca
