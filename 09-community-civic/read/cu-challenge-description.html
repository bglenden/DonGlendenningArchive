Strategic proposal draft (March 4, 2007) for CFPEI's Community Challenge Program encouraging Island communities to establish permanent endowment funds. Program matches community deposits at $5,000/year with Foundation grants of $1,000/year over five years, creating sustainable $25,000+ permanent community funds. Includes undesignated fund definition, community committee requirements, CFPEI liaison role, and governance structure. Reflects Don's vision for community-level philanthropic development.


Draft March 4, 2007


COMMUNITY FOUNDATION OF PRINCE EDWARD ISLAND


(Name of Partner) Community Challenge Program



Background Most charitable giving is focused on meeting immediate needs and for specific causes.  Reflection and informal consultation indicates a parallel need to develop longer term plan and resources in the charitable sector at the community level and not to be solely dependent upon the efforts of particular interest groups regardless of how relevant and important such initiatives may be. 


With this view in mind, CFPEI pr5oposes a Community Challenge Program to encourage communities, not just organizations, to build resources to meet future anticipated an unanticipated community needs.


Objectives: to encourage and assist Island communities to establish an endowment building initiative as one of its tools for long-term community development


Concept CFPEI will invite communities throughout the Island to establish and grow an endowment fund, the earnings from which will be available in perpetuity in support of the charitable purposes of the local community.  

Under an agreement with a community, CFPEI will make a grant of $1,000 to a charity, or charities, of the participating community’s choice annually for a five year period on condition that the community establish and add to the endowment at the rate of at least $5,000 per year during the same period.  At the end of the five years, the community fund should be large enough to continue the practice in perpetuity.


Definition A community fund is an undesignated endowment from which grants may be made for any charitable purpose in the community as allowed under regulations of the Canada Customs and Revenue Agency.










Requirements:

A committee of  residents to lead each local initiative

An agreement between each participating community and CFPEI 

Each community fund will be a permanent endowment administered by The Community Foundation of Prince Edward Island

CFPEI will issue charitable receipts

Grants to local charities will only be made after a community adds an increment of $5,000 to the endowment.

While existing endowments may be transferred to a community fund, they cannot be used meet matching requirements

Organizations to which grants are made must meet CRA.requirements as qualified donees.

In order to ensure maximum flexibility, community funds will be undesignated.

CFPEI will appoint a Director as liaison with each community committees


Community Committee  

Consist of a minimum of three persons from the community

Conducts fund raising activities

Convenes public presentation 

Liaises with CFPEI Board on a regular basis

Recommends organizations to receive community grants
