Program description for 2017 Excellence in Education Award expanding beyond schools to community organizations ($2,500 award). Reflects philosophy that "it takes a community to raise a child," shifting focus to educational initiatives by non-school community groups. Proposals must be community-led, address actual educational needs, build on existing resources, be equitable, include implementable activities, and reflect best practices. Documentation requirements: 3-page maximum, group-generated rather than professionally contracted, written format. Proposals should address educational need, underlying principles, proposed activity, likely future, and relevant information. April 30, 2017 deadline, submissions via Education 20/20 or Community Foundation of PEI office. Award represents strategic expansion to recognize broader educational ecosystem beyond institutional settings.



EXCELLENCE IN EDUCATION AWARD

$2,500


BACKGROUND

Education 20/20 Inc. is pleased to offer an Excellence in Education Award in the amount of $2,500 for the purpose of supporting and encouraging a quality education for Prince Edward Islanders. In the belief that it takes a community to raise a child, the 2017 Award will recognize educational initiative of a community organization other than a school or learning centre. Community groups are invited to present their ideas on the use and organization of resources in their community to meet a community educational need. The call for proposals is open to any group, either already existing or coming together for the purpose of making a submission.


EACH PROPOSAL SHALL;

  • Be community led

  • Address an actual community education issue

  • Reflect and build on existing community resources

  • Be equitable

  • Include activities that would actually be initiated if resources allowed

  • Be based on best practices and leading edge thinking in the field of education


DOCUMENTATION:

  • Shall include but not be limited to a written format

  • Shall arise from the group and not be the result of a contract with professionals

  • Be limited to 3 pages


PROPOSALS

Nominations or applications should address the following:

  • Description of an educational need

  • Educational principles on which submission is based.

  • Proposed activity

  • Likely future of the initiative

  • Oher relevant information


OTHER

  • Competition closes: April 30, 2017

  • Costs of the proposal. if any, are to be borne by the group making the proposal

  • Proposal may be sent to the address below or left at the office of the Community Foundation of Prince Edward Island, 119-121 Queen St. Charlottetown, PEI (Tel:902-892-3440)

  • Selection will be based on documentation received.

  • Final decisions will rest with Education 20/20 Inc.

  • Participants may be invited to present their proposals publicly
