An October 26, 2009 letter from Don to Catherine Callbeck highlighting the Community Foundation's commitment to literacy issues and proposing an initiative to convene literacy stakeholders for dialogue. Don notes that despite a year of conversations and Foundation grant-making to home literacy projects, the fundamental problem remains: literacy providers operate in isolation without coordination. He proposes funding small-scale "dialogue" activities (copying, mailing, meeting space) to bring stakeholders together, offering personal attention to lead the initiative. This letter exemplifies Don's conviction that improved communication and coordination could leverage existing resources more effectively.
Community Foundation & Civic Life


October 26, 2009


Hon. Catherine Callbeck

Bedeque

PEI C0B 1C0

Canada



Dear Senator Callbeck:



 About a year ago we spoke on a number occasions about the importance of literacy and the need for training.  


Since then, I have had a number of discussions with others on the subject and the Foundation has made a grant to the Summerside Home and School Association to assist in their project to encourage new mothers to read to their children.


Recently, the Board of Governors held a discussion as to community needs with literacy training was identified one of the priorities.  In fact, the Foundation has invited Barbara McNutt, Coordinator of Literacy Training, to address the issue at our next meeting.  


One of the problems with the current arrangements is that the various delivering agencies operate in isolation from each other.  It is time, I suggest, to engage stakeholders (those who need, provide and pay for) in dialogue about the issue.  Out of the dialogue should come greater coordination if not cooperation?  Hopefully, also, we will identify key people to lead the initiative in the longer run 


Initiating dialogue will require a small amount of funding (copying, mailing, meeting space, perhaps for travel  from outside the Charlottetown area, etc.  Given our earlier conversation, I am wondering if you would be prepared to help.  Because I believe strongly in the need, I am prepared, Catherine, to give this initiative some personal attention.


I would be delighted to provide further information or meet at a mutually convenient time to discuss in detail.


Sincerely


Don Glendenning

Director
