A proposal document outlining five fund administration options for the 2009 Canada Games Dreams & Champions Legacy Trust at CFPEI. Options range from an Interest Fund (full CFPEI management) to a Managed Fund (allowing withdrawal at donor discretion) to custom arrangements. Each option specifies investment management, granting policies, and agreement structures. The document demonstrates CFPEI's capacity to accommodate diverse donor needs and trust management preferences while maintaining professional investment and reporting standards.

The 2009 Canada Games Dreams & Champions Legacy Trust (the Trust) has been established to assist provincial sporting organizations help emerging; Island athletes develop, train, and compete at increasing levels.  The trust will be managed by a committee (the Trust Committee) made up by representatives from Sport PEI, the Province of PEI, and the Host Society.

Options for the Trust Fund Hosting available at the Community Foundation.

As an Interest Fund

(Administration, investment, full granting service)

  • Fund Agreement signed by the Trust and the Foundation
  • Principal transferred to the Community Foundation
  • Invested with the Community Foundation’s Portfolio Manager and according to the Community Foundation’s Statement of Investment Guidelines & Principles
  • Annual granting amount determined by the Community Foundation’s granting policy
  • Grants awarded  annually as specified in the Fund Agreement 

As a Donor Advised Fund

(Administration, investment, Trust Committee advised granting service)


  • Fund Agreement signed by the Trust and the Foundation
  • Principal transferred to the Community Foundation
  • Invested with the Community Foundation’s Portfolio Manager and according to the Community Foundation’s Statement of Investment Guidelines & Principles
  • Annual granting amount determined by the Community Foundation’s granting policy
  • Grants awarded annually as directed by the Trust Committee

As an Agency Fund

(Administration, investment, annual award remitted to the Trust)


  • Fund Agreement signed by the Trust and the Foundation
  • Principal transferred to the Community Foundation
  • Invested with the Community Foundation’s Portfolio Manager and according to the Community Foundation’s Statement of Investment Guidelines & Principles
  • Annual granting amount determined by the Community Foundation’s granting policy
  • Money sent to the Trust for disbursement at their discretion

As a Managed Fund

(Administration, investment, respond to withdrawal requests from Trust)


  • Fund Agreement signed by the Trust and the Foundation
  • Principal transferred to the Community Foundation
  • Invested with the Community Foundation’s Portfolio Manager and according to the Community Foundation’s Statement of Investment Guidelines & Principles
  • The Trust, at its discretion and giving on 30 days’ notice,  withdraws part or all of the fund

As a Modified version of any of the previous 4 options  

(Administration, investment, other services as negotiated)

  • Still covered by a Fund Agreement signed by the Trust and the Foundation
  • Perhaps  with a different Portfolio Manager
  • Perhaps with a different SIP&G
