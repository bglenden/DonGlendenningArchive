Executive briefing from Don addressing the Foundation's role in the Farm Equipment Fund initiative. He describes the core committee, storage needs for artifacts at Orwell Corner, renovation of the Stanley Barn, and recommends a $250,000 endowment ($100,000 for building). Emphasizes the Foundation's supportive role without direct fundraising involvement.


COMMUNITY FOUNDATION OF PRINCE EDWARD ISLAND


Date: Draft -  TIME \@ "MMMM d, yyyy" September 9, 2005

To: Executive

From: Don

Subject: Farm Equipment Fund



From some time snow we have been talking with officials of the PEI Museum and Heritage Foundation about the recovery of early agricultural equipment, especially equipment manufactured on Prince Edward Island.  The initiative has a number of elements including:

The identification of artifacts

Retrieval

Restoration/refurbishment as appropriate

Display

Documentation, and

Demonstration


The core group leading the initiative includes: Dr. Tom Hall, Jack Cutcliffe, JoAnn McQuaid, Henry Compton, Tom LeClair and Tony Glencross: I have been acting as chair.  Already some implements and machinery have been retrieved as a result of our involvement in the initiative.  One of the stumbling blocks has been the availability of suitable storage for artifacts, especially larger pieces of equipment.  In fact, many pieces of farm equipment at Orwell are exposed to the weather.  


Our committee has concluded that a building for storage and display is crucial to the initiative.  Two viable alternatives emerged.  One alternative is to renovate an existing barn, known as the Stanley Barn, adjacent to the new museum building; another is to construct a new building for the same purpose.  In either case, buildings and equipment will be owned by the PEI Museum and Heritage Foundation.  


After a visit to the site and considerable discussion, Committee members favour the renovation of the existing building because:

It is located adjacent to the main museum building,

It is an authentic farm structure, and

The cost of construction and renovation is not out of line.


Funds will be required to ensure adequate dry storage space.  In addition, the committee is of the view that a long term benefit would result from an endowment to provide ongoing support for such related activities as equipment identification and retrieval, restoration, public information and research.  We anticipate a fund of $250,000 of which $100,000 will be for building restoration.

Research


CFPEI does not normally engage in fund raising activities, therefore, I recommend that CFPEI:


Offer general support for the initiative

Promote the initiative in our reports and documents

Promote the Farm Equipment Fund that CFPEI has already approved.
