Draft letter from Don to Professor Roger Black following a pre-Christmas breakfast, highlighting CFPEI's 2008 achievements (60K+ grants, 160+ endowments nationally). Don discusses Black's potential legacy related to his UPEI teaching career, visual arts collection, and faculty support, positioning CFPEI as the mechanism for a lasting contribution. Community Foundation & Civic Life

Dear Roger:


I enjoyed our pre-Christmas breakfast and being updated on your most recent trip to Ottawa; I trust that you are none the worse for having overnighted in your car in Gagetown, New Brunswick.  Our discussions also included the work and progress of the Community Foundation of Prince Edward Island and it is about that topic that I write this letter.


I am sorry that you did not receive your invitation to our awards night on August 20, 2008.  I was out of Province at the time but am told that it was one of the best ever.  During the reception we were able to report grants in excess of $60,000 made during the past year.  A list of awards and other Foundation activities is found in enclosed Community Reporter.    What isn’t so public is the increased operating support thanks to the generosity of Directors, volunteers and friends.  It is too soon yet to know the impact of market conditions on our ability to make grants in 2009 but we are optimistic that our policy of granting based on a four year end-of-year Fair Market Value should reduce the 2008 impact somewhat.



You know from our conversation, Roger, about my support of community foundations, and there are some 160 across Canada today.  Community foundations are unique in that they focus on endowments, granting in perpetuity, a broad range of community interests and the fact that they represent the interests of the donor.  On PEI, we are the only public foundation; all others serve a very limited interest.  Among our grantees we list UPEI and Holland College students, hospitals, youth groups, health organizations, the planting of memorial trees, heritage, arts, etc.  We also administer funds for other charitable organizations and are currently in active discussion with a number of individuals and organizations who have or want to arrange long term financial support for causes of interest.  Having been involved in one way or another with the establishment of more than 40 endowments, I feel comfortable in saying that CFPEI is the most experienced organization on the Island when it comes to designing charitable funds and plans reflecting donor interests and community needs..


A couple of things less well known about CFPEI is the role we play as “conveners” meaning that we bring people or organizations together to explore common interests in improving our community; as an example I point to our role with the arts community resulting in the channeling of $800,000 into the arts over a 4 year period.  A second service is that of flow-through grants to other charities. For example, one US donor, who  wishes to remain anonymous,  makes a gift to CFPEI along with instructions as to where grants are to be made.


Our normal services include:

Designing and establishing endowments. Investing and monitoring performance, and auditing and reporting on to both the public and the Canada Revenue Agency

Making grants that reflect a donor’s wishes and create value within the charitable sector; a donor’s wishes could be as simple as naming charities of interest to which grants are to be made

providing information and advice to individuals, organizations and other foundations on existing and potential community needs

accepting donations of any kind including cash, real estate, personal property, stocks, bonds and other financial instruments; this could include acting as Executor to an estate


When thinking of you and your legacy to Prince Edward Island, the following come quickly to mind:

Influence on the life of thousands of students at UPEI in their preparation for a career in education

Support for the visual arts and collection of paintings by both budding and established artists

Dedication to the interests and welfare of faculty both in Prince Edward island and throughout the country

 Reputation as an astute and successful investor 


Decisions about one’s estate are both important and personal; increasingly people tell me that, after family have been taken care of, they want to give back to the community that supported them.  Many are opting to make that provision now rather than after death.


I, and we, at the Community Foundation of Prince Edward Island, have appreciated your interest and support in times past and would welcome an opportunity,  preferably to assist you but if not then your Executor, in hosting an endowment to carry your legacy into the future.


If you require additional information or would like to explore options and ideas, do not hesitate to contact me


Warm personal regards


Don Glendenning

Director
