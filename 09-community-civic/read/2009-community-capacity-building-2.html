1.0 Community Capacity Building 


As non-profit  organizations play increasingly important roles in our society, it becomes even more critical for them to perform effectively.   Staff, volunteers and Board members of Non-governmental Organizations are showing a growing interest in practices and principles that will help them build high-performing mission-driven organizations, rather than just stronger programs. Traditional foundations and venture philanthropists have also shown a new commitment to investing in the organizational capacities that they fund. 


Despite this growing emphasis on capacity building, the non-profit sector by and large lacks a widely shared definition of what that means, particularly for their own organizations. Part of the reason for this situation results from lack of donor support for capacity building.  Funders tend to support specific initiatives or programs and which often did not include the the development of the organization itself. 


The Seven elements - identified by Venture Philanthropy Partners (Washington, DC) have identified the following seven elements of a non-profit organization that are key to their success: 


Aspirations - mission, purpose, vision and overarching goals of the organizations. 


Strategies - performance goals, planning for program growth, establishing program relevance, identifying new programs, planning for an adequate funding base. 


Organizational Skills - the capacity in managing its performance, doing its planning, generating its funding, sustaining its external relationships, conducting its public relations/marketing, and evaluating its overall effectiveness. 


Human Resources: - determining appropriate staffing levels, securing board commitments, increasing the effectiveness of the ED, increasing the management team's, staff's and volunteer's effectiveness. 


Systems and Infrastructure - the effectiveness and expansion of the organizations' planning systems, decision-making processes, financial systems, HR management, knowledge management, physical infrastructure, technical infrastructure. 


Organizational Structure - effectiveness of the organization's governance model, program design, interfunctional coordination and job/position design. 


Culture - insuring that the cultural foundation of the organization contains shared beliefs, shared values about performance, and share practices. 






2.0 Concept 


The Foundation proposes to take a leadership role in assisting charitable organizations in Prince Edward Island to increase their capacity through the provision of customized support and to increase the capacity of communities through ensuring that the support at the community level is coordinated for the benefit of the community. 


The project will be private sector led with support services provided by and through the Community Foundation of Prince Edward Island.  



3.0 Purpose: 


The purpose of the project is to increase the capacity of a community through strengthening its charitable and non-governmental organizations. 



4.0 The Foundation 


The Community Foundation of Prince Edward Island (CFPEI) was incorporated in 1993 and has a Revenue Canada charitable tax number (8900 1 1190 RR 00 1) and is one of 120 community foundations in Canada. CFPEI has a Provincial mandate and is governed by 12 Directors from across the Province who are representative of the Island community. The Foundation is committed to enhancing the quality of life in Prince Edward Island and operates with maximum transparency and public accountability. 


Community foundations provide an infrastructure for people with many different charitable interests to make long-term contributions to their communities through the establishment of endowments. Foundations solicit and accept charitable gifts for the purpose of establishing endowments, pool the funds for investment and management purposes, and make grants, which reflect the wishes of donors. 


A second major focus is to provide leadership and support to other charitable organizations. It is the latter issue to which this proposal is directed. 


In addition to ongoing endowment building, CFPEI is currently involved with two projects relevant to this proposal: 


• Arts Stabilization 


CFPEI has taken a lead role in developing an arts and heritage stabilization program for Prince Edward Island. A "made in PEl model" has now been developed and has been presented to both the Federal and Provincial governments for approval and funding. The Community Capacity Building proposal incorporates many of the proven approaches from the stabilization model. 


• Organizational capacity building 


In making grants from undesignated funds to Island charitable organizations during the past year, CFPEI has focused on capacity building and no change is proposed. During the past year grants in the amount of $55, 000 were paid out and at least an additional $45 000 in grants for similar purposes is planned for the next 16 months. 


5.0 Need 


In 2001, the Community Foundation of Prince Edward Island conducted a survey of some 40 community groups and organizations with a view to learning more about their organizational needs. In response to the survey, copy attached, organizations identified the following major issues: 


• Fiscal sustainability 

• Increasing Community Profile 

• Volunteer Recruitment and Retention 

• Board Development 

• Training and Development 


Participants in the survey suggested that the Community Foundation of Prince Edward Island could be helpful by: 


• Providing grants to assist organizations with capacity building initiatives 

• Offering or arranging training and development opportunities 

• Assisting with board/organizational development activities 

• Direct provision of technical assistance 

• Initiating and coordinating activities to increasing profile and visibility, and 

• Building community capacity. 


A more recent discussion with about a dozen representatives from organizations surveyed, narrowed the priority focus to issues of: 


• Board development 

• Increasing organizational profile 

• Communications and networking 


• Sharing and partnering 


6.0 Clientele 


The target audience is organizations in Prince Edward Island who deliver charitable service to Prince Edward Islanders. Further criteria include: 


• Being registered with CCRA for at least 5 years

• Having paid staff 

• A recognizable office

• An elected governing Board

• A public annual meeting 

• An published annual report including a financial statement 


7.0 Components 


Sectoral support will include a customized and judicious mix of: 


• Coaching provided by an HRD specialist with significant business, management and voluntary sector experience and critical analysis skills to work directly with organizations. 


• Customized technical assistance provided by a team of consultants, on a fee for service basis in such matters as govemance, financial management, proposal preparation, project management, fundraising and the use of technology. 


• Training as identified by the HRD specialist in consultation with an organization financial planning and record keeping, business plans, proposal preparation, preparation of public information releases, etc. 


• Information and networking activities and opportunities through workshops, retreats, electronic clearinghouse, newsletters, distance leaming, organizational profiling and recognition, cost saving through group purchasing, public information and advocacy. 


• Special purpose grants based on recommendations of consulting team for such things as purchase of a piece of hardware or software, an evaluation of services provided, a development audit, or a business plan. 


7.0 Project deliverables 


Project deliverables include: 


• a cadre of persons available on Prince Edward Island to provide technical assistance to charitable organizations, 


• a current business plan for each participating organization, 


• technical assistance on a customized basis delivered directly to selected organizations, 


• grants to selected charitable organization to assist in their specialized capacity building initiatives, 


• a strengthened skill base among participants through the provision 


of training, 


• a series of networking opportunities for participating organizations I 


• a report by an extemal evaluator, and 


• an electronic newsletter/clearing house focused on the charitable sector in Prince Edward Island.

8.0 Project Benefits 


The potential benefits include: 


• stronger charitable organizations, 


• executive directors with new or enhanced skills, 


• a greater likelihood of organizational sustainability, 


• organizations with greater capability to deliver programs and services, 


• better decision-making on behalf of clients, and 


• stronger communities. 


8.0 Implementation 


8.2 Project management 


The project will be managed by The Community Foundation of Prince Edward Island with the aid of a Project Advisory Committee chaired by a Foundation Director and will include a preponderance of community members. The project will require the hiring of one contract employee on a part-time basis to implement and direct project activities. Whenever possible, partnerships will be established with existing organizations for both advice and service.
