A full nomination letter to the Office of the Secretary to the Governor General of Canada, dated approximately 2015. Don supports John Robinson's nomination for the Governor General's Award, detailing Robinson's 20-year relationship with Don, his success as an Island businessman and progressive farmer, and his family's engagement in church, community, and arts/culture. The letter emphasizes Robinson's leadership with the Community Foundation since 1999, including establishing sound investment policies, engaging summer visitors, and growing assets significantly. Don concludes that Robinson exemplifies community values and deserves the award.
Community Foundation & Civic Life


Office of the Secretary to the Governor General of Canada

Chancellery of Honours

Rideau Hall

1 Sussex Drive

Ottawa ON K1A 0A1

 


John Robinson

I am honoured to support the nomination of John Robinson for the Governor General’s Award for a smart and caring Canadian.

I’ve known John for 20 years, first as a successful Island business man and entrepreneur, a man, well known in the agriculture industry for progressive farming practices, leadership in the sector and stewardship of the land.  While his business was farming, his interests included the agricultural sector and the Island as a whole.  The Robinson family was, and still is, active in their church, supporters of community organizations and events, and play leadership roles in many areas especially in supporting arts and culture. 

John was elected Director of the Community Foundation of Prince Edward Island in 1999 and soon adopted a proprietary interest in its future, served wherever help was needed, including three terms as President.  In the early years of the Foundation, John led the Foundation in the establishment of sound investment policies and business practices.  John recognized PEI summer visitors as significant contributors to PEI life, especially to our arts and culture, and took steps to engage them in summer activities, most notably a reception on July 4 for US visitors and a summer lecture series so that guests could get to know the Island he loved.  Not surprising, John also registered the Community Foundation with the US internal Revenue Service so that US donors to island charitable causes could claim donations to the Foundation on their US income tax returns

John is currently leading the Foundation efforts in the area of development; he sees the Foundation as a key component of the provinces infrastructure, a component committed to building a pool of funds to be available in perpetuity for the betterment of the Island community – and he is having  considerable success.  Under his direction, and thanks to his energy and leadership, Foundation assets grew from under one to over seven million dollars – and he has visions for continuing growth.

In addition to the above, John has an unusually high commitment to the cause of community foundations, is a fine person, a respected member of the community, a world traveler, a gracious host, a beloved father and grandfather, and a good friend.  I can think of no better person to epitomize the concept of a smart and caring community

Sincerely


Donald Glendenning C.M.
