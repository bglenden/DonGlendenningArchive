Adult literacy levels on PEI 

Islanders will have a chance to learn about literacy training for adults at a presentation and discussion at the Stratford Town Hall on Wednesday, September 14, 2016 at 9:00 am. The session, held in the Southport Room, will deal with services available, what more needs to be done, and, generally, why literacy is important to the future success of PEI. The presentation will also provide information on PEI levels as compared to the rest of Canada, and, in particular, to the other Atlantic Provinces.  

It is widely recognized that boosting our education levels will boost the Island economy. The matter of preparedness for the workplace was identified as an issue by The Community Foundation of Prince Edward Island (CFPEI) in its survey of Island youth in late 2013. According to the Conference Board of Canada, education drives success, yet some 40 percent of our adult workforce lacks education skills needed for a modern economy. Responsibility for literacy training in PEI is shared; the Department of Education, Early Learning and Culture serves young people still in school and the Department of Workforce and Advanced Learning. serves out of school youth and adults. The focus on adults will be of special interest to employers.


The workshop is being is being organized by Education 20/20 and will be led by a representative of the Department of Workforce and Advanced Learning, Charlottetown, PE. Everyone welcome.
