Dear Mr. Funder:


This letter comes to introduce an organization and an idea; both designed to exert a long-term positive impact on public education.  In saying this, I refer to Education First Canada Inc. an organization devoted to improving education, not only for Islanders, but for every Canadian.  


The nature and quality of education will, to a large extent, determine our future as individuals, communities and countries.  We are convinced that education is important, that improvement is needed and that there is wide support for change.  While education systems are instituting changes, the changes fall short of what is actually required; changes fail to deal with a number of underlying weaknesses such as autonomy, standards, flexibility, and engagement.  Furthermore, the required changes are not likely to happen unless the public demands it. Traditional efforts to bring about change have not worked.  Some innovative, perhaps even bold, measures are required, but we think that a better education system is worth it and we’d like to play a leading role in making change happen.


The quality of education improves when, among other things,

  • Decision making and accountability is delegated to school and teacher levels
  • The local community, and especially parents, are engaged in decision making.
  • Stakeholders are well informed.


Information is the fuel for change; it doesn’t guarantee change but certainly supports it.  Compared with other services, however, information about education is in short supply.  Our health, for example, is the better for having information about cancer, diabetes, Alzheimers, the dangers of smoking as well as matters relating to our local health services.  We intend to work towards more information about   education matters such as early detection of reading problems, helping with homework, standards or independent study. 


Our plan includes engaging educators in discussion, partnering with other organizations, encouraging innovation and rewarding best practices.  We have already convened a couple of working groups and have invited Elizabeth Jeffery, a recent national award winner, and other Island teachers, to share their views and experiences with their peers.  We’ve also had some informal discussion with the UPEI Dean of Education about engaging teachers in training in some of our activities; and we have other ideas as well including debates, competitions and presentations.


We also are pleased to announce that the Community Foundation of Prince Edward Island supports our educational initiatives.  As a result, contributions to Education First Canada Inc. will be receipted and administered by the Foundation until our registration with Revenue is finalized.


Our group cannot make this happen alone; we need your help – both encouragement and financial support and preferably over a three year period.  I would welcome an opportunity to meet, to provide more details about our plans.   I’ll be in touch within the next few days seeking an appointment.


Sincerely



Don Glendenning, C.M.

President
