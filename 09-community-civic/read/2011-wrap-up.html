Memorandum dated April 13, 2011 from Don Glendenning to File with observations about the cemetery workshop. Notes approximately 30 participants attended, comments on room logistics and program reception, and observations that participants were engaged with discussion continuing nearly an hour after the session ended. Includes recommendations for future sessions: consider Saturday dates, larger square room, seek second sponsor (possibly monument maker), and provides suggestions for topics (fundraising, insurance costs, stone care, endowment management, cemetery association formation). References participant feedback requesting follow-up letter from CFPEI and questionnaire survey on future topics.
Community Foundation & Civic Life

Date: April 13, 2011


To: File


Subject: Cemetery Workshop Wrap-up


From: Don Glendenning


A few observations and thoughts:


Numbers:  About 30 people; I’ve heard that others planned to come but thought the session was on Saturday

Room: adequate, below ground, tables of 6 or 8, tables a bit close together, the arrangement certainly facilitated discussion among participants.

Program was well received

Participants were engaged; discussion carried on for nearly an hour after session closed

Date:  seemed to be an appropriate time of year

Future sessions:

Participants want another session next year

Consider a Saturday

Larger room and preferably a square one.

Look for a second sponsor, monument maker?

If funds allow, a fresh donut in the morning would be good

Future topics: Fund raising, cost of insurance, care of stones, nurturing your nest egg, formation of an association (could we foster?)

Suggestions passed along to me: CFPEI send a letter to participants outlining steps to establish fund with us; a questionnaire survey of participants as to future topics,
