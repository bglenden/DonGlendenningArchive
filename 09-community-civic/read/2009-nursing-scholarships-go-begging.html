Nursing Scholarships go Begging


Scholarships worth four thousand dollars for nurses in their third or fourth year of training in any Canadian university have not received a single application.  The scholarships, one at the Community Foundation of Prince Edward Island (CFPEI) and the other at the Eastern Kings Health Foundation, are valued at $2,000 each and are for students in training who agree to accept employment at the Souris hospital for one or  two years after graduation.  Time is running out and, unless applications are received soon, it will be too late for this year.


While on the Island during the summer of 2007, Rear Admiral Louise C and James Wilmot, an American couple and long time summer residents of the Island, approached the Community Foundation with a view to establishing a nursing scholarship.  Don Glendenning, on behalf of CFPEI, brokered an arrangement with the Eastern Kings Health Foundation who decided to match the Wilmot’s offer.


In commenting on the Wilmot scholarship, Dr. Glendenning, Past President of the CFPEI, thanked the Wilmot’s and the Eastern Kings Health Foundation for their financial support to make the scholarship a reality and added “The establishment of these scholarships is a classic example of the role of a community foundation, linking people who care with causes that matter.”


Information about the Rear Admiral Louise C and James Wilmot Scholarship, and other scholarships offered by the Foundation, may be found ( HYPERLINK "http://www.cfpei.ca" www.cfpei.ca); information about the matching scholarship may be obtained from the Eastern Kings Health Foundation  (Tel: 902-687-7150))
