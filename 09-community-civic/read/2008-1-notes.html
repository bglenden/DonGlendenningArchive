Meeting notes from Don's 'Little Sanhedrin' committee planning the 2008 US Citizens Day event, including discussions with provincial government partners, facility arrangements at the Culinary Institute of Canada, and survey planning. Documents Don's collaborative leadership style and commitment to recognizing American residents' contributions to PEI's community and economy.

Date: January 7, 2008


To: Garth

Anne

John

Donnie


From: Don


Subject: US Citizens Day reception


The above noted Little Sanhedrin gathered at Casa Mia on Monday morning at: 9:00 am to discuss plans for the coming year.  A brief summary of discussions follow:


The results of our discussions will be presented to the CFPEI Board for approval: (Don to place on agenda) Subject to that approval, the following are proposed/recommended.

John reported briefly on discussions with the Province and sufficient interest for them to release Don MacDonald to us to work on the initiative

There was general agreement that the US citizens Day and Summer Islanders’ event are part of a larger initiative, i.e. to thank summer visitors and to harness their talents and resources in support of the things they find so attractive.

Agreed to recommend that the two activities operate separately again this year but that the Chair of the US Day Committee be a member of the Summer Islanders planning committee

 Recommend that the 2008 US Citizens Day be held at the Culinary Institute (Garth: will you make the initial contact?)

CFPEI proposes to undertake a survey of participants with a view to determining their interests and suggestions (Donnie to lead initiative)

A committee needs to be struck; we were advised by Garth that he will participate but not chair the committee.  (Suggestions for members and a chair to be submitted to Donnie ASAP)

Agreed to meet again in a month to further the planning process to address the following agenda:

-Facilities

-Committee including chair.

-Survey


Dona
