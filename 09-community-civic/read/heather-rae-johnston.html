A personal letter from Don Glendenning to Heather Rae Johnston (Seacay Corporation) thanking her for a match-grant challenge and reporting on recent growth in the Community Foundation. Illustrates Don's leadership in CFPEI and his skill in leveraging corporate partnerships for community impact.
Community Foundation & Civic Life

Heather Rae Johnston


Re:  #??


I know that we have recently submitted our final report under the above noted agreement; however, I want to add a personal note to again express my thanks to you and to the Seacay Corporation.


The Match-Grant offer presented CFPEI with a significant challenge but also gave us a great boost.  In addition to the financial support, it moved us to seek out community leaders to explain the role of a community foundation and to make the case for support.


During the past few months in particular, we have found a definite upsurge of interest in the Foundation; since the beginning of 2006 alone, a family has established a substantial health care endowment, we are partnering with a heritage group and a community leader has established a fund to provide perpetual care to a city park.  In addition, we are in active discussion with a number of organizations and expect to see substantial funds coming our way during the present year or the next.


We are also in discussion with a potential partner to introduce a challenge grant program to some of our small communities.


Again, I thank you for your understanding and support.  Should other initiatives or opportunities come to your attention that you think would be of interest to us, we would be delighted to consider them.


Unfortunately, our paths did not cross during the term of our contract.  Should your travels bring you to PEI, we would be delighted to have you drop in for a visit.


Warmest personal regards


Don Glendenning

Director
