A September 2005 resignation letter from Don to Ken Gillis (IAHSP Board Chair), ccing President of CFPEI John Robinson. Don cites two concerns: (1) the Board declined his recommendation to hire an external consultant for mid-term review, instead undertaking incomplete self-review; (2) on the day addressing additional six organizations, he abstained from voting due to lack of comfort with available information about Round One strengths/weaknesses. Don reiterates recommendation for external consultant review before further financial commitments, states this would improve program delivery, and expresses continued support for the program's potential. This resignation demonstrates Don's high standards for accountability, his principled stand on governance issues, and his willingness to step down over matters of organizational integrity.


September 13, 2005


Ken Gillis, Chair

IAHSP Board

West Royalty 

PEI


Dear Ken:


As you know I have been involved with the Island Arts and Heritage Stabilization Program since its inception.  I believe in the program and its potential for having a positive impact on the arts and heritage sector in Prince Edward Island and was delighted, therefore, to become a member of the decision-making Board.


It is with considerable regret, therefore, that I tender my resignation effective immediately.


You will recall that, late last fall, I spoke about securing the services of an outside consultant to undertake a mid-term review.  I felt, and still feel, that information and insights gained by such a review would have been useful as we moved into the second phase of the program.  The recommendation was not made because of any knowledge that the program was not being effective.  Rather, I thought that such a review would aid future decision-making.  The Board decided not to hire an outside consultant but to undertake its own mid-term review.  I agree to the change.  A review was commenced but has not been completed.


Today dealt with proposals for an additional six organizations.  You may have noted that I participated in discussions but abstained from voting.  I did this because of my lack of comfort with the information available especially information about the strengths and weaknesses during our first round of organizations.


In tendering my resignation from the Board, I wish you and the project well.  I also urge you, even at this late date, to secure the services of an outside consultant to undertake a review to ascertain what changes, if any, would materially improve the IAHSP Program including its delivery.  You may find that no changes are necessary; if so, I will be delighted.  If you act on this suggestion, it would probably be best not to make any further significant financial commitments until the review is completed.


Because the Community Foundation of Prince Edward Island is the body that brought the IAHSP into existance and has a continuing responsibility, I am sending a copy of this letter to its President, John Robinson, for his information.


Sincerely



Don Glendenning

62 Queen Elizabeth Drive

Charlottetown, C1A 3A9


Cc: President, CFPEI
