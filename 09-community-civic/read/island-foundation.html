The Island Foundation

Background

In 1993 The Community Foundation of Prince Edward Island was incorporated under Part 2? of the Companies Act of Prince Edward Island. The Foundation is a charitable organization with a focus on enhancing the quality of life in the communities of Prince Edwards Island. It provides a means for individuals, family groups, corporations and private foundations to support charitable activities of interest to them.

Recently some Directors engaged in conversations with a number of leading Islanders about the future of the Foundation, its potential as a force for good in the community and some steps that would make it more attractive to Islanders as an alternative to private foundations. The writer found support for the idea and believes that the dialogue should continue.

Briefly stated, the vision includes an Island Foundation;

  • Based on the community foundation model

  • The leader in the third sector support for PEI

  • With its own Act

  • With assets of $50-100 million

  • With a significant portion of the funds being undesignated in order to meet emerging needs

  • Providing a local, readily available and inexpensive service to people interested in establishing charitable endowments

  • Providing an eventual home for assets now held by existing family and public foundations

  • With oversight in perpetuity by an Island based Board of Directors

  • With flexibility in the making of grants to meet new and emerging needs

  • With a focus on supporting activities generic to maintaining the uniqueness of the PEI community.

The community foundation model is favoured because:

  • It is focused on service to Islanders and the Island

  • The disbursement of grants remains with a local board of prominent Islanders in perpetuity

  • It is impartial representing all donor interests and Island needs.

  • Has the broad interests of the Island in view

  • Is committed to maintaining endowments in perpetuity

Activities/Services:

  • Broad range of fundraising activities

  • Repository for existing “abandoned” fund



Next Steps

  • Continue exploratory discussions

  • Proceed with tabling “an Act to Establish the Island Foundation”

  • Establish a small board committee to continue discussions

  • Invite Fred Hyndman be our main contact with other interested Islanders.

  • Use the Upson reception as an appropriate time and venue to hold a formal founding meeting.

  • If, the Upson reception is not feasible, that a dinner meeting be convened, prior to the tabling of the “Act to Create the Island Foundation” for the purpose of discussing the proposal.
