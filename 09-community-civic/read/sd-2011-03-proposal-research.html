Dated January 25, 2011 (author attribution omitted in document), this proposal addresses the nonprofit sector's capacity challenge in securing available funding. Don notes that six arts organizations eligible for federal matching grants lack capacity to apply, and larger organizations have reduced fundraising due to budget constraints. He proposes CFPEI provide selective assistance to third-sector organizations: identifying funding opportunities matching organizational interest, assessing application requirements and internal documentation availability, and providing application preparation support. The proposal reflects Don's understanding of nonprofit sector constraints and CFPEI's potential leverage role.



COMMUNITY FOUNDATION OF PRINCE EDWARD ISLAND


SD- 2011- 3

Date: January 25, 2011

Prepared by:


To: Directors


Subject: Proposal Research and Technical Support


Background


CFPEI has encountered promising funding opportunities for itself and other third sector organizations but it appears that small organizations have neither the time nor expertise in preparing proposals even when the potential and payoff is obvious. For example, at least six arts organizations are eligible for matching grants under a federal initiative; all know of the opportunity but only one has made application. On enquiry, we are told that the current staff are too busy to complete the application process; from a CFPEI perspective, the task appears quite routine.We are also aware that one of the larger and more successful third sector organizations has reduced it fund raising activity due to budget cuts. CFPEI is of the view that selective assistance to organizations would prove financially beneficial both to organizations and the Province. .

Proposal


CFPEI proposes to assist third dector organizations to::

  • Identify funding opportunities consistent with an organization’ interest and capabilities

  • Identify the application requirements and assess the availability of internal information required and available for completing the application,

  • On securing the required documentation, assist in the preparation of the actual application.


Additional funding will be required for the task, however, long term funding will come from charging a fee for services renedered – perhaps in the form of a tariff on successful projects.


Recommendation:


  1. That approval be granted to fund the service
