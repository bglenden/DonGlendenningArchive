Letter from Don Glendenning to Harry Holman dated March 6, 2009, written from Florida detailing the development of the "Leave a Legacy" arts brochure. Don explains he earmarked private donations to extend the Island Arts and Heritage Stabilization Project (IAHSP) impact. Scott MacAuley proposed a multi-panel brochure featuring six organizations plus front/back matter. Five organizations responded positively; Carrefour initially didn't participate but Michelle Blanchard later responded with French panel. Don discusses unsuccessful attempts to fund French translation (estimated $800-1,000 plus printing costs). Explains Carrefour wouldn't participate in English-only version with French panel. References Donald Arsenault (past CFPEI Director) and seeks advice from Harry Holman regarding translation grant opportunities.
Community Foundation & Civic Life

Date: March 6, 2009


To: Harry Holman


From: Don Glendenning


Subject: Arts brochure


Greetings from the sandy shores of Florida!  I am having email problems; I can receive but not send so am handling my messages via web mail.


Some background on the arts brochure:


I felt strongly that we should try to extend the impact of the IAHSP project and was able to earmark some private donations to CFPEI for that purpose.  The funds are not part of the funds transferred to CFPEI on wind-up of IAHSP.

I approached the six original organizations for ideas; I had in mind a workshop with an outside resource person.  There was little interest but Scott MacAuley proposed a “Leave a Legacy” brochure with each organization having its own panel – six organizations plus a front and back panel would make eight panels.

The original six were canvassed and five responded early and positively.  Panels were developed and edited a number of times.  We also met once in Summerside.

 did not show any interest although I included them on the mailing list.  Sometime in January, after Michelle Blanchard returned to her post, she responded indicating an interest and Michelle send me a panel in French.  She thought that she might be able to find funds for a printing in French as well.  She was not successful.

I talked with John Robinson, CFPEI President, and we were delighted with the idea of publishing the brochure in both languages but we did not have the money to do so.  

I obtained estimates from Quik Kopy and John Arenburg for printing and design.  After receiving the panel in French, I checked on translation services for that panel and received an estimate from Latin Access $90 plus GST or 48 cents per word.   I now understand that there is a flat fee plus a “per word” fee and the rate per word may be less for the full document.

Estimating 1600 words for the final brochure would mean a translation cost of $800 plus tax plus the translation of one panel from French to English would come to about $1,000.  It is way out beyond our capability even if we cut the number of brochures to a minimum.

I met with Michelle prior to leaving for Florida and she confirmed that she had not found funds for translation, that Carrefour would not participate in an English only version (with a French panel).  I told her that we did not have funds for a brochure in both languages even if we reduced the number of brochures being printed.

I had tried to reach Donald Arsenault prior to visiting Carrefour but he was not available; Donald is a past Director of CFPEI and I thought that he might have some advice.  He returned my call after my visit to Carrefour and I explained the project and our dilemma.

While in transit, I received an Email from Donald suggesting that I get in touch with you.

At this point, other than money, the only issue that I am uncertain about is how to accommodate the additional words resulting from translating into the French language

I assume that Donald sent a copy of the draft brochure but, if not, please let me know and I will forward a copy.


Please let me know what documentation you need or steps to be taken to apply for a grant for purposes of translation.


Regards


Don
