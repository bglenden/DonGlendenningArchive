Quality Education


Education helps us prepare for and navigate life’s road, a road with many twists and turns, and one that engages us in a number of life roles, that of an individual, family member, learner, citizen, consumer and producer. The preparation for life’s roles is called education and, the better prepared we are, the better we are able to cope with all that we encounter or create. A quality education not only prepares us to cope, but also to embrace and enjoy the experience, and to even make the road better for those who follow.


A UNICEF Report of 2000 describes a quality education system as one with:

  • “Learners who are healthy, well-nourished and ready to participate and learn, and supported in learning by their families and communities;

  • Environments that are healthy, safe, protective and gender sensitive, and provide adequate resources and facilities;

  • Content that is reflected in relevant curricula and materials for the acquisition of basic skills, especially in the areas of literacy, numeracy and skills for life, and knowledge in such areas as gender, health, nutrition, HIV/AIDS prevention and peace;

  • Processes through which trained teachers use child-centered teaching approaches in well-managed classrooms and schools and skillful assessment to facilitate learning and reduce disparities.”



Workshop: January 20, 2014


The challenges of young people on PEI will be the subject of a workshop on January 20, 2014. The workshop will lead of with a review of a recent study commissioned by the Community Foundation of Prince Edward Island, Taking the Pulse of Island Youth. Following a presentation by Steve McQuaid, vice President of the Foundation, participants will engage in round-table discussions about the findings, especially those related to the issue of “preparedness”.


The workshop is open to the public and will be held in the Southport Room, Stratford Town Hall, at 7:00 p.m.
