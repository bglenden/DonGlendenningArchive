Draft Press Release for       , 2008


Check Readiness of Site


Members of the Living Trust committee of the  Community Foundation of Prince Edward Island visited, together with officials of the Department of Tourism, visited birch Crossing, Elmwood, PEI yesterday to check the site for planting of memorial and friendship trees later this year.  The site is one of two currently designated so far but others are planned for future years. Space has ben set aside for planting a cluster of trees on each side of the entrance to The Confederation Trail  as well as sites for general planting and named trees.


Living Trust is a service provided by the CFPEI whereby people may make a donation to be used for the planting of friendship or memorial trees.  The result is, not just a tribute; they also enhance and beautify our environment, provide shelter for wild life, reduce erosion and improve the quality of the air we breathe.  Grants may be made for the enhancement of any public space, however, the initial focus will be on trail entrances.  


The Living Trust provides an opportunity for people to thank a friend, remember a relative, of mark a special occasion.  Individual interested in plantings in places of special significance should contact the Foundation.  Donors will be given a receipt to be used when filing Income Tax returns in Canada or the USA 


Persons interested in learning more about, or contributing to the Living Trust, may do so by contacting the Community Foundation office, 892-3440..
