Strategic planning document from May 2010 presenting multiple options for the future of US Citizens' Day programming. Options include: discontinuing the event while thanking participants, inviting municipalities to organize celebrations, establishing higher budgets ($1,000+), showcasing Island arts through cultural programming, emphasizing CFPEI institutional identity, and establishing dedicated US citizen funds. Includes suggestion of US student scholarships at PEI institutions. Document reflects Don's analytical approach to evaluating program sustainability, cost-benefit analysis, and strategic fit within CFPEI's mission.

May 17, 2010

COMMUNITY FOUN DATION OF PRINCE EDWARD ISLAND

US Citizens’ Day (2010)


Ideas for 2011


Considerations:


Is purpose still valid?

Has it provided any benefits to CFPEI

Is it good for PEI

Is it appropriate for CFPEI

What is/should be its priority for CFPEI

How could it be improved?

What would lighten the burden of organizing




Options/Ideas

Discontinue; inform participants thank organizers

Invite City, cities, PEI municipalities to organize

Establish a budget - $1,000:  seek sponsors, request contributions, 

Make it a showcase for Island Arts – visual, performing, etc – paid performer, background music, song leader

Make it a CFPEI function for US citizens;make a stronger statement about CFPEI interest, our logos, brochures in site, handout package to participants, more proactive program, 

Feature a US citizen who is making a difference on PEI.

August: letter/Email to attendees thanking them for coming, asking them to confirm Email addresses, names of others to invite, and include most recent Newsletter (rather than prepare a separate handout)

November: another mail out with latest Newsletter, inform about plans for coming year, and requesting a donation

Later in the year, send a note telling that we are planning for next year and ask for a donation.

In future, we may consider a scholarship for a US student studying at a PEI institution
