July 4, 2010 proposal to establish CFPEI Challenge Fund offering financial incentives for endowment creation/expansion. Models include matching funds, arts partnership matching (1:4 multiplier with DOCH), and flow-through grants. Short-term goal $100,000; long-term $1,000,000. Open to all sectors except faith-based/partisan (unless donor-initiated). Reflects Don's sophisticated understanding of leverage financing to democratize access to professional fund administration for smaller organizations unable to sustain independent fundraising. Shows innovative thinking about sector-wide capacity building.

July 4, 2010


CHALLENGE FUNDS


We’ve spoken on occasion about offering a financial incentive to organizations or individuals to encourage the creation or expansion an endowment fund held by CFPEI.  Although CFPEI has not used that approach, it has been involved in two instances where we were challenged. 


PROPOSAL:


That CFPEI adopt the challenge fund idea as a mechanism for encouraging charitable organizations to ensure their future through the establishment of 


BENEFITS:


All donations flow through to a charity with multipliers varying from 1-5

A selling point to both CFPEI and the donor when soliciting

Builds for the long term

Builds another stream of income for charitable causes

Future governments  will be unable to deliver the dollars that charitable issues need

Currently only the larger organizations can afford to undertake significant fundraising initiatives

Large organizations are getting a disproportionate share of charitable dollars

The future of charitable organizations is one way to ensure the balanced society that the Island needs

CFPEI is the only charitable secorganization commited to long term support for the broad charitable sector in PEI


MODELS


There are a number of approaches to issuing challenges including:

Matching funds.  In our initial challenge, CFPEI was offered $100,000 to flow through as grants on condition that we build a matching endowment.  In this case, we were also provided with some money for administrative purposes.

In the case of the Arts Stabilization Program, we were one of four partners and were required to raise $200,000 from the private sector.

DOCH has established a matching grant fund whereby they will match endowment funds established by approved arts organizations; the endowment funds must be hosted by a community foundation.  Indian River Festivalhas entered such a partnership agreement and received its first matching grants.  The following additional arts organizations are eligible: College of Piping, Victoria Playhouse, Carrefour, Jubilee Theatre, PEI Symphony Orchestra and the Confederation Centre. In this instance, a challenge of $1,000 to an arts organization for matching funds could result in a $4,000 addition to the endowment

CFPEI has offered to provide a $1,000 flow-through grant to a charitable cause in Souris as soon as the Souris fund is increased by $5,000.is finalizing arrangements to apply the same approach to expansion of the Visual Arts Fund


THOUGHTS:


Endowments to be the property of CFPEI

That CFPEI create a multi-purpose challenge fund 

Name of fund: ??(charitable incentive Funds, Futures Fund, Next Generation Fund, New  Heritage Fund, Growth fund,  or??

Goal: $100,000 short term; $1,000,000 long term

Granting strategy: open but including any or all of the above

Open to  all sectors 

Faith-based or partisan political organizations included only if initiated by a donor wishing to offer the challenge.


RECOMMENDATION:


That we identify a small committee of Directors and community members to spearhead the initiative
