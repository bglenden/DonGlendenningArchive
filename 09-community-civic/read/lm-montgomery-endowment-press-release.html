FOUNDATION AND LAND TRUST PARTNER IN NEW FUND


The Community Foundation of Prince Edward Island and the L. M. Montgomery Land Trust are partnering in the establishment of an endowment for the preservation of scenic coastal agricultural lands in PEI.  The result is the L. M. Montgomery Land Trust Operating Endowment Fund established for the purpose of making grants annually towards the operating costs of the Trust.


In making the announcement, CFEI President, John Robinson, welcomed the establishment of the fund and commended the Trust for its efforts in preserving PEI as a unique and special little part of the world – a view shared by Islanders and visitors alike.  


The L. M. Montgomery Land Trust Operating Endowment Fund becomes one of a number of funds within the Community Foundation of Prince Edward Island, a non-profit organization administered by a volunteer board of directors, which oversees permanent endowments set up by members of the community.  The Foundation is a registered charity and donations are tax deductible in Canada and the USA


The L. M. Montgomery Land Trust, established in 1994, is a registered non-profit charity established for the purpose of preserving scenic coastal agricultural lands from development.  The focus of the Trust is on a scenic stretch of coastline from French river to Seaview, an area of roughly 3,000 acres.


Persons interested in further information about the Fund or wishing to make a contribution to support the work of the Trust should contact the Community Foundation at 119-121 Queen Street, Charlottetown, or by telephone at 892-3440
