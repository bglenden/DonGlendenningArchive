November 19, 2007


COMMUNITY FOUNDATION OF PRINCE EDWARD ISLAND


Living Memorial Trusts


1. Background


Living memorials are green spaces set aside in memory of a person, place or event; the key characteristic is that it is a natural space and plant life continues to grow.   A living memorial may be as simple as a single tree or bush but also may be a garden, a park or a forest.  


Living Memorials are more than just a tribute to loved ones; living memorials enhance and beautify our environment, provide shelter for our wildlife, reduce erosion and improve the quality of the air we breathe.  Living memorials may bring comfort, hope and peace to visitors and ensure that generations to come will know the beauty of a green plant.  


In addition to actual living plants and, depending on the nature of the space , a living memorial may also include comfortable benches on which to rest, a scenic view or viewing platform, path enhancement, footbridges, picnic areas, wildlife areas, handicap accessibility, and erection of shelters.  


2. Why a living memorial?


People usually donate to a Living Memorial to recognize or honour a person, organization or event at a time of a major life change including:


a special birthday, 

a retirement,

the birth of a child,

the death of a family member, friend or colleague,

recognition of long and loyal service,

recognition of outstanding service

the relocation of a colleague or friend



3. The CFPEI Living Memorial Trust


Initial funding for The Living Memorial Trust at the Community Foundation of Prince Edward Island was provided by the family of Dr. Charles Keuper, a retired physician with a deep love for the natural beauty of his adopted home.  Dr. Keuper was an avid and tireless worker in the establishment of Trails PEI and a person who savoured outdoor life to the full.  Through the Living Memorial Trust, Dr. Keuper’s family honors his memory and encourages others with similar interests to do likewise. 


The Living Memorial Trust is one of a number permanent endowments at The Community Foundation of Prince Edward Island, a non-profit organization managed by a volunteer Board of Directors, which oversees permanent endowments set up by members of the community.  The purpose of the Living Memorial Trust is to make grants to enhance outdoor public green spaces throughout Prince Edward Island. 


Contributions to The Living Memorial Trust are welcomed to mark a milestone in the life of a person or organization and all donations will be recorded in a Memorial Register.  Individual interested in plantings in places of special significance should contact the Foundation.  Donors will be given a receipt to be used when filing Income Tax returns in Canada or the USA 



4.   Inaugural Planting


On July 21, 2007, two clusters of three red oak trees were planted at the entrance to the Confederation Trail at Wood Islands in memory of Dr. Keuper.  The planting was made possible through the efforts of many people and organizations including the Keuper Family, Wood Island Development corporation, Department of Forestry, Department of Transportation and Public Works and the PEI Department of Tourism.


5    Future Plans


Future plans include:


1 General contributions

Contributions of any size, not otherwise designated, will be added to the endowment if the donor provides a “10-year direction” or, otherwise added to flow-through component.  Each donation of $10.00 or more will be receipted and, if in recognition of friendship or a memorial, a card will be sent to individual or family as appropriate.  The name of donor and recipient/donee will be entered in the Memorial book.  This component is now operational.



2 Individual trees at an undesignated location


Contributions of $250 or more will be accepted for the planting of a single tree in a CFPEI designated location.  Receipts and announcements will be issued as appropriate and names entered into the Memorial book.  Planting will normally take place in late spring and locations will be determined by the granting committee annually.  Unless otherwise informed, a plaque will be placed nearby and information will be entered into the book of remembrance.



3 Individual trees at designated locations


Contributions of $500 or more will be accepted for planting of a single tree at a designation provided that the owner of the property gives consent.  Receipts and announcements will be issued as appropriate and names entered into the Memorial book.  Planting will normally take place in late spring.  Unless otherwise informed, a plaque will be placed nearby and information will be entered into the book of remembrance.  Additional costs for grading, fill, transportation and a reception will be the responsibility of the donor


4 Clusters of trees at designated locations


Contributions of $1, 000 or more will be accepted for planting of a cluster of trees at a designated location provided that the owner of the property gives consent.  Receipts and announcements will be issued as appropriate and names entered into the Memorial book.  Planting will normally take place in late spring.  Unless otherwise informed, a plaque will be placed nearby and information will be entered into the book of remembrance.  Additional costs for grading, fill, transportation and a reception will be the responsibility of the donor


5 Memorial Book


The Foundation will maintain a Memorial book to record names of donors and persons in whose name the donation is  being made.  The book will be maintained at the Foundation Office and will be in a form and format designed for easy updating


6 Publicity/promotion/marketing


The Living Memorial Trust will be actively reported and promoted through press releases, advertisements, CFPEI presentations, etc.


7 Administration


Contributions will be receipted, however, on request a donors name may be kept confidential.  Contributions may be made via the CFPEI website.  Arrangements for planting will be made by CFPEI not the donor.
