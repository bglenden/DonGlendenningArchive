A May 2014 draft proposal addressing workforce readiness gaps identified through multiple sources including Vital Signs surveys and Chamber of Commerce reports. Don proposes conducting further surveys of youth populations across PEI to confirm findings, define skill gaps in math, literacy, and life skills, and develop action plans. The proposal includes various survey options from focused community studies to in-depth impact analyses on individual young people. Intended deliverables include comprehensive reports, action plans, and recommendations. Potential funding sources include HRDC, employment councils, and educational institutions, showing Don's systematic approach to evidence-based policy development.

Draft

May 1, 2014


Preparedness for the workplace


Background:

  • Education and work are crucial to future of individuals and our community
  • Vital signs: Preparedness for work identified as an issue in the  survey
  • Workshops: Education 20/20 public consultations identified widespread concern about  preparation for work
  • Recent report of Greater Charlottetown Area Chamber of Commerce refers to levels of math, literacy and life skills
  • PEIHSF survey, although numbers were small, identified that 50 percent of those who responded were not well prepared for the following year
  • I believe that there was an SSTA study that reported the same conclusions


Conclusion:  

  • In order to plan a program to fill the gaps, more detail needed
  • Impacts on availability of skilled workers
  • Impacts on our economic activity
  • Committing people to a lifetime of low paying jobs


Proposal:  

To conduct a further survey of the youth population:

  • Confirm the findings
  • Further  define the gaps 
  • Provide a basis for helping fill the gaps


Survey options:

  • Original sample 
  • Select a single community ie West Prince
  • Same questionnaire to all stakeholders: youth, parents, employers, community leaders, schools
  • In depth impact  on a dozen young people
  • Identify improvement opportunities for each


Make recommendations as to how to reduce the gaps

Deliverables:

  • Report
  • Action plan
  • Options
  • Propose guidelines for addressing gaps?
  • Recommendations


Funding:

  • HRDC
  • PEI Employment councis’
  • A sectorial council, Construction
  • English or French school Board



Notes/question/ideas:

  • Why do are we doing further study?

Possible partners: 

  • Chamber of Committee,
  • PEIHSF, SSTA, 
  • Education 20/20, 
  • West Prince Development authority, others?
