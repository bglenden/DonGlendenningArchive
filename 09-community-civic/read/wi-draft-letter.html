Draft letter from Don Glendenning to Women Institutes soliciting sponsorship for Youth In Philanthropy (YIP) program. Notes recruitment/retention challenges for charitable board members, especially in smaller communities (uses cemetery trustees as example). Describes YIP initiative: school-based groups explore community needs related to youth, allocate $1,000 for grants, establish endowment funds. Seeks $2,500 sponsorship per school or donations. Proposes introducing program in at least three schools in coming year for minimum three-year commitment. Includes enclosures about CFPEI and YIP project.
Community Foundation & Civic Life


January 18, 2011


President

Women Institutes

NOTE:I think that there is a PEI President as well as a president for individual institutes.  Seems to me that the National President is from PEI this year.


Dear 


In the course of our activities at CFPEI, we have become aware of a major concern among charitable organizations about their ability to recruit, develop and retain board members.  The issue is quite serious; it seems, especially in smaller communities Trustees for cemeteries are a case in point.


Recently, we have become aware of an initiative at other community foundations designed to encourage young people to get to know the needs of their communities and practice their volunteerism.  In effect, school based school groups are encouraged to explore the needs of their community, especially those related to youth, and are allocated $1,000 for which they may determine grants.  In addition, each participating school is challenged to establish an endowment fund in support of youth needs.  Our objective is to introduce the program in at least three schools during the coming year and, once enrolled,  to maintain the program for at least three years.


We are looking for sponsors for the program and wonder if Womens’ Institutes  would be interested in sponsoring a school for $2,500 or, if not, making a donation to the cause.  I am enclosing some information about the Foundation and about the Youth In Philanthropy project and, as well,  a self addressed envelope should you be interested in giving support.  Should you need more information about the Foundation or the Youth in Philanthropy project, do not hesitate to contact me.  Should you be interested,  we would be happy to arrange a meeting of provide a speaker.


We look forward to hearing from you and hopefully to your financial support.


Thanks in advance for your consideration.


Sincerely,
