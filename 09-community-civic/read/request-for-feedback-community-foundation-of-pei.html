A strategic planning document requesting feedback from Foundation stakeholders on building capacity in PEI's third sector. Don articulates the Foundation's role as a community development agency rather than merely a charity, emphasizing capacity-building, partnership facilitation, and advocacy for the non-profit sector. His vision includes increasing Island awareness, achieving operational self-sufficiency, and leveraging the Foundation's unique broad mandate to strengthen PEI communities.

B uilding Capacity of PEI’s Third Sector

An Initiative of the Community Foundation of PEI

A Three-Year Strategy


Pre-Session Request for Feedback


The Community Foundation of PEI (CFPEI) has engaged MRSB Consulting Services to assist in the development of A Strategy to Build Capacity of the Third Sector in Prince Edward Island – a CFPEI-lead initiative. During the strategic planning session to be held on Thursday, January 20th (4:00 pm to 8:30 pm), we will discuss key components of this three-year strategy including overall objectives, opportunities, challenges, and go forward goals and strategies.

In preparation for the session, we would appreciate your submitting your responses to the questions provided below to Wendy Drake by fax at (902) 892-1768 or email wendy.drake@mrsbgroup.com by no later than 4:00 pm on January 12th, 2011. Your responses will be confidential and will help to lead the discussion during the planning session. Should you have any questions, please do not hesitate to contact Wendy at (902) 368-2643. Your feedback is greatly appreciated!


  1. What role should CFPEI play in building capacity of the Third Sector on PEI?

  • CFPEI should play a lead role

  • It is the only organization whose mandate, no only allows, but requires a broad vision

  • While all sectors will play a role in shaping the PEI of the future, the third sector has the greatest potential for preserving/further developing the Island’s uniqueness.

  1. What are the strengths of CFPEI that will enable the Foundation to carry out this role?

  • Breadth of mandate as compared with others in the sector

  • Ability to translate visions or others into contributions to the the Island

  • Because we are not focused on a specific cause or service, we constantly scan the landscape for needs and resources to meet them

  • We recognize that many Islanders can identify gaps in the third sector and can make some resources available to address some issues. CFPEI calls those gaps to their attention and provides a mechanism whereby those issues can be addressed



  1. What are the biggest opportunities in terms of services and support provided by CFPEI that will build the capacity of PEI’s Third Sector with respect to the following:

    1. Third Sector organizations?

  • Better planning among organizations at the community level

  • Offer organizational development activities across the sector

  • Help the sector develop a collective voice

  • Provide recognition for jobs well done

  • Sometimes solving a problem only requires a leader to convene the stakeholders; other foundations play this role; it is a natural for a community foundation

    1. The Sector as a whole?

  • Foster better flow of information and exchange of views

  • Negotiate on behalf of the sector for services such as auditing, accounting, IT, etc.

  • Encourage partnerships and joint projects

  • Help PEt obtain its fair share of off-Island dollars from national and international foundations







  1. What are the biggest challenges CFPEI will face in carrying out this strategy?

  • Securing operating dollars to fulfill the mandate

  • Orienting Directors to the larger view; some Directors still see us as a charitable organization rather than a community development agency with a charitable number.

  • View our grant making as providing leadership and creating value not just alleviating poverty,



  1. What can CFPEI do to overcome these challenges?

  • We need sufficient operating dollars so that we can turn our attention to our role

  • more focused vision

  • Better planning

  • A greater range of strengths among directors

  • Be more active in telling our story

  1. In your opinion, how can CFPEI market its services and supports to Third Sector organizations?

  • We spend a disproportionate amount of time and energy on administrative matters


  • Speakers list

  • More publicity

  • Higher profile projects

  • More focus on what we do rather than what we are

  1. What does success look like in the next three years with respect to the building capacity of the Third Sector and the leadership role of the Community Foundation of PEI?

  • More convening of community groups for the purpose of addressing community issues

  • Increased endowment

  • More community services

  • More cooperative ventures with us as a lead



Please provide any other comments.



Looking forward to the results of the study



Don

Thank you!
