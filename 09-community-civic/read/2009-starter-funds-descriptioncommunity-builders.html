Programmatic document describing CFPEI's "Community Builders Legacy Fund" concept, designed to encourage individuals to establish endowments reflecting their values and passions. Outlines the philosophy that community builders quietly or publicly contribute to causes (environment, heritage, education, health, religion) and can formalize their legacy through living endowments. Details the simple process of establishing funds and maintaining donor input. Reflects Don's belief in democratizing philanthropy and making legacy giving accessible to diverse community members beyond major donors.



COMMUNITY BUILDERS LEGACY FUND



WHAT IS A COMMUNITY BUILDER?


Community Builders are people who are interested in making Prince Edward Island an even better place to live – both for this generation and generations to follow.  Community builders want to give back to their community and want to start doing now.  Community builders are thinkers, planners, and doers in all walks of life.  They realize that their successes resulted from help by many people and, through establishing a legacy fund; they can say thanks while making the community a better place for others.


WHAT DO COMMUNITY BUILDERS DO?


Community Builders can be recognized by what they do, even though all community builders do not do the same thing.  Some build quietly and out of sight, others build publicly; most have a passion whether for the environment, heritage, education, health, religion, or the general welfare of friends and neighbours.  They are active in volunteering, contributing, leading, supporting and giving.  They help out today but also think about tomorrow.  They also set aside funds to support their passions even when they are no longer here to do so.



WHAT IS A LEGACY FUND?


A Legacy Fund is an endowment the income from which is available in perpetuity to support good works and causes identified by the community builder.  Such endowments may be created at the time of death; however, increasingly community builders participate in both the planning and building of an endowment while they are still alive.  In this way, they can participate in the design of their legacy and the decision-making as to which causes gifts may be given.  Community builders also may, if they so desire, continue as advisors to their investments even after assigning ownership to CFPEI.



HOW DOES ON START A LEGACY FUND?


Starting a Legacy Fund is easy.  The first step is to talk to someone at the Community foundation of Prince Edward Island.  They will explain options available and the procedures to be followed.  Once your interests and approach becomes clear, you shoulod talk with your financial advisor about what you can afford and how you will ay for it.  The actual establishment is easy.  You make a contribution and receive a charitable receipt.  Then you enter a simple agreement with the Foundation setting out the name and purpose of the fund  and the conditions, if any, guiding the disbursement of grants. Agreements confirm any discussions between the donor and the Foundation as to how the funds will be invested, purposes for which grants will be given and related matters.  The actual paperwork takes only a few minutes to comlete and you receive a copy for your records.
