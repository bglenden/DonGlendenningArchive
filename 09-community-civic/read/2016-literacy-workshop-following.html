Adult literacy levels on PEI 


Literacy levels of PEI adults (ages 16-65} are at the OECD average level; the same can be said for numeracy. That’s good news for PEI but still leaves more than 40 percent of the Island workforce lacking the skill level needed to participate in a modern economy.


The numbers were presented by Barbara McNutt, Manager of Literacy/Essential Skills, Department of Workforce and Advanced Learning, Charlottetown, as part of an overview of adult literacy services available on PEI. The figures come from a survey in 24 countries by the Programme for the International of Assessment Adult Competencies (PIAAC). MacNutt also noted that the average age of enrolees in PEI adult upgrading programs is declining, especially in the Charlottetown area. The reason is not clear but may reflect, at least in part, that high school students skip some high school courses knowing that they can be picked up later at Holland College.


It is obvious from the discussion that literacy is viewed as a critical issue for the future of PEI. Gaps were identified by the Community Foundation during its survey of youth in late 2013, and again by the Charlottetown Chamber of Commerce in a presentation to a Standing Committee of the Legislature. Although not mentioned specifically, it is likely part of the “transition issue” identified in the media release by the Council of Ministers of Education in July 2016. According to Don Glendenning, President of Education 20/20, “I cannot think of a substantial conversation about education that did not include a reference to the improvement of basic skill levels, especially literacy and numeracy”.
