If giving is one of the family values you want to pass along, we can help your family build a tradition of giving.

Community Builders are people who are interested in making Prince Edward Island an even better place to live – both for this generation and generations to follow. Community builders want to give back to their community and want to start doing it now. Community builders are thinkers, planners, and doers in all walks of life. They realize that their successes resulted from help by many people and, through establishing a legacy fund, they can say thanks while making the community a better place for others.


What do Community Builders do?

Community Builders can be recognized by what they do, even though all community builders do not do the same thing. Some build quietly and out of sight, others build publicly; most have a passion whether for the environment, heritage, education, health, religion, or the general welfare of friends and neighbours. They are active in volunteering, contributing, leading, supporting and giving. They help out today but also think about tomorrow. They also set aside funds to support their passions even when they are no longer here to do so.


What is a Community Builder Fund?

A Community Builder Fund is an endowment the income from which is available in perpetuity to support good works and causes identified by the community builder. Such endowments may be created at the time of death; however, increasingly community builders participate in both the planning and building of an endowment while they are still alive. In this way, they can participate in the design of their legacy and the decision-making as to which causes gifts may be given. Community builders also may, if they so desire, continue as advisors to their investments even after assigning ownership to CFPEI.

The Community Foundation of Prince Edward Island (The Foundation) is dedicated to improving the quality of life for all Islanders. We are a local organization with deep roots in the community, however we are part of a nationwide network, one of 168 in Canada.


Currently, The Foundation administers 50 funds with a value at almost $1.3 million. In a typical year, The Foundation provides grants in excess of $60,000 to Island charitable organizations such as the QEH, CPR Anytime (Heart & Stroke), library books for the improvement of reading, transportation for the disabled, scholarships, support of youth athletics and initiatives such as SAVE (Students Against Violence Everywhere), environmental, counseling, Home Heating Program, etc.


The Foundation is committed to creating value within the charitable sector; we are experienced in designing and administering endowments throughout the Island in all charitable sectors. We are proud of our record as stewards of community charitable resources (the charitable dollar) and operate in an open, transparent and responsive manner. Our services are open to you without cost as you think about your charitable legacy.
