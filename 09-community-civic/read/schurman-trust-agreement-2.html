We, The Trustees of the Robert C. Schurman Memorial Trust Fund, (The Donors), are now desirous of transferring the capital of the said Trust to the Community Foundation of Prince Edward Island (The Foundation) and, furthermore, have the authority to do so and are doing so pursuant to section 4 of the 1973 agreement, and for the purpose set out in Section 3 of the 1973 agreement. In so doing, the Foundation agrees to establish the Robert C. Schurman Memorial Trust, under the conditions described below:



 

  1. The Donor(s) wish to establish an endowment at the Foundation to honour the lifetime contribution of Robert C. Schurman to minor hockey on Prince Edward Island, and agree to transfer an initial amount of $ __,___ into the Fund at the Foundation at the time of signing of this agreement


  1. The Fund shall be known as the Robert C. Schurman Memorial Trust Endowment Fund.


  1. The purpose of the Fund is to enable the Foundation to award grants for the improvement of the Minor Hockey Program on Prince Edward Island covering all classifications up to and including juvenile classification and provided such expenditures are within the aims and objectives of the Prince Edward Island Minor Hockey Council.


  1. The Foundation shall provide a cheque annually payable to The P.E.I. Amateur Sport Trust Fund for onward transmission to the Minor Hockey Council of Prince Edward Island.


  1. The Foundation will, where applicable, issue Donors with charitable tax receipts for all donations made to the Fund.


  1. It is the intent of the Foundation to maintain the permanent endowment for charitable purposes, without expenditure of the principal.


  1. The Capital of the Fund shall be invested according to the Foundations’ “Statement of Investment Policies and Guidelines” of which a current copy is attached as Appendix A.


  1. The amount available for granting from the permanent endowment will be determined annually by the Foundations’ Granting Policy. The current policy specifies the amount available for granting as 4% of the rolling average of the 4 prior year-end fair market values (FMV) of the portfolio.


  1. Grants are to be made only to qualified recipients as defined by the CCRA, for the purpose stated.


  1. he Foundation may charge an annual administration fee for endowment funds, currently set at one (1%) percent of the FMV, which is consistent with charges to other funds of similar size and nature under its management.


11 The Foundation will promote the Fund in its reports and promotional materials.


12 The Fund shall remain open for appropriate future donations, and the Foundation will issue charitable receipts as required.


13 Although assets of the Fund may be combined with other assets of the Foundation for purposes of investment, the Fund will be separately identified in Foundation reports.


13 The Foundation agrees to provide the Donors with information about the Fund at least annually.


  1. hould the cause for which the Fund was established cease to exist or for some other reason cease to be appropriate, the Foundation may use the income from the Fund for a purpose which is compatible with the purpose originally intended.


  1. By accepting this gift from the Donors, The Foundation releases the Donors from any and all future obligations.


OR


The Trustees of the fund, provided they act in good faith, shall not be held liable for any loss occasioned to the trust fund except for the loss caused by the Trustees dishonesty, gross negligence of wilful breach of trust



The Donors acknowledge that they have received information about the Foundation, including investment and granting policies, and that the Fund will be administered in accordance with Foundation policies.
