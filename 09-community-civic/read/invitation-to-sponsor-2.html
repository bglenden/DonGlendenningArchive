Formal letter from Don Glendenning to a prospective sponsor (addressed to "Gerry") soliciting $100 sponsorship for Beyond Measure. References previous education film screening and presents the film as addressing test-driven education causing student stress. Emphasizes that efficient curricula have become ineffective, leading to increased demand for school counselors. Invites participation and discussion; explains anticipated financial shortfall despite $5 admission; requests contribution from Community Foundation or direct mailing. Demonstrates Don's personal outreach style, connecting film themes to observable community consequences (counselor demand).

Dear Gerry:

As you may recall, our group sponsored an educational fil, earlier this year; today we are announcing another, Beyond Measure, which comes very highly recommended.

“Every day we hear stories about America’s troubled education system. And we’re told that. in order to fix what’s broken, we need to narrow our curricula, standardize our classrooms, and find new ways to measure students and teachers. But what if these “fixes” are making our schools worse?”


Beyond Measure, based on a book of the same name, is. about schools in the USA; but it could be about schools in Canada also; it’s about schools that narrow their offerings, add more tests and generally stress their students. Students are so busy studying that they have no time to learn. Obsession with efficiency has blinded us to the issue of effectiveness. No wonder waiting lists for school counsellors are getting longer.


The film will be shown at The City Cinema, 64 King Street, Charlottetown on Sunday, October 16, from 2-4:00 pm – with discussion to follow. We’d like you to be in the audience to view the film and participate in the discussion to follow. Although we plan to charge an entrance fee of $5.00, there will still be a shortfall of several hundred dollars. This letter comes as a request for some financial assistance, say $100, to help offset that shortfall, but any support will be appreciated.



Cheques should be made out to the Education 20/20 Inc. and mailed to the above address. For convenience, your cheque may also be dropped off at the Community Foundation of Prince Edward Island, Suite 105, Queen Square Place, 119-121 Queen St., Charlottetown, C1A 4B3. Donations of $25.00 or more are eligible for a charitable receipt.



I thank you for your support and we look forward to seeing you at the film.

Sincerely

Don Glendenning
