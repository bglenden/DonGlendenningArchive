Similar numeracy initiative letter addressed to Robert Irving, co-CEO of J.D. Irving Limited. Don presents the same gap analysis between improved math test scores and unchanged practical shop-floor math skills. He requests help covering the $15,000 cost of a two-day brainstorming analysis using the Community Foundation as intermediary for charitable receipts. This letter demonstrates Don's ability to engage private business leadership in education initiatives and his understanding that numeracy affects workforce competitiveness.
Community Foundation & Civic Life


February 25, 2019

Robert Irving, co—CEO

J. D. Irving Limited

300 Union St

Saint John, NB E2L 4M3 1-3-DI


Dear

Math Instruction on PEI


Although PEI math scores on National and international tests have risen, friends tell me that math has not improved on the shop floor; I’ve been exploring why.


Islanders need math; they need to be able to measure, sort, calculate, group and estimate numbers, space, time, distance, etc. and they need to do it quickly, easily, accurately; and consistently. This approach to math is called numeracy and it involves the application of math to every-day lives. Numeracy is being adopted elsewhere; usually in addition to traditional math.


But we need to do more, to begin with we need to identify math skills used by Islanders. Making an analysis involves bringing together a small group for a two- day of brainstorming session. It’s a proven technique, we used it regularly at Holland College but apparently there is no one on PEI now with the expertise. We’ll have to bring someone in at a cost $15,000. The making of an analysis is described in the attached paper.


I am wondering if you can help to help cover the cost. Although we are a charitable organization, we opened an account at The Community Foundation, 121 Queen Street, Charlottetown, PE, C1A 4B3; they will accept donations on our behalf and issue charitable receipts. Enclosed you will find a description of the project, a brochure about Education 20/20 Inc. and some notes about my search


I appreciate your consideration of this request and would be happy to explain further and answer any questions. Thanks in advance


Sincerely,



Don Glendenning

1-1-num
