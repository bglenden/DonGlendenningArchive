A follow-up letter dated May 4, 2013, updating Michael Schurman on Education 20/20 Inc.'s progress since February 2012. Don reports that workshops have been held, awards are on schedule, and a newsletter has been distributed. He mentions additional committee work organizing community workshops, website completion, expanded board membership, and investigation of adult reading levels. Don requests sustained support of $1,000 annually for three years and emphasizes that education change requires public support and information dissemination. He reiterates that the Community Foundation will receive and receipt donations.

Dear Mr. Schurman:


This is a follow-up to my letter of February 1 in which I spoke about education, the need for change, the newly created Education 20/20 Inc. and about plans for workshops, a newsletter, and award to schools; since then workshops have been held, the award is on schedule and a copy of our first newsletter was mailed to you a few days ago.


Recently a committee has been put in place to organize additional workshops in a number of Island communities, finishing touches are being put on a website, additional Directors have been added and we’ve been investigating the matter of low reading levels among adult Islanders. The enclose invitation to a workshop on May 14 on “on-line course delivery” may be of interest.


There is widespread agreement, Mike, that education needs to change, but change does not come easy. Change in education will be driven primarily through the impact of information and public support; your financial support does both. While any and all financial support is appreciated, a donation of $1,000 a year for three years would provide some stability to the initiative.


As I mentioned earlier, contributions on our behalf should be directed to the Community Foundation of Prince Edward Island, 119 Queen Street, Charlottetown, PE C1A 4B3 who will issue charitable receipts.


Together, we can make a difference


Sincerely



Don Glendenning

President
