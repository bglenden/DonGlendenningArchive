Community Foundation News

No. Draft , 2006



What we do:


In practice, The Foundation carries out four functions:

  • Informing Islanders of needs and opportunities and assisting them in designing charitable responses to match their interests and resources.

  • Providing a liaison and coordination role with individuals and organizations engaged in charitable work.

  • Making grants to reflect donors wishes.

  • Administering and investing funds.


What we’ve done since January 1, 2006:


  • Held conversations with agencies and donors with an interest in our services

  • Added $xx for new and existing endowments.

  • Accepted an invitation to participate in the distribution of monies arising from a class action suit in the transportation field.

  • Publicized our scholarship offerings

  • Revised our web pages generally; added application forms of scholarships; added newsletters


What we’re doing now:


  • Organizing a US citizens Day

  • Completing arrangements for a new family fund in the health field

  • Preparing to report on our activities in 2005 including our annual meeting

  • Seeking funds for a community development project

  • Meeting with a committee of summer Islanders to explore mutual interests

  • Holding discussions about a youth project for which we have a small amount of funding


On the horizon:


  • Wind-up of arts stabilization project (IAHSP)

  • Visit to neighbouring community foundations
