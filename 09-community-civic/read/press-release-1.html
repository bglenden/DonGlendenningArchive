Draft Press Release for       , 2008


Living Memorial Trust Established


Islanders have another option when contributing to the Living Trust of The Community Foundation of Prince Edward  Island; the Foundation, in partnership with the Department of tourism, has designated Birch Crossing, Elmwood, PEI  as its planting site for 2008.  Space has ben set aside for planting a cluster of trees on each side of the entrance to The Confederation Trail  as well as sites for general planting and named trees.


This is the second in the Foundation’s designated sites; the other being theConfederation Trail entrance in Wood Island where, last year, a cluster of trees was planted on each side of the entrance to the Confederation Trail in memory of Dr. Charles Keuper.


The Living Trust was established through the efforts of the Keuper Family to remember the life of Dr. Charles Keuper, a physician who came to PEI after retirement in 1979 and worked tirelessly to protect and enhance the Island’s natural beauty.    


Living Memorials are more than just a tribute; living memorials enhance and beautify our environment, provide shelter for wild life, reduce erosion and improve the quality of the air we breathe.  Grants may be made for the enhancement of any public space, however, the initial focus will be on trail entrances.  


The Living Trust provides an opportunity for people to thank a friend, remember a relative, of mark a special occasion.  Individual interested in plantings in places of special significance should contact the Foundation.  Donors will be given a receipt to be used when filing Income Tax returns in Canada or the USA 


Persons interested in learning more about, or contributing to the Living Trust, may do so by contacting the Community Foundation office, 892-3440..
