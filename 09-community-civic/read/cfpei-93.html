Newsletter #93 (January 2009) providing comprehensive overview of the Community Foundation of PEI's core functions and capabilities. Describes the Foundation's role in building endowments, creating community value, providing leadership, and accepting diverse forms of contributions. Glendenning uses the newsletter format to educate donors and community members about philanthropic vehicles available through CFPEI, emphasizing accessibility and flexibility in charitable giving.


Community Foundation News

No. 93 January 19, 2009


CFPEI:

  • Has three main functions: building endowments, making grants that create value and providing community leadership


CFPEI:

  • Promotes long term giving through the establishment of endowments

  • Applies a granting policy that creates value within the charitable sector

  • Exerts leadership through convening and liaison

CFPEI

  • Connects people who care with causes that matter

  • Designs endowments to support the interests of donors

  • Invests funds and monitors performance

  • Provides regular reports to donors on fund performance and granting activity

  • Is subject to an annual audit each year by an external auditor

  • Provides an annual public report to donors

  • Files an annual report with the Canada Revenue Agency and the US Internal Revenue Agency

  • Makes grants that reflect a donor’s wishes

  • Allows a donor to keep an estate intact while making grants to charities of interest to the donor

  • Provides information and advice to individuals, organizations and other foundations on existing and potential community needs

  • Accepts donations of any kind including cash, real estate, personal property, stocks, bonds and other financial instruments

  • Can help you create a legacy to fit your interests

  • Provides an easy way to you to give back to your community
