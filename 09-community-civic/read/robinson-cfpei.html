Community Foundation & Civic Life

John Robinson

I’m pleased to say a few words about John Robinson and to thank him for his support and leadership in the life of the community in general and the work of  the Community Foundation in particular

I’ve known John for 20 years, first as a successful Island business man and entrepreneur, a man, well known in the agriculture industry for progressive farming practices, leadership in the sector and stewardship of the land.  While his business was farming, his interests included the agricultural sector and the Island as a whole.  The Robinson family was, and still is, active in their church, supporters of community organizations and events, and play leadership roles in many areas especially in supporting arts and culture. 

I met john Robinson when he joined the Board of the Community Foundation of Prince Edward Island in 1999; he soon adopted a proprietary interest in its future, served wherever help was needed, including three terms as President.  In the early years of the Foundation, John led the Foundation in the establishment of sound investment policies and business practices.  John recognized PEI summer visitors as significant contributors to PEI life, especially to our arts and culture, and took steps to engage them in summer activities, most notably a reception on July 4 for US visitors and a summer lecture series so that guests could get to know the Island he loved.  

Perhaps his greatest contribution, however, was that of positioning the  Foundation as a key component of the provinces infrastructure, a component committed to building a pool of funds to be available in perpetuity for the betterment of the Island community – and he is having  considerable success.  Under his direction, and thanks to his energy and leadership, Foundation now has  assets  of thirteen million dollars – and he has visions for continuing growth.

In addition to the above, John has an unusually high commitment to the cause of community foundations, is a fine person, a respected member of the community, a world traveler, a gracious host, a beloved father and grandfather, and a good friend.  I can think of no better person to epitomize the concept of a smart and caring community

I now know him as a friend, someone interested in wat I’m up to, my family, my woodlot , the Foundation ‘ and the community at large. Thank you, John for your many contributions to me, my family and our chosen home.

Sincerely

Donald Glendenning  C.M.
