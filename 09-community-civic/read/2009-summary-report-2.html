COMMUNITY FOUNDATION OF PRINCE EDWARD ISLAND

Agricultural Heritage Report


A small committee linked to the Community Foundation has been promoting the conservation and restoration of Island-made agricultural equipment; the continuing core committee consists of Don Glendenning who serves as Chair, Dr. Tom Hall, Tony Glencross, and Charley Coles.  

Accomplishments:


Conducted an exploratory survey or early Island farm equipment and published a preliminary report, Made in PEI; Copies are available from the Foundation office.

Arranged for a number of articles to be donated to PEI museums including: a side panel of a Hall thresher used in the Cambridge area used by the original owner to record the date and number of bushels threshed; a Hall thresher, items from a carriage shop in Elliotvale, wheels from a Hall and Stavert conversion tractor, as well as some miscellaneous articles

Discussions were held with a number of representatives of PEI Museums to highlight the existence of PEI made equipment and the need to inventory and preserve it for future generations.

An Agricultural Heritage Fund, an endowment, was established within the Community Foundation of Prince Edward Island.

Support to the PEI Association of Community Museums in its virtual display of farm equipment.

Several invitational meetings for the purpose of information exchange and networking for private collectors and other interested Islanders.

An occasional newsletter directed to private collectors would be welcomed

Support for a workshop on equipment restoration

A tour of the collection of Dr. Roy Montgomery

.

Conclusions 


Additional dry and secure storage space is needed especially for farm equipment.

Linkages between museums and private collectors should be strengthened

There are a significant number of pieces of Island-made farm equipment that should be saved

A comprehensive electronic database of Island-made farm machinery and equipment should be established

Private collectors could benefit from technical assistance especially in conservation

Private collectors are interested in continuing to network and to visit other private collectors.

The Agricultural Heritage Fund at CFPEI should be promoted as a mechanism to provide future funding for special projects.


Proposed Next Steps


Update and expand  “Made in PEI”.

Convene an Island-wide meeting of private collectors in early 2010

Undertake a quite fund raiser to increase both operating and endowment funds
